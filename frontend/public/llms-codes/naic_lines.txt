[NL] NAIC Lines of Business -- NL is the NAIC code that groups all non-life insurance lines on the annual statement, encompassing property, casualty and other non-life coverages. It is used to report combined financial results for these lines rather than separating them individually.
[NL.01] Fire -- NL.01 is the NAIC line-of-business code for fire insurance, covering policies that protect property against loss or damage caused by fire. It appears on annual statements to report premiums, reserves, losses and expenses specifically related to fire coverage.
[NL.02] Allied Lines -- NL.02 Allied Lines includes insurance contracts that provide coverage for miscellaneous property and casualty risks not otherwise classified, such as general liability, motor liability, and other specialty lines.
[NL.03] Farmowners Multiple Peril -- NL.03 represents the Farmowners Multiple Peril line of business in the NAIC Annual Statement, covering insurance for farm property and liability. It typically includes protection for farm structures, equipment, livestock, crops, and related losses from perils such as fire, wind, hail, and liability incidents arising from farm operations.

[NL.06] Mortgage Guaranty -- NL.06 Mortgage Guaranty is the NAIC line-of-business code for insurers that issue guarantees protecting lenders against borrower default on mortgage loans, typically covering principal and interest obligations if the borrower fails to pay. This category includes entities that provide mortgage-related credit enhancement but do not hold the mortgage itself.
[NL.08] Ocean Marine -- NL.08 - Ocean Marine refers to insurance coverage for vessels, cargo and related liabilities associated with maritime activities on the open sea. It includes policies for hull protection, marine liability, protection and indemnity, and cargo loss or damage for ships engaged in international or domestic ocean transport. This line of business is reported separately on NAIC annual statements to track the financial performance of marine insurers.
[NL.09] Inland Marine -- NL.09 Inland Marine is a NAIC line of business covering insurance for movable property and equipment, including goods in transit, stored items, and items installed at a location but not affixed to the structure. It encompasses coverage for transportation, contractors' equipment, and other mobile assets that are not part of a fixed premises.
[NL.10] Financial Guaranty -- The NL.10 line of business, labeled Financial Guaranty, covers insurers that issue policies guaranteeing the payment of principal and interest on bonds, loans, leases and other financial obligations, or that insure the performance of such obligations. These companies assume credit risk and provide a financial safety net for investors and lenders.
[NL.11] Medical Malpractice -- NL.11 refers to the line of business for medical malpractice coverage, reporting premiums, losses and expenses incurred by insurers that underwrite liability protection for health-care professionals against claims of professional negligence. It appears on the NAIC Annual Statement to track the financial performance of this specialty liability market.
[NL.12] Earthquake -- NL.12 - Earthquake is the NAIC line-of-business code used to report premiums, losses and expenses for policies covering damage caused by seismic activity. Insurers offering residential or commercial earthquake coverage file under this code as part of the broader property insurance category.
[NL.13] Group Accident and Health -- NL.13 denotes the Group Accident and Health line of business in the NAIC Annual Statement, covering policies that provide medical, disability and accidental death benefits to groups of individuals, often through employers or associations. This classification aggregates premiums, losses and expenses related to group health, accident and related health insurance coverage.
[NL.14] Credit Accident and Health -- NL.14 Credit Accident and Health is a line of business in which insurers offer health and accident coverage that is tied to a credit arrangement, often included as part of a loan or financing package. The policy pays for medical expenses, disability or death benefits caused by accidents while the credit remains outstanding.
[NL.15] Other Accident and Health -- NL.15 "Other Accident and Health" covers insurance policies that provide coverage for accidental injuries, sickness, or health-related expenses that do not fall under the standard accident, sickness, or disability lines, including miscellaneous personal accident, travel medical, and supplemental health benefits. It aggregates a variety of ancillary health products not separately categorized in the NAIC schedule.
[NL.16] Workers Compensation -- NL.16 represents the Workers Compensation line of business on the NAIC Annual Statement, covering statutory policies that provide employers with benefits for employees who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses, including medical expenses, wage replacement and rehabilitation costs, and reporting premiums, claims and loss reserves for this coverage.
[NL.17] Other Liability -- NL.17 Other Liability is a catch-all line of business on the NAIC Annual Statement used by insurers to report liability coverage that does not fall into the standard categories such as general, professional, or product liability. It captures miscellaneous or specialty liability exposures, allowing regulators to track premiums, losses and reserves for these less common risks.
[NL.18] Products Liability -- NL.18 Products Liability is the NAIC line-of-business code for insurance that covers manufacturers, distributors, and retailers against legal responsibility for bodily injury or property damage caused by their products. It includes claims arising from product defects, failures, or improper labeling, and is reported on the annual statement of insurers.
[NL.19] Private Passenger Auto Liability -- NL.19 denotes the Private Passenger Auto Liability line of business on the NAIC Annual Statement. It captures the insurer's liability exposure for accidents involving privately-owned passenger automobiles, including bodily injury and property damage claims. The data are used for regulatory monitoring of underwriting and reserving adequacy.
[NL.20] Commercial Auto Liability -- The NAIC code NL.20 designates the Commercial Auto Liability line of business on insurers' annual statements. It captures liability coverage for vehicles used in business operations, including reported premiums, losses, and related expense items for bodily injury and property-damage claims.
[NL.21] Auto Physical Damage -- NL.21 records the premiums, losses and related financial data for the Auto Physical Damage line of business, which covers property damage to insured automobiles such as collision, comprehensive and other non-bodily-injury coverages. This entry is used by insurers to report the profitability and exposure of that specific auto coverage on the NAIC annual statement.
[NL.22] Aircraft -- NL.22 refers to the Aircraft line of business in the NAIC annual statement. It captures all insurance written on aircraft, including hull, liability, passenger and cargo coverage, and is used for reporting premiums, losses and expenses for aviation risks
[NL.23] Fidelity -- NL.23 Fidelity is a NAIC annual-statement line-of-business code that captures premiums, claims, reserves and related financial data for fidelity insurance, which covers loss caused by employee dishonesty, theft or fraud. Insurers report this information for regulatory oversight and industry statistics.

[NL.26] Burglary and Theft -- NL.26 covers insurance policies that protect against losses from burglary and theft, including forced entry, larceny and related property damage. It is used by insurers to report premiums, claims and expenses for these perils on both residential and commercial accounts.
[NL.27] Boiler and Machinery -- The NL.27 Boiler and Machinery line records premiums, losses and expenses for policies that insure boilers, pressure vessels, and other mechanical equipment against breakdown, explosion or related damage, typically under equipment-breakdown coverage. It is used by insurers to report the financial results of this specialized property risk.
[NL.28] Credit -- NL.28 Credit refers to insurance coverage that protects borrowers and lenders against loss arising from a debtor's inability to meet credit obligations. It includes products such as credit-life, credit-disability, and other credit-protection policies that are written on loans, lines of credit, or other forms of financing.
[NL.29] International -- NL.29 International refers to the line of business on the NAIC annual statement that reports premiums, claims, and expenses from insurance contracts covering foreign risks or policies issued to non-U.S. entities. It aggregates all activities involving international exposure under one reporting category.
[NL.30] Warranty -- NL.30 - Warranty represents the line of business on the NAIC annual statement that records premiums, losses and related expenses for warranty insurance, which provides coverage to manufacturers, retailers or service providers for repair, replacement or compensation when a sold product fails to meet its promised performance within a specified period.
[NL.34] Aggregate Other Lines -- NL.34 - Aggregate Other Lines is a catch-all category on the NAIC Annual Statement that combines premiums, losses, expenses and reserves for all lines of insurance not separately listed elsewhere, providing a total figure for miscellaneous business activities.
