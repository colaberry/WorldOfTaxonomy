[01] Live Animals -- Code 01 in the Schedule B system classifies live animals, encompassing all mammal, bird, reptile, fish and other vertebrate species that are alive when shipped. The category covers animals exported for breeding, food, research, pets, exhibitions or any other purpose.
[02] Meat and Edible Meat Offal -- Code 02 includes raw, unprocessed meat from livestock and poultry and edible offal such as liver, kidneys, heart, and other consumable internal organs, whether fresh, chilled, or frozen, for reporting in the Schedule B export statistics.
[03] Fish, Crustaceans, Molluscs -- Entry 03 includes all fish, crustaceans and molluscs exported from the United States, covering fresh, frozen, dried, canned and otherwise processed seafood products. It is the designation used by exporters and customs for reporting these commodities under the Schedule B system.
[04] Dairy Produce; Birds' Eggs; Honey -- Schedule B code 04 covers dairy products such as milk, cheese, butter and related items, together with bird eggs and honey that are exported from the United States. These commodities are grouped for statistical reporting and customs documentation.
[05] Products of Animal Origin -- Schedule B code 05 covers all commodities derived from animals, including meat, fish, dairy products, eggs, live animals, skins, hides, and related by-products such as animal fats and oils. These items are reported for export statistics to track United States trade in animal-origin goods.
[06] Live Trees and Other Plants -- Schedule B code 06 includes live trees, shrubs, vines, and other living plants such as seeds, bulbs, cut foliage and potted specimens that are exported from the United States. The classification is used by customs and trade statistics to record shipments of horticultural and landscaping products.
[07] Edible Vegetables and Certain Roots -- Schedule B code 07 covers all edible vegetables and a select group of roots that are exported from the United States, including fresh, chilled, frozen, dried, and processed forms such as canned or pickled items, but excluding non-food plant parts.
[08] Edible Fruit and Nuts -- Schedule B entry 08 covers all edible fresh, frozen, dried, or otherwise processed fruits and nuts that are exported from the United States, including varieties such as apples, citrus, berries, almonds, walnuts and other commercial fruit and nut products.
[09] Coffee, Tea, Mate and Spices -- Entry 09 covers the export of coffee and tea products, including roasted or green coffee beans, ground coffee, tea leaves, instant tea and related beverages such as mate, as well as a range of culinary spices like cinnamon, pepper, cloves, nutmeg, and other dried or ground flavoring agents.
[10] Cereals -- Code 10 in the Schedule B classification covers cereals, encompassing raw and processed grains such as wheat, rice, corn, barley, oats, rye and related products. The entry is used by exporters to report shipments of these staple agricultural commodities to foreign markets, facilitating statistical tracking and compliance with U.S. export regulations.
[11] Milling Industry Products -- Schedule B code 11 groups commodities produced by the milling sector such as wheat flour, cornmeal, other grain flours, meal, grits, and related processed grain products for export. It encompasses both refined and coarse milled items that are sold as food ingredients or finished grain-based foods. The classification helps U.S. customs track the value and volume of these milling outputs in international trade.
[12] Oil Seeds, Oleaginous Fruits -- Schedule B code 12 designates oil seeds and oleaginous fruits, including commodities such as soybeans, rapeseed, sunflower seed, sesame, peanuts and other oil-producing seeds and fruits. These products are classified for U.S. export reporting to track the trade of edible and industrial oils and meals derived from them.
[13] Lac; Gums, Resins and Other Vegetable Saps -- Entry 13 groups natural plant-derived products such as lac, a resin secreted by insects, and a range of gums, resins and vegetable saps harvested from trees and shrubs; these materials are used in adhesives, food additives, pharmaceuticals, and other industrial applications.
[14] Vegetable Plaiting Materials -- Schedule B code 14 includes raw or processed plant-derived fibers used for plaiting, such as wicker, rattan, bamboo and similar vegetable materials that are woven into baskets, furniture, mats and other woven products for export.
[15] Animal, Vegetable or Microbial Fats and Oils -- Code 15 designates all animal, vegetable or microbial fats and oils exported from the United States, whether crude, refined, edible, industrial or specialty. It includes products such as lard, butterfat, vegetable oils, fish oils, microbial lipids and their derivatives used in food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, lubricants and other applications.
[16] Preparations of Meat, Fish or Crustaceans -- Code 16 covers all processed foods made from meat, fish or crustaceans, including items that have been cooked, cured, smoked, canned, frozen, dried or otherwise prepared for consumption. It includes mixtures, blends and ready-to-eat products such as sausages, pâtés, fish sticks and marinated seafood.
[17] Sugars and Sugar Confectionery -- Schedule B code 17 covers raw and refined sugars, including granulated, powdered and liquid forms, as well as sugar-based foods such as candy, chocolate-coated confections, chewing gum, and other sweetened confectionery products manufactured or prepared for export. It excludes sugar substitutes and products primarily composed of other ingredients with only incidental sugar content.
[18] Cocoa and Cocoa Preparations -- Entry 18 includes raw cocoa beans, cocoa liquor, cocoa butter, cocoa powder, chocolate and other processed cocoa products used in food, beverage, cosmetic and pharmaceutical applications, covering both unroasted and roasted beans and any mixtures or derivatives made from them.
[19] Preparations of Cereals, Flour, Starch or Milk -- Schedule B entry 19 classifies products that are preparations made primarily from cereals, flour, starch or milk, including breakfast cereals, baked goods, instant mixes and sauces where these ingredients are the main component. It is used to code such processed food exports from the United States.
[20] Preparations of Vegetables, Fruit, Nuts -- Schedule B code 20 covers processed foods made from vegetables, fruit or nuts, including canned, frozen, dried, pickled, pureed or otherwise prepared products such as sauces, jams, juices and nut pastes. These items are classified for export reporting when they have been altered from their raw state through cooking, preserving or blending.
[21] Miscellaneous Edible Preparations -- Code 21 includes a wide variety of prepared food items that are not listed under more specific categories, such as sauces, soups, ready-to-eat meals, mixed or processed edible products and other miscellaneous food preparations intended for consumption.
[22] Beverages, Spirits and Vinegar -- Code 22 in the Schedule B system covers all beverages, spirits and vinegar exported from the United States, including water, soft drinks, fruit juices, coffee, tea, beer, wine, distilled spirits and various types of vinegar used for culinary or industrial purposes. It groups together both non-alcoholic and alcoholic drinks and related acidic condiments for statistical reporting.
[23] Residues and Waste from Food Industries -- Code 23 in the Schedule B system covers residues and waste generated by food processing operations, including by-products, scraps and unusable material removed during manufacturing. These items are classified for export reporting and are not considered finished food products.
[24] Tobacco and Manufactured Tobacco Substitutes -- Code 24 includes all tobacco products such as cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco, chewing and snuff, together with manufactured tobacco substitutes like herbal cigarettes, nicotine-containing vaping liquids and other non-leaf products used as tobacco replacements.
[25] Salt; Sulphur; Earths and Stone -- Schedule B code 25 includes common salt-rock, sea and evaporated varieties-elemental sulphur and its compounds, and a range of earths and stone products such as limestone, gypsum, sand, gravel, clay, and other natural mineral stones intended for export.
[26] Ores, Slag and Ash -- Schedule B code 26 includes natural and processed metallic ores, industrial slag and various types of ash that are exported from the United States. [27] Mineral Fuels, Oils and Products of Their Distillation -- Schedule B code 27 covers all mineral fuels, oils and products derived from their distillation, including crude petroleum, refined petroleum products such as gasoline, diesel and jet fuel, lubricating oils, and liquid hydrocarbons used for energy or industrial purposes. It groups these commodities for export reporting and statistical analysis.
[28] Inorganic Chemicals -- Schedule B code 28 groups all inorganic chemicals exported from the United States, encompassing acids, bases, salts, oxides, hydroxides, sulfides and other non-organic compounds used in industrial, agricultural and laboratory applications. The category aids customs and trade statistics by aggregating these substances under a single, searchable export classification.
[29] Organic Chemicals -- Schedule B code 29 designates organic chemicals, encompassing carbon-based substances such as solvents, polymers, acids, alcohols, and pharmaceutical ingredients that are shipped from the United States. The classification is used by U.S. Customs and trade statisticians to record and analyze exports of these chemical products.
[30] Pharmaceutical Products -- Schedule B code 30 designates all pharmaceutical products exported from the United States, encompassing medicines, vaccines, biologics, diagnostic preparations, and other therapeutic agents formulated for human or animal use. The category includes finished drug formulations, bulk active ingredients, and over-the-counter remedies classified under the Harmonized System.
[31] Fertilisers -- In Schedule B, code 31 covers fertilisers, including nitrogen, phosphorus and potash based products and compound formulations used to enhance plant growth. It is used for reporting US export shipments of these agricultural chemicals.
[32] Tanning, Dyeing Extracts; Paints; Varnishes -- This schedule B category (code 32) covers chemicals and extracts used for leather tanning and textile dyeing, together with liquid and paste paints and varnishes intended for protective or decorative coating, all tracked for U.S. export statistics.
[33] Essential Oils and Resinoids; Perfumery -- Schedule B entry 33 covers essential oils and resinoids intended for perfumery, including natural extracts and distilled liquids derived from aromatic plants, seeds, bark, flowers, or fruits, as well as resinous substances obtained by solvent extraction. These products are classified for export reporting as fragrance ingredients rather than bulk agricultural commodities.
[34] Soap, Washing Preparations, Lubricating Preparations -- Schedule B code 34 covers soaps and washing preparations such as bar soaps, liquid detergents, and other cleaning agents, as well as lubricating preparations including oils, greases and related compounds used for reducing friction. These items are classified for export reporting purposes under a single commodity group.
[35] Albuminoidal Substances; Modified Starches; Glues -- Code 35 in Schedule B covers albuminoidal substances such as gelatin and casein, chemically modified starches used as thickeners, stabilizers and binders, and a variety of glues and adhesives derived from animal, plant or synthetic sources, which are exported from the United States for food processing, textile, paper, pharmaceutical and other industrial applications.
[36] Explosives; Pyrotechnic Products; Matches -- Schedule B code 36 covers all explosive materials and related pyrotechnic items, including fireworks, detonators, primers, blasting agents and safety fuses, as well as various types of matches used for lighting. The classification is used by U.S. exporters to report shipments of these high-risk commodities for statistical and regulatory purposes.
[37] Photographic or Cinematographic Goods -- Schedule B code 37 includes photographic and cinematographic goods such as cameras, lenses, photographic film, video recorders, projectors, related accessories and processing chemicals, covering items used to capture, reproduce or display still or moving images for export reporting and trade statistics.
[38] Miscellaneous Chemical Products -- Schedule B code 38 covers a broad range of chemical substances that do not fall under more specific categories, including organic and inorganic compounds, intermediates, reagents, and specialty chemicals used in manufacturing, agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and research. Items are generally classified by their primary chemical composition and intended industrial application rather than by detailed product function.
[39] Plastics and Articles Thereof -- Schedule B code 39 covers plastics and all articles made from them, ranging from basic polymer granules and sheets to finished goods such as containers, tubes, films, and molded parts. It includes both thermoplastic and thermosetting polymers, as well as composite products incorporating plastics, but excludes rubber and natural fiber items. This classification is used for U.S. export reporting of all plastic-based commodities.
[40] Rubber and Articles Thereof -- Code 40 includes natural and synthetic rubber and all articles made from these materials, such as tires, tubes, hoses, belts, gaskets, molded and extruded rubber products, and other finished goods used in industrial, automotive, and consumer applications.
[41] Raw Hides and Skins (Other Than Furskins) and Leather -- Code 41 covers raw animal hides and skins, excluding furskins, as well as leather derived from those hides. It includes unprocessed pelts and leather that has been tanned but not yet fashioned into finished goods, and is used for products such as footwear, upholstery, and garments.
[42] Articles of Leather; Saddlery and Harness -- Entry 42 covers finished leather products used for riding and animal equipment, including saddles, bridles, reins, harnesses, and related accessories. It encompasses any article primarily composed of leather that functions as saddlery or harness gear, but excludes other leather goods such as footwear, bags or apparel which fall under different Schedule B headings.
[43] Furskins and Artificial Fur -- Schedule B entry 43 classifies furskins-processed animal pelts ready for further manufacturing-and artificial fur, the synthetic textile that imitates real hair, both used primarily in clothing, accessories and upholstery. The code is used by U.S. exporters to report shipments of these fiber products in trade statistics.
[44] Wood and Articles of Wood; Wood Charcoal -- Schedule B code 44 includes rough and processed timber, lumber, plywood, veneers, wood panels, and assorted manufactured wood items such as furniture, flooring, and building components, as well as charcoal produced from wood. It covers both domestically sourced and imported wood products destined for export from the United States.
[45] Cork and Articles of Cork -- Code 45 includes raw cork and all finished products made from cork, such as stoppers, sheets, flooring, insulation and other articles derived from the bark of the cork oak. The entry is used for statistical tracking of U.S. exports of both unprocessed cork and the range of cork-based items.
[46] Manufactures of Straw, Esparto or Other Plaiting Materials -- Code 46 in the Schedule B covers finished products made from straw, esparto or similar plaiting fibers, including items such as baskets, hats, mats, furniture components and other woven goods. [47] Pulp of Wood or of Other Fibrous Cellulosic Material -- Entry 47 includes pulp produced from wood or other fibrous cellulosic materials, such as chemical, mechanical and semi-chemical pulps used for making paper, cardboard and related products, covering both bleached and unbleached forms while excluding raw wood chips and lumber.
[48] Paper and Paperboard -- Schedule B code 48 refers to paper and paperboard, encompassing a wide range of manufactured paper products such as writing paper, printing paper, coated and uncoated board, folding cartons, and packaging containers. The classification groups items by material type and intended use for export reporting, distinguishing them from other paper-related categories like newsprint or specialty pulp. It aids U.S. customs in tracking the volume and value of paper-based goods shipped abroad.
[49] Printed Books, Newspapers, Pictures -- Code 49 covers printed publications such as books, newspapers, periodicals, magazines, pamphlets and related printed matter, as well as pictorial materials including photographs, prints, postcards and other visual reproductions. [50] Silk -- Silk (code 50) covers natural silk fibers harvested from silkworms and the products made from them, including raw or peeled silk, silk yarn, and woven or knitted silk fabrics. The entry is used for reporting exports of these high-value textile materials.
[51] Wool, Fine or Coarse Animal Hair -- Schedule B code 51 covers wool and animal hair, both fine and coarse varieties, in any condition from raw sheepskins to processed fibers. [52] Cotton -- Schedule B code 52 refers to cotton, encompassing raw and processed forms such as bales of lint, cotton fibers, waste and by-products, as well as certain unspun yarns and fabrics that have not been further manufactured into finished garments. [53] Other Vegetable Textile Fibres -- Schedule B code 53 covers vegetable-derived textile fibers that are not listed under more specific headings, such as sisal, jute, hemp, flax, and other plant-based fibers used for yarn, rope, or fabric production. [56] Wadding, Felt and Nonwovens -- Schedule B code 56 covers wadding, felt and nonwoven textile products, including any fibrous padding, sheet felt, or bonded fabric made from natural or synthetic fibers that are not woven or knitted. The category includes high-performance, flame-retardant, engineered or otherwise uniquely constructed woven materials that are not covered by other textile headings and are reported for U.S. export statistics.
[59] Impregnated, Coated, Covered or Laminated Textile Fabrics -- Schedule B code 59 covers textile fabrics that have been impregnated, coated, covered or laminated with other materials such as polymers, rubber, metal or other layers to alter their performance. It includes untrimmed hides, tanned or untanned leather, and pelts ready for further manufacturing or sale abroad.
[X] Pulp of Wood; Paper and Paperboard -- Schedule B code X groups together the raw and processed materials derived from wood fibers, including wood pulp, as well as finished paper and paperboard products such as printing paper, writing paper, cartons, and other coated or uncoated sheet forms used for packaging, publishing and industrial applications.
[XI] Textiles and Textile Articles -- Code XI covers all textile products and related articles exported from the United States. It includes natural and synthetic fibers, yarns, fabrics, garments, home furnishings and other manufactured textile goods. The classification is used for customs reporting and trade statistics.
[XII] Footwear, Headgear, Umbrellas -- Schedule B code XII groups together all exported articles of personal apparel and accessories that protect the feet, head or provide weather shelter, including shoes, boots, sandals, hats, caps, helmets and umbrellas. The classification is used by U.S. customs and trade statistics to record the volume and value of these items shipped abroad. It covers both domestic manufacturers and foreign producers supplying the U.S. market.
[XIII] Articles of Stone, Plaster, Cement, Ceramics, Glass -- Schedule B Chapter XIII covers finished products manufactured from stone, plaster, cement, ceramics and glass such as tiles, bricks, structural components, sanitary ware, decorative objects and other articles used in construction,
[XIV] Natural or Cultured Pearls, Precious Metals, Jewellery -- Schedule B entry XIV includes exports of natural and cultured pearls, precious metal articles and all types of jewellery made from those metals, such as necklaces, bracelets, rings and related ornamental pieces. It covers both finished goods and components intended for further manufacturing.
[XIX] Arms and Ammunition -- Schedule B code XIX covers all arms and ammunition exported from the United States, including firearms, artillery, small-arms, missiles, explosives, ammunition, and related components or accessories for military, law-enforcement or civilian use. The classification is used by the Census Bureau to record and analyze the volume and value of these defense-related commodities in trade statistics.
[XV] Base Metals and Articles of Base Metal -- Code XV includes primary base metals such as copper, aluminum, lead, zinc and nickel and the fabricated articles made from them, including sheets, plates, bars, wires, pipes and fittings. It is used in the Schedule B export system to classify shipments of uncoated or coated base-metal products.
[XVI] Machinery and Mechanical Appliances; Electrical Equipment -- Code XVI groups together a wide array of industrial and consumer machines, mechanical appliances, and related electrical equipment. It includes items such as engines, pumps, compressors, turbines, and heating or cooling apparatus, as well as generators, transformers, circuit breakers, wiring devices and other electrical components used to generate, distribute or control electricity.

[XVIII] Optical, Photographic, Cinematographic, Measuring Instruments -- Schedule B code XVIII covers optical, photographic and cinematographic equipment and measuring instruments, encompassing devices such as cameras, lenses, microscopes, telescopes, spectrometers, surveying tools and related accessories used for visual imaging, light manipulation and precise measurement. This classification groups items that capture or analyze images and those that provide quantitative data in scientific, industrial or consumer applications.
[XX] Miscellaneous Manufactured Articles -- Schedule B code XX groups together manufactured items that do not fit into any other specific category, covering a wide range of finished products such as small hardware, decorative objects, and assorted fabricated goods. It is used for statistical reporting of exports that are miscellaneous in nature and lack a more precise classification.
