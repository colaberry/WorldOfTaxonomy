They deliver comprehensive patient services, including examinations, procedures, and prescribing medications, and may practice singly or within group or institutional environments.

This includes allergists, immunologists and related specialists who perform testing, prescribe therapies, and manage conditions such as asthma, hay fever, allergic rhinitis, eczema and primary-immune deficiencies.
[A02] Anesthesiology -- A02-Anesthesiology identifies health-care providers who specialize in administering anesthesia and monitoring patients' vital functions during surgical, diagnostic or therapeutic procedures, as well as managing pain and perioperative care. The taxonomy groups physicians, nurse anesthetists and other qualified professionals who deliver these services.
[A03] Colon and Rectal Surgery -- A03 in the NUCC Health Care Provider Taxonomy identifies clinicians who specialize in colon and rectal surgery. [A04] Dermatology -- A04 denotes Dermatology, a medical specialty focused on diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of skin, hair, nail, and mucous membrane disorders. [A06] Family Medicine -- A06 Family Medicine identifies health-care providers whose primary focus is comprehensive outpatient care for individuals and families across all ages, emphasizing preventive services, chronic disease management, and coordination of specialty referrals. These clinicians typically hold an MD or DO and practice in private offices, clinics, or integrated health systems.
[A07] Internal Medicine -- A07 designates Internal Medicine, a specialty dedicated to the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases in adult patients. Internists deliver comprehensive, continuous care for both acute and chronic conditions, often coordinating with subspecialists to manage complex health issues.
[A08] Medical Genetics -- Medical Genetics providers specialize in diagnosing, counseling, and managing inherited disorders using genetic testing and family history analysis. They work with patients and families to assess risk, interpret results, and guide treatment or preventive strategies.
[A09] Neurological Surgery -- A09 designates providers who specialize in neurological surgery, performing operative procedures on the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves to treat disorders such as tumors, aneurysms, trauma, and congenital anomalies. These clinicians include neurosurgeons and surgical teams that manage pre-operative assessment, intra-operative care, and post-operative follow-up for patients requiring complex neuro-critical interventions.
[A10] Neurology -- Neurology (code A10) is a medical specialty devoted to the diagnosis, treatment and management of disorders of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves and muscles. Practitioners in this field address conditions such as epilepsy, stroke, multiple sclerosis, Parkinson's disease and neuropathic pain.
[A11] Nuclear Medicine -- A11 in the NUCC Health Care Provider Taxonomy identifies providers who specialize in nuclear medicine. These professionals perform diagnostic-imaging and therapeutic procedures using radioactive substances, including PET scans, bone scans, and radionuclide therapy, and they may work in hospitals, imaging centers, or research facilities.

[A25] Urology -- A25 identifies the urology specialty, encompassing providers who diagnose, medically manage, and surgically treat disorders of the urinary tract and male reproductive system, including conditions of the kidneys, bladder, ureters, prostate and related structures.

[D06] Pediatric Dentistry -- D06 in the NUCC Health Care Provider Taxonomy identifies a pediatric dentist, a dental specialist who delivers preventive, diagnostic, and therapeutic oral health services specifically to infants, children, adolescents and patients with special health care needs, focusing on growth-related dental issues, behavior management, and family-centered care.
[D07] Prosthodontics -- Prosthodontics is a dental specialty that focuses on restoring and replacing missing or damaged teeth, including the design, fabrication and placement of crowns, bridges, dentures, and dental implants.
