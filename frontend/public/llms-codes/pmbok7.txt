[PM] PMBOK 7 Domains -- PM refers to the collection of seven performance domains defined in the Project Management Body of Knowledge 7th Edition, outlining the areas of expertise a project manager must address to achieve value delivery, including stakeholder, team, development approach, planning, project work, delivery, and measurement. These domains provide a holistic framework for managing projects across diverse environments.

[PM.02] Team -- The Team performance domain focuses on creating, nurturing, and sustaining a collaborative, high-performing project team, emphasizing shared purpose, clear roles, effective communication, and continuous development. It guides project managers in fostering a culture of trust, empowerment, and adaptability to achieve project outcomes.
[PM.03] Development Approach and Life Cycle -- The Development Approach and Life Cycle performance domain defines how a project selects and adapts its delivery method-predictive, adaptive, or hybrid-and outlines the sequential phases or iterations that guide work from initiation through closure, ensuring the chosen approach aligns with stakeholder needs, organizational context, and the intended product or service.
[PM.04] Planning -- PM.04 Planning is the performance domain that guides the systematic definition of the project's objectives, scope, and approach, and the development of a cohesive roadmap for delivering value. It integrates stakeholders' expectations, resources, and constraints to create actionable plans that enable informed decision-making throughout the project lifecycle.
[PM.05] Project Work -- Project Work is the performance domain that focuses on the activities, tasks, and deliverables that transform inputs into outputs to achieve project objectives. It encompasses the execution of plans, coordination of resources, and monitoring of progress to ensure value delivery. Effective management of project work integrates other domains and supports stakeholder outcomes.
[PM.06] Delivery -- PM.06 Delivery is the performance domain that ensures project outcomes are produced and handed over to stakeholders in a timely, reliable manner. It emphasizes coordinating people, processes and technology to create, validate and transition results, maintaining alignment with quality standards and stakeholder expectations.
[PM.07] Measurement -- PM.07 Measurement is the performance domain that emphasizes the systematic collection, analysis, and interpretation of data to assess project progress and outcomes. It guides the use of quantitative and qualitative metrics, ensuring that decisions are evidence-based and that value delivery is continuously monitored and optimized.
[PM.08] Uncertainty -- PM.08 Uncertainty is the performance domain that guides project teams in recognizing, analyzing, and addressing unknown factors that could impact project objectives. It emphasizes systematic identification of risks and opportunities, assessment of their potential effects, and the development of responsive strategies to maintain value delivery.
[PM.09] Principles: Stewardship -- PM.09 addresses the stewardship principle, emphasizing the responsibility of project managers to act as caretakers of resources, knowledge, and stakeholder interests throughout a project's lifecycle. It calls for ethical conduct, sustainable use of assets, and transparent communication to preserve value and trust for current and future initiatives. This principle underpins all performance domains by guiding decision-making toward long-term benefit and accountability.
[PM.10] Principles: Team -- PM.10 highlights the Team principle, which stresses that project success depends on a cohesive, empowered, and diverse group working toward a shared purpose. It encourages psychological safety, clear roles, collaborative decision-making, and continuous learning to foster high-performing teamwork throughout the project lifecycle.
[PM.11] Principles: Stakeholders -- PM.11 identifies the stakeholder principle, which stresses the need to continuously recognize, analyze, and engage all individuals, groups, or organizations that can affect or be affected by a project. It guides project teams to align expectations, foster collaboration, and manage stakeholder interests throughout the project lifecycle.
[PM.12] Principles: Value -- The Value principle guides project teams to prioritize outcomes that create measurable benefit for stakeholders, ensuring that decisions, resources, and deliverables align with the organization's strategic objectives. It encourages continuous assessment of results against expected value to support purposeful, impact-focused delivery.
[PM.13] Principles: Systems Thinking -- PM.13 highlights the Systems Thinking principle, encouraging practitioners to view a project as an integrated component of a larger ecosystem of people, processes, and outcomes. It stresses understanding interdependencies, feedback loops, and the broader impact of decisions, enabling more holistic planning and adaptive management throughout the project life cycle.

[PM.15] Principles: Tailoring -- PM.15 outlines the tailoring principle, which directs project teams to adapt methods, processes, and governance structures to the specific project context. It advises selecting appropriate practices, adjusting deliverables, and aligning with stakeholder needs to ensure relevance and effectiveness.

[PM.17] Principles: Complexity -- The Complexity principle acknowledges that projects often involve many interrelated elements, ambiguous requirements and dynamic environments, requiring adaptive planning and stakeholder collaboration. It guides teams to assess and balance simplicity with necessary intricacy, using suitable structures, governance and tools to manage interdependencies, risks and changing conditions.
[PM.18] Principles: Risk -- PM.18 outlines the Risk principle of the PMBOK 7th Edition, emphasizing that risk should be continuously identified, assessed, and responded to throughout the project life cycle. It guides teams to integrate risk thinking into decision-making, stakeholder engagement, and performance monitoring to enhance project resilience and value delivery.
[PM.19] Principles: Adaptability -- Adaptability emphasizes the need for project teams to remain flexible and adjust their methods, plans, and decisions as circumstances evolve. It encourages continuous learning, responsiveness to new information, and the willingness to modify approaches to sustain value delivery and meet stakeholder expectations throughout the project lifecycle.
[PM.20] Principles: Change -- PM.20 outlines the principle of Change, asserting that projects inevitably encounter shifts in scope, environment, and stakeholder needs and must respond proactively. It encourages continuous assessment of impacts, flexible planning, and adaptive decision-making to ensure the project remains aligned with its objectives while embracing necessary transformations.
