[annex_i] Annex I - EU Harmonisation Legislation (list) -- Annex I enumerates the European Union statutes that the AI Regulation aligns with, specifying the harmonisation legislation covering sectors such as data protection, digital services and consumer protection. It serves as a reference list for legal instruments that must be applied in conjunction with the AI rules.
[annex_iii] Annex III - High-Risk AI Systems (use cases by area) -- Annex III of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 identifies AI systems that are considered high-risk by specifying use cases across various sectors. It lists applications such as biometric identification, critical infrastructure management, education and training, employment and workers' management, law-enforcement and border control, as well as medical and safety-critical devices, indicating that these systems must meet the regulation's conformity-assessment, transparency and monitoring requirements.
[art_10] Art 10 - Data and data governance -- Article 10 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 sets out requirements for data and data governance in AI, mandating that providers ensure data quality, relevance, representativeness and traceability throughout the system life-cycle. It obliges operators to document data sources, preprocessing steps and any bias mitigation measures, and to maintain up-to-date datasets for monitoring and compliance.
[art_13] Art 13 - Transparency and information to deployers -- Article 13 of the EU Regulation 2024/1689 obliges providers of AI systems to ensure transparency by supplying deployers with clear, concise information on the system's capabilities, intended purpose, limitations, risk level and required safeguards, together with appropriate documentation and user-facing notices. This aims to enable informed decision-making and responsible use of AI.
[art_14] Art 14 - Human oversight -- Article 14 of the EU Regulation 2024/1689 requires that AI systems incorporate appropriate human oversight to mitigate risks, ensuring that users can monitor, understand and intervene in the system's operation. Providers must implement clear procedures, technical safeguards and fallback mechanisms that enable effective human control over outcomes.
[art_15] Art 15 - Accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity -- Article 15 of the EU AI Regulation sets out mandatory requirements for the accuracy, robustness and cybersecurity of AI systems, obliging providers to ensure that outputs meet defined performance standards, that the systems can withstand errors, adversarial attacks and environmental variations, and that appropriate technical and organisational safeguards protect data integrity and prevent unauthorised access.
[art_5] Art 5 - Prohibited AI practices (social scoring, biometric categorisation, etc.) -- Article 5 of the EU AI Regulation enumerates AI systems that are outright prohibited, including those used for social scoring of individuals, biometric categorisation for discriminatory purposes, real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces, and any system that manipulates human behaviour to cause physical or psychological harm. The article establishes mandatory bans on these practices to safeguard fundamental rights and ensure trustworthy AI deployment across the Union.
[art_50] Art 50 - Transparency obligations for certain AI systems -- Article 50 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 sets out transparency obligations for specific AI systems, requiring providers to supply clear information about the system's capabilities, limitations, and intended use. It mandates that users be informed when interacting with AI-generated content or decisions, ensuring they can understand and assess the influence of the technology. Compliance is monitored through documentation and labeling requirements to promote accountability and trust.
[art_51] Art 51 - Classification of general-purpose AI models with systemic risk -- Article 51 of the EU AI Regulation defines how general-purpose AI systems that could create systemic risk are to be classified. It establishes risk-based criteria, mandatory documentation and supervisory procedures so such models are monitored, registered and subject to stricter conformity assessments.
[art_53] Art 53 - Obligations for providers of GPAI models -- Article 53 defines the duties that providers of generative pre-trained AI (GPAI) models must meet under the EU AI Regulation. It obliges them to ensure safety, transparency and accountability by maintaining comprehensive documentation, conducting risk assessments, implementing post-market monitoring and complying with any required conformity-assessment procedures.
[art_55] Art 55 - Obligations for GPAI models with systemic risk -- Article 55 requires providers of generative-pre-trained AI models that could create systemic risk to conduct thorough risk assessments, implement continuous monitoring and mitigation measures, ensure transparency of model capabilities and data, and report relevant findings to competent authorities on a regular basis.
[art_6] Art 6 - Classification rules for high-risk AI systems -- Article 6 sets out the criteria used to determine whether an AI system qualifies as high-risk, requiring assessment of its intended purpose, sectoral impact, and potential risks to health, safety or fundamental rights.
[art_64] Art 64 - AI Office -- Article 64 establishes the European Artificial Intelligence Office, a dedicated body that supports the coordination and consistent application of the AI Regulation across Member States. It is tasked with providing technical expertise, issuing guidance, and facilitating cooperation between national authorities, the Commission and other stakeholders.
[art_65] Art 65 - European Artificial Intelligence Board -- Article 65 creates the European Artificial Intelligence Board, a Union-level body composed of the heads of national supervisory authorities and the European Commission. Its tasks include facilitating consistent application of the AI Regulation, issuing guidance, assisting the Commission, and promoting cooperation among Member States.
[art_72] Art 72 - Market surveillance and control -- Article 72 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 sets out the framework for market surveillance and control of AI systems, granting competent authorities the power to verify conformity, request documentation, and impose corrective actions or restrictions when non-compliance is detected. It establishes coordinated oversight among member-state bodies, defines procedures for investigations and reporting, and outlines sanctions for breaches to ensure the safe deployment of AI within the EU market.
[art_9] Art 9 - Risk management system -- Art 9 of the EU AI Regulation obliges providers of high-risk AI systems to establish and maintain a comprehensive risk-management system that continuously identifies, assesses, mitigates and monitors risks throughout the system's design, development, deployment and post-market phases, ensuring compliance with safety, transparency and accountability requirements.
[art_99] Art 99 - Penalties -- Article 99 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 sets out the penalties for breaches of the AI rules, requiring Member States to impose effective, proportionate and dissuasive fines. It also allows for other corrective measures such as suspension of activities, withdrawal of products from the market, or remedial actions to ensure compliance.
[title_1] Title I - General Provisions -- Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 provides a harmonised EU framework for artificial intelligence. Title I defines the regulation's scope, key concepts and objectives, setting out requirements for safety, fundamental rights protection and market transparency.
[title_10] Title X - Penalties -- Title X of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 establishes the penalties for non-compliance with the EU's harmonised AI rules, outlining the categories of infringements and the scale of administrative fines. It also details the enforcement procedures that competent authorities must follow when imposing sanctions.
[title_2] Title II - Prohibited AI Practices -- Title II of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 defines AI systems and uses that are banned throughout the Union, including real-time biometric identification in public spaces, AI-driven manipulation of human behaviour, and systems that cause physical or psychological harm, with limited exceptions for law-enforcement or public-interest purposes under strict safeguards.
[title_3] Title III - High-Risk AI Systems -- Title III of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 establishes the criteria and obligations for high-risk AI systems, defining which applications are subject to stricter oversight. It outlines requirements for risk assessment, data quality, transparency, human oversight, and post-market monitoring to ensure safety, fundamental rights, and ethical compliance across the European Union.

[title_5] Title V - General-Purpose AI Models -- Title V of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 sets out specific obligations for providers and users of general-purpose AI models, defining them as systems intended for a broad range of tasks across multiple sectors. It requires transparency measures, conformity assessment, and risk-management procedures to ensure that such models meet the Union's safety, security and fundamental-rights standards before being placed on the market.
[title_6] Title VI - Governance -- Title VI of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 establishes the governance framework for AI in the Union, defining the roles and responsibilities of national competent authorities, the European Artificial Intelligence Board, and the Commission in overseeing compliance, handling incidents, and ensuring consistent enforcement of the harmonised AI rules across member states.
[title_7] Title VII - EU Database for High-Risk AI -- Title VII creates an EU-wide database that records high-risk artificial-intelligence systems placed on the market or put into service, requiring providers to submit detailed technical and compliance information. The database enables regulators to monitor, assess and enforce the harmonised AI rules, enhances transparency for authorities and the public, and supports risk mitigation measures across member states.
[title_8] Title VIII - Post-Market Monitoring and Enforcement -- This title sets out obligations for providers and users of AI systems to conduct post-market monitoring, report high-risk incidents and cooperate with national authorities. It establishes mechanisms for market surveillance, conformity-assessment updates and enforcement actions, including penalties for non-compliance.
[title_9] Title IX - Codes of Conduct -- Title IX establishes the framework for voluntary codes of conduct that AI providers and users may adopt to demonstrate compliance with the AI Regulation. It specifies how codes are to be drafted, submitted to competent authorities, and monitored, offering a flexible pathway for industry alignment with the harmonised rules.
