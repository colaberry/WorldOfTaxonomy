[10_1] 10.1 General -- Clause 10.1 establishes the requirement for organizations to determine and implement processes for continual improvement of the quality management system. It emphasizes that improvement should be based on analysis of data, audit results, customer feedback, and performance metrics.
[10_2] 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 9001:2015 requires organizations to identify actual or potential nonconformities, assess their impact, and initiate corrective actions to eliminate causes and prevent recurrence. It mandates documenting the nature of the nonconformity, the corrective steps taken, and verifying the effectiveness of those actions, while also ensuring that relevant records are maintained and communicated to interested parties.
[10_3] 10.3 Continual improvement -- Entry 10_3 in ISO 9001:2015 addresses continual improvement, requiring an organization to systematically enhance the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of its quality management system. It calls for the use of data analysis, corrective actions and performance metrics to identify opportunities for improvement and to implement changes that increase customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 requires an organization to identify the relevant interested parties-such as customers, suppliers, regulators, and employees-and determine their needs and expectations that affect the quality management system, ensuring these requirements are considered when establishing and maintaining the system.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the QMS -- Clause 4.3 requires an organization to identify the boundaries and applicability of its quality management system by considering its products, services, processes, locations, and relevant statutory and regulatory requirements, ensuring the defined scope reflects all activities that can affect conformity and customer satisfaction.
[4_4] 4.4 QMS and its processes -- Clause 4.4 of ISO 9001:2015 requires an organization to define, implement and maintain the processes needed for its quality management system and to manage their interactions. It emphasizes documenting process inputs, outputs, criteria and resources, and measuring performance to achieve continual improvement.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- ISO 9001:2015 clause 5.1 requires top management to actively lead and demonstrate commitment to the quality management system, establishing the quality policy, setting measurable objectives, and ensuring the system is integrated into business processes. It also mandates providing necessary resources, communicating the importance of effective quality management, and fostering a culture of continual improvement throughout the organization.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- Clause 5.2 requires the organization to establish, implement and maintain a quality policy that is appropriate to its purpose and context, provides a framework for setting quality objectives, and includes a commitment to satisfy applicable requirements and pursue continual improvement.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 of ISO 9001:2015 mandates that an organization determine and document the roles, responsibilities and authorities needed for its quality management system. This ensures that each function knows its duties, has the authority to act and that accountability is clearly established throughout the organization.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- ISO 9001:2015 clause 6.1 requires an organization to determine risks and opportunities that could affect the intended results of its quality management system, plan and implement actions to address them, and integrate these actions into its processes. The goal is to prevent undesirable effects, promote continual improvement, and ensure that the system can achieve its planned outcomes.
[6_2] 6.2 Quality objectives and planning -- Clause 6.2 requires an organization to set quality objectives that are measurable, relevant to its functions and consistent with the quality policy, and to develop a plan detailing the resources, responsibilities, timelines and methods needed to achieve those objectives, while monitoring progress and reviewing results.
[6_3] 6.3 Planning of changes -- Entry 6.3 of ISO 9001:2015 requires organizations to plan any changes to the quality management system in a controlled manner, assessing potential impacts on processes, resources and compliance, and integrating risk-based decisions. The plan must define objectives, responsibilities, and necessary documentation, and ensure that changes are communicated to relevant parties before implementation.
[7_1] 7.1 Resources -- Clause 7.1 of ISO 9001:2015 requires an organization to identify and supply the resources needed for the effective operation of its quality management system, covering personnel, infrastructure, work environment, and monitoring and measuring equipment. It also mandates that these resources be suitable, maintained and available whenever required to achieve intended product and service quality.
[7_2] 7.2 Competence -- Clause 7.2 of ISO 9001:2015 mandates that an organization identify the competence required for personnel whose work influences product or service quality, ensure they acquire the necessary skills through training, education, or experience, and retain documented evidence of that competence.
[7_3] 7.3 Awareness -- Clause 7.3 of ISO 9001:2015 mandates that an organization ensure all persons doing work under its control are aware of the quality management system's relevance to their roles, the quality policy, objectives, and their contribution to meeting requirements, as well as the consequences of non-conformance. This awareness helps align daily activities with the organization's commitment to quality and continual improvement.
[7_4] 7.4 Communication -- Clause 7.4 requires the organization to determine internal and external communication needs, establish processes for effective information exchange, and ensure relevant parties receive appropriate messages related to the QMS.
[7_5] 7.5 Documented information -- Entry 7_5 specifies that an organization must create, update, and retain documented information needed to support its quality management system, ensuring it is adequately protected, accessible, and retrievable. It covers the type, format, control methods and retention periods for both required documents and records.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 9001:2015 requires organizations to identify the processes needed for their quality management system, plan their implementation and control, and define criteria, resources and responsibilities to ensure consistent achievement of product and service requirements, customer satisfaction and compliance with applicable regulations.
[8_2] 8.2 Requirements for products and services -- Clause 8.2 of ISO 9001:2015 defines the organization's obligation to identify, verify and fulfill all functional, statutory and customer-specified requirements for its products and services, and to ensure any deviations are communicated and resolved in accordance with the quality management system.
[8_3] 8.3 Design and development -- Clause 8.3 of ISO 9001:2015 sets out requirements for the planning, execution, and control of design and development activities, ensuring that inputs are defined, outputs meet the intended use, and changes are managed. It mandates verification, validation, review, and documentation of each stage to guarantee that the resulting product or service conforms to customer and regulatory expectations.
[8_4] 8.4 Control of externally provided processes, products and services -- Clause 8.4 of ISO 9001:2015 sets out requirements for organizations to ensure that any externally supplied processes, products or services meet specified quality criteria. It mandates selection, evaluation and ongoing monitoring of suppliers, verification of conformity before use, and documented controls with corrective actions when non-conformance is detected.

[8_6] 8.6 Release of products and services -- Clause 8.6 of ISO 9001:2015 requires organizations to verify that products and services meet all specified requirements before they are delivered to customers, including any applicable statutory or regulatory obligations. The release process must be controlled through documented evidence, such as inspection records or test results, to ensure that only compliant outputs are authorized for distribution.
[8_7] 8.7 Control of nonconforming outputs -- Clause 8.7 requires an organization to identify, document and control any product, service or output that does not meet specified requirements, preventing its unintended use or delivery. It mandates corrective actions such as rework, repair, segregation, or disposal, and the maintenance of records detailing the decisions and actions taken.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 9001:2015 requires organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the performance and effectiveness of the quality management system, ensuring that relevant data are collected, interpreted and used for continual improvement and compliance with the standard.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 of ISO 9001:2015 sets out the requirements for internal audits of a quality management system. Organizations must plan, conduct and document audits at defined intervals to verify conformity, evaluate effectiveness and implement corrective actions based on the audit findings.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 of ISO 9001:2015 mandates that top management periodically review the quality management system to confirm its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must examine inputs such as audit results, customer feedback, process performance, and changes in internal or external context, and produce decisions on improvements, resource needs and any required changes to the quality policy or objectives.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 9001:2015 addresses improvement within a quality management system, requiring organizations to continually enhance their processes and overall performance. It outlines requirements for handling nonconformities, implementing corrective actions, and using data-driven analysis to drive ongoing refinements that increase effectiveness and meet stakeholder expectations.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 9001:2015 establishes the "Context of the Organization," requiring entities to identify relevant internal and external issues, determine the needs and expectations of interested parties, and define the scope of their quality management system. This foundation ensures the QMS is aligned with the organization's strategic environment and relevant regulatory and market conditions.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 in ISO 9001:2015 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment to the quality management system, establishing a clear quality policy, setting measurable objectives, and ensuring responsibilities are defined. It emphasizes the need for aligning the QMS with the organization's strategic direction, fostering a culture of continual improvement, and engaging staff at all levels.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 9001:2015 specifies the planning activities needed to meet quality objectives, covering the identification of risks and opportunities, the establishment of measurable goals, and the determination of actions, resources and timelines required to integrate these elements into the organization's quality management system.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 9001:2015 defines the support needed for an effective quality management system, covering the provision of resources, the competence and awareness of personnel, internal and external communication, and the creation, control, and retention of documented information. These elements ensure the organization can consistently meet customer and regulatory requirements.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 9001:2015 outlines the requirements for an organization's operational processes, covering the planning, implementation and control of activities that produce products or services. It addresses the management of resources, design and development, control of externally provided processes, and the execution of production, service provision and post-delivery activities to ensure conformity with customer and regulatory requirements.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 9001:2015 outlines the requirements for performance evaluation, guiding organizations to monitor, measure, analyse, and evaluate the effectiveness of their quality management system. It covers internal auditing, management review, and the use of data and metrics to drive continual improvement and ensure compliance with the standard.
