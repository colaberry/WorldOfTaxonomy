[PIC] Rotterdam Convention Annexes -- The Rotterdam Convention PIC (Prior Informed Consent) mechanism requires that parties obtain the importing country's consent before exporting listed hazardous chemicals and pesticides. Its annexes enumerate the specific substances subject to this consent procedure, facilitating shared responsibility and safe trade of potentially dangerous chemicals.
[PIC.01] Aldrin -- Aldrin is a persistent organochlorine insecticide once widely used for soil pests but now recognized for its high toxicity, bioaccumulation and long-range environmental transport. Under the Rotterdam Convention's Prior Informed Consent (PIC) mechanism it is listed as a hazardous pesticide requiring consent before export to protect human health and the environment.
[PIC.02] Chlordane -- Chlordane is an organochlorine insecticide formerly used for termite control and agricultural pest management but now largely prohibited because of its persistence, bioaccumulation and toxicity to wildlife and humans. Under the Rotterdam Convention it is listed in the Prior Informed Consent (PIC) procedure, requiring exporting countries to obtain consent before shipping the chemical to importing parties.

[PIC.04] Heptachlor -- Heptachlor is a chlorinated cyclodiene insecticide formerly used for termite and agricultural pest control, recognized for its high toxicity, environmental persistence, and potential to bioaccumulate. Under the Rotterdam Convention it is listed as PIC.04, requiring Prior Informed Consent before any international trade because of its significant risks to human health and ecosystems.
[PIC.05] Hexachlorobenzene -- Hexachlorobenzene is a chlorinated aromatic compound formerly used as a fungicide and in industrial processes. Its high toxicity, persistence, and tendency to bioaccumulate have led to its listing in the Rotterdam Convention as a priority chemical that requires informed consent for international trade. The classification seeks to safeguard human health and the environment from exposure to this hazardous substance.

[PIC.07] Mercury compounds -- The Rotterdam Convention Prior Informed Consent (PIC) listing PIC.07 covers mercury compounds, which are hazardous substances that can cause serious environmental and health effects. Trade of these chemicals is restricted, requiring exporting nations to obtain the importing country's written consent before shipment. This measure aims to prevent harmful exposure and promote safe handling of mercury-containing products.
[PIC.08] Asbestos (various forms) -- The Rotterdam Convention's Prior Informed Consent (PIC) entry PIC.08 covers asbestos in all its commercial forms, including chrysotile, amosite, crocidolite and other mineral fibers. It lists asbestos as a hazardous commodity whose export requires informed consent from the importing country because of serious health risks such as lung disease and cancer. This entry aims to ensure transparent trade and protect public health.
[PIC.09] Lead compounds -- Lead compounds are listed under the Rotterdam Convention's Prior Informed Consent (PIC) Procedure as chemicals that pose significant health or environmental risks, especially to workers and communities in developing countries. The listing requires exporters to obtain consent from importing countries before trade, helping to manage hazards associated with lead use in batteries, pigments, and industrial processes.
[PIC.10] Tributyltin compounds -- Tributyltin compounds are organotin biocides historically used in marine antifouling paints and some agricultural applications. Under the Rotterdam Convention they are listed as a pesticide in commerce (PIC.10) subject to Prior Informed Consent because of their high toxicity to aquatic organisms and potential endocrine-disrupting effects.
[PIC.DN] Designated National Authority -- The Rotterdam Convention's PIC system uses the code PIC.DN to identify a country's Designated National Authority, the official body tasked with managing the convention's implementation, including receipt and processing of prior informed consent notifications, coordination of import/export decisions, and communication with the secretariat and other parties.
[PIC.I] Industrial Chemicals Listed -- The Rotterdam Convention PIC code PIC.I refers to industrial chemicals listed under the convention. These chemicals are subject to prior informed consent procedures. They are used in various industrial applications.
[PIC.III] Annex III - PIC Chemicals -- Annex III of the Rotterdam Convention lists chemicals that are subject to the Prior Informed Consent (PIC) procedure, meaning their import and export require the exporting country to notify the importing country and obtain its consent. The list includes a range of pesticides, industrial chemicals and related substances that have been identified as hazardous and that parties have agreed to control through the convention's consent mechanism.

[PIC.P] Pesticides Listed -- Under the Rotterdam Convention's Prior Informed Consent (PIC) procedure, the entry PIC.P designates pesticides that are listed as hazardous chemicals requiring consent before they can be imported or exported. Countries that have ratified the convention must obtain prior informed consent from the importing nation for any trade involving these listed pesticides, ensuring that health and environmental risks are evaluated and managed.
[PIC.SH] Severely Hazardous Pesticide Formulation -- The Rotterdam Convention code PIC.SH identifies pesticide formulations that are classified as severely hazardous because they pose high acute or chronic toxicity to humans or the environment. Such products are subject to the Prior Informed Consent procedure, which requires exporting countries to obtain explicit approval from importing nations before trade.
