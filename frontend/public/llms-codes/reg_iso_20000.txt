[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- The entry specifies that an organization must detect and record any nonconformities in its IT service management system, determine their causes, and implement corrective actions to eliminate those causes and prevent recurrence. It requires clear responsibilities for reporting, evaluating, and tracking corrective measures and evidence that the actions taken are effective.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 requires the organization to establish, implement and maintain processes for continual improvement of its IT service management system, using performance data, audits and corrective actions to enhance service quality and efficiency. It emphasizes systematic review, identification of improvement opportunities and the application of changes to achieve measurable gains.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 requires the organization to determine external and internal factors that influence its ability to deliver IT services, identify relevant interested parties and their needs, and define the scope of its IT service management system based on this understanding. This analysis forms the foundation for aligning the service management approach with business objectives and environmental conditions.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 requires an organization to determine who the relevant interested parties are, capture their needs and expectations, and assess how these affect the intended outcomes of the IT service management system, ensuring they are considered in planning and continual improvement.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to lead the IT service management system by establishing a clear service policy, setting measurable objectives, and ensuring adequate resources and competencies. It mandates visible commitment to continual improvement, alignment of the system with strategic goals, and active involvement in performance monitoring and communication throughout the organization.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- Entry 5.2 requires the organization to create a documented IT service management policy that reflects its strategic objectives, outlines the scope of the service management system, and defines responsibilities. The policy must be communicated to all relevant stakeholders and reviewed periodically to ensure it remains appropriate and effective.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 requires organizations to establish documented processes for planning and controlling service operations, aligning resources, schedules and procedures with defined service requirements and policies. The clause mandates ongoing monitoring of performance, management of changes, and implementation of corrective actions to ensure services meet agreed service levels and maintain continual improvement.
[8_2] 8.2 Service portfolio -- Clause 8.2 requires the organization to establish and maintain a service portfolio that records all services under consideration, in operation, and retired, linking them to business needs and defining their status, objectives and resource commitments.
[8_3] 8.3 Relationship and agreement -- Clause 8.3 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 specifies the requirements for establishing, maintaining and reviewing the relationships and agreements between the service provider and its customers or external parties, ensuring that contract terms, service-level agreements and communication processes are defined, documented and regularly monitored to achieve the agreed service outcomes.
[8_4] 8.4 Supply and demand -- The clause requires organizations to plan and control the balance between service capacity and customer demand, establishing processes for demand forecasting, capacity planning, and service-level agreement management. It calls for continual monitoring, adjustment of resources, and transparent communication of changes to meet agreed service outcomes.
[8_5] 8.5 Service design, build and transition -- Section 8.5 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 defines the requirements for service design, build and transition within an IT service management system. It requires organizations to plan service designs, develop and test service components, and manage the controlled transition of those services into operational use while meeting defined objectives and stakeholder expectations.
[8_6] 8.6 Resolution and fulfilment -- Clause 8.6 requires the service provider to establish and maintain processes for resolving incidents and fulfilling service requests, ensuring that each is handled within agreed timeframes and according to documented procedures. It mandates tracking, escalation, and communication with users, as well as recording outcomes to support continual improvement of service delivery.
[8_7] 8.7 Service assurance -- Clause 8.7 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 defines the service assurance requirements, obliging organizations to monitor, measure and evaluate service performance against agreed service level targets, detect deviations, and apply corrective actions to maintain and continually improve service quality.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- The clause requires the organization to plan and implement processes for monitoring service delivery, measuring performance against defined objectives, analysing the collected data, and evaluating outcomes to determine effectiveness and drive continual improvement of the IT service management system.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Entry 9.2 requires the organization to conduct planned internal audits of its IT service management system at defined intervals, ensuring that processes conform to the standard's requirements and are effectively implemented. Audit results must be recorded, reported to management, and used to drive corrective actions and continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 requires the organization's top management to periodically assess the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the IT service management system, reviewing performance metrics, audit results, incidents and improvement actions to ensure continual improvement and alignment with business objectives.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 defines the continual improvement requirements for an IT service management system, mandating the organization to identify, plan and implement improvements to processes, services and the overall system, and to monitor the effectiveness of these changes. It emphasizes the use of measurement data, corrective actions and management review to drive ongoing enhancements and achieve better service quality.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 requires an organization to identify and analyze its internal and external issues, relevant interested parties and their needs, and to define the scope of its IT service management system. It sets the foundation for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving the service management system in alignment with the organization's strategic direction. This contextual understanding informs all subsequent requirements of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment to the IT service management system, establishing a clear service-management policy and aligning it with the organization's strategic direction. It mandates assigning responsibilities, ensuring resources, and integrating service-management objectives throughout the business to drive continual improvement and meet stakeholder expectations.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 defines the planning requirements for an IT service management system, requiring organizations to set service management objectives, assess risks and opportunities, and develop action plans that align with the overall service strategy. The clause also calls for regular review of these plans to ensure they remain effective and support continual improvement.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support of the SMS -- Clause 7 of ISO/IEC 20000-1:2018 defines the support requirements for an IT Service Management System, covering the provision of resources, competence, awareness, communication and documented information needed to operate and maintain services effectively. It sets expectations for handling incidents, service requests and continual improvement activities, ensuring that the SMS is adequately resourced and aligned with organizational objectives.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation of the SMS -- Clause 8 outlines how an organization must operate its IT Service Management System, detailing the processes for delivering services, managing incidents, service requests, problems, and changes, as well as the monitoring, measurement, and reporting activities required to ensure consistent service performance and continual improvement. It emphasizes control of operational activities, resource allocation, and communication with stakeholders to meet agreed service levels.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 outlines the processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the performance of the IT service management system, covering service level achievement, internal audits and management reviews to ensure continual improvement and compliance with the standard.
