[CC] CCSS Domains -- The CC code identifies the Common Core State Standards domains, which are the primary subject areas the standards
[CC.01] ELA: Reading Literature -- CC.01 in the Common Core State Standards covers English Language Arts reading of literature, guiding students to analyze key ideas, themes, structures, and the author's craft in narrative texts. Learners cite textual evidence and interpret language choices to deepen comprehension and critical thinking.
[CC.02] ELA: Reading Informational Text -- CC.02 in the Common Core State Standards designates the English Language Arts expectation that students read informational texts with sufficient comprehension to identify main ideas, determine the author's purpose, and summarize key details. It requires them to interpret the structure of the text, evaluate evidence, and make connections across multiple sources, building critical analysis and research skills.
[CC.03] ELA: Writing -- CC.03 refers to the Common Core State Standards for third-grade English Language Arts writing. It outlines expectations for students to produce opinion pieces, narratives, and informative texts, focusing on organization, development, and use of conventions.
[CC.04] ELA: Speaking and Listening -- CC.04 in the Common Core State Standards addresses English Language Arts expectations for speaking and listening in fourth grade. Students are expected to actively participate in discussions, ask and answer questions, summarize spoken information, and present ideas clearly using appropriate
[CC.05] ELA: Language -- CC.05 in the Common Core State Standards identifies expectations for English Language Arts focusing on language. It emphasizes mastery of vocabulary, grammar, syntax, and the conventions of standard English, guiding students to analyze and apply these elements in reading and writing across grade levels.
[CC.06] Math: Counting and Cardinality (K) -- CC.06 in the Common Core State Standards covers Counting and Cardinality for kindergarten, guiding students to recognize, name and write numbers, count objects accurately, and understand that the final number spoken denotes the set's quantity, establishing the foundation for future arithmetic concepts.
[CC.07] Math: Operations and Algebraic Thinking -- CC.07 in the Common Core State Standards addresses seventh-grade mathematics focused on Operations and Algebraic Thinking. It guides students to interpret and generate numerical expressions, apply properties of operations to simplify equations, and develop an understanding of proportional relationships and solving real-world problems using algebraic methods.
[CC.08] Math: Number and Operations (Base 10) -- CC.08 covers the Common Core standards for eighth-grade mathematics that focus on whole-number operations, properties of numbers, and the use of place value in the base-10 system. It emphasizes fluency with addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of multi-digit numbers, as well as estimating and solving real-world problems that require these operations.

[CC.10] Math: Measurement and Data -- CC.10 in the Common Core State Standards outlines grade-level expectations for mathematics focused on measurement and data. Students learn to select and apply appropriate units, solve problems involving volume, surface area, and conversion, and to collect, organize, display, and interpret numerical data using charts, graphs, and statistical measures. This standard builds foundational skills for real-world quantitative reasoning.
[CC.11] Math: Geometry -- CC.11 in the Common Core State Standards designates the high-school mathematics geometry strand, outlining expectations for students to reason formally about shapes, prove properties of triangles and circles, apply similarity and congruence, use coordinate and transformational methods, and solve real-world problems involving measurement, proof, and logical argument.
[CC.12] Math: Ratios and Proportional Relationships -- CC.12 covers the mathematics domain of ratios and proportional relationships, emphasizing students' ability to represent, compare, and compute ratios and rates. It guides learners to analyze and model proportional relationships, use them to solve real-world problems, and interpret scaling and similarity across various contexts.
[CC.13] Math: Statistics and Probability -- CC.13 in the Common Core State Standards defines the K-12 mathematics expectations for statistics and probability, specifying how students should collect, organize, display, and interpret data, reason about chance, and apply probabilistic models to solve problems. The standard emphasizes the use of visual representations, measures of central tendency, and variability, preparing learners to make evidence-based decisions across real-world contexts.
[CC.14] Math: Functions -- CC.14 in the Common Core State Standards outlines high school mathematics expectations for functions, covering definitions, representations, and applications of linear, quadratic, exponential and other function types, and emphasizing analysis, composition and problem-solving using functional models.
[CC.15] Math: Modeling (HS) -- CC.15 in the Common Core State Standards addresses high-school mathematics modeling, requiring students to formulate, analyze, and refine mathematical representations of real-world situations. Learners use functions, equations, and data to predict outcomes, evaluate assumptions, and communicate findings, linking mathematical reasoning to practical problems.
[CC.16] ELA: Reading in History/Social Studies -- CC.16 focuses on developing students' ability to read and comprehend informational texts in history and social studies, emphasizing skills such as analyzing primary and secondary sources, identifying main ideas, and interpreting evidence within historical contexts.
[CC.17] ELA: Reading in Science/Technical -- CC.17 in the Common Core State Standards designates the ELA expectation that students read and comprehend informational texts in science and technical subjects, analyzing complex ideas, interpreting domain-specific vocabulary, and integrating evidence across multiple sources to build a coherent understanding of the material.
