It targets comprehensive measures such as safety nets, access to basic services, and policies that foster inclusive economic growth.

[SDG1.1] Eradicate extreme poverty ($1.25/day) -- SDG 1.1 calls for the complete eradication of extreme poverty, defined as living on less than $1.25 per day. It seeks to lift all individuals out of severe deprivation by ensuring universal access to basic income, essential services and social protection, thereby eliminating the most acute forms of economic hardship worldwide.
[SDG11] Sustainable Cities and Communities -- SDG 11 focuses on creating inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable urban environments. It seeks to ensure access to affordable housing, reliable public transport, green public spaces, and effective disaster risk reduction, while promoting resource-efficient building practices and participatory planning to improve the quality of life in cities and communities worldwide.
[SDG11.1] Safe and affordable housing -- SDG 11.1 targets universal access to adequate, safe and affordable housing and essential services by 2030, including the upgrading of slums and informal settlements, with the goal of improving living conditions and reducing vulnerability for all populations.
[SDG11.2] Sustainable transport systems -- SDG 11.2 calls for the development of safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems that improve connectivity for all, particularly vulnerable groups, and expand public transport options. It aims to reduce the environmental impact of mobility by promoting low-carbon, resilient infrastructure and encouraging walking, cycling, and efficient mass-transit solutions.
[SDG11.3] Inclusive and sustainable urbanisation -- SDG 11.3 aims to make urbanisation inclusive and sustainable by improving resource-efficient development, ensuring access to affordable housing and basic services, and strengthening participatory planning that benefits all residents while reducing environmental impacts.
[SDG11.6] Reduce environmental impact of cities -- SDG 11.6 aims to lessen the environmental footprint of urban areas by improving air quality, managing waste and hazardous materials, and reducing the adverse effects of cities on ecosystems. It calls for integrated policies, green infrastructure and sustainable resource use to make cities more livable and resilient.
[SDG1.2] Reduce poverty by at least half -- SDG1.2 is the target within the UN Sustainable Development Goals that aims to reduce the proportion of people living in poverty by at least half by 2030, using income and multidimensional poverty measures, and calls for policies that expand social protection, access to basic services and decent work opportunities.
[SDG12] Responsible Consumption and Production -- SDG12 aims to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns by promoting resource efficiency, waste reduction, and environmentally sound practices. It calls for policies that encourage responsible management of natural resources, eco-friendly designs, and awareness of sustainable lifestyles.
[SDG12.2] Sustainable management of resources -- SDG12.2 calls for the sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources, aiming to decouple economic growth from environmental degradation. It encourages policies that promote resource efficiency, recycling and sustainable consumption across supply chains, supporting a transition to a circular economy.
[SDG12.4] Responsible management of chemicals -- SDG12.4 calls for environmentally sound management of chemicals and waste throughout their life-cycle, aiming to reduce harmful releases, minimize health and ecosystem risks, and promote safer alternatives and practices. It supports policies and capacities for proper handling, disposal, and reduction of hazardous substances to achieve sustainable consumption and production.
[SDG12.5] Reduce waste generation -- SDG12.5 is a target under Goal 12 - Responsible Consumption and Production that calls for substantially reducing waste generation through prevention, reduction, recycling and reuse. It aims to lower environmental impact, conserve resources and promote sustainable waste-management practices worldwide.
[SDG1.3] Social protection systems and floors -- SDG 1.3 calls for the establishment and strengthening of universal social protection systems and safety nets that guarantee a basic minimum for all, especially the poor, vulnerable, and those in precarious employment, thereby reducing inequality and enhancing resilience against economic and health shocks.
[SDG13] Climate Action -- SDG13 focuses on urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. It calls for strengthening resilience and adaptive capacity, integrating climate measures into policies, and mobilizing financial resources to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The goal supports international agreements such as the Paris Agreement.
[SDG13.1] Resilience to climate hazards -- SDG13.1 calls for strengthening the resilience and adaptive capacity of all countries to climate-related hazards and natural disasters, and for integrating climate risk management into national policies, strategies and planning.
[SDG13.2] Climate change into policy and planning -- SDG13.2 focuses on integrating climate-change mitigation and adaptation into national policies, strategies and planning. It calls for mainstreaming climate considerations across sectors to build resilient societies and economies, supporting the overall Goal 13 to combat climate change.
[SDG13.3] Education and capacity on climate change -- Goal 13.3 aims to strengthen education, awareness and human-capacity building on climate change mitigation, adaptation and resilience. It calls for integrating climate knowledge into formal and non-formal curricula, training professionals, and raising public understanding so societies can better respond to climate impacts and support sustainable development.
[SDG1.4] Equal rights to resources and services -- SDG 1.4 seeks to guarantee that everyone has equal legal and de-facto rights to own, control and benefit from natural resources, financial services, land, housing and basic infrastructure. It calls for removing discriminatory policies and strengthening inclusive institutions so vulnerable groups can access essential services and assets.

[SDG14.1] Prevent marine pollution -- SDG14.1 targets the prevention and substantial reduction of marine pollution from land-based activities, including debris, nutrients, and hazardous substances, in order to protect ocean health and sustain the ecosystems that support food security and livelihoods.
[SDG14.2] Protect marine ecosystems -- SDG 14.2 aims to protect and restore marine and coastal ecosystems, emphasizing the preservation of biodiversity and the sustainable management of marine resources through measures such as marine protected areas, pollution reduction and habitat restoration.

[SDG1.5] Resilience of poor and vulnerable -- SDG 1.5 focuses on building the resilience of people living in poverty and in vulnerable situations, ensuring they can withstand and recover from economic, social and environmental shocks. It calls for targeted policies that strengthen safety nets, improve access to essential services, and promote adaptive capacities to reduce the risk of falling back into poverty.
[SDG15] Life on Land -- SDG 15 seeks to protect, restore and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, including forests, drylands, and biodiversity. It aims to combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation, and ensure the conservation of mountain ecosystems and the variety of plant and animal species.
[SDG15.1] Conserve terrestrial ecosystems -- SDG 15.1 calls for the conservation, restoration and sustainable management of terrestrial ecosystems, emphasizing the protection of forests, combating desertification, halting biodiversity loss and ensuring land resources support both people and nature. It underpins broader efforts to maintain ecosystem services, preserve natural habitats and promote resilience against climate change.
[SDG15.2] Halt deforestation -- SDG15.2 calls for measures to stop the loss of forest areas, aiming to achieve a worldwide halt to deforestation and forest degradation, thereby protecting biodiversity and climate regulation. It supports sustainable forest management, restoration and the promotion of land-use policies that preserve forest ecosystems.
[SDG15.5] Reduce loss of biodiversity -- SDG15.5 aims to significantly reduce the loss of biodiversity by implementing measures such as protecting ecosystems, combating habitat degradation and promoting sustainable use of natural resources, aligning with Goal 15 of the UN 2030 Agenda.
[SDG16] Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions -- SDG 16 seeks to promote peaceful and inclusive societies, ensure access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable institutions at every level. It targets reducing violence, strengthening the rule of law, combating corruption, and enhancing transparency and participation in governance.
[SDG16.1] Reduce violence and death rates -- SDG 16.1 aims to significantly lower the incidence of violence and related deaths worldwide, promoting peaceful societies and strengthening the rule of law. It calls for measurable reductions in homicide rates, conflict-related fatalities, and other forms of violent crime.
[SDG16.3] Access to justice for all -- SDG 16.3 seeks to promote the rule of law by ensuring that everyone can obtain effective, affordable legal assistance and a fair trial, removing barriers to justice and strengthening institutions that uphold rights. It aims to create inclusive, transparent systems for dispute resolution, contributing to peaceful and equitable societies.
[SDG16.6] Accountable and transparent institutions -- SDG 16.6 is a target under Goal 16 - Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions - that aims to develop effective, accountable and transparent institutions at all levels. It promotes public access to information, inclusive decision-making, and robust anti-corruption measures to strengthen governance.
[SDG16.9] Legal identity for all -- SDG16.9 aims to provide legal identity for all, including birth registration, by 2030. This target supports access to services, protection of rights, and reliable statistical data. It seeks to ensure everyone has official documentation recognized by law.
[SDG17] Partnerships for the Goals -- SDG 17, Partnerships for the Goals, seeks to mobilize and enhance global cooperation to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals by strengthening financing, technology transfer, capacity-building, trade and systemic issues. It emphasizes multi-stakeholder partnerships among governments, the private sector, civil society and international institutions.
[SDG17.1] Strengthen domestic resource mobilisation -- SDG 17.1 aims to bolster countries' ability to generate their own financial resources through more effective, transparent and equitable tax systems and other domestic revenue sources. It promotes policies that increase fiscal space for development, reduce reliance on external aid, and ensure that public finances are managed responsibly to support sustainable growth.
[SDG17.16] Enhance global partnerships -- SDG 17.16 calls for strengthening multi-stakeholder partnerships that mobilize and share knowledge, technology, finance and capacity-building across governments, businesses, civil society and academia to accelerate the implementation of the 2030 Agenda worldwide.
[SDG17.17] Effective partnerships -- SDG 17.17 encourages the creation of inclusive, multi-stakeholder partnerships that pool resources, knowledge and expertise to advance the 2030 Agenda. It calls for cooperation among governments, civil society, the private sector and academia to build capacity, share technology and align actions across sectors.
[SDG17.3] Mobilise financial resources -- SDG 17.3 seeks to boost domestic and international financial resources for development by enhancing tax collection, improving public-finance management and encouraging official development assistance, private investment and innovative financing mechanisms to support the 2030 Agenda.
[SDG2] Zero Hunger -- SDG2, Zero Hunger, seeks to end hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture worldwide. It targets eliminating all forms of malnutrition and ensuring that all people have access to safe, nutritious food by 2030.
[SDG2.1] End hunger and ensure food access -- SDG 2.1 aims to eradicate hunger by ensuring that all people have regular, affordable access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food, while promoting sustainable agricultural practices that increase productivity and resilience of food systems. It supports policies that improve food distribution, reduce waste, and protect vulnerable populations from food insecurity.
[SDG2.2] End all forms of malnutrition -- SDG 2.2 aims to end all forms of malnutrition by 2030, targeting a reduction in the number of children under five who are stunted, wasting, or overweight, and ensuring access to nutritious, safe, and affordable food for all populations.
[SDG2.3] Double agricultural productivity -- Target 2.3 of the UN Sustainable Development Goals seeks to double the agricultural productivity and incomes of small-scale food producers, including farmers, fishers and pastoralists, by 2030. It promotes sustainable practices, access to technology, reliable markets and resilient supply chains to enhance food security and rural livelihoods.
[SDG2.4] Sustainable food production systems -- SDG 2.4 calls for the development and scaling up of sustainable food production systems that increase agricultural productivity and incomes while preserving ecosystems. It promotes climate-smart practices, efficient use of natural resources and reduced food waste, aiming to secure safe, nutritious food for all without compromising the environment for future generations.
[SDG2.5] Genetic diversity of seeds and crops -- SDG2.5 calls for the preservation and sustainable use of the genetic diversity of seeds, cultivated plants and farmed and wild animal species, ensuring access to appropriate germplasm and supporting resilient agricultural systems. It aims to safeguard biodiversity that underpins food security, nutrition and climate adaptation.
[SDG3] Good Health and Well-Being -- SDG 3 aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages, targeting reductions in maternal and child mortality, the fight against communicable and non-communicable diseases, and universal access to quality health services, medicines and vaccines. It seeks to strengthen health systems, improve health financing and support research and development for health innovations.
[SDG3.1] Reduce maternal mortality -- SDG 3.1 is a target under Goal 3 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, seeking to lower the global maternal mortality ratio to fewer than 70 deaths per 100 000 live births by 2030. It emphasizes expanded access to quality prenatal, obstetric and postnatal care and the removal of barriers such as inadequate health-system capacity, poverty and gender inequality.
[SDG3.2] End preventable deaths of children -- SDG 3.2 targets the reduction of preventable child mortality, seeking to lower under-five deaths to at least 25 per 1,000 live births and neonatal deaths to at least 12 per 1,000 by 2030. It emphasizes improving nutrition, immunisation, access to quality health care and disease prevention to ensure children survive their early years.
[SDG3.3] End AIDS, TB, malaria, NTDs -- SDG 3.3 targets the elimination of the major infectious disease epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases by 2030, urging expanded prevention, treatment and health-system capacity. It calls for universal access to vaccines, diagnostics and medicines to achieve these ends.
[SDG3.4] Reduce NCDs and mental health -- SDG 3.4 seeks to lower premature mortality from non-communicable diseases by one-third and to promote mental health and well-being by 2030.
[SDG3.8] Universal health coverage -- SDG 3.8 seeks universal health coverage, guaranteeing that all individuals obtain quality essential health services, medicines and vaccines without suffering financial hardship. It emphasizes strengthening health systems, expanding coverage, and safeguarding people from catastrophic health expenses.

It targets universal primary and secondary education, improves early childhood development, and addresses skills gaps through vocational training and higher education access.
[SDG4.1] Free and quality primary/secondary ed -- SDG 4.1 is a target of Goal 4 that aims to guarantee free, equitable and quality primary and secondary education for all children, ensuring they complete these levels and acquire the necessary basic knowledge and skills by 2030.
[SDG4.3] Access to TVET and higher education -- SDG 4.3 targets universal, affordable and quality access to technical-vocational education and training (TVET) as well as higher education, aiming to equip all learners with relevant skills for decent work, sustainable livelihoods and inclusive economic development.

[SDG4.7] Education for sustainable development -- SDG 4.7 calls for education systems to incorporate knowledge, skills, values and attitudes that promote sustainable development, including climate action, gender equality, peace and global citizenship, so that learners can contribute to a more inclusive, resilient and thriving world.
[SDG5] Gender Equality -- SDG 5 aims to achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls by eliminating discrimination, violence, and harmful practices, ensuring equal participation in leadership, education, and the labor market, and promoting shared responsibility for unpaid care work.
[SDG5.1] End discrimination against women -- SDG5.1 aims to eliminate all forms of discrimination against women and girls in law, policy and practice. It calls for legal reforms, effective enforcement mechanisms and public awareness to secure equal rights and opportunities for women worldwide.
[SDG5.2] Eliminate violence against women -- SDG 5.2 calls for the elimination of all forms of violence against women and girls, including domestic abuse, sexual assault, trafficking and harmful practices such as child marriage and female genital mutilation, by strengthening legal frameworks, providing support services and promoting cultural change.
[SDG5.5] Women in leadership -- SDG5.5 seeks to ensure women's full and effective participation and equal opportunities for leadership at all levels of decision-making in political, economic and public life. It calls for policies and measures that increase the share of women in leadership positions and remove barriers to their advancement.
