[MS] MIL-STD Categories -- MS denotes the MIL-STD Categories used by the U.S. Department of Defense to group military standards by functional area or application. These categories organize standards such as logistics, engineering, or testing, facilitating retrieval and compliance across defense programs.

[MS.02] EMC Testing (MIL-STD-461) -- MS.02 designates the electromagnetic compatibility testing category, which applies the requirements of MIL-STD-461 to verify that military equipment and subsystems meet prescribed emission and susceptibility limits, ensuring they can operate without interfering with or being disrupted by other electronic systems. This testing is mandatory for DoD procurement and integration.
[MS.03] Reliability (MIL-STD-785) -- MS.03 addresses reliability as defined in MIL-STD-785, providing the Department of Defense's standard methodology for specifying, measuring, and analyzing the reliability of equipment and systems. It establishes criteria for failure rates, mean time between failures, and test procedures to ensure that defense materiel meets required performance and durability expectations.
[MS.04] Maintainability (MIL-STD-470) -- MS.04 denotes the Maintainability category defined in MIL-STD-470, which establishes metrics and methods for assessing how easily a system or component can be repaired, serviced, or inspected throughout its life cycle. It guides the design of equipment to reduce downtime, simplify logistics, and lower total ownership costs by quantifying maintenance tasks, required skill levels, and support resources.
[MS.05] System Safety (MIL-STD-882) -- MS.05 denotes the System Safety category, referencing DoD MIL-STD-882 which sets the standard for safety engineering in acquisition programs. The standard requires hazard analysis, risk assessment, and safety documentation to ensure that systems are designed, operated and maintained with acceptable risk levels.

[MS.07] Human Factors (MIL-STD-1472) -- MS.07 designates Human Factors, referencing MIL-STD-1472, which provides guidelines for ergonomic design, user-interface layout, workload management, safety, and performance considerations to ensure military equipment and systems are usable, reliable, and reduce operator error.
[MS.08] Software Development (MIL-STD-498) -- MS.08 denotes the Software Development category governed by MIL-STD-498, a U.S. Department of Defense standard that defines processes, documentation and quality requirements for developing, integrating and maintaining defense-related software systems.
[MS.09] Data Item Descriptions (MIL-STD-963) -- MS.09 designates the Data Item Descriptions component of MIL-STD-963, a standard that specifies the format, content and usage of data item description (DID) documents. DIDs define the technical data, contract deliverables and reporting requirements that contractors must provide for DoD acquisition programs, ensuring uniformity and interoperability across projects.
[MS.10] Technical Manuals (MIL-STD-40051) -- MS.10 refers to Technical Manuals as defined by MIL-STD-40051, which provide detailed instructions for the operation, maintenance, troubleshooting, and repair of defense systems and equipment. These manuals are standardized to ensure consistent support and logistics across the U.S. Department of Defense.
[MS.11] Packaging (MIL-STD-2073) -- MS.11 designates the Packaging category in the US DoD MIL-STD system, referencing MIL-STD-2073. This standard defines requirements for the design, testing, marking, and handling of containers, pallets, and other packaging used to protect and transport military materiel throughout its lifecycle. It ensures uniformity and reliability of packaging across all branches of the armed services.
[MS.12] Marking (MIL-STD-130) -- MS.12 covers the marking requirements for military-type equipment and supplies as defined in MIL-STD-130, specifying permanent identification methods such as stenciling, laser engraving, and adhesive labels. It details dimensions, legibility, durability, and data content to ensure items are correctly identified throughout their lifecycle. This category is used to standardize item marking for logistics, maintenance, and traceability across DoD inventory.
[MS.13] Symbology (MIL-STD-2525) -- MS.13 identifies the Symbology (MIL-STD-2525) category, which defines the standard set of graphical symbols and icons the U.S. Department of Defense uses to depict units, equipment, installations, and actions on maps, displays and digital systems, ensuring shared understanding and interoperability across joint and coalition forces.
[MS.14] Interoperability (MIL-STD-6016) -- MS.14 refers to the Interoperability category defined by MIL-STD-6016, which establishes requirements for data-link communication and interface control among U.S. Navy weapons and sensors. The standard specifies message formats, timing, and protocol rules that enable disparate platforms to exchange tactical information reliably, supporting integrated combat operations.
