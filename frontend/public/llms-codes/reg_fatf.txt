[auth] F - Powers and Responsibilities of Authorities -- The "F - Powers and Responsibilities of Authorities" section defines the legal and operational authority granted to domestic and international bodies for preventing, detecting, and investigating money-laundering and terrorist-financing activities, including powers to obtain information, enforce compliance, and cooperate with other jurisdictions. It sets standards for independence, resources, and accountability to ensure effective oversight and enforcement.
[coop] G - International Cooperation -- The "coop" entry (G - International Cooperation) outlines FATF's requirements for jurisdictions to work together in preventing money laundering and terrorist financing, including the exchange of financial intelligence, mutual legal assistance, and coordinated investigative actions across borders. It stresses the need for formal agreements, timely communication, and joint efforts to trace illicit funds and disrupt financing networks worldwide.
[inst] E - Transparency of Legal Persons -- The "inst" entry under FATF International Standards on Combating Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing, titled "E - Transparency of Legal Persons," sets out requirements that states ensure legal persons and arrangements disclose their beneficial-owner information, keep accurate ownership records, and make those records available to competent authorities to deter money-laundering and terrorist-financing abuse.
[ml] B - Money Laundering and Confiscation -- The entry outlines the FATF's definition of money laundering and the required measures for detecting, preventing, and reporting illicit proceeds, as well as the legal framework for confiscating assets linked to criminal activity. It sets standards for risk-based controls, customer due-diligence, and international cooperation to disrupt financial crime.
[prev] D - Preventive Measures -- The "prev" entry under FATF's D-Preventive Measures outlines the basic safeguards that financial institutions and designated non-financial businesses must apply to deter money laundering and terrorist financing. It requires customer due-diligence, ongoing monitoring, record-keeping, reporting of suspicious activity and the establishment of internal controls, policies and training programs.

[r10] R.10 Customer due diligence -- R.10 requires financial institutions to identify and verify the identity of customers, understand the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship, and assess the associated money-laundering and terrorist-financing risks. It obliges firms to collect reliable documentation on the customer and, where applicable, the beneficial owner, to apply ongoing monitoring and to conduct enhanced due diligence for higher-risk customers or transactions.
[r11] R.11 Record keeping -- The FATF standard R.11 obliges covered entities to maintain comprehensive, accurate records of all transactions and related information for a minimum of five years, ensuring the data is readily accessible for regulatory and law-enforcement review. Proper record keeping enables monitoring, detection, and investigation of money-laundering and terrorist-financing activities.
[r12] R.12 Politically exposed persons -- R.12 defines politically exposed persons (PEPs) as individuals who hold or have held prominent public functions, together with their immediate family members and close associates, and requires financial institutions to apply enhanced due-diligence measures to mitigate the higher risk of corruption and money laundering linked to these relationships.
[r15] R.15 New technologies (virtual assets) -- R.15 highlights the AML/CFT risks linked to emerging technologies such as virtual assets and crypto-related services. It requires jurisdictions to extend FATF standards to virtual-asset service providers, mandating registration, customer due-diligence, transaction monitoring and record-keeping, and to ensure that these entities are subject to the same supervisory and enforcement regimes as traditional financial institutions.
[r16] R.16 Wire transfers -- R.16 establishes FATF requirements for wire transfers, requiring financial institutions to verify the identities of both originators and beneficiaries, retain detailed transaction records, and monitor transfers for suspicious activity to combat money laundering and terrorist financing.
[r2] R.2 National cooperation and coordination -- R.2 requires countries to set up a framework that guarantees effective cooperation and coordination among all relevant national authorities, such as law-enforcement, financial intelligence units and supervisory bodies, to detect, investigate and prevent money laundering and terrorist financing. It stresses the need for information sharing, joint analysis and common procedures to ensure consistent AML/CFT implementation.
[r20] R.20 Reporting of suspicious transactions -- R.20 requires obligated entities such as banks, securities firms and designated non-financial businesses to file timely reports to competent authorities when they detect or suspect that a transaction or activity may be linked to money laundering or terrorist financing, providing sufficient details to enable further investigation.
[r24] R.24 Transparency of legal persons -- R.24 obliges jurisdictions to ensure that legal persons and arrangements disclose their beneficial owners, requiring accessible, reliable, and up-to-date information on natural persons who ultimately own or control them, and mandating that authorities can obtain this data promptly for anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorism financing purposes.

[r26] R.26 Regulation and supervision of financial institutions -- R.26 outlines the need for authorities to establish clear, risk-based rules governing the licensing, operation and ongoing supervision of banks, securities firms, insurers and other financial entities, ensuring they implement effective anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorist-financing controls. It requires periodic inspections, reporting obligations and the power to impose corrective measures or sanctions where deficiencies are identified.
[r29] R.29 Financial intelligence units -- R.29 outlines the role of national financial intelligence units, which serve as central agencies that collect, analyse and disseminate suspicious transaction reports to combat money laundering and terrorist financing, coordinate with law-enforcement and regulatory bodies, and cooperate with foreign FIUs through information-sharing networks.
[r3] R.3 Money laundering offence -- R.3 outlines the FATF definition of a money-laundering offence, specifying the conduct that must involve the acquisition, use or concealment of proceeds from a crime, the intent to disguise their illegal origin, and the requisite knowledge or recklessness of the offender.
[r30] R.30 Responsibilities of law enforcement -- The entry outlines the duties that law-enforcement agencies must fulfill to detect, investigate, prevent, and prosecute money-laundering and terrorist-financing activities. It requires timely communication with financial intelligence units, collection of reliable information, and coordination with other authorities to ensure effective enforcement of anti-money-laundering measures.
[r36] R.36 International instruments -- R.36 details the suite of international legal instruments that underpin the FATF standards, such as United Nations conventions, European Union directives and regional anti-money-laundering agreements. It explains how these instruments are integrated into national AML/CFT regimes and used to assess compliance.

[tf] C - Terrorist Financing and Proliferation -- The FATF entry "C - Terrorist Financing and Proliferation" (code tf) outlines the international standards for detecting, preventing and disrupting the financing of terrorist activities and the spread of weapons of mass destruction.
