[ability_to_repay] Ability-to-Repay / Qualified Mortgage (ATR/QM) -- The ability-to-repay (ATR) requirement under the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau's mortgage rules obligates lenders to verify that a borrower can reasonably afford the loan, considering income, assets, debts and other expenses. A Qualified Mortgage (QM) is a loan that meets ATR standards plus additional criteria such as limits on points, fees, and loan terms, giving lenders certain liability protections.
[collections] Debt Collection Regulations -- The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau's Debt Collection Regulations in 12 CFR Chapter X establish rules that debt collectors must follow when pursuing consumers, banning abusive, deceptive, and unfair practices. The regulations require clear validation notices, detailed dispute processes, and compliance with the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act, giving the CFPB authority to enforce these standards.
[consumer_protection] Consumer Protection Regulations -- The Consumer Protection Regulations, found in Chapter X of Title 12 of the Code of Federal Regulations, are rules issued by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. They establish standards and enforcement mechanisms to safeguard consumers in financial transactions, covering areas such as lending, credit reporting, debt collection, and mortgage servicing. The regulations aim to promote transparency, fairness, and accountability in the marketplace.
[fdcpa_rules] FDCPA Implementing Rules -- The FDCPA Implementing Rules are regulations issued by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau that specify how the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act is applied. They establish standards for debt collectors on prohibited conduct, debt validation, required disclosures, and enforcement procedures to protect consumers.
[higher_priced] Higher-Priced Mortgage Loan Rules -- The higher_priced entry refers to the CFPB's rules for higher-priced mortgage loans, which set special disclosure, underwriting and qualification requirements for loans with interest rates or fees above a statutory threshold. These regulations aim to protect borrowers by ensuring they receive clear information and are evaluated under stricter standards than standard mortgages.

It covers requirements on loan terms, advertising, underwriting standards, and borrower protections to ensure fair and transparent credit practices.

It aims to ensure transparency and fairness for users of prepaid cards and similar financial products.

It standardizes the "truth-in-savings" statements for checking, savings and term deposit accounts, promoting transparency and informed decision-making.
[reg_e] Regulation E - Electronic Fund Transfers (12 CFR 1005) -- Regulation E, found at 12 CFR 1005, implements the Electronic Fund Transfer (EFT) Rule, which establishes consumer protections for electronic transactions such as ATM withdrawals, point-of-sale debits, and online transfers. It requires banks to disclose terms, resolve errors promptly, limit consumer liability for unauthorized transfers, and maintain records to safeguard consumer rights.

It prohibits abusive, deceptive, and unfair practices, requires validation of debts, and outlines disclosure and communication standards.

It caps fees that can be charged to merchants, requires transparency in fee disclosures, and sets rules for routing and network selection to promote competition and protect consumers.
[reg_p] Regulation P - Privacy of Consumer Financial Information (12 CFR 1016) -- Regulation P, found in 12 CFR 1016, establishes rules that require financial institutions to safeguard the privacy of consumer financial information, limit disclosures, and provide customers with notice of their privacy practices. It also outlines consumer rights to opt out of certain information sharing.

It governs how credit information is collected, shared and corrected, and outlines consumers' rights to obtain, dispute and limit the disclosure of their credit reports.

It establishes consumer-friendly standards for error resolution, cancellation and liability, aiming to increase transparency and protect senders in cross-border remittances.
[reporting] Reporting and Disclosure Regulations -- The Reporting and Disclosure Regulations in 12 CFR Chapter X require covered entities to submit periodic data to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau and to provide specified information to consumers about products, services, and practices. They establish formats, timelines, and content standards for these reports and disclosures, ensuring transparency and facilitating the agency's oversight of financial markets.
[small_biz_1071] Section 1071 - Small Business Lending Data Collection -- Section 1071 of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau regulations requires certain lenders to collect and report data on small business loans, including information on applicants, approvals, terms, and outcomes, to enhance transparency and monitor fair lending practices.
[udaap] UDAAP - Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices -- The UDAAP provision within the CFPB's regulations sets standards for what constitutes unfair, deceptive or abusive acts or practices in consumer financial products and services. It defines prohibited conduct, grants the agency enforcement authority, and offers guidance to help businesses avoid violating consumer-protection rules.
