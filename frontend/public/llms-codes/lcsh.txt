[LC] LCSH Broad Topics -- The LC entry "LCSH Broad Topics" designates the highest‐level, generic subject headings in the Library of Congress Subject Headings system. These broad topics serve as umbrella terms that group more specific headings, facilitating hierarchical browsing and consistent categorization across library collections.
[LC.A] General Works -- The LC.A heading designates the General Works category, covering reference materials such as encyclopedias, dictionaries, almanacs, bibliographies and other broad-scope publications not confined to a specific subject area. It serves as the top-level class for works that provide overview or introductory information across disciplines.
[LC.B] Philosophy, Psychology, Religion -- LC.B is the top-level Library of Congress Subject Heading for works dealing with philosophy, psychology and religion, grouping together texts on theoretical reflection, the scientific study of mental processes and behavior, and the study of faith traditions, doctrines and spiritual practice.
[LC.C] Auxiliary Sciences of History -- Auxiliary Sciences of History is a subject heading for works that treat the supporting disciplines used to investigate the past, such as archaeology, paleography, epigraphy, numismatics, chronology and related methodological studies. It helps locate materials that focus on the techniques and tools that enable historical research.
[LC.D] World History -- LC.D is the Library of Congress Subject Heading code for World History, grouping works that present a broad chronological narrative of global events, cultural exchanges, and major civilizational developments from ancient times to the present.
[LC.E] History of the Americas (General) -- LC.E denotes the Library of Congress Subject Heading for general histories of the Americas, encompassing broad surveys of North, Central and South America without focusing on a particular country, period, or thematic subfield. It is used to catalogue works that treat the continent as a whole, providing introductory or comprehensive overviews of its historical development.
[LC.G] Geography, Anthropology -- LC.G is the Library of Congress Subject Heading that links resources dealing with the cultural aspects of place, environment, and human groups. It is applied to works that combine geographic analysis with anthropological perspectives, such as studies of settlement patterns, landscape use, and spatial expressions of culture. This interdisciplinary heading helps users locate material that bridges physical space and social meaning.
[LC.H] Social Sciences -- LC.H is the Library of Congress Subject Heading for Social Sciences, a broad top-level term that groups works dealing with the systematic study of human society and social relationships, including fields such as sociology, economics, political science, anthropology, and related interdisciplinary research.
[LC.J] Political Science -- LC.J is the Library of Congress Subject Heading for Political Science, encompassing works on the theory and practice of government, political institutions, public policy, comparative politics, political behavior, and related methodological studies. It serves as a broad, interdisciplinary classification for all political science literature.
[LC.K] Law -- LC.K is the code for the subject heading "Law" in the Library of Congress Subject Headings skeleton. It is applied to works that discuss legal systems, statutes, jurisprudence and related topics across all jurisdictions, providing a broad category for cataloging and retrieval.
[LC.L] Education -- LC.L is the Library of Congress Subject Heading for education, covering topics such as teaching methods, curricula, schools, educational theory and policy. It groups together works that discuss how learning is organized, delivered and evaluated across all levels and settings.
[LC.M] Music -- LC.M is the top-level Library of Congress Subject Heading for music, encompassing works, concepts, and activities related to musical composition, performance, theory, history, and education. It serves as the primary entry point for cataloguing and searching materials about music across all formats and genres.
[LC.N] Fine Arts -- LC.N designates the Fine Arts heading in the Library of Congress Subject Headings, encompassing visual disciplines such as painting, sculpture, drawing, architecture, and related artistic media. It is used to index works that discuss aesthetic theory, history, techniques, and criticism of the visual arts.
[LC.P] Language and Literature -- LC.P is the Library of Congress Subject Heading that groups resources dealing with language and literature as an integrated field, encompassing linguistic theory, the study of language structure and use, as well as literary criticism, history and analysis. It is applied to works that explore the relationship between linguistic forms and literary expression.
[LC.Q] Science -- LC.Q designates the broad class of Science within the Library of Congress Classification system. It encompasses works on natural, physical, and biological sciences, including interdisciplinary studies, and serves as the parent category for more specific subclasses such as mathematics, astronomy, chemistry, geology, and related scientific disciplines.
[LC.R] Medicine -- LC.R is the Library of Congress Subject Heading that designates the broad field of medicine, encompassing the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease, as well as related research, medical education
[LC.S] Agriculture -- LC.S designates the subject heading "Agriculture," covering the science, practice and economics of cultivating plants and raising livestock, including crop production, soil management, agronomy, livestock breeding, farm infrastructure, and related policy and research. It is used by libraries to index books, journals and other resources about agricultural methods, technology, history and sustainability.
[LC.T] Technology -- LC.T is the Library of Congress Subject Heading used to classify material dealing with technology, the systematic application of scientific and engineering knowledge to create tools, processes, and systems. It encompasses works on industrial methods, technical innovation, and the societal impact of technological development.
[LC.U] Military Science -- LC.U denotes the subject heading "Military Science," covering the organized study of armed forces, including their tactics, strategy, organization, training, logistics, and related institutions, as well as the theoretical and practical aspects of warfare and defense policy.
[LC.Z] Bibliography, Library Science -- LC.Z denotes the Library of Congress Subject Heading for Bibliography in Library Science, covering works that discuss the principles, methods, history and theory of bibliography as a field of study within librarianship. It is used to classify texts that examine bibliographic description, citation practices, and the organization of bibliographic information.
