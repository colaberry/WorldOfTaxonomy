[T-C74] Adrenal Gland -- T-C74 denotes malignant neoplasms arising in the adrenal gland, including primary adrenal cortical carcinoma and tumors of the adrenal medulla such as pheochromocytoma. These cancers are classified by histologic type and may present with hormonal excess or an abdominal mass.
[T-C75] Other Endocrine Glands and Related Structures -- In ICD-O-3 the topography code C75 identifies neoplasms arising in other endocrine glands and related structures not covered by separate site codes, such as the pituitary, parathyroid, pineal and related neuroendocrine tissues. It is used to record malignant, in-situ or benign tumors of these organs when a more specific topography is unavailable. This code groups together a heterogeneous set of endocrine-related cancers for statistical and clinical reporting.
[T-C76] Other and Ill-Defined Sites -- T-C76 denotes neoplasms occurring in sites that are not otherwise classified or are poorly defined, covering primary tumors of unknown or unspecified origin. It is used when the anatomic location cannot be precisely identified, allowing registration of the malignancy without attributing it to a specific organ system.
[T-C77] Lymph Nodes -- T-C77 denotes malignant tumors that have spread to regional lymph nodes, indicating nodal metastasis. It is used in the ICD-O-3 to record the site of secondary involvement for staging and epidemiologic purposes, distinguishing nodal disease from primary lymphoid neoplasms.
[T-C80] Unknown Primary Site -- T-C80 denotes tumors whose primary location cannot be identified despite diagnostic evaluation. These cases are grouped under unknown primary site and are used for statistical and treatment-planning purposes, reflecting the lack of a defined organ of origin.
[TOPO] Topography (Anatomic Site) -- The TOPO entry in ICD-O-3 designates the topography component, which records the anatomic site of a neoplasm using a four-digit code linked to a specific body region. It complements the morphology code to fully describe a cancer case for registries and research, enabling consistent reporting of tumor location across institutions.
