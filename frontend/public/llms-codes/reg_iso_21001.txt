[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Clause 10.1 requires educational organizations to identify any nonconformities in their management system, investigate their causes and implement corrective actions to eliminate them. It also mandates recording the findings, reviewing the effectiveness of the actions taken and making necessary changes to prevent recurrence.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to establish processes for continual improvement of their management system and learning outcomes. It mandates the use of performance data, internal audits, corrective actions and the identification of improvement opportunities to enhance effectiveness and stakeholder satisfaction.
[10_3] 10.3 Opportunities for improvement -- Clause 10.3 of ISO 21001 2018 directs educational organisations to identify and act on opportunities for improvement of their management system, using monitoring, analysis of data and stakeholder feedback to enhance effectiveness and learner outcomes.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- The 4.1 clause requires an educational organization to determine internal and external factors that influence its purpose and strategic direction, such as legal, technological, cultural, and market conditions. It must identify stakeholders, their needs and expectations, and assess how these elements affect the management system's ability to achieve intended outcomes. This understanding forms the basis for setting objectives, risks, and opportunities throughout the standard.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to identify and analyze the needs, requirements and expectations of all relevant interested parties, such as students, parents, staff, regulators and partners. This understanding supports the design of the management system, ensures that services meet stakeholder expectations and helps mitigate risks associated with misaligned objectives.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the EOMS -- This clause requires the organization to define the boundaries and applicability of its Educational Organization Management System, considering the educational activities, stakeholders, locations, and interfaces with other management systems. The defined scope forms the basis for establishing objectives, processes, and compliance obligations.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management of educational organizations to demonstrate leadership and commitment to the educational management system, ensuring alignment with strategic direction, providing necessary resources, and promoting continual improvement. They must establish the policy, assign responsibilities, and engage stakeholders to embed quality education objectives.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- Clause 5.2 of ISO 21001:2018 mandates that an educational organization create, implement and maintain a policy for its management system. The policy must articulate the organization's purpose, context and commitment to meet learner requirements, comply with applicable obligations and pursue continual improvement.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- The entry specifies that an educational organization must identify, document and communicate the roles, responsibilities and authorities of personnel involved in the management system. It requires clear assignment of duties, adequate competence, and defined reporting lines to ensure effective implementation and continual improvement of the system.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- The clause requires the organization to identify and evaluate risks and opportunities that could affect its educational management system, then plan and implement appropriate actions to address them, integrate these actions into its processes, and monitor their effectiveness.
[6_2] 6.2 Objectives and planning to achieve them -- ISO 21001:2018 sets out requirements for a management system in educational organizations. Clause 6.2 obliges the organization to establish measurable objectives that reflect its educational purpose and to design plans that allocate resources, assign responsibilities, define processes and set monitoring and review mechanisms to achieve those goals.
[7_1] 7.1 Resources -- Clause 7.1 of ISO 21001:2018 specifies that an educational organization must identify, allocate and maintain the resources needed to operate its management system and achieve its educational objectives, covering infrastructure, learning environment, human competence, financial and informational support.
[7_2] 7.2 Competence -- Clause 7.2 requires educational organizations to determine and provide the competence needed for persons affecting the management system, including teachers, staff and support personnel. Competence must be achieved through appropriate education, training or experience, documented, and periodically evaluated to ensure ongoing effectiveness and continuous improvement.
[7_3] 7.3 Awareness -- ISO 21001 7.3 Awareness requires educational organizations to ensure that personnel and relevant stakeholders understand the management system, its purpose, policies and procedures, and how their roles contribute to achieving quality education outcomes, fostering a culture of continuous improvement.
[7_4] 7.4 Communication -- Clause 7.4 requires the organization to determine what, when and how to communicate information relevant to the educational management system, both internally and externally. It mandates establishing processes for effective exchange of policies, objectives, performance results, changes and responsibilities, ensuring that relevant parties receive accurate, timely information to support the system's operation and continual improvement.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for planning and controlling the operations that affect their management system, ensuring they align with the quality policy, objectives and stakeholder needs. This includes defining actions, resources, responsibilities, criteria for monitoring performance and mechanisms for corrective action.
[8_2] 8.2 Requirements for educational products and services -- ISO 21001:2018 clause 8.2 requires educational organizations to ensure that all learning products and services meet the needs of learners and other interested parties, comply with applicable legal and regulatory requirements, and are designed, delivered and evaluated consistently and effectively. It also mandates documented processes for planning, designing, delivering, monitoring and continually improving these educational offerings.
[8_3] 8.3 Design and development -- Clause 8.3 of ISO 21001:2018 specifies the requirements for designing and developing educational products and services, outlining how organizations must plan, control, and verify these processes to meet learner needs, statutory obligations, and quality objectives, while managing changes and ensuring appropriate documentation.
[8_4] 8.4 Control of externally provided processes -- Clause 8.4 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to establish controls over any external processes, products or services that can affect their management system, ensuring they are selected, evaluated and monitored against defined criteria. The organization must maintain documented information on these controls, assess risks, communicate requirements to providers and verify that outsourced activities meet the intended quality and compliance objectives.
[8_5] 8.5 Delivery of educational products and services -- Clause 8.5 of ISO 21001:2018 specifies requirements for delivering educational products and services, ensuring they meet learner needs, statutory and regulatory obligations, and the organization's quality objectives. It covers planning, execution, monitoring, and continual improvement of delivery processes to achieve consistent, effective outcomes.
[8_6] 8.6 Release of educational products and services -- Section 8.6 addresses the processes for releasing educational products and services, ensuring they meet defined quality criteria before delivery to learners or stakeholders. It requires documented verification, validation and approval steps, and records
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to establish processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the performance of their management system. It ensures that data on learner outcomes, stakeholder satisfaction and internal audits are systematically collected and reviewed to drive continual improvement.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 requires educational organizations to conduct internal audits at planned intervals to verify that the management system conforms to ISO 21001, its own processes, and any applicable requirements. Audits must be objective and independent of the area being audited, with documented findings and corrective actions that support continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- In ISO 21001:2018 clause 9.3, top management of an educational organization must conduct periodic reviews of the management system to confirm its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review considers audit results, performance indicators, stakeholder feedback, changes in internal or external context and any identified risks, and results in documented decisions for improvement.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 outlines the requirements for continual improvement of an educational organization's management system, mandating the identification of opportunities, the planning and implementation of corrective actions, and the measurement of results. It emphasizes a systematic approach to enhance effectiveness, efficiency and stakeholder satisfaction over time.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 requires educational organizations to determine internal and external issues relevant to their purpose, identify interested parties and their needs, and define the scope of the management system.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 specifies the leadership duties required for an effective educational organization management system, mandating top management to demonstrate commitment, set the education-focused quality policy and strategic objectives, and assign clear responsibilities. It also requires leaders to promote continual improvement, ensure integration of the system into organizational processes and engage stakeholders to support the intended outcomes.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to establish a systematic planning process that identifies and addresses risks and opportunities, sets measurable learning-service objectives, and determines necessary resources and actions. It also outlines how to plan and control changes to the management system to ensure continual improvement.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 21001:2018 outlines the support requirements for educational organizations, specifying the resources, competence, awareness and communication needed to implement and maintain the management system. It also addresses the control of documented information, ensuring that necessary data are available, protected and properly managed throughout the organization.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 21001:2018 specifies the operational requirements for educational organizations, detailing how they must plan, implement and control the processes needed to deliver learning services and achieve planned outcomes. It covers resource management, curriculum design and delivery, monitoring, evaluation and continual improvement of educational activities to meet learner and stakeholder needs.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 21001:2018 requires educational organizations to monitor, measure, analyse and evaluate the effectiveness of their management system and educational outcomes. It outlines processes for internal audits, management review and continual improvement, ensuring that performance data inform decisions and drive enhancements in teaching, learning and stakeholder satisfaction.
