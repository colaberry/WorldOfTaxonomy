[ECF] e-CF Dimensions -- The e-CF Dimensions entry defines the three overarching categories - Knowledge, Skills and Attitudes - that structure the e-Competence Framework. It explains how each dimension groups related competence elements and supports mapping ICT professional profiles across the European labour market, providing a common language for describing and assessing competencies.
[ECF.A] Plan -- The "Plan" competence (ECF.A) in the e-Competence Framework describes the ability to define the scope, objectives, resources, schedule and risk-mitigation measures for ICT initiatives, ensuring alignment with business goals and regulatory requirements and providing a basis for subsequent implementation and monitoring.
[ECF.A1] IS and Business Strategy Alignment -- ECF.A1 in the e-Competence Framework version 4 describes the competence of aligning information systems with the organisation's business strategy. It covers defining IT-business roadmaps, ensuring governance and coordination so that technology initiatives directly support corporate objectives and deliver value.
[ECF.A2] Service Level Management -- ECF.A2 Service Level Management is the competence that ensures agreed service performance is defined, documented and monitored through Service Level Agreements. It involves negotiating targets with customers, measuring actual service delivery against those targets, reporting results and taking corrective actions to maintain the required quality and reliability of IT services.
[ECF.A3] Business Plan Development -- ECF.A3 Business Plan Development denotes the competence to create comprehensive business plans that outline strategic objectives, market positioning, risk assessment and financial forecasts. It involves analysing stakeholder requirements, defining operational models, and presenting actionable road-maps that support decision-making and resource allocation. This skill ensures that organisations can articulate viable growth strategies and attract investment or approval.
[ECF.A4] Product/Service Planning -- Product/Service Planning involves defining market needs, specifying requirements and creating a roadmap for developing and delivering a product or service. It includes analysing customer expectations, assessing feasibility and coordinating resources to ensure the solution meets quality, cost and time targets.
[ECF.A5] Architecture Design -- Architecture Design is the ability to define the overall structure of ICT solutions, selecting components, standards and integration patterns to meet functional and non-functional requirements. It involves analysing business needs, creating high-level models, evaluating alternatives and ensuring alignment with enterprise guidelines and security policies.
[ECF.A6] Application Design -- The entry denotes the competence to design software applications, encompassing the analysis of user needs, definition of functional and technical architecture, selection of appropriate technologies, and planning of user interfaces, data structures, and system integration. It also includes ensuring usability, security, scalability and alignment with business objectives.

[ECF.A9] Innovating -- Innovating involves generating and implementing original ideas, methods or solutions that add value, improve efficiency or create new products and services. It requires using creative thinking, research, experimentation and knowledge of market trends to design and test novel approaches, and then promoting their adoption across the organization.
[ECF.B] Build -- Build is an e-Competence Framework competence that covers the ability to create, develop, and integrate ICT solutions
[ECF.B1] Application Development -- ECF.B1 - Application Development denotes the competence to analyse user needs, design, code, test and maintain software applications using appropriate development methods, programming languages and tools, while ensuring quality, security and alignment with organisational standards and project constraints.
[ECF.B2] Component Integration -- The e-CF code ECF.B2 - Component Integration - defines the competence to plan, organise and execute the assembly of software or hardware components into a coherent system, ensuring compatibility, performance and security. It includes analysing specifications, selecting appropriate interfaces, coordinating testing, and managing any required adaptations to achieve seamless operation within the overall solution.
[ECF.B3] Testing -- Testing involves planning, designing and executing test procedures to verify that ICT solutions meet functional and non-functional requirements, analysing results, documenting defects and ensuring quality standards are achieved, and recommending corrective actions where necessary.
[ECF.B4] Solution Deployment -- Solution Deployment involves planning and executing the installation, configuration, testing and handover of IT solutions to meet agreed specifications, ensuring they operate effectively in the target environment. It includes coordinating resources, managing change, documenting the deployment process and providing initial support to users.
[ECF.B5] Documentation Production -- Documentation Production is the competence to create, organise and maintain clear, complete and standards-compliant written or electronic records that support business processes. It covers drafting reports, manuals, specifications, logs and updating databases, while applying appropriate formatting, version control and distribution methods.
[ECF.C] Run -- Run covers the day-to-day operation and maintenance of ICT services, ensuring systems perform reliably, incidents are resolved, and service levels are met. It includes monitoring, troubleshooting, applying patches and managing changes to keep the environment stable.
[ECF.C1] User Support -- The ECF.C1 "User Support" competency covers the ability to provide assistance, training and troubleshooting for IT users, ensuring they can effectively operate applications, services and devices. It includes diagnosing problems, delivering clear instructions, documenting resolutions and maintaining a responsive, customer-focused service environment.

[ECF.C3] Service Delivery -- ECF.C3 Service Delivery defines the competence required to plan, deliver, monitor and improve services so they meet user needs and agreed quality standards. It covers managing service requests, incidents, problems and service-level agreements, ensuring timely, reliable and compliant outcomes.
[ECF.C4] Problem Management -- Problem Management (ECF.C4) in the e-Competence Framework v4 refers to the systematic practice of identifying the root causes of incidents, analysing them, and implementing permanent solutions to prevent recurrence. It involves coordinating with technical teams, documenting known errors, and improving service reliability through proactive prevention and continuous learning.
[ECF.D] Enable -- Enable refers to the competence area that ensures ICT solutions are prepared, configured and made ready for use, covering activities such as gathering requirements, designing solutions, setting up environments, testing and supporting user adoption. It focuses on aligning resources, infrastructure and stakeholders so that services can be deployed effectively.
[ECF.D1] Information Security -- The e-CF v4 entry ECF.D1 - Information Security defines the competence to protect an organisation's information assets by identifying risks, applying appropriate security measures, and ensuring compliance with relevant policies and regulations. It covers knowledge of threats, controls, incident handling and the ability to advise on secure practices across ICT environments.
[ECF.D2] ICT Quality Strategy -- ICT Quality Strategy defines and maintains the policies, standards and processes that ensure information and communication technology services meet agreed quality levels, align with business objectives and comply with regulations. It includes setting quality targets, monitoring performance, handling incidents, and driving continual improvement across the ICT environment.
[ECF.D3] Education and Training -- Education and Training involves analysing learning needs, designing and delivering appropriate instructional programmes, and evaluating their effectiveness to support personal and professional development. It includes creating curricula, selecting methods and resources, facilitating learning sessions, and providing feedback to improve competence across individuals and organisations.
[ECF.E] Manage -- Manage involves planning, organising, coordinating and controlling resources to achieve objectives, ensuring effective use of time, budget and personnel, and monitoring progress while adapting to changes.
[ECF.E1] Forecast Development -- Forecast Development involves analysing data, trends and variables to produce reliable predictions of future business, market or operational conditions. It includes selecting suitable techniques, building and testing models, and presenting the results so that managers can make informed strategic decisions.
[ECF.E2] Project Management -- The e-CF v4 entry ECF.E2 "Project Management" defines the competence to plan, organise, monitor and control projects to achieve defined objectives within scope, time, cost and quality constraints. It covers setting project goals, allocating resources, managing risks, communicating with stakeholders and ensuring deliverables meet agreed standards. This competence is applicable across all sectors that undertake temporary endeavours to produce unique results.
[ECF.E3] Risk Management -- Risk Management (ECF E3) covers the systematic identification, analysis and prioritisation of potential threats to an organisation's objectives, followed by the planning and implementation of measures to mitigate or exploit those risks. It requires an understanding of risk sources, impact assessment, and continual monitoring to ensure controls remain effective and aligned with strategic goals.
[ECF.E4] Relationship Management -- The ECF.E4 Relationship Management competence covers the ability to establish, develop and sustain productive interactions with internal and external stakeholders. It involves understanding stakeholder needs, building trust, influencing outcomes, and maintaining networks that support organisational objectives. This competence is essential for effective collaboration, negotiation and long-term partnership success.
[ECF.E5] Process Improvement -- The entry ECF.E5 - Process Improvement - defines the competence to analyse, design and optimise organisational processes to increase efficiency, quality and value. It includes identifying improvement opportunities, applying systematic methods, monitoring results and embedding changes to sustain performance gains.
[ECF.E6] ICT Quality Management -- ICT Quality Management involves planning, implementing and monitoring quality assurance activities for ICT services and products. It ensures compliance with standards, manages risk, measures performance and drives continuous improvement to meet stakeholder expectations.
[ECF.E7] Business Change Management -- Business Change Management involves planning, leading and controlling organisational transformation initiatives to align processes, technologies and people with strategic goals. It includes assessing impact, developing change strategies, stakeholder communication, training and monitoring adoption to ensure sustainable benefits.
[ECF.E8] Information Security Management -- The entry ECF.E8 - Information Security Management - defines the competence to design, implement and oversee security policies, procedures and controls that protect an organisation's information assets. It includes assessing risks, ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements, managing incidents, and promoting a security-aware culture across the enterprise.
[ECF.E9] IS Governance -- ECF.E9 IS Governance is the competence to establish, apply and monitor governance structures, policies and procedures that align information-system activities with organisational objectives, manage related risks, ensure regulatory compliance and maximise the value of IT investments.
