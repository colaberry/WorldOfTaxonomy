It enumerates substances of very high concern that may only be placed on the market or used when specific authorisation is granted, helping to phase out risks associated with these chemicals.

[dossier_eval] Dossier Evaluation (Art 40-43) -- The dossier evaluation (Art 40-43) under REACH (EC No 1907/2006) is the systematic review by the European Chemicals Agency of registration dossiers submitted by manufacturers or importers, checking data completeness, compliance with testing requirements and the adequacy of risk-management measures, and forming the basis for subsequent authorisation or restriction decisions.
[downstream] Downstream User Obligations (Art 37-39) -- Downstream users of chemicals covered by the REACH regulation must fulfil obligations set out in Articles 37 to 39, which require them to obtain and pass on safety information, ensure safe handling and use, and, where necessary, inform the European Chemicals Agency of any concerns or incidents. These duties apply to the entire supply chain beyond the original manufacturer.
[echa_role] ECHA Role and Committees -- The entry outlines the European Chemicals Agency's (ECHA) responsibilities under REACH and describes its governance structure, including the Management Board, Scientific Committee, and various risk-assessment and risk-management committees that support decisions on registration, evaluation, authorisation and restriction of chemicals. It details how these bodies interact with the European Commission and member-state authorities to implement the regulation.
[registration] Registration Requirements (Art 5-24) -- The entry details the mandatory steps companies must follow to register chemical substances with the European Chemicals Agency under REACH, covering data submission, dossier preparation, fees, timelines and compliance obligations as prescribed in Articles 5-24.
[restrictions] Annex XVII - Restrictions -- Annex XVII of the REACH regulation lists chemicals that are prohibited or subject to specific limits on manufacture, import, placing on the market or use within the EU. The restrictions may apply to particular substances, mixtures or articles and often set maximum concentration levels, exposure conditions or ban certain applications.
[sds] Safety Data Sheets (Art 31-36) -- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are the standardized documents required under REACH (Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006) that convey essential hazard and handling information for substances and mixtures. They must be prepared and supplied by manufacturers or importers in accordance with Articles 31-36, ensuring workers and downstream users can manage chemical risks safely.
[substance_eval] Substance Evaluation (Art 44-48) -- The REACH regulation (EC No 1907/2006) requires the European Chemicals Agency to assess certain substances for safety risks under Articles 44-48. This "substance evaluation" process identifies candidates for further regulatory action, such as authorisation or restriction, based on hazard and exposure information submitted by industry and other stakeholders. It aims to protect human health and the environment by ensuring that high-concern chemicals are rigorously examined.
[svhc] Substances of Very High Concern (SVHC) (Art 57) -- Under REACH (EC No 1907/2006) the "svhc" entry designates Substances of Very High Concern listed in Annex IV, identified because they pose serious hazards such as carcinogenic, mutagenic or reproductive toxicity, persistence, bioaccumulation, or equivalent risks. Inclusion triggers obligations for communication, possible authorisation under Article 57 and eventual restriction to protect human health and the environment.
[title_1] Title I - General Issues -- Title I of the REACH regulation sets out the general framework, defining key terms, the scope of the regulation and the overall principles governing the registration, evaluation, authorisation and restriction of chemicals within the European Union. It establishes the basic obligations of manufacturers, importers and downstream users and outlines the procedural structure for the system's operation.
[title_10] Title X - ECHA -- Title X is the tenth section of the EU REACH regulation (EC No 1907/2006) and outlines the general provisions governing the registration, evaluation, authorisation and restriction of chemicals, including definitions, scope, and basic procedural rules applied by ECHA.
[title_2] Title II - Registration of Substances -- Title II of the REACH regulation sets out the compulsory registration of chemical substances produced or imported into the European Union in quantities of at least one tonne per year. It requires registrants to submit a dossier to the European Chemicals Agency containing data on physicochemical properties, hazards, uses and safe-use guidance, enabling assessment of risks to human health and the environment.
[title_3] Title III - Data Sharing and Avoidance of Unnecessary Testing -- Title III of the REACH regulation establishes a framework for sharing information on chemicals among manufacturers, importers and downstream users, requiring the creation of joint dossiers and the use of existing data to prevent duplicate testing, especially on vertebrate animals, and to promote the use of alternative methods wherever possible.
[title_5] Title V - Downstream User Obligations -- Title V of REACH sets out the duties of downstream users who incorporate chemicals into articles or mixtures, require safe-use information, or modify substances. It obliges users to assess risks, request additional data from suppliers when needed, and comply with any restrictions or authorisations that apply to the chemicals they handle. The provisions aim to ensure safe use throughout the supply chain and protect human health and the environment.
[title_6] Title VI - Evaluation -- Title VI of the REACH regulation sets out the evaluation process for chemical substances, where the European Chemicals Agency reviews registration dossiers and conducts scientific assessments to verify safe use. The evaluation determines whether additional risk-management measures or further data are required to protect human health and the environment.
[title_7] Title VII - Authorisation -- Title VII establishes the authorisation system for substances of very high concern, requiring companies to obtain permission before use and to demonstrate that risks are adequately controlled or that socio-economic benefits outweigh those risks.
[title_8] Title VIII - Restrictions -- Title VIII of REACH establishes the legal framework for imposing restrictions on the manufacture, placing on the market, or use of certain hazardous substances, mixtures or articles to protect human health and the environment, detailing the list of restricted items and the conditions for exemptions or phase-outs.
[title_9] Title IX - Fees and Charges -- Title IX under REACH outlines the fees and charges that companies must pay to the European Chemicals Agency for services such as dossier evaluation, registration, and authorisation, as well as for the handling of substances of very high concern. It specifies fee categories, payment timelines, and exemptions, ensuring that cost recovery supports the implementation and enforcement of the regulation.
