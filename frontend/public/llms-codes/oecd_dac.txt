[100] Social Infrastructure and Services -- Code 100 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Codes classifies aid directed toward social infrastructure and services, encompassing projects that deliver education, health care, social protection and related community facilities. It groups funding intended to improve human well-being and promote social development.
[110] Education -- The 110 Education code designates aid directed to formal schooling and training at all levels, including primary, secondary, tertiary, vocational and adult education, as well as literacy and capacity-building programmes that enhance knowledge and skills. It covers expenditures for school infrastructure, teaching materials, teacher salaries and related support services.
[111] Education, Level Unspecified -- The code 111 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Classification designates spending on education without specifying the level of schooling, covering all activities aimed at teaching, learning and related support services across primary, secondary, vocational or higher education. It is used in reporting to capture general education assistance when the exact educational tier is not identified.
[112] Basic Education -- Code 112 - Basic Education denotes aid directed to the provision of primary and lower secondary schooling, including early-childhood and foundational literacy, numeracy and life-skill programs. It covers construction and operation of schools, teacher training, curricula development and related support aimed at expanding access to compulsory education for children and youth.
[113] Secondary Education -- Code 113 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose classification denotes aid or development finance devoted to secondary education. It includes support for general secondary schools, vocational and technical training, and other programs that prepare adolescents for higher education or entry into the labour market.
[114] Post-Secondary Education -- Code 114 denotes post-secondary education, covering activities provided by universities, colleges, vocational institutes and other tertiary-level institutions, as well as related support services such as scholarships, research grants and student counseling. This sector purpose is used to classify aid aimed at expanding access to higher education and improving its quality.
[115] Early Childhood Education -- Code 115 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose classification designates Early Childhood Education, covering programs that provide pre-primary schooling, preschool, and other learning activities for children typically aged three to six, aimed at fostering cognitive and social development before entering formal elementary education.
[116] Education Policy and Administrative Management -- Code 116 denotes aid aimed at formulating and overseeing education policies, including planning, governance, curriculum standards, and administrative reforms that improve the overall management of education systems. It covers activities that support the design, implementation, and evaluation of educational strategies and institutional capacities.
[120] Health -- The OECD DAC CRS code 120 designates the health sector, covering aid and projects that promote public health, medical care, disease prevention, and the development of health infrastructure and services. It includes activities such as vaccination programs, health education, hospital construction, and the provision of essential medicines. This code is used to track and report resources directed toward improving population health outcomes.
[121] Health, General -- Code 121 - Health, General, covers all primary and secondary health-care services and public-health activities that are not otherwise specified. It includes medical treatment, disease prevention, health promotion, the supply of medicines and medical equipment, and the operation of hospitals, clinics and other health facilities.
[122] Basic Health -- The code 122 "Basic Health" in the OECD DAC CRS classification groups assistance aimed at providing fundamental medical services and infrastructure, such as primary health-care delivery, disease prevention, basic treatment and the necessary equipment and facilities to support these activities. It covers projects that address essential health needs for populations, especially in low-income and vulnerable settings.
[123] Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) -- Code 123 under the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Codes (CRS) designates activities addressing non-communicable diseases (NCDs). It covers aid aimed at preventing, treating and researching chronic conditions such as cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes and chronic respiratory illnesses, as well as strengthening health systems and policies to reduce NCD burdens.
[130] Population Policies -- Code 130 - Population Policies refers to aid activities that support the design, implementation or evaluation of government policies affecting a country's demographic trends, such as family-planning programs, reproductive-health services, fertility and mortality research, and measures to influence population size, age structure or distribution.
[131] Population Policy and Administration -- The 131 code designates activities that formulate and manage national population policies, including family-planning programs, demographic research, census operations, vital-statistics systems and related administrative support for tracking and influencing population trends.
[140] Water Supply and Sanitation -- Code 140 in the OECD DAC CRS sector-purpose classification covers water supply and sanitation activities, including the construction and rehabilitation of water-delivery systems, treatment and distribution infrastructure, wastewater collection and treatment, and hygiene promotion aimed at improving public health and environmental quality.
[141] Water Supply and Sanitation - Large Systems -- Code 141 designates large-scale water supply and sanitation projects, such as municipal piped networks, water-treatment plants, and centralized wastewater facilities. It covers aid intended to develop, expand, or improve these extensive infrastructure systems that serve sizable populations.
[142] Basic Drinking Water Supply and Sanitation -- Code 142 in the OECD-DAC CRS sector purpose list denotes projects that deliver basic drinking-water supply and sanitation services, including the construction or rehabilitation of water intake, treatment and distribution systems, household connections, latrines and sewage disposal facilities, and other essential infrastructure that ensures safe, affordable access to potable water and basic hygiene for underserved populations.
[143] River Development -- Code 143 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose classification denotes projects aimed at developing rivers, including activities such as river channel improvement, flood control, navigation enhancement, and the creation or rehabilitation of waterways for irrigation, hydropower, and ecosystem services. It groups aid initiatives that modify or manage river systems to support economic, social, or environmental objectives.
[150] Government and Civil Society -- Code 150 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Codes classifies activities that support government institutions and civil-society organisations, such as capacity-building, policy advice and public-administration strengthening. It covers projects aimed at improving governance, democratic processes and the effectiveness of non-governmental groups.
[151] Government and Civil Society - General -- Code 151 groups aid aimed at general support for national and local governments and civil-society groups, covering activities that strengthen institutional capacity, governance, policy development and broad public services without being tied to a specific sector such as health or education. It is used for projects that provide overall administrative assistance, legal reforms, or capacity-building programmes for public agencies and NGOs.
[152] Conflict, Peace and Security -- Code 152 in the OECD DAC CRS sector purpose classification groups aid aimed at preventing or managing armed conflict, supporting peacebuilding and reconciliation, strengthening security institutions, and providing humanitarian assistance in conflict-affected areas. It covers programs that promote stability, rule of law, disarmament, and post-conflict reconstruction.
[160] Other Social Infrastructure and Services -- Code 160 - Other Social Infrastructure and Services groups aid activities that develop or maintain facilities and services for the general public's welfare but are not classified elsewhere, such as community centres, recreational programs, social safety-net facilities, and other non-medical social amenities that support quality of life.
[200] Economic Infrastructure and Services -- The 200 Economic Infrastructure and Services code groups aid directed toward the construction, operation and maintenance of physical infrastructure such as transport networks, power and water supply, telecommunications and related service facilities, as well as the provision of the services that enable these systems to function. It captures projects that improve basic economic foundations and support broader development objectives.
[210] Transport and Storage -- The 210 Transport and Storage sector covers activities that move passengers and freight by road, rail, air, water or pipelines and includes warehousing and storage services. In the OECD DAC CRS system it categorises aid that supports transport infrastructure, logistics and related capacity-building projects in developing countries.
[220] Communications -- Code 220 denotes the communications sector, encompassing activities that deliver telephone, internet, broadcasting and satellite services. It covers the development, expansion and support of infrastructure and networks to improve connectivity.
[230] Energy -- Code 230 under the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Codes (CRS) designates aid directed toward the energy sector, covering projects that develop, upgrade or maintain power generation and supply infrastructure, promote renewable energy sources, improve energy efficiency, and support related research and policy initiatives. It groups all development assistance aimed at enhancing a country's energy capacity and sustainability.
[231] Energy Policy -- The code 231 - Energy Policy refers to aid activities that support the formulation, implementation and evaluation of national or regional policies and strategies aimed at improving energy supply, efficiency, security and sustainability, including measures for renewable energy, conservation and climate-friendly energy planning.
[232] Energy Generation, Renewable Sources -- Code 232 identifies aid and development finance directed toward the creation and expansion of renewable energy generation, including solar, wind, hydro, geothermal and biomass projects. It is used in the OECD DAC sector purpose classification to track investments that increase clean power capacity and reduce reliance on fossil-fuel sources.
[233] Energy Generation, Non-Renewable Sources -- Code 233 in the OECD DAC CRS classification identifies aid projects that generate electricity from non-renewable sources such as coal, oil, natural gas and nuclear power. It covers activities that develop, operate or upgrade conventional power plants and related infrastructure to expand or sustain fossil-fuel based energy capacity.
[240] Banking and Financial Services -- The OECD DAC CRS sector purpose code 240 covers institutions that provide banking and other financial services, including deposits, loans, payment processing, insurance, and investment management. It groups entities that mobilize savings and allocate capital to support development objectives.
[250] Business and Other Services -- The OECD DAC Sector Purpose Code 250 - Business and Other Services groups private-sector enterprises that provide commercial activities and ancillary services, ranging from wholesale and retail trade to transport, storage, communications, construction, finance, insurance, and other professional or personal services not classified elsewhere. It is used in the CRS to identify aid directed toward supporting the development of business operations and service-industry activities.
[300] Production Sectors -- Code 300 in the OECD-DAC CRS sector purpose list designates "Production Sectors" and covers aid activities that aim to increase output, efficiency or value-addition in economic activities such as agriculture, manufacturing, mining, energy and related processing industries.
[310] Agriculture, Forestry and Fishing -- The 310 code classifies aid directed to activities that improve agricultural production, manage forests sustainably, and support fisheries. It covers projects such as crop development, livestock health, forest conservation, and fishery management aimed at boosting food security and rural livelihoods. This sector purpose is used in the OECD DAC Creditable Reporting System for tracking development assistance.
[311] Agriculture -- OECD-DAC sector purpose code 311 designates assistance directed to agriculture, encompassing activities that develop crop production, livestock, horticulture and related agribusiness services. It is used in the CRS to classify aid that supports food security, rural livelihoods and the modernisation of farming systems.
[312] Forestry -- The 312 Forestry code designates aid directed toward activities that manage, conserve, and restore forest resources, including sustainable forest management, reforestation, afforestation, timber production, and related research and capacity-building efforts. It groups projects that aim to protect biodiversity, improve livelihoods, and mitigate climate change through the stewardship of forest ecosystems.
[313] Fishing -- Code 313 under the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Codes identifies activities related to fishing, including capture fisheries, aquaculture, and associated support services.
[320] Industry, Mining, Construction -- The OECD DAC sector purpose code 320 groups aid and development activities aimed at the industry, mining, and construction sectors. It covers projects that support manufacturing, extraction of minerals, and the building of infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and housing. This code is used in the CRS to classify assistance that promotes economic growth and structural development in these core economic areas.
[321] Industry -- Code 321 - Industry covers official development assistance directed to activities that develop or expand industrial capacity, including manufacturing, mining, construction and related services, with the aim of boosting productive capacity and employment in developing countries.
[322] Mineral Resources and Mining -- Code 322 covers activities related to the exploration, extraction, processing and beneficiation of mineral resources, including mining of coal, metal ores, industrial minerals and related support services. It also includes quarrying and the development of mining infrastructure.
[323] Construction -- Code 323 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose classification designates the construction sector, covering activities that involve the erection, alteration, repair, and demolition of buildings, civil engineering works, and related infrastructure projects, as well as associated services such as site preparation and building-material supply.
[330] Trade Policies and Regulations -- The OECD DAC Sector Purpose Code 330 identifies aid directed toward the formulation, implementation and evaluation of trade policies, the removal of trade barriers and the establishment of regulatory frameworks that facilitate the flow of goods and services across borders. It encompasses support for trade negotiations, customs reforms, standards harmonisation and measures that improve market access for developing-country exporters.
[331] Trade Policies and Regulations -- The 331 code classifies development assistance that supports the design, implementation or reform of trade policies and regulatory frameworks, including measures to improve market access, customs procedures, standards and trade facilitation. It covers projects aimed at strengthening institutions and capacity to negotiate, enforce and monitor trade agreements and related regulations.
[332] Tourism -- Code 332 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose classification identifies the tourism sector, covering activities that facilitate travel and recreation such as transport services, accommodation, sightseeing, entertainment and related support services. It groups expenditures aimed at promoting or delivering tourist experiences.
[400] Multi-Sector / Cross-Cutting -- Code 400 in the OECD DAC CRS sector-purpose classification denotes multi-sector or cross-cutting activities, where development assistance supports objectives that cut across several thematic areas rather than a single sector. It is used for reporting projects that have broad, integrated impacts.
[410] General Environment Protection -- Code 410 - General Environment Protection groups aid that safeguards ecosystems and natural resources, reduces pollution, promotes sustainable land, water and air quality management, and supports climate-change mitigation and biodiversity conservation. It includes projects such as reforestation, waste-management improvements, renewable-energy promotion and environmental-policy development.
[430] Other Multi-Sector -- Code 430 Other Multi-Sector classifies aid activities that span more than one sector and do not fit into the standard sector categories. It is used for projects or programmes that address cross-cutting issues, such as policy dialogue, capacity-building or coordination efforts that involve multiple thematic areas.
[431] Urban Development and Management -- Code 431 - Urban Development and Management classifies aid directed at improving the planning, construction, and operation of urban spaces. It includes projects that develop housing, transport, water, sanitation, and land-use systems to enhance the livability and sustainability of cities and towns.
[432] Rural Development -- Code 432 - Rural Development covers activities that improve the economic, social and environmental conditions in rural areas, including agricultural support, infrastructure, livelihood diversification and natural resource management.
[500] Commodity Aid / General Programme Assistance -- Code 500 denotes commodity aid and general programme assistance, covering in-kind contributions such as food, medicine or other goods, as well as broad program support that is not targeted to a specific sector. It is used by donors in OECD DAC reporting to classify aid flows that provide tangible items or overall program funding.
[510] General Budget Support -- General Budget Support (code 510) refers to non-earmarked financial assistance provided directly to a recipient country's government to help finance its overall public sector budget and policy priorities, aiming to strengthen fiscal management and promote development outcomes.
[520] Development Food Assistance -- Code 520 in the OECD DAC CRS purpose classification designates Development Food Assistance, covering aid that provides food commodities, food-distribution programmes, nutrition support and related logistics to alleviate hunger and improve food security in developing countries. It includes both emergency relief and longer-term food-aid projects aimed at vulnerable populations.
[530] Other Commodity Assistance -- The 530 "Other Commodity Assistance" code is used for aid delivered as non-food commodities such as medical supplies, seeds, construction materials or other tangible goods provided directly to beneficiaries or through partners, distinguishing these items from cash, in-kind, or food-specific assistance in OECD DAC reporting.
[600] Action Relating to Debt (Bilateral) -- Code 600 refers to bilateral actions by official donors to address a partner country's external debt, including debt forgiveness, restructuring and rescheduling, with the aim of improving debt sustainability and supporting development objectives.
[601] Action Relating to Debt -- The 601 "Action Relating to Debt" code captures aid activities that address a borrower's debt burden, including debt relief, restructuring, rescheduling, forgiveness, and the provision of concessional loans. It is used in the OECD DAC CRS to report assistance aimed at improving the financial position of the recipient country.
[700] Humanitarian Aid -- Code 700 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Classification denotes Humanitarian Aid, covering resources allocated for emergency relief, disaster response, and conflict-related assistance that aim to save lives, protect health, and alleviate immediate suffering. It is distinct from longer-term development programmes and is reported in the CRS to track short-term crisis interventions.
[720] Emergency Response -- The 720 Emergency Response code designates humanitarian assistance provided to address the immediate needs arising from natural or man-made disasters, conflicts or other crises. It covers relief activities such as food distribution, shelter, water, sanitation, health services and logistics support aimed at stabilising affected populations until longer-term recovery programs can begin. This purpose code is used in reporting to track short-term disaster-relief expenditures.
[730] Reconstruction Relief -- Code 730 denotes funds provided for reconstruction relief, which support rebuilding of infrastructure, housing and basic services in areas affected by conflict or natural disasters. It is classified under the OECD DAC purpose codes for aid aimed at restoring essential facilities and livelihoods after emergencies.
[740] Disaster Prevention and Preparedness -- Code 740 in the OECD DAC Sector Purpose Classification refers to financing aimed at reducing the risk and impact of natural or technological hazards. It covers activities such as early-warning systems, risk assessments, community training, emergency planning and other measures that strengthen a country's capacity to prevent or better respond to disasters.
