[AP] APGAR Components -- The AP entry outlines the five criteria used to calculate the APGAR newborn assessment score: Appearance (skin color), Pulse (heart rate), Grimace (reflex irritability), Activity (muscle tone), and Respiration (breathing effort). Each component is rated from 0 to 2, and the sum provides a rapid evaluation of a newborn's physiological condition at one and five minutes after birth.
[AP.01] Appearance (Skin Color) -- The Appearance (Skin Color) component of the APGAR score assesses the newborn's coloration to gauge oxygenation, scoring 0 for a completely blue or pale infant, 1 for a pink trunk with blue extremities, and 2 for an entirely pink body. This rapid visual check helps determine the infant's circulatory and respiratory adaptation in the first minutes after birth.
[AP.02] Pulse (Heart Rate) -- AP.02 records the newborn's pulse, measured as heart beats per minute, as one of the five components of the APGAR assessment. It is evaluated at one and five minutes after birth, with rates above 100 bpm considered normal and lower values indicating possible need for medical attention.
[AP.03] Grimace (Reflex Irritability) -- The Grimace component of the APGAR score evaluates the newborn's reflex response to stimulation, such as a gentle pinch or suction, indicating irritability. A score of 0 reflects no response, 1 denotes a weak grimace, and 2 represents a vigorous cry or pull-back, contributing to the overall assessment of the infant's condition at birth.
[AP.04] Activity (Muscle Tone) -- Activity (Muscle Tone) is the second component of the APGAR newborn assessment, evaluating the infant's muscular firmness and movement. Scores range from 0 for limp, flaccid tone to 2 for active motion with flexed extremities, contributing to the overall health rating at 1 and 5 minutes after birth.
[AP.05] Respiration (Breathing) -- In the APGAR newborn assessment, the respiration (breathing) component evaluates the infant's effort to breathe, assigning a score of 0 if there is no breathing, 1 for slow or irregular respiration, and 2 for a vigorous cry or regular breathing. This score is recorded at one- and five-minute intervals after delivery to help gauge the newborn's immediate health status.
[AP.06] Score 0 (Absent/Blue) -- In the APGAR newborn assessment, a score of 0 indicates the infant shows no response and appears limp and blue or pale, reflecting the lowest rating for that parameter. The code AP.06 records this absent/blue condition in the overall APGAR scoring system.
[AP.07] Score 1 (Intermediate) -- Code AP.07 denotes an intermediate APGAR newborn assessment, indicating a total score in the moderate range (typically 3-6). This categorization reflects that the infant shows some signs of distress yet maintains sufficient heart rate, respiration and muscle tone to require only limited medical support. It is used to monitor early post-birth adaptation and guide immediate care decisions.
[AP.08] Score 2 (Normal/Active) -- Score 2 denotes a newborn who is normal and active, with a heart rate above 100 bpm, regular breathing, active muscle tone, prompt response to stimulation, and pink colour without cyanosis. This level reflects a healthy infant requiring only routine care.
[AP.09] Total Score 7-10: Normal -- The APGAR total score of 7-10 denotes a newborn who is in good condition, with adequate breathing, heart rate, muscle tone, reflex response and skin color. These infants typically require no urgent medical intervention and are considered normal.
[AP.10] Total Score 4-6: Moderately Abnormal -- The APGAR Newborn Assessment Score of 4-6 indicates a moderately abnormal condition, suggesting that the infant requires close observation and possible intervention to support breathing, heart rate, and other vital functions. This range reflects moderate distress that may improve with basic resuscitative measures.
[AP.11] Total Score 0-3: Critically Low -- AP.11 denotes a total APGAR score of 0 to 3, indicating a critically low condition in a newborn. This range reflects severe depression requiring immediate medical intervention
