[BS] Bristol Stool Types -- The Bristol Stool Form Scale (code BS) classifies human feces into seven distinct types ranging from hard lumps to liquid, providing a simple visual aid for evaluating
[BS.01] Type 1: Separate Hard Lumps -- Type 1 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale describes separate hard lumps that resemble nuts and are difficult to pass. This form reflects very slow intestinal transit and is commonly linked to constipation.
[BS.02] Type 2: Lumpy Sausage -- Type 2 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale describes a lumpy sausage-shaped stool that retains its form but shows distinct cracks on the surface, indicating a firm but not hard consistency. It suggests slower transit through the colon than type 3, but not the constipation associated with the hardest types.
[BS.03] Type 3: Sausage with Cracks -- Type 3 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale describes a stool that looks like a sausage with surface cracks, indicating a fairly normal consistency and transit time. It is softer than Type 2 but firmer than Type 4, and is generally considered a healthy bowel habit.
[BS.04] Type 4: Smooth Soft Sausage (Ideal) -- Type 4 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale represents a smooth, soft sausage-shaped stool that retains its form without cracks or fragments. It is considered the ideal consistency, indicating normal colonic transit and healthy digestion.
[BS.05] Type 5: Soft Blobs -- BS.05 refers to Type 5 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale, describing stools that appear as soft, sausage-shaped pieces with a smooth surface that may break apart into softer blobs. This consistency is generally considered normal, indicating a moderate transit time through the colon and adequate fiber intake. It falls between the firmer Type 4 and the looser Type 6.
[BS.06] Type 6: Fluffy/Mushy Pieces -- Type 6 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale consists of fluffy or mushy pieces with ragged edges that may be passed easily. The consistency suggests a relatively rapid transit time through the colon, often associated with mild diarrhea or a diet low in fiber. It indicates a stool that is softer than the ideal form but not entirely liquid.
[BS.07] Type 7: Entirely Liquid -- BS.07 denotes Type 7 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale, representing stool that is completely liquid with no solid pieces, indicating rapid transit through the colon. This form is often associated with diarrhea and may suggest infection, dietary issues, or medication effects.
[BS.08] Constipation Indicator (1-2) -- BS.08 on the Bristol Stool Form Scale identifies the constipation indicator, encompassing stool types 1 and 2. Type 1 describes separate hard lumps that are difficult to pass, while type 2 is lumpy, sausage-shaped but with cracks on the surface; both suggest slow intestinal transit and are used clinically to gauge constipation severity.
[BS.09] Normal Range (3-5) -- The Bristol Stool Form Scale code BS.09 designates the normal range, covering stool types 3-5 which are smooth, sausage-shaped with or without surface cracks. These forms indicate typical consistency and transit time, reflecting healthy bowel function.
[BS.10] Diarrhea Indicator (6-7) -- The BS.10 entry designates the high-frequency end of the Bristol Stool Form Scale, indicating stool types 6 and 7 which are mushy, watery, or liquid. These forms are commonly associated with diarrhea and suggest rapid intestinal transit or an underlying gastrointestinal disturbance.
