[IS] IMO Ship Types -- IS is the code for the IMO Ship Types classification, which groups vessels into the International Maritime Organization's standard categories-such as cargo, tanker, passenger, and specialized ships-to support safety regulations, reporting requirements, and statistical analysis.
[IS.01] Oil Tanker -- IS.01 designates vessels built to carry crude oil or refined petroleum products in bulk, equipped with dedicated cargo tanks and safety features that reduce spill and fire hazards. The classification ensures compliance with IMO standards for cargo handling, stability, and pollution prevention on oil-carrying ships.
[IS.02] Chemical Tanker -- A chemical tanker is a cargo vessel constructed and equipped to carry bulk liquid chemicals, either as single pure substances or in segregated compartments for multiple products. It features specialized tanks, piping, and safety systems to prevent contamination, corrosion and fire hazards, complying with IMO regulations for hazardous cargo transport.
[IS.03] LNG Carrier -- IS.03 denotes an LNG carrier, a vessel built to transport liquefied natural gas in bulk. It features insulated, cryogenic cargo tanks-often spherical or membrane type-to keep the gas at about -162 °C and near atmospheric pressure. These ships are equipped with specialized pumping, boil-off gas handling, and safety systems for handling the highly flammable cargo.
[IS.04] LPG Carrier -- IS.04 designates a ship built to carry liquefied petroleum gas in bulk. These vessels are equipped with insulated cargo tanks, low-pressure or refrigerated systems, and meet IMO standards for safety and environmental protection during LPG transport.
[IS.05] Bulk Carrier -- IS.05 identifies a bulk carrier, a cargo vessel built to transport unpackaged bulk commodities such as grain, ore, coal or cement in large, open holds. These ships have minimal internal divisions, maximizing cargo capacity and enabling efficient loading and unloading.
[IS.06] General Cargo Ship -- The IMO ship type code IS.06 designates a General Cargo Ship, a vessel built to transport assorted break-bulk or packaged goods rather than standardized containers. These ships usually feature multiple cargo holds and on-board loading gear to handle diverse cargo types.
[IS.07] Container Ship -- IS.07 designates a container ship, a cargo vessel built to transport standardized intermodal containers in stacked loads on deck and in holds. The type emphasizes efficient handling of 20-foot and 40-foot containers using onboard equipment or shore-based gantry cranes, enabling rapid loading, unloading, and transshipment across global trade routes.
[IS.08] Ro-Ro Ship -- IMO ship type IS.08 denotes a roll-on/roll-off (Ro-Ro) vessel designed to transport wheeled cargo such as cars, trucks, and trailers, which are driven on and off the ship via built-in ramps or movable decks. The classification includes vehicle ferries, car carriers and other dedicated cargo ships.
[IS.09] Passenger Ship -- IS.09 identifies a passenger ship, a vessel whose main purpose is to carry people rather than cargo, encompassing cruise ships, ferries, ocean liners and similar craft. These ships are equipped with passenger accommodations, life-saving appliances and safety systems required for the transport of large numbers of individuals.
[IS.10] Cruise Ship -- IS.10 designates a cruise ship, a passenger vessel built primarily for leisure voyages with extensive accommodation, dining, entertainment and recreational facilities. It is intended to operate on scheduled or chartered cruises, offering amenities such as cabins, restaurants, theaters and pools, and is subject to IMO safety and environmental regulations specific to large passenger ships.
[IS.11] Fishing Vessel -- IS.11 in the IMO Ship Type Classification designates fishing vessels, covering ships whose primary purpose is the catching, processing or transport of fish and other marine life. The category includes trawlers, seiners, longliners, and any other craft equipped for commercial or artisanal fishing operations. It excludes vessels used solely for research, recreation or cargo transport.
[IS.12] Offshore Supply Vessel -- IS.12 identifies an offshore supply vessel, a ship built to transport equipment, supplies and personnel to offshore installations such as oil and gas platforms. These vessels feature large deck areas, cargo handling gear and often dynamic positioning systems to support safe and efficient offshore operations.
[IS.13] Tug -- IS.13 identifies a tug in the IMO Ship Type Classification. Tugs are small, high-power vessels built to assist larger ships with manoeuvring, towing, and berthing, featuring robust hulls and strong propulsion systems for delivering high thrust in confined waters.
[IS.14] Dredger -- A dredger is a specialized vessel built to excavate, lift, and transport sediment, sand, or debris from the seabed or riverbed, often to maintain navigation channels, ports, or to reclaim land. These ships are equipped with suction pumps, buckets, or mechanical arms and operate in coastal, harbor, or offshore environments. Their design prioritises stability, robust power, and equipment for handling large volumes of material.
[IS.15] Naval Vessel -- IS.15 identifies naval vessels, which are warships owned or operated by a nation's navy for defense, combat, and related missions. These ships are not used for commercial transport and are typically armed and equipped for military operations, including patrol, escort, and amphibious support.
