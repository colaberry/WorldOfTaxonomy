[IMO] IMO Vessel Types -- The IMO Vessel Type Codes are a standardized set of identifiers defined by the International Maritime Organization to classify ships by their primary function, such as bulk carrier, container ship, tanker or passenger vessel. These codes facilitate uniform reporting, safety regulations and statistical analysis across the global shipping industry. The entry "IMO" denotes the overall classification system used by the IMO for these vessel types.

[IMO.02] Container Ship -- IMO.02 designates container ships, vessels built to carry cargo in standardized intermodal containers. These ships feature large, open decks and cell guides that allow containers to be stacked efficiently, making them a core element of worldwide trade.
[IMO.03] General Cargo Ship -- IMO.03 designates a General Cargo Ship, a vessel built to transport assorted packaged goods in bulk or break-bulk form across multiple holds, without the specialized equipment of tankers, container ships, or refrigerated carriers. These ships handle a wide range of cargo types, from minerals to machinery, and are identified in the IMO system for safety, regulation, and statistical purposes.
[IMO.04] Oil Tanker -- IMO.04 designates an Oil Tanker, a merchant ship built to carry crude oil, refined petroleum products, or other liquid hydrocarbons in bulk. These vessels are subject to specific safety, construction, and environmental regulations under the International Maritime Organization to mitigate spill risks and protect marine ecosystems.

[IMO.06] LNG Carrier -- IMO.06 designates LNG carriers, vessels built to transport liquefied natural gas in insulated, cryogenic tanks at about -162 °C. These ships are equipped with specialized containment systems and may feature dual-fuel engines to reduce emissions while moving the cargo between production facilities and import terminals.
[IMO.07] LPG Carrier -- IMO.07 designates a liquefied petroleum gas carrier, a specialized tanker built to transport LPG in bulk under pressure or low temperature. These vessels are equipped with Hermetic tanks or cylinder bundles to safely contain the gas during voyages.
[IMO.08] Ro-Ro Cargo Ship -- IMO.08 identifies a roll-on/roll-off cargo ship, a vessel built with internal ramps that allow wheeled cargo such as cars, trucks and trailers to be driven on and off the vessel efficiently, facilitating rapid loading and unloading of goods.
[IMO.09] Passenger Ship -- IMO.09 designates a passenger ship, a vessel built principally to transport people rather than cargo. It includes ferries, cruise liners and other vessels whose primary function is carrying passengers, often with accommodations and safety features specific to human transport.
[IMO.10] Cruise Ship -- IMO.10 designates a cruise ship, a passenger vessel built primarily to provide leisure voyages with accommodation, dining, entertainment and amenities. Cruise ships operate on scheduled itineraries and are classified by the International Maritime Organization for safety and regulatory purposes.
[IMO.11] Fishing Vessel -- IMO.11 identifies vessels whose principal activity is catching fish or other aquatic resources, encompassing a range of craft from small boats to large trawlers and purse seiners. In the IMO ship-type coding system, these are classified as fishing vessels for regulatory, safety and statistical purposes.

The code groups all commercial and work‐craft tugs under a single vessel type.

[IMO.15] Naval Vessel -- IMO.15 denotes a naval vessel, which is a warship or other ship owned and operated by a nation's armed forces. These vessels are used for military purposes such as defense, patrol, or combat and are not classified as commercial or civilian ships. The code helps distinguish them in maritime databases and reporting.
[IMO.16] Yacht -- IMO.16 denotes a yacht, a privately owned recreational vessel typically under 24 meters in length, used for pleasure cruising, racing or day sailing rather than commercial transport, and equipped for comfort and leisure activities on the water.
