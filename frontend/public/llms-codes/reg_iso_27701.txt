[5_1] 5.1 General -- Clause 5.1 outlines the general requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving a Privacy Information Management System, specifying how it must align with the organization's overall information security management system, define its scope, and address relevant privacy obligations and stakeholder expectations.
[5_2] 5.2 Context of the organization -- In ISO/IEC 27701:2019, clause 5.2 directs organizations to identify internal and external issues that influence their privacy information management system, determine the relevant interested parties, and define the scope of the PIMS based on these factors.
[5_3] 5.3 Leadership -- ISO/IEC 27701 5.3 Leadership requires top management to demonstrate commitment to the privacy information management system, set a privacy policy aligned with organizational objectives, allocate resources, and establish clear responsibilities. It also mandates that leadership ensures integration of privacy controls into the overall management processes and promotes a culture of continual improvement.
[5_4] 5.4 Planning -- Entry 5_4 in ISO/IEC 27701:2019 outlines the planning phase for a Privacy Information Management System, requiring organizations to define privacy objectives, assess risks to personal data, allocate resources, and integrate privacy controls with the existing ISO/IEC 27001 ISMS to ensure systematic implementation and continual improvement.
[5_5] 5.5 Support -- Section 5.5 of ISO/IEC 27701:2019 outlines the support requirements for a privacy information management system, specifying the need for adequate resources, competent personnel, and appropriate awareness programs. It also details internal and external communication processes and mandates the creation, control, and retention of documented information relevant to privacy controls. This clause ensures that organizations have the necessary foundation to implement and maintain effective privacy safeguards.
[5_6] 5.6 Operation -- Section 5.6 outlines the requirements for establishing, implementing and controlling the processes needed to operate the Privacy Information Management System, ensuring that privacy controls are applied consistently across the organization. It mandates ongoing monitoring, measurement and improvement of privacy-related activities to meet the objectives of the PIMS.
[5_7] 5.7 Performance evaluation -- Section 5.7 requires organizations to regularly monitor and assess the effectiveness of their privacy information management system, using appropriate metrics, internal audits and management reviews to determine performance, identify improvements and ensure compliance with privacy objectives.
[5_8] 5.8 Improvement -- Clause 5.8 requires the organization to continually improve its privacy information management system by using results from monitoring, audits, incidents and corrective actions to identify and implement enhancements to privacy controls and processes. It mandates documented procedures for assessing performance, determining improvement opportunities and applying changes that increase the effectiveness of the PIMS.
[7_1] 7.1 General -- The 7.1 General clause sets out the overall framework for the privacy information management system, defining its purpose, scope and applicability, and establishing the fundamental concepts and terminology that underpin the extension of ISO/IEC 27001 to address privacy protection.
[7_2] 7.2 Conditions for collection and processing -- Entry 7.2 in ISO/IEC 27701:2019 specifies the conditions that must be met before personal data can be collected or processed, requiring that activities be lawful, fair and transparent, limited to defined purposes, and based on appropriate consent or other legal grounds, while also ensuring data minimisation and the protection of data subjects' rights.
[7_3] 7.3 Obligations to PII principals -- Section 7.3 outlines the obligations an organization has toward individuals whose personal data it processes, requiring identification, documentation and fulfillment of legal, contractual and ethical responsibilities such as transparency, data-subject rights and overall accountability.
[7_4] 7.4 Privacy by design and privacy by default -- Entry 7.4 requires that organizations embed privacy principles into the design of processes, systems and products from the outset and ensure that, by default, only personal data necessary for a specific purpose is collected, processed and retained, minimizing exposure and supporting compliance.
[7_5] 7.5 PII sharing, transfer and disclosure -- Entry 7_5 in ISO/IEC 27701:2019 defines the requirements for sharing, transferring and disclosing personal data, requiring organizations to identify a lawful basis, obtain consent where needed, and document the purpose, recipients and security measures. It also mandates contractual or other safeguards for third-party processing and regular monitoring of compliance.
[8_1] 8.1 General -- Section 8.1 gives a concise overview of the ISO/IEC 27701 privacy extension, defining its purpose, scope and how it integrates with the core ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management System, and introducing the key concepts and objectives that underpin the privacy-specific controls.
[8_2] 8.2 Conditions for collection and processing -- Section 8.2 of ISO/IEC 27701:2019 outlines the criteria an organization must satisfy before collecting or processing personal data, requiring a lawful basis, clear purpose, data minimisation and transparency to data subjects. It also mandates that processing be necessary, proportionate and documented in privacy policies.
[8_3] 8.3 Obligations to PII principals -- Clause 8.3 in ISO/IEC 27701:2019 outlines the organization's responsibilities toward individuals whose personal data is processed, requiring transparent communication about data handling, provision of access and correction mechanisms, and respect for the rights to object, restrict, or erase personal information as defined by applicable privacy regulations.
[8_4] 8.4 Privacy by design and privacy by default -- Clause 8.4 requires organisations to embed privacy considerations into the design and architecture of information systems and business processes, ensuring that appropriate technical and organisational safeguards are built in from the outset. It also mandates that default settings limit the collection, use, retention and disclosure of personal data to the minimum necessary for the intended purpose, thereby operationalising the principles of privacy-by-design and privacy-by-default.
[8_5] 8.5 PII sharing, transfer and disclosure -- ISO/IEC 27701:2019 Clause 8.5 defines requirements for controlling the sharing, transfer and disclosure of personally identifiable information (PII) within an organization's privacy information management system. It mandates that PII be released only for legitimate purposes, with documented consent, appropriate contractual safeguards and compliance with applicable legal and regulatory obligations. The clause also outlines procedures for evaluating third-party arrangements, applying risk-based controls and maintaining records of all PII exchanges.
[a1] A.7 Additional ISO 27002 guidance for PII controllers -- ISO/IEC 27701:2019 extends ISO/IEC 27001 with privacy-specific requirements, and entry a1 supplies supplementary guidance from ISO 27002 for organisations that act as personal data controllers, detailing how to apply existing security controls to protect personally identifiable information.
[a2] A.8 Additional conditions for processing -- A.8 sets out extra requirements that must accompany any processing activity under the privacy-information management system. It requires organizations to identify and document lawful bases, obtain valid consent where needed, and ensure processing respects data-subject rights and any contractual or regulatory constraints. Compliance with these conditions supports the overall objectives of ISO/IEC 27701.

It maps privacy requirements to the underlying ISO 27001 controls, providing a structured set of objectives for protecting personal data throughout its lifecycle.
