[UC] UNEP Chemical Categories -- UC denotes chemicals that are not assigned to any specific UNEP hazard category because their properties are unknown, data are lacking, or they fall outside the defined criteria. The code is used as a catch-all placeholder in the UNEP Chemical Classification system.
[UC.01] Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs) -- UC.01 identifies Persistent Organic Pollutants, a group of synthetic chemicals that resist environmental degradation, accumulate in the food chain, and pose risks to human health and ecosystems. These substances include PCBs, DDT and dioxins, and are subject to international control measures under the Stockholm Convention.
[UC.02] Heavy Metals -- UC.02 Heavy Metals refers to a UNEP category that groups chemical substances containing metallic elements with high atomic weight and density, such as lead, mercury, cadmium and arsenic, which are known for their persistence, bioaccumulation potential and toxic effects on human health and the environment.
[UC.03] Ozone-Depleting Substances -- UC.03 designates chemicals that contribute to stratospheric ozone depletion, such as chlorofluorocarbons, halons, carbon tetrachloride and related compounds. These substances are regulated internationally under the Montreal Protocol because their release accelerates ozone loss, increasing ultraviolet radiation exposure and associated health and environmental risks.
[UC.04] Greenhouse Gases -- UC.04 classifies greenhouse gases, substances that trap infrared radiation and drive global warming, such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and various fluorinated compounds, and is used to monitor emissions, assess climate impact, and guide mitigation policies.
[UC.05] Endocrine Disruptors -- UC.05 identifies chemicals that interfere with the normal functioning of hormonal systems in humans or wildlife. These endocrine disruptors can alter synthesis, release, transport, binding, action, or elimination of hormones, leading to adverse developmental, reproductive, neurological, or immune effects.
[UC.06] PFAS (Forever Chemicals) -- PFAS, known as "forever chemicals," are a group of synthetic fluorinated compounds that resist degradation and accumulate in the environment and living organisms. UNEP classifies them as persistent, bio-accumulative, and toxic substances, highlighting their widespread use in firefighting foams, textiles, and food packaging and the associated risks of groundwater contamination, wildlife harm, and human health impacts.
[UC.07] Pesticides -- UC.07 in the UNEP Chemical Classification Categories groups substances employed to manage or eliminate pests such as insects, weeds, fungi, rodents and other organisms that threaten crops, public health or ecosystems. These chemicals include synthetic agro-chemicals and natural biocides, and they are subject to regulation because of their potential environmental persistence and toxicity to non-target species.
[UC.08] Industrial Chemicals -- UC.08 in the UNEP Chemical Classification Categories designates industrial chemicals, which are substances manufactured or used in large-scale processes such as manufacturing, construction, and energy production. The category groups compounds that are typically produced in bulk and may pose environmental or health risks, requiring specific regulatory oversight and monitoring.
[UC.09] Pharmaceutical Residues -- UC.09 - Pharmaceutical residues covers active pharmaceutical ingredients, their metabolites and degradation products that enter the environment through manufacturing discharges, human and veterinary use, improper disposal or excretion, and can be detected in water, soil and biota, posing potential risks to ecosystems and human health.
[UC.10] Microplastics -- UC.10 designates microplastics, synthetic polymer particles smaller than five millimetres that result from the breakdown of larger debris or are manufactured for use in products such as cosmetics and abrasives. These fragments persist in water, soil and air, posing ecological risks by being ingested by organisms and acting as vectors for pollutants. The category is used by UNEP to track sources, pathways and impacts of these pervasive contaminants.
[UC.11] Hazardous Waste Chemicals -- UC.11 identifies chemicals classified as hazardous waste under UNEP guidelines, indicating substances that pose significant toxicity, persistence, or ecological risk and therefore require strict handling, treatment, and disposal controls to protect human health and the environment.
[UC.12] Nanomaterials -- The UNEP Chemical Classification Category UC.12 groups nanomaterials-engineered particles with at least one dimension between 1 nm and 100 nm-and related substances that exhibit size-dependent properties distinct from their bulk counterparts. It covers their production, use, environmental fate, and potential health and ecological impacts, supporting risk assessment and regulatory oversight.

It serves as a reference point for national and regional chemical safety strategies.
