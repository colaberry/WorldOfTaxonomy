[art_5] Art 5 - Obligations (self-executing) -- Article 5 of the EU Digital Markets Act sets out the core obligations that designated gatekeepers must comply with automatically, without the need for further legislation. It requires them to ensure fair access for business users, prohibit discriminatory treatment, and guarantee interoperability and data portability. The article is self-executing, meaning the duties apply directly once a gatekeeper is identified.
[art_6] Art 6 - Obligations susceptible of being further specified -- Article 6 of the EU Digital Markets Act provides that the obligations imposed on designated core platform services can be further detailed through delegated acts, enabling the Commission to specify requirements such as data access, interoperability, fairness and transparency as needed to address market dynamics.
[art_7] Art 7 - Obligations for messaging interoperability -- Article 7 of the EU Digital Markets Act requires designated core platform services that provide messaging to ensure technical interoperability with competing services, allowing users to exchange messages across platforms. The rule mandates that the platform supplies open APIs, documentation and reasonable terms so that alternative providers can build compatible messaging features, while safeguarding security, privacy and the integrity of the service.
[compliance_func] Compliance Function (Art 28) -- The compliance function mandated by Article 28 of the EU Digital Markets Act requires designated gatekeepers to set up an internal unit that monitors, assesses and ensures adherence to the DMA's obligations, reports breaches to the Commission and coordinates remedial actions. This unit must be independent, adequately resourced and have direct access to senior management.
[cps] Core Platform Services (Art 2) -- The Digital Markets Act defines Core Platform Services as the essential online services that gatekeepers must provide under Article 2, including online intermediation platforms, search engines, social networking, messaging, operating systems, cloud computing, and online advertising services. These services are subject to the Act's obligations and enforcement mechanisms to ensure fair competition and user protection.
[data_portability] Data portability (Art 6(9)) -- Under the Digital Markets Act, Article 6(9) obliges designated platforms to provide users with a right of data portability, allowing personal data to be supplied in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format. This enables individuals to transfer their data to another service, promoting competition and consumer choice.
[designation] Gatekeeper Designation Criteria (Art 3) -- The Digital Markets Act sets out the conditions under which a platform is formally designated as a "gatekeeper." Article 3 lists quantitative thresholds such as an annual turnover of at least €6.5 billion in the EU or a market value of €65 billion, together with qualitative criteria including a core platform service and a significant, entrenched impact on the internal market.
[enforcement] Enforcement and Remedies -- The enforcement entry outlines how the EU Digital Markets Act will be applied, detailing investigative powers, penalties and corrective actions. It specifies the authority of national regulators to conduct market investigations, impose fines and require structural or behavioral remedies to restore competition. This framework ensures that gatekeepers comply with the Act's obligations and that breaches are promptly addressed.
[fair_access] Fair access to app stores (Art 6(12)) -- The Digital Markets Act obliges designated gatekeepers to provide business users with non-discriminatory, transparent conditions for accessing their app stores, prohibiting unjustified blocking or limitation of apps. This ensures that developers can offer their software on the platform on equal terms, fostering competition and consumer choice.
[fines] Fines up to 10% of global turnover (Art 30) -- The Digital Markets Act authorises regulators to levy fines of up to ten percent of a company's worldwide annual turnover for breaches of its competition and gatekeeper obligations, as set out in Article 30. The penalty is intended to ensure compliance with rules on fairness, transparency and interoperability.
[investigation] Market Investigation (Art 16-17) -- The Digital Markets Act (EU) 2022/1925 authorizes the European Commission to conduct market investigations under Articles 16 and 17 to assess whether gatekeepers are harming competition. Such investigations examine the structural and functional characteristics of core platform services, identify unfair practices and can lead to remedial measures.
[no_combine] Prohibition on combining personal data across services (Art 5(2)) -- The Digital Markets Act bars gatekeepers from merging personal data obtained through separate core platform services, requiring that information collected in one service cannot be combined with data from another to build unified user profiles. This restriction, set out in Article 5(2), aims to prevent cross-service tracking and protect user privacy by keeping data silos distinct.
[non_compliance] Non-Compliance Proceedings (Art 29) -- The entry records non-compliance proceedings defined in Article 29 of the EU Digital Markets Act (Regulation 2022/1925). It refers to investigations and enforcement actions taken by national or EU authorities when a designated gatekeeper fails to meet the Act's obligations, potentially resulting in fines, corrective orders or other sanctions.

[quantitative] Quantitative Thresholds (Art 3(2)) -- The entry outlines the numeric criteria in Article 3(2) that determine when a platform is classified as a gatekeeper under the EU Digital Markets Act. Companies must exceed a yearly turnover of €6.5 billion in the EU or have at least 45 million active users, and must operate a core platform service with at least 10 million users. These thresholds trigger the obligations and supervision set out in the DMA.

It defines the responsibilities and obligations that apply once a platform is classified as a gatekeeper under the Act.
