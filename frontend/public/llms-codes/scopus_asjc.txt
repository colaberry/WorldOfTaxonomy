[ASJC] ASJC Subject Areas -- The ASJC (All Science Journal Classification) is Scopus's system for grouping journals into 27 broad subject areas and over 300 more detailed fields, each identified by a numeric code. The entry labeled ASJC refers to the entire classification framework, which enables users to filter, search and analyze scholarly output by discipline across the database.

[ASJC.11] Agricultural and Biological Sciences -- ASJC.11 groups journals focused on agriculture, crop and animal science, horticulture, forestry, food science, and related biological disciplines such as ecology, genetics, and microbiology, reflecting research that advances the production, management, and sustainability of agricultural systems and the biological sciences underpinning them.
[ASJC.12] Arts and Humanities -- ASJC.12 - Arts and Humanities covers disciplines that study human culture, expression, and historical legacy, including literature, philosophy, visual and performing arts, languages, archaeology, and cultural heritage. It groups journals and research outputs that focus on creative, interpretive, and critical analysis of artistic and humanistic subjects.
[ASJC.13] Biochemistry, Genetics and Molecular Biology -- ASJC.13 groups scholarly output that investigates the chemical and molecular foundations of living systems, encompassing biochemistry, genetics and molecular biology. It includes studies of cellular pathways, enzyme mechanisms, DNA and RNA structure and function, genomics, proteomics and related experimental techniques.
[ASJC.14] Business, Management and Accounting -- ASJC.14 denotes the Business, Management and Accounting subject area in the Scopus classification, covering scholarly work on business studies, management theory and practice, accounting principles, finance, entrepreneurship, organizational behavior, strategy and related disciplines that examine the operation and governance of enterprises.
[ASJC.15] Chemical Engineering -- ASJC.15 is the Scopus All Science Journal Classification code for Chemical Engineering, encompassing research on the design, operation, and optimization of chemical processes. It includes topics such as transport phenomena, reaction engineering, process control, and emerging areas like sustainable and bio-process engineering.
[ASJC.16] Chemistry -- ASJC.16 is the Chemistry category in the Scopus All Science Journal Classification, encompassing research on the composition, properties, and reactions of matter as well as analytical, theoretical, and applied aspects of organic, inorganic, physical and materials chemistry. It is used to group journals and articles for indexing and retrieval.
[ASJC.17] Computer Science -- ASJC.17 identifies the Computer Science subject area in the Scopus All Science Journal Classification system. It covers research on algorithms, software engineering, artificial intelligence, data structures, computational theory and related practical applications. Journals assigned this code publish work that advances both the theoretical foundations and real-world uses of computing.
[ASJC.18] Decision Sciences -- ASJC 18 Decision Sciences covers the study and application of quantitative and analytical methods for improving decision-making in organizations. It includes topics such as operations research, management science, optimization, simulation, and analytical modeling of complex systems used to support strategic, tactical, and operational choices.
[ASJC.19] Earth and Planetary Sciences -- ASJC 19 covers Earth and Planetary Sciences, encompassing research on the structure, composition, and processes of Earth and other planetary bodies. It includes geology, geophysics, atmospheric science, oceanography, planetary exploration, and related interdisciplinary studies that investigate the physical and chemical properties of planets and their environments. This category is used to classify scholarly articles dealing with natural Earth systems and comparative planetary research.
[ASJC.20] Economics, Econometrics and Finance -- ASJC 20 groups research focused on the theory and practice of economics, econometric methods, and financial analysis. It includes studies on macro- and micro-economic policy, financial markets, investment, and quantitative modeling of economic phenomena. Journals classified under this code publish work relevant to scholars, policymakers, and practitioners in these disciplines.
[ASJC.21] Energy -- ASJC 21 denotes the Energy subject area in Scopus, covering research on the production, conversion, storage and utilization of energy, including both renewable and non-renewable sources, as well as related policies, technologies and engineering developments. It is used to index journals and articles that focus on all aspects of energy science and practice.
[ASJC.22] Engineering -- ASJC.22 designates Engineering, encompassing core branches such as civil, mechanical, electrical, chemical and aerospace engineering along with interdisciplinary areas like materials, environmental and biomedical engineering. Publications classified under this code address the design, analysis, construction, and operation of systems and processes in both industrial and research contexts.
[ASJC.23] Environmental Science -- ASJC.23 denotes the Environmental Science field in Scopus' All Subject Classification. It encompasses research on natural processes, human impacts and sustainable management of ecosystems, covering topics such as climate change, pollution, biodiversity loss and resource conservation.
[ASJC.24] Immunology and Microbiology -- ASJC.24 designates the Scopus subject area "Immunology and Microbiology," encompassing research on the immune system, host-pathogen interactions, vaccine development, and the biology of bacteria, viruses, fungi and parasites, as well as related molecular and cellular mechanisms. This category groups journals and articles that investigate both fundamental and applied aspects of immune responses and microbial life.
[ASJC.25] Materials Science -- ASJC.25 is the Materials Science category in Scopus's All Science Journal Classification system. It groups journals covering the study of the properties, processing and applications of materials, including metals, ceramics, polymers and composites. The classification helps researchers locate relevant literature across interdisciplinary material-related fields.
[ASJC.26] Mathematics -- ASJC 26 designates the Scopus subject area for Mathematics, encompassing research in pure and applied mathematics, including theoretical, computational, statistical, and interdisciplinary work that employs mathematical methods. This classification groups journals and articles that advance the development and application of mathematical concepts across scientific fields.
[ASJC.27] Medicine -- ASJC.27 denotes the Medicine category in Scopus's All Science Journal Classification system, covering clinical and biomedical research, health care, pharmacology, epidemiology, and related disciplines. It is used to group journals and articles for indexing, discovery, and analytical reporting.
[ASJC.28] Neuroscience -- ASJC 28 groups journals that publish research on the structure and function of the nervous system, spanning molecular, cellular, behavioral and clinical neuroscience. It covers neural development, neurophysiology, neuropharmacology, cognitive and systems neuroscience, as well as the mechanisms of neurological disorders.
[ASJC.29] Nursing -- ASJC 29 designates Nursing, encompassing research on the theory, practice, education, management and policy of nursing and related health-care disciplines. It includes studies on patient care, clinical techniques, nursing informatics, workforce issues, professional development, and interdisciplinary collaboration within health-service settings.
[ASJC.30] Pharmacology, Toxicology and Pharmaceutics -- ASJC.30 groups research on the study of drug actions, adverse effects and the development, formulation and delivery of medicinal products. It includes pharmacodynamics, pharmacokinetics, toxicology, clinical trials, drug design and pharmaceutical technology.
[ASJC.31] Physics and Astronomy -- ASJC.31 is the Scopus subject category for Physics and Astronomy, encompassing research on fundamental physical principles, experimental and theoretical studies, and observational and theoretical astronomy. It includes sub-fields such as astrophysics, cosmology, particle physics, condensed matter, and related interdisciplinary work.
[ASJC.32] Psychology -- ASJC.32 denotes the field of Psychology, encompassing the scientific study of mental processes, behavior, cognition, emotion, and social interaction. The category includes sub-disciplines such as clinical, developmental, cognitive, and social psychology and related applied research areas.
[ASJC.33] Social Sciences -- The ASJC.33 code in the Scopus All Science Journal Classification denotes the broad "Social Sciences" subject area, encompassing journals that publish research in disciplines such as sociology, political science, economics, anthropology, psychology, and related fields that study human society and social behavior.
[ASJC.34] Veterinary -- ASJC.34 Veterinary covers research on animal health, disease prevention, clinical veterinary practice, animal physiology and related biomedical studies. It is the Scopus subject tag applied to publications dealing with veterinary medicine and animal science.
[ASJC.35] Dentistry -- ASJC.35 groups publications that focus on dental science and oral health, including clinical dentistry, prosthodontics, periodontics, oral surgery, and related research on dental materials, epidemiology, and public health aspects of the mouth and teeth.
[ASJC.36] Health Professions -- ASJC.36 Health Professions groups research on the training, practice, and management of allied health workers such as nurses, physiotherapists, occupational therapists, dietitians, pharmacists and other clinical support staff, focusing on education, service delivery, policy and interdisciplinary collaboration. It covers both clinical outcomes and professional development across hospital and community settings.
