[EQF] EQF Levels -- The European Qualifications Framework (EQF) is a reference system that links national qualifications across Europe by defining eight levels based on learning outcomes. EQF Levels provide a common language for comparing and recognizing qualifications, supporting mobility, lifelong learning and the transparency of skills and knowledge.
[EQF.1] Level 1 - Basic general knowledge -- EQF Level 1 signifies basic general knowledge and skills that enable an individual to perform simple tasks, usually acquired through primary education or informal learning. It reflects essential literacy, numeracy and the ability to follow straightforward instructions.
[EQF.2] Level 2 - Basic factual knowledge -- EQF Level 2 denotes basic factual knowledge and understanding, usually attained after lower secondary education. Holders can apply simple concepts, follow routine instructions and perform elementary tasks with limited autonomy, often under supervision or guidance.
[EQF.3] Level 3 - Knowledge of facts and principles -- EQF Level 3 denotes a learner who has reliable knowledge of facts, principles and concepts within a specific field, and can apply this understanding to routine tasks and problem-solving situations. The level reflects a functional competence that bridges basic knowledge and more advanced, specialized expertise.
[EQF.4] Level 4 - Factual and theoretical -- Level 4 on the European Qualifications Framework denotes knowledge of facts, principles and concepts across a broad range of subjects, with the ability to apply this theoretical understanding in routine and practical contexts. Holders can exercise autonomy in carrying out defined tasks, analyse situations, solve problems and assume responsibility for results within a specific occupational or technical area.
[EQF.5] Level 5 - Comprehensive, specialized -- EQF Level 5 denotes comprehensive, specialised knowledge and skills, typically linked to short-cycle higher education or advanced vocational training. Holders can manage complex tasks, solve problems and exercise considerable autonomy within their specialised field.
[EQF.6] Level 6 - Advanced knowledge (Bachelor) -- EQF Level 6 denotes advanced knowledge and expertise, typically acquired through a bachelor-degree program. Holders can apply complex theoretical concepts, exercise critical judgment, and develop innovative solutions within a professional or academic context.
[EQF.7] Level 7 - Highly specialised (Master) -- EQF Level 7 represents a highly specialised learning outcome comparable to a master's degree, requiring advanced knowledge of a field of work or study and the ability to apply that knowledge to complex, unpredictable problems. Holders demonstrate critical understanding, autonomy in decision-making and the capacity to develop new concepts or solutions in a professional context. This level reflects the competence to lead, innovate and conduct research at a sophisticated level.
[EQF.8] Level 8 - Frontier knowledge (Doctoral) -- Level 8 of the European Qualifications Framework denotes the highest academic level, representing frontier knowledge acquired through doctoral studies. Holders demonstrate the ability to conduct original research, develop new theories or innovations, and critically evaluate complex problems, contributing independently to the advancement of their field.
[EQF.C] Competence Descriptor -- EQF.C is a competence descriptor within the European Qualifications Framework that defines the set of knowledge, skills and autonomy expected for a particular qualification level. It provides a concise statement of what a learner should be able to do, understand and apply in professional or academic contexts, supporting comparability across European education and training systems.
[EQF.K] Knowledge Descriptor -- EQF.K is the Knowledge Descriptor within the European Qualifications Framework, specifying the factual, theoretical and procedural knowledge a learner must have at each qualification level. It details the depth and breadth of understanding required, from basic concepts at lower levels to complex, specialised knowledge at higher levels.
[EQF.S] Skills Descriptor -- In the European Qualifications Framework a Skills Descriptor (code EQF.S) specifies the concrete abilities, competencies and performance outcomes that a learner must demonstrate at a particular qualification level. It details how knowledge is applied, the capacity to solve problems and carry out tasks autonomously, supporting transparency and comparability across European education and training systems.
[EQF.V] Validation and Recognition -- EQF.V designates the set of procedures for validating and recognizing qualifications and learning outcomes across EU member states, allowing informal or non-formal learning to be formally acknowledged and ensuring that credentials are comparable throughout the European Qualifications Framework.
