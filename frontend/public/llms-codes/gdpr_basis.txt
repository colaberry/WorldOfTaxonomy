[GB] GDPR Legal Bases -- GB denotes the GDPR Legal Bases category, outlining the six lawful grounds for processing personal data-consent, contract performance, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, and legitimate interests. It guides organizations in selecting the appropriate basis for each data-processing activity.
[GB.01] Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) -- GB.01 denotes the GDPR lawful basis of consent under Article 6(1)(a). It applies when a data subject has given a clear, informed and freely given agreement to the specific processing of their personal data.
[GB.02] Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) -- GB.02 - Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) is the GDPR lawful basis that permits processing of personal data when it is necessary for the performance of a contract to which the data subject is party, or to take steps at the data subject's request prior to entering into such a contract. It ensures that processing is tied directly to contractual obligations and rights.
[GB.03] Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)) -- GB.03 refers to the GDPR lawful basis for processing personal data when the controller must comply with a legal obligation. Under Article 6(1)(c) processing is permissible if required by EU or member-state law, such as tax, employment or health- and safety regulations. This basis does not require consent but must be demonstrably necessary to meet the obligation.
[GB.04] Vital Interests (Art. 6(1)(d)) -- Under GDPR, processing is allowed when it is necessary to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another person, such as in life-saving medical emergencies where consent cannot be obtained. This lawful basis applies when the individual is unable to give consent and the action is essential to preserve health or life.
[GB.05] Public Task (Art. 6(1)(e)) -- GB.05 denotes the GDPR lawful basis "Public task" under Article 6(1)(e), permitting personal data processing when it is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority. This basis is commonly applied by governmental and public-sector bodies to fulfill duties such as public health, education, safety or regulatory functions.

[GB.07] Special Category: Explicit Consent -- GB.07 identifies the GDPR lawful basis for handling special-category personal data when explicit consent is obtained. It requires a clear, specific, informed, and unambiguous statement from the data subject authorising the particular processing activity.
[GB.08] Special Category: Employment -- GB.08 outlines the GDPR lawful basis for handling special-category data in an employment context, allowing processing of sensitive information such as health, trade-union membership or biometric data when it is necessary to fulfil employment contracts, comply with labour law obligations, or protect vital interests of the employee or others. The entry emphasizes that such processing must be proportionate, documented, and accompanied by appropriate safeguards to protect the data subject's rights.
[GB.09] Special Category: Vital Interests -- GB.09 refers to the GDPR provision that allows processing of special categories of personal data when it is necessary to protect someone's vital interests, such as life-saving medical interventions, where the data subject is incapable of giving consent.
[GB.10] Special Category: Public Interest -- GB.10 defines the GDPR lawful basis that allows processing of special-category data when it is essential for a substantial public interest recognised in Union or Member State law, provided appropriate safeguards protect the data subject's rights.
[GB.11] Special Category: Legal Claims -- Under GDPR, processing special-category data is allowed when it is necessary for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims, including obtaining, asserting or defending a court decision. This lawful basis lets organisations handle sensitive information such as health or biometric data in the context of litigation, arbitration or other legal proceedings.
[GB.12] Special Category: Substantial Public Interest -- GB.12 designates the GDPR lawful basis that allows processing of special-category personal data when it is necessary for reasons of substantial public interest, such as public health or safety, provided appropriate safeguards are applied.
[GB.13] Special Category: Health/Social Care -- GB.13 outlines the GDPR lawful bases that permit processing of special-category data concerning health or social-care matters, such as explicit consent, vital interests, substantial public interest, or legal obligations, ensuring that such sensitive information is handled with heightened protection and justification.
