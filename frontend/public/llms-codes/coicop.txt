[CP01] Food and non-alcoholic beverages -- The code CP01 categorises household spending on food and non-alcoholic beverages within the Classification of Individual Consumption According to Purpose, 2018 revision. It includes all purchases of edible items and drinks that contain no alcohol, covering groceries, dining-out meals and related consumables for personal use.
[CP011] Food -- CP011 covers household expenditures on food, including groceries, beverages, and meals purchased for home consumption as well as food and drink bought at restaurants, cafés, and take-away outlets. It reflects the total monetary outlay by individuals and families for items intended for personal nutrition and enjoyment. This category is used to track consumer spending patterns on edible goods in national accounts.
[CP012] Non-alcoholic beverages -- CP012 includes all non-alcoholic drinks intended for personal consumption, such as bottled and mineral water, soft drinks, fruit juices, tea, coffee and similar ready-to-drink or concentrate beverages, classified under individual consumption for personal use.
[CP02] Alcoholic beverages, tobacco and narcotics -- CP02 records household spending on alcoholic beverages, tobacco products and narcotic drugs for personal use. It includes purchases such as beer, wine, spirits, cigarettes, cigars and any legal or illicit narcotics consumed by individuals. This category measures the share of consumption budgets devoted to these substances.
[CP021] Alcoholic beverages -- CP021 - Alcoholic beverages covers personal consumption of drinks containing ethanol such as beer, wine, spirits, liqueurs and other fermented or distilled products, whether purchased for home use or consumed on-the-spot in establishments. It excludes non-alcoholic drinks and alcoholic products used for industrial or medicinal purposes.
[CP022] Tobacco -- CP022 refers to tobacco, covering cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco, chewing tobacco and related smoking accessories purchased for personal consumption. It includes all manufactured tobacco products used for smoking or oral use, but excludes non-tobacco nicotine delivery devices.
[CP023] Narcotics -- CP023 refers to the personal consumption of narcotics, encompassing illegal or controlled psychoactive substances used by individuals for non-medical purposes, including both illicit drugs and prescription medications taken without a valid prescription. The entry captures expenditures on these substances for personal recreation or dependence.
[CP03] Clothing and footwear -- CP03 groups all household spending on garments and shoes, including everyday clothing, specialized apparel such as sportswear, protective gear, and related accessories, as well as footwear for personal use. It also covers routine repair or replacement of these items but excludes uniforms provided by an employer.
[CP031] Clothing -- CP031 covers expenditures on garments and footwear bought for personal use, including clothing accessories, protective wear, and items specially designed for specific activities. It excludes uniforms provided by employers and second-hand clothing acquired through resale.
[CP032] Footwear -- CP032 - Footwear covers all spending on shoes, boots, sandals, sports and other types of footwear purchased for personal use, including related accessories such as socks when sold together, and services directly related to obtaining or maintaining footwear. This category is part of the Classification of Individual Consumption According to Purpose (2018 revision).
[CP04] Housing, water, electricity, gas and other fuels -- CP04 covers household expenses for shelter and basic utilities, including rent, mortgage payments, maintenance, water supply, electricity, gas and other fuels used for heating, cooking and lighting. It aggregates all costs directly related to providing a dwelling and keeping it operational for everyday living.
[CP041] Actual and imputed rentals for housing -- It records household spending on rented accommodation and the notional rent of owner-occupied dwellings, representing the value of housing services consumed. The entry includes actual rent payments, lease fees and imputed rent estimated from market rates for owned housing.
[CP042] Maintenance and repair of the dwelling -- CP042 includes household spending on routine upkeep and minor repairs needed to keep a dwelling functional and presentable, such as painting, plumbing, electrical work, and replacement of worn parts, recorded as maintenance and repair of the dwelling rather than capital improvements.
[CP043] Water supply and related services -- CP043 records household expenditures for water provision and associated services, covering the purchase of water for domestic use, fees for distribution networks, sewage collection and drainage, and wastewater treatment.
[CP044] Electricity, gas and other fuels -- CP044 covers household spending on energy carriers such as electricity, natural gas, LPG, coal, oil and other fuels that are purchased for domestic use, including heating, lighting, cooking and powering appliances. It aggregates all expenditures on these fuels regardless of the specific source or supplier.

[CP051] Furniture, furnishings and carpets -- CP051 covers household goods used to furnish and decorate living spaces, including tables, chairs, sofas, beds, mattresses, cabinets, and similar furniture, as well as soft furnishings such as curtains and cushions and floor coverings like carpets, rugs and other textile or synthetic floorings. It captures consumer expenditure on these items for personal and residential use.
[CP052] Household textiles -- Household textiles covers all fabric goods used in homes for personal and household purposes, such as bed linen, towels, curtains, tablecloths, cushions and other soft furnishings. The category includes both newly purchased and second-hand items intended for everyday domestic use, excluding clothing and industrial textiles.
[CP053] Household appliances -- CP053 covers household appliances, encompassing the purchase of durable goods used for domestic tasks such as refrigerators, washing machines, dishwashers, ovens, air-conditioners and similar equipment, as well as related accessories. It excludes non-durable consumer goods and services not directly tied to the appliance itself.
[CP054] Glassware, tableware and household utensils -- CP054 covers domestic glassware, tableware and household utensils such as plates, bowls, drinking glasses, cups, cutlery, cookware and small kitchen tools used by individuals for personal consumption, excluding professional or industrial equipment. The category groups together items primarily intended for everyday eating, drinking and food preparation in private households.
[CP055] Tools and equipment for house and garden -- CP055 refers to tools and equipment used for domestic and garden purposes, such as hand tools, power tools, lawn mowers, gardening machines, and related accessories. The category groups items that facilitate household maintenance, repair, and outdoor landscaping activities.
[CP056] Goods and services for routine household maintenance -- CP056 includes expenditures on items and services required to keep a dwelling in normal condition, such as repairs, cleaning, gardening, pest control and minor renovations. It covers routine upkeep rather than major improvements or expansions.
[CP06] Health -- CP06 - Health covers household expenditures on medical and dental services, hospital and outpatient care, pharmaceutical products, medical devices, preventive and rehabilitative treatments, and other health-related goods and services.
[CP061] Pharmaceutical products -- CP061 covers pharmaceutical products, encompassing prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vaccines, and related health-care items purchased for personal use. The category includes all substances formulated for diagnosis, treatment, or prevention of disease that are acquired by individuals for consumption. It excludes medical devices and non-medicinal health supplements.
[CP062] Other medical products and equipment -- CP062 includes medical products and equipment that are not listed under other specific categories, such as over-the-counter health aids, prosthetic and orthotic devices, diagnostic tools and other medical supplies purchased by households for personal use.
[CP063] Outpatient services -- Outpatient services are medical procedures and professional care received without being admitted to a hospital, covering doctor consultations, diagnostic tests, minor surgeries, and therapy sessions. In the 2018 revision of the Classification of Individual Consumption According to Purpose, the CP063 entry groups household spending on these non-inpatient health services.
[CP064] Hospital services -- CP064 Hospital services covers all medical care provided by hospitals, including inpatient treatment, surgeries, emergency and day-care procedures, diagnostic and therapeutic services, as well as post-hospital rehabilitation. It aggregates expenses incurred by individuals for these health-care activities under the 2018 COICOP revision.
[CP07] Transport -- CP07 Transport records household spending on all travel-related items, including the purchase and lease of motor vehicles, fuel, public transport fares, vehicle maintenance, insurance, parking fees and related services used by individuals for personal mobility.
[CP071] Purchase of vehicles -- CP071 records household spending on acquiring motor vehicles for personal transport, covering purchase price, taxes, registration fees and related costs for new-or-used cars, motorcycles and similar vehicles.
[CP072] Operation of personal transport equipment -- CP072 covers the operating costs of personal transport equipment, including fuel, electricity, lubricants, maintenance, repairs, insurance, road taxes, registration fees, parking charges, tolls and other expenses incurred to keep privately owned vehicles such as cars, motorcycles, bicycles and electric scooters in service.
[CP073] Transport services -- CP073 Transport services includes household spending on passenger and freight transport, such as bus, rail, air and water travel, vehicle hire, taxi and ride-sharing, freight forwarding and related parking and toll charges.
[CP08] Information and communication -- CP08 - Information and communication includes household spending on equipment, services and content used for obtaining, processing and transmitting information. It covers purchases and rentals of computers, telephones, internet access, software, broadcasting subscriptions and related services, as well as expenditures on printed and electronic media and related repairs.
[CP081] Information and communication equipment -- CP081 includes personal devices used for receiving, transmitting, processing or storing information, such as computers, smartphones, tablets, printers and related accessories. It also covers telecommunications equipment like routers, modems and wearable gadgets that households use for communication and digital media consumption.
[CP082] Telephone and facsimile services -- CP082 covers telephone and facsimile services used by individuals, including subscription fees, line rental, call and fax usage charges for landline and mobile networks, as well as related ancillary fees. It reflects expenditures on voice communication and fax transmission for personal purposes.
[CP083] Internet access services -- CP083 covers services that provide households with connectivity to the internet, including broadband, dial-up, mobile data and satellite connections purchased for personal use. It encompasses subscription fees, usage charges and related technical support needed to access online content, communication platforms and cloud-based applications. This category excludes equipment purchases such as routers or computers.
[CP09] Recreation, sport and culture -- CP09 covers household spending on leisure activities, including admission fees for movies, concerts, museums, and sporting events, as well as expenses for equipment, clothing, and accessories used for recreation, sport and cultural pursuits. It also includes payments for membership fees, subscriptions, and other services related to the participation in or enjoyment of these activities.
[CP091] Audio-visual, photographic and information processing equipment -- CP091 covers consumer goods employed for audio-visual, photographic and information processing functions. It includes items such as televisions, video-recorders, cameras, computers, tablets and printers that households use for entertainment, image capture and data handling.
[CP092] Other recreational goods and sports goods -- CP092 includes consumer items bought for leisure that are not listed in more specific categories, covering a range of recreational and sports products such as camping gear, bicycles, fitness accessories, hobby supplies and other goods used for personal enjoyment and physical activity.
[CP093] Recreational and sporting services -- CP093 refers to expenditures on recreational and sporting services, including fees for participation in organized sports, admission to leisure and cultural events, use of sports facilities, equipment rentals, and related personal fitness activities. It captures household spending on leisure pursuits and organized physical activities.
[CP094] Cultural services -- CP094 refers to cultural services, encompassing household spending on activities that provide artistic, entertainment, or heritage experiences such as museum visits, theatrical performances, cinema attendance, concerts, sporting events, libraries and cultural festivals. This category captures expenditures aimed at personal enjoyment, education and cultural enrichment.
[CP095] Newspapers, books and stationery -- CP095 records household outlays on printed media and paper supplies, covering purchases of newspapers, magazines, books, notebooks, writing paper and other stationery items used for personal reading, study or office work. It reflects consumer spending on information, leisure and basic writing materials.
[CP096] Package holidays and other package services -- CP096 records spending on organized travel products that bundle transport, lodging, meals and related services into a single purchase, such as package holidays sold by tour operators. It also covers other bundled tourism offerings like cruise packages, guided tours and all-inclusive resort deals that are marketed as a unit rather than separately.
[CP10] Education services -- CP10 covers household spending on education services, including tuition, fees, textbooks, supplies, transportation and related costs for pre-primary, primary, secondary, tertiary and other formal schooling, whether provided by public or private institutions.
[CP101] Pre-primary and primary education services -- CP101 includes household spending on pre-primary and primary education services, such as tuition fees, textbooks and other school supplies, for children before and during primary school. It represents individual consumption of basic educational provision.
[CP102] Secondary education services -- CP102 captures household spending on secondary education services, including tuition and other fees for middle and high schools, associated instructional materials, transportation, and related support services that enable students to attend and complete secondary schooling. This category reflects personal consumption aimed at obtaining formal education beyond the primary level.
[CP103] Post-secondary non-tertiary education services -- CP103 covers education services provided after compulsory secondary schooling that do not lead to a tertiary degree, such as vocational training, adult continuing-education courses, community-college programs, language schools and other non-degree post-secondary instruction. It includes fees for instruction, related examinations and certification, but excludes university-level degree programmes.
[CP104] Tertiary education services -- CP104 covers expenditures on tertiary education, such as university, college and vocational training services. It includes tuition and fees, instructional materials, examination costs and related support services that households pay to obtain post-secondary qualifications.
[CP105] Education services not elsewhere classified -- CP105 includes education services that do not fall under the main categories of primary, secondary, tertiary or vocational training. It covers private tutoring, adult education, language courses, extracurricular lessons and other informal or specialised learning activities not classified elsewhere.
[CP11] Restaurants and accommodation services -- CP11 covers household spending on meals eaten away from home and on lodging services. It includes expenditures for restaurant meals, take-away food, catering, hotel and motel accommodation, as well as related taxes and service charges. This entry aggregates all consumption directed toward eating out and staying overnight.
[CP111] Food and non-alcoholic beverages serving services -- CP111 covers establishments that prepare and serve meals, snacks and non-alcoholic drinks to consumers, including restaurants, cafés, take-away outlets and catering services. It includes activities where the primary output is the provision of food and non-alcoholic beverages for immediate consumption on-site or for take-away.
[CP112] Accommodation services -- CP112 includes household spending on lodging such as hotels, motels, guesthouses, and other short-term rented accommodations used for personal travel. It also covers fees for campsites, holiday rentals and similar accommodation services.
[CP12] Insurance and financial services -- CP12 groups household outlays for insurance premiums, banking and investment fees, pension contributions and other financial products and services that protect against risk or manage money. It captures expenditures on life, health, property and casualty insurance as well as fees for financial intermediation and advisory services.
[CP121] Life insurance -- CP121 records household spending on life insurance premiums, covering policies that provide financial protection to beneficiaries after the insured's death as well as related death-benefit or burial insurance contracts. It reflects the amount households allocate to secure future income for dependents.
[CP122] Non-life insurance -- CP122 records household spending on non-life insurance, covering premiums for policies that protect against property damage, liability, motor accidents, health expenses not covered by medical insurance, and other casualty risks. It reflects the cost of risk-transfer products purchased by individuals for personal use.
[CP123] Financial intermediation services -- The CP123 entry covers services provided by banks, credit unions, insurance firms and other financial intermediaries that households use to manage money, obtain credit, make payments and protect assets. It includes activities such as deposit taking, loan issuance, payment processing, insurance contracts and investment facilitation consumed for personal financial needs.
[CP13] Personal care, social protection and miscellaneous goods and services -- CP13 groups expenditures that individuals make for personal care items such as cosmetics, toiletries, hair-dressing and other grooming services, together with social-protection outlays like health-insurance premiums, social-security contributions and benefits, and a catch-all category of miscellaneous goods and services that do not fall under other specific purpose classifications. This category reflects spending aimed at personal well-being, safety and ancillary needs.
[CP131] Personal care services -- Personal care services comprise activities performed for the individual's hygiene, grooming and appearance, such as hairdressing, beauty treatments, nail care, waxing, and other similar services aimed at maintaining personal cleanliness and aesthetic presentation. These services are consumed directly by households for personal well-being rather than for professional or medical purposes.
[CP132] Social protection services -- CP132 includes spending on services that provide financial or in-kind assistance to individuals, such as unemployment benefits, pensions, social security contributions and other welfare programmes. It covers both compulsory and voluntary payments to social insurance schemes and direct cash transfers aimed at reducing poverty and supporting vulnerable groups.
[CP133] Other services not elsewhere classified -- CP133 - Other services not elsewhere classified is a residual category in the 2018 revision of the Classification of Individual Consumption According to Purpose. It captures individual-focused services that do not fit into any specific listed group, such as miscellaneous personal assistance, niche entertainment, and ad-hoc professional services.
[CP14] Expenditure of residents abroad and non-resident expenditure -- CP14 records the consumption of households when they travel overseas and the consumption of non-residents within the domestic economy, including tourism, accommodation, meals and related services.
[CP141] Expenditure of residents abroad -- CP141 records all spending by residents while they are abroad, covering transport to and within foreign destinations, accommodation, meals, entertainment, and other personal consumption items incurred during trips. It aggregates both short-term tourism expenses and longer stays such as study or work abroad, reflecting the total value of goods and services purchased outside the resident's home country.
[CP142] Non-resident expenditure in the domestic territory -- CP142 records the goods and services purchased by non-resident individuals while they are present in the domestic territory, such as tourism spending, short-term rentals, and other personal consumption. These expenditures are included in the domestic consumption component of national accounts because the resources are used within the country's borders.
