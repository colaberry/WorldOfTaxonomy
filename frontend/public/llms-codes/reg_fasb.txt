[asu_2014_09] ASU 2014-09 - Revenue from Contracts with Customers (ASC 606) -- The 2014-09 update issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board introduces ASC 606, a new revenue-recognition framework for contracts with customers. It replaces previous industry-specific guidance with a principles-based model that requires entities to identify performance obligations, allocate transaction price, and recognize revenue as those obligations are satisfied. The standard aims to improve comparability and transparency of revenue reporting across industries.
[asu_2016_01] ASU 2016-01 - Recognition and Measurement of Financial Assets -- ASU 2016-01 issued by the FASB clarifies how companies should recognize and measure financial assets, introduces the fair-value option, revises classification of financial assets, and modifies disclosures to improve transparency.
[asu_2016_02] ASU 2016-02 - Leases (ASC 842) -- ASU 2016-02, issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, updates the lease accounting guidance in ASC 842. It requires lessees to record a right-of-use asset and a corresponding lease liability for most operating and finance leases, changing balance-sheet presentation and disclosure requirements for entities that adopt the new standard.
[asu_2016_10] ASU 2016-10 - Revenue: Identifying Performance Obligations -- ASU 2016-10 updates the revenue-recognition guidance to require entities to identify each distinct performance obligation in a contract with a customer, separating promised goods or services that are accounted for separately. It clarifies criteria for distinguishing combined versus distinct promises and sets out how to allocate the transaction price among the identified obligations.
[asu_2016_12] ASU 2016-12 - Revenue: Narrow-Scope Improvements -- ASU 2016-12, issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, refines ASC 606 by making narrow-scope improvements to revenue-recognition guidance. It clarifies transition methods, provides additional examples, and aligns certain disclosures with IFRS for entities that have already adopted the new standard. The update is effective for annual reporting periods beginning after December 15, 2020, with earlier adoption permitted.
[asu_2016_13] ASU 2016-13 - Current Expected Credit Losses (CECL) -- The FASB Accounting Standards Update 2016-13 establishes the current expected credit loss (CECL) model, which replaces the prior incurred-loss approach by requiring entities to estimate and record lifetime expected credit losses for financial assets measured at amortized cost. It changes the timing and methodology of loan-loss provisioning, affecting banks, credit unions and other lenders.
[asu_2020_04] ASU 2020-04 - Reference Rate Reform (LIBOR Transition) -- ASU 2020-04, issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, provides guidance for entities transitioning from LIBOR to alternative reference rates, detailing accounting and disclosure requirements for affected contracts. It outlines remediation steps, measurement adjustments, and required disclosures to ensure consistent financial reporting during the transition.
[asu_2020_05] ASU 2020-05 - Leases: Effective Date Extension -- ASU 2020-05 postpones the effective date for the new lease-accounting standards under Topic 842, moving the start date for public-company filers to December 15 2021 and for private-company filers to December 15 2022, giving entities additional time to implement the required changes to lease classification, measurement and disclosure.

[asu_2023_01] ASU 2023-01 - Investments - Equity Method and Joint Ventures -- ASU 2023-01 updates the accounting guidance for investments accounted for under the equity method and for joint ventures. It clarifies the measurement of an investor's share of earnings, the treatment of losses, and disclosure requirements, aiming to improve consistency with the underlying economics of these relationships and to align the guidance with recent standard-setting initiatives.
[asu_2023_07] ASU 2023-07 - Segment Reporting Improvements -- ASU 2023-07, issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, revises the Accounting Standards Codification's segment reporting guidance to improve the relevance and comparability of disclosed information. It mandates that entities present profit or loss, assets and liabilities for each reportable segment and aligns certain disclosures with international standards, enhancing transparency for investors.
[asu_2023_09] ASU 2023-09 - Income Tax Disclosure Improvements -- ASU 2023-09, issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board, updates income-tax disclosure requirements. It mandates that entities provide more detailed information about tax expense components, temporary differences, and the effective-tax-rate reconciliation, enhancing the transparency and comparability of tax reporting for users of financial statements.

[credit_losses] Credit Losses Standards -- The Financial Accounting Standards Board Accounting Standards Update identified by the code "credit_losses" introduces the Current Expected Credit Loss (CECL) model for measuring credit losses on financial assets. It revises ASC 326 to require entities to estimate lifetime expected losses at the time of asset acquisition, replacing the previous incurred-loss approach. This change aims to provide more timely and forward-looking information about credit risk.
[disclosure] Disclosure Framework Standards -- The Disclosure Framework Standards are an Accounting Standards Update issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board that revises the Codification's disclosure requirements. They define the overall structure for entity-wide, industry-specific, and transaction-specific disclosures, setting the principles and content needed for transparent financial reporting.
[financial_inst] Financial Instruments Standards -- The Financial Instruments Standards entry aggregates FASB Accounting Standards Updates that revise and clarify the recognition, measurement, presentation, and disclosure requirements for financial assets, liabilities, and derivatives. It guides preparers and auditors in applying consistent accounting treatments for loans, investments, and other financial contracts.
[leases] Lease Accounting Standards -- The Financial Accounting Standards Board's Accounting Standards Update on leases, known as ASC 842, revises lease accounting guidance, requiring lessees to recognize most leases on the balance sheet as right-of-use assets and lease liabilities, and provides guidance on classification, measurement and disclosures for both lessees and lessors.
[revenue] Revenue Recognition Standards -- The Financial Accounting Standards Board's Accounting Standards Update titled "Revenue Recognition Standards" implements ASC 606, establishing a unified framework for recognizing revenue from contracts with customers. It requires entities to identify performance obligations, allocate transaction prices, and recognize revenue as those obligations are satisfied, improving comparability across industries and jurisdictions.
[sustainability] Sustainability Disclosure Standards -- The Sustainability Disclosure Standards are an Accounting Standards Update issued by the Financial Accounting Standards Board that establishes mandatory ESG-related reporting requirements for public companies. They require entities to disclose material information on climate-related risks, governance, strategy and performance, aligning financial reporting with sustainability considerations.
