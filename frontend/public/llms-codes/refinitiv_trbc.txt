[TRBC] TRBC Economic Sectors -- The Refinitiv TRBC (Thomson Reuters Business Classification) is a global taxonomy that organizes publicly listed companies into a hierarchy of economic sectors, industry groups, industries and sub-industries. The TRBC entry represents the top-level grouping of all companies by their primary economic activity, providing a standardized framework for market analysis and investment research.

[TRBC.51] Basic Materials -- TRBC.51 Basic Materials is the top-level sector that groups companies engaged in the extraction, production and processing of natural resources. It covers industries such as chemicals, construction materials, metals and mining, forest products, and fertilizers, as well as firms that provide related services.
[TRBC.52] Industrials -- TRBC.52 is the Industrials sector within Refinitiv's Thomson Reuters Business Classification system. It groups companies that produce capital goods, provide services to other businesses, or operate transportation and infrastructure assets, including aerospace, construction, machinery and logistics. The sector is used for investment analysis and benchmarking.
[TRBC.53] Consumer Cyclicals -- The TRBC (Thomson Reuters Business Classification) Consumer Cyclicals sector groups companies that produce non-essential goods and services whose demand rises and falls with economic cycles, such as automobiles, apparel, hotels, and leisure businesses. These firms are particularly sensitive to changes in consumer spending and overall business confidence.
[TRBC.54] Consumer Non-Cyclicals -- The Consumer Non-Cyclicals category groups companies that produce essential goods and services whose demand remains steady regardless of economic conditions, such as food, beverages, household and personal products, and everyday retail items. In the Refinitiv TRBC hierarchy, TRBC.54 identifies this sector as a distinct segment of the broader consumer industry.
[TRBC.55] Financials -- TRBC.55 denotes the Financials sector in Refinitiv's taxonomy, grouping companies that provide banking, insurance, asset management, real estate and other financial services. It aggregates firms whose primary revenue derives from borrowing, lending, risk mitigation, investment or property-related activities.
[TRBC.56] Healthcare -- TRBC 56 represents the Healthcare industry within Refinitiv's TRBC classification. It groups companies that develop, produce, or provide medical services, equipment, pharmaceuticals, biotech, and health-insurance. The sector captures firms involved in patient care, diagnostics, and related support services.
[TRBC.57] Technology -- TRBC.57 is the Technology sector in the Refinitiv Thomson Reuters Business Classification, grouping companies that create, produce, or distribute technology products and services. It includes hardware manufacturers, software developers, semiconductor firms, IT consulting and outsourcing providers, and related digital infrastructure businesses.
[TRBC.58] Telecommunication Services -- Telecommunication Services comprises firms that deliver voice, data and video transmission across wired, wireless and satellite networks, including fixed-line carriers, mobile operators, broadband providers and related infrastructure companies that enable connectivity and related communication services.
[TRBC.59] Utilities -- TRBC.59 Utilities is the Refinitiv sector grouping companies that produce, transmit, and distribute essential services such as electricity, gas, water and waste management. These firms often operate regulated networks and include both traditional utility providers and newer renewable-energy operators. The classification tracks the performance of entities that supply fundamental infrastructure to households and businesses.
[TRBC.60] Academic and Educational Services -- TRBC 60 groups companies that provide formal and informal learning, including schools, universities, training firms, education-technology platforms, curriculum developers and test-preparation services. The classification covers entities that generate revenue from tuition, certification, educational consulting and related support services for individuals and organizations.
[TRBC.61] Government Activity -- TRBC.61 covers companies and entities that are primarily engaged in providing public services, policy implementation and regulatory functions at national, regional or local levels. This includes ministries, agencies and other public-sector bodies that manage defence, public safety, education, health and infrastructure. The segment groups firms whose revenue derives mainly from government operations and public administration.
