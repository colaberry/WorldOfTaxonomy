[CB] CBD GBF Targets -- The CB entry refers to the Convention on Biological Diversity's Global Biodiversity Framework Targets, a set of quantitative goals adopted to halt biodiversity loss, restore ecosystems, protect species and genetic diversity, and enhance means of implementation by 2030. The targets provide a common, science-based agenda for governments, indigenous peoples and other stakeholders to align policies and actions worldwide.
[CB.01] Target 1: Spatial Planning -- Target 1 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the development and implementation of comprehensive spatial planning that aligns land, sea and freshwater use with biodiversity objectives, safeguards ecologically critical areas and ensures that development activities operate within sustainable limits.
[CB.02] Target 2: Restoration -- Target 2 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for restoring degraded ecosystems and habitats, with an aim to recover at least 30 percent of degraded land and 30 percent of marine areas by 2030. It stresses science-based, inclusive restoration planning, implementation and monitoring to boost ecosystem services and biodiversity resilience.

[CB.04] Target 4: Halt Species Extinction -- CB.04, Target 4 of the Convention on Biological Diversity Global Biodiversity Framework, calls for halting species extinction by strengthening conservation actions, reducing direct threats, and improving monitoring and data on known and undiscovered species, with the objective of preventing further loss by 2030.
[CB.05] Target 5: Sustainable Harvesting -- Target 5 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the sustainable harvesting of wild species, ensuring that extraction levels do not exceed the capacity of ecosystems to regenerate. It promotes practices that maintain genetic diversity, ecosystem functions, and the livelihoods of communities dependent on natural resources.
[CB.06] Target 6: Invasive Alien Species -- Target 6 seeks to minimize the introduction and spread of invasive alien species that threaten ecosystems, livelihoods, and health. It calls for national and regional strategies, early detection, rapid response, and capacity-building, aiming to significantly reduce the abundance and distribution of high-risk invasive species by 2050.
[CB.07] Target 7: Reduce Pollution -- Target 7 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the reduction of pollution that threatens ecosystems, species and human health. It sets measurable goals to limit chemical waste, nutrient runoff, plastic debris and other contaminants, promoting cleaner production, sustainable waste management and restoration of polluted habitats.
[CB.08] Target 8: Climate Change Impacts -- Target 8 urges parties to integrate biodiversity into climate-change mitigation and adaptation strategies, reduce climate-driven threats to ecosystems, and boost the resilience of species and habitats by enhancing ecosystem-based approaches and safeguarding climate-vulnerable areas.
[CB.09] Target 9: Sustainable Use -- Target 9 calls for the sustainable use of biodiversity, ensuring that the exploitation of ecosystems, species and genetic resources does not exceed their capacity to regenerate. It aims to promote practices that maintain ecosystem services, reduce over-harvesting, and integrate equitable benefit-sharing while supporting livelihoods and economic development.
[CB.10] Target 10: Agriculture/Forestry Biodiversity -- Target 10 calls for the integration of biodiversity values into agricultural and forestry practices, promoting sustainable land-use systems that conserve genetic resources, protect ecosystem services and reduce habitat loss. It seeks to support farmers and forest managers with incentives, technical guidance and policies that enhance resilience, productivity and the contribution of these sectors to overall biodiversity goals.
[CB.11] Target 11: Nature-Based Solutions -- Target 11 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the implementation of nature-based solutions to protect, restore and sustainably manage ecosystems, enhance climate resilience and support livelihoods, aiming to achieve ecosystem health and deliver co-benefits for biodiversity and people.
[CB.12] Target 12: Urban Biodiversity -- Target 12 calls for the protection, restoration and sustainable management of biodiversity within urban and peri-urban areas, promoting green infrastructure, nature-based solutions and inclusive city planning that supports native species and ecosystem services. It encourages cities to integrate biodiversity considerations into policies, land use and public awareness, fostering resilient urban environments and healthier communities.
[CB.13] Target 13: Access and Benefit Sharing -- Target 13 under the Convention on Biological Diversity Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the establishment of transparent, fair and equitable mechanisms for accessing genetic resources and sharing the benefits arising from their use. It seeks to strengthen legal and policy frameworks, support capacity-building for resource-holding communities, and promote incentives that balance conservation with sustainable utilization.
[CB.14] Target 14: Policy Integration -- CB.14 is Target 14 of the Convention on Biological Diversity Global Biodiversity Framework, calling for the integration of biodiversity considerations into all relevant policies, plans and strategies at national and sub-national levels. The aim is to mainstream biodiversity across sectors so that decision-making, financing and incentives support conservation and sustainable use.
[CB.15] Target 15: Business Reporting -- Target 15 calls for businesses to publicly disclose their impacts and dependencies on biodiversity using standard metrics and reporting frameworks, improving transparency and guiding sustainable decision-making.
[CB.16] Target 16: Sustainable Consumption -- Target 16 under the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the adoption of sustainable consumption and production patterns that minimise resource use, waste and pollution, while encouraging responsible consumer choices and circular-economy practices. It seeks to align market incentives and public policies with biodiversity-friendly behaviours and efficient use of natural assets.
[CB.17] Target 17: Biosafety -- Target 17 of the Convention on Biological Diversity Global Biodiversity Framework aims to strengthen biosafety by preventing harmful impacts of living-modified organisms and invasive species on biodiversity, ecosystems and human health. It calls for national risk-assessment systems, capacity building and international cooperation to manage and mitigate biological risks.
[CB.18] Target 18: Harmful Subsidies -- Target 18 of the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for governments to identify, phase out and re-direct subsidies that harm biodiversity, such as those supporting unsustainable fisheries, agriculture, forestry or fossil-fuel extraction, and to replace them with incentives that promote conservation and sustainable use. It aims to align public finance with the Convention on Biological Diversity's goals, fostering ecosystem resilience and climate mitigation.
[CB.19] Target 19: Resource Mobilization -- Target 19 of the Convention on Biological Diversity's Global Biodiversity Framework calls for the mobilization of adequate, predictable and sustainable financial resources, technology and capacity-building support to implement biodiversity actions at national, regional and global levels. It emphasizes diversified funding streams, public-private partnerships and equitable access to resources, especially for developing countries.
[CB.20] Target 20: Capacity Building -- Target 20 under the Global Biodiversity Framework calls for strengthening capacity at national, regional and local levels to implement biodiversity policies, monitor ecosystems and manage resources effectively. It emphasizes training, technical assistance, knowledge exchange and institutional development to enable diverse stakeholders, especially developing countries, to meet conservation and sustainable use objectives.
[CB.21] Target 21: Knowledge Management -- Target 21 calls for the development and sharing of knowledge systems that support biodiversity conservation, sustainable use and benefit-sharing. It aims to improve data availability, promote both traditional and scientific knowledge, and strengthen capacity for monitoring and reporting.
[CB.22] Target 22: Indigenous Peoples -- Target 22 seeks to recognize, respect and integrate the rights, traditional knowledge and stewardship of Indigenous peoples in the implementation of biodiversity conservation and sustainable use, ensuring their full and effective participation in related decision-making processes.
[CB.23] Target 23: Gender Equality -- Target 23 of the Convention on Biological Diversity Global Biodiversity Framework calls for gender equality by promoting equal participation of women and men in biodiversity decision-making, ensuring fair access to resources, and integrating gender perspectives across policies and actions.
