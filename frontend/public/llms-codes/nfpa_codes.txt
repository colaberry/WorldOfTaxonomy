[NF] NFPA Code Categories -- The NF entry groups the National Fire Protection Association's suite of fire-protection standards, each identified by a numeric designation such as NFPA 1, NFPA 70, or NFPA 101. These codes cover topics ranging from fire prevention and building construction to electrical safety and emergency response, providing consensus-based requirements that jurisdictions, designers, and insurers use to assess and mitigate fire hazards.
[NF.01] NFPA 1 - Fire Code -- NF.01 refers to NFPA 1 - Fire Code, the comprehensive standard that establishes minimum fire safety requirements for new and existing structures, including fire prevention, building construction, occupancy, and fire protection systems. It is used by authorities to enforce consistent fire safety measures and reduce risk.
[NF.02] NFPA 10 - Portable Fire Extinguishers -- NF.02 designates NFPA 10, the standard that sets requirements for portable fire extinguishers, covering their selection, installation, inspection, maintenance and testing. The code ensures extinguishers are matched to specific fire hazards and remain reliable throughout their service life.
[NF.03] NFPA 13 - Sprinkler Systems -- NF.03 refers to NFPA 13, the standard that specifies the design, installation, and maintenance of automatic fire sprinkler systems. It details system components, hydraulic calculations, water supply requirements, and testing procedures to ensure effective fire suppression in a variety of occupancies.
[NF.04] NFPA 25 - Inspection/Testing Water Systems -- NF.04 refers to NFPA 25, the standard that prescribes requirements for the periodic inspection, testing, and maintenance of water-based fire protection systems such as sprinklers, standpipes, and fire pumps. It establishes procedures, frequencies, and documentation practices to ensure these systems remain functional and ready to suppress fires.
[NF.05] NFPA 30 - Flammable Liquids Code -- NF.05 refers to NFPA 30, the Flammable Liquids Code, which establishes standards for the safe storage, handling, and use of flammable and combustible liquids. It outlines classification of liquids, container and facility design requirements, fire protection measures, and operational procedures to minimize fire hazards and protect life and property.
[NF.06] NFPA 54 - National Fuel Gas Code -- NF.06 refers to NFPA 54, the National Fuel Gas Code, which sets requirements for the safe design, installation, and inspection of fuel-gas piping systems, appliances, and related equipment in residential, commercial, and industrial settings. The code addresses material specifications, pressure testing, venting, and protection against fire and explosion hazards. It is widely adopted by authorities having jurisdiction to ensure consistent fuel-gas safety practices.
[NF.07] NFPA 70 - National Electrical Code -- NF.07 refers to NFPA 70, the National Electrical Code, which sets the minimum requirements for safe electrical design, installation, and inspection in the United States. It covers wiring methods, equipment standards, grounding, and protection against electrical hazards, helping prevent fires and shocks. The code is updated on a three-year cycle and is widely adopted by jurisdictions and industry.
[NF.08] NFPA 72 - Fire Alarm Code -- NF.08 designates NFPA 72, the National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code, which establishes requirements for the design, installation, testing and maintenance of fire alarm and emergency communication systems. It specifies performance criteria for detection devices, notification appliances and system integration to safeguard life and property.
[NF.09] NFPA 80 - Fire Doors and Windows -- NF.09 refers to NFPA 80, the standard that outlines requirements for the installation, inspection, testing and maintenance of fire doors, fire windows and their hardware to ensure they perform as rated in fire-resistance assemblies, helping to control the spread of fire and smoke.
[NF.10] NFPA 90A - HVAC Systems -- NF.10 refers to NFPA 90A, the standard for the installation of heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning (HVAC) systems. It establishes requirements for design, construction, operation and maintenance to protect life and property from fire hazards associated with HVAC equipment. Compliance helps ensure proper fire barrier integration and safe system performance.
[NF.11] NFPA 101 - Life Safety Code -- NF.11 refers to NFPA 101, the Life Safety Code, which establishes minimum building design, construction, protection and occupancy requirements to safeguard people from fire and related hazards. It covers means of egress, fire alarm and suppression systems, interior finishes, and special provisions for various occupancies, aiming to ensure safe evacuation and reduce injury or loss of life.
[NF.12] NFPA 110 - Emergency Power -- NF.12 designates NFPA 110, the standard that establishes requirements for the installation, performance, testing and maintenance of emergency power supply systems used to support fire protection and life-safety equipment. It covers generators, uninterruptible power supplies, transfer switches and related components to ensure reliable power during outages.
[NF.13] NFPA 704 - Hazard Diamond -- NF.13 refers to NFPA 704, the standard hazard-identification system that uses a colored diamond to convey a material's health, flammability, instability and any special hazards. The four quadrants are numbered 0-4, while a bottom-center "W" symbol denotes specific risks such as water reactivity.
[NF.14] NFPA 1500 - Firefighter Safety -- NF.14 refers to NFPA 1500, the national standard that defines a comprehensive occupational safety and health program for fire departments. It outlines requirements for training, personal protective equipment, health assessments, incident reporting, and emergency response procedures to protect firefighters on the fireground and in the station.
[NF.15] NFPA 1600 - Emergency Management -- NF.15 refers to NFPA 1600, a standard that establishes a comprehensive framework for emergency management covering preparedness, mitigation, response, continuity and recovery. It provides requirements and guidelines for organizations and communities to develop, implement and maintain effective emergency programs.
[NF.16] NFPA 2001 - Clean Agent Fire Suppression -- NF.16 refers to NFPA 2001, the standard for clean agent fire-suppression systems. It outlines requirements for design, installation, testing and maintenance of gaseous agents that extinguish fires without leaving residue, suitable for areas housing sensitive equipment.
