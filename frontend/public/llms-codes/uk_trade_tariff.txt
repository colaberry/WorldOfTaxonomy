[UT] UK Tariff Sections -- UT is the entry code for the UK Tariff Sections in the UK Global Tariff Schedule, grouping the full list of import duty rates into thematic sections such as agriculture, textiles and chemicals. Each section aggregates related commodity codes, providing a structured overview of the rates and any preferential treatments applied under UK trade policy.
[UT.01] Live Animals -- The UT.01 entry of the UK Global Tariff Schedule covers all live animals, including livestock, poultry, fish and other species, whether imported, exported or moved within the UK. It applies to any animal that is alive at the point of shipment and is subject to the relevant veterinary and phytosanitary requirements. This code is used to determine the applicable duty rates and regulatory controls for transporting living animals.
[UT.02] Vegetable Products -- UT.02 groups all vegetable products entering the United Kingdom, covering fresh, chilled, frozen, dried and processed forms such as canned, pickled or otherwise prepared vegetables. The code determines the applicable duty rates, tariff quotas and any phytosanitary requirements under the UK Global Tariff.
[UT.03] Fats and Oils -- UT.03 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule covers the import of fats and oils, including animal fats such as butter and lard, vegetable oils, and other edible or industrial fats. The entry specifies duty rates, applicable measures and any preferential treatment for these products when entering the UK market.
[UT.04] Prepared Foodstuffs -- UT.04 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule covers prepared foodstuffs, which are foods that have been processed, cooked, mixed or otherwise treated to be ready for consumption without further preparation. This category includes canned, frozen, cooked, seasoned or otherwise ready-to-eat items such as meals, snacks, sauces and ready-made dishes.
[UT.05] Mineral Products -- UT.05 applies to mineral products including cement, lime, gypsum and similar processed mineral building-materials imported into the United Kingdom, with duty rates and origin rules set out in the UK Global Tariff Schedule.
[UT.06] Chemical Products -- UT.06 is the UK Global Tariff code for chemical products, covering a broad range of inorganic and organic chemicals, industrial chemicals, polymers, fertilizers and related compounds used in manufacturing, agriculture and scientific research. It groups these items for customs duty and statistical purposes.
[UT.07] Plastics and Rubber -- UT.07 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule groups all plastic and rubber commodities, covering raw polymers, intermediate compounds and finished articles such as sheets, tubes, molded parts and tyres. The entry sets the applicable import duty rates and any preferential treatment for these materials, distinguishing them from other chemical or textile categories.
[UT.08] Raw Hides and Leather -- UT.08 applies to untreated animal skins and raw hides, as well as leather that has been tanned but not yet fashioned into finished products, encompassing both bovine and other species in their basic, uncut form for further manufacturing.
[UT.09] Wood and Cork -- UT.09 covers goods made from wood and cork, such as sawn timber, veneers, plywood, chipboard and raw or processed cork, but excludes finished furniture, flooring or other articles primarily composed of different materials.
[UT.10] Paper and Paperboard -- UT.10 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule classifies all paper and paperboard products
[UT.11] Textiles -- UT.11 is a heading in the UK Global Tariff Schedule that groups together textile products such as woven and knitted fabrics, yarns, fibers and related finished goods used in apparel, home furnishings and industrial applications. The code determines the applicable import duty rates, quotas and any preferential or restrictive trade measures for these items.
[UT.12] Footwear and Headgear -- UT.12 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule covers all types of footwear and headgear, applying uniform customs duties to items such as shoes, boots, sandals, sports shoes and all categories of hats, caps, helmets and similar protective or decorative head coverings.
[UT.13] Stone and Ceramics -- UT.13 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule applies to imports of stone and ceramic products, covering natural and engineered stone, tiles, bricks, porcelain, earthenware, and similar goods used for construction, decoration or household purposes. The entry defines the tariff rates, import licensing and any applicable trade measures for these materials.
[UT.14] Precious Metals -- UT.14 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule applies to the import of precious metals such as gold, silver, platinum and related alloys, whether in bulk form, bars, ingots or uncoated jewellery components. The entry generally carries a zero-or low-rate duty, but importers must meet provenance and valuation requirements and may be subject to anti-dumping or countervailing measures where applicable.
[UT.15] Base Metals -- UT.15 groups non-ferrous base metals-copper, aluminium, lead, zinc, nickel and their common alloys-in the UK Global Tariff Schedule, applying the standard duty rate to raw metal, semi-finished shapes, sheets and bars imported for further processing or use. This entry excludes precious metals and specialised high-grade alloys, which are covered under separate codes.
[UT.16] Machinery -- UT.16 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule covers a broad range of machinery, including industrial, agricultural and construction equipment, as well as associated parts and accessories. The entry applies to complete machines and assemblies that are not classified under more specific headings, providing a generic tariff rate for such goods.
[UT.17] Vehicles and Aircraft -- UT.17 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule groups all types of motor vehicles, aircraft and their related parts, accessories and equipment for customs valuation and duty assessment. The heading applies to complete vehicles and aircraft as well as components intended for assembly or repair, establishing the default duty rates for these categories.
[UT.18] Optical and Medical -- UT.18 covers a range of optical and medical products, including eyeglasses, lenses, microscopes, surgical instruments and diagnostic equipment. Items classified here are subject to the UK Global Tariff rates and any relevant duty concessions or controls. The category groups consumer and professional devices used for vision correction, imaging and health
[UT.19] Arms and Ammunition -- UT.19 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule classifies arms and ammunition, covering firearms, rifles, pistols, artillery pieces, ammunition, explosives, missiles and related parts and accessories intended for military or law enforcement use.
[UT.20] Miscellaneous Articles -- UT.20 in the UK Global Tariff Schedule groups together miscellaneous articles that do not fall under any other specific heading, covering a wide range of goods such as small hardware, decorative items, and assorted manufactured parts. It serves as a catch-all provision for products that are otherwise uncategorised within the tariff framework.
[UT.21] Works of Art -- UT.21 covers original works of art and antiques intended for decorative or cultural use, such as paintings, sculptures, drawings, prints, photographs and other objects of artistic, historic or scientific interest; it excludes mass-produced reproductions and functional items not primarily artistic.
