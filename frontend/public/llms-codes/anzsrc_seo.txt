[AS] ANZSRC SEO Divisions -- AS denotes the ANZSRC Socio-Economic Objective (SEO) Divisions, the top-level categories that group research and development activities into broad socio-economic fields such as agriculture, health, engineering and the environment, enabling consistent reporting, funding allocation and statistical analysis across Australian and New Zealand institutions.
[AS.10] Defence -- AS.10 Defence covers activities related to the planning, development, procurement, operation and support of national and regional defence forces, including the production and maintenance of military equipment, training of personnel and associated research and development. It includes both public and private sector organizations that provide goods and services to defence agencies. This objective is used for statistical reporting on government and industry involvement in security and defence functions.
[AS.11] Plant Production and Plant Primary Products -- The AS.11 code covers activities involving the cultivation of crops and the harvest of primary plant products such as grains, fruits, vegetables, nuts, oilseeds and fibers, including associated research, breeding and basic processing of these raw plant materials. It is used to classify work that creates or studies the raw botanical outputs of agriculture.
[AS.12] Animal Production and Animal Primary Products -- AS.12 covers the raising of livestock and the production of primary animal commodities such as meat, milk, eggs, wool, hides and related products, including breeding, feeding, health management and initial processing activities. It falls under the broader Agricultural Sciences socio-economic objective in the ANZSRC classification.
[AS.13] Mineral Resources -- AS.13 Mineral Resources covers activities related to the exploration, extraction and processing of mineral ores, aggregates and other geological materials for use in manufacturing, construction and related sectors. It includes mining, quarrying, and associated support services such as mineral processing and on-site transport.
[AS.14] Energy -- AS.14 Energy includes activities focused on the extraction, generation, conversion, distribution and supply of energy resources such as coal, oil, gas, electricity, heat and renewable sources. It covers the production, transmission and retail of energy, as well as research, development and support services aimed at improving energy efficiency, sustainability and infrastructure.
[AS.15] Manufacturing -- AS.15 in the ANZSRC Socio-Economic Objective Classification covers manufacturing, the production of goods by processing, assembling or fabricating raw materials and components. It includes a wide range of activities from food processing and textiles to metalworking, electronics and machinery production.
[AS.16] Construction -- AS.16 - Construction comprises the planning, design, erection, alteration, repair, maintenance and demolition of buildings, civil engineering works and related structures, including residential, commercial, industrial and infrastructure projects such as roads, bridges, tunnels and water facilities. It also covers associated services such as site preparation, surveying, project management and specialist trades.
[AS.17] Transport -- AS.17 Transport includes the movement of people and goods by road, rail, air and sea, covering passenger services, freight handling and related logistical support. It also encompasses operation, maintenance and management of transport infrastructure and vehicle fleets, together with planning, regulation and associated support services.
[AS.18] Information and Communication Services -- The ANZSRC Socio-Economic Objective code AS.18 - Information and Communication Services - groups enterprises that produce, process, transmit or distribute information and communication content, including publishing, broadcasting, telecommunications, data processing, internet services, and related information technology activities.
[AS.19] Health -- AS.19 Health covers activities aimed at preserving, restoring, and improving physical and mental well-being, including medical care, disease prevention, health promotion, and rehabilitation services delivered by hospitals, clinics, public health agencies, and related institutions. It also includes research and development of health technologies, pharmaceuticals, and health policy.
[AS.20] Education and Training -- AS.20 covers the provision of education and training services, ranging from early childhood and primary schooling to secondary, vocational, tertiary and adult learning programs. It includes institutions, curricula development, teaching staff, learner assessment and support services, as well as organizations delivering professional development, apprenticeships and continuing education.
[AS.21] Indigenous -- AS.21 covers research and development focused on the social, economic and cultural wellbeing of Indigenous peoples, including the study and preservation of Indigenous knowledge, languages, health, education, land rights and community development, and efforts to address disparities and promote self-determination.
[AS.22] Law, Politics, Community Services -- AS.22, Law, Politics, Community Services, comprises activities focused on the administration of justice, political processes, public policy and governance, as well as the delivery of community and social welfare services. It includes legal practice, government agencies, legislative and regulatory bodies, and organisations providing community support and social assistance.
[AS.23] Cultural Understanding -- AS.23 Cultural Understanding covers research and activities that enhance awareness, appreciation and interpretation of diverse cultural traditions, values and practices. It aims to foster intercultural dialogue, preserve cultural heritage and improve social cohesion through education, media and community engagement.
[AS.24] Environment -- The AS.24 objective includes activities directed at protecting, managing and improving the natural environment, covering conservation, pollution control, environmental monitoring, sustainable resource use and the development of policies and technologies to maintain ecosystem health.
[AS.25] Expanding Knowledge -- AS.25 Expanding Knowledge includes activities that create, disseminate and apply new scientific and scholarly understanding, covering basic research, development and innovation across all disciplines. It is used to classify projects and organisations focused on generating knowledge that underpins future economic and social progress.
