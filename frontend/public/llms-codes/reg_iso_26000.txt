[4_1] 4.1 Accountability -- Accountability in ISO 26000 requires organizations to accept responsibility for their decisions and impacts on society, the environment, and stakeholders, and to be transparent in reporting performance, ensuring that mechanisms are in place to answer questions and address concerns.
[4_2] 4.2 Transparency -- In ISO 26000, section 4.2 defines transparency as the open and honest disclosure of information about an organisation's policies, decisions, activities and impacts related to social responsibility. It calls for making relevant data accessible and understandable to stakeholders, enabling informed dialogue and accountability.
[4_3] 4.3 Ethical behaviour -- Section 4.3 of ISO 26000:2010 focuses on ethical behaviour, stating that organisations should act with integrity, fairness and respect for law and societal norms. It outlines principles for transparent decision-making, avoidance of conflicts of interest and the promotion of a culture of accountability and trust.
[4_4] 4.4 Respect for stakeholder interests -- ISO 26000:2010 section 4.4 stresses that organizations should identify and respect the interests of all stakeholders, including employees, customers, suppliers, communities and the environment, ensuring their concerns are considered in decision-making and that relationships are managed transparently and fairly.
[4_5] 4.5 Respect for the rule of law -- ISO 26000:2010 outlines principle 4.5 Respect for the rule of law, urging organisations to comply with all applicable laws and regulations, uphold legal frameworks, and act ethically within the jurisdictions where they operate, ensuring their activities do not undermine legal order. This principle reinforces the need for transparent, accountable behaviour that aligns corporate actions with societal expectations of lawful conduct.
[4_6] 4.6 Respect for international norms of behaviour -- ISO 26000:2010 identifies "Respect for international norms of behaviour" as a core principle requiring organisations to align their actions with globally recognised standards such as the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, ILO conventions, environmental treaties and anti-corruption frameworks, thereby ensuring that their conduct contributes to sustainable development and avoids practices that conflict with established international expectations.
[4_7] 4.7 Respect for human rights -- ISO 26000:2010 defines respect for human rights as a core principle of social responsibility, requiring organisations to avoid infringing on internationally recognised rights and to address any adverse impacts their activities may cause. It calls for policies and practices that align with the UN Guiding Principles, promote dignity, non-discrimination and fair treatment for employees, communities and other stakeholders, and encourages transparent reporting and remediation mechanisms.
[6_2] 6.2 Organizational governance -- ISO 26000:2010 defines organizational governance as the system by which an entity makes and implements decisions, sets policies and objectives, and manages its resources and relationships to achieve its purpose responsibly and ethically. It emphasizes transparency, accountability, stakeholder engagement, and alignment of governance structures with the organization's social responsibility commitments.
[6_3] 6.3 Human rights -- Section 6.3 of ISO 26000:2010 outlines the human-rights component of social responsibility, emphasizing respect for internationally recognised rights, the duty to avoid infringing on others' rights, and the need for organizations to identify, assess and address human-rights impacts throughout their operations and relationships.
[6_4] 6.4 Labour practices -- Section 6.4 of ISO 26000:2010 specifies that organizations should promote fair labour practices, ensuring safe and healthy working conditions, equal opportunity, freedom of association, reasonable wages, and respect for workers' rights throughout their own operations and supply chains.
[6_5] 6.5 The environment -- ISO 26000 2010 section 6.5 outlines an organization's responsibility to protect the environment, urging the identification and mitigation of environmental impacts, compliance with applicable laws, and the adoption of sustainable practices such as resource efficiency, waste reduction, and pollution prevention to contribute to long-term ecological health.
[6_6] 6.6 Fair operating practices -- Fair operating practices refer to ethical conduct in business activities, including anti-corruption measures, responsible lobbying, respect for property rights, and transparent competition. The principle calls for organizations to avoid fraud, bribery and undue influence while ensuring their operations respect legal norms and societal expectations. Implementing these practices helps build trust with stakeholders and contributes to sustainable economic development.
[6_7] 6.7 Consumer issues -- This section of ISO 26000:2010 outlines an organization's responsibilities toward consumers, including fair marketing, product safety, consumer health, privacy protection and effective grievance mechanisms. It stresses transparent communication, responsible advertising and the need to ensure that products and services meet safety and quality standards.
[6_8] 6.8 Community involvement and development -- Entry 6.8 in ISO 26000:2010 addresses community involvement and development, urging organizations to actively engage with local stakeholders, support initiatives that improve social and economic conditions, and foster ongoing dialogue that contributes to sustainable community growth.
[7_1] 7.1 Relationship of an organization to social responsibility -- ISO 26000:2010 section 7.1 explains how an organization relates to social responsibility, describing the need to align its purpose, values and governance with ethical behavior, stakeholder expectations and sustainable development, and to embed responsible practices throughout its operations and decision-making.
[7_2] 7.2 Understanding an organization and its context for SR -- Section 7.2 of ISO 26000 explains that an organization must assess its internal and external environment, including stakeholders, legal, economic, cultural and environmental factors, to determine how social responsibility aligns with its purpose, strategy and operations. This understanding of context helps shape appropriate SR actions.

[7_4] 7.4 Communication on social responsibility -- Section 7.4 of ISO 26000:2010 outlines how organizations should communicate their social-responsibility commitments and performance. It advises transparent reporting, appropriate channels, and stakeholder engagement, ensuring information is accessible, credible and consistent with the organization's CSR strategy and objectives.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Principles of Social Responsibility -- Clause 4 of ISO 26000:2010 sets out the fundamental principles that underpin social responsibility, including accountability, transparency, ethical behavior, respect for stakeholder interests, respect for human rights, respect for the rule of law and respect for international norms. These principles provide a foundational framework for organizations to align their policies and actions with responsible and sustainable practices.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Recognizing Social Responsibility -- Clause 5 of ISO 26000:2010 outlines how organizations identify and acknowledge their social responsibilities, emphasizing the importance of stakeholder awareness, ethical values, and the alignment of activities with societal expectations. It guides entities to assess impacts, engage with affected parties, and integrate responsibility into decision-making processes. This recognition forms the foundation for implementing the standard's broader principles.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Core Subjects of Social Responsibility -- Clause 6 of ISO 26000:2010 outlines the core subjects of social responsibility, identifying seven priority areas such as human rights, labour practices, the environment, fair operating practices, consumer issues, community involvement and development, and organisational governance, guiding organisations in integrating responsible practices across their operations.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Guidance on Integrating SR -- Clause 7 of ISO 26000:2010 offers practical guidance for embedding social responsibility into an organization's policies, strategies and operational processes. It explains how to align governance structures, stakeholder engagement and core business activities with socially responsible values, enabling systematic integration of SR throughout the entity.
