[ext_authority] Competent Authorities (Art 11-12) -- The entry refers to the sections on competent authorities established by Directive (EU) 2019/1937, which set out the responsibilities of national and EU bodies designated to receive whistleblower reports, ensure confidentiality, and safeguard the rights of persons reporting breaches of Union law.
[ext_channel] Establishment of External Reporting Channels (Art 11) -- The entry explains that Article 11 of the EU Whistleblower Directive obliges Member States and covered entities to set up independent external reporting channels, allowing persons to disclose breaches of Union law confidentially, with appropriate safeguards, feedback and follow-up procedures.
[external] External Reporting Channels -- The entry identifies external reporting channels that organisations must provide for individuals to report suspected breaches of Union law under Directive (EU) 2019/1937. These channels enable whistleblowers to submit information outside the internal structure, often through independent bodies, ensuring confidentiality, protection from retaliation and compliance with EU-wide whistleblowing standards.
[int_channel] Establishment of Internal Reporting Channels (Art 8) -- The entry explains that under Article 8 of Directive (EU) 2019/1937 organisations must create internal reporting channels that are easily accessible, confidential and capable of receiving reports on breaches of Union law, with procedures for handling the reports, informing the whistle-blower of progress and protecting them from any retaliation.
[internal] Internal Reporting Channels -- The entry outlines the internal reporting channels required by EU Directive 2019/1937, which protects whistle-blowers who disclose violations of Union law. It details how organisations must establish secure, confidential procedures for employees to report concerns internally before any external disclosure.
[int_followup] Obligation to Follow Up (Art 9(1)(f)) -- The "Obligation to Follow Up" article obliges entities that receive whistleblowing reports under Directive 2019/1937 to promptly assess the information, take appropriate investigative or remedial measures, and keep the complainant informed of progress while safeguarding the confidentiality of the report and the reporter. It ensures that reported breaches of Union law are actively pursued rather than merely recorded.
[int_procedure] Procedure for Internal Reporting (Art 9) -- The entry refers to Article 9 of the EU Whistleblower Protection Directive (2019/1937), which sets out the internal reporting procedure that organisations must establish for workers to report suspected breaches of Union law. It requires a safe, confidential channel, a clear timeline for the employer's response, and safeguards against retaliation while the matter is examined before any external disclosure.
[material_scope] Material Scope (public procurement, financial services, environment, etc.) -- The material scope sets out the areas of Union law that may be reported under Directive 2019/1937, covering breaches in public procurement, financial services, environmental protection, consumer safety, public health, transport safety and other sectors governed by EU legislation.
[penalties] Penalties (Art 23-25) -- The penalties section (Articles 23-25) of Directive (EU) 2019/1937 establishes sanctions for persons who retaliate against whistle-blowers or breach confidentiality, and it also outlines liability for entities that fail to protect reporting persons. It provides for administrative fines, possible imprisonment and measures to repair damage caused by violations.
[personal_scope] Personal Scope (employees, contractors, shareholders, etc.) -- The personal scope of the EU Whistleblowing Directive sets out the categories of individuals who may receive protection, covering employees, contractors, shareholders and other persons with a contractual or functional link to a reporting entity. It ensures that anyone in these groups who discloses a breach of Union law benefits from the Directive's safeguards.
[prohibition] Prohibition of Retaliation (Art 19) -- Article 19 of the EU Whistleblowing Directive forbids any retaliation against persons who disclose breaches of Union law, requiring Member States to provide protective measures and effective remedies for those who report such violations.
[protection] Protection of Reporting Persons -- Directive (EU) 2019/1937 establishes common rules to protect individuals who report breaches of Union law, guaranteeing confidentiality, preventing retaliation and ensuring safe reporting channels across member states. It aims to encourage whistleblowing by providing legal safeguards and supporting mechanisms for reporting persons.
[pub_conditions] Conditions for Public Disclosure Protection (Art 15) -- Article 15 of the EU Whistleblowing Directive sets out the conditions under which a person who makes a public disclosure is eligible for protection, requiring that the disclosure be made in good faith, that the information concerns a breach of Union law, and that the reporter has exhausted internal reporting routes or that those routes are unavailable or ineffective.
[remedies] Remedies and Burden of Proof (Art 21-22) -- The entry summarises Articles 21-22 of the EU Whistleblower Protection Directive, which guarantee whistle-blowers effective remedies such as corrective action, compensation and protection against retaliation, and assign the burden of proof to the alleged violator, requiring them to demonstrate that the reported conduct was lawful.
[scope] Scope and Definitions -- The entry outlines the Directive's territorial and subject-matter reach, specifying that it applies to all EU member states and covers whistleblowing on breaches of Union law across public and private sectors. It also defines key terms such as "whistleblower," "reporting channel," and "protected disclosure," establishing the framework for consistent interpretation and implementation.
[support] Support Measures (Art 20) -- Article 20 of the EU Whistleblower Protection Directive sets out support measures for persons who report breaches of Union law, including the provision of legal, financial and psychological assistance, protection against retaliation and access to safe reporting channels, aimed at ensuring that whistle-blowers can report concerns without fear of adverse consequences.
