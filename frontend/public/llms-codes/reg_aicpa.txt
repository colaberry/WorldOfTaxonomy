[accounting] Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review (SSARS) -- The Statements on Standards for Accounting and Review (SSARS) are AICPA Professional Standards that set requirements for Certified Public Accountants conducting accounting and review engagements for non-public entities. They detail the procedures, documentation, and reporting formats CPAs must follow to ensure the reliability of financial statements and the credibility of review reports.
[ar_c_80] AR-C 80 - Compilation Engagements -- AR-C 80 sets the AICPA's professional standards for compilation engagements, outlining the accountant's role in assembling financial statements from client-provided information without providing assurance. It defines the procedures, reporting requirements, and ethical obligations required for compilations, and clarifies the differences between compilations, reviews, and audits. The guidance helps practitioners produce clear, compliant financial statements for non-public entities.
[ar_c_90] AR-C 90 - Review of Financial Statements -- AR-C 90 establishes the guidelines for accountants conducting a review of financial statements, detailing the procedures, reporting standards and responsibilities required to provide limited-assurance that the statements are free of material misstatement. It defines the scope of work, the nature of inquiries and analytical procedures, and the required elements of the review report.
[at_c_205] AT-C 205 - Examination Engagements -- AT-C 205 sets the professional standards for auditors performing examination engagements, detailing the required procedures to obtain sufficient evidence, document findings, and issue reports that provide reasonable assurance that financial statements are free from material misstatement. It also outlines ethical obligations, documentation requirements, and communication responsibilities with those charged with governance.
[at_c_320] AT-C 320 - Reporting on an Examination of Controls (SOC 1) -- AT-C 320 establishes the requirements for CPA firms to issue a SOC 1 report on a service organization's controls that affect user entities' financial reporting. It specifies the content, structure and auditor's opinion, including a description of the controls, testing performed and results, to help users evaluate the reliability of outsourced financial processes.

[audit_attest] Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) -- The audit_attest entry refers to the Statements on Auditing Standards, a series of authoritative pronouncements issued by the Auditing Standards Board of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants. SAS provide detailed guidance on the performance, documentation, and reporting of audit and attest engagements, establishing the professional standards that CPAs must follow when evaluating financial statements and related information.
[consulting] Statements on Standards for Consulting Services (SSCS) -- The Statements on Standards for Consulting Services (SSCS) are the AICPA's authoritative guidelines that define professional standards for accountants providing consulting engagements. They address engagement planning, execution, documentation, reporting and quality-control requirements to ensure consistent, ethical and competent consulting practice.
[et_1] ET Section 1 - Conceptual Framework for Members -- ET Section 1 outlines the conceptual framework that guides AICPA members in professional decision-making, defining the fundamental principles, objectives, and concepts underlying ethical and technical standards. It establishes the basis for interpreting and applying the Institute's standards, ensuring consistency and integrity across the profession.
[et_confidentiality] Confidentiality (ET 1.700) -- The ET_confidentiality entry (ET 1.700) in the AICPA Professional Standards outlines a CPA's duty to safeguard client information, specifying that confidentiality must be maintained unless authorized disclosure is required by law, client consent, or a professional obligation.
[ethics] Code of Professional Conduct -- The AICPA Code of Professional Conduct is the ethics component of the institute's Professional Standards, establishing the fundamental principles and rules that guide certified public accountants in delivering services with integrity, objectivity, independence, confidentiality and due care, and outlining the responsibilities they owe to clients, employers, the public and the profession.
[et_independence] Independence Rule (ET 1.200) -- The Independence Rule (ET 1.200) is a section of the AICPA Professional Standards that sets the independence requirements for CPAs performing attestation and other assurance services. It requires practitioners to be independent in both fact and appearance, outlines prohibited financial relationships, business interests, and personal ties that could impair objectivity, and provides guidance on assessing and maintaining independence throughout an engagement.
[et_integrity] Integrity and Objectivity (ET 1.100) -- The ET 1.100 standard defines the fundamental requirement that CPAs must act with integrity and maintain objectivity in all professional engagements, prohibiting misleading statements and ensuring unbiased judgment. It establishes expectations for independence, avoidance of conflicts of interest, and consistent ethical behavior to protect public trust in the accounting profession.
[quality] Quality Management Standards -- The "quality" entry in the AICPA Professional Standards outlines the requirements for a firm's system of quality control, covering leadership responsibilities, ethical behavior, client acceptance, engagement performance, monitoring and documentation. It directs certified public accountants to maintain consistent, high-quality services in compliance with professional standards.
[sas_134] SAS 134 - Auditor Reporting and Amendments -- SAS 134, issued by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants, outlines the auditor's reporting responsibilities and the procedures for amending audit reports. It clarifies when and how an auditor must modify an opinion, disclose changes, and communicate significant matters to users of the financial statements.

[sas_146] SAS 146 - Quality Management for an Engagement -- SAS 146 is an AICPA professional standard that establishes quality-management requirements for CPA engagements, mandating firms to create policies, procedures and monitoring systems that ensure compliance with professional standards and enhance service quality. It delineates the responsibilities of engagement partners, quality-control reviewers and specifies documentation needed to demonstrate effective quality management.
[sqms_1] SQMS 1 - A Firm's System of Quality Management -- SQMS 1 outlines the requirement for a CPA firm to develop and maintain a system of quality management that governs its overall responsibilities, policies, procedures and monitoring processes to ensure consistent compliance with professional standards and continual improvement of service quality.
[sqms_2] SQMS 2 - Engagement Quality Reviews -- The SQMS 2 standard sets requirements for engagement quality reviews, requiring a qualified, independent reviewer to evaluate a CPA firm's audit, review, or compilation engagement before the final report is issued. The review must assess compliance with relevant professional standards, the adequacy of the engagement team's work, and the appropriateness of the conclusions reached, and it must be documented in a written report.
[ssae_18] SSAE 18 - Attestation Standards: Clarification and Recodification -- SSAE 18 is the AICPA's clarified and recodified attestation standard that supersedes previously issued SSAE 15 and related guidance. It establishes unified criteria for service-organization reporting, aligns U.S. practice with the international ISAE 3402 framework, and details the requirements for issuing and reviewing SOC reports covering controls related to financial reporting and other user-entity concerns.
[ssars_25] SSARS 25 - Materiality in a Review -- SSARS 25 sets the materiality thresholds and considerations auditors must apply when performing a review of financial statements, guiding the degree of testing and the nature of conclusions needed to determine whether the statements are fairly presented in all material respects.
