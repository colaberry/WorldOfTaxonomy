[admin_proceedings] Administrative Proceedings -- The admin_proceedings section of the SEC's key regulations (17 CFR) sets out the procedures for administrative actions taken by the Commission, including the filing of complaints, conduct of hearings, and issuance of orders or sanctions. It governs how the SEC enforces securities laws outside of the federal courts.
[beneficial_own] Beneficial Ownership Reporting (13d, 13f, 13g) -- Beneficial ownership reporting requires individuals or entities that acquire or hold a specified share of a public company's securities to file disclosures with the SEC. Form 13D applies to owners of more than 5 percent who intend to influence the issuer, while Form 13G is a shorter filing for passive investors. Form 13F obligates institutional managers with at least $100 million in equity securities to report their quarterly holdings.
[civil_actions] Civil Actions in Federal Court -- The civil_actions entry details the procedures for initiating and defending civil lawsuits in federal court under the Securities and Exchange Commission's regulations, covering jurisdiction, standing, permissible remedies, and the roles of the SEC as plaintiff or defendant.
[disclosure] Disclosure Regulations -- The Disclosure Regulations under 17 CFR set the reporting requirements that public companies, brokerage firms and investment advisers must follow when providing material information to investors, including periodic reports, prospectuses and other filings with the SEC. They aim to ensure transparency, protect investors and maintain market integrity
[enforcement_sec] Enforcement Framework -- The enforcement_sec entry details the SEC's enforcement framework, describing the authority, procedures, and penalties used when securities laws are violated. It outlines how the Commission conducts investigations, files civil actions, and imposes administrative sanctions, and clarifies the roles of staff and the Commission in making enforcement decisions.
[form_10k] Form 10-K Annual Report Requirements -- Form 10-K is the annual report the SEC requires from publicly traded companies
[form_10q] Form 10-Q Quarterly Report Requirements -- Form 10-Q is a quarterly report required by the Securities and Exchange Commission for publicly listed companies. It contains unaudited financial statements, management's discussion of results, and disclosure of material events for each fiscal quarter, allowing investors to evaluate the company's ongoing performance and condition.
[form_8k] Form 8-K Current Report Requirements -- Form 8-K is the SEC's current report that companies must file within four business days after a material event such as a merger, asset sale, change in control, or departure of a director. The filing provides investors with timely disclosure of significant corporate developments and ensures compliance with the SEC's 17 CFR reporting requirements.
[governance] Corporate Governance Regulations -- The "governance" entry in the SEC's Key Regulations (17 CFR) sets out the rules public companies must follow to maintain effective corporate governance, including board structure, fiduciary duties, disclosure obligations and shareholder-rights protections. These regulations are designed to enhance transparency, accountability and investor confidence in U.S. securities markets.
[insider_trading] Insider Trading Rules (Section 16, Rule 10b5-1) -- The SEC's insider trading rules under Section 16 and Rule 10b5-1 prohibit corporate insiders and anyone acting on material non-public information from buying or selling securities, and require timely disclosure of transactions. Violations can trigger civil penalties, disgorgement, and criminal prosecution.
[penalties] Disgorgement and Civil Monetary Penalties -- The entry outlines the SEC's authority to require disgorgement of ill-gotten profits and to impose civil monetary penalties for violations of securities laws, detailing the procedures, calculation methods, and enforcement mechanisms stipulated in 17 CFR.
[proxy_rules] Proxy Rules (14a Series) -- The SEC's proxy rules in the 14a series set the legal framework for shareholder voting and proxy solicitations by public companies. They prescribe the content, timing, and format of proxy statements, require disclosure of material information, and establish procedures to ensure fair and transparent proxy voting, including anti-fraud safeguards.
[reg_a] Regulation A - Small Offerings (17 CFR 230.251-263) -- Regulation A, codified at 17 CFR 230.251-263, establishes a simplified registration framework for small public offerings, permitting issuers to raise up to $75 million in a 12-month period with reduced disclosure and reporting obligations. It creates two tiers of offerings-Tier 1 (up to $20 million) and Tier 2 (up to $75 million)-each with distinct eligibility, investor limits, and ongoing compliance requirements.
[reg_ats] Regulation ATS - Alternative Trading Systems (17 CFR 242.300-303) -- Regulation ATS, found in 17 CFR 242.300-303, sets the SEC's rules for alternative trading systems that match buyers and sellers outside traditional exchanges. It defines an ATS, requires registration, ongoing reporting, and establishes standards for fair access, market integrity and investor protection.
[reg_cf] Regulation Crowdfunding (17 CFR 227) -- Regulation Crowdfunding, identified by the code reg_cf in the SEC's 17 CFR, sets the framework for private companies to raise funds from non-accredited investors through registered online platforms. It establishes investment limits, mandatory disclosure standards, and requires the crowdfunding portal to be SEC-registered and FINRA-compliant.
[reg_d] Regulation D - Private Placements (17 CFR 230.500-508) -- Regulation D, found in 17 CFR §§230.500-508, sets the rules under which companies may offer and sell securities without registering them with the SEC by using private-placement exemptions. It defines the criteria for accredited investors, limits on offering amounts, and the filing requirements for Form D. The regulation facilitates capital raising while protecting investors
[reg_fd] Regulation FD - Fair Disclosure (17 CFR 243) -- Regulation FD, found in 17 CFR 243, requires public companies to disclose material information to all investors at the same time, preventing selective sharing with analysts or insiders. The rule promotes market fairness and transparency by mandating prompt, broad dissemination of significant corporate news.
[reg_g] Regulation G - Non-GAAP Financial Measures (17 CFR 244) -- Regulation G requires public companies that present non-GAAP financial measures in SEC filings to disclose those measures, provide a reconciliation to the most directly comparable GAAP measures and explain why they are useful to investors. The rule aims to enhance transparency and prevent misleading financial presentations.
[registration] Registration and Offering Regulations -- The SEC's registration and offering regulations under 17 CFR set the mandatory filing and disclosure requirements for companies seeking to sell securities to the public. They outline the registration statement process, prospectus contents, and exemptions, ensuring investor protection and market transparency. These rules govern how offerings are registered, reviewed, and approved before securities can be issued.

[reg_s] Regulation S - Offshore Offerings (17 CFR 230.901-905) -- Regulation S, found in 17 CFR part 230 sections 901-905, sets out the SEC's exemption for offshore securities offerings. It permits issuers to sell securities to non-U.S. persons without registration, provided the offer is made outside the United States and complies with specific location, investor and resale restrictions.
[reg_sci] Regulation SCI - Systems Compliance and Integrity -- Regulation SCI, issued under 17 CFR, sets mandatory standards for the design, testing, operation and security of the information technology systems that support securities trading and reporting. It requires covered entities to implement robust controls, conduct regular audits, and file compliance reports with the SEC to ensure system integrity and protect market stability.
[reg_sho] Regulation SHO - Short Sales (17 CFR 242.200-204) -- Regulation SHO, codified at 17 CFR 242.200-204, establishes the rules that govern short selling of securities, including the mandatory locate requirement, the close-out of fail-to-deliver positions, and related reporting obligations, all intended to promote market integrity and curb abusive short-selling practices.
[reg_sk] Regulation S-K - Non-Financial Statement Disclosures (17 CFR 229) -- Regulation S-K, found at 17 CFR 229, sets the mandatory non-financial statement disclosure requirements for public companies filing with the SEC, covering topics such as corporate governance, executive compensation, risk factors, and other qualitative information that must accompany financial reports. It standardizes the format and content of these disclosures to promote transparency and consistency across filings.
[reg_sx] Regulation S-X - Financial Statement Requirements (17 CFR 210) -- Regulation S-X (17 CFR 210) sets the detailed financial-statement and reporting requirements that public companies must follow when filing documents with the SEC, prescribing the form, content, and accounting principles for statements, footnotes, and supporting schedules. It also establishes the qualifications for auditors and the procedures for filing and revising financial information, ensuring consistency and transparency in securities disclosures.
[rule_10b5] Rule 10b-5 - Employment of Manipulative and Deceptive Devices -- Rule 10b-5, found in the SEC's 17 CFR, prohibits any person from using manipulative or deceptive devices in connection with the purchase or sale of securities, covering fraud, false statements, and omissions that mislead investors, and provides a civil cause of action for violations.
[trading] Trading and Market Regulations -- The SEC's Trading and Market Regulations in 17 CFR set the framework for how securities are bought, sold, and quoted on U.S. exchanges. They define permissible trading practices, enforce anti-manipulation rules, require reporting of trades, and establish standards for market transparency, broker-dealer conduct, and the operation of clearing and settlement systems.
[whistleblower] SEC Whistleblower Program (Rule 21F) -- The SEC Whistleblower Program, created under Rule 21F of the Securities Exchange Act regulations, encourages individuals to report securities law violations by offering monetary awards and protection from retaliation. It details who may qualify, how awards are calculated, and the procedures for submitting tips to the Commission.
[xbrl] Inline XBRL Requirements -- The SEC's Inline XBRL Requirements, codified in 17 CFR, require public companies to file financial statements using the Inline XBRL format that embeds XBRL data directly within HTML documents. This standard improves data accessibility and enables automated extraction for investors, analysts, and regulators.
