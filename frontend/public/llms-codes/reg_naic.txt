[advertising] Advertisements of Accident and Health Insurance (#570) -- The entry establishes model rules governing the content and presentation of accident and health insurance advertisements. It requires that ads be truthful, not misleading, and include essential disclosures such as coverage limits, exclusions, and premium information, aligning state regulations with consumer protection goals.
[cyber_notification] Cybersecurity Event Notification -- The NAIC Model Cybersecurity Event Notification requires insurers to promptly report qualified cyber incidents to state insurance departments, detailing the nature of the breach, the data affected, and any remedial actions taken. The rule aims to improve regulatory oversight and promote coordinated responses to cyber threats across the insurance industry.

[disclosure] Life Insurance Disclosure Model Regulation (#580) -- The NAIC Life Insurance Disclosure Model Regulation (#580) establishes standardized requirements for insurers to provide policyholders with clear, concise information about coverage terms, costs, benefits, and consumer protections. It aims to improve transparency, promote informed decision-making, and ensure consistent disclosure practices across states.
[guaranty] Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association Model Act (#520) -- The guaranty entry refers to the NAIC's Model Act that creates a state-run Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association to protect policyholders when an insurer becomes insolvent, outlining funding mechanisms, governance, and coverage limits for claims.
[holding_co] Insurance Holding Company System Model Act (#440) -- The Insurance Holding Company System Model Act (NAIC Model Law #440) provides a uniform regulatory framework for the organization, oversight and supervision of insurance holding company systems. It establishes criteria for registration, reporting, capital adequacy, corporate governance and enforcement to promote financial stability and protect policyholders.
[ids_model] Insurance Data Security Model Law (#668) -- The Insurance Data Security Model Law (#668) is a NAIC template that sets baseline cybersecurity and privacy requirements for insurers, mandating reasonable safeguards, risk assessments, breach notification, and oversight mechanisms. It is intended for adoption by state regulators to enhance protection of personal insurance data across jurisdictions.
[licensing] Licensing and Registration -- The NAIC licensing entry provides model statutes and regulations that standardize the qualifications, application procedures, fees, continuing-education requirements and renewal processes for insurance producers and agents, ensuring uniformity across states in how insurers and their representatives obtain and maintain licensure.
[market_conduct] Market Conduct and Consumer Protection -- The market_conduct entry outlines the NNAIC's model statutes and regulations aimed at ensuring fair, transparent practices by insurers in their dealings with consumers. It addresses issues such as advertising standards, sales practices, claim handling, dispute resolution, and safeguards to protect policyholders from deceptive or abusive behavior.
[own_risk] Own Risk and Solvency Assessment (ORSA) Model Act (#505) -- The Own Risk and Solvency Assessment (ORSA) Model Act (NAIC Model #505) provides a uniform statutory framework that requires insurers to conduct an internal, forward-looking evaluation of their risk profile, capital adequacy and solvency position. It guides states in adopting consistent regulations that promote proactive risk management and ensure that insurers maintain sufficient resources to meet obligations.
[privacy] Privacy of Consumer Financial Information (#672) -- The NAIC Model Law on Privacy of Consumer Financial Information (#672) establishes standards for insurers to protect the confidentiality and security of customers' financial data. It requires reasonable safeguards, limits disclosures to authorized parties, and obligates insurers to obtain consent before sharing personal financial information.
[producer_lic] Producer Licensing Model Act (#218) -- The Producer Licensing Model Act (NAIC Model Law #218) establishes uniform standards for the licensing and regulation of insurance producers across states. It defines eligibility criteria, required examinations, continuing-education obligations, and disciplinary processes to promote consumer protection and a consistent regulatory framework.
[product_reg] Product Regulation -- The NAIC product_reg entry summarizes the association's model laws and regulations governing insurance product oversight. It outlines requirements for filing, approval, valuation and ongoing monitoring of insurance contracts to protect consumers and promote market stability. States may adopt these standards to harmonize product regulation across jurisdictions.
[rate_review] Rate and Form Filing Requirements -- The NAIC Model Law on rate and form filing requirements sets out uniform standards for insurers to submit proposed premiums, rating methodologies and policy language to state regulators for review and approval. It aims to promote consistency, transparency and consumer protection by ensuring rates are not excessive, inadequate or unfairly discriminatory before they are used in the market.
[rbc] Risk-Based Capital (RBC) for Insurers Model Act (#312) -- The NAIC Model Risk-Based Capital (RBC) Act (#312) provides a uniform framework for setting capital requirements that reflect the size, risk profile and financial condition of insurers. It establishes quantitative standards and supervisory tools to ensure insurers hold sufficient capital to support their underwriting and investment risks, and serves as a template for states adopting or adapting RBC regulations.
[reinsurance] Credit for Reinsurance Model Law (#785) -- The Credit for Reinsurance Model Law (NAIC Model #785) establishes uniform provisions allowing insurers to receive regulatory credit for transferring risk through reinsurance, specifying the criteria for eligibility, documentation, and accounting treatment. It aims to promote consistent underwriting practices and facilitate the use of reinsurance as a risk-management tool across jurisdictions.
[solvency] Solvency and Financial Regulation -- The NAIC's "solvency" entry covers model statutes and regulations that set capital and reserve requirements, risk-based supervision standards, and financial reporting rules to ensure insurers remain financially sound and able to meet policyholder obligations. It provides a uniform framework for states to assess and enforce insurers' financial health and governance practices.
[standard_val] Standard Valuation Law (#820) -- The Standard Valuation Law (Model #820) is a template statute drafted by the National Association of Insurance Commissioners to set uniform rules for valuing insurance company assets and liabilities. It guides states in adopting consistent valuation methods, reporting requirements, and solvency oversight, promoting comparability across jurisdictions.
[unfair_claims] Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (#900) -- The Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (model law #900) is a NAIC model regulation that establishes standards for insurers on handling, investigating, and paying claims, prohibiting deceptive or unfair practices. It requires timely communication, good-faith conduct, and provides claimants with basic procedural protections.
[unfair_trade] Unfair Trade Practices Act (#880) -- The Unfair Trade Practices Act (#880) is a NAIC model law that defines prohibited insurance practices such as misrepresentation, false advertising, and unfair discrimination, and establishes standards for enforcement and consumer protection. It provides a framework for states to adopt uniform rules that promote fair competition and safeguard policyholders from deceptive conduct.
[uniform_cert] Uniform Certificate of Authority Application -- The Uniform Certificate of Authority Application is a standardized form developed by the National Association of Insurance Commissioners for insurers seeking legal authority to operate in a state. It consolidates required financial, ownership and compliance information, facilitating consistent review by state regulators and streamlining the licensing process across jurisdictions.
