[ST] STCC Groups -- ST denotes the STCC Groups category within the Standard Transportation Commodity Code system, which groups related commodity codes for streamlined reporting and analysis in freight transportation. These groups organize commodities by common characteristics to facilitate billing, regulatory compliance, and data aggregation across carriers and shippers.
[ST.01] Farm Products -- ST.01 identifies farm products as a transportation commodity category, covering raw agricultural goods such as crops, livestock and produce shipped from farms to markets. This code is used by carriers and logistics providers to classify and bill shipments of agricultural items.
[ST.08] Forest Products -- ST.08 designates forest products, including lumber, plywood, pulp, paper, and other wood-based materials, for transportation reporting and billing. The code standardizes how carriers, shippers, and regulators classify, route, and track these commodities across road, rail, and water modes.
[ST.10] Metallic Ores -- ST.10 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates metallic ores, encompassing raw mineral aggregates such as iron, copper, gold, silver, bauxite and other naturally occurring metal-bearing rocks that are extracted for industrial processing and shipment.
[ST.11] Coal -- ST.11 identifies coal as a standard transportation commodity, covering raw or processed coal shipped by rail, truck, barge or other modes. The code is used in freight documentation, carrier billing and regulatory reporting to classify shipments of solid fossil fuel.
[ST.13] Crude Petroleum -- ST.13 identifies crude petroleum, the unrefined liquid hydrocarbon extracted from the earth and transported in bulk by pipeline, rail, tanker or truck for downstream refining into fuels and other petroleum products. It covers all grades of raw oil before any processing or blending.
[ST.14] Non-Metallic Minerals -- ST.14 groups non-metallic mineral commodities such as sand, gravel, limestone, gypsum, clay and similar raw materials that are transported for construction, manufacturing or industrial use. These items are classified together for freight billing, routing and regulatory reporting under the Standard Transportation Commodity Code system.
[ST.19] Ordnance or Accessories -- ST.19 classifies all ordnance and related accessories, including weapons, ammunition, missiles, explosive devices and the parts, mounts, safeties and other equipment needed to operate, maintain or secure them during transportation. This category is used to identify and regulate the movement of military or law-enforcement fire-power and its supporting components.
[ST.20] Food or Kindred Products -- ST.20 classifies shipments primarily consisting of edible items and related agricultural products, including fresh, frozen, processed foods, beverages and animal feed. It is used to identify cargo that is intended for consumption or further food-processing, distinguishing it from industrial, hazardous, or non-food commodities for transport reporting and regulation.
[ST.24] Lumber or Wood Products -- ST.24 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates lumber and wood products, encompassing rough and finished timber, dimensional lumber, wood panels, plywood, veneer and other processed wood items used in construction, manufacturing and related industries.
[ST.26] Pulp, Paper or Allied Products -- ST.26 designates the category of pulp, paper and allied products, including manufactured paper, cardboard, newsprint, tissue, and related specialty papers, as well as raw pulp and processed fibers used in paper manufacturing. It groups these items for transportation and logistics purposes.
[ST.28] Chemicals or Allied Products -- ST.28 designates the transportation of chemicals and allied products, encompassing industrial chemicals, hazardous substances, fertilizers, pesticides, and related materials that require special handling, packaging, and regulatory compliance during shipment. This classification helps carriers and regulators identify and manage the safety and environmental requirements associated with moving these goods.
[ST.29] Petroleum or Coal Products -- ST.29 identifies petroleum and coal commodities within the Standard Transportation Commodity Code system, covering refined fuels, lubricants, bitumen, and solid coal used for energy and industrial purposes. It is used by carriers and logistic providers to classify, track, and bill shipments of these energy products across transport modes.
[ST.32] Clay, Concrete, Glass, Stone -- ST.32 designates bulk construction and manufacturing materials including clay, concrete, glass and stone, which are commonly shipped in large, non-hazardous loads such as bulk containers, pallets or rolls. These commodities are handled as standard freight for building and industrial projects.
[ST.33] Primary Metal Products -- ST.33 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates primary metal products, encompassing raw metal forms such as ingots, billets, bars, sheets and plates of steel, aluminum, copper and other base metals that have not been fabricated into finished goods. The code is used by carriers and shippers to identify and track shipments of these unprocessed metal commodities.
[ST.34] Fabricated Metal Products -- ST.34 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code identifies fabricated metal products, covering items such as structural steel, metal frames, prefabricated components and other processed metal goods used in construction and manufacturing. These commodities are generally shipped in bulk and require specific handling guidelines for weight, securing and safety.
[ST.35] Machinery -- ST.35 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates machinery, encompassing industrial equipment, engines, and related components shipped for manufacturing, construction, agricultural, or other commercial purposes. The code helps carriers and logistics firms classify, handle, and bill transportation of heavy or specialized machinery.

[ST.42] Empty Containers -- ST.42 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates empty containers, referring to reusable shipping or transport containers that are not carrying cargo, such as empty pallets, drums, boxes or intermodal units awaiting loading or return to a supplier.
[ST.43] Mail or Contract Traffic -- ST.43 denotes transportation of mail and contract traffic, covering items moved under official postal agreements or private contracts for regular or special shipments. The code applies to shipments that are not commercial freight but are handled as part of postal services or contractual arrangements between shippers and carriers.
[ST.44] Freight All Kinds (FAK) -- ST.44 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code denotes Freight All Kinds, a pricing category that aggregates all cargo types under a single rate structure, simplifying billing and rate negotiations for shippers handling diverse shipments.
[ST.46] Misc. Mixed Shipments -- ST.46 in the Standard Transportation Commodity Code designates miscellaneous mixed shipments, used for consolidated freight that combines several different commodities not otherwise separately classified. It applies to loadings where the items are packaged together for transport but lack a specific commodity code, facilitating routing and billing.
[ST.48] Hazardous Materials -- ST.48 designates shipments of hazardous materials, covering any substances that present risks to health, safety, or property during transport. The code is used in logistics and regulatory reporting to ensure proper handling, packaging, labeling and compliance with safety regulations.
[ST.49] Hazardous Waste -- ST.49 designates hazardous waste, encompassing any material classified as dangerous under environmental and transportation regulations and requiring special packaging, labeling, documentation, and handling procedures for safe movement by road, rail, air, or water. It is used by carriers and shippers to identify regulated waste and ensure compliance with hazardous-material transport rules.
