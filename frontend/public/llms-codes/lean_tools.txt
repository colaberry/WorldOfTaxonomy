[LT] Lean Tools -- Lean Tools are a set of techniques used to identify and eliminate waste, improve flow, and increase value in production processes. They include methods such as 5S, Kaizen, value-stream mapping and poka-yoke, each aimed at streamlining operations and fostering continuous improvement.
[LT.01] 5S (Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain) -- LT.01 designates the 5S system, a lean manufacturing tool that organizes the workplace through five steps-Sort, Set in order, Shine, Standardize, and Sustain. It seeks to reduce waste, improve safety, and boost productivity by creating and maintaining a clean, orderly environment.

[LT.03] Kanban -- Kanban is a visual signal system used in lean manufacturing to control production flow and inventory levels. It employs cards or electronic signals to trigger replenishment only when demand occurs, enabling just-in-time delivery and reducing waste.
[LT.04] Kaizen (Continuous Improvement) -- Kaizen, identified as LT.04, is a lean-manufacturing tool that promotes continuous, incremental improvement across all organizational levels. It relies on employee participation to systematically eliminate waste, enhance processes, and sustain long-term efficiency gains.
[LT.05] Poka-Yoke (Error Proofing) -- Poka-Yoke, coded LT.05, refers to error-proofing techniques used in lean manufacturing to prevent defects by designing processes, fixtures, or devices that make incorrect actions impossible or immediately detectable. It aims to improve quality, reduce rework, and support continuous improvement.
[LT.06] Just-in-Time (JIT) -- Just-in-Time (JIT) is a lean manufacturing tool that aligns production schedules directly with customer demand, aiming to minimize inventory levels, shorten lead times, and improve overall flow efficiency. It depends on reliable suppliers, accurate forecasting, and pull-based scheduling to deliver components exactly when needed.
[LT.07] Andon (Visual Signal) -- Andon is a visual signaling system in lean manufacturing that displays the real-time status of production equipment or workstations, typically using lights, boards, or displays. When a problem occurs, the signal alerts operators and supervisors, prompting immediate response to address issues and minimize downtime.
[LT.08] Gemba Walk -- LT.08 Gemba Walk is a Lean Manufacturing tool that involves managers and supervisors regularly visiting the shop floor to observe actual work, interact directly with operators, and spot waste or safety issues. By seeing processes in real time, it drives continuous improvement and reinforces a culture of problem-solving.
[LT.09] Heijunka (Level Loading) -- Heijunka, also known as level loading, is a lean scheduling method that spreads production volume and mix evenly across each work period, minimizing peaks and valleys in workflow. By aligning output to takt time, it reduces inventory, eases bottlenecks and improves the system's ability to respond quickly to demand fluctuations.
[LT.10] SMED (Quick Changeover) -- SMED (Single-Minute Exchange of Die) is a lean manufacturing tool that reduces setup and changeover times to under ten minutes, enabling faster production runs and smaller batch sizes. It systematically separates internal and external setup tasks, streamlines operations, and lowers lead times.
[LT.11] Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) -- Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) is a systematic approach that integrates equipment maintenance into daily production processes to maximize overall equipment effectiveness. It engages operators in routine inspections and preventive tasks, aiming to reduce downtime, defects, and accidents while fostering continuous improvement and ownership across the workforce.
[LT.12] A3 Problem Solving -- LT.12 A3 Problem Solving is a concise, single-page report that guides teams through defining an issue, analyzing root causes, developing countermeasures, and planning implementation. The format encourages visual communication and a disciplined, step-by-step approach, making it a core tool for continuous improvement in lean manufacturing.
[LT.13] Hoshin Kanri (Policy Deployment) -- Hoshin Kanri, also known as policy deployment, is a strategic planning approach that aligns an organization's long-term vision with day-to-day operations. It translates top-level objectives into specific, measurable targets, cascades them through all levels, and ensures continuous review and adjustment.
[LT.14] Takt Time -- Takt time is the rhythm at which a product must be completed to satisfy customer demand, calculated by dividing the available production time by the required quantity. It serves as a pacing metric that helps synchronize each work cell, balance workflow, and identify bottlenecks in lean manufacturing systems.
