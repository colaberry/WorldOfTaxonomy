[AL] EU Allergens -- The entry AL in the EU 14 Major Food Allergens listing identifies the set of allergens that must be highlighted on packaged foods under EU labeling law. It covers the fourteen priority allergens-cereals containing gluten, crustaceans, eggs, fish, lupin, milk, molluscs, mustard, nuts, peanuts, sesame, soybeans, sulphur dioxide and other sulfiting agents, and celery.
[AL.01] Cereals Containing Gluten -- Cereals containing gluten-such as wheat, rye, barley, oats and their cross-contaminated products-are listed as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens. They must be clearly identified on ingredient labels because they can trigger reactions in people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.
[AL.02] Crustaceans -- AL.02 designates crustaceans, one of the EU's 14 major food allergens, encompassing shrimp, crab, lobster and related shellfish. Products containing these ingredients must be clearly labelled to protect consumers with crustacean-related allergic reactions.
[AL.03] Eggs -- Eggs are listed as allergen code AL.03 in the EU's set of 14 major food allergens, requiring mandatory labeling whenever egg protein is present in a food product. This includes both egg white and yolk proteins, which can trigger allergic reactions ranging from mild skin symptoms to severe anaphylaxis. The classification helps consumers identify and avoid foods containing eggs.
[AL.04] Fish -- AL.04 identifies fish as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens, requiring mandatory labeling on pre-packed foods to alert consumers with fish-related allergy. It covers muscle tissue from finned fish and related products, and is regulated under EU Regulation 1169/2011 for allergen information.
[AL.05] Peanuts -- AL.05 designates peanuts as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens, mandating explicit labeling on pre-packed foods. Peanut proteins can provoke severe IgE-mediated reactions, including anaphylaxis, in sensitised individuals.
[AL.06] Soybeans -- AL.06 identifies soybeans as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens. Soybean proteins can trigger allergic reactions and must be clearly indicated on ingredient lists under EU food-labeling regulations.
[AL.07] Milk (Lactose) -- AL.07 Milk (Lactose) identifies lactose, the main carbohydrate in cow's milk, as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens. Products containing lactose must be clearly labeled to inform consumers with lactose intolerance or milk allergy, ensuring they can avoid potential adverse reactions.
[AL.08] Tree Nuts -- AL.08 designates tree nuts as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens. It includes nuts harvested from trees-such as almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pistachios and Brazil nuts-known to provoke allergic reactions. Foods containing any of these nuts must be clearly labeled under EU allergen-labelling rules.
[AL.09] Celery -- Celery is one of the 14 major allergens mandated for labeling in the EU; it can cause IgE-mediated reactions such as urticaria, respiratory symptoms and anaphylaxis, especially in people sensitized to pollen or other vegetables, and must be declared on pre-packaged foods.
[AL.10] Mustard -- Mustard (code AL.10) is one of the EU's 14 major food allergens that must be declared on pre-packed foods. It can trigger IgE-mediated reactions ranging from mild skin irritation to severe anaphylaxis, especially in individuals sensitised to mustard seed proteins. The requirement applies to all food items containing mustard in any form, including powders, sauces and extracts.
[AL.11] Sesame Seeds -- The EU 14 Major Food Allergens list designates sesame seeds (code AL.11) as one of the mandatory allergens that must be declared on food packaging. Sesame can trigger severe reactions in sensitive individuals and may appear as an ingredient or through cross-contamination in processed foods.
[AL.12] Sulphites (SO2 > 10mg/kg) -- AL.12 records sulphites as one of the EU's fourteen major food allergens, requiring labelling when present at concentrations exceeding ten milligrams per kilogram. This threshold helps protect consumers who are sensitive to sulphite additives.
[AL.13] Lupin -- Lupin (Lupinus albus) is a legume protein listed as AL.13 among the EU's 14 major food allergens. It is used in flour, bakery products and protein isolates, and can trigger IgE-mediated reactions similar to peanut allergy, so its presence must be declared on pre-packed foods.
[AL.14] Molluscs -- AL.14 designates molluscs as one of the EU's 14 major food allergens. Molluscs, including species such as shrimp, crab, lobster and squid, can provoke allergic reactions in susceptible people and must be clearly labelled on food products.
