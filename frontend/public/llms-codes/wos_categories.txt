[WoS] WoS Research Areas -- The WoS Research Areas is a taxonomy employed by Clarivate's Web of Science to assign scholarly works to disciplinary fields for indexing, citation analysis, and targeted searching. It groups journals and articles into broad domains such as physical sciences, life sciences, social sciences, and humanities, providing a standardized framework for research evaluation.
[WoS.01] Life Sciences and Biomedicine -- WoS.01 identifies the Life Sciences and Biomedicine research area in the Web of Science classification. It groups journals and articles covering biology, medical research, clinical sciences and related interdisciplinary fields, providing a broad category for indexing and analysis.
[WoS.02] Physical Sciences -- WoS.02 denotes the Physical Sciences research area in the Web of Science classification. It groups journals and articles covering disciplines such as physics, chemistry, astronomy, earth and environmental sciences, and related applied fields. The category is used for indexing and analytics across the natural and engineering sciences.
[WoS.03] Technology -- WoS.03 Technology encompasses research that investigates the creation, implementation, and societal impact of tools, systems, and processes, spanning fields such as engineering, computer science, information technology, and applied sciences, and includes studies on innovation, design, and technology management.
[WoS.04] Social Sciences -- The Social Sciences research area in the Web of Science groups journals and publications that examine human behavior, societies, and institutions, encompassing fields such as anthropology, economics, political science, psychology, sociology, and education. It is used to filter and analyze scholarly output that addresses social structures, interactions, and policy-related topics.
[WoS.05] Arts and Humanities -- WoS.05 identifies the Arts and Humanities research area in the Web of Science classification. It aggregates journals, articles and other scholarly outputs covering disciplines such as literature, philosophy, history, visual and performing arts, and cultural studies, enabling subject-specific indexing and analysis.

[WoS.LS1] Agriculture -- WoS.LS1 identifies the Agriculture research area within the Web of Science classification. It encompasses scholarly work on crop and livestock production, agronomy, horticulture, agricultural economics, food security, and related technologies and environmental management.
[WoS.LS2] Biochemistry and Molecular Biology -- WoS.LS2 designates the Web of Science research area of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, encompassing studies of the chemical reactions and molecular structures that underlie cellular processes, gene expression, protein function, and metabolic pathways, and supporting interdisciplinary work that links chemistry with genetics and cell biology.
[WoS.LS3] Ecology -- WoS.LS3 denotes the Web of Science research area "Ecology," which encompasses scientific work on the relationships between organisms and their physical surroundings, the structure and function of ecosystems, biodiversity, and environmental processes that shape natural habitats.
[WoS.LS4] Neuroscience -- WoS.LS4 designates the Web of Science research area "Neuroscience," encompassing the scientific study of the nervous system from molecular and cellular mechanisms to cognitive, behavioral, and clinical aspects. It includes disciplines such as neurobiology, neuropsychology, neuroimaging, and related biomedical research.
[WoS.LS5] Pharmacology -- The Web of Science research area designated WoS.LS5 - Pharmacology covers studies of drug action and interaction at molecular, cellular, and organismal levels, including the discovery, development, and therapeutic use of chemical agents. It spans experimental, clinical, and translational research on mechanisms, efficacy, safety, and pharmacokinetics.

It aggregates journal titles and articles that advance knowledge of molecular structures, chemical processes, and applications in industry, medicine, and the environment.
