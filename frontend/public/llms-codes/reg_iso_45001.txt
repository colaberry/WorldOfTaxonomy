[10_1] 10.1 General -- Clause 10.1 of ISO 45001:2018 sets out the general requirements for an occupational health and safety management system, mandating that an organization establish, implement, maintain and continually improve the system to meet the standard's objectives and legal obligations. It emphasizes the need for documented processes, resource allocation and performance evaluation to ensure effective risk control and worker safety.
[10_2] 10.2 Incident, nonconformity and corrective action -- Entry 10.2 requires organizations to implement a systematic process for identifying and reporting occupational incidents and nonconformities, investigating their causes, and taking corrective action to eliminate hazards and prevent recurrence, thereby supporting continual improvement of the occupational health and safety management system.
[10_3] 10.3 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.3 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to establish, implement and maintain processes for continual improvement of its occupational health and safety management system. It calls for identifying improvement opportunities, planning and executing changes, and regularly reviewing their effectiveness to enhance overall OHS performance. This systematic approach ensures that safety outcomes are progressively enhanced over time.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- The clause requires the organization to identify internal and external factors that influence its occupational health and safety objectives, such as legal, technological, cultural and economic conditions. It also mandates considering the needs and expectations of workers, suppliers, regulators and other interested parties to define the scope and direction of the OHS management system.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of workers and other interested parties -- Clause 4.2 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to identify workers and other interested parties, determine their relevant needs and expectations, and evaluate how these influence the occupational health and safety management system. By understanding stakeholder requirements, the organization can ensure the system addresses legal, contractual, and other obligations that affect health and safety performance.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the OH&S management system -- Clause 4.3 requires an organization to define the boundaries and applicability of its OH&S management system, taking into account internal and external issues, interested parties, and relevant legal requirements. The determined scope specifies the sites, processes, activities and products covered, ensuring the system is relevant and effective.

[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to actively lead and demonstrate commitment to the occupational health and safety management system, ensuring the OH&S policy is established, communicated and aligned with strategic direction, providing necessary resources, assigning responsibilities, and fostering a culture of continual improvement and legal compliance.
[5_2] 5.2 OH&S policy -- Clause 5.2 requires an organization to create and maintain an occupational health and safety policy that reflects its purpose, context and strategic direction, demonstrates commitment to compliance, risk mitigation and continual improvement, and is communicated to all workers and interested parties. The policy must be documented, implemented throughout the organization and reviewed for ongoing suitability.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 requires an organization to identify, assign and communicate the specific roles, responsibilities and authorities needed for its occupational health and safety management system, ensuring that each function has the competence and accountability required to achieve the system's objectives. It also mandates that these assignments be documented, reviewed and made known to relevant personnel and external parties as appropriate.
[5_4] 5.4 Consultation and participation of workers -- ISO 45001 5.4 requires organizations to actively consult workers and their representatives on occupational health and safety matters, ensuring they are involved in decision-making, hazard identification, and risk control. This participation helps improve safety performance, foster engagement, and comply with legal and other OH&S obligations.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 requires an organization to identify occupational health and safety risks and opportunities, determine appropriate actions to eliminate hazards or reduce risks, and exploit opportunities for improvement. These actions must be planned, implemented, and integrated into the OH S management system, with responsibilities, resources, and timelines clearly defined.
[6_2] 6.2 OH&S objectives and planning to achieve them -- Clause 6.2 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to establish measurable occupational health and safety objectives aligned with its policy, legal obligations and risks, and to define the processes, responsibilities, resources and timelines needed to achieve those objectives, ensuring they are documented, communicated and integrated into the overall management system.
[7_1] 7.1 Resources -- Clause 7.1 requires an organization to identify, allocate and maintain the resources necessary for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving its occupational health and safety management system, covering competent personnel, infrastructure, work environment, technology and any other inputs essential to achieving the OH-S objectives.
[7_2] 7.2 Competence -- ISO 45001 7.2 Competence requires an organization to identify the knowledge, skills and experience needed for its OH&S tasks, provide appropriate training or other actions to achieve that competence, and evaluate the effectiveness of these measures, keeping documented evidence of competence and any required updates.
[7_3] 7.3 Awareness -- Clause 7.3 of ISO 45001:2018 requires the organization to ensure that workers and relevant interested parties are aware of the occupational health and safety policy, their roles and responsibilities, and the importance of complying with procedures and controls, so that they can contribute effectively to the OH&S management system.
[7_4] 7.4 Communication -- Section 7.4 of ISO 45001:2018 specifies the communication requirements for an occupational health and safety management system. It obliges organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for internal and external communication of OHS information, ensuring that the content is appropriate, timely and effective.
[7_5] 7.5 Documented information -- Clause 7.5 of ISO 45001:2018 mandates that an organization create and maintain documented information necessary for its occupational health and safety management system, covering both documents and records. It requires procedures for controlling this information, ensuring it is appropriately identified, reviewed, updated, accessible to relevant personnel, and protected from loss or unauthorized alteration.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to plan and control the processes needed to achieve its occupational health and safety objectives, including hazard identification, risk assessment, legal and other requirements, and the establishment of criteria for performance, then integrate these controls into day-to-day operations.
[8_2] 8.2 Emergency preparedness and response -- Clause 8.2 of ISO 45001:2018 requires organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for identifying potential emergency situations, assessing their impacts, and preparing appropriate response procedures. It mandates training, testing and continuous improvement of emergency plans to ensure timely, effective action that protects workers, visitors and the environment.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to establish, implement and maintain processes for monitoring and measuring occupational health and safety performance, assessing compliance with legal and other requirements, and analysing data to determine the effectiveness of the OH&S management system, enabling informed evaluation and continual improvement.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 requires the organization to plan and conduct internal audits at intervals needed to verify that its occupational health and safety management system complies with ISO 45001 and its own documented requirements, and to evaluate its effectiveness. Audits must be objective, impartial and their findings reported to top management for corrective action.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- The 9.3 Management review clause requires top management to periodically assess the OH&S management system's performance, compliance and effectiveness, using inputs such as audit results, incident data, and stakeholder feedback, and to document decisions on improvements, resource needs and changes to policies or objectives.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 45001:2018 outlines the requirements for continual improvement of an occupational health and safety management system, directing organizations to identify non-conformities, implement corrective actions, and evaluate performance in order to enhance safety outcomes and prevent future incidents.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 45001:2018 requires an organization to identify relevant internal and external issues that affect its occupational health and safety objectives, understand the needs and expectations of workers and other interested parties, and define the scope and boundaries of its OHS management system accordingly.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership and Worker Participation -- Clause 5 requires top management to lead the occupational health and safety management system by establishing policy, assigning responsibilities, providing resources, and ensuring integration, while actively involving workers in decision-making, consultation, and continual improvement.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 45001:2018 sets out the planning requirements for an occupational health and safety management system. It requires organizations to identify hazards, assess risks and opportunities, determine legal and other obligations, and establish measurable OHS objectives and programs to achieve them, ensuring that these elements are integrated into the overall management system.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 45001:2018 specifies the support required for an occupational health and safety management system, including the provision of resources, competence, awareness, communication and documented information. It also requires organizations to maintain appropriate infrastructure, work environment and emergency preparedness to enable the system's effective operation.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 45001:2018 sets out the operational requirements for an occupational health and safety management system, specifying how organizations must plan, implement and control processes to eliminate hazards and reduce OH&S risks. It covers safe-work procedures, management of change, procurement and contractor control to ensure day-to-day activities align with the system's OH&S objectives.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 45001:2018 sets out the requirements for performance evaluation of an occupational health and safety management system. It mandates systematic monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation of OH S performance, internal audits and management reviews to verify conformity, effectiveness and drive continual improvement.
