[administration] Administration and Governance -- The Administration and Governance entry for the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) outlines the organisational structure, responsibilities, and procedural rules for managing the scheme. It details the role of the European Commission, member-state authorities, and supporting bodies that oversee reporting, verification, and enforcement of carbon-price collection on imported goods.
[aluminium] Aluminium (CN Chapter 76) -- The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) applies a carbon import charge to aluminium, classified under CN Chapter 76, based on the product's embedded emissions. Importers must report verified emissions data and acquire CBAM certificates to match the carbon cost of EU-produced aluminium, aiming to curb carbon leakage and promote climate-compatible trade.
[authorised_declarant] Authorised CBAM Declarant -- The authorised CBAM declarant is an entity or individual approved by the European Union to submit Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism declarations for imported goods, confirming that the required emissions data and compliance documentation have been provided. This status enables the holder to act on behalf of importers in meeting the EU's reporting, verification and payment obligations under the CBAM scheme.
[cement] Cement (CN Chapter 25) -- Under the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956), cement classified in Customs Nomenclature Chapter 25 is subject to a carbon price on imports. The measure aims to level the playing field for EU producers by accounting for the high CO₂ emissions associated with cement manufacturing and to support the bloc's climate-reduction objectives.
[certificates] CBAM Certificates -- CBAM certificates are trade documents issued under the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) that represent one tonne of CO2-equivalent emissions embedded in imported goods. Importers must acquire and surrender the appropriate number of certificates to cover the reported emissions, and the certificates are tradable within the EU market.
[definitive] Definitive System (from Jan 2026) -- The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) creates a definitive system that will apply from January 2026, requiring importers of covered goods to declare the embedded emissions and purchase CBAM certificates to offset the carbon cost, thereby preventing carbon leakage and ensuring a level playing field with EU producers.
[electricity] Electricity -- Under the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956), the electricity entry requires importers to report the carbon intensity of imported electricity and to surrender CBAM certificates equivalent to the EU ETS price for those emissions, ensuring that foreign electricity faces the same carbon cost as domestic generation. This aligns imported power with the EU's climate mitigation objectives.
[embedded_emissions] Calculation of Embedded Emissions -- The entry defines the methodology for calculating the greenhouse-gas emissions that are embedded in imported goods under the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism. It outlines data requirements, emission factors and allocation rules used to determine the carbon content attributable to each product.
[fertilisers] Fertilisers (CN Chapter 31) -- The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) covers imports of fertilisers classified under Customs Nomenclature Chapter 31, requiring importers to report embedded CO₂ emissions and pay a carbon price equivalent to the EU emissions trading system cost. This aims to prevent carbon leakage by equalising the carbon cost of imported fertilisers with that of domestically produced products.
[hydrogen] Hydrogen (CN 2804) -- The Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (EU) 2023/956 applies a carbon price to imports of hydrogen classified under customs tariff heading CN 2804, covering liquid hydrogen and related products. Importers must report the carbon content of the hydrogen and purchase CBAM certificates equivalent to the EU carbon cost, ensuring a level playing field with domestic producers subject to the EU Emissions Trading System.
[iron_steel] Iron and Steel (CN Chapter 72-73) -- The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) imposes a carbon charge on imports of iron and steel classified under customs chapters 72-73, aligning the cost of embedded CO₂ emissions with the EU Emissions Trading System price. The measure aims to curb carbon leakage and level the competitive field for EU producers.
[national_authority] National Competent Authorities -- The entry identifies the national competent authorities designated by each EU member state to oversee and enforce the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956). These bodies are responsible for handling reporting, verification, and compliance procedures related to carbon-border taxes and related customs processes.
[price] Price Based on EU ETS Allowance Price -- The entry records the price element of the EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956), indicating that the charge levied on imported goods is calculated using the current price of EU Emissions Trading System allowances. This linkage aligns border taxes with the EU's internal carbon market.
[purchase] Purchase of CBAM Certificates -- The entry refers to the acquisition of certificates required under the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, which offset the carbon content of imported goods; companies must purchase these CBAM certificates to meet the obligations set out in Regulation 2023/956.
[reporting] Reporting Obligations -- Under the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) importers of covered goods must submit detailed emissions reports to the European Commission. The reporting obligations require annual electronic declarations of the quantity of goods, their embedded CO2 content, the calculation methodology and verification evidence, enabling the calculation of the corresponding border carbon levy.
[scope] Scope and Covered Goods -- The scope section of the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (Regulation 2023/956) sets out which imported products are subject to the levy, focusing on carbon-intensive sectors such as cement, electricity, fertilizers, iron and steel, and aluminium. It defines the criteria for covered goods, including thresholds, geographic origin and exemptions, establishing the boundaries for the mechanism's application.
[surrender] Surrender of Certificates -- The entry describes the mandatory step where importers must surrender CBAM certificates that correspond to the verified carbon content of goods entering the EU. Surrender of these certificates finalises the emission charge payment and provides proof of compliance with the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism.
[transitional] Transitional Phase Reporting (Oct 2023 - Dec 2025) -- The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism's transitional phase requires importers of covered goods to submit quarterly reports on the embedded carbon content of their shipments from October 2023 through December 2025, without yet paying the CBAM levy. This reporting builds a database of emissions data to support the full system's rollout after the transition ends.
