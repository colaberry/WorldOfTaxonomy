[1_1] Social and environmental assessment and management system -- The entry defines the requirement for a project-level system that systematically identifies, assesses, avoids, mitigates and monitors environmental and social risks and impacts throughout all phases of a project. It mandates documented procedures, stakeholder engagement and regular reporting to ensure compliance with IFC Performance Standards.

[1_3] Management programmes -- The Management programmes standard requires borrowers to develop and operate systematic plans that identify, assess, monitor and mitigate environmental and social risks throughout a project's life cycle. It calls for clear institutional responsibilities, training, documentation and continuous improvement to ensure compliance with the IFC Performance Standards.
[1_4] Stakeholder engagement -- Stakeholder engagement under IFC Performance Standard 1 requires project developers to identify, inform and involve individuals, groups and institutions affected by or interested in the activity throughout its life cycle. It calls for transparent communication, meaningful consultation, and the incorporation of stakeholder feedback into decision-making to address concerns, mitigate risks and enhance project sustainability.
[2_1] Working conditions and management of worker relationships -- The standard requires project sponsors to provide safe, healthy workplaces and to uphold fair labor practices, including reasonable working hours, adequate wages, and non-discrimination. It also mandates clear communication, collective bargaining where applicable, and accessible grievance mechanisms to manage worker relationships responsibly.
[2_2] Occupational health and safety -- The standard requires project developers to identify and mitigate occupational health and safety risks throughout the lifecycle, ensuring safe workplaces, adequate training, emergency preparedness and access to medical services for workers and contractors.
[2_3] Supply chain labor -- The entry covers the management of labor practices throughout a project's supply chain, requiring compliance with the International Finance Corporation's Performance Standard 2 on Labor and Working Conditions. It emphasizes fair wages, safe workplaces, non-discrimination, freedom of association and mechanisms to monitor and remediate violations in upstream and downstream suppliers.
[3_1] Resource efficiency (greenhouse gases, water, waste) -- The standard requires projects to optimise the use of resources by quantifying and reducing greenhouse-gas emissions, conserving water and minimising waste throughout the project lifecycle. It calls for baseline assessments, measurable targets, mitigation plans and ongoing monitoring to ensure that resource consumption is efficient and environmental impacts are limited.
[3_2] Pollution prevention -- The entry outlines requirements for identifying, avoiding and minimizing pollution from project activities, including waste, emissions to air, water and soil, and the use of hazardous substances. It emphasizes implementing control measures, monitoring performance and adopting best-available technologies to protect human health and the environment. Compliance demonstrates a commitment to sustainable operations and risk reduction.
[5_1] Displacement and resettlement -- The entry covers how projects must identify, avoid and minimize involuntary displacement, and when displacement is unavoidable, it requires the development of a resettlement action plan that ensures affected persons receive fair compensation, suitable replacement housing or land, and restoration of livelihoods, with participation and grievance mechanisms in place. It aligns with IFC Performance Standard 5 to manage social risks and protect displaced communities.
[5_2] Economic displacement -- Entry 5_2 of the IFC Performance Standards addresses economic displacement, requiring projects to identify and avoid adverse impacts on local businesses, jobs and income sources, and to provide appropriate compensation, livelihood restoration or alternative opportunities when displacement cannot be avoided. It aims to safeguard affected communities from loss of economic wellbeing and ensure that benefits are shared fairly.
[6_1] Protection and conservation of biodiversity -- The entry 6_1 under IFC Performance Standard 6 requires projects to avoid, mitigate and manage impacts on biodiversity, safeguarding threatened species and critical habitats. It calls for baseline assessments, conservation plans, and ongoing monitoring to ensure that the project contributes to the protection and sustainable use of ecosystems.
[6_2] Management of living natural resources -- The standard requires that projects using or affecting living natural resources-such as wildlife, fisheries, forests, and genetic material-be planned and operated to maintain biodiversity and ecosystem services. It calls for assessments of impacts, mitigation measures, and monitoring to ensure sustainable use and avoid over-exploitation or irreversible loss.
[ps1] PS1 - Assessment and Management of E&S Risks and Impacts -- PS1 is the first International Finance Corporation Performance Standard, requiring project developers to identify, assess and manage environmental and social risks and impacts throughout a project's life cycle. It mandates a structured process of screening, scoping, impact evaluation and mitigation, ensuring that significant risks are addressed before implementation.

[ps3] PS3 - Resource Efficiency and Pollution Prevention -- PS3 sets out requirements for improving resource efficiency and preventing pollution across a project's lifecycle. It obliges clients to adopt measures that reduce energy and water consumption, manage waste responsibly, and limit emissions, thereby minimizing adverse environmental impacts.
[ps4] PS4 - Community Health, Safety and Security -- PS4 of the IFC Performance Standards requires borrowers to identify, assess and manage risks and impacts on community health, safety and security that arise from project activities. It mandates implementation of mitigation measures, emergency response plans and ongoing monitoring to protect workers, local residents and vulnerable groups, and to ensure that operations do not exacerbate crime, accidents or health hazards.
[ps5] PS5 - Land Acquisition and Involuntary Resettlement -- PS5 requires project developers to assess, avoid, and mitigate adverse effects of land acquisition and involuntary resettlement, ensuring that affected persons receive fair compensation, adequate compensation, and livelihood restoration. The standard mandates transparent consultation, culturally appropriate resettlement planning, and monitoring to protect vulnerable groups and promote sustainable outcomes.
[ps6] PS6 - Biodiversity Conservation and Sustainable Management of Living Natural Resources -- PS6 requires project developers to identify, avoid, minimize and mitigate adverse impacts on biodiversity, protect critical habitats and promote the sustainable use of living natural resources. It calls for biodiversity assessments, the creation of management plans and ongoing monitoring to ensure conservation outcomes and the responsible stewardship of ecosystems.
[ps7] PS7 - Indigenous Peoples -- PS7 sets out the IFC's requirements for recognizing and managing risks and impacts on indigenous peoples throughout project cycles. It mandates early identification, meaningful consultation, respect for cultural heritage, and the attainment of free prior informed consent where required, ensuring that benefits are shared and rights are protected.
[ps8] PS8 - Cultural Heritage -- PS8 focuses on cultural heritage, requiring project participants to identify and assess any heritage assets that could be affected, avoid or mitigate adverse impacts, and develop management plans for protection or restoration. It emphasizes the involvement of local communities and relevant authorities, ensuring that heritage values are respected and preserved throughout the project lifecycle.
