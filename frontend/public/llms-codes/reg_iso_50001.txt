[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- ISO 50001:2018 clause 5.1 requires top management to lead the Energy Management System by establishing a clear energy policy, assigning responsibilities, and providing necessary resources. It obliges leaders to integrate energy objectives into the organization's strategic planning, promote continual improvement, and demonstrate commitment through active participation and communication. This ensures that energy performance is considered a core element of overall business governance.
[5_2] 5.2 Energy policy -- Section 5.2 of ISO 50001:2018 requires an organization to develop a documented energy policy that demonstrates top-management commitment to continual improvement of energy performance, compliance with legal requirements, and integration of energy considerations into business processes. The policy must be communicated to all relevant personnel and made available to interested parties, providing a basis for setting energy objectives and targets.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 of ISO 50001:2018 requires an organization to identify and evaluate risks and opportunities that could impact the energy management system's ability to meet its objectives, and to plan and implement actions to address them. These actions must be integrated into the EMS processes, monitored, and reviewed for effectiveness.
[6_2] 6.2 Objectives, energy targets and planning -- The entry outlines how an organization must define measurable energy-related objectives and set specific targets aligned with its energy policy. It requires developing a documented plan that integrates these goals into operational processes, assigns responsibilities, and establishes timelines for achievement.
[6_3] 6.3 Energy review -- Entry 6.3 mandates that an organization perform an energy review to identify and analyze its significant energy uses, establish a baseline of energy consumption and performance, and pinpoint opportunities for improvement, serving as the foundation for setting targets and implementing an effective energy management system.
[6_4] 6.4 Energy performance indicators -- Clause 6.4 of ISO 50001:2018 requires organizations to define, calculate and monitor energy performance indicators that reflect key aspects of energy use and efficiency, linking them to established objectives and targets. These indicators must be measurable, appropriate to the organization's scope, and used to evaluate improvement over time.
[6_5] 6.5 Energy baseline -- Section 6.5 of ISO 50001:2018 requires organizations to develop an energy baseline that represents historical energy use under normal operating conditions, serving as a reference point for measuring performance improvements. The baseline must be based on reliable data, consider relevant variables, and be periodically reviewed to ensure its continued relevance.

[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 obliges an organization to set up documented processes that plan, implement and control activities affecting energy performance, allocating resources and defining responsibilities to achieve its energy objectives. It requires the establishment of operational controls, monitoring of key parameters, and corrective actions when actual results deviate from the planned energy-performance targets.
[8_2] 8.2 Design -- Section 8.2 of ISO 50001:2018 outlines the design requirements for an energy management system, specifying how organizations must establish, implement and maintain processes that address energy policy, objectives and legal obligations. It guides the creation of documented procedures, allocation of resources and integration of energy performance indicators to ensure systematic planning and continual improvement.
[8_3] 8.3 Procurement -- Clause 8.3 of ISO 50001:2018 requires organizations to integrate energy considerations into their procurement processes, ensuring that purchased equipment, services and supplies support energy performance objectives, that suppliers are evaluated on energy criteria, and that contracts reflect these requirements.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation of energy performance -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 50001:2018 requires organizations to set up processes for monitoring and measuring energy use and performance, to analyse the collected data, evaluate results against objectives, and identify improvement opportunities, ensuring the information is accurate, documented and used for continual energy-performance enhancement.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 of ISO 50001:2018 requires the organization to plan and conduct internal audits of its energy-management system at defined intervals, evaluating conformity with the standard and the EMS policy, recording findings, and using results to drive continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 of ISO 50001:2018 requires top management to conduct regular reviews of the energy management system to confirm its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must consider performance metrics, audit findings, non-conformities, corrective actions and improvement opportunities, and result in decisions on changes to policy, objectives, resources or other elements of the system.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 50001:2018 defines the improvement requirements for an energy management system, mandating organisations to monitor performance, identify energy-saving opportunities, and implement corrective actions. It emphasizes a cycle of continual enhancement to achieve the energy policy's objectives and increase overall energy efficiency.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 50001:2018 requires an organization to determine its context by identifying relevant internal and external issues, interested parties, and the scope of its energy-management system. This assessment ensures that the EMS aligns with strategic goals and addresses factors influencing energy performance.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 of ISO 50001:2018 defines the leadership responsibilities for an energy management system. It requires top management to demonstrate commitment, establish an energy policy, assign roles and authorities, and ensure the provision of resources needed to achieve continual improvement in energy performance.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 50001:2018 sets out the planning requirements for an energy management system. It requires organizations to establish energy objectives, targets and action plans based on a review of energy performance, legal requirements and significant energy uses. The clause also covers risk identification and the development of a documented energy management plan.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 defines the support necessary for an energy management system, covering the provision of resources, the competence and awareness of personnel, internal and external communication, and the control of documented information to ensure effective operation and continual improvement.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 50001:2018 specifies the operational requirements for an energy management system, detailing how organizations must plan, implement, and control processes that affect energy performance, including monitoring, measurement and corrective actions to ensure continual improvement.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 50001:2018 specifies that organizations must monitor, measure, analyze and evaluate their energy performance, establish performance indicators, and conduct internal audits and management reviews to ensure continual improvement of the energy management system.
