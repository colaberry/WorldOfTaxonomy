[FC] FIFA Confederations -- FC denotes the FIFA Confederations, the group of continental football governing bodies recognized by FIFA, including UEFA in Europe, CONMEBOL in South America, CONCACAF in North and Central America and the Caribbean, CAF in Africa, AFC in Asia and OFC in Oceania. These confederations organize regional competitions and coordinate member associations under FIFA's global framework.
[FC.01] AFC (Asia) -- FC.01 designates the Asian Football Confederation, the governing body for association football in Asia and Australia, comprising 47 national associations. It organises continental competitions such as the AFC Asian Cup and Champions League and represents its members within FIFA.
[FC.02] CAF (Africa) -- The CAF, or Confederation of African Football, is the governing body for association football across the African continent, overseeing national and club competitions such as the African Cup of Nations and the CAF Champions League. It represents Africa within FIFA, sets regulations, and promotes the sport's development throughout the region.
[FC.03] CONCACAF (North/Central America, Caribbean) -- FC.03 designates CONCACAF, the FIFA-affiliated confederation that governs football in North America, Central America and the Caribbean, overseeing national teams, club competitions such as the Champions League, and regional development programs across its 41 member associations.
[FC.04] CONMEBOL (South America) -- FC.04 denotes CONMEBOL, the South American football confederation that governs the sport across 10 member nations, organizes tournaments such as the Copa América and the Libertadores, and represents the region within FIFA's global structure. It oversees national team and club competitions, development programs, and international match coordination throughout the continent.
[FC.05] OFC (Oceania) -- The Oceania Football Confederation (OFC) is the FIFA-recognised governing body for association football in the Oceania region. It administers national team and club competitions for its member nations and territories, including New Zealand, Fiji, and many Pacific island states, and is one of six continental confederations under FIFA.
[FC.06] UEFA (Europe) -- FC.06 refers to UEFA, the European football confederation that governs the sport across the continent. It organizes major tournaments such as the European Championship and the Champions League and represents its member national associations within FIFA.
[FC.07] FIFA World Cup -- The FIFA World Cup is the premier international football tournament organized by FIFA, featuring national teams from each of the six continental confederations competing every four years for the sport's most prestigious title. It showcases a month-long showcase of matches, culminating in a final that determines the world champions and generates massive global viewership and cultural impact.
[FC.08] FIFA Women's World Cup -- The FIFA Women's World Cup is the premier international tournament for women's national football teams, organized by FIFA and staged every four years. It gathers qualifying squads from all six continental confederations to compete for the title of world champion.
[FC.09] FIFA Club World Cup -- The FIFA Club World Cup is an annual tournament organized by FIFA that pits the champion clubs from each of the six continental confederations, plus a host-nation representative, against one another. It determines the world's top club team and typically takes place in December.
[FC.10] FIFA U-20 World Cup -- The FIFA U-20 World Cup is a biennial international tournament organized by FIFA for national teams composed of players aged 20 or younger. It brings together youth squads from all six FIFA continental confederations, serving as a showcase for emerging talent and often influencing qualification for the Olympic football competition.
[FC.11] FIFA U-17 World Cup -- FC.11 denotes the FIFA U-17 World Cup, the global championship for male players under seventeen organized by FIFA and contested by national teams that qualify through their respective continental confederations. Held every two years, the tournament showcases emerging talent and serves as a pathway toward senior international competition.
[FC.12] FIFA Futsal World Cup -- The FIFA Futsal World Cup, identified by code FC.12, is the premier global tournament for men's national futsal teams organized by FIFA. Held every four years, it brings together the top qualifiers from each continental confederation to compete for the title of world champion in the fast-paced, indoor version of football.
[FC.13] FIFA Beach Soccer World Cup -- The FIFA Beach Soccer World Cup is FIFA's premier global tournament for beach soccer, featuring national teams from each continental confederation competing for the world title. Listed as entry FC.13, it falls under the FIFA Continental Football Confederations classification.
