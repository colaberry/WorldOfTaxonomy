[SC] SCOR Processes -- SCOR Processes are the core activities defined in the Supply Chain Operations Reference Model that structure supply-chain management into five distinct categories: Plan, Source, Make, Deliver and Return. Each process maps best-practice tasks, performance metrics and data flows, enabling organizations to benchmark, integrate and improve their end-to-end operations. The model provides a common language for aligning strategy, technology and execution across the supply chain.
[SC.01] Plan -- SC.01 - Plan defines the processes for aligning demand and supply, establishing policies, and setting performance targets across the supply chain. It involves forecasting, demand management, and resource planning to ensure the network can meet customer requirements efficiently.
[SC.02] Source -- SC.02 Source covers the activities required to procure goods or services needed for production, including identifying and qualifying suppliers, negotiating terms, and placing orders, while managing inbound logistics and ensuring compliance with quality and cost objectives. It links demand planning to supplier performance monitoring.
[SC.03] Make -- The Make process converts inputs into finished goods, covering production scheduling, manufacturing, assembly, testing, packaging and release for delivery. It manages resources, work-in-process inventory and quality controls to meet demand, cost and timing objectives.
[SC.04] Deliver -- SC.04 Deliver covers all activities required to fulfill a customer order, from order receipt and processing through outbound logistics, transportation, and final delivery to the customer's location, including any associated after-sales service and handling of returns.
[SC.05] Return -- SC.05 Return defines the set of processes used to manage product returns and reverse logistics, covering receipt, inspection, disposition, repair, recycling or disposal, and any associated reimbursements or credits. It ensures that returned items are efficiently handled to recover value, meet regulatory requirements and maintain customer satisfaction.
[SC.06] Enable -- SC.06 Enable defines the set of processes that coordinate and manage resources, information, and relationships across the supply chain so that all participants are prepared to execute downstream activities, ensuring alignment, readiness and smooth flow of operations.
[SC.07] Orchestrate -- SC.07 Orchestrate defines the coordination of all supply-chain activities across partners, aligning processes, information, and resources to achieve synchronized execution. It focuses on planning, scheduling and managing inter-company flows to improve responsiveness and efficiency.
[SC.08] Plan Supply Chain -- SC.08 - Plan Supply Chain covers the strategic and tactical processes that balance demand forecasts with supply capabilities, establish inventory policies, and define resource requirements across the network. It creates synchronized plans that guide execution, coordinate activities, and set performance targets for the entire supply chain.
[SC.09] Source Stocked Product -- The SC.09 "Source Stocked Product" process in the Supply Chain Operations Reference Model covers the activities required to procure items that are already held in inventory, ensuring that sufficient stocked inventory is available to meet demand without creating new production orders. This includes supplier selection, order placement, and receipt of pre-stocked goods.
[SC.10] Source Make-to-Order -- SC.10 Source Make-to-Order defines the process of acquiring raw materials or components specifically for individual customer orders, aligning procurement tightly with production schedules. It emphasizes responsive sourcing to minimize inventory while ensuring that each order's unique specifications are met.
[SC.11] Source Engineer-to-Order -- SC.11 - Source Engineer-to-Order refers to the sourcing process where a supplier designs and manufactures a product specifically to meet the unique requirements of a customer order, integrating engineering activities with procurement. This approach combines custom design, material acquisition, and production planning to deliver tailor-made solutions on demand.
[SC.12] Make-to-Stock -- SC.12 in the Supply Chain Operations Reference Model defines the make-to-stock process, where products are manufactured in advance based on forecasted demand and held in inventory for immediate shipment. This approach balances production efficiency with service level goals, enabling rapid order fulfillment while managing stock risks.
[SC.13] Make-to-Order -- Make-to-Order is a supply-chain configuration in which production is triggered only after a customer order is received. It aligns demand and supply, reduces inventory, and requires responsive planning, scheduling, and execution across procurement, manufacturing, and distribution.
[SC.14] Deliver Stocked Product -- SC.14 Deliver Stocked Product covers the outbound logistics activity of shipping finished goods that are already in inventory to customers, encompassing order receipt, picking, packing, transportation, and delivery confirmation while tracking performance metrics such as order lead time and fill rate.
[SC.15] Deliver MTO Product -- SC.15 in the Supply Chain Operations Reference Model defines the processes for delivering a made-to-order (MTO) product, encompassing order confirmation, scheduling, manufacturing execution, packaging, and final shipment to the customer. It emphasizes alignment of production with specific customer specifications and tight coordination of logistics to ensure timely, accurate fulfillment.
[SC.16] Deliver ETO Product -- SC.16 covers the delivery of Engineer-to-Order (ETO) products, guiding the coordination of final logistics, shipment, and installation to ensure the customized item meets the customer's specifications, and confirming performance through post-delivery verification.
