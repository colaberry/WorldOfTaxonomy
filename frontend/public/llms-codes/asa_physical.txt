[AS] ASA Physical Status -- AS denotes the ASA Physical Status classification, a widely used system that assigns patients to categories I through VI based on their pre-operative health, ranging from a healthy individual to a brain-dead donor, to help assess surgical risk.
[AS.01] ASA I: Normal Healthy Patient -- ASA I denotes a patient who is in good overall health without systemic disease, exhibiting normal physiological function and no significant medical problems that could affect anesthesia or surgery. This classification is used to indicate a low peri-operative risk and serves as the baseline category for healthy individuals.
[AS.02] ASA II: Mild Systemic Disease -- ASA II denotes a patient with mild systemic disease that imposes minimal functional limitations but is not life-limiting. Examples include controlled hypertension, well-regulated diabetes, or mild asthma. These patients generally tolerate anesthesia without a significant increase in risk.
[AS.03] ASA III: Severe Systemic Disease -- ASA III denotes a patient with severe systemic disease that imposes a definite functional limitation but is not life- threatening. Examples include uncontrolled diabetes or hypertension, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, morbid obesity, or a recent myocardial infarction; the condition is serious enough to affect perioperative risk and management.
[AS.04] ASA IV: Severe Life-Threatening Disease -- ASA IV designates a patient with a severe systemic disease that poses a constant threat to life, such as recent myocardial infarction, severe chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, or uncontrolled diabetes with end-organ damage. This level signals a high perioperative risk and guides anesthetic planning.
[AS.05] ASA V: Moribund Patient -- ASA V designates a moribund patient whose severe systemic disease makes survival unlikely without the proposed surgery, and who may be dying despite ongoing medical care. Such patients are at the highest risk of peri-operative complications, and their condition is considered a constant threat to life.
[AS.06] ASA VI: Brain-Dead Organ Donor -- ASA VI designates a patient who is brain-dead and maintained solely for organ procurement. The individual shows no viable neurologic function, is physiologically dependent on life support, and is classified for the purpose of organ donation.
[AS.07] E Modifier: Emergency Surgery -- The ASA Physical Status Classification System grades a patient's pre-operative health from I (healthy) to VI (brain-dead donor). Adding the "E" modifier denotes that the procedure is an emergency, meaning it cannot be delayed without jeopardizing the patient's outcome, regardless of the underlying ASA class. This designation helps clinicians anticipate higher risk and plan resources accordingly.

[AS.09] Mallampati Airway Classification -- The Mallampati Airway Classification is a bedside tool used to estimate the ease of endotracheal intubation by grading how much of the oropharyngeal structures are visible when the patient opens their mouth and protrudes the tongue. It assigns patients to classes I-IV, with higher classes indicating a greater likelihood of a difficult airway and informing anesthetic planning.
[AS.10] ASA Monitoring Standards -- AS.10 outlines the minimum monitoring standards required during anesthesia, mandating continuous ECG, non-invasive blood pressure, pulse oximetry, and capnography, with additional observations such as temperature, urine output, or invasive lines added according to the patient's ASA physical status and the complexity of the procedure.
