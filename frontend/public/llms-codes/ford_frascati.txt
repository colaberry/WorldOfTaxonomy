[3] Medical and Health Sciences -- Medical and Health Sciences (code 3) encompasses research aimed at understanding, preventing, diagnosing and treating diseases, as well as improving health care delivery, public health, and medical technology. It includes studies in clinical medicine, epidemiology, health services, pharmacy, nursing, and related biomedical fields, covering both basic and applied investigations that advance human health.
[3.1] Basic Medicine -- Basic Medicine (code 3.1) covers research aimed at advancing fundamental understanding of human health and disease, including studies of physiology, pathology, microbiology, immunology, genetics and related biological processes. It focuses on generating knowledge about disease mechanisms, risk factors and the biological basis for health interventions, without immediate application to specific clinical treatments.
[3.2] Clinical Medicine -- Code 3.2 Clinical Medicine includes research and development focused on preventing, diagnosing, treating and managing human disease. It spans basic, applied and experimental studies of pathophysiology, therapeutic interventions, medical technologies and patient-care practices, as well as clinical trials and epidemiological investigations.
[3.3] Health Sciences -- The 3.3 Health Sciences field comprises research and experimental development focused on human health, disease mechanisms, medical interventions, and health services. It includes biomedical research, clinical studies, epidemiology, public health, and related disciplines that aim to improve diagnosis, treatment, prevention, and health policy.
[3.4] Medical Biotechnology -- Medical biotechnology covers R&D that applies biological science to develop medical products and technologies, including diagnostic tests, vaccines, gene and cell therapies, and recombinant proteins. It encompasses the manipulation of living organisms or their components to improve disease detection, treatment, and prevention, integrating molecular biology, genetics, and bioengineering.
[3.5] Other Medical Sciences -- Code 3.5 "Other Medical Sciences" groups research activities in medicine that are not covered by the narrower sub-fields of clinical medicine, medical biotechnology or public health. It includes work on medical ethics, health services, alternative and complementary therapies, medical imaging, and other interdisciplinary or emerging medical topics.
[4] Agricultural and Veterinary Sciences -- It includes research and development on the science and technology of crops, livestock, horticulture, forestry, fisheries and related ecosystems, as well as veterinary medicine, focusing on improving productivity, disease control, nutrition and the sustainability of agricultural and animal production systems.
[4.1] Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries -- This field covers research and experimental development activities related to the production, management and conservation of crops, livestock, forests and aquatic resources. It includes studies of agronomy, animal husbandry, forestry, fisheries, aquaculture and associated technologies.
[4.2] Animal and Dairy Sciences -- The 4.2 Animal and Dairy Sciences field covers research and experimental development on the biology, breeding, nutrition, health and management of livestock and dairy production. It includes studies of animal genetics, milk composition, disease control, feed efficiency and technologies that improve animal welfare and dairy product quality, supporting agricultural productivity and sustainability.
[4.3] Veterinary Sciences -- The Veterinary Sciences field includes research and development focused on animal health and welfare, covering disease prevention, diagnosis, treatment, zoonotic disease study, animal nutrition, breeding and reproductive technologies, and related diagnostic and therapeutic methods.
[4.4] Agricultural Biotechnology -- Agricultural biotechnology comprises R&D that applies biological techniques to enhance crops, livestock and related farming systems. It includes genetic engineering, molecular breeding, bio-fertilizers and biopesticides designed to increase productivity, improve pest and disease resistance, and promote environmental sustainability.
[4.5] Other Agricultural Sciences -- The code 4.5 in the OECD Frascati Manual covers agricultural sciences that are not classified elsewhere, including research on crop and livestock breeding, soil and water management, pest control, agronomy, and other interdisciplinary studies aimed at improving agricultural production and sustainability.
[5] Social Sciences -- Code 5 - Social Sciences covers research on human behaviour, societies and institutions, encompassing disciplines such as economics, sociology, psychology, political science, anthropology and related fields. It includes both basic and applied studies that generate knowledge about social structures, relationships, policies and processes affecting communities and populations.
[5.1] Psychology and Cognitive Sciences -- The 5.1 Psychology and Cognitive Sciences field includes research on mental processes, behavior and individual differences, covering topics such as perception, learning, memory, language, reasoning, emotion, neuropsychology, developmental and social psychology, as well as related experimental and theoretical investigations.
[5.2] Economics and Business -- The 5.2 category "Economics and Business" groups research and experimental development that advances knowledge of economic theory, financial systems, market behavior, management practices, accounting, entrepreneurship and related policy analysis. It includes studies of macro- and micro-economics, corporate strategy, marketing, operations, and the quantitative methods used to evaluate economic performance.
[5.3] Educational Sciences -- Educational sciences covers research on teaching and learning processes, curriculum development, assessment, educational policy and the management of schools and other institutions. It includes studies of pedagogy, learning outcomes, instructional technologies and the social contexts that shape education.
[5.4] Sociology -- Field 5.4 Sociology comprises research that systematically examines human societies, social relationships and institutions, including topics such as social structures, culture, stratification, group dynamics and social change. It encompasses both qualitative and quantitative studies aimed at understanding patterns of social behavior and the processes that shape collective life.
[5.5] Law -- The 5.5 Law category covers research and experimental development aimed at understanding, interpreting, and improving legal systems, legislation, and judicial processes. It includes studies of legal theory, comparative law, law policy, and the application of law in societal contexts.
[5.6] Political Science -- The 5.6 Political Science field comprises research on the theory and practice of politics, including the analysis of political institutions, processes, behavior, public policy, comparative politics, international relations and political methodology, aiming to understand how power is acquired, exercised and distributed in societies.
[5.7] Social and Economic Geography -- Code 5.7 encompasses research on the spatial organization of societies and economies, analysing patterns of settlement, land use, regional development and the interaction between social processes and geographic space. It includes studies of urban and rural dynamics, regional inequality, mobility and the effects of economic activity on the environment.
[5.8] Media and Communications -- The 5.8 Media and Communications field includes research and development activities related to the creation, distribution, and reception of information through electronic and digital channels, covering broadcasting, telecommunications, internet services, content production, and the underlying network and communication technologies.
[5.9] Other Social Sciences -- Code 5.9 in the OECD Frascati Manual groups research and experimental development in social-science fields that are not covered by the main categories, such as anthropology, demography, gender studies, cultural studies and interdisciplinary social research. It encompasses investigations of societal structures, cultural phenomena and human behaviour that fall outside economics, sociology, political science, education and psychology.
[6] Humanities and the Arts -- The code 6 field, Humanities and the Arts, groups research and experimental development activities that explore human culture, expression and thought. It includes disciplines such as history, literature, philosophy, languages, visual and performing arts, and related cultural-heritage work, covering both basic inquiry and applied creative production.
[6.1] History and Archaeology -- 6.1 History and Archaeology includes research that examines past human activities through documentary sources, material remains, and scientific analysis, aiming to reconstruct events, societies, and cultures and to develop methods for interpreting historical and archaeological data. The field covers chronological studies, historiography, excavation, artifact analysis, and interdisciplinary approaches linking anthropology, geography, and heritage management.
[6.2] Languages and Literature -- Code 6.2 Languages and Literature groups research on the structure, use and acquisition of languages, including linguistics, phonetics, semantics and sociolinguistics, as well as the study of literary works, criticism, theory and textual analysis. It encompasses investigations into language development, multilingualism, translation, literary history and the cultural impact of written and oral expression. This field is part of the broader social-science classification in the OECD Frascati Manual.
[6.3] Philosophy, Ethics and Religion -- The 6.3 field comprises scholarly work that examines fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values and meaning, as well as the systematic study of moral principles and religious beliefs. It includes research in philosophy, ethics, theology, comparative religion and the history of ideas, covering both theoretical analysis and applied ethical issues across the social sciences and humanities.
[6.4] Arts (art history, performing arts, music) -- The 6.4 category of the OECD Frascati Manual groups research and development activities focused on the arts, such as art history, performing arts and music. It encompasses the creation, performance, analysis and preservation of artistic works and the study of artistic practices.
[6.5] Other Humanities and the Arts -- Code 6.5 in the OECD Frascati Manual 2015 groups research activities that fall outside the main humanities and arts categories, covering interdisciplinary cultural studies, heritage preservation, folklore, and other creative or scholarly pursuits not otherwise classified. It captures work that contributes to knowledge and practice in the broader cultural sector.
