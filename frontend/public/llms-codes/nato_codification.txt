[NC] NATO Supply Groups -- NC is the NATO Codification System code for NATO Supply Groups, a classification that clusters items sharing common logistical characteristics such as function, usage, and material composition. It enables standardized identification, procurement, and support of equipment across NATO member nations, facilitating interoperability and efficient supply chain management.
[NC.10] Weapons -- NC.10 is the NATO Codification System designation for weapons, encompassing a wide range of military ordnance such as firearms, artillery, missiles and related equipment used by armed forces, with standardised data for logistics and supply management.
[NC.11] Nuclear Ordnance -- NC.11 designates the Nuclear Ordnance group in the NATO Codification System, covering all nuclear weapons and related components such as warheads, bombs, missile payloads and associated safety and handling equipment. The classification provides a standardized data set for logistics, inventory control and interoperability among NATO members.

[NC.13] Ammunition and Explosives -- NC.13 groups items that are ammunition, explosive charges, and related components used by NATO forces, covering cartridges, projectiles, fuzes, propellants, and demolition explosives. It serves as the primary class for managing inventory, logistics, and documentation of munitions across member nations.
[NC.14] Guided Missiles -- NC.14 in the NATO Codification System designates guided missiles, encompassing all self-propelled weapons that employ internal or external guidance to engage targets. The category includes air-to-air, surface-to-air, anti-ship, cruise and tactical land-attack missiles, regardless of launch platform or propulsion type.
[NC.15] Aircraft and Airframe Components -- NC.15 designates all components that form an aircraft's structure or airframe, including wings, fuselage sections, tail assemblies, landing gear, control surfaces and related sub-assemblies, and is used for logistics, supply and inventory management within NATO.
[NC.16] Aircraft Components and Accessories -- NC.16 groups all items that are parts, sub-assemblies or accessories specifically designed for aircraft, including structural pieces, engine components, flight-control mechanisms, avionics modules and supporting hardware such as brackets, fasteners and safety devices. The classification aids logistics and supply chains by standardising identification of any component used in the construction, operation or maintenance of military or civilian aircraft.
[NC.17] Aircraft Launching/Landing Equipment -- NC.17 in the NATO Codification System designates equipment used for launching and recovering aircraft, including catapults, arresting gear, launch rails, recovery nets and related support hardware. It covers all items that facilitate safe take-off and landing operations on carriers, airfields or other platforms where conventional runway assistance is required.
[NC.19] Ships, Small Craft, Pontoons -- NC.19 in the NATO Codification System covers small maritime vessels and floating structures such as pontoons, used for transport, storage or temporary platforms in naval and civilian operations. The category groups lightweight craft, barges and modular floatation devices that are not classified as full-size ships, facilitating standardized identification and logistics support across allied forces.
[NC.20] Ship and Marine Equipment -- NC.20 in the NATO Codification System groups items used on ships and other watercraft, covering a wide range of equipment from navigation and communication gear to propulsion and safety systems. The category provides a standardized reference for NATO members to identify, procure and manage maritime hardware.
[NC.22] Railway Equipment -- NC.22 designates railway equipment within the NATO Codification System, covering all items used for rail transport such as locomotives, freight and passenger cars, track structures, signaling devices and maintenance tools. The code groups these items for standardized identification, logistics support and supply chain management across NATO member nations.
[NC.23] Ground Effect Vehicles -- Ground Effect Vehicles are craft that travel just above a surface, using the aerodynamic lift generated by the wing's interaction with the nearby ground or water to achieve high speed with reduced fuel consumption. They are employed for military transport, reconnaissance, and research, and are designated NC.23 in the NATO Codification System.
[NC.24] Tractors -- NC.24 designates tractor-type vehicles and equipment within the NATO Codification System, covering agricultural, industrial and haulage tractors, their power units, chassis, and integral attachments such as hitches and PTO drives. The code groups items used for pulling, towing or powering implements, regardless of fuel type or propulsion method.
[NC.25] Vehicular Equipment Components -- The NC.25 group in NATO Codification System covers components fitted to vehicles, such as winches, lights, mirrors, and other equipment that can be removed and replaced. It is part of group 25 for vehicular equipment components, used for inventory and logistics across NATO.
[NC.26] Tires and Tubes -- NC.26 covers all types of pneumatic tires and associated inner tubes for land, sea and air vehicles, including spare and retreaded tires, as well as the tubes that provide inflation.
[NC.28] Engines and Turbines -- The NC.28 group covers all types of engines and turbines, including internal-combustion, gas-turbine, jet and steam units used on land, sea or air platforms, as well as their spare parts, accessories and ancillary equipment. It also encompasses components such as compressors, turbines, gearboxes, control units and fuel-system elements that are integral to these power-producing machines.
[NC.29] Engine Accessories -- NC.29 in the NATO Codification System groups together engine accessories, the ancillary components that support the operation of a vehicle's powerplant. Items classified here include fuel pumps, oil filters, starters, alternators, cooling fans and other devices that are attached to or integrated with the engine but are not part of the core engine block or rotating assembly.
[NC.30] Mechanical Power Transmission Equipment -- NC.30 designates mechanical power transmission equipment, including gears, shafts, couplings, belts, chains and related components used to transfer rotational or linear energy between machines. Items are cataloged for logistics and supply within NATO forces, facilitating standardised identification and procurement.
