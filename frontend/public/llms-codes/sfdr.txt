[PAI-1] GHG Emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3) -- PAI-1 under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation requires financial market participants to disclose the greenhouse-gas emissions associated with their investments, covering direct emissions (Scope 1), indirect energy-related emissions (Scope 2) and all other indirect emissions across the value chain (Scope 3). This metric helps assess climate-related adverse impacts of investment decisions.

[PAI-11] Lack of UNGC Compliance Processes -- PAI-11 refers to an investment decision-making process that does not incorporate the United Nations Global Compact's principles or lacks procedures to verify compliance with its ten principles, indicating a gap in aligning portfolio activities with internationally recognised human rights, labour, environment and anti-corruption standards. This shortfall is considered an adverse sustainability impact under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation.
[PAI-12] Unexplained Gender Pay Gap -- PAI-12 flags an unexplained gender pay gap as an adverse sustainability indicator under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation. It requires financial entities to disclose any significant disparity in remuneration between male and female employees that cannot be justified by role, experience or performance, indicating potential gender-bias risks in an organisation's labour practices. This information helps investors assess the entity's social governance and commitment to gender equality.
[PAI-13] Board Gender Diversity -- PAI-13 under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation measures the principal adverse impact of gender diversity on a company's board, requiring disclosure of the proportion of women directors, any policies or targets aimed at improving balance, and the rationale behind the board composition.
[PAI-14] Controversial Weapons Exposure -- PAI-14 Controversial Weapons Exposure is a principal adverse impact indicator under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation that requires financial market participants to disclose the extent of their investment exposure to companies involved in the design, production, sale or use of weapons considered unacceptable, such as landmines, cluster munitions, chemical, biological or nuclear arms, and related support activities.
[PAI-2] Carbon Footprint -- PAI-2 Carbon Footprint is a principal adverse impact indicator under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation. It obliges financial market participants to disclose the greenhouse-gas emissions generated by their investment portfolios, expressed in absolute terms and relative intensity, to enable investors to assess climate-related risks.
[PAI-3] GHG Intensity of Investee Companies -- PAI-3 measures the greenhouse-gas intensity of companies in which an investor holds positions, expressed as emissions per unit of revenue or output. It assesses how the investee's carbon footprint relates to its economic activity, helping investors identify exposure to climate-related risks and align portfolios with low-carbon objectives.
[PAI-4] Fossil Fuel Sector Exposure -- PAI-4 under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation requires entities to disclose the proportion of their investments that are exposed to companies operating in the fossil-fuel sector, covering activities such as extraction, production, processing and distribution of coal, oil and gas. The metric helps investors gauge climate-related financial risks linked to fossil-fuel reliance.
[PAI-5] Non-Renewable Energy Consumption -- Under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, PAI-5 is the principal adverse impact indicator that measures an investment's exposure to non-renewable energy consumption. It quantifies the amount of energy derived from coal, oil and gas used by the entities in a portfolio, highlighting climate-related risk and the alignment of the investment with transition to low-carbon activities.
[PAI-6] Energy Consumption Intensity by High-Impact Sector -- PAI-6 quantifies the energy consumption per unit of output in sectors with high environmental impact, such as heavy industry, transport and power generation. It tracks changes in this intensity over time, allowing investors to gauge how their portfolio influences energy use and associated climate-related risks.
[PAI-7] Biodiversity-Sensitive Area Activities -- PAI-7 refers to investments in activities carried out in biodiversity-sensitive areas such as protected habitats, nature reserves and ecosystems of high conservation value, requiring disclosure of potential impacts on biodiversity and the steps taken to avoid, mitigate or remediate any adverse effects.
[PAI-8] Emissions to Water -- PAI-8, Emissions to Water, captures a company's discharge of pollutants and hazardous substances into water bodies, including the volume, toxicity and likelihood of adverse ecological effects. It requires reporting of quantitative or qualitative data on wastewater management, treatment processes and compliance with water-related regulations, enabling investors to assess water-related environmental risks.
[PAI-9] Hazardous Waste Ratio -- PAI-9 measures the proportion of hazardous waste produced relative to total waste generated by an entity, expressed as a percentage. It indicates the firm's contribution to environmentally harmful waste streams and is used under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation to assess adverse environmental impacts.
[SFDR-6] Article 6 - No Sustainability Focus -- SFDR-6 corresponds to Article 6 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, covering financial products that have no sustainability focus. These products do not promote environmental or social characteristics and lack a sustainable investment objective, so the regulator only requires a brief statement confirming the absence of sustainability considerations. The disclosure is limited to a clear, concise note that the product is not marketed as sustainable.
[SFDR-6-EX] Article 6 with ESG exclusions only -- SFDR-6-EX refers to products classified under Article 6 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation that rely solely on ESG exclusion screens. These products do not promote sustainable investment objectives, but must disclose the specific ESG criteria used to rule out certain assets.
[SFDR-6-STD] Standard Article 6 - No sustainability claims -- The SFDR-6-STD entry defines the baseline requirement for financial products that make no sustainability claims. Under Article 6, firms must disclose that the product does not integrate environmental, social or governance criteria, and provide transparent information on why sustainability risks or objectives are not considered.
[SFDR-8] Article 8 - ESG Characteristics Promoted -- Article 8 under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation requires financial products that promote environmental, social and governance characteristics to disclose how those characteristics are met, the methodologies used and any sustainability indicators, while ensuring that the product does not have a sustainable investment objective.
[SFDR-8-ENV] Article 8 - Environmental characteristics -- Article 8 products are financial instruments that promote environmental or social characteristics without having a sustainable investment objective. Under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation they must disclose how those environmental characteristics are met, including the methodology, data sources, and any alignment with the EU taxonomy.
[SFDR-8-GOV] Article 8 - Governance characteristics -- The SFDR-8-GOV entry refers to Article 8 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, which obliges financial products that promote environmental or social characteristics to disclose how their governance arrangements support those objectives. It requires transparency on board oversight, risk management, internal policies and procedures that ensure sustainable investment decisions are made responsibly.
[SFDR-8-SI] Article 8 with partial sustainable investment -- Funds classified under Article 8 that only partially align with sustainable investment criteria must disclose the proportion of investments deemed sustainable, describe the environmental or social characteristics they promote, and explain how those characteristics are met without claiming a fully sustainable portfolio.
[SFDR-8-SOC] Article 8 - Social characteristics -- SFDR-8-SOC refers to Article 8 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, which mandates that financial products promoting environmental or social characteristics must disclose how social factors are integrated into investment decisions. It requires transparent reporting on the methodologies used, the sustainability impact of holdings, and how the product aligns with recognised social objectives, enabling investors to assess the social dimension of the product's performance.
[SFDR-8-TAX] Article 8 - EU Taxonomy-aligned portion -- SFDR-8-TAX refers to the portion of Article 8 sustainable-finance products that are aligned with the EU Taxonomy. It requires managers to disclose how and to what extent their investments meet the taxonomy's environmental criteria, including quantitative metrics and the share of taxonomy-eligible assets. This transparency helps investors assess the environmental credibility of the product.
[SFDR-9] Article 9 - Sustainable Investment Objective -- SFDR-9 refers to Article 9 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, which covers financial products that pursue a sustainable investment objective. These products must invest in assets that contribute to an environmental or social goal, ensure no significant harm to other objectives, and provide detailed disclosures on methodology, sustainability indicators and taxonomies used to measure impact.
[SFDR-9-ENV] Article 9 - Environmental objective -- SFDR-9-ENV refers to Article 9 products under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation that pursue a specific environmental objective. These financial instruments must invest in assets that contribute to that objective, demonstrate a measurable positive environmental impact, and disclose how sustainability risks are managed and outcomes are monitored.
[SFDR-9-RED] Article 9 - Carbon reduction objective -- The SFDR-9-RED entry refers to Article 9 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, which mandates that financial products with a carbon-reduction objective must demonstrate how they contribute to lowering greenhouse-gas emissions. It requires detailed disclosure of the strategy, targets, and metrics used to achieve the carbon-reduction goal, ensuring transparency for investors seeking environmentally sustainable investments.
[SFDR-9-SOC] Article 9 - Social objective -- SFDR-9-SOC refers to Article 9 products that pursue a social objective, meaning the investment strategy aims to generate measurable positive social impacts while ensuring that any investments do not significantly harm other sustainability factors. Managers must disclose how the social objective is met
[SFDR-9-TAX] Article 9 - EU Taxonomy-aligned (100%) -- EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation Article 9 products require investment in sustainable activities and must meet the EU Taxonomy's environmental criteria. The SFDR-9-TAX entry indicates a fund that is fully (100%) taxonomy-aligned, meaning all its investments correspond to activities deemed environmentally sustainable under the EU Taxonomy framework.
[SFDR-PAI] Principal Adverse Impacts (PAI) -- Under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, the Principal Adverse Impacts (PAI) requirement obliges financial market participants to disclose how their investment decisions negatively affect environmental and social factors. The SFDR-PAI entry outlines the metrics and reporting standards that firms must use to assess and publish these adverse impacts, enhancing transparency for investors.
[SFDR-RTS] Regulatory Technical Standards Disclosures -- The SFDR-RTS entry refers to the Regulatory Technical Standards that specify how financial market participants must disclose sustainability information under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (EU 2019/2088). These standards detail the format, timing and content of disclosures on environmental, social and governance risks, impacts and objectives to ensure consistent, comparable reporting across the EU.
