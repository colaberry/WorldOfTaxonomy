[KC] Koppen Climate Types -- KC refers to the Köppen-Geiger climate classification, a system that sorts global climates into major types based on average temperature and precipitation patterns. The code groups the five primary climate categories-tropical, arid, temperate, continental and polar-allowing scientists to compare and map climatic zones worldwide.
[KC.01] Af: Tropical Rainforest -- The Af climate, known as tropical rainforest, features consistently high temperatures and heavy, year-round rainfall with no month receiving less than 60 mm. It is typical of equatorial regions such as the Amazon basin, Congo basin and parts of Southeast Asia.
[KC.02] Am: Tropical Monsoon -- Am, the tropical monsoon climate, features consistently high temperatures above 18 °C and a pronounced seasonal shift in rainfall. A short, relatively dry period occurs when the sun is lowest, but even the driest month receives enough precipitation to prevent true aridity, typically exceeding 60 mm and meeting the Köppen formula threshold. The remaining months experience heavy, often daily, rain driven by monsoonal wind patterns, supporting lush vegetation and high annual totals.
[KC.03] Aw: Tropical Savanna -- Aw denotes a tropical savanna climate with a pronounced dry season in the low-sun months and a wet season in the high-sun months; all months remain above 18 °C, and total annual rainfall is sufficient, but the dry season receives less than 60 mm and less than one-tenth of the wet-season total.
[KC.04] BWh: Hot Desert -- The BWh climate is a hot desert zone characterized by extremely low annual precipitation, often below 250 mm, and consistently high temperatures with mean monthly highs well above 30 °C. Evaporation far exceeds rainfall, producing arid landscapes dominated by sparse vegetation and clear skies, typical of regions such as the Sahara, Arabian Peninsula and parts of Australia.
[KC.05] BWk: Cold Desert -- BWk denotes a cold desert climate in the Köppen-Geiger system, characterized by arid conditions with very low precipitation and mean annual temperatures below 18 °C. It occurs in interior high-latitude regions such as parts of Central Asia, the western United States, and other continental interiors where cold winters and dry summers prevail.
[KC.06] BSh: Hot Semi-Arid -- BSh denotes a hot semi-arid climate characterized by low annual rainfall just enough to limit vegetation to grasses and scattered shrubs, while temperatures remain high, especially in summer. These regions experience a pronounced dry season, moderate evaporation, and are typically found on the edges of true deserts in subtropical latitudes.
[KC.07] BSk: Cold Semi-Arid -- BSk denotes a cold semi-arid climate, typical of mid-latitude interior regions where annual precipitation is low but not sufficient to classify the area as desert. Winters are cool to cold with mean temperatures below 0 °C, while summers are warm and dry, and potential evapotranspiration exceeds rainfall. The climate supports sparse grassland or steppe vegetation rather than dense forest.
[KC.08] Cfa: Humid Subtropical -- Cfa denotes a humid subtropical climate
[KC.09] Cfb: Oceanic -- The Cfb Oceanic climate is a temperate type with mild winters and warm, not hot, summers, and precipitation spread fairly evenly throughout the year. It lacks a distinct dry season and features moderate temperature ranges due to maritime influences, typical of western Europe, the Pacific Northwest, parts of New Zealand and southern Chile.
[KC.10] Csa: Hot-Summer Mediterranean -- KC.10 denotes the Csa hot-summer Mediterranean climate in the Köppen-Geiger system, featuring dry, hot summers with the warmest month averaging above 22 °C and mild, wetter winters; at least four months have average temperatures above 10 °C and summer precipitation is less than one-third of that in the wet season.
[KC.11] Csb: Warm-Summer Mediterranean -- Csb denotes a warm-summer Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and dry, warm (but not hot) summers; the warmest month stays below 22 °C while at least four months exceed 10 °C, and summer precipitation is less than one-third of the wettest winter month.
[KC.12] Dfa: Hot-Summer Humid Continental -- KC.12 denotes the Dfa climate in the Köppen-Geiger system, a humid continental type with hot summers and cold winters. Temperatures exceed 22 °C in the warmest month while at least four months stay above 10 °C, and precipitation is fairly evenly distributed throughout the year. This climate occurs in interior mid-latitude regions such as the US Midwest, southern Canada, and parts of Eastern Europe and East Asia.
[KC.13] Dfb: Warm-Summer Humid Continental -- The Dfb climate is a humid continental type with warm-summer characteristics; it experiences cold, often snowy winters and mild to warm summers where the average temperature of the warmest month stays below 22 °C. Precipitation is fairly evenly distributed throughout the year, with no pronounced dry season, and the climate is typical of regions such as the northeastern United States, southern Canada, and parts of central Europe.
[KC.14] Dfc: Subarctic -- Dfc is a subarctic climate marked by long, very cold winters and short, cool summers; at least one month averages above 10 °C while the coldest month remains below -3 °C, and precipitation is relatively evenly distributed throughout the year, supporting boreal forests in high-latitude regions.
[KC.15] ET: Tundra -- The ET (Tundra) climate is defined by having no month with an average temperature above 10 °C, while at least one month averages above 0 °C, indicating a short, cool summer and long, harsh winter. Permafrost is common, limiting vegetation to mosses, lichens and low shrubs.
[KC.16] EF: Ice Cap -- EF denotes an ice cap climate, characterized by permanent ice and snow cover with all monthly average temperatures below 0°C. It occurs in polar regions such as Antarctica and the interior of Greenland, where precipitation is low and the surface remains frozen year-round.
