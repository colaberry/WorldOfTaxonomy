[01] Live animals -- Code 01 of the EU Combined Nomenclature 2024 includes all live animals, covering mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians and fish presented in a living state at the time of trade, whether intended for food, breeding, research or other uses. The provision applies to any species regardless of origin or purpose of import or export.
[02] Meat and edible meat offal -- Chapter 02 includes fresh, chilled or frozen meat of mammals, poultry and other livestock together with edible off-al such as liver, kidneys, heart, tripe and other organs, provided they are intended for human consumption and excluding fish and crustacean products.
[03] Fish and crustaceans, molluscs and other aquatic invertebrates -- Entry 03 of the EU Combined Nomenclature groups together all fish and fish products, as well as crustaceans, molluscs and other aquatic invertebrates, whether fresh, chilled, frozen, dried or otherwise prepared for market. It covers whole or cut specimens, fillets, roe and related off-cuts, including preparations such as smoked or salted fish, and includes both wild-caught and farmed varieties.
[04] Dairy produce; birds eggs; natural honey; edible products of animal origin -- Code 04 covers a broad group of edible animal-derived foods, including fresh, skimmed, powdered and processed dairy products such as milk, butter, cream and cheese, as well as bird eggs, natural honey and other edible animal origin items like meat spreads, gelatin and animal fats.
[05] Products of animal origin, not elsewhere specified or included -- Code 05 gathers miscellaneous animal-origin goods that are not classified elsewhere in the nomenclature, including animal fats and oils, skins, hides and pelts, wool, hair, other animal fibres and various by-products such as bone meal or gelatin. It serves as a catch-all for animal-derived products that do not fall under separate headings for meat, dairy, live animals or primary animal commodities.
[06] Live trees and other plants; bulbs, roots and similar -- The CN 06 heading covers all living trees, shrubs, perennial plants and other vegetative material intended for planting, together with bulbs, tubers, rhizomes, roots and similar propagation units. It includes whole plants and parts that can be planted directly, but excludes cut flowers, seeds and processed plant products that fall under other headings.

[08] Edible fruit and nuts; peel of citrus fruit or melons -- Code 08 of the EU Combined Nomenclature groups together edible fruit and nuts, including fresh, dried or processed forms, and also the peel of citrus fruits and melons. These products are classified for customs and statistical purposes and covered by the common external tariff rates applicable to agricultural goods.
[09] Coffee, tea, mate and spices -- Code 09 in the EU Combined Nomenclature groups coffee, tea, yerba mate and a wide range of spices intended for human consumption. It includes unroasted and roasted coffee beans, ground coffee, tea leaves, mate leaves, and dried or ground herbs, spices and spice mixes, irrespective of packaging, for customs duty and trade statistics.
[10] Cereals -- Code 10 in the EU Combined Nomenclature 2024 groups all cereal grains such as wheat, rye, barley, oats, maize, rice and other edible cereal crops, whether whole, cleaned, or hulled, and includes cereal seed for sowing; it forms the basis for customs duty rates, trade statistics and monitoring of agricultural markets across the Union.

It serves as a broad category for trade statistics on these agricultural products.
[13] Lac; gums, resins and other vegetable saps and extracts -- Code 13 covers natural plant-derived products such as lac, various gums, resins and other vegetable saps and extracts used in food, cosmetics and industrial applications. [15] Animal, vegetable or microbial fats and oils and their cleavage products -- Code 15 covers animal, vegetable or microbial fats and oils, whether crude, refined, hydrogenated or fractionated, and their cleavage products such as fatty acids, glycerides and other derivatives.

It excludes fresh or chilled meat and fish and does not apply to preparations that are primarily or exclusively composed of other ingredients. It classifies both bulk sugar commodities and finished sugary treats for customs and statistical purposes.

It also includes ready-to-eat mixes and food bases used for further processing, ensuring that miscellaneous culinary products are captured for customs and statistical purposes.
[22] Beverages, spirits and vinegar -- Chapter 22 of the EU Combined Nomenclature classifies all non-alcoholic and alcoholic drinks and vinegar. The category covers both material destined for disposal or recycling and fodder formulated from these substances.

[26] Ores, slag and ash -- The CN heading 26 covers natural inorganic substances such as ores and other mineral products that have been extracted, as well as residues resulting from metal smelting, refining or similar processes, including slag, ash, and certain waste materials derived from these operations. It excludes any products that have been processed into intermediate or finished goods.

[S07] Plastics and Articles Thereof; Rubber and Articles Thereof -- The S07 heading covers primary plastics and rubber materials as well as finished goods made from them, including polymerized plastics, extruded or molded sheets, rods and tubes, and articles such as containers, pipes, hoses, belts, flooring and footwear components. It also includes rubber in both natural and synthetic forms and all rubber products, ranging from tyres and tubes to seals, gaskets and molded goods. This classification groups together the raw polymeric substances and their manufactured articles for customs and statistical purposes.
[S08] Raw Hides and Skins, Leather, Furskins and Articles Thereof -- S08 includes raw animal hides and skins together with leather and furskins, and also the finished articles made from these materials such as tanned leather goods, sheepskins, calfskin and other processed products.
[S09] Wood and Articles of Wood; Wood Charcoal; Cork and Cork Articles -- S09 groups unprocessed timber and its basic products, such as sawn, planed or split wood, wood chips, bolts, poles and panels, together with wood charcoal used as fuel. It also includes cork and all articles made from cork, ranging from raw sheets to finished goods like stoppers, flooring and insulation products, covering both raw materials and simple manufactured items derived from wood or cork.
[S10] Pulp of Wood; Paper and Paperboard; Articles Thereof; Printed Books -- S10 in the EU Combined Nomenclature 2024 classifies wood pulp, paper and paperboard, and all articles produced from these materials, including printed books and similar printed or bound products.
[S11] Textiles and Textile Articles -- S11 groups all textile products and their finished forms, covering yarns, woven, knitted and non-woven fabrics, as well as items such as towels, blankets, curtains, upholstery cloth and other textile articles not classified elsewhere. The heading excludes clothing and footwear, which fall under separate sub-headings.
[S12] Footwear, Headgear, Umbrellas, Walking Sticks and Similar Articles -- S12 in the EU Combined Nomenclature groups a wide range of personal accessories, covering all footwear, headgear, umbrellas and walking sticks, together with comparable items such as cane-type supports and protective hats. The chapter includes both knitted and non-knitted products, whether made of leather, textile, plastic, rubber or other materials, but excludes specialised safety shoes or garments that fall under separate headings. It serves as a catch-all for everyday wearable articles.
[S13] Articles of Stone, Plaster, Cement, Ceramics, Glass and Glassware -- S13 groups finished articles made primarily of stone, plaster, cement, ceramics, glass or glassware, covering a wide range of products such as tiles, sanitary ware, decorative objects, tableware and other household or industrial items. The classification applies to goods that have been shaped, glazed or otherwise processed, rather than raw or semi-finished materials. It is used for customs duties, trade statistics and regulatory purposes within the EU.
[S14] Natural or Cultured Pearls, Precious Metals, Jewellery, Coins -- S14 groups commodities classified as natural or cultured pearls, articles made of precious metals, various types of jewellery, and coins for use in trade statistics and customs duties. Items are assessed under a common rate unless a specific sub-heading provides a different duty.
[S15] Base Metals and Articles of Base Metal -- S15 covers base metals such as copper, nickel, lead, zinc and their alloys as well as finished products made from these metals, including plates, bars, rods, wires and forged items that are not further specified elsewhere in the nomenclature.
[S16] Machinery and Mechanical Appliances; Electrical Equipment -- S16 groups together all machinery, mechanical appliances and electrical equipment, including their parts, accessories and installations used in manufacturing, construction, agriculture and domestic settings. The chapter covers devices for processing, cooling, heating, power generation, telecommunications, lighting and household appliances, together with related components and spare parts.
[S17] Vehicles, Aircraft, Vessels and Associated Transport Equipment -- S17 groups together all means of transport and related equipment, including road vehicles, aircraft, ships and other vessels, as well as parts, accessories and ancillary items used for their operation, maintenance or construction, covering both complete units and components that are integral to the transport system.
[S18] Optical, Photographic, Medical or Surgical Instruments and Apparatus -- S18 groups together a range of precision devices used for visual, imaging and health-care purposes, such as microscopes, telescopes, cameras, lenses, endoscopes, diagnostic and therapeutic medical or surgical instruments, and related apparatuses that employ optical or photographic technology. It covers both standalone equipment and components designed for professional or clinical use.
[S19] Arms and Ammunition; Parts and Accessories Thereof -- S19 in the EU Combined Nomenclature 2024 groups all weapons and firearms, ammunition and related explosives, and the parts, accessories and components used to assemble, repair or operate them. The heading applies to complete arms, individual ammunition cartridges, shells and projectiles, as well as magazines, sights, triggers and other detachable items, and is subject to specific customs duties and export-control regulations.
[S20] Miscellaneous Manufactured Articles -- The S20 chapter groups together a wide variety of manufactured goods that are not covered by more specific headings, such as household utensils, office supplies, small mechanical devices and other assorted articles made of metal, plastic or wood. It functions as a residual category for miscellaneous manufactured articles requiring customs treatment.
[S21] Works of Art, Collectors Pieces and Antiques; EU Special Codes -- S21 groups items classified as works of art, collectors' pieces and antiques, covering paintings, sculptures, drawings, prints, photographs, objets d'art and other cultural goods of artistic, historical or archeological significance. It also includes EU-specific special codes used for customs duty, statistical reporting and trade policy purposes.
