[RW] RCRA Waste Categories -- RW denotes the RCRA Waste Categories, a system the EPA uses to group hazardous waste streams based on their origin, composition, and regulatory status under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act. The categories help determine handling, treatment, and disposal requirements, ensuring compliance with federal hazardous waste management standards.
[RW.01] F-Listed Wastes (Non-Specific Source) -- RW.01 refers to the F-listed hazardous wastes in the EPA's RCRA system, which are wastes containing specific hazardous constituents but not tied to a particular industrial process. These non-specific source wastes are regulated because the listed chemicals pose a threat to health or the environment regardless of where they originate.
[RW.02] K-Listed Wastes (Specific Source) -- RW.02 refers to K-listed hazardous wastes, a group of specific source wastes identified by name in the RCRA regulations. These include particular industrial by-products such as certain spent solvents, sludges, and waste streams from specific manufacturing processes, and they are regulated as hazardous regardless of their physical or chemical characteristics.
[RW.03] P-Listed Wastes (Acutely Hazardous) -- RW.03 designates the EPA's P-Listed hazardous wastes, which are classified as acutely hazardous because they contain toxic chemicals at concentrations that can cause immediate health effects. These wastes are subject to the most stringent RCRA regulations for identification, handling, transportation, storage, and disposal to protect human health and the environment.
[RW.04] U-Listed Wastes (Toxic) -- RW.04 denotes the EPA's "U-Listed" hazardous wastes, which are specific process wastes identified as toxic because they contain characteristic contaminants such as heavy metals, solvents, or pesticides. These wastes are regulated under RCRA, requiring generators to determine eligibility, label, track, and dispose of them according to strict handling and reporting requirements.
[RW.05] D-Characteristic Ignitability (D001) -- RW.05 designates waste that meets the RCRA D001 ignitability characteristic, meaning it can readily catch fire or sustain combustion. This includes liquids with a flash point below 60 °C, solids that are combustible or have a high surface area, and certain gases, all of which trigger hazardous waste handling and disposal requirements.
[RW.06] D-Characteristic Corrosivity (D002) -- RW.06 denotes the D-Characteristic Corrosivity (D002) category under EPA's RCRA hazardous waste rules, identifying waste that exhibits corrosive properties based on pH criteria-typically pH ≤2 or ≥12.5 for liquids, or strong acid or base content for solids, indicating the material can damage living tissue or equipment. This classification helps regulators determine appropriate handling, labeling, and disposal requirements.
[RW.07] D-Characteristic Reactivity (D003) -- RW.07 identifies waste classified under the D-Characteristic for reactivity, known as D003. These are materials that are unstable, readily decompose or react violently with water, air, or other substances, posing a fire or explosion hazard. The classification triggers specific handling, storage, and disposal requirements under RCRA.
[RW.08] D-Characteristic Toxicity (D004-D043) -- RW.08 identifies hazardous waste that meets the D-characteristic for toxicity, referencing the EPA's list of toxic constituents from D004 to D043 such as arsenic, lead, mercury and certain pesticides. Waste classified under this code is subject to stringent RCRA regulations for labeling, storage, transportation and disposal to protect human health and the environment.
[RW.09] Mixed Waste (Radioactive + Hazardous) -- RW.09 designates waste that simultaneously contains hazardous constituents regulated under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act and radioactive material subject to atomic energy regulations. Such mixed waste must be managed, treated, and disposed of in compliance with both EPA hazardous-waste rules and NRC/DOE radioactive-waste requirements, often requiring specialized containment, labeling, and tracking to ensure protection of health and the environment.
[RW.10] Universal Waste -- Universal waste is a category of hazardous waste that includes commonly discarded items such as batteries, pesticide containers, mercury-bearing equipment and fluorescent lamps, and is subject to simplified handling, storage and recycling rules under the RCRA to promote safer management.
[RW.11] Used Oil -- RW.11 refers to used oil generated from industrial or commercial activities, including lubricants, hydraulic fluids, and related products
[RW.12] E-Waste -- RW.12 identifies discarded electronic and electrical equipment-including computers, phones, televisions, and appliances-that contain hazardous components such as lead, mercury, cadmium, or brominated flame retardants. Under RCRA these items are classified as hazardous waste and must be managed, stored, and disposed of in accordance with federal hazardous waste regulations.
[RW.13] Pharmaceutical Waste -- RW.13 is the EPA RCRA designation for pharmaceutical waste, covering discarded drugs, vaccines, antibiotics, and related materials that may contain hazardous constituents. This category requires specific handling, storage, and disposal procedures to prevent risks to human health and the environment.
[RW.14] Conditionally Exempt SQG Waste -- RW.14 denotes conditionally exempt small-quantity generator (SQG) waste under the EPA's RCRA program. These wastes are generated in quantities below the hazardous-waste threshold and meet specific criteria that allow them to be treated as non-hazardous, exempting generators from full hazardous-waste regulatory requirements while still subject to minimal reporting and handling rules.
