It sets the lowest level of capital that firms must hold at all times to cover basic risks and protect policyholders, with the amount calibrated to each insurer's risk profile.

[pillar_3] Pillar 3 - Reporting and Disclosure -- Directive 2009/138/EC, known as Solvency II, defines Pillar 3 as the regulatory framework for insurers and reinsurers to disclose quantitative and qualitative information on their solvency, risk profile and governance, ensuring market transparency and comparability across the EU.
[public_disc] Public Disclosure Requirements -- The EU Directive 2009/138/EC mandates public disclosure requirements for insurance and reinsurance firms, obliging them to provide transparent information on financial condition, risk profile, governance and key policies. [scr] Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR) -- The Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR) is the core quantitative capital standard defined by Directive 2009/138/EC, setting the amount of assets insurers must hold to limit the probability of insolvency to 0.5 % over one year. It is calculated using a prescribed standard formula or an approved internal model to capture market, underwriting and operational risk exposures.

[standard_formula] Standard Formula -- The Standard Formula is the prescriptive method set out in Directive 2009/138/EC for calculating insurance and reinsurance firms' capital requirements under Solvency II. [supervisory_review] Supervisory Review Process -- The supervisory review process under Directive 2009/138/EC, known as Solvency II, sets out how national regulators assess insurers' risk management, governance and capital adequacy. [technical_prov] Technical Provisions -- Technical provisions are the amounts insurers must set aside to meet future policyholder liabilities, covering premiums, claims, expenses and a risk margin as required by Directive 2009/138/EC, also known as Solvency II.
