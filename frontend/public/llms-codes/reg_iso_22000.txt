[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- In clause 10.1 of ISO 22000:2018, organizations must identify any nonconformities in their food safety management system, assess their causes and determine appropriate corrective actions. The process requires documenting the issue, implementing measures to eliminate the cause, verifying effectiveness, and retaining records to prevent recurrence. This ensures continual improvement and compliance with food safety requirements.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 22000:2018 mandates that an organization systematically identify, evaluate and act on opportunities for enhancing its food safety management system, using performance data, audit results and management review outcomes to drive continual improvement.
[10_3] 10.3 Update of the FSMS -- Clause 10.3 of ISO 22000:2018 requires an organization to update its Food Safety Management System whenever changes occur that could affect food safety, documenting the modifications, reviewing their impact on system effectiveness, and communicating the updates to relevant parties. This ensures the FSMS remains current and continues to meet the standard's safety requirements.

[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the FSMS -- ISO 22000:2018 clause 4.3 requires an organization to define the limits and applicability of its Food Safety Management System, specifying the products, processes, sites and interactions that are covered. The scope must be documented, justified and communicated, ensuring that all relevant hazards and regulatory requirements are addressed within the defined boundaries.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- In ISO 22000:2018 clause 5.1 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment by establishing, implementing and maintaining the food safety management system, ensuring the safety policy, objectives and necessary resources are defined, and fostering a culture of continual improvement.
[5_2] 5.2 Food safety policy -- The food safety policy is a top-level statement of an organization's commitment to food safety, establishing objectives, scope, and responsibilities while ensuring compliance with legal and customer requirements. It must be documented, communicated to all relevant parties, and periodically reviewed for effectiveness.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 of ISO 22000:2018 requires an organization to identify food-safety hazards and other risks, then plan and implement actions to eliminate, mitigate, or control them while also taking advantage of opportunities for improvement. These actions are integrated into the food safety management system and must be monitored, evaluated, and updated as needed.

[7_1] 7.1 Resources -- ISO 22000:2018 clause 7.1 requires an organization to identify, allocate and maintain all resources needed for effective operation of its food safety management system, covering human competence, infrastructure, work environment, equipment, and monitoring tools, ensuring they are suitable, available and continually reviewed.
[7_4] 7.4 Communication -- Entry 7.4 of ISO 22000:2018 requires the organization to establish and maintain effective communication processes for food-safety information, both internally among functions and externally with interested parties. It specifies that relevant details about hazards, policies, procedures, changes and performance must be identified, documented and shared promptly to ensure awareness and coordination across the food-safety management system.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 22000:2018 requires organizations to plan, implement and control the processes needed to achieve their food-safety objectives, including hazard identification, determination of control measures, and allocation of resources. It mandates documented procedures for operational planning, monitoring, and corrective actions to ensure consistent product safety throughout the supply chain.
[8_2] 8.2 Prerequisite programmes (PRPs) -- Entry 8.2 in ISO 22000:2018 defines prerequisite programmes (PRPs) as the foundational food-safety activities that control the operational environment, infrastructure, and basic conditions necessary for safe food production. It specifies how organizations must identify, implement and maintain these programmes to eliminate or reduce hazards before the HACCP plan is applied.
[8_3] 8.3 Traceability system -- Section 8.3 of ISO 22000:2018 specifies that an organization must establish, implement and maintain a documented traceability system capable of identifying product identity, ingredients and each step of the supply chain, both forward and backward. The system must enable rapid response to safety incidents, support product recalls or withdrawals, and be integrated with the overall food safety management and HACCP processes.
[8_4] 8.4 Emergency preparedness and response -- Clause 8.4 of ISO 22000:2018 requires organizations to establish, implement and maintain procedures for emergency preparedness and response, ensuring that potential food-safety incidents such as contamination, natural disasters or equipment failures are anticipated, controlled and mitigated. It mandates identification of hazards, assignment of responsibilities, provision of resources, training, communication protocols and periodic testing or review to maintain effective readiness.
[8_5] 8.5 Hazard analysis (HACCP) -- Section 8.5 of ISO 22000:2018 describes the hazard analysis requirement of the HACCP system. It directs organizations to identify, evaluate and document all biological, chemical and physical hazards that could affect food safety at each step of the chain, establishing control points and criteria for their prevention. This analysis provides the foundation for the food safety management system.
[8_6] 8.6 Updating PRP and hazard analysis -- The clause requires that the food-safety team periodically review and revise both the prerequisite programs and the hazard analysis whenever new information, changes in product or process, monitoring results, audit findings, or regulatory updates arise, ensuring that the management system remains current and effective in controlling identified hazards.
[8_7] 8.7 Control of monitoring and measuring -- Clause 8.7 of ISO 22000:2018 requires organizations to control all monitoring and measuring activities that affect food safety, ensuring that methods are validated, equipment is calibrated and maintained, and results are recorded and reviewed. It establishes procedures for handling non-conforming measurements, preserving traceability, and demonstrating that monitoring data remain reliable for decision-making.
[8_8] 8.8 Verification related to PRPs and the hazard control plan -- In ISO 22000:2018 the 8.8 clause requires organizations to verify that prerequisite programs and the hazard control plan are effectively implemented and remain suitable for controlling food safety hazards. Verification activities must be documented, systematic and reviewed regularly to ensure ongoing compliance.
[8_9] 8.9 Control of product and process nonconformities -- Clause 8.9 of ISO 22000:2018 requires organizations to identify, document and control any nonconforming product or process, ensuring it is isolated, evaluated and either corrected, reprocessed, or disposed of to prevent unintended use. It also mandates records of actions taken and verification of effectiveness.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 22000:2018 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating food-safety performance and the effectiveness of the management system. It calls for documented procedures, appropriate indicators, regular data collection, and periodic review to identify trends, non-conformities and opportunities for continual improvement.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- ISO 22000:2018 clause 9.2 mandates that organizations perform internal audits at planned intervals to confirm that the food-safety management system meets the standard's requirements and operates effectively. Audits must be carried out by competent personnel, documented with findings and corrective actions, and followed up to ensure continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 requires top management to conduct regular reviews of the food safety management system to confirm its continued suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must consider performance metrics, audit results, customer feedback, non-conformities and changes in internal or external issues, and result in decisions on improvements, resource needs and any necessary modifications to the system.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 22000:2018 focuses on improvement, requiring organizations to monitor performance, identify non-conformities and take corrective actions, and to continually enhance the effectiveness of the food safety management system through systematic review and preventive measures. It emphasizes a cycle of evaluation, learning and systematic upgrades.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 requires an organization to identify and evaluate internal and external issues that affect its food-safety objectives, to determine the needs and expectations of relevant interested parties, and to define the scope of its ISO 22000 management system. This contextual analysis ensures that the food-safety management system is tailored to the organization's specific circumstances and stakeholder requirements.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 outlines the leadership responsibilities of top management in a food-safety management system, requiring them to demonstrate commitment, establish the food safety policy, set objectives, provide necessary resources, and ensure effective communication and continual improvement throughout the organization.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 sets out the planning requirements for an ISO 22000 food safety management system. It directs organisations to identify hazards, establish food-safety objectives, determine actions to achieve those objectives, and integrate the planning process into the overall management system.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 22000:2018 outlines the support requirements for a food safety management system, specifying the resources, competence, awareness, communication and documented information needed to ensure effective operation and continual improvement. It emphasizes providing adequate infrastructure, environment, and control of documented procedures to maintain food safety throughout the organization.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 22000:2018 specifies the operational requirements of a food safety management system, detailing how organizations must plan, control, and monitor food-safety activities across the supply chain. It includes requirements for prerequisite programs, hazard control measures, validation, verification and emergency preparedness to ensure safe production and handling of food.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 22000:2018 defines the performance evaluation requirements for a food safety management system. It covers monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation of the system, internal audits, and management review to ensure effectiveness and continual improvement.
