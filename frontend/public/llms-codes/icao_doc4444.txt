[FR] ICAO Flight/Airspace -- FR is an ICAO code that designates the general flight rule or airspace category applicable to a region, indicating that the airspace operates under standard flight rules without special restrictions. It is used in aeronautical information publications to identify areas where normal VFR or IFR procedures apply.
[FR.01] VFR (Visual Flight Rules) -- FR.01 designates VFR, the visual flight regime where pilots operate aircraft primarily by sight, maintaining separation from terrain, obstacles and other traffic without reliance on instrument guidance. It requires specific weather minima-clear skies, adequate visibility and distance from clouds-set by ICAO for each airspace class and may be limited to daylight unless approved for night VFR.
[FR.02] IFR (Instrument Flight Rules) -- FR.02 designates IFR, which means aircraft operate under Instrument Flight Rules using onboard instruments and ATC clearances for navigation and separation, typically required in controlled airspace, reduced visibility, or adverse weather conditions. It indicates that pilots must follow prescribed procedures and maintain communication with air traffic services.

[FR.04] Class A Airspace -- Class A airspace is high-altitude controlled airspace where only aircraft operating under instrument flight rules may enter, and all flights are subject to positive air-traffic control separation. It typically extends from flight level 180 up to the upper limit of controlled airspace, providing a uniform environment for IFR operations worldwide.
[FR.05] Class B Airspace -- Class B airspace is a controlled area surrounding major international airports, extending from the surface up to a designated upper limit, typically between 10 000 feet and 12 000 feet mean sea level. All aircraft operating in this zone must obtain an ATC clearance, and both IFR and VFR flights are subject to specific entry procedures and speed restrictions.
[FR.06] Class C Airspace -- Class C airspace is a controlled zone surrounding major airports, extending from the surface up to a designated upper limit, typically 4 000 ft or higher. Both IFR and VFR flights must obtain ATC clearance, and VFR pilots are required to have two-way radio communication, meet prescribed visibility and cloud-clearance minima, and remain within the airspace's lateral boundaries.
[FR.07] Class D Airspace -- Class D airspace is controlled airspace surrounding airports that have an operational control tower, where air traffic control provides separation for IFR flights and traffic information for VFR flights. Pilots must maintain two-way radio communication with ATC and follow any ATC instructions while operating within this airspace.
[FR.08] Class E Airspace -- Class E airspace is controlled airspace that begins at a designated lower limit-often the surface, 700 ft, 1,200 ft, or another altitude-and extends up to 18,000 ft MSL. VFR operations are allowed without an ATC clearance, though pilots must comply with visibility and cloud-clearance requirements and are subject to IFR separation services when necessary.
[FR.09] Class F Airspace -- Class F is a miscellaneous airspace category used where neither controlled nor uncontrolled conditions apply, typically for special activities such as military operations, parachute jumps, or glider flights. [FR.10] Class G Airspace -- Class G airspace is uncontrolled airspace where air traffic control does not provide separation services; pilots operate under visual-flight rules and are responsible for their own separation. It typically extends from the surface up to a designated altitude, often 1 200 ft AGL or higher in remote areas, and may be used for low-level VFR operations and certain IFR flights with prior clearance.
[FR.11] Prohibited Area -- A Prohibited Area is airspace designated by ICAO where all flight operations are forbidden unless specifically authorized by the controlling authority. It is established to protect national security, hazardous activities, or other sensitive sites, and entry without permission can result in enforcement action.
[FR.12] Restricted Area -- A Restricted Area is a defined portion of airspace where flight is prohibited except by permission from the controlling authority, typically established for military operations, security zones, or hazardous activities; unauthorized entry may be subject to interception or penalties.
[FR.13] Danger Area -- A Danger Area is a designated airspace where activities such as weapons testing, live-fire exercises, or other hazardous operations are conducted, posing a risk to aircraft. Entry into the area is prohibited unless the pilot obtains prior clearance from the controlling authority, and the area is typically marked on aeronautical charts.
[FR.14] RVSM (Reduced Vertical Separation) -- RVSM (Reduced Vertical Separation) is a set of ICAO flight-rule provisions that lower the standard vertical separation between aircraft from 2,000 ft to 1,000 ft within the flight level band of FL 290 to FL 410 (approximately 29 000-41 000 ft). It enhances airspace capacity while maintaining safety, requiring aircraft and operators to meet equipment, performance, and certification criteria for operation in the reduced-separation airspace.
