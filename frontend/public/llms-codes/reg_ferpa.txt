[def_attendance] Attendance at or Connected to an Institution (99.3) -- The entry defines how schools may collect, use, and disclose student attendance records under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, outlining parental and student rights, permissible disclosures, and exceptions. It applies to any attendance information that is directly tied to an educational institution, as classified in section 99.3.
[definitions] Definitions and Scope -- The "definitions and scope" entry of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. § 1232g) establishes the key terms used throughout the statute-such as "education record," "school," and "eligible student"-and delineates the law's applicability to institutions that receive federal funds, describing the rights of students and parents to access, amend, and control the disclosure of those records.
[def_institution] Educational Agency or Institution (99.1) -- The entry defines an "educational agency or institution" as any public or private school, college, university or other entity that receives federal funds and keeps education records of students. Under FERPA this definition determines which organizations are subject to the law's privacy and access provisions.
[def_parent] Parent Definition (99.3) -- Under FERPA, a "parent" is defined as a child's natural or adoptive parent, a legal guardian, or any individual with legal authority to act on the child's behalf, including those holding power of attorney. This definition determines who may access a student's education records.
[def_student] Eligible Student (99.3) -- An Eligible Student under FERPA is any person enrolled in a post-secondary institution who is either a regular student or, when the school receives federal funds, a student receiving financial assistance under a federal program, such as Title IV.
[disc_exceptions] Exceptions to Consent (99.31) -- The entry "Exceptions to Consent (99.31)" outlines the limited circumstances under which schools may disclose personally identifiable education records without prior written consent from the student or parent, as permitted by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974. These exceptions include disclosures to school officials with legitimate educational interests, other schools where the student is transferring, certain law-enforcement agencies, and situations involving health or safety emergencies.
[disc_financial_aid] Financial Aid Purposes (99.31(a)(4)) -- Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, schools may disclose a student's education records without consent when the information is needed for financial aid purposes, as defined in 20 U.S.C. § 1232g and the regulation at 99.31(a)(4). This entry, coded disc_financial_aid, identifies that specific permissible disclosure category.
[disc_general] General Consent Requirement (99.30) -- The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 mandates that an educational institution must obtain a written, specific consent from a student or parent before releasing any personally identifiable information from the student's education records, except in narrowly defined situations such as health and safety emergencies, compliance with a court order, or transfer of records to another school. This general consent requirement is codified at 20 USC 1232g and summarized under entry disc_general.
[disc_health_safety] Health or Safety Emergency (99.31(a)(10), 99.36) -- Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, the health or safety emergency provision permits schools to disclose a student's education records without prior consent when an immediate threat to the health or safety of the student or others is identified, in accordance with sections 99.31(a)(10) and 99.36. This exception is intended to enable timely protective actions while balancing privacy rights.
[disc_judicial] Judicial Order or Subpoena (99.31(a)(9)) -- Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, a judicial order or subpoena (99.31(a)(9)) allows a school to disclose a student's education records when compelled by a court order, subpoena, or similar legal process. This exception permits compliance with lawful governmental requests while still protecting other privacy provisions.
[disclosure] Disclosure and Consent -- The Disclosure and Consent entry under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 outlines how schools may release a student's personally identifiable education records. It requires written consent from the student or parent, except for specified permissible disclosures such as health or safety emergencies.
[disc_recordkeeping] Recordkeeping Requirements (99.32) -- The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 mandates that educational institutions keep detailed records of each request for access to, amendment of, or disclosure of student education records, the institution's response, and any related communications. These records must be retained for a minimum period, typically three years from the date the request was made, to ensure compliance and enable verification of proper handling.
[disc_school_official] School Officials with Legitimate Educational Interest (99.31(a)(1)) -- Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 USC 1232g), a "school official with legitimate educational interest" is any employee, contractor or volunteer who needs to review a student's education records to fulfill a professional duty related to the student's education, such as teaching, counseling, administration or health services. Access is permitted only when the official can demonstrate that the information is necessary to perform that specific function.
[disc_studies] Studies for Educational Agencies (99.31(a)(6)) -- The disc_studies entry refers to research and analysis undertaken by educational agencies to evaluate compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, which governs the protection of student education records. It includes studies of data handling, privacy safeguards, and policy implementation as specified in section 99.31(a)(6).
[disc_transfer] Transfer to Other Schools (99.31(a)(2)) -- The entry refers to the FERPA provision that permits a student to have his or her education records transferred to another school, as outlined in 99.31(a)(2). It requires the originating institution to provide a complete and accurate copy of the requested records while preserving the privacy protections mandated by the act.
[enf_office] Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO) -- The Family Policy Compliance Office (FPCO) is the agency designated to enforce the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 U.S.C. § 1232g). It oversees compliance with the law, fields complaints, and provides guidance to educational institutions on protecting student education records and ensuring parental access rights.
[enforcement] Enforcement and Compliance -- The enforcement and compliance provisions of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act give the Department of Education authority to investigate complaints, impose penalties, and require institutions to safeguard student education records, while providing individuals the right to appeal and outlining civil and criminal sanctions for violations.
[enf_penalties] Funding Termination Remedies (99.67) -- The entry details the remedies that apply when a school's federal funding is terminated under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, specifying corrective actions, restitution, and possible legal recourse to address violations.
[enf_procedures] Investigation Procedures (99.63-99.67) -- The entry outlines the procedural steps that educational institutions must follow when investigating alleged violations of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act. It specifies notice requirements, timelines, the handling of records, and safeguards to protect student privacy and ensure due process throughout the inquiry.
[rec_definition] Definition of Education Records (99.3) -- The entry defines "education records" as any records, files, documents, or other materials that contain personally identifiable information directly related to a student and that are maintained by an educational agency or institution, or by a party acting for the agency. It includes records such as grades, transcripts, class lists, student photographs, and disciplinary files, but excludes records kept by a law enforcement unit, a medical record, or a sole-custody parent's personal notes.
[rec_directory] Directory Information (99.3, 99.37) -- The Directory Information entry identifies the types of student records that schools may disclose without prior written consent under FERPA, such as name, address, and enrollment status, unless the student opts out. It references sections 99.3 and 99.37 of the regulation, guiding institutions on permissible disclosures and required notifications.
[rec_excluded] Records Excluded from Definition (99.3(b)) -- The "Records Excluded from Definition" entry identifies categories of information that do not qualify as education records under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act, such as law-enforcement records, medical files, and records maintained by a school for personal use. Because they fall outside the FERPA definition, these records are not subject to the Act's privacy and access requirements.
[rec_maintenance] Maintenance and Security of Records -- The entry outlines FERPA's obligations to keep education records safe and accurate, specifying how institutions must store, protect, retain, and dispose of student files. It details technical and administrative safeguards, access controls, and procedures for updating records to ensure privacy compliance.
[records] Education Records -- The entry defines "education records" under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, which includes any records, files, documents, and other materials containing information directly related to a student and maintained by an educational agency or institution, whether in electronic or physical form, that are not classified as exempt from FERPA's privacy protections.
[right_amend] Right to Request Amendment of Records (99.20) -- Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974, the right_amend entry grants a student the ability to request that a school correct or amend education records they believe are inaccurate, misleading, or violative of their privacy. The institution must review the request, provide a written explanation of its decision, and may deny the amendment only if it determines the record is accurate and complies with the law.
[right_annual] Annual Notification of Rights (99.7) -- The entry "right_annual" refers to the annual notification required by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (20 U.S.C. § 1232g). Schools must inform students and parents of their FERPA rights each year, covering access to education records, the ability to seek amendment, and limits on disclosure. This notification ensures continued awareness of privacy protections.
[right_complaint] Right to File a Complaint (99.63-99.64) -- The entry explains that any student, parent, or eligible student may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education if they believe a school has violated the privacy protections of the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act. It outlines the filing process, required documentation, and the Department's authority to investigate and enforce compliance.
[right_consent] Right to Consent to Disclosures (99.30) -- The entry refers to the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 U.S.C. § 1232g), specifically the "right to consent to disclosures" provision that obliges schools to obtain written permission from a student or parent before releasing personally identifiable education records, except where FERPA permits disclosures without consent.
[right_inspect] Right to Inspect and Review Education Records (99.10) -- The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act of 1974 (20 USC 1232g) gives parents and eligible students the right to inspect and review the student's education records maintained by a school. Schools must permit access within a reasonable time, allow copies for a fee, and provide a process to request amendment of inaccurate information.
[rights] Student Rights and Protections -- The rights entry outlines the privacy and access rights granted to students under FERPA, including the ability to inspect and amend education records, control disclosure of personally identifiable information, and receive notification of record releases. It also details institutional obligations to protect confidentiality and procedures for filing complaints.
