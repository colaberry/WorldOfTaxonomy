[eco_modulation] Eco-Modulated Fees -- The eco_modulation entry sets fees that vary with a producer's environmental performance, rewarding lower waste generation and higher recyclability of packaging. Under Regulation (EU) 2025/40, fees are reduced for companies that meet stricter targets and increased for those that fall short, creating a financial incentive to improve packaging design and waste management.
[epr] Extended Producer Responsibility -- The entry epr refers to the EU Regulation 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste, which establishes extended producer responsibility obligations requiring manufacturers to finance collection, recycling and recovery and to meet set recovery targets. It aims to shift waste-management costs to producers, encourage eco-design and lessen environmental impact.
[epr_scheme] EPR Scheme Requirements -- The entry outlines the mandatory Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) scheme requirements set out in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste, specifying producers' obligations to finance collection, recycling and recovery of packaging, to meet defined targets and to report annually on quantities placed on the market and the performance of the scheme.
[hazardous] Hazardous Substance Restrictions -- Regulation (EU) 2025/40 establishes strict limits on hazardous substances used in packaging and the resulting waste, requiring manufacturers to substitute or eliminate materials that pose risks to human health and the environment. The rule supports the EU's circular-economy goals by ensuring that packaging can be safely recycled or disposed of without contaminating ecosystems.
[labelling] Labelling and Information -- The Labelling and Information entry sets out the mandatory labeling requirements for packaging covered by Regulation (EU) 2025/40, detailing the data that must appear on each product package to inform consumers, recyclers and waste-management authorities. It prescribes the format, symbols and content-including material composition, reuse or recycling instructions-and ensures consistent disclosure across the EU market.
[material_id] Material Identification -- Material Identification is a data element defined in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 that requires producers to specify the type of material used in each packaging component, enabling accurate sorting and recycling. The code material_id is used to reference this information in compliance databases.
[minimisation] Packaging Minimisation -- The entry "Packaging Minimisation" in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste defines minimisation as the set of measures aimed at reducing the amount and weight of packaging placed on the market, encouraging design for durability, reuse and recyclable materials. It obliges producers to implement strategies that lower material use and avoid unnecessary packaging, supporting the EU's circular-economy objectives.
[plastic_targets] Plastic Recycling Targets -- The entry "plastic_targets" captures the specific recycling and recovery percentages for plastic packaging mandated by Regulation (EU) 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste, outlining the incremental goals that Member States must achieve to reduce landfill and improve circularity.
[recyclability] Recyclability Requirements -- The recyclability requirements set out in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 define the minimum proportion of packaging that must be recyclable, specify testing methods, and establish reporting obligations for producers to ensure compliance with EU circular-economy goals.
[recycled_content] Minimum Recycled Content -- The entry defines the minimum proportion of recycled material that must be incorporated into any packaging placed on the EU market under Regulation (EU) 2025/40. It sets the calculation method, establishes threshold percentages for different material categories, and outlines the obligations of producers to demonstrate compliance through documentation and reporting.
[recycling_targets] Recycling Targets by Material -- The entry outlines the material-specific recycling rates that Member States must meet under Regulation (EU) 2025/40, specifying percentages for plastics, metal, paper, cardboard, glass and wood to be achieved by set deadlines. It details national obligations for monitoring, reporting and corrective actions to ensure the EU's circular-economy targets are met.
[reduction_targets] Overall Packaging Waste Reduction Targets -- The reduction_targets entry defines the EU-wide packaging waste reduction goals established by Regulation (EU) 2025/40, specifying percentage cuts that member states must achieve by 2030 and 2040 and linking these targets to the waste hierarchy, recycling rates, and other mandatory measures.
[refill_obligations] Refill Obligations for Beverage Containers -- The entry "refill_obligations" details the requirements under Regulation (EU) 2025/40 that producers must ensure beverage containers are designed for easy refilling, encourage the use of reusable bottles, and provide infrastructure or services to support refilling, thereby reducing packaging waste and promoting a circular economy.
[reusability_label] Reusability Information -- The reusability_label entry defines the information that must be provided on packaging to indicate its potential for reuse, including criteria, labeling format and consumer guidance under Regulation (EU) 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste.
[reuse] Reuse and Refill -- The entry "Reuse and Refill" (code reuse) in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 sets out measures to promote repeated use of packaging and the development of refill systems, aiming to cut packaging waste. It establishes targets for reusable packaging, design standards, and reporting obligations for member states to support a circular economy.
[reuse_targets] Reuse Targets by Packaging Type -- The "reuse_targets" entry sets quantitative reuse goals for each packaging type-such as glass, plastic, metal and paper-required under Regulation (EU) 2025/40. It specifies the percentage of each material that must be collected and reused or refilled, establishing measurable benchmarks for producers to reduce packaging waste and promote circularity.
[sorting_label] Sorting Labels and QR Codes -- Sorting labels and QR codes must be placed on packaging to identify material composition and provide clear disposal instructions, enabling efficient separate collection and recycling in line with Regulation (EU) 2025/40 on packaging and packaging waste.
[sustainability] Sustainability Requirements -- The sustainability requirements in Regulation (EU) 2025/40 establish mandatory standards to minimise the environmental impact of packaging and its waste. They set targets for reuse, recyclability, material composition and lifecycle assessment that producers and distributors must meet, with compliance monitored through reporting, verification and enforcement mechanisms.
[targets] Reduction and Recycling Targets -- The targets entry sets mandatory quantitative goals for reducing packaging waste and increasing recycling rates within the EU, specifying percentage thresholds that must be met by member states at defined future dates. It requires regular monitoring and reporting to ensure progress toward a circular economy and to enforce compliance with the overall packaging waste reduction objectives.
