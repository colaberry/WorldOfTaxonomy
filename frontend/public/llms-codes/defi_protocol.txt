[DF] DeFi Protocol Types -- DF identifies the categories of decentralized finance protocols, organizing them by core function such as lending, swapping, derivatives, asset management, and bridge services. The classification aids researchers, developers, and regulators in comparing design choices, risk profiles, and governance structures across the DeFi ecosystem.
[DF.01] Decentralized Exchange (DEX) -- A Decentralized Exchange (DEX) is a blockchain-based platform that enables peer-to-peer trading of digital assets without a central intermediary. It relies on smart contracts to match orders, provide liquidity, and settle trades automatically, enhancing transparency and user control while reducing custodial risk.
[DF.02] Lending/Borrowing Protocol -- DF.02 denotes decentralized finance platforms that facilitate peer-to-peer lending and borrowing without intermediaries. Users can supply assets to earn interest while borrowers obtain loans by posting collateral, with rates set algorithmically or through market dynamics. The protocol automates loan terms, collateral management, and liquidation processes via smart contracts.
[DF.03] Yield Aggregator -- Yield aggregator is a decentralized finance protocol that automatically moves user assets across multiple lending and staking platforms to capture the highest available yield, optimizing returns while managing risk. It often integrates with various liquidity pools and uses smart contracts to rebalance positions without user intervention.
[DF.04] Stablecoin Protocol -- DF.04 Stablecoin Protocol designates decentralized finance systems that create or manage digital tokens pegged to a stable asset, usually a fiat currency, using algorithmic, collateralized, or hybrid mechanisms to preserve price stability. These protocols facilitate low-volatility transactions, lending, and payments on blockchain networks without central intermediaries.
[DF.05] Liquid Staking -- Liquid staking is a DeFi protocol type that lets users lock proof-of-stake assets while receiving a tokenized representation of the staked amount. The derivative token can be traded, used as collateral, or moved across platforms, allowing holders to earn staking rewards without sacrificing liquidity.
[DF.06] Derivatives Protocol -- A Derivatives Protocol is a DeFi platform that enables creation, trading, and settlement of synthetic financial contracts such as options, futures, or perpetual swaps, allowing users to gain exposure to underlying assets without holding them directly. It typically uses smart contracts to automate margin requirements, pricing, and liquidation, and often incorporates on-chain oracles for price feeds.
[DF.07] Insurance Protocol -- DF.07 describes insurance protocols in decentralized finance, which automate risk coverage, premium collection, underwriting and claim settlement through smart contracts. These platforms provide on-chain protection against events such as smart-contract failures, price volatility or asset loss, often using oracles and tokenized collateral to trigger payouts without traditional insurers.
[DF.08] Oracle Network -- DF.08 refers to oracle networks within decentralized finance protocols, which supply reliable off-chain data to smart contracts. These systems aggregate, verify, and transmit information such as price feeds, weather data, or event outcomes, enabling DeFi applications to operate securely and autonomously without trusting a single data source.
[DF.09] Bridge/Cross-Chain -- DF.09 refers to decentralized finance protocols that enable the transfer of assets and data across distinct blockchain networks. These bridge or cross-chain solutions lock tokens on the source chain, issue representative tokens on the destination chain, and maintain security through validators or smart-contract mechanisms, facilitating interoperability and liquidity between ecosystems.
[DF.10] DAO Governance -- DF.10 denotes DeFi protocols that rely on DAO governance, where token holders collectively vote on upgrades, fee structures and funding decisions. The model emphasizes community-driven control and transparent, on-chain decision making.
[DF.11] Launchpad/IDO -- DF.11 refers to Launchpad or Initial DEX Offering platforms that let new blockchain projects raise capital by issuing tokens directly to investors through a smart-contract-driven sale. These protocols handle token distribution, often include vetting or marketing support, and may provide initial liquidity on decentralized exchanges.
[DF.12] Real World Assets (RWA) -- DF.12 Real World Assets (RWA) denotes DeFi protocols that tokenise off-chain assets such as real estate, commodities, invoices or other physical collateral, enabling them to be lent, traded or used as blockchain-based collateral. These systems link traditional finance with decentralized markets, offering fractional ownership and liquidity while exposing participants to the risks of the underlying real-world assets.
[DF.13] Perpetual Protocol -- DF.13 Perpetual Protocol denotes a decentralized finance platform that enables the creation and trading of perpetual swap contracts without an expiry date. It utilizes automated market makers and on-chain price oracles to provide continuous liquidity and leveraged exposure to various crypto assets, allowing users to hedge or speculate while retaining custody of their collateral.
[DF.14] Restaking Protocol -- A restaking protocol is a DeFi primitive that lets users lock assets already staked on a primary blockchain to earn additional rewards on secondary platforms while preserving the original network's security guarantees. By leveraging the existing stake as collateral, it provides dual-reward opportunities without requiring separate collateral deposits.
