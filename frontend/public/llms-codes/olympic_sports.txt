[OS] Olympic Sport Categories -- OS is the IOC code that designates the grouping of all Olympic sports into categories such as Summer, Winter and mixed-discipline events. These categories structure the program for scheduling, qualification and statistical reporting, helping organizers and federations manage sport-specific rules and athlete eligibility.
[OS.01] Aquatics (Swimming, Diving, Water Polo) -- OS.01 covers the Olympic aquatics disciplines, encompassing competitive swimming, diving and water polo, all governed by World Aquatics. These sports are featured in the Summer Games and each has its own event formats and qualification criteria.
[OS.02] Athletics (Track and Field) -- OS.02 designates the Olympic sport category of Athletics, commonly called track and field. It covers a wide range of events including sprints, middle- and long-distance runs, hurdles, jumps, throws and combined events, and has been a core discipline in every Summer Games.
[OS.03] Ball Sports (Basketball, Football, Volleyball) -- OS.03 designates the IOC's "Ball Sports" category, grouping together team events that use a ball as the primary playing element. It includes basketball, football (soccer) and volleyball, each recognised as a distinct Olympic discipline under a shared classification for scheduling, governance and statistical reporting.
[OS.04] Combat Sports (Boxing, Judo, Wrestling) -- OS.04 identifies the group of Olympic combat sports, encompassing boxing, judo and wrestling, which are contested as separate disciplines governed by their respective international federations. These events have historic roots in the Games and involve direct physical competition between athletes.
[OS.05] Cycling -- OS.05 - Cycling is an Olympic sport category that comprises four disciplines: track cycling, road cycling, mountain bike, and BMX racing. Each discipline features individual and team events contested on specialized venues such as velodromes, road courses, off-road circuits, and BMX tracks, with medals awarded for speed, endurance, and technical skill.
[OS.06] Gymnastics -- OS.06 Gymnastics covers the Olympic discipline that includes artistic, rhythmic and trampoline events, featuring individual and team competitions on apparatus such as floor, vault, bars, beam, ribbon and tumbling. It is governed by the International Gymnastics Federation and has been part of the modern Games since 1896.
[OS.07] Racquet Sports (Tennis, Badminton, Table Tennis) -- OS.07 denotes the Olympic Sport category for racquet sports, encompassing tennis, badminton and table tennis, which are governed by their respective international federations and feature in the Summer Games program. These disciplines involve play with rackets or paddles to strike a ball or shuttlecock over a net, and each includes singles and doubles events.
[OS.08] Rowing and Canoeing -- OS.08 groups the Olympic water-based endurance disciplines of rowing and canoeing, covering both sweep and sculling events in flatwater rowing and sprint, slalom, and marathon races in canoe/kayak. The category reflects the shared reliance on strength, technique and aerobic capacity across these distinct yet related sports.
[OS.09] Sailing -- Sailing is an Olympic discipline where athletes race wind-propelled boats in designated courses on open water, with events organized by gender, crew size and boat class. It debuted at the 1900 Games and has remained a regular part of the Olympic programme, showcasing a range of keelboats, dinghies and multihulls.
[OS.10] Shooting and Archery -- OS.10 groups the Olympic disciplines of shooting and archery, covering precision target events that test accuracy and control with firearms or bows. Both sports feature individual and team competitions across multiple distances and equipment categories.
[OS.11] Equestrian -- Olympic Sport category OS.11 Equestrian includes horse-based competitions such as dressage, jumping and eventing, where riders and horses perform set patterns or navigate cross-country courses. The sport is governed by the International Federation for Equestrian Sports (FEI) and has been part of the Summer Games since 1900.
[OS.12] Winter Sports (Skiing, Skating, Ice Hockey) -- OS.12 groups the Olympic winter sports that take place on snow or ice, including alpine and cross-country skiing, figure skating, speed skating and ice hockey, under a single classification used for event organization, scheduling and statistical reporting.
[OS.13] Skateboarding and Surfing -- OS.13 groups the action-sport disciplines of skateboarding and surfing, which were introduced to the Summer Olympic programme in Tokyo 2020 and will continue in future Games. Both events are overseen by their international federations and feature street, park, and wave-riding competitions that showcase youthful, urban-culture formats within the Olympic movement.
[OS.14] Climbing -- OS.14 - Climbing covers the Olympic sport of sport climbing, which debuted at the Tokyo 2020 Games and includes three disciplines: lead, bouldering and speed. The events are overseen by the International Federation of Sport Climbing and feature athletes competing on artificial walls to test endurance, power and agility.
[OS.15] Breaking (Breakdancing) -- Breaking, also known as breakdancing, is listed as a new Olympic sport under code OS.15 in the IOC classification. It will debut at the Paris 2024 Games, featuring judging criteria based on athleticism, creativity, and musicality.
