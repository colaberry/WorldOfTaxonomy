[IB] IBC Chapters -- The International Building Code 2021 entry "IB - IBC Chapters" refers to the organized sections of the code, each covering a specific aspect of building design, construction, and safety such as occupancy classifications, fire protection, structural requirements, and egress. The chapter format provides a systematic framework for jurisdictions, designers, and contractors to locate and apply the applicable regulations throughout a project.
[IB.01] Scope and Administration -- IB.01 establishes the purpose and authority of the International Building Code, specifying that it sets minimum standards for the design, construction, alteration and occupancy of buildings to protect public health, safety and welfare. It also outlines the code's organization, the process for adoption by jurisdictions, and the roles and responsibilities of officials in its administration.
[IB.02] Definitions -- IB.02 in the 2021 International Building Code provides the glossary of terms used throughout the code, establishing precise definitions for concepts such as "building," "occupancy," "structural element," and "fire resistance." These definitions ensure consistent interpretation and application of the code's requirements across all jurisdictions.
[IB.03] Use and Occupancy Classification -- The IBC 2021 defines Use and Occupancy Classification to group buildings and spaces based on their intended function and the type of activities they will contain. This classification determines the applicable performance criteria, construction requirements, and life-safety provisions for each occupancy type.
[IB.04] Special Detailed Hazards -- IB.04 in the 2021 International Building Code identifies the portion covering special detailed hazards, providing extra design, construction and safety requirements for situations such as hazardous material storage, high-risk occupancies, and unique fire protection needs that exceed the standard code provisions.
[IB.05] Building Heights and Areas -- IB.05 defines the maximum building height and floor-area limits for each occupancy and construction type, requiring compliance with fire-resistance ratings, required setbacks and structural limits. It prescribes the calculations used to determine allowable height and area based on building classification, fire-area grouping and local amendment provisions.
[IB.06] Types of Construction -- IB.06 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the classification of building construction by the fire-resistance rating of structural elements, dividing structures into Types I through V and further subcategories such as Type I-A, I-B, II-A, II-B, III-A, III-B, IV and V. The code uses these types to determine permissible uses, occupancy limits, and required safety features for each building.
[IB.07] Fire and Smoke Protection -- IB.07 in the 2021 International Building Code deals with fire and smoke protection. It sets requirements for building assemblies, compartments and systems that limit the spread of fire and smoke, including fire-resistance ratings, smoke barriers and automatic suppression provisions.
[IB.08] Interior Finishes -- IB.08 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the fire-performance criteria for interior finish materials, specifying allowable flame spread, smoke development, and ignitability ratings. It details testing methods, material classifications, and installation requirements for walls, ceilings, and other interior surfaces to protect occupants and limit fire spread.
[IB.09] Fire Protection Systems -- IB.09 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the requirements for fire protection systems, covering the design, installation, and maintenance of fire alarms, sprinkler systems, and related components to ensure life safety and property protection.

[IB.12] Interior Environment -- IB.12 in the 2021 International Building Code sets requirements for the interior environment of buildings, covering ventilation, temperature control, lighting and acoustic performance. The provision ensures occupant health, safety and comfort by linking design criteria to mechanical, fire and energy standards.
[IB.13] Energy Efficiency -- IB.13 in the 2021 International Building Code establishes minimum energy-performance requirements for new and renovated buildings, covering the building envelope, lighting, HVAC, and service water heating. It aligns with ASHRAE 90.1 standards to reduce overall energy consumption and improve sustainability.
[IB.14] Exterior Walls -- IB.14 outlines the design and construction requirements for exterior walls, specifying allowable materials, fire-resistance ratings, structural support, and weather-tightness criteria to ensure safety and durability in compliance with the 2021 International Building Code.
[IB.15] Roof Assemblies -- IB.15 outlines the requirements for roof assemblies in the 2021 International Building Code, detailing design loads, fire-resistance ratings, weather protection, and material compatibility to ensure safe and durable roofing systems.
[IB.16] Structural Design -- IB.16 in the 2021 International Building Code establishes the requirements for structural design, outlining the criteria for strength, stability, and serviceability of building components, specifying acceptable materials, analysis methods, and performance standards to ensure safety and compliance.
[IB.17] Soils and Foundations -- IB.17 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines requirements for evaluating soil conditions and designing foundations, including site investigation, geotechnical reporting, allowable bearing capacities, and permissible foundation types such as spread footings, mat foundations and piles, to ensure structural stability.
[IB.18] Concrete -- IB.18 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the requirements for concrete as a structural material, covering classification of concrete grades, minimum compressive strength, mix design, durability, and fire-resistance ratings. It also specifies testing procedures, allowable tolerances, and quality-control measures that must be documented for all concrete work in building construction.
[IB.19] Steel -- IB.19 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the requirements for steel construction, covering permitted steel grades, design methods, fire-resistance ratings, connection details and allowable stress values, and specifying how steel members must be inspected and documented to ensure structural safety.
[IB.20] Wood -- IB.20 in the 2021 International Building Code designates wood as an approved building material, outlining its allowable uses, structural grading, and required fire-resistance ratings. The entry sets standards for grading, moisture content, and protection measures such as fire-blocking and treatment to ensure safety and durability in residential and low-rise construction.
[IB.21] Masonry -- IB.21 in the 2021 International Building Code defines the requirements for masonry construction, including the use of brick, concrete block, stone and other masonry units, their allowable load-bearing capacities, fire-resistance ratings, and the methods for proper bonding, reinforcement, and moisture protection to ensure structural stability and safety.
[IB.22] Plumbing -- The IB.22 entry in the 2021 International Building Code outlines the requirements for plumbing systems within buildings, referencing the International Plumbing Code for detailed specifications on water supply, drainage, venting, and fixture installation. It establishes the minimum standards for design, materials, and performance to ensure health, safety, and sanitation in all occupied structures.
[IB.23] Mechanical -- IB.23 in the 2021 International Building Code covers the mechanical systems requirements for buildings, specifying standards for heating, ventilation, air-conditioning, and related equipment installation, performance, and inspection to ensure safety and energy efficiency.
[IB.24] Electrical -- IB.24 in the 2021 International Building Code sets the minimum standards for electrical systems in new and renovated structures, covering wiring methods, protective devices, grounding, and integration with fire and life-safety systems. It ensures that electrical installations meet safety and performance criteria to protect occupants and property.
[IB.25] Elevators and Escalators -- IB.25 in the 2021 International Building Code outlines requirements for the design, installation, operation and maintenance of elevators and escalators, addressing safety devices, fire protection, accessibility, load capacities and inspection procedures to ensure safe vertical transportation within buildings.
