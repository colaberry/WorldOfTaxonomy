[cfr_260] 40 CFR Part 260 - Hazardous Waste Management System: General -- Part 260 of Title 40 of the Code of Federal Regulations implements the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, establishing the general framework for the hazardous waste management system. It defines key terms, outlines the responsibilities of generators, transporters and treatment facilities, and sets standards for waste identification, handling and recordkeeping.
[cfr_261] 40 CFR Part 261 - Identification and Listing of Hazardous Waste -- Part 261 of Title 40 of the Code of Federal Regulations implements the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act by defining what materials are considered hazardous waste. It provides criteria for identifying waste streams, lists specific waste codes, and establishes standards for waste determination, classification, and exclusion. The section guides regulators, generators, and transporters in complying with federal hazardous waste management requirements.
[cfr_262] 40 CFR Part 262 - Standards for Generators -- 40 CFR Part 262 sets the federal standards that hazardous waste generators must follow under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act. It outlines generator categories, record-keeping, labeling, and reporting requirements to ensure safe handling, storage, and transportation of hazardous waste.
[cfr_264] 40 CFR Part 264 - Standards for TSD Facility Owners and Operators -- 40 CFR Part 264 sets mandatory standards for owners and operators of treatment, storage, and disposal facilities handling hazardous waste under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act. It prescribes requirements for facility design, operation, monitoring, recordkeeping, closure, and corrective actions to protect human health and the environment.
[cfr_268] 40 CFR Part 268 - Land Disposal Restrictions -- 40 CFR Part 268 implements the Land Disposal Restrictions under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, setting standards that prohibit the disposal of hazardous waste onto land unless it meets treatment standards that reduce toxicity. The rule establishes specific treatment levels and testing requirements for various waste streams, and it outlines procedures for variance requests, corrective action, and compliance enforcement. It aims to protect human health and the environment by ensuring hazardous waste is properly treated before land disposal.
[sec_3001] Sec 3001 - Identification and Listing of Hazardous Waste -- Section 3001 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes the EPA's authority to identify and list hazardous waste, defining the criteria for what materials are classified as hazardous and must be regulated under the Act, with periodic reviews and updates to the listings.
[sec_3002] Sec 3002 - Standards for Generators of Hazardous Waste -- Section 3002 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes mandatory standards for anyone who generates hazardous waste, requiring proper identification, labeling, and manifesting of waste shipments, as well as stringent record-keeping, storage, and reporting requirements. It also mandates training for personnel and compliance with handling practices to protect human health and the environment.
[sec_3003] Sec 3003 - Standards for Transporters of Hazardous Waste -- Section 3003 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes federal standards for hazardous waste transporters, requiring EPA registration, minimum insurance, proper packaging and labeling, and detailed record-keeping to ensure safe and compliant movement of hazardous materials.
[sec_3004] Sec 3004 - Standards for HW Treatment, Storage, and Disposal Facilities -- Section 3004 establishes federal performance standards for hazardous-waste treatment, storage and disposal facilities under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, requiring proper design, operation, monitoring, closure and corrective-action practices to ensure protection of human health and the environment.

[sec_3006] Sec 3006 - Authorized State Programs -- Section 3006 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes the framework for states to develop authorized hazardous waste programs that meet federal standards. It authorizes the EPA to approve state plans, permitting states to implement and enforce their own regulations for waste handling, treatment, storage, and disposal while remaining consistent with national requirements.
[sec_3007] Sec 3007 - Inspections -- Under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, Section 3007 authorizes EPA and authorized state agencies to conduct inspections of facilities that treat, store, or dispose of hazardous waste to verify compliance with permitting, recordkeeping and reporting requirements, and to enforce corrective actions when violations are identified.
[sec_3008] Sec 3008 - Federal Enforcement -- Section 3008 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act grants the EPA authority to enforce the law, permitting inspections, compliance orders and civil penalties to ensure proper handling of hazardous waste. This federal enforcement provision underpins the agency's ability to monitor, investigate and sanction violations of the hazardous waste program.
[sec_3010] Sec 3010 - Notification Requirements -- Section 3010 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act sets out the mandatory notification requirements for entities that generate, transport, treat, store, or dispose of hazardous waste. Facilities must inform the EPA and the appropriate state agency about their operations, providing details on waste types, quantities, and site locations, which initiates permitting and compliance oversight.
[sec_4001] Sec 4001 - Objectives and Criteria for Solid Waste Disposal -- Section 4001 sets out the primary goals and standards for solid waste disposal under the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, directing that waste management protect human health and the environment, promote safe disposal practices, and encourage recovery and recycling. It establishes criteria for locating, operating, and closing disposal facilities, including requirements for permits, monitoring, and compliance with federal and state regulations. This framework guides agencies in evaluating and authorizing waste disposal sites.

[sec_9003] Sec 9003 - Release Detection, Prevention, and Correction -- Section 9003 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes requirements for detecting releases of hazardous substances, mandates preventive measures to avoid such releases, and prescribes corrective actions to remediate any releases that occur.
[sec_9005] Sec 9005 - Inspections, Monitoring, and Testing -- Section 9005 of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act governs the inspection, monitoring, and testing program for facilities that treat, store, or dispose of hazardous waste. It authorizes the EPA and authorized state agencies to conduct compliance inspections, require performance and emission monitoring, and mandate sampling and testing to verify that waste management practices meet statutory standards.
[sub_a] Subtitle A - General Provisions -- Subtitle A of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act contains the core statutory framework for managing hazardous waste, establishing definitions, responsibilities, and enforcement mechanisms. It sets out general provisions governing the identification, handling, transportation, treatment, storage, and disposal of hazardous substances, as well as requirements for permits, recordkeeping, and citizen suits. This subtitle forms the basis for the federal hazardous waste program and its implementation by the Environmental Protection Agency.
[sub_b] Subtitle B - Office of Solid Waste and EPA Authorities -- Subtitle B of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act establishes the Office of Solid Waste and defines the EPA's authority to regulate both hazardous and non-hazardous solid waste. It provides the framework for permitting, enforcement and standards aimed at protecting human health and the environment.
[sub_c] Subtitle C - Hazardous Waste Management -- Subtitle C establishes the federal framework for hazardous waste management, defining how hazardous waste is identified, generated, transported, treated, stored and disposed of under a cradle-to-grave system. It authorizes the EPA to issue regulations, permits and enforcement actions aimed at protecting human health and the environment.
[sub_d] Subtitle D - State or Regional Solid Waste Plans -- Subtitle D of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act creates a framework for states and regional agencies to develop and implement comprehensive solid waste management plans, setting standards for waste handling, disposal and reduction. It requires states to assess waste streams, establish programs and enforce regulations aimed at protecting public health and the environment.
[sub_e] Subtitle E - Duties of the Secretary of Commerce -- Subtitle E of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act sets out the duties of the Secretary of Commerce, assigning responsibility for overseeing the nation's hazardous-waste program, coordinating with the Environmental Protection Agency, and enforcing compliance with the Act's provisions. It also authorizes the Secretary to develop policies, issue regulations, and provide technical assistance to states and private entities handling solid and hazardous waste.
[sub_f] Subtitle F - Federal Responsibilities -- Subtitle F of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act sets out the federal government's duties for hazardous-waste management, giving the EPA authority to develop and enforce standards, oversee state programs, and provide technical assistance and funding. It also establishes mechanisms for intergovernmental coordination and compliance monitoring.
[sub_g] Subtitle G - Miscellaneous Provisions -- Subtitle G gathers various ancillary provisions of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, addressing enforcement authority, civil and criminal penalties, citizen suits, and rules for the EPA's administrative procedures, as well as technical amendments and other miscellaneous regulatory details not covered in the main sections.
[sub_h] Subtitle H - Research and Development -- Subtitle H of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act authorizes research and development activities aimed at improving waste management practices. It funds the creation, testing and demonstration of innovative treatment, recycling and disposal technologies, and supports data collection to inform future regulatory decisions and enhance environmental protection.
