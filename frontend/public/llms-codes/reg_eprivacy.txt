[art_1] Art 1 - Scope and aim -- Article 1 defines the Directive's purpose and scope, establishing rules for protecting personal data and privacy in electronic communications across the EU. It sets out that the legislation applies to public and private electronic service providers and aims to ensure the confidentiality of communications while balancing the free flow of information.
[art_13] Art 13 - Unsolicited communications (opt-in for marketing) -- Article 13 of Directive 2002/58/EC establishes that any electronic marketing communication must be sent only with the recipient's prior consent, imposing an opt-in requirement and prohibiting unsolicited messages unless the recipient has explicitly agreed to receive them.
[art_15] Art 15 - Application of certain provisions of GDPR -- Article 15 sets out how particular provisions of the GDPR are applied to the e-privacy framework established by Directive 2002/58/EC, ensuring that data-protection rules for electronic communications are aligned with the wider Union privacy regime and detailing the scope and transitional measures for GDPR implementation in this context.
[art_15a] Art 15a - Implementation and enforcement -- Article 15a of Directive 2002/58/EC establishes the framework for implementing and enforcing the privacy and data-protection rules in electronic communications, obliging Member States to designate competent authorities, define penalties and ensure effective supervisory mechanisms.
[art_2] Art 2 - Definitions -- Article 2 of Directive 2002/58/EC provides the key definitions used throughout the regulation, including "electronic communications service", "public communications network", "traffic data", "location data", "interception", and "personal data". These terms establish the scope for privacy and data-processing obligations in electronic communications.
[art_5] Art 5 - Confidentiality of communications -- Article 5 of Directive 2002/58/EC sets the confidentiality principle for electronic communications, mandating that the content of any communication must not be intercepted, monitored or stored without the user's explicit consent, except where authorised by law for reasons such as national security. Service providers are required to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to safeguard that confidentiality.
[art_5_3] Art 5(3) - Cookie consent requirement -- Art 5(3) of Directive 2002/58/EC obliges Member States to ensure that any storage or access to information on a user's terminal equipment, such as cookies, occurs only after the user has given informed consent. The provision forms the legal basis for cookie-consent requirements in electronic communications.
[art_6] Art 6 - Traffic data -- Article 6 of the 2002/58/EC Directive sets out the rules for handling traffic data, which are information processed for the conveyance of a communication, such as source, destination, time, length, and route. It requires that traffic data be erased or made anonymous once its purpose is fulfilled, unless the data are needed for billing, quality of service, or legal obligations, and it obliges providers to ensure confidentiality and security of the data.
[art_9] Art 9 - Location data other than traffic data -- Article 9 of the 2002/58/EC Directive sets rules for the processing of location data that is not traffic-related, requiring that such data be obtained only with the user's informed consent and used solely for the purpose for which it was collected, unless a specific legal exception applies. It also mandates safeguards for storage, security and the right of individuals to access, rectify or delete their location information.

[enforcement] Enforcement -- Enforcement under Directive 2002/58/EC designates the set of powers and procedures given to national regulators and the European Commission to monitor compliance with the rules on personal data processing and privacy in electronic communications, investigate breaches, and apply corrective actions or penalties where needed.
[marketing] Direct Marketing -- The entry classifies activities that use electronic communications to send unsolicited commercial messages directly to individuals. Under Directive 2002/58/EC, such direct-marketing must respect users' privacy, obtain prior consent where required and provide clear opt-out mechanisms, ensuring that personal data are processed lawfully in electronic channels.
