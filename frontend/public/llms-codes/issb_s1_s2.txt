[IS] ISSB Standards -- The IS entry refers to the ISSB Standards, the International Sustainability Standards Board's set of globally consistent sustainability disclosure standards. They form the core of the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards framework, guiding entities on how to report climate-related and other ESG information in a comparable, transparent manner.
[IS.01] S1: General Requirements -- S1: General Requirements establishes the core principles for preparing and presenting sustainability information under the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards, requiring entities to disclose material sustainability risks, opportunities and impacts in a transparent, comparable and reliable way. It defines key concepts such as materiality, completeness and comparability and aligns disclosures with the IFRS Foundation's sustainability reporting framework.
[IS.02] S1: Governance Disclosures -- IS.02, titled S1: Governance Disclosures, is an IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard that obliges entities to reveal how their governance structures oversee sustainability matters. It requires details on board responsibilities, committees, policies, risk management, and internal controls related to environmental, social and governance issues.
[IS.03] S1: Strategy Disclosures -- IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard IS.03 (S1) requires entities to disclose how sustainability matters shape their overall business strategy, including the impact of climate-related risks and opportunities on the organization's objectives, resource allocation, and value creation over the short, medium and long term. It also asks for an explanation of the governance processes used to integrate sustainability considerations into strategic planning.
[IS.04] S1: Risk Management Disclosures -- The IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard IS.04, titled S1: Risk Management Disclosures, requires entities to describe how sustainability-related risks are identified, assessed, and managed, and to explain the governance processes overseeing those risks. It also calls for information on the potential impacts of these risks on the entity's strategy, financial position, and performance.
[IS.05] S1: Metrics and Targets -- The IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard IS.05, titled S1: Metrics and Targets, obliges entities to disclose quantitative sustainability information, including the metrics used to assess performance and the specific targets set for environmental, social and governance outcomes. It also requires reporting on progress toward those targets and any variances from them.
[IS.06] S1: Connected Information -- IS.06 refers to the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard S1: Connected Information, which sets out requirements for entities to disclose how sustainability-related data is linked to financial reporting, including the integration of environmental, social and governance metrics with the entity's financial statements. The standard aims to ensure that users can understand the relationship between sustainability information and the entity's financial position and performance.
[IS.07] S2: Climate-Related Physical Risks -- The IS.07 entry under the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards specifies the S2 requirement on climate-related physical risks. It obliges entities to disclose how acute and chronic physical impacts of climate change affect their operations, strategy, financing and performance, and to detail the metrics, scenarios and governance processes used to assess and manage those risks.
[IS.08] S2: Climate-Related Transition Risks -- IS.08 (S2) is the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard that requires entities to disclose climate-related transition risks, including how policy, technology, market and reputational shifts toward a low-carbon economy could affect their business model, strategy, financial performance and cash flows, and the measures taken to mitigate those risks.
[IS.09] S2: Climate-Related Opportunities -- IS.09, titled S2: Climate-Related Opportunities, is an IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standard that requires entities to disclose how climate change creates business opportunities, including transition and physical opportunities, and the impact of those opportunities on strategy, business model, financial planning and performance. The standard guides reporting on the identification, assessment and quantification of such opportunities to inform stakeholders about potential value creation and risk mitigation.
[IS.10] S2: Scope 1 GHG Emissions -- IS.10 - S2: Scope 1 GHG Emissions requires entities to disclose the amount of greenhouse-gas emissions generated directly from sources they own or control, describing the measurement methodology, reporting period, and any significant assumptions or uncertainties, to enable users of financial statements to assess the entity's direct climate-related impact.
[IS.11] S2: Scope 2 GHG Emissions -- IS.11 requires entities to disclose their Scope 2 greenhouse-gas emissions, the indirect emissions from purchased electricity, heat, steam or cooling. The standard mandates reporting both location-based and market-based emission figures, detailing the methodology, emission factors and any reduction initiatives applied, and requires explanation of data sources, coverage and uncertainties to enable users to assess the entity's indirect climate impact.

[IS.14] S2: Internal Carbon Price -- IS.14 in the IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards outlines the S2 requirement for Internal Carbon Price, directing entities to disclose the methods and assumptions used to assign a price to carbon emissions in their internal decision-making. It explains how such pricing influences investment choices, risk assessments and financial performance, and requires reporting on its scope, methodology and any related impacts.
[IS.15] S2: Remuneration Linked to Climate -- IS.15 S2: Remuneration Linked to Climate requires entities to disclose how their compensation policies and practices are tied to climate-related risks, opportunities and performance metrics, including any incentives for achieving climate targets, and to explain the governance processes that ensure alignment with the organization's climate strategy.
