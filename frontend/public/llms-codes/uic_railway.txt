[UI] UIC Code Categories -- The UI entry groups the UIC railway classification codes into categories based on function, vehicle type, and operational characteristics, providing a systematic framework for standardizing identification across international rail networks.
[UI.01] Infrastructure (Track/Gauge) -- UI.01 refers to the railway infrastructure concerning track and gauge, detailing the physical components such as rails, sleepers, ballast and the specific gauge width. It provides a standardized framework for describing track geometry and compatibility across different national rail networks.
[UI.02] Signaling Systems (ERTMS/ETCS) -- UI.02 covers the European Rail Traffic Management System and its core component, the European Train Control System, detailing technical standards, functional levels, interoperability requirements and deployment practices for train-borne and trackside signaling, automatic train protection and traffic management across the European rail network.
[UI.03] Rolling Stock - Passenger -- UI.03 designates passenger rolling stock in the UIC system, covering all rail vehicles built or equipped primarily for carrying passengers such as coaches, multiple units and related equipment, and specifying compliance with UIC technical, safety and interoperability standards.
[UI.04] Rolling Stock - Freight -- UI.04 designates the category of rolling stock used for freight transport, covering all freight wagons, freight cars, and related equipment. It includes specifications on loading capacity, dimensions, couplings, and safety standards for goods movement on rail networks.
[UI.05] Rolling Stock - Locomotive -- UIC code UI.05 designates the category for locomotives within the UIC rolling-stock classification, defining specifications, technical parameters and service classifications for traction units used on member railways. It provides a standardized framework for identifying and comparing locomotive types across international networks.
[UI.06] Traction Power Systems -- UI.06 covers the electrical systems that supply power to railway traction equipment, including overhead lines, third-rail, substations, and associated control and safety devices. It defines standards for voltage, frequency and interfacing with rolling stock.
[UI.07] Operations and Traffic Management -- UI.07 covers the planning, coordination and real-time control of train movements across a railway network, including scheduling, dispatching, signalling, crew assignment and traffic flow optimization to ensure safety, punctuality and efficient use of infrastructure. It also addresses the use of traffic-management centres and related information systems.
[UI.08] Safety Standards -- UI.08 in the UIC Railway Classification Codes designates the set of safety standards that apply to railway systems worldwide. It defines minimum requirements for equipment design, infrastructure, operational procedures and staff training to ensure safe train movements, accident prevention and compliance with international safety regulations.
[UI.09] Environmental Standards -- UI.09 outlines the environmental standards applicable to railway operations, covering emissions limits, noise control, energy efficiency, and waste management for both rolling stock and infrastructure, and specifies testing methods and compliance procedures to ensure sustainable performance across the network.
[UI.10] Telecommunications -- UI.10 in the UIC Railway Classification Codes designates the telecommunications category, covering all communication systems employed on rail networks such as signalling data links, voice and driver-cab radios, and network infrastructure that support operational and safety functions.
[UI.11] Interoperability (TSI) -- UI.11 denotes the Interoperability category that addresses the European Technical Specifications for Interoperability (TSI), specifying the safety, technical and operational requirements needed for railway vehicles and infrastructure to operate seamlessly across borders.
[UI.12] Loading Gauge Standards -- UI.12 establishes the dimensional criteria for the railway loading gauge used by UIC member networks, detailing maximum vehicle width, height, and clearance tolerances to ensure safe passage through tunnels, bridges, platforms and other structures. The code harmonises national standards, provides methods for verifying compliance and guides the design of infrastructure and rolling stock to maintain interoperability across international rail corridors.
[UI.13] Noise and Vibration Standards -- UI.13 defines the standards for permissible noise levels and vibration characteristics of railway vehicles and infrastructure, specifying measurement methods, speed-related limits, track-condition criteria, and required mitigation measures to ensure compliance with European and international regulations.
[UI.14] Station Design Standards -- UI.14 defines the standardized criteria for the layout, dimensions, and accessibility features of railway stations across UIC member networks, covering platform height, circulation areas, safety signage, and passenger amenities to ensure interoperability and consistent service quality.
