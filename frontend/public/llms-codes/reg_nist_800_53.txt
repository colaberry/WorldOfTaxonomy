[ac] AC - Access Control -- The AC family contains controls that define how organizations limit system access to authorized users, processes, and devices, specifying requirements for identification, authentication, authorization, session management, and account management. It also mandates regular review and adjustment of access rights to ensure they align with current roles and responsibilities.
[ac_1] AC-1 - Policy and Procedures -- AC-1 requires an organization to create, document, and distribute an access control policy and the procedures that support it. The policy defines the overall framework for managing access to information systems, and the procedures detail how the policy will be implemented, maintained, and reviewed.
[ac_17] AC-17 - Remote Access -- AC-17 specifies that remote access to organizational systems must be authorized, securely configured, and continuously monitored. It requires the use of approved authentication mechanisms, encrypted communications, and logging of remote sessions, with periodic reviews to ensure access privileges remain appropriate and aligned with security policies.
[ac_2] AC-2 - Account Management -- AC-2 establishes processes for creating, modifying, disabling, and removing user accounts and associated privileges across the organization. It requires periodic review of account status, enforcement of least-privilege principles, and secure management of account identifiers and passwords to protect information systems.
[ac_3] AC-3 - Access Enforcement -- AC-3 requires the information system to enforce approved authorizations for logical and physical access, ensuring that only authorized users, processes, or devices can perform specific actions. It mandates the implementation of access enforcement mechanisms-such as role-based controls, rule-based filters, or policies-that restrict access based on the organization's access control policy. The control also calls for periodic review and updates of these mechanisms to address changes in authorizations or system configurations.
[ac_6] AC-6 - Least Privilege -- AC-6 requires that users, processes, and devices receive only the access needed to perform their authorized functions. Organizations must enforce least privilege by assigning minimal permissions, reviewing and adjusting rights regularly, and removing unnecessary privileges. This limits the attack surface and reduces the potential impact of compromised accounts or software.
[at] AT - Awareness and Training -- The AT control family focuses on establishing and maintaining a security and privacy awareness program for all personnel, ensuring they understand relevant policies, procedures, and their responsibilities. It requires role-based training, regular refreshers, and assessment of the program's effectiveness to reduce human-related risks.
[au] AU - Audit and Accountability -- The AU family defines requirements for audit logging, ensuring actions are recorded, protected, and examined to detect and respond to security events, maintain accountability, and support investigations. It includes control
[au_2] AU-2 - Event Logging -- AU-2 requires the organization to define what types of events are to be recorded, configure logging mechanisms to capture those events, and ensure logs are protected, retained, and reviewed. It specifies that logs must include sufficient detail such as timestamps, source identifiers, and outcome information to support investigation and accountability. The control also mandates procedures for coordinating log generation across system components and for periodic assessment of logging effectiveness.
[au_6] AU-6 - Audit Record Review, Analysis, and Reporting -- AU-6 mandates that organizations routinely review audit records, conduct analysis to identify unusual or inappropriate events, and generate reports for designated officials. The control emphasizes using automated tools and correlation techniques to support timely detection, investigation, and corrective action.
[ca] CA - Assessment, Authorization, and Monitoring -- The CA family defines processes for assessing security controls, obtaining authorizations to operate, and continuously monitoring information systems to ensure ongoing risk management. It includes controls for planning, conducting, and reporting assessments, granting and maintaining authorizations, and establishing continuous monitoring strategies.
[cm] CM - Configuration Management -- CM addresses the establishment and maintenance of secure configurations for information system components, requiring organizations to define baseline settings, track hardware and software inventories, and control changes through documented change-control processes. It also mandates regular assessments to verify that configurations remain consistent with approved baselines and that unauthorized modifications are detected and corrected.
[cm_6] CM-6 - Configuration Settings -- CM-6 mandates that organizations establish, document, and maintain baseline configuration settings for information system components, and enforce those settings using automated mechanisms. It also requires periodic review and updating of the baselines to reflect changes in technology, threats, and organizational requirements.
[cp] CP - Contingency Planning -- The CP family specifies controls for developing, maintaining and exercising plans that ensure the information system can recover and continue operation after a disruption. It includes requirements for contingency planning policies, backup strategies, disaster-recovery and system-resilience testing, training, and coordination of interdependent resources to support continuity of mission-critical functions.
[ia] IA - Identification and Authentication -- The IA (Identification and Authentication) family defines controls that ensure users, processes, and devices are uniquely identified and authenticated before granting access to information systems. It includes requirements for establishing identity, managing authenticators, and protecting authentication mechanisms, aiming to prevent unauthorized use and support appropriate accountability across all system components.
[ia_2] IA-2 - Identification and Authentication (Organizational Users) -- IA-2 requires the organization to uniquely identify each user and enforce authentication mechanisms before granting access to information systems. It mandates the use of passwords or other authenticators that meet defined complexity, length, and change requirements, and recommends multi-factor authentication for privileged or remote access.
[ia_5] IA-5 - Authenticator Management -- IA-5 addresses the lifecycle management of authenticators used to verify identities, covering selection, provisioning, distribution, revocation and replacement. It requires organizations to define policies for secure storage, periodic review and disposal of authenticators to maintain confidentiality, integrity and availability.
[ir] IR - Incident Response -- The IR family defines security and privacy controls for establishing, maintaining, and improving an organization's incident response capabilities. It includes requirements for incident response planning, detection, analysis, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident reporting to reduce impact and support continuous improvement.
[ir_4] IR-4 - Incident Handling -- IR-4 establishes an organization's incident handling capability, requiring documented processes for detecting, analyzing, and responding to security incidents. It mandates coordination of response activities, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident reporting to improve future resilience.
[ma] MA - Maintenance -- The MA (Maintenance) family defines controls for planning, performing, and supervising system maintenance activities to preserve security and privacy, including the use of authorized tools, scheduling, supervision of personnel, and protection of maintenance ports and interfaces, and it addresses both on-site and remote maintenance.
[mp] MP - Media Protection -- Media Protection (MP) defines controls to safeguard information stored on physical and digital media throughout its lifecycle. It includes requirements for media access, marking, storage, transport, and secure disposal or sanitization to prevent unauthorized disclosure, alteration, or loss.
[pe] PE - Physical and Environmental Protection -- PE controls cover measures that protect an organization's facilities, assets, and supporting infrastructure from physical threats and environmental hazards. They include requirements for access control, monitoring, intrusion detection, fire protection, and environmental controls such as temperature, humidity, and power management to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information systems.
[pl] PL - Planning -- The PL - Planning family includes controls that ensure the organization develops, maintains, and reviews comprehensive plans to support the implementation of security and privacy programs, including risk
[pm] PM - Program Management -- The PM family in NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 addresses program management, providing controls for planning, organizing, and overseeing an organization's security and privacy initiatives. It guides the allocation of resources, assignment of responsibilities, and coordination of activities to ensure the program aligns with strategic objectives and risk management requirements.
[ps] PS - Personnel Security -- The PS family defines controls that protect an organization by ensuring that individuals with access to information systems are trustworthy and properly managed. It covers personnel screening, security awareness and training, role-based access, handling of personnel changes, and termination procedures to prevent insider threats.
[pt] PT - PII Processing and Transparency -- PT - PII Processing and Transparency requires organizations to handle personally identifiable information in accordance with applicable privacy laws and policies, providing clear notice of collection, purpose, and retention, and ensuring that data is used only for authorized purposes. It also mandates mechanisms for individuals to access, correct, or delete their information and for the organization to maintain transparent documentation of processing activities.
[ra] RA - Risk Assessment -- RA - Risk Assessment is a family of controls that require organizations to identify, evaluate, and prioritize risks to operations, assets, and individuals. It includes processes for assessing threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, and impact, and for documenting risk levels to inform decision-making and mitigation planning.
[ra_5] RA-5 - Vulnerability Monitoring and Scanning -- RA-5 requires the organization to employ automated tools to regularly scan its information system and hosted environments for vulnerabilities, assess the findings, and prioritize remediation based on risk. It also mandates tracking of remediation actions, reporting of significant vulnerabilities to stakeholders, and updating of scanning processes to address new threats and changes in the system.
[sa] SA - System and Services Acquisition -- The SA family addresses the planning, procurement, and management of information system components and services to ensure they meet security and privacy requirements. It guides organizations in establishing acquisition strategies, evaluating vendors, integrating security controls into contracts, and verifying that purchased hardware, software, and services align with approved risks and compliance objectives.

[sc_7] SC-7 - Boundary Protection -- SC-7 requires organizations to monitor and control inbound and outbound network traffic at the system perimeter and between internal trust zones, using mechanisms such as firewalls, gateways, and intrusion detection systems. The control also mandates regular testing, configuration management, and logging of boundary protection devices.
[sc_8] SC-8 - Transmission Confidentiality and Integrity -- SC-8 requires organizations to protect the confidentiality and integrity of information transmitted across networks by employing cryptographic mechanisms and other security measures. The control mandates the use of approved encryption, authentication, and integrity checks for both internal and external communications and calls for monitoring to detect unauthorized modifications.
[si] SI - System and Information Integrity -- The System and Information Integrity (SI) family defines controls that ensure the accuracy, reliability, and security of information and information systems by detecting, preventing, and correcting flaws and malicious activity. It includes measures such as malware protection, patch management, security monitoring, vulnerability scanning, and incident response to maintain system integrity.
[si_2] SI-2 - Flaw Remediation -- SI-2 requires organizations to establish a process for identifying, reporting, and correcting software flaws in a timely manner, including the application of security-relevant patches and updates. The control mandates testing, verification of remediation effectiveness, and documentation of actions taken to reduce vulnerabilities and maintain system integrity.
[si_4] SI-4 - System Monitoring -- SI-4 requires the organization to establish and maintain mechanisms to monitor the information system to detect, log, and respond to security-relevant events. It includes continuous monitoring of system components, analysis of audit records, and timely notification of identified anomalies or incidents to support effective incident response and ongoing risk management.
[sr] SR - Supply Chain Risk Management -- The SR family addresses the planning, assessment, and mitigation of risks associated with the acquisition, development, and maintenance of information system components and services throughout the supply chain. It requires organizations to establish processes for evaluating suppliers, enforcing security requirements, monitoring for threats, and responding to incidents that could affect system integrity, confidentiality, or availability.
