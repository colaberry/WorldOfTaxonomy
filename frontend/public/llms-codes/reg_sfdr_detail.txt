[art_10] Art 10 - Website Disclosures for Art 8/9 Products -- Article 10 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (EU) 2019/2088 sets out the mandatory website disclosures for financial products classified under Articles 8 or 9, requiring them to publish clear information on sustainability characteristics, investment objectives, methodology, data sources and any adverse environmental or social impacts, ensuring transparency for investors.
[art_11] Art 11 - Periodic Reporting for Art 8/9 Products -- Article 11 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation obliges managers of Article 8 (environmental or social characteristics) and Article 9 (sustainable investment) products to provide periodic reports. These reports must detail the product's sustainability performance, the methodology used, and the extent to which the investment outcomes meet the disclosed objectives, enabling investors to assess ongoing compliance with the regulation.
[art_3] Art 3 - Sustainability Risk Policies (entity) -- Article 3 obliges entities covered by the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation to adopt policies that identify and assess sustainability risks, integrate those risks into their investment decisions and describe the likely impact on returns. It also requires them to disclose any measures taken to mitigate such risks.
[art_4] Art 4 - PAI Consideration at Entity Level -- Article 4 obliges financial market participants and advisers to assess and disclose principal adverse impacts of their investment decisions on sustainability factors at the entity level. They must integrate PAI considerations into their policies, explain how they identify, prioritize and address them, and report the resulting outcomes.
[art_5] Art 5 - Remuneration Policies and Sustainability Risks -- Article 5 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation requires financial market participants to incorporate sustainability risks into their remuneration policies, ensuring that compensation structures do not encourage excessive risk taking and are aligned with environmental, social and governance objectives. It also obliges firms to disclose how these policies address sustainability risks and the extent to which remuneration is linked to the achievement of ESG targets.
[art_6] Art 6 - Pre-Contractual Sustainability Risk Disclosures -- Article 6 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation requires financial market participants to disclose in pre-contractual documents whether sustainability risks are relevant to the investment product, and if so, how those risks might affect returns, while also stating when sustainability risks are deemed immaterial. This enables investors to assess potential environmental, social, or governance impacts before committing capital.
[art_8] Art 8 - Light Green Products (environmental/social characteristics) -- Article 8 of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation covers "light-green" financial products that promote environmental or social characteristics without having a sustainable investment objective. It requires firms to disclose how these characteristics are met, the sustainability risks considered, and the methodologies, benchmarks or indicators used to assess the product's ESG performance.
[art_9] Art 9 - Dark Green Products (sustainable investment objective) -- Article 9 of the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation defines "dark-green" products as financial instruments that pursue a sustainable investment objective, requiring the investee companies to meet specific environmental criteria and to disclose how the objective is achieved. These products must demonstrate a measurable contribution to environmental goals while avoiding significant harm to other sustainability objectives.
[do_no_harm] Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) Assessment -- The Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) assessment is a key component of the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, requiring financial products to demonstrate that their investments do not cause material adverse effects on any of the EU's environmental or social objectives. It involves evaluating compliance with specific thresholds, monitoring ongoing performance, and documenting evidence that the investment activities remain aligned with the regulation's mitigations for significant harm.
[entity_level] Entity-Level Disclosures -- The entry specifies the sustainability information that financial market participants must disclose at the entity level under the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation, covering governance, strategy and processes for integrating sustainability risks, principal adverse impact statements, and alignment with the EU taxonomy, along with relevant targets, metrics and performance data.
[minimum_safeguards] Minimum Safeguards (OECD, UN Guiding Principles) -- The minimum safeguards entry requires that financial products covered by the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation respect internationally recognised standards, specifically the OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises and the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, to ensure they do not contribute to adverse environmental or social impacts. Compliance involves assessing and mitigating any potential harms in line with these principles before marketing the product.

[rts] Regulatory Technical Standards -- Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) are detailed rules adopted under the EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) 2019/2088 that prescribe how financial market participants must disclose sustainability information, including templates for pre-contractual, website and periodic disclosures, and the criteria for classifying environmentally sustainable investments.

[taxonomy_alignment] EU Taxonomy Alignment -- EU Taxonomy Alignment indicates how closely an economic activity follows the EU taxonomy's criteria for environmental sustainability, showing whether it makes a substantial contribution to the taxonomy's climate and environmental objectives while meeting the "do no significant harm" and minimum safeguard requirements. This alignment is used in disclosures under the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation to assess the sustainability credentials of investments.
