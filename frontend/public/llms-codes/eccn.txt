[0A] Nuclear Test Equipment (Systems and Equipment) -- Entry 0A on the Commerce Control List controls nuclear test equipment, encompassing systems and devices used to conduct, support, or monitor nuclear explosions, such as instrumentation, data-acquisition hardware, containment structures and related support hardware. These items are subject to export licensing due to their potential impact on nuclear weapons development.
[0B] Nuclear Test Equipment (Test/Inspection Equipment) -- The 0B entry on the Commerce Control List controls equipment used to test, inspect or evaluate nuclear explosions, including diagnostic, measurement, monitoring and related support devices. It covers hardware and software that can be employed in nuclear-weapon development or verification activities and is subject to strict export licensing to protect national security.
[0C] Nuclear Materials (Materials) -- 0C controls nuclear materials such as uranium, thorium, plutonium and other fissile or fertile substances in ore, compound, metal or processed fuel form. These items are subject to export restrictions to prevent the spread of nuclear weapons and related technology.
[0D] Nuclear Software -- Entry 0D on the Commerce Control List covers software specially designed or specifically adapted for nuclear weapons, nuclear reactors, or related nuclear-energy equipment, including programs for design, simulation, modelling, control, and analysis of such systems. Export of this software generally requires a license because it is deemed highly sensitive dual-use technology.
[0E] Nuclear Technology -- The 0E entry on the Commerce Control List covers nuclear technology, including the design, development, production, and use of nuclear reactors, fuel-cycle facilities, related equipment and software. Items such as reactor components, fuel-fabrication apparatus and related technical data are controlled and require an export license depending on the destination and end-use.
[1A] Materials, Chemicals - Systems and Equipment -- ECCN 1A covers equipment, systems and related hardware used to produce, process, or handle chemicals and other materials. It includes reactors, mixers, pumps, filtration units and control systems that are not specifically listed elsewhere. The control focuses on technology that can be applied to the manufacture of dual-use chemicals and related processes.
[1B] Materials, Chemicals - Test/Inspection Equipment -- ECCN 1B covers test and inspection equipment designed specifically for use with materials and chemicals, such as analytical instruments, spectrometers, and detectors that measure composition, purity, or performance. These items are controlled because they can be used to develop or verify chemical processes and therefore may have dual-use implications for national security and non-proliferation. Export of such equipment requires a license when shipped to destinations subject to the Commerce Control List.
[1C] Chemicals and Materials -- ECCN 1C covers chemicals and related materials, including industrial chemicals, toxic or harmful substances, and certain organic and inorganic compounds that are subject to export controls for national security and non-proliferation reasons. Items in this category are regulated because of their potential use in weapons, chemical warfare, or illicit activities.
[1D] Materials, Chemicals - Software -- 1D controls software used for the design, development, production, processing, or use of materials and chemicals, including programs that model chemical reactions, material properties, or manufacturing processes. The category targets dual-use software that could enable the creation of advanced materials, pharmaceuticals, or weapons-related substances, and is subject to licensing requirements depending on the destination, end-user and end-use.
[1E] Materials, Chemicals - Technology -- Entry 1E on the Commerce Control List covers technology and technical data related to the development, production, processing, use and testing of materials and chemicals, including specialized equipment, software and engineering information. It applies to items such as formulations, synthesis methods, analytical techniques and manufacturing processes that enable the creation or modification of chemical substances or advanced materials. Control is aimed at preventing proliferation of dual-use chemical and material technologies.
[2A] Materials Processing - Systems and Equipment -- ECCN 2A on the Commerce Control List covers dual-use systems and equipment used for processing raw materials, including crushers, grinders, mixers, extruders, furnaces and related control and monitoring devices. The classification applies to hardware and software that transform, shape, blend or otherwise treat bulk substances for industrial, scientific or commercial purposes, and is subject to export licensing based on destination and end-use.
[2B] Materials Processing - Test/Inspection Equipment -- ECCN 2B covers test and inspection equipment used in materials processing, such as spectrometers, X-ray diffractometers, particle analyzers and other devices that measure, evaluate or verify the physical or chemical properties of raw or intermediate materials during manufacturing. These items are controlled because they can be used to assess product quality, detect defects or monitor process parameters, and therefore may have dual-use applications.
[2D] Materials Processing - Software -- Entry 2D covers software specifically designed or adapted to control, monitor, or simulate materials processing operations such as casting, forging, extrusion, and heat treatment. It includes programs that adjust process parameters, predict material properties, or manage production equipment and is subject to export controls for dual-use technologies.
[2E] Materials Processing - Technology -- Entry 2E on the Commerce Control List governs technology for processing materials, including the methods, equipment and software used to transform raw substances into finished products such as metals, ceramics, polymers and composites. It controls the export of technical data, assistance and know-how related to these processing techniques.
[3A] Electronics - Systems and Equipment -- ECCN 3A identifies electronic systems and equipment that are subject to U.S. export controls, including high-performance computers, communications and radar systems, and specialized test, measurement and control devices. Items in this category are assessed for national security and foreign policy risks, and their export requires a licence depending on destination, end-use and end-user.
[3B] Electronics - Test/Inspection Equipment -- ECCN 3B on the Commerce Control List covers electronic test and inspection equipment, including instruments, devices, software and related technology used to measure, examine, calibrate, or analyze electronic components and systems. Items in this category are subject to U.S. export controls that vary with the destination country, end-use and end-user, and may require a license for certain shipments.
[3C] Electronics - Materials -- ECCN 3C items are electronic-related materials such as semiconductor substrates, photoresists, and other specialized chemicals and polymers used in the manufacture, fabrication or testing of electronic components. These items are controlled for national security and proliferation reasons and may require a license for export to certain destinations, depending on the end-use and the recipient.
[3D] Electronics - Software -- Entry 3D on the Commerce Control List covers software that is used in electronic equipment, including programs for design, development, testing, simulation, and operation of electronic devices and systems. It also includes software that controls or monitors electronic hardware, as well as related technical data and documentation. This category is subject to export licensing based on the software's functionality and the end-use.
[3E] Electronics - Technology -- The 3E entry on the Commerce Control List governs technology related to the design, development, production, and use of electronic equipment and components. It includes technical data, software, and instructions for manufacturing semiconductors, printed-circuit boards, integrated circuits, and other electronic assemblies, as well as processes for testing, inspection, and quality control of such hardware.
[4A] Computers - Systems and Equipment -- The 4A entry covers general-purpose computers and their associated systems and equipment, including complete computer units, peripheral devices, and related support hardware. It also controls components and assemblies that are integral to those computers, as well as software and technology needed for their design, development, or production. This category is applied to items that can be used in both civilian and military contexts, subject to export licensing based on end-use and destination.
[4D] Computers - Software -- Entry 4D on the Commerce Control List governs computers and software, covering general-purpose computing equipment, data-processing systems, and related software and firmware, while excluding items that are specifically listed under other ECCNs. This classification applies to hardware and programs designed for general computing functions rather than specialized or military-grade systems.
[4E] Computers - Technology -- Entry 4E controls technology related to computers, including technical data, engineering services, and software necessary for the design, development, production, operation, installation, maintenance, or repair of computer systems. It covers advanced architectures, high-performance processing units, and specialized components that enable sophisticated data processing, networking, or storage functions. Access to this technology is subject to export licensing requirements.
[5A1] Telecommunications - Systems and Equipment -- 5A1 applies to telecommunications systems and equipment that transmit, receive, process, route or convert communications, such as switches, routers, multiplexers, repeaters, and related hardware. Items falling under this category are controlled for national security reasons and may require an export license for certain destinations, especially when they have advanced performance characteristics or are intended for use in restricted end-users.
[5A2] Information Security - Systems and Equipment -- 5A2 on the Commerce Control List controls the export of information security systems, equipment and related components, including cryptographic hardware, secure communications devices, and the software and technology needed to develop, produce, or use them, encompassing tools for encryption, authentication, access control and data protection.
[5D1] Telecommunications - Software -- 5D1 covers software specially designed or modified for telecommunications equipment, including programs that enable the design, development, production, operation or maintenance of transmission, switching, routing, or networking systems. The classification applies to dual-use software that can be used for both civilian and military communications and is subject to licensing based on the destination, end-user and end-use. It does not include general-purpose or commercial off-the-shelf software without specific telecom functionality.
[5D2] Information Security - Software -- ECCN 5D2 on the Commerce Control List governs software designed or developed for information-security functions, including cryptographic encryption, authentication, key management, and data-integrity or secure-communication capabilities. Export of such software may require a license for shipments to countries, entities, or end-uses subject to U.S. export controls.
[5E1] Telecommunications - Technology -- 5E1 controls technology related to telecommunications, including the design, development, production, and testing of equipment and software used for transmitting, receiving, or processing
[5E2] Information Security - Technology -- ECCN 5E2 covers technology for the design, development, production, installation, or use of information security systems, including cryptographic security, encryption and decryption methods, key management, authentication, and access control. It applies to technical data, software, and assistance that enable the creation or operation of such security solutions.
[6A] Sensors and Lasers - Systems and Equipment -- Entry 6A of the Commerce Control List covers sensors and lasers together with the associated systems, assemblies, and equipment that employ these technologies for detecting, measuring, ranging, targeting, surveillance, or guidance purposes, including optical, infrared, ultraviolet, and laser-based devices and their integration into larger platforms. It also encompasses related accessories, mounting hardware, and control or processing units that enable these sensor or laser functions.
[6B] Sensors and Lasers - Test/Inspection Equipment -- ECCN 6B covers sensors, lasers and related test-inspection equipment used to measure, detect or analyze physical properties, including optical, infrared and radiation devices. These items are subject to export controls because they can be employed in military or dual-use applications, and a license may be required for shipments to restricted countries or end-users.
[6C] Sensors and Lasers - Materials -- Entry 6C of the Commerce Control List covers materials specially designed or modified for use in sensors and laser systems, including optical crystals, laser host compounds, photonic-bandgap structures, and related specialty chemicals and substrates that enable the fabrication, testing or operation of these devices. The control applies to both raw and processed forms when they are intended for sensor or laser applications.
[6D] Sensors and Lasers - Software -- ECCN 6D controls a range of dual-use sensors and laser equipment, together with software needed for the design, development, production, operation, installation, maintenance, or use of those items. The entry covers optical and infrared sensors, laser sources, and related control or analysis programs, and is subject to licensing based on the destination, end-user and intended application.
[6E] Sensors and Lasers - Technology -- The 6E entry controls technology for the design, development, production, or use of sensors and lasers, including related technical data, software and equipment. It covers items such as optical, infrared, ultraviolet and other electromagnetic detection or ranging devices, as well as laser sources and associated components. This category is subject to export licensing to protect national security and prevent proliferation of dual-use capabilities.
[7A] Navigation - Systems and Equipment -- ECCN 7A covers navigation systems and equipment, such as inertial navigation units, satellite-based positioning devices, and related hardware and software used to determine or control the movement of vehicles, vessels, or aircraft. Items in this category are subject to export controls based on their performance, end-use and destination.
[7B] Navigation - Test/Inspection Equipment -- Entry 7B of the Commerce Control List governs equipment used to test, calibrate, or inspect navigation systems, including simulators, bench-test sets, and diagnostic tools for GPS, inertial, or other position-determination devices. The control applies to hardware that verifies performance or assists in troubleshooting navigation hardware, but not the navigation equipment itself. Export of such items may require a license depending on destination, end-use, and the controlling country's policy.
[7D] Navigation - Software -- ECCN 7D refers to dual-use navigation software that performs positioning, route planning, waypoint generation, guidance, or other navigational functions for aircraft, vessels, ground vehicles, or space systems. The software is typically installed on computers to process data from sensors, maps, or satellite signals, and can be used for both civilian and military applications. Export of this software is controlled because it can enhance the precision and effectiveness of navigation capabilities.
[7E] Navigation - Technology -- The 7E entry on the Commerce Control List covers navigation technology, including hardware, software and technical data for systems that determine position, velocity or orientation. It applies to inertial, satellite-based, magnetic and other navigation equipment, as well as related design, testing and integration information.
[8A] Marine - Systems and Equipment -- The ECCN 8A covers marine systems and equipment, including propulsion, steering, navigation, communication and safety devices used on vessels. These items are controlled for export to manage the transfer of technology that could enhance naval capabilities, and licensing requirements vary by destination, end-user and the presence of advanced features.
[8B] Marine - Test/Inspection Equipment -- ECCN 8B controls equipment and related software used to test, measure, inspect or monitor marine vessels, components and systems, including nondestructive-test instruments, sonar and vibration analysers, and associated calibration and documentation tools. It also covers accessories, spare parts and training materials needed to operate such test and inspection equipment, subject to export licensing requirements for certain destinations.
[8C] Marine - Materials -- The 8C entry governs marine materials such as hull and superstructure components, deck and bulkhead structures, and associated alloys, composites, coatings and repair-oriented items used in the construction, modification or maintenance of vessels. Items listed under 8C are subject to export licensing when destined for countries or end-users for which the United States imposes controls for national-security reasons.
[8D] Marine - Software -- The 8D entry on the Commerce Control List controls software designed for use on marine vessels and related equipment, including navigation, propulsion, communications, and vessel-control systems. It applies to software that performs functions such as data processing, simulation, or analysis for maritime operations and is subject to export licensing requirements.
[8E] Marine - Technology -- The 8E entry on the Commerce Control List covers marine technology, including equipment, components, software and technical data used in the design, construction, operation, repair or maintenance of ships, boats and other watercraft, as well as related propulsion, navigation, and safety systems.
[9A] Aerospace - Systems and Equipment -- ECCN 9A refers to "Aerospace - Systems and Equipment" on the Commerce Control List. It controls aircraft, spacecraft, and related hardware such as airframes, propulsion systems, flight-control and navigation equipment, and certain aerospace test and support devices. The classification applies when these items incorporate advanced technology, have specific performance characteristics, or are designed for military or dual-use applications.
[9B] Aerospace - Test/Inspection Equipment -- The ECCN 9B category covers aerospace test, measurement and inspection equipment, such as calibration rigs, test benches, nondestructive evaluation tools and other devices used to verify the performance or integrity of aircraft, spacecraft and related components. Export of these items may require a licence depending on the destination, end-use and party involved.
[9C] Aerospace - Materials -- Entry 9C on the Commerce Control List governs aerospace-related materials such as advanced polymers, composites, metallic alloys, and other specialty substances used in aircraft and spacecraft manufacturing, covering items that contribute to propulsion, structural integrity, or performance. These materials are subject to export licensing requirements because they can enhance the capabilities of foreign military or aerospace programs.
[9D] Aerospace - Software -- 9D controls software that is specially designed or modified for aerospace applications, including design, analysis, simulation, flight-control, navigation and maintenance functions for aircraft, spacecraft, missiles and related systems. It covers source code, object code and related technical data that enable development, production or operation of such equipment, and is subject to EAR export controls.
[9E] Aerospace - Technology -- 9E controls technology and technical data related to aerospace systems, including design, development, production, operation, maintenance and testing of aircraft, spacecraft, missiles, UAVs and related components. It also covers engineering services, software and technical information necessary for the manufacture, refurbishment or use of such aerospace items.
[CAT0] Nuclear and Miscellaneous -- Category 0 of the Commerce Control List controls nuclear materials, facilities, equipment and related technology, including reactors, fuel, isotopes and associated technical data, as well as miscellaneous items that fall under nuclear non-proliferation regulations.
[CAT1] Materials, Chemicals, Microorganisms, Toxins -- CAT1 on the Commerce Control List groups a broad range of dual-use items that include raw materials, industrial chemicals, biological agents, microorganisms and toxins. These items are subject to export licensing because they can be used for weapons development, proliferation activities or other threats to national security.
[CAT2] Materials Processing -- CAT2 covers materials processing technologies and equipment
[CAT3] Electronics -- CAT3 covers a broad range of electronic equipment, components and assemblies such as computers, consumer appliances, audio-visual devices and related parts, primarily for civilian applications but subject to export controls because of potential dual-use. It includes both finished products and certain sub-systems that could be incorporated into military or strategic systems.
[CAT4] Computers -- CAT4 covers a broad range of general-purpose and specialized computer systems, including central processing units, motherboards, memory, storage devices, and peripheral equipment, as well as the software and firmware required to operate them. It also encompasses related accessories, network adapters, and components that enable data processing, communications, or control functions in commercial, scientific, or industrial applications. This category is subject to export controls based on the performance and technology levels of the items.
[CAT5] Telecommunications and Information Security -- Category 5 of the Commerce Control List governs telecommunications and information-security items, encompassing hardware, software and technology used for transmitting voice, data, video and other signals, as well as equipment and encryption components that protect network communications. Control applies to both commercial and dual-use products that provide or enhance secure communications, network management and information-security functions.
[CAT6] Sensors and Lasers -- CAT6 covers sensors and lasers that emit, detect, or measure electromagnetic radiation, including laser devices, optical sensors, and related components. It applies to items that can be used for precision targeting, range-finding, or other dual-use applications, and is subject to export licensing depending on the destination, end-use, and technical specifications.
[CAT7] Navigation and Avionics -- CAT7 governs the export of equipment, components, software and technology used for navigation, guidance, flight control and related avionics, including inertial navigation systems, radar, GPS receivers and aircraft electronic displays. Items designed for civilian aviation are generally subject to less restrictive licensing than those specifically adapted for military or dual-use platforms.
[CAT8] Marine -- CAT8 controls items related to marine technology, including ships, submersibles, propulsion systems, navigation and communication equipment, and related parts and accessories, primarily for civil and commercial use, subject to licensing when exported to certain destinations.
[CAT9] Aerospace and Propulsion -- CAT9 on the Commerce Control List covers a broad range of aerospace and propulsion items, including aircraft, spacecraft, missiles, rockets, jet and turbine engines, related components, and associated technical data. The category is subject to export licensing controls because of its potential military and dual-use applications.
[EAR99] Not on Commerce Control List -- EAR99 is the catch-all classification for items that are not specifically listed on the Commerce Control List. Such items are subject to the least restrictive export controls and normally can be shipped without a license, except when the destination is prohibited, restricted, or involves a known end-user of concern.
