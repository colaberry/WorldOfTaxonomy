[commissioning] Commissioning Standards -- Commissioning standards define the systematic process of planning, executing, and documenting the verification of HVAC, refrigeration, and building-performance systems to ensure they meet design intent, operate efficiently, and satisfy owner requirements. The standards outline roles, procedures, testing methods, and acceptance criteria for design, installation, and functional performance, providing a framework that improves quality, reduces operational risk, and supports sustainable building operation.
[energy] Energy Standards -- The ASHRAE Energy entry groups the association's core standards that set minimum energy-efficiency and performance requirements for heating, ventilation, air-conditioning, refrigeration and overall building systems. These documents guide designers, engineers and code officials toward sustainable, cost-effective building operation.
[guideline_0] Guideline 0 - The Commissioning Process -- Guideline 0 outlines the commissioning process for HVAC and building systems, defining steps for planning, testing, and verification to ensure performance meets design intent. It serves as a foundational reference for ASHRAE members implementing systematic quality assurance.
[guideline_1_1] Guideline 1.1 - HVAC&R Technical Requirements for the Commissioning Process -- Guideline 1.1 specifies the technical requirements that must be met during the commissioning of HVAC and refrigeration systems, covering performance verification, functional testing, and documentation of results to ensure equipment operates according to design intent and efficiency targets. It provides a systematic approach for planning, executing, and reporting commissioning activities in line with ASHRAE standards.
[hvac] HVAC Design Standards -- ASHRAE's HVAC Design Standards are a collection of key guidelines that define the planning, sizing, and performance criteria for heating, ventilation, air-conditioning, and refrigeration systems to achieve energy efficiency, indoor air quality, and occupant comfort. The hvac entry references core documents such as ASHRAE 90.1, 62.1, 55, and 15, providing technical direction for equipment selection, system design, and compliance verification.
[iaq] Indoor Air Quality Standards -- The IAQ entry covers ASHRAE standards that define acceptable indoor air quality levels, specifying ventilation rates, contaminant limits, and testing methods to ensure healthy building environments. It includes key documents such as ASHRAE 62.1 and 62.2, which guide the design, operation, and maintenance of HVAC systems to meet those air quality requirements.
[refrigeration] Refrigeration Standards -- The refrigeration entry in the ASHRAE Key Standards for HVAC, Refrigeration, and Building Performance compiles the essential standards that define design, installation, safety, performance and energy-efficiency requirements for refrigeration systems, referencing key documents such as ASHRAE 15, 34 and related guidelines used by engineers and facility managers.
[std_100] Standard 100 - Energy Efficiency in Existing Buildings -- Standard 100 provides guidelines for improving energy efficiency in existing commercial and residential buildings, outlining assessment methods, retrofitting strategies, and performance metrics. It assists facility managers and engineers in identifying cost-effective measures to reduce consumption and meet regulatory targets.
[std_105] Standard 105 - Standard Methods of Determining, Expressing, and Comparing Building Energy Performance -- Standard 105 establishes uniform procedures for measuring and reporting a building's energy use, defining metrics such as source- and site-energy consumption, energy use intensity, and normalized performance indices. The methods enable consistent comparison of energy performance across different building types, climates, and operational conditions.
[std_15] Standard 15 - Safety Standard for Refrigeration Systems -- ASHRAE Standard 15 establishes safety requirements for the design, construction, installation, operation and maintenance of refrigeration systems to protect personnel and the environment from hazards such as pressure, temperature and refrigerant exposure, and includes guidelines for system components, venting, and protective equipment. It is widely referenced by engineers, manufacturers and regulatory agencies to ensure safe, reliable refrigeration practices.
[std_15_ref] Standard 15 - Safety Standard for Refrigeration Systems and Nomenclature -- Standard 15, issued by ASHRAE, establishes safety requirements for the design, construction, installation, operation, and maintenance of refrigeration systems, and defines consistent nomenclature for components and hazards. It aims to protect personnel, property, and the environment by prescribing engineering controls, protective devices, labeling, and documentation practices for both commercial and industrial refrigeration applications.
[std_170] Standard 170 - Ventilation of Health Care Facilities -- ASHRAE Standard 170 specifies minimum ventilation rates, airflow patterns, and pressure relationships for health-care spaces to control airborne contaminants and protect patients, staff, and visitors. It covers operating rooms, isolation rooms, patient wards, and support areas, providing design and performance criteria that align with infection-control guidelines.
[std_189_1] Standard 189.1 - Standard for the Design of High-Performance Green Buildings -- ASHRAE Standard 189.1 sets minimum requirements for designing, constructing, and operating high-performance green buildings, addressing energy efficiency, water use, indoor environmental quality, and overall sustainability. [std_202] Standard 202 - Commissioning Process for Buildings and Systems -- Standard 202 outlines a systematic commissioning process for new and existing buildings and their mechanical, electrical, and control systems, detailing planning, design review, installation verification, functional testing and documentation requirements to confirm that performance meets design intent and operational efficiency.
[std_228] Standard 228 - Standard Method of Evaluating Zero Net Energy Building Performance -- Standard 228 outlines a method for quantifying zero net energy building performance, specifying how to measure total energy use, on-site renewable generation, and the resulting net energy balance. It serves as a verification tool for designers and engineers to demonstrate compliance with ZNE targets across various climates.
