[ann_1] Annex 1 - Definitions -- Annex 1 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade sets out the official definitions of key terms used in the agreement, such as technical regulation, standard, and conformity assessment, to ensure consistent interpretation and application by all members.
[ann_2] Annex 2 - Technical Expert Groups -- Annex 2 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade sets up Technical Expert Groups that give scientific and technical advice to the TBT Committee, helping develop standards and resolve technical disputes. The groups are composed of experts nominated by member governments and operate on a voluntary, collaborative basis.
[ann_3] Annex 3 - Code of Good Practice for Standards -- Annex 3 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade sets out a Code of Good Practice for Standards, guiding members on the development, use and recognition of standards to prevent unnecessary obstacles to trade. It emphasizes transparency, voluntary consensus, relevance to legitimate objectives and proportionality in applying standards.
[ann_3_1] Acceptance of Code of Good Practice -- The WTO Technical Barriers to Trade Agreement includes an annex provision, labeled ann_3_1, that addresses the acceptance of a Code of Good Practice. It encourages members to recognize and adopt such codes as a means of facilitating conformity assessment and reducing unnecessary trade barriers.
[ann_3_2] Obligations under the Code -- The entry outlines the duties that WTO members must fulfill under the Technical Barriers to Trade Agreement, specifying how they should apply the code's provisions, ensure non-discriminatory treatment of foreign products, and maintain transparency in technical regulations, standards and conformity-assessment procedures.
[art_10] Article 10 - Information about Technical Regulations -- Article 10 obliges WTO members to furnish, upon request, detailed information on any technical regulation they have adopted or are considering, including its text, purpose, scope, applicability and any related standards or conformity-assessment procedures. The provision promotes transparency, allowing other members to assess the regulation's impact on trade and to seek alternatives or adjustments where necessary.

[art_12] Article 12 - Special and Differential Treatment -- Article 12 grants developing and least-developed countries special and differential treatment, permitting longer time-frames for meeting technical regulations and offering technical assistance and capacity-building measures to help them comply with the agreement's standards.
[art_13] Article 13 - TBT Committee -- Article 13 creates the TBT Committee, a body that monitors implementation of the Technical Barriers to Trade Agreement, reviews members' technical regulations and standards, and serves as a forum for consultations, information exchange, and dispute prevention.
[art_2] Article 2 - Technical Regulations by Central Government Bodies -- Article 2 obliges central government bodies to ensure that technical regulations they adopt are non-discriminatory, avoid unnecessary obstacles to trade, and are based on relevant international standards where available. It also requires transparency, allowing affected parties to comment before final adoption.
[art_3] Article 3 - Technical Regulations by Local Government Bodies -- Article 3 defines technical regulations that may be issued by local government bodies, specifying that such measures must be transparent, non-discriminatory and not more trade-restrictive than necessary to achieve legitimate objectives such as safety, health or environmental protection. It requires that the regulations be based on relevant international standards where possible and that they be made available to interested parties for comment before adoption.
[art_4] Article 4 - Conformity Assessment Procedures (Preparation, Adoption, Application) -- Article 4 requires WTO members to make their conformity-assessment procedures for products transparent, non-discriminatory and consistent with relevant international standards, covering the preparation, adoption and application stages. It encourages the use of equivalence and mutual recognition to avoid unnecessary trade barriers while allowing each country to maintain legitimate safety and quality objectives.
[art_5] Article 5 - Conformity Assessment Procedures by Central Government Bodies -- Article 5 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade requires central government bodies to establish transparent, non-discriminatory conformity assessment procedures, ensuring that products meet applicable standards and regulations without unnecessary barriers to trade. It also mandates that such procedures be based on international standards where they exist and that governments provide timely information on assessment requirements and results.
[art_6] Article 6 - Recognition of Conformity Assessment by Central Government Bodies -- Article 6 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade obliges member governments to accept conformity-assessment results-such as testing, inspection or certification-performed by bodies designated by the central authority of another member, provided the procedures are transparent, non-discriminatory and meet the agreement's essential principles. This facilitates market access by reducing redundant assessments while allowing each government to maintain oversight of the designated bodies.
[art_7] Article 7 - Procedures for Assessment of Conformity by Local Government Bodies -- Article 7 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade requires that local government bodies use transparent, non-discriminatory procedures when assessing conformity of products with technical regulations, ensuring that assessment methods, timelines and documentation are clearly defined and applied consistently to all relevant parties.
[art_8] Article 8 - Procedures for Assessment by Non-Governmental Bodies -- Article 8 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade sets out rules for the use of non-governmental bodies in assessing conformity of products, requiring that procedures be transparent, based on clear and public criteria, and provide participants with an opportunity to be heard. It obliges members to ensure the bodies are impartial, independent, and that decisions can be appealed or reviewed in accordance with the agreement's principles.
[art_9] Article 9 - International and Regional Systems -- Article 9 of the WTO Agreement on Technical Barriers to Trade encourages members to base their technical regulations and conformity assessment procedures on international standards or, where appropriate, regional standards, and requires that any deviation be no more trade-restrictive than necessary to achieve legitimate objectives.
