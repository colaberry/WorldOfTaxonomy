[10] Energy -- The ICB code 10 - Energy classifies firms that generate, produce, or supply energy, covering oil and gas exploration, production, refining and marketing, power generation, renewable sources, and the equipment and services that support these activities.
[1010] Oil, Gas and Coal -- The ICB code 1010 groups companies operating in the oil, natural-gas and coal sectors, covering activities such as exploration, extraction, production, refining, distribution and the provision of related equipment and services. This classification captures firms from upstream to downstream energy businesses.
[15] Basic Materials -- The ICB code 15 designates the Basic Materials sector, covering firms that extract, produce or process raw natural resources. It includes industries such as mining, chemicals, forestry, construction materials and commodities that supply essential inputs for other sectors.
[1510] Chemicals -- The ICB sector 1510 Chemicals comprises companies that develop, produce and sell basic and specialty chemicals used in industrial processes, agriculture, health and consumer products. It includes firms engaged in petrochemicals, polymers, fertilizers, adhesives, paints and other chemical intermediates. The sector reflects the global chemical industry's role in supplying essential materials for many downstream sectors.
[1520] Basic Resources -- Basic Resources comprises companies that extract, mine, quarry or produce primary raw materials such as metals, minerals, coal and other commodities. These firms focus on the initial stages of the supply chain, turning natural resources into usable inputs for other industries. The sub-industry is part of the broader Materials sector in the ICB system.
[20] Industrials -- In the Industry Classification Benchmark the code 20 identifies the Industrials sector, covering companies that manufacture capital goods, provide commercial services and operate transportation, logistics and infrastructure. It groups firms such as machinery makers, aerospace and defence producers, and providers of essential industrial services.
[2010] Construction and Materials -- ICB code 2010 - Construction and Materials groups firms that build infrastructure, residential and commercial projects, and supply the raw and processed materials needed for construction. It includes contractors, engineering and construction services, as well as producers of cement, aggregates, steel, and other building supplies.
[2020] Industrial Goods and Services -- The ICB 2020 sector groups companies that produce or provide equipment, components, and services used in manufacturing, construction, and infrastructure. It includes aerospace, industrial machinery, engineering services, and related logistics. The sector is part of the broader Industrials supersector.
[30] Consumer Discretionary -- Code 30 in the Industry Classification Benchmark denotes the Consumer Discretionary sector, covering firms that sell non-essential goods and services such as retail, automobiles, consumer durables, leisure and travel. These companies are sensitive to consumer income and confidence, and their performance often fluctuates with economic cycles.
[3010] Automobiles and Parts -- The ICB code 3010 covers companies that design, manufacture and sell motor vehicles and related components. It includes manufacturers of cars, trucks and buses as well as producers of engines, brakes, tires and other parts used in vehicle assembly and maintenance. The classification sits within the Consumer Goods sector and the Automobiles & Parts industry group.
[3020] Consumer Products and Services -- ICB code 3020 classifies companies that manufacture, distribute, or retail consumer goods and related services, including food, beverages, personal-care items, household products, and leisure-related services. These firms target individual buyers, providing everyday products that are directly used by households rather than industrial customers.
[3030] Media -- The ICB code 3030 Media groups companies that create, distribute or monetize content across television, radio, publishing and digital platforms, including broadcasters, newspaper and magazine publishers, and streaming-service providers. It captures firms whose primary business is delivering audio-visual or written content to audiences and generating revenue from advertising, subscriptions or licensing.
[3040] Retailers -- The ICB code 3040 Retailers groups companies that sell consumer goods directly to individuals through physical stores, online platforms, or catalogue sales. It includes a wide range of outlets such as department stores, specialty retailers, supermarkets, and e-commerce sites, covering both food and non-food product categories. This sector reflects businesses focused on the final stage of the distribution chain to end-users.
[3050] Travel and Leisure -- The ICB code 3050 - Travel and Leisure classifies firms that generate revenue from tourism, hospitality and recreational activities. It covers airlines, hotels, cruise operators, travel agencies, theme parks, sports and entertainment venues, and other companies that provide services and experiences for people's leisure and travel needs.
[35] Consumer Staples -- The ICB code 35 designates the Consumer Staples sector, encompassing firms that supply essential everyday products such as food, beverages, tobacco, personal care items and household goods. These companies are characterized by stable demand, relatively low price volatility and often generate consistent dividends.
[3510] Food, Beverage and Tobacco -- The ICB code 3510 identifies the Food, Beverage & Tobacco sector, part of the Consumer Staples supersector. It groups companies that manufacture, process, and distribute food products, alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages, and tobacco items, ranging from packaged food producers to beverage bottlers and cigarette manufacturers. This sector reflects businesses whose primary revenue comes from consumable goods with relatively stable demand.
[3520] Personal Care, Drug and Grocery -- The ICB code 3520 denotes the Personal Care, Drug and Grocery industry, encompassing companies that manufacture, market and sell consumer health and hygiene products, over-the-counter medicines and pharmacy items, as well as grocery retailers that provide food and everyday household goods. It groups businesses from cosmetics and toiletries to drugstores and supermarkets under a single sector.
[40] Health Care -- The ICB code 40 designates the Health Care sector, encompassing firms that develop, produce, and distribute pharmaceuticals, biotechnology products, medical devices, and diagnostic tools, as well as providers of health services and facilities. This sector groups companies involved in the research, manufacturing, and delivery of health-related goods and services.
[4010] Health Care -- The Health Care industry (ICB code 4010) comprises firms that deliver medical services, develop and produce pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and medical equipment, and provide related support services such as diagnostics and health-care facilities. It spans hospitals and clinics, drug and vaccine manufacturers, biotech research companies, and manufacturers of devices and diagnostic tools that enable patient treatment and disease management.
[45] Financials -- The ICB code 45 denotes the Financials sector, which groups companies that provide banking, insurance, asset management and other diversified financial services. It includes firms ranging from commercial banks and credit institutions to insurers, investment managers and specialized finance providers. This sector captures the core activities of lending, risk mitigation, investment and financial intermediation.
[4510] Banks -- Code 4510 in the Industry Classification Benchmark designates the "Banks" sub-industry, part of the Financial Services sector. It groups companies whose primary activities are taking deposits, providing loans, and offering a full range of commercial and retail banking services. These firms are typically regulated financial institutions that handle payments, wealth management and related financial products.
[4520] Financial Services -- The ICB code 4520 designates the Banking sector, encompassing institutions that accept deposits, originate loans, manage payment and settlement services, and offer related financial products to individuals, businesses and governments. It includes commercial banks, savings banks, retail banks and their subsidiaries that operate primarily in these core banking activities.
[4530] Insurance -- The ICB code 4530 groups companies that provide insurance services, including life, non-life, and reinsurance. These firms underwrite risk, collect premiums, and manage claims for individuals and businesses.
[50] Technology -- In the Industry Classification Benchmark, code 50 designates the Technology sector, covering firms that create, produce, or support hardware, software, semiconductors, IT services and internet-related businesses. It groups together sub-industries such as software, computer equipment, communications equipment, and electronic components.
[5010] Software and Computer Services -- The ICB code 5010 designates the Software and Computer Services sector, covering businesses that develop, license, and maintain software applications as well as firms that provide IT consulting, data processing, and managed technology services. Companies in this category range from enterprise-resource-planning providers to cloud-based platform operators, reflecting the breadth of modern digital infrastructure and software solutions.
[5020] Technology Hardware and Equipment -- The ICB sub-industry 5020 groups firms that design, manufacture and sell technology hardware and equipment, including computers, peripherals, communications gear, consumer electronics and related components such as semiconductors. These companies supply the physical infrastructure that underpins digital and communication services.
[55] Telecommunications -- The ICB code 55 groups companies that provide voice, data and internet services, operating fixed-line, mobile, satellite and broadband networks. It includes network operators, service providers and infrastructure firms that enable communication between individuals and businesses. The sector reflects the core communications backbone
[5510] Telecommunications -- The ICB code 5510 groups companies that deliver voice, data and internet services, covering both fixed-line and mobile networks, as well as related infrastructure and service providers. It also includes firms that operate satellite communications, broadband platforms and other telecommunication utilities.
[60] Utilities -- The ICB code 60 groups companies that generate, transmit and distribute essential services such as electricity, gas and water. Utilities are typically regulated providers that offer stable, dividend-paying businesses and are less sensitive to economic cycles.
[6010] Utilities -- 6010 Utilities includes firms that generate, transmit and distribute electricity, gas, water and related services. These companies are usually regulated and provide essential public infrastructure such as power generation, renewable energy, and water treatment and distribution.
[65] Real Estate -- Code 65 in the Industry Classification Benchmark denotes the Real Estate sector, encompassing firms that develop, own, lease, and manage properties, as well as real-estate investment trusts that generate income from rental and property-related activities. This sector groups together companies whose primary business is the acquisition, development, and operation of residential, commercial, and industrial real-estate assets.
[6510] Real Estate -- The ICB code 6510 "Real Estate" covers companies that own, develop, manage or lease property and provide related services. It includes residential, commercial and industrial property developers, real estate investment trusts and firms offering property management, brokerage and valuation. This sector reflects the performance of the broader real-estate market and its income-generating assets.
