[4_1] 4.1 Purpose and benefits of risk assessment -- The entry explains that risk assessment aims to identify potential threats, evaluate likelihood and impact, and support decision-making; benefits include prioritising controls, allocating resources efficiently, improving stakeholder confidence, and enhancing organisational resilience.
[4_2] 4.2 Risk assessment and the risk management framework -- The entry explains how a risk assessment is performed as an integral part of the overall risk-management framework, linking the selection and application of assessment techniques to the broader processes for identifying, analysing, evaluating and treating risks in accordance with IEC 31010:2019. It guides practitioners on aligning assessment outcomes with governance, policy and continuous improvement.
[4_3] 4.3 Risk assessment and the risk management process -- Entry 4.3 in IEC 31010:2019 explains how risk assessment fits within the broader risk management process, detailing the sequence of identifying hazards, analyzing likelihood and consequences, evaluating risk levels, and determining treatment options before monitoring and review. It emphasizes the iterative nature of assessment and its role in informing decision-making throughout the management cycle.
[5_1] 5.1 Overview -- The 5.1 Overview introduces the purpose and scope of the risk-assessment techniques covered in IEC 31010, outlines the structure of the chapter and defines key terms, providing a concise context for selecting and applying the methods described later in the standard.
[5_2] 5.2 Risk identification -- 5.2 Risk identification outlines the structured process of pinpointing risk sources, events, causes and potential consequences within a given context, guiding the selection of techniques such as checklists, brainstorming, interviews and historical data analysis to ensure a comprehensive inventory of risks before further assessment.
[5_3] 5.3 Risk analysis -- Risk analysis is a systematic process for identifying potential hazards, estimating their likelihood and consequences, and evaluating overall risk exposure. It produces quantitative or qualitative assessments that help prioritize risks and inform subsequent treatment or mitigation decisions.
[5_4] 5.4 Risk evaluation -- Risk evaluation is the step where estimated risk levels are compared with established acceptance criteria to decide if they are tolerable. It assesses the magnitude, likelihood and consequences of each risk, determines required actions and helps prioritize treatment options.
[annex_a] Annex A - Comparison of Risk Assessment Techniques
[annex_b] Annex B - Risk Assessment Techniques -- Annex B of IEC 31010 2019 compiles a catalogue of risk-assessment techniques that can be applied in risk-management projects, ranging from qualitative methods such as brainstorming and check-lists to quantitative approaches including Monte Carlo simulation and fault-tree analysis, and provides guidance on selecting the most suitable technique based on the nature of the risk, available data and required level of detail.
[b1] B.1 Brainstorming -- B.1 Brainstorming is a qualitative risk-assessment technique that gathers a group of knowledgeable participants to generate a wide range of possible risk events, causes and consequences through free-flow discussion. The method relies on collective expertise and creativity, encouraging rapid idea capture without immediate judgement to identify potential hazards early in the risk analysis process.
[b10] B.10 Event tree analysis -- Event tree analysis is a forward-looking, diagrammatic technique that starts from a defined initiating event and maps out subsequent possible outcomes, incorporating success or failure of safety functions to quantify the likelihood of each scenario. It is used to assess complex risks and supports decision-making in IEC 31010:2019.
[b11] B.11 Cause-and-consequence analysis -- Cause-and-consequence analysis is a systematic method that identifies potential causes of a risk event and maps the resulting consequences, often using cause or fault trees. It visualizes the logical links between initiating factors and outcomes to support decisions on control measures.

[b13] B.13 Monte Carlo simulation -- Monte Carlo simulation is a quantitative risk-assessment technique that uses random sampling to model uncertainty and generate probability distributions of possible outcomes. It enables analysts to evaluate complex systems or processes where analytical solutions are impractical, supporting decision-making by quantifying the likelihood and impact of risk events.
[b14] B.14 Bayesian analysis -- Bayesian analysis is a quantitative risk-assessment technique that uses probability theory to update the likelihood of risk events as new evidence emerges. By combining prior information with observed data, it produces revised risk estimates, enabling more informed decisions under uncertainty.
[b2] B.2 Structured interviews -- Structured interviews are a qualitative risk assessment technique that involves guided conversations with subject-matter experts to elicit knowledge about potential hazards, their causes and impacts. The interview protocol is predefined to ensure consistency, while allowing interviewees to elaborate on context-specific risk information.
[b3] B.3 Delphi technique -- The Delphi technique is a structured forecasting method that gathers expert judgments through several questionnaire rounds, using anonymous responses and controlled feedback to build consensus on risk factors, likelihoods and impacts. It helps reduce groupthink and provides a systematic way to assess uncertainties in complex risk scenarios.
[b4] B.4 Checklists -- Checklists are structured lists of predefined questions or items used to systematically identify potential hazards, assess controls, and verify compliance during risk assessment. They guide assessors through relevant criteria, promote consistency, and help capture overlooked risks, making the process more efficient and repeatable.
[b5] B.5 Preliminary hazard analysis -- Preliminary hazard analysis is an early-stage, systematic technique used to identify potential hazards associated with a system, process or activity and to evaluate their possible causes and effects. It helps focus later, more detailed risk-assessment efforts by highlighting the most significant risk drivers.
[b6] B.6 HAZOP study -- B.6 HAZOP study is a systematic, team-based technique for identifying hazards and operability problems in process designs by applying guide words to examine deviations from intended function. It supports risk assessment by revealing potential causes, consequences and existing safeguards, helping decision-makers prioritize risk reduction measures.
[b7] B.7 HACCP -- B.7 HACCP in IEC 31010:2019 denotes Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points, a systematic preventive method used to identify and assess food-safety hazards, determine critical control points, and define monitoring and corrective actions to manage risks throughout the production process.
[b8] B.8 FMEA and FMECA -- B.8 FMEA and FMECA are systematic techniques used to identify possible failure modes of a system, evaluate the effects of those failures on performance, and prioritize them based on severity, occurrence and detectability. FMECA extends the analysis by assigning a criticality rating that quantifies the risk associated with each failure mode, supporting more informed risk-reduction decisions.
[b9] B.9 Fault tree analysis -- Fault tree analysis (FTA) is a deductive, top-down method for identifying the combinations of hardware and software failures, human errors and external events that can lead to a defined undesired system state. It represents these pathways in a logical diagram of events and gates, enabling quantitative or qualitative risk assessment and the prioritisation of corrective actions.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Core Concepts -- Clause 4 presents the fundamental concepts that underpin risk assessment in IEC 31010:2019, defining risk, risk sources, risk targets and the relationship between risk, uncertainty and decision-making. It establishes the terminology and basic principles that guide the selection and application of risk-assessment techniques.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Risk Assessment Process -- Clause 5 of IEC 31010:2019 outlines the systematic steps for conducting a risk assessment, guiding organizations to define the context, identify hazards, evaluate likelihood and consequences, and prioritize risks. It emphasizes documentation, stakeholder involvement, and the selection of appropriate assessment techniques to support informed decision-making.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Selecting Risk Assessment Techniques -- Clause 6 of IEC 31010:2019 outlines the process for choosing appropriate risk assessment techniques. It guides practitioners to match techniques to the context, objectives and data availability while considering resource constraints and stakeholder needs.
