[ann_b] Annex B - Transparency of SPS Regulations -- Annex B of the WTO Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures sets out requirements for the transparency of SPS regulations, obligating members to notify proposed measures, publish final standards and provide access to relevant scientific information, thereby ensuring that trade partners can assess compliance and avoid unnecessary barriers.

This ensures transparency, allowing trading partners and stakeholders to review and understand the standards that govern food safety and plant health. [ann_c] Annex C - Control, Inspection and Approval Procedures -- Annex C of the WTO SPS Agreement sets out the rules that members must follow when establishing control, inspection and approval procedures for sanitary and phytosanitary measures, requiring transparency, scientific justification and non-discriminatory treatment of imports.
[art_1] Article 1 - General Provisions -- Article 1 establishes the purpose and scope of the Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures, defining the obligations of WTO members to protect human, animal and plant health while ensuring that measures are not applied in a manner that creates unnecessary barriers to trade. It sets out basic principles such as scientific justification, non-discrimination, and the use of internationally recognized standards.
[art_10] Article 10 - Special and Differential Treatment -- Article 10 of the WTO SPS Agreement provides developing and least-developed members with special and differential treatment, allowing them greater flexibility, longer implementation periods and access to technical assistance when adopting sanitary and phytosanitary measures, while requiring that those measures remain scientifically justified and not disguised as trade barriers.
[art_11] Article 11 - Consultations and Dispute Settlement -- Article 11 outlines the procedure for members to request consultations when a sanitary or phytosanitary measure is contested, specifying time limits for initiating talks and the agenda for discussion. If the parties cannot resolve the issue through consultations, the matter may be referred to the WTO dispute-settlement system for further adjudication.
[art_12] Article 12 - Administration -- Article 12 of the WTO Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures sets out the administrative requirements for SPS measures, mandating that they be applied in a transparent, consistent and non-discriminatory manner, with clear procedures for review and appeal, adequate resources and a legal framework to ensure effective implementation.
[art_13] Article 13 - Implementation -- Article 13 obliges WTO members to promptly notify other members of any sanitary or phytosanitary measure that could affect trade, providing a description of the measure, the scientific justification, and the reason for its adoption. The article also requires members to respond to inquiries and allow interested parties to comment on the proposed measure, ensuring transparency in the implementation of SPS regulations.
[art_2] Article 2 - Basic Rights and Obligations -- Article 2 establishes the fundamental rights and duties of WTO members under the SPS Agreement, requiring that any sanitary or phytosanitary measure be applied only to protect human, animal or plant health and be based on scientific principles. It also obliges members to ensure that such measures are not applied arbitrarily or as disguised restrictions on international trade.
[art_3] Article 3 - Harmonization -- Article 3 of the WTO Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures requires members to align their SPS regulations with relevant international standards, guidelines or recommendations whenever they exist, and to ensure that any higher level of protection is scientifically justified. This provision encourages the harmonization of SPS measures across trading partners while allowing limited deviations for legitimate health or safety reasons.
[art_4] Article 4 - Equivalence -- Article 4 of the WTO Agreement on the Application of Sanitary and Phytosanitary Measures defines "equivalence" as the mutual acceptance by members of each other's SPS measures that achieve the same level of health protection, even if the regulatory approaches differ. It requires members to consider scientific evidence, encourage information exchange, and may allow temporary exemptions while equivalence is assessed.
[art_5] Article 5 - Assessment of Risk and Determination of Level of Protection -- Article 5 of the WTO SPS Agreement requires each member to assess the risks associated with pests, diseases, or contaminants before imposing sanitary or phytosanitary measures, and to base any restrictions on scientific evidence and the level of protection deemed appropriate for human, animal or plant health. The provision also obliges members to consider international standards where they exist and to ensure that measures are not more trade-restrictive than necessary.
[art_6] Article 6 - Adaptation to Regional Conditions -- Article 6 permits WTO members to adapt sanitary and phytosanitary measures to differing regional or sub-national conditions, provided the variations are grounded in scientific evidence, are not arbitrary, and do not constitute a disguised restriction on international trade. The provision allows distinct levels of protection in different areas of a country when justified by local circumstances.
[art_7] Article 7 - Transparency -- Article 7 of the WTO SPS Agreement requires members to ensure that any sanitary or phytosanitary measure is promptly published, accessible, and transparent, allowing interested parties to review the measure's content and rationale. It also obliges governments to provide opportunities for public comment and to disclose any related scientific evidence, technical information, and the procedures used in decision-making. This promotes predictability and accountability in trade-related health regulations.
[art_8] Article 8 - Control, Inspection and Approval Procedures -- Article 8 requires that any sanitary or phytosanitary measure involving control, inspection or approval be applied only to the extent necessary to protect human, animal or plant life or health, be based on scientific principles and established international standards, and be administered transparently, consistently and without unjustified discrimination.
[art_9] Article 9 - Technical Assistance -- Article 9 obliges WTO members to provide developing countries with technical assistance and capacity-building to help them implement the SPS Agreement, including training, sharing of expertise and access to resources that improve their sanitary and phytosanitary standards.
