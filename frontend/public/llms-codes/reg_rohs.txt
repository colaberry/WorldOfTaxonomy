[annex_iii] Annex III - Exemptions -- Annex III of the RoHS 2 Directive lists the specific exemptions that permit the use of otherwise restricted hazardous substances in certain applications or product types, often subject to time-limited or conditional approval. It details which substances may be used, the justification for each exemption, and any applicable dates or restrictions, providing a framework for manufacturers to comply while addressing technical or economic constraints.
[annex_iv] Annex IV - Exemptions for Medical Devices -- Annex IV of the EU Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive 2011/65/EU (RoHS 2) enumerates the specific exemptions that apply to medical devices, permitting limited use of restricted substances when no technically or medically feasible alternative exists and the device is essential for patient care.
[art_2] Art 2 - Scope (EEE categories) -- Article 2 of the RoHS 2 Directive sets out the scope of the restriction, specifying which types of electrical and electronic equipment are covered. It lists the seven main EEE categories-large household appliances, small household appliances, IT and telecommunications equipment, consumer equipment, lighting, electrical and electronic tools, and medical devices-defining the products to which the substance limits apply.
[art_3] Art 3 - Definitions -- Article 3 of the RoHS 2 Directive supplies the core terminology for the regulation, defining terms such as restricted substances, electrical and electronic equipment, homogeneous material and manufacturer, among others, to ensure a common understanding for compliance and enforcement. These definitions establish the scope of the hazardous-substance bans applied to products placed on the EU market.
[art_4] Art 4 - Prevention (restriction of substances) -- Article 4 of the RoHS 2 Directive sets out the preventive obligations for manufacturers and importers, requiring that all electrical and electronic equipment placed on the EU market contain no restricted hazardous substances above the specified concentration limits. It also mandates the preparation and retention of technical documentation to demonstrate compliance.

[ce_marking] CE Marking and Declaration of Conformity -- The CE marking signifies that a product meets EU health, safety and environmental requirements, while the Declaration of Conformity is the accompanying document where the manufacturer confirms compliance with all applicable directives, including the RoHS 2 restriction on hazardous substances.
[dbp] DBP - max 0.1% (added RoHS 3) -- DBP, or dibutyl phthalate, is limited to a maximum of 0.1 % by weight in any homogeneous material under the EU RoHS Directive, a restriction that was introduced with the RoHS 3 amendment. This limit applies to electronic and electrical equipment placed on the EU market.
[dehp] DEHP - max 0.1% (added RoHS 3) -- DEHP, a phthalate plasticiser, is limited to a maximum of 0.1 % by weight in homogeneous materials under the EU Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive 2011/65/EU, with this specific limit introduced in the RoHS 3 amendment. The restriction applies to electrical and electronic equipment placed on the European market, ensuring that products comply with the latest hazardous substance limits.
[dibp] DIBP - max 0.1% (added RoHS 3) -- Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP) is a plasticiser that may be present in electronic and electrical equipment. Under the EU RoHS 2 directive its concentration is limited to a maximum of 0.1 % by weight in any homogeneous material, a restriction that was introduced with the later RoHS 3 amendment. This limit aims to reduce exposure to hazardous substances in consumer products.
[enforcement] Market Surveillance and Enforcement -- The enforcement entry outlines the market-surveillance framework that EU member states must apply to ensure compliance with RoHS 2, including authority powers to inspect products, request documentation, and verify that electronic and electrical equipment does not contain prohibited hazardous substances above the set limits. It specifies procedures for non-compliance actions such as corrective measures, product withdrawals, and penalties, aiming to protect health and the environment while maintaining a level playing field for manufacturers.
[exemptions] Exemptions -- The exemptions entry identifies specific substances, materials, or applications that are temporarily or permanently excluded from the RoHS 2 restrictions, permitting the use of otherwise prohibited hazardous substances under defined conditions such as limited production volumes, particular product categories, or until suitable alternatives become available.
[hex_chrome] Hexavalent Chromium (Cr6+) - max 0.1% -- Hexavalent chromium (Cr6+) is restricted under the EU RoHS 2 directive, which limits its presence in electrical and electronic equipment to a maximum concentration of 0.1 percent by weight, measured on a homogeneous material basis. This restriction aims to reduce exposure to a known carcinogen and protect human health and the environment.
[lead] Lead (Pb) - max 0.1% -- Under the EU RoHS 2 directive, lead (Pb) in any homogeneous material of covered electrical or electronic equipment may not exceed 0.1 % by weight. This limit applies to all components, sub-assemblies and raw materials used in the product. Exceeding the threshold requires a special exemption or the product is non-compliant.
[mercury] Mercury (Hg) - max 0.1% -- The RoHS 2 directive limits mercury in electrical and electronic equipment to a maximum of 0.1 percent by weight on a homogeneous material basis, meaning the substance cannot exceed that concentration in any individual component. This restriction is intended to reduce the environmental and health risks posed by mercury when products are manufactured, used, or discarded. Compliance requires manufacturers to verify that mercury levels stay below the specified limit.
[obligations] Producer Obligations -- Under the EU RoHS 2 directive, producers of electronic and electrical equipment must ensure their products meet the restricted-substance limits, maintain technical documentation, and provide EU conformity declarations. They must also register each product in the national database, report non-compliant items, and cooperate with authorities for market surveillance.
[pbb] Polybrominated biphenyls (PBB) - max 0.1% -- Polybrominated biphenyls (PBB) are brominated flame-retardant chemicals that can persist in the environment and pose health risks. Under the EU RoHS 2 directive they are listed as a restricted substance, with a maximum allowable concentration of 0.1 % by weight in homogeneous materials.
[pbde] Polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDE) - max 0.1% -- Polybrominated diphenyl ethers are brominated flame-retardant chemicals used in plastics, textiles and electronic casings. Under the EU RoHS 2 restriction, products placed on the market may contain PBDEs only up to a maximum concentration of 0.1 percent by weight, with higher levels prohibited.
[restricted] Restricted Substances -- The RoHS 2 (Directive 2011/65/EU) classifies certain chemicals as "restricted," meaning they may be used in electrical and electronic equipment only up to a maximum concentration of 0.1 % by weight, with lead, mercury, cadmium and hexavalent chromium limited to 0.01 %. Exceeding these limits is prohibited for products placed on the EU market.
[scope] Scope and Definitions -- The Scope and Definitions section outlines the range of electrical and electronic equipment to which the RoHS 2 directive applies, specifying the product categories and the substances subject to restriction. It defines key terms such as "restricted substances," "substantial proportion," and exemption criteria, establishing the geographical and regulatory boundaries of the law.
[tech_doc] Technical Documentation -- Technical documentation under RoHS 2 is the set of records a manufacturer must keep to demonstrate that a product complies with the restriction of hazardous substances. It includes the bill of materials, substance declarations, test reports, risk assessments, and any corrective actions taken, and must be retained for ten years after the last unit is placed on the market. This documentation enables authorities to verify compliance during inspections or market surveillance.
