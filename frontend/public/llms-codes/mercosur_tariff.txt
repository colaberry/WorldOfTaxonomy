[MT] NCM Sections -- The NCM Sections are the highest-level groupings within the Mercosur Common External Tariff, aligning the regional Nomenclatura Comum do Mercosul with the Harmonized System's 21 broad categories of goods. They organize products into sections such as "Animal products," "Chemicals," and "Machinery," facilitating customs duties, trade statistics, and regulatory oversight across Mercosur member states.
[MT.I] Live Animals -- This heading gathers all living animals, domesticated or wild, presented in their natural state for breeding, transport, or consumption, including livestock, poultry, fish and other species. Under the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff it defines the duty rate applied to such live animals.

[MT.III] Fats and Oils -- MT.III includes animal and vegetable fats and oils, both crude and refined, together with their derived products such as tallow, butter, margarine, cocoa butter and related fractions, irrespective of hydrogenation or other processing.
[MT.IV] Prepared Foodstuffs -- MT.IV in the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff groups all prepared foodstuffs, covering ready-to-eat, cooked, preserved or otherwise processed consumables for human use. The heading applies to items that have undergone industrial preparation such as cooking, canning, drying, fermenting or the addition of additives, and excludes raw agricultural products.
[MT.IX] Wood -- The MERCOSUR Common External Tariff entry MT.IX groups all wood products that are not further processed into furniture or paper, including logs, timber, sawn or planed wood, and other untreated or minimally treated wood used for construction, manufacturing or fuel.
[MT.V] Mineral Products -- MT.V covers all mineral products imported into the MERCOSUR customs area, including ores, metals, industrial minerals and related semi-finished materials. These items are classified for the common external tariff rates applied by member countries.
[MT.VI] Chemical Products -- MT.VI groups all chemical products imported under the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff, ranging from inorganic compounds and fertilizers to organic polymers and industrial intermediates. These items are taxed at the uniform external tariff rates set for the bloc, with specific duty percentages determined by detailed sub-headings and any applicable trade agreements.

[MT.X] Paper -- MT.X in the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff (NCM) covers paper and paper-based goods, including sheets, rolls and cardboard used for printing, writing, packaging and similar applications. The classification applies to uncoated, coated, recycled and specialty papers, distinguishing them from pulp, paperboard and paper products with additional processing.
[MT.XI] Textiles -- MT.XI is the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff chapter for textiles, covering yarns, fibers, woven and knitted fabrics, as well as finished textile articles such as garments, household textiles and technical fabrics, all subject to the region's external tariff schedule.
[MT.XII] Footwear -- The MERCOSUR Common External Tariff (NCM) code MT.XII classifies all footwear for tariff purposes within the Southern Common Market. It includes shoes, boots, sandals and other foot coverings, organized by material, construction and intended use, and determines the duty rate applied to imports into member countries.
[MT.XIII] Ceramics and Glass -- MT.XIII under the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff groups together ceramic and glass articles, ranging from porcelain and stoneware tableware to tiles, sanitary fixtures, and structural ceramics, as well as flat glass, container glass, glassware and other manufactured glass products. The chapter applies the common external tariff rates for these items when they are imported into MERCOSUR member states.
[MT.XIV] Precious Metals -- MT.XIV in the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff (NCM) applies to imports of precious metals such as gold, silver, platinum, palladium, rhodium and associated alloys. It defines the tariff rates and any preferential treatment for these metals when entering the MERCOSUR customs area, using a common external duty schedule for all member states.
[MT.XV] Base Metals -- MT.XV designates the base metals chapter of the MERCOSUR Common External Tariff, covering non-ferrous metals such as copper, aluminum, lead, zinc and their alloys. These products are subject to the uniform customs duties applied to imports from non-member countries across the MERCOSUR bloc.
[MT.XVI] Machinery -- The MERCOSUR Common External Tariff code MT.XVI groups all kinds of machinery, including industrial, agricultural and construction equipment, as well as their parts and accessories. It applies a uniform external duty across member countries for machines used in manufacturing, processing, or power generation, and covers both complete units and sub-assemblies. This category is used to standardize trade tariffs on a broad range of mechanical devices.
[MT.XVII] Vehicles -- The MERCOSUR Common External Tariff code MT.XVII groups all motor vehicles, including cars, trucks, buses and other self-propelled road transport, along with their parts and accessories, for the purpose of customs duties and trade regulation among member countries. This category applies to complete vehicles as well as partially assembled units intended for final assembly.
