[AM] ArchiMate Layers -- The AM entry represents the ArchiMate Layers, which organize the ArchiMate Enterprise Architecture Modeling Language into three primary layers: the Business layer describing organizational processes and goals, the Application layer detailing software applications and their interactions, and the Technology layer covering infrastructure and hardware that support the applications.
[AM.01] Strategy Layer -- The Strategy Layer in ArchiMate defines the high-level direction of an enterprise, modeling its capabilities, resources, outcomes, and courses of action that support strategic intent. It links business goals to the underlying architecture, enabling planners to align initiatives with long-term objectives. This layer provides a coherent view of how strategy translates into actionable business elements.
[AM.02] Business Layer -- The Business Layer in ArchiMate defines the structural and behavioral aspects of an organization's core business, representing entities such as actors, roles, processes, functions, services, and objects that deliver value. It captures how the business operates, the services it provides to customers, and the relationships between internal and external stakeholders, forming the foundation for aligning strategy with operations.
[AM.03] Application Layer -- The Application Layer in ArchiMate defines the software applications, their components, and the services they provide to support business processes and functions. It models the logical and physical aspects of applications, showing how they interact, depend on each other, and fulfill the needs of the Business Layer.
[AM.04] Technology Layer -- The Technology Layer in ArchiMate represents the physical and logical computing infrastructure that underpins an enterprise's applications and services. It captures elements such as hardware, operating systems, networks, and system software, describing how these resources are organized, provisioned, and managed to support higher-level architectural concerns.
[AM.05] Physical Layer -- The Physical Layer in ArchiMate (code AM.05) captures the tangible, hardware-based components of an enterprise's infrastructure, such as servers, devices, network equipment and facilities. It models how these concrete resources are deployed and interconnected, providing a foundation for higher-level logical and application layers in the architecture.
[AM.06] Implementation and Migration Layer -- The Implementation and Migration Layer in ArchiMate models the concrete realization of architecture, covering work packages, deliverables, and the transition from current to target states. It defines the relationships between solutions, their deployment, and the steps required to move the organization toward its future architecture.
[AM.07] Motivation Layer -- The Motivation Layer in ArchiMate defines the why of an enterprise architecture, representing stakeholders, drivers, assessments, goals, principles, requirements and constraints that influence design decisions. It links high-level motivations to specific architectural elements, supporting traceability from strategic intent to implementation.
[AM.08] Active Structure Elements -- Active Structure Elements are the tangible or logical components in an ArchiMate model that possess behavior and can act upon or be acted upon, such as business actors, application components, devices, and system software. They represent the "thing" that performs functions, stores data, or provides services within the architecture.
[AM.09] Behavior Elements -- Behavior elements in ArchiMate represent the dynamic aspects of an architecture, describing the actions, events, processes, and functions that occur within or across components. They capture how elements behave over time, linking to the business, application, and technology layers to model service execution, workflow, and system operations.
[AM.10] Passive Structure Elements -- AM.10 defines passive structure elements in ArchiMate as the static components that hold or process data but do not exhibit behavior on their own, such as data objects, application components, and business objects. These elements model the underlying information or physical resources that support processes and services.
[AM.11] Composite Elements -- Composite elements are reusable groupings that encapsulate related ArchiMate concepts-such as elements, relationships, and views-into a single higher-level construct. They enable modelers to represent complex structures by nesting components while preserving the underlying semantics, facilitating modularity and reuse across enterprise architecture models.
[AM.12] Relationships -- The AM.12 entry defines the set of relationships used in ArchiMate to connect elements within an enterprise architecture model. It groups relationships into structural links such as composition and aggregation, dynamic links like triggering and flow, and dependency links such as access and assignment, establishing how concepts interact and depend on each other.
[AM.13] Viewpoints -- In ArchiMate, viewpoints are predefined perspectives that direct which elements, relationships and other model aspects are selected and presented to address specific stakeholder concerns. Each viewpoint specifies the allowed concepts and their visual conventions, ensuring consistent communication across architecture layers.
