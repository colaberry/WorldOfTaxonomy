[agents] Use of Agents (Art 19) -- Under PSD2 Article 19, payment service providers may appoint agents to act on their behalf in delivering payment services. Agents can be natural or legal persons, must be identified to the provider, and the provider remains liable to the user for the agent's actions, subject to transparency and compliance requirements.
[aisp] Account Information Service Provider (AISP) -- An Account Information Service Provider (AISP) is a payment-service entity defined by the EU's Payment Services Directive 2 (2015/2366) that, with user consent, accesses bank account data to aggregate and present financial information, but does not initiate payments.
[auth] Authorisation of Payment Institutions (Art 5-11) -- The authorisation section of PSD2 establishes the criteria that payment institutions must satisfy to receive a licence from national regulators. Articles 5-11 detail the registration process, required capital, governance standards and ongoing supervisory obligations aimed at safeguarding consumers and maintaining market integrity.
[incident_report] Incident Reporting (Art 96) -- The incident_report entry under the EU Payment Services Directive 2 (Regulation 2015/2366) defines the mandatory reporting of security or operational incidents that affect payment service providers. Article 96 requires swift notification to national authorities and affected users to mitigate risks and ensure market transparency.
[info_req] Information Requirements (Art 38-60) -- The entry outlines the information obligations imposed on payment service providers by the EU's Payment Services Directive 2, covering Articles 38-60. It details the pre-contractual disclosures, ongoing information about transactions, fees and rights to statements that must be provided to users, aiming to ensure transparency and consumer protection in electronic payments.
[liability] Liability for Unauthorised Transactions (Art 73-74) -- The PSD2 establishes that the payment service provider is liable for any unauthorized transaction unless the user acted fraudulently or failed to keep authentication data secure. Under Articles 73-74, liability may shift to the user if they neglected to follow required security procedures, and the provider must reimburse the consumer promptly after verification.
[open_banking] Access to Payment Account (Open Banking) (Art 66-67) -- Under the EU Payment Services Directive 2, Articles 66-67 require payment account providers to grant authorised third-party providers secure access to account data and payment-initiation functions through open-banking interfaces, subject to the customer's explicit consent and regulated technical standards.

[refund] Refund Rights (Art 76-77) -- Under Articles 76-77 of the EU Payment Services Directive 2, payers may demand a refund when a payment is unauthorised, erroneous or not executed as agreed, and the payment service provider must return the funds without undue delay, typically within ten days, unless the provider can demonstrate that the payer was responsible.
[rts_sca] RTS on SCA and Common and Secure Communication -- The RTS on Strong Customer Authentication and Common and Secure Communication sets out the technical requirements that EU payment-service providers must follow under the Payment Services Directive 2. It defines how strong customer authentication is to be applied, the risk-based exemptions allowed, and the standards for secure, interoperable communication between providers, merchants and users.
[sca] Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) (Art 97) -- Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) is a PSD2 requirement that obliges payment service providers to verify a user's identity with at least two independent elements-knowledge, possession or inherence-before authorising electronic transactions. The rule aims to boost security and curb fraud across most online and remote payments.
[scope] Scope and Definitions (Art 1-4) -- The scope article sets out the overall reach of the Payment Services Directive 2, defining which payment services and providers fall under its regulation across the European Economic Area. It also establishes key terms such as "payment service provider," "payment transaction," and "electronic money" to ensure consistent interpretation throughout the directive.
[security] Operational and Security Risk Management (Art 95) -- Under PSD2 (EU) 2015/2366, Article 95 mandates that payment-service providers establish a comprehensive operational and security risk-management framework. This requires identifying, assessing, mitigating and continuously monitoring threats to the integrity, confidentiality and availability of payment systems, and reporting significant risks to competent authorities.
[title_1] Title I - Subject Matter, Scope, Definitions -- Title I of the Payment Services Directive 2 outlines the directive's purpose and legal framework, defines the range of payment services covered, and provides key definitions such as payment service provider, consumer, and electronic money, establishing the scope of the EU-wide regulatory regime.
[title_2] Title II - Payment Service Providers -- Title II of the EU Payment Services Directive 2 sets out the framework for payment service providers, defining the categories of entities such as banks, payment institutions and electronic-money institutions that may offer payment services and establishing the authorization, registration and supervisory requirements they must meet.
[title_3] Title III - Transparency of Conditions and Information Requirements -- Title III of the EU Payment Services Directive 2 sets out rules to ensure that payment service providers give clear, timely and understandable information to users. It mandates disclosure of fees, exchange rates, execution times and any other contractual terms before a transaction is initiated, helping consumers compare services and make informed choices.
[title_4] Title IV - Rights and Obligations in Relation to Provision of Payment Services -- Title IV of the Payment Services Directive 2 establishes the rights of payment-service users and the corresponding obligations of providers, detailing requirements for transparent information, liability for unauthorized transactions, and procedures for dispute resolution. It also governs contract terms, termination conditions and the handling of complaints, ensuring a uniform framework for consumer protection across the EU.
[title_5] Title V - Operational and Security Risks -- The PSD2 Title V defines requirements for managing operational and security risks in payment services, mandating robust risk-assessment procedures, incident-reporting obligations and safeguards for secure communication and data protection across providers. It sets standards for resilience, authentication, and oversight to protect users and maintain market integrity.
