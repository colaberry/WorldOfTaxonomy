[CA] Corporate Action Types -- CA denotes Corporate Action Types in the ISO 15022 standard, grouping events such as dividends, stock splits, mergers and tender offers that affect securities holders. It classifies the nature of the corporate action for consistent processing and reporting across financial institutions.
[CA.01] Cash Dividend -- A cash dividend is a corporate action in which a company distributes a portion of its earnings to shareholders in cash, usually declared on a per-share basis and paid on specified record and payment dates. The distribution reduces retained earnings and cash on the company's balance sheet while providing investors with immediate income.
[CA.02] Stock Dividend -- A stock dividend is a corporate action where a company distributes additional shares to existing shareholders instead of cash, typically on a proportional basis to their current holdings. This increases the number of shares outstanding, often reducing the market price per share while preserving total shareholder equity.
[CA.03] Stock Split -- CA.03 refers to a stock split, a corporate action where a company increases the number of its outstanding shares by dividing each existing share into multiple parts while proportionally reducing the share price, leaving the total market value unchanged. This event typically aims to improve liquidity and make the shares more affordable to investors.
[CA.04] Reverse Stock Split -- CA.04 identifies a reverse stock split, a corporate action in which a company consolidates a specified number of existing shares into a smaller number of new shares, raising the par value per share and reducing the total share count.
[CA.05] Rights Issue -- CA.05 identifies a rights issue, a corporate action in which a company offers its existing shareholders the right to buy additional shares, typically at a discount and in proportion to their current holdings. The aim is to raise capital while giving shareholders priority before new investors can purchase the new shares.
[CA.06] Bonus Issue -- CA.06 - Bonus Issue refers to a corporate action where a company issues additional shares to existing shareholders at no cost, usually on a fixed ratio, using retained earnings or reserves. The extra shares increase the total share count without requiring cash from investors, often to improve liquidity or adjust the market price.
[CA.07] Merger/Acquisition -- CA.07 denotes a corporate action where a company merges with or acquires another entity, resulting in the reallocation-or-conversion of securities for holders.

[CA.09] Tender Offer -- A Tender Offer is a corporate action in which an issuer or a third party invites shareholders to sell all or part of their holdings at a specified price, usually above market, within a defined time frame. It is commonly used in take-overs, restructurings or share-repurchase programmes and may be conditional on reaching a minimum acceptance level.
[CA.10] Share Buyback -- CA.10 designates a share buyback, a corporate action in which a company repurchases its own shares from shareholders, reducing the total number of outstanding shares. This voluntary transaction can be executed on the open market or via a tender offer and may support the share price or improve earnings per share.
[CA.11] Exchange Offer -- An exchange offer is a corporate action where an issuer invites security holders to swap their existing securities for different securities, cash, or other assets, often to restructure debt or change the capital structure. The offer specifies the terms, price and deadline for participation.
[CA.12] Warrant Exercise -- CA.12 denotes a warrant exercise corporate action, where the holder of a warrant elects to convert the instrument into the underlying securities at the predetermined exercise price. The event results in the issuance of new shares or other securities to the warrant holder.
[CA.13] Conversion -- CA.13 Conversion is a corporate action where holders exchange one class of securities for another, such as converting preferred shares into common stock or debt into equity, according to a set ratio and terms. The conversion may be optional for the holder or triggered automatically by a specified event.
[CA.14] Bankruptcy/Liquidation -- CA.14 identifies a corporate action in which the issuer files for bankruptcy or proceeds to liquidation, leading to the termination or restructuring of its securities. This event typically results in the cancellation of outstanding securities and the distribution of any remaining assets to holders according to the legal priority of claims.
[CA.15] Name Change/ISIN Change -- CA.15 signifies a corporate action in which the issuer changes its legal name and, when required, its International Securities Identification Number. The update revises all related documentation and market records so that the security is correctly identified for trading, clearing and settlement.
[CA.16] Coupon Payment (Bond) -- CA.16 denotes a Coupon Payment (Bond), a corporate action where the issuer disburses the scheduled interest amount to bondholders on the coupon date, typically reflecting the bond's nominal rate and payment frequency. This event does not affect principal and is recorded as a cash distribution to investors.
[CA.17] Maturity/Redemption -- CA.17 denotes a maturity or redemption corporate action, occurring when a debt or investment instrument reaches its scheduled end date and the issuer returns principal, with any accrued interest, to the security holders. The event may involve cash payment or the delivery of new securities as specified in the terms.
[CA.18] Proxy Voting -- CA.18 identifies proxy voting, a corporate action where shareholders authorize another party to vote on their behalf at a meeting. It covers the issuance of proxy cards, electronic voting, and related communications, enabling shareholders to influence corporate decisions without personal attendance.
