[conformity] Conformity Assessment -- In the Cyber Resilience Act, conformity assessment refers to the process by which manufacturers demonstrate that their digital products meet essential security requirements before being placed on the EU market, involving testing, documentation, and, where required, third-party certification.
[coordinated_disc] Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 defines coordinated vulnerability disclosure as a structured process where security researchers report discovered software flaws directly to vendors, who then work with them to develop fixes before public release, ensuring a controlled and responsible handling of vulnerabilities throughout the EU market.
[csirt_reporting] Reporting to CSIRT/ENISA -- Under the Cyber Resilience Act, organizations must report cybersecurity incidents to a Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT) and to ENISA. The csirt_reporting entry outlines mandatory reporting procedures, timelines, content requirements and coordination mechanisms to ensure rapid response and EU-wide situational awareness. It aligns with EU regulations to improve overall cyber resilience.
[documentation] Documentation and Instructions (Art 13) -- Article 13 of the EU Cyber Resilience Act requires manufacturers to supply clear user documentation and instructions that describe the product's security features, update procedures, and safe-use guidelines, enabling users to maintain compliance and mitigate risks throughout the device's lifecycle.
[exclusions] Exclusions (medical devices, vehicles, aviation) -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 excludes from its security-by-design obligations certain high-risk sectors, specifically medical devices, vehicles and aviation equipment, which remain regulated under separate, sector-specific frameworks. These exclusions limit the Act's applicability to most other digital products and services.
[market_surv] Market Surveillance -- The market_surv entry under the EU Cyber Resilience Act (2024/2847) outlines the framework for market surveillance, requiring authorities to monitor and enforce compliance of connected products with the Act's security and safety standards, and to take corrective actions when non-conformities are identified. It ensures that products placed on the EU market maintain a baseline level of cyber resilience throughout their lifecycle.
[obligations] Manufacturer Obligations -- Under the Cyber Resilience Act manufacturers of digital products placed on the EU market must embed security by design, carry out risk assessments, supply timely patches and updates, keep detailed technical documentation, and promptly disclose any vulnerabilities. They also must follow conformity-assessment procedures and cooperate with supervisory authorities.
[penalties_cra] Penalties (up to EUR 15 million or 2.5% turnover) -- The Cyber Resilience Act imposes fines of up to €15 million or 2.5 % of a company's annual worldwide turnover for non-compliance with security and safety obligations for digital products sold in the EU. Penalties are applied per infringement and may be combined with other corrective measures.
[product_scope] Products with Digital Elements (Art 2) -- The product_scope entry defines the range of items covered by the Cyber Resilience Act, specifically any product that incorporates digital elements such as hardware, software, or a combination, as set out in Article 2. It includes consumer and industrial goods that rely on embedded or connected digital functionality, ensuring they must meet the Act's cybersecurity requirements.
[reporting_vuln] Actively Exploited Vulnerability Reporting - 24h (Art 14) -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 mandates that providers disclose any vulnerability actively exploited in the wild within 24 hours of awareness, as stipulated in Article 14. The reporting_vuln entry specifies the rapid notification procedure to inform authorities and affected parties, aiming to limit further exploitation and enhance overall cyber safety.
[requirements] Essential Cybersecurity Requirements -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 establishes essential cybersecurity requirements for digital products placed on the EU market. It mandates that manufacturers embed security by design, conduct risk assessments, manage vulnerabilities throughout the product lifecycle, and provide clear user guidance, aiming to raise overall cyber-resilience.
[risk_assess] Cybersecurity Risk Assessment (Art 13) -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 mandates a cybersecurity risk assessment under Article 13, requiring manufacturers and importers of digital products to identify, evaluate and mitigate security risks throughout the product lifecycle. The assessment must be documented, regularly updated, and made available to competent authorities and users to ensure compliance and enhance overall cyber resilience.
[sbom] Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) (Art 13(11)) -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 mandates that manufacturers of connected products supply a Software Bill of Materials, identified as entry code "sbom" under Article 13(11). An SBOM is a standardized, machine-readable inventory of all software components, their versions, dependencies and provenance, enabling transparency, vulnerability tracking and compliance throughout the product lifecycle.
[scope] Scope and Definitions -- The "Scope and Definitions" entry outlines the boundaries of the Cyber Resilience Act, specifying which digital products and services fall under its requirements and establishing key terms such as "digital product," "security," and "vulnerability." It clarifies the obligations for manufacturers, distributors, and importers within the European Union and defines the contextual meaning of concepts essential for compliance.
[security_design] Security by Design (Annex I Part I) -- The entry refers to the "Security by Design" requirement set out in Annex I Part I of the EU Cyber Resilience Act (2024/2847). It mandates that manufacturers embed security measures throughout the product development lifecycle, conduct risk assessments early, and ensure that technical and organisational safeguards are integrated before placing the product on the EU market.
[security_updates] Security Updates for Product Lifetime (Art 13(8)) -- The Cyber Resilience Act requires that manufacturers supply security updates for the entire declared lifetime of a product, ensuring that identified vulnerabilities are remedied and the device remains protected against evolving threats. Article 13(8) sets the obligation to maintain these updates as a core element of product compliance.
[self_assess] Self-Assessment (Default) -- In the Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847, the self_assess entry defines the default self-assessment process that manufacturers must follow to evaluate their products' cybersecurity compliance before market placement. It applies to lower-risk items, requiring a documented risk analysis and a conformity statement rather than external certification.
[third_party] Third-Party Assessment (Critical Products Class I/II) -- The entry defines the mandatory third-party assessment procedure for critical products classified as Class I or II under the EU Cyber Resilience Act (2024/2847). It requires independent verification of security and compliance features before market placement, ensuring that high-risk digital goods meet the regulation's resilience standards.
[vulnerability] Vulnerability Handling -- The Cyber Resilience Act (EU) 2024/2847 includes a Vulnerability Handling entry that sets out mandatory processes for identifying, reporting, and remediating security flaws in covered digital products, requiring manufacturers to monitor, assess risk, and provide timely patches or mitigations to maintain compliance and protect users.
[vuln_handling] Vulnerability Handling Requirements (Annex I Part II) -- The Vulnerability Handling Requirements in Annex I Part II of the EU Cyber Resilience Act mandate that manufacturers of digital products establish clear processes for receiving, assessing, and responding to security vulnerabilities. They must set defined timelines for disclosure, provide timely patches or mitigations, and keep users informed about risks and remediation measures, ensuring ongoing product security throughout its lifecycle.
