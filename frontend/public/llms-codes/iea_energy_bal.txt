[IE] IEA Energy Balance -- IE denotes the IEA Energy Balance, a standardized accounting framework that aggregates a country's primary energy production, conversion processes, consumption by sector, and losses to provide a comprehensive picture of national energy supply and demand. It underpins statistical reporting and policy analysis within the International Energy Agency's energy balance methodology.
[IE.01] Production -- IE.01 Production records the primary generation of energy within a country, encompassing extraction of natural resources, conversion of fuels into electricity or heat, and any other processes that create usable energy before it is distributed or consumed. It serves as the starting point for the IEA Energy Balance, quantifying the total amount of energy made available for subsequent use and transformation.
[IE.02] Imports -- IE.02 denotes the energy imports flow in the IEA Energy Balance, covering all primary energy carriers purchased from other countries, such as oil, natural gas, coal, electricity and renewable fuels. These quantities are recorded before any conversion or transformation processes within the reporting nation.
[IE.03] Exports -- IE.03 - Exports refer to the quantity of primary energy that leaves a country's energy system for use in other nations, measured in physical units such as petajoules or tonnes of oil equivalent. The flow includes all forms of energy transferred abroad by pipeline, ship, or other transport and is subtracted from domestic supply in the energy balance.
[IE.04] International Marine Bunkers -- IE.04 International Marine Bunkers records the fuel used by merchant vessels while operating in international waters, capturing the energy consumption and associated emissions of ships that are not accounted for in any single nation's domestic statistics. It is a distinct flow category within the IEA Energy Balance that isolates offshore maritime fuel use from land-based and domestic marine bunkering.
[IE.05] International Aviation Bunkers -- IE.05 International Aviation Bunkers records the amount of aviation fuel consumed by aircraft on international routes, including take-off, cruise and landing phases. The fuel is attributed to the country supplying it and is treated as a separate energy flow in the IEA balance to capture cross-border aviation demand.
[IE.06] Stock Changes -- IE.06 - Stock Changes records the net variation in energy stocks held between accounting periods, capturing increases such as accumulation of inventories and decreases like withdrawals or consumption. It reflects the difference between opening and closing stock levels for each energy carrier and is used to balance supply and demand in the IEA energy balance.
[IE.07] Total Primary Energy Supply (TPES) -- IE.07 Total Primary Energy Supply (TPES) quantifies the total amount of primary energy entering a country's energy system, comprising domestic production, imports, and net changes in stock while subtracting exports. It is expressed in units such as kilotonnes of oil equivalent or petajoules and forms the basis for calculating overall energy consumption and intensity.
[IE.08] Transfers -- IE.08 - Transfers records energy movements between sectors or processes that occur without conversion, such as electricity passed from transmission to distribution networks or heat exchanged between industrial streams. It captures the physical flow of energy that is relocated to balance supply and demand across the overall system.
[IE.09] Statistical Differences -- Statistical differences are a balancing item used in the IEA energy balance to reconcile mismatches between reported energy supplies and uses, accounting for measurement errors, rounding and data gaps. The figure can be positive or negative, adjusting totals so that supply equals demand within the statistical system.
[IE.10] Electricity Plants -- IE.10 identifies electricity plants within the IEA energy-balance framework, covering all facilities that transform primary energy-such as coal, gas, nuclear, hydro, wind or solar-into electrical power for distribution and consumption. It aggregates generation output across varied plant types and fuel sources.
[IE.11] CHP Plants -- IE.11 refers to combined heat and power (CHP) plants, facilities that simultaneously generate electricity and useful thermal energy from a single fuel source. In the IEA energy-balance framework, CHP is recorded as a combined output stream, linking primary energy inputs to both electricity and heat outputs for allocation and efficiency analysis.
[IE.12] Heat Plants -- IE.12 Heat Plants represent facilities that produce thermal energy for distribution, such as district-heating stations, combined heat and power units, and industrial boilers. They are accounted for as a source of useful heat in the IEA energy balance, tracking the generation, conversion, and supply of heat to end-users.
[IE.13] Industry -- IE.13 Industry represents the energy flows associated with the industrial sector, covering manufacturing, processing and construction activities. It records the fuel inputs and the useful energy outputs such as heat and electricity that are consumed by industry in the IEA energy balance.
[IE.14] Transport -- IE.14 Transport covers all energy flows linked to the transportation sector, recording fuel consumption by road, rail, air and marine modes for both passenger and freight services. It tracks the amount of primary energy supplied, transformed and finally used to move people and goods.
[IE.15] Residential -- IE.15 Residential records the energy consumed by households for space heating, water heating, cooking, appliances, lighting and other domestic services, expressed as final consumption by fuel type and aggregated across the national residential sector. It links to primary energy inputs and accounts for net imports, stock changes and transformation losses within the residential end-use.
[IE.16] Commercial and Public Services -- IE.16 refers to the energy used by the commercial and public services sector, encompassing activities such as retail trade, office work, hospitality, education, health care, cultural institutions and government administration. It aggregates electricity, heat, fuels and other energy carriers consumed in buildings and operations that provide goods, services and public functions.
[IE.17] Agriculture/Forestry -- IE.17 Agriculture/Forestry represents the energy flows associated with farming and forest activities, covering the fuel used by tractors, irrigation pumps, processing equipment, and timber harvesting machinery, as well as the energy consumed in the production of agricultural inputs and forest product processing. It aggregates both direct and indirect energy use in these sectors.
[IE.18] Non-Energy Use -- IE.18 Non-Energy Use records the portion of primary energy that is consumed for purposes other than providing heat, power or mechanical work, such as feedstocks in chemical production, fuel for lubricants, or material processing. It captures energy diverted into industrial processes where the energy's chemical value, rather than its thermal or mechanical output, is the primary function. This category helps separate true energy services from material inputs in national energy balances.
