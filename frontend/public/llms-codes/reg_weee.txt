[best_available] Best Available Treatment Techniques -- The Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) Directive requires that all waste electrical and electronic equipment be managed using the Best Available Treatment Techniques, meaning the most effective, economically feasible methods for recovery, recycling and safe disposal while minimizing environmental impact and protecting human health.

[collection] Collection and Take-Back -- The WEEE Directive requires producers to set up systems for the collection and take-back of waste electrical and electronic equipment, ensuring that end-users can return discarded devices to designated points. These schemes aim to channel e-waste to proper treatment facilities for recycling, recovery and safe disposal.
[collection_rate] Collection Rate Targets (Art 7) -- The collection_rate entry records the targets set by the WEEE Directive for each Member State's annual collection of electrical and electronic waste. Under Article 7 these targets are expressed as a percentage of the average weight of those products placed on the market in the previous three years, establishing mandatory recovery levels for different product groups.
[epr] Extended Producer Responsibility -- The WEEE Directive 2012/19/EU entry "epr" refers to Extended Producer Responsibility, a policy that obliges manufacturers and importers of electrical and electronic equipment to finance and manage the collection, treatment, recycling and safe disposal of their products once they become waste, shifting the cost and responsibility from public authorities to the producers.
[financing] Financing of Collection and Treatment -- The entry defines the financial arrangements required to support the collection, recycling and treatment of waste electrical and electronic equipment under the WEEE Directive, outlining producer responsibilities, fee structures, cost-recovery schemes and funding sources that ensure proper management of e-waste throughout its lifecycle.
[producer] Producer Responsibility -- The "producer" entry refers to any natural or legal person who places a WEEE-covered product on the EU market for the first time, thereby assuming responsibility for financing the collection, treatment and recycling of that equipment under the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive.
[recovery_target] Recovery Targets by Category (Annex V) -- The recovery_target entry in the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive 2012/19/EU sets the mandatory minimum recovery rates for each WEEE category as detailed in Annex V, defining the percentage of material that must be recovered through recycling, reuse or other recovery operations to ensure high resource preservation and limited landfill disposal.
[recycling_target] Recycling Targets by Category (Annex V) -- The recycling_target entry in Annex V of the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive 2012/19/EU sets EU-wide minimum recycling rates for each WEEE category, such as large appliances, IT equipment, lighting, and medical devices. It specifies the required percentages of weight that must be recovered and recycled from collected waste, establishing mandatory performance targets for member states and treatment facilities.
[registration] Producer Registration -- Under the EU Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive 2012/19/EU, producers of electrical and electronic equipment are required to register with the competent national authority. Producer registration creates an official record that enables compliance with collection, treatment and reporting obligations.

[separate_collection] Separate Collection Requirements (Art 5) -- The WEEE Directive mandates that each EU Member State establish systems for the separate collection of waste electrical and electronic equipment, ensuring that manufacturers and distributors provide dedicated collection points or bins distinct from general waste. Art 5 requires these schemes to be easily accessible to consumers, to enable efficient recovery and recycling of WEEE, and to support the tracking of quantities collected for reporting purposes.
[take_back] Producer Take-Back Obligations -- Under the WEEE Directive, producers must arrange and finance the collection of end-of-life electrical and electronic equipment from users, ensuring that it is returned to authorised treatment facilities. They must also report quantities, meet national recovery targets and bear responsibility for the safe disposal or recycling of the products they place on the market.
[targets] Recycling Targets -- The "targets" entry in the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive 2012/19/EU specifies the mandatory recycling percentages that EU member states must achieve for collected electronic waste. It sets progressive goals for different product categories, requiring a minimum share of material to be recovered and reused rather than disposed of.
[treatment] Treatment and Recovery -- The "treatment" entry under the Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive 2012/19/EU covers the steps taken to dismantle, process and recover valuable materials from discarded electrical and electronic devices, ensuring hazardous substances are safely managed and reusable components are reclaimed for recycling. It defines the technical and environmental requirements that operators must meet to achieve environmentally sound recovery of WEEE.
[treatment_req] Treatment Requirements (Art 8) -- The Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive (2012/19/EU) mandates that member states establish systems for the collection, treatment and environmentally sound recovery of WEEE. Article 8 sets specific treatment requirements, obliging producers and recyclers to meet minimum recycling and recovery targets, ensure safe dismantling, and prevent hazardous substances from entering the waste stream.
