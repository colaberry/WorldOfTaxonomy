[auth] Authorisation of Investment Firms -- The "auth" entry outlines the regulatory framework for granting authorisation to investment firms under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR. It details the eligibility criteria, capital and governance requirements, and supervisory procedures that firms must satisfy before being permitted to provide investment services and carry out regulated activities across the EU.
[best_exec] Best Execution (Art 27) -- Best execution under Article 27 of MiFID II requires investment firms to take all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result for their clients when executing orders, considering price, costs, speed, likelihood of execution and settlement, and any other relevant factors. Firms must implement policies, monitor performance, and regularly review execution arrangements to demonstrably meet this duty.
[conduct] Conduct of Business Obligations -- The conduct entry under MiFID II and MiFIR sets out the obligations firms must follow when providing investment services, covering client communication, suitability assessment, best execution and the management of conflicts of interest.
[consolidated_tape] Consolidated Tape Provider -- A Consolidated Tape Provider is an entity designated under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive (MiFID II) and MiFIR to collect, consolidate and disseminate real-time trade data for securities across multiple trading venues, ensuring transparent and equal access to market information for all participants.
[esma] ESMA Powers -- ESMA Powers refers to the authority granted to the European Securities and Markets Authority under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and the accompanying MiFIR regulation, enabling it to develop technical standards, supervise market participants, and enforce harmonised rules across the EU's financial markets.
[inducements] Inducements and Costs Disclosure -- Inducements and Costs Disclosure requires investment firms to inform clients about any remuneration, fees, or non-monetary benefits they receive or pay that could influence the provision of investment services, and to clearly separate those costs from the firm's own income. The aim is to ensure transparent reporting of all charges and potential conflicts of interest so clients can assess the true cost of the services provided.
[mtf] Multilateral Trading Facilities (MTFs) -- Multilateral Trading Facilities (MTFs) are regulated trading venues that bring together multiple third-party buyers and sellers of financial instruments, operating under the EU's MiFID II and MiFIR framework. They provide transparent, non-discriminatory access to markets, competing with regulated exchanges and offering alternative liquidity sources.
[ncas] National Competent Authorities -- The entry identifies the national competent authorities that oversee the implementation and enforcement of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and the accompanying MiFIR regulation within each EU member state. These bodies are responsible for licensing, market supervision and ensuring compliance with investor-protection and transparency rules.
[otf] Organised Trading Facilities (OTFs) -- Organised Trading Facilities are regulated venues that enable multilateral trading of non-equity financial instruments, operated by investment firms under the MiFIR framework. They must meet transparency, reporting and governance requirements, and are distinct from traditional regulated markets and Multilateral Trading Facilities.

[product_gov] Product Governance Requirements -- Product governance requirements under MiFID II/MiFIR set out the duties of firms when designing, approving, distributing and reviewing金融 products, ensuring they are targeted at appropriate client categories and that risks, costs and features are clearly disclosed. The rules aim to protect investors by preventing the sale of unsuitable or mis-sold products.
[recording] Recording of Telephone Conversations and Electronic Communications -- Under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR, firms are required to record telephone conversations and electronic communications that relate to client orders, advice or execution. The recordings must be retained for a minimum period, securely stored and made available for supervisory review and audit.
[rm_access] Access to Regulated Markets -- The rm_access entry defines the conditions under which firms may obtain permission to trade on regulated markets covered by the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and the accompanying MiFIR regulation. It outlines eligibility criteria, licensing requirements and supervisory obligations that ensure participants meet transparency, investor protection and market integrity standards.
[rm_auth] Authorisation of Regulated Markets -- The rm_auth entry covers the requirement that a market operator obtain official authorisation to function as a regulated market under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR. This authorisation ensures the venue complies with the EU rules on transparency, market integrity, reporting and investor protection before it can admit and trade financial instruments.
[sanctions] Administrative Sanctions -- Administrative sanctions under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive (MiFID II) and MiFIR are regulatory penalties imposed by national competent authorities for breaches of the EU financial markets regime. They may include fines, restrictions on business activities, licence suspensions or revocations, and other corrective measures designed to enforce compliance and protect market integrity. The sanctions aim to deter misconduct, ensure fair competition, and safeguard investor protection across the European Union.
[si] Systematic Internalisers -- Systematic internalisers are investment firms that, on a regular basis, execute client orders for their own account outside a regulated market or MTF, providing liquidity by quoting bid and offer prices for financial instruments. They must meet transparency, reporting and best-execution obligations under MiFID II and MiFIR.

[title_1] Title I - Scope and Definitions -- Title I outlines the overall scope of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive and MiFIR, specifying which firms, markets and financial instruments fall under the regime, and provides essential definitions for terms such as investment firm, regulated market and financial instrument to ensure consistent application of the rules.
[title_2] Title II - Authorisation and Operating Conditions -- Title II sets out the licensing and ongoing requirements for firms providing investment services under the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive and MiFIR, specifying the capital, organisational, governance and conduct standards needed to operate in EU markets and detailing the procedures for authorising participants and the supervisory conditions they must meet.
[title_3] Title III - Regulated Markets -- Title III of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR establishes the framework for regulated markets, defining the requirements for admission of instruments, the operation of trading venues, transparency obligations and supervisory oversight to ensure fair, orderly and efficient trading in the EU.
[title_4] Title IV - Trading Venues -- Title IV of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR establishes the regulatory framework for trading venues, including regulated markets, multilateral trading facilities and organised trading facilities, outlining requirements for transparency, access and supervisory oversight to promote fair, orderly and efficient markets.
[title_5] Title V - Market Data and Transparency -- Title V of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and the accompanying MiFIR sets out the rules governing market data and transparency, requiring firms to publish pre- and post-trade information to ensure fair and efficient pricing, and establishing standards for data collection, consolidation and reporting across EU trading venues.
[title_6] Title VI - Supervision -- Title VI of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive 2014/65/EU and MiFIR sets out the supervisory framework for financial markets, defining the powers and responsibilities of national competent authorities and the European Securities and Markets Authority. It covers coordination of supervision, powers to intervene, and requirements for reporting and enforcement to ensure market integrity and investor protection.
