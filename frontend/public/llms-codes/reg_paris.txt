[13_1] Biennial Transparency Reports -- Biennial Transparency Reports are periodic submissions by Parties to the UNFCCC that detail their greenhouse-gas emissions, mitigation actions and support provided or received, enabling collective assessment of progress under the Paris Agreement.
[13_2] Technical Expert Review -- The Technical Expert Review under the Paris Agreement (UNFCCC) is an independent assessment triggered when a Party's submission is deemed incomplete or inconsistent, ensuring that mitigation and adaptation plans meet transparency and ambition requirements. It provides expert findings and recommendations that feed into compliance and further negotiation processes.
[13_3] National Inventory Reports -- National Inventory Reports are periodic submissions by Parties to the UNFCCC that detail their greenhouse-gas emissions and removals using standardized methodology. These reports provide the quantitative basis for tracking each country's progress toward its Paris Agreement mitigation commitments and for informing global stocktakes.

[6_4] Art 6.4 - Sustainable Development Mechanism -- Article 6.4 establishes the Sustainable Development Mechanism, a UN-facilitated carbon market that enables participating Parties to generate and transfer emission reductions while promoting sustainable development. It provides a framework for internationally transferred mitigation outcomes that can be used to meet nationally determined contributions and can be transferred between Parties.
[art_10] Article 10 - Technology Development and Transfer -- Article 10 requires Parties to promote the development and transfer of environmentally sound technologies, supporting capacity-building and facilitating access to finance for climate mitigation and adaptation. It calls for cooperation among public and private actors and the establishment of mechanisms to track, share and disseminate technology solutions.
[art_13] Article 13 - Enhanced Transparency Framework -- Article 13 creates an enhanced transparency framework that requires all Parties to regularly submit national inventory reports and implementation reports, and to undergo technical expert reviews and multilateral assessments. The system aims to build trust, ensure accountability, and facilitate the global stocktake by providing comparable, consistent and transparent information on emissions, mitigation actions and support.
[art_14] Article 14 - Global Stocktake -- Article 14 sets out the Global Stocktake, a periodic five-year assessment of the Parties' collective progress toward the long-term temperature goals of the Paris Agreement. It reviews emissions reductions, adaptation efforts and support provisions, and informs the updating of nationally determined contributions and related actions.
[art_2] Article 2 - Long-Term Goals -- Article 2 establishes the overarching long-term climate objectives of the Paris Agreement, aiming to keep the increase in global average temperature well below 2 °C above pre-industrial levels and to pursue efforts to limit it to 1.5 °C. It also calls for enhancing the ability of Parties to adapt to climate impacts, fostering climate-resilient development, and making finance flows compatible with a low-carbon, sustainable pathway.
[art_3] Article 3 - Nationally Determined Contributions -- Article 3 of the Paris Agreement outlines the requirement for each Party to prepare, communicate and maintain nationally determined contributions, reflecting its highest possible ambition and its common but differentiated responsibilities. These contributions are to be updated every five years to reflect progress and evolving capabilities.
[art_4] Article 4 - Mitigation -- Article 4 of the Paris Agreement requires each Party to develop, submit and regularly update a nationally determined contribution that sets out its plans to reduce greenhouse-gas emissions, and to implement domestic mitigation actions aimed at limiting global temperature rise in line with the agreement's long-term goal.
[art_5] Article 5 - REDD+ (Forests) -- Article 5 of the Paris Agreement establishes the framework for REDD+, a mechanism that supports developing countries in reducing emissions from deforestation and forest degradation, conserving forest carbon stocks, and enhancing forest management. It encourages the participation of parties in results-based finance, capacity-building, and knowledge-sharing to promote forest-related climate mitigation and sustainable development.
[art_6] Article 6 - Cooperative Approaches (Carbon Markets) -- Article 6 of the Paris Agreement establishes a framework for cooperative approaches that allow Parties to pursue voluntary collaboration through internationally transferred mitigation outcomes and a dedicated mechanism to ensure environmental integrity, enabling the use of carbon markets to meet nationally determined contributions.
[art_7] Article 7 - Adaptation -- Article 7 of the Paris Agreement obliges Parties to strengthen their ability to adapt to adverse climate impacts, enhance resilience and reduce vulnerability, and to integrate adaptation considerations into national planning, development strategies and poverty eradication efforts, while fostering cooperation and support for developing countries.
[art_8] Article 8 - Loss and Damage -- Article 8 of the Paris Agreement acknowledges that climate-related loss and damage can occur, especially in vulnerable developing nations, and calls for cooperative approaches to address irreversible harms. It underscores the need for effective support, financing and knowledge sharing to help affected countries cope with the impacts that cannot be avoided or adapted to.
[art_9] Article 9 - Finance -- Article 9 sets out the financial framework for the Paris Agreement, calling on developed countries to provide consistent, predictable, and adequate climate finance to support mitigation and adaptation actions in developing nations. It establishes the Green Climate Fund, outlines reporting requirements, and encourages private sector mobilization to meet the collective goal of limiting global temperature rise.
[ndc] NDC Components -- The ndc entry in the Paris Agreement classification identifies the key elements of a country's Nationally Determined Contribution, including mitigation targets, adaptation measures, means of implementation and support, and transparency frameworks. These components form the basis for reporting progress under the UNFCCC.
