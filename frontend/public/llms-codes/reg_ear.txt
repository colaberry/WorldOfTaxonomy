[ccl_0] Category 0 - Nuclear Materials, Facilities, and Equipment -- Category 0 includes nuclear-related items that are specially designed for the production, processing, use or handling of nuclear material; it covers reactors, enrichment and reprocessing equipment, related instrumentation, and associated facilities such as nuclear power plants and test reactors. These items are subject to the strictest export controls because of their potential applications in weapons-grade nuclear programs.
[ccl_1] Category 1 - Special Materials and Related Equipment -- Category 1 under the Export Administration Regulations covers special materials and related equipment, such as certain chemicals, biological agents, and specialized manufacturing tools, that are controlled due to their potential dual-use applications. Export of these items requires a license from the U.S. Department of Commerce unless an exception applies, and they are listed in the Commerce Control List with specific ECCNs. This category aims to prevent the proliferation of technologies that could threaten national security.
[ccl_2] Category 2 - Materials Processing -- Category 2 under the Export Administration Regulations governs materials-processing items, including equipment, software and technical data used to transform raw substances into intermediate or finished products. It sets licensing rules for the export, reexport and transfer of these items based on destination, end-use and end-user.
[ccl_3] Category 3 - Electronics -- Category 3 under the Export Administration Regulations (15 CFR Parts 730-774) controls electronic equipment, components and related technology, including items such as semiconductors, computers, telecommunications and consumer electronics. Items in this category may require an export license depending on the destination, end-use and end-user, and are subject to the EAR's licensing thresholds and prohibitions.
[ccl_4] Category 4 - Computers -- Category 4 under the Export Administration Regulations controls general-purpose digital computers, their components, and related equipment, including mainframes, minicomputers, microcomputers, and associated peripheral devices. It also covers software designed for these systems and any technology needed for their design, development, production, or use. This category ensures that advanced computing hardware and software are monitored for national security and foreign policy reasons.
[ccl_5] Category 5 - Telecommunications and Information Security -- Category 5 of the Export Administration Regulations covers the export, re-export, and transfer of commodities, software, and technology that are essential for telecommunications and information security. It includes equipment, components, and technical data for network infrastructure, encryption, and secure communications, as well as related services and support. Controls aim to protect national security and foreign policy interests by limiting access to advanced telecom and cyber-security capabilities.
[ccl_6] Category 6 - Sensors and Lasers -- Category 6 of the Export Administration Regulations governs dual-use sensors, lasers and related equipment, including infrared and optical detectors, rangefinders, LIDAR systems, laser assemblies, and components used for measurement, targeting, or imaging. Controls are based on performance parameters such as wavelength, power output and resolution, reflecting the potential for military or strategic use. Export requires a license unless an exemption applies.
[ccl_7] Category 7 - Navigation and Avionics -- Under the Export Administration Regulations, Category 7 covers navigation and avionics equipment and related technology. It includes devices such as inertial navigation systems, flight-control computers, radar and communication equipment used on aircraft or spacecraft, along with the associated technical data and software.
[ccl_8] Category 8 - Marine -- Category 8 under the Export Administration Regulations includes marine equipment, vessels and related technology, such as propulsion systems, navigation and communication gear, hull components, and submersible hardware. It also covers associated software, technical data and certain marine-related weapons, applying to both commercial and military maritime applications.
[ccl_9] Category 9 - Aerospace and Propulsion -- Category 9 under the Export Administration Regulations covers items related to aerospace and propulsion, including aircraft, missiles, space vehicles, engines and related components. It controls the export of technology and equipment that could be used in military or dual-use aerospace applications and requires licensing for certain destinations and end-uses.
[part_730] Part 730 - General Information -- Part 730 supplies the introductory framework for the Export Administration Regulations, outlining the scope of the rules, key definitions, and the basic responsibilities of exporters and other regulated parties in handling dual-use items and related technology.
[part_732] Part 732 - Steps for Using the EAR -- Part 732 details the procedural steps for applying the Export Administration Regulations, covering how to determine jurisdiction, assess licensing requirements and follow compliance measures when exporting items subject to the EAR.
[part_734] Part 734 - Scope of the EAR -- Part 734 sets out the overall reach of the Export Administration Regulations, describing which items, technologies, activities and parties are subject to the rules and clarifying the purpose and limits of the EAR.
[part_736] Part 736 - General Prohibitions -- Part 736 of the Export Administration Regulations sets out the general prohibitions that apply to the export, reexport, transfer, or temporary import of items subject to the EAR. It defines activities that are prohibited without a license, such as providing controlled items to prohibited end-users, destinations, or for prohibited end-uses, and specifies the circumstances that trigger the need for an authorization. This section forms the baseline enforcement framework for all EAR-controlled commerce.
[part_738] Part 738 - Commerce Control List Overview -- Part 738 gives an overview of the Commerce Control List, the regulatory inventory of items subject to U.S. export controls under the EAR. It explains how the list is organized by categories and control reasons, helping exporters identify licensing requirements for specific products.
[part_740] Part 740 - License Exceptions -- Part 740 of the Export Administration Regulations sets out the license-exception provisions that allow exporters to ship items subject to the EAR without obtaining a specific license, provided they meet defined criteria and record-keeping requirements. It lists categories such as L1, L2, and GOV, describing the permissible uses, destination limits, and compliance obligations for each exception.

[part_744] Part 744 - Control Policy: End-User and End-Use Based -- Part 744 of the Export Administration Regulations establishes a control policy that restricts exports based on end-user and end-use criteria, identifying prohibited parties such as military end-users, terrorism-related entities, and certain high-risk activities. It authorizes the Department of Commerce to deny or restrict licenses when items may be used in weapons of mass destruction, missile programs, or other nationally sensitive purposes.
[part_746] Part 746 - Embargoes and Other Special Controls -- Part 746 of the Export Administration Regulations defines U.S. embargoes and other special controls that restrict the export, reexport, and transfer of items to designated countries, entities, or individuals. It outlines the legal basis for prohibitions, licensing requirements and administration of sanctions, specifying restricted destinations and compliance obligations for regulated parties.
[part_748] Part 748 - Applications, Classification, Advisory Opinions -- Part 748 of the Export Administration Regulations sets out the procedures for submitting license applications, how to determine the EAR classification of items, and the process for requesting advisory opinions from the Bureau of Industry and Security.
[part_750] Part 750 - Application Processing, Issuance, and Denial -- Part 750 outlines the procedures the Bureau of Industry and Security follows to process export-license applications, including required documentation, review timelines, and criteria for approval or denial. It also details the authority for issuing, modifying, suspending or revoking licenses and the record-keeping obligations of applicants and officials.
[part_754] Part 754 - Short Supply Controls -- Part 754 of the Export Administration Regulations establishes controls on the export, reexport, and transfer of items that are in short supply in the United States, requiring licenses and reporting to prevent shortages. It defines short-supply items, licensing criteria, and enforcement provisions.
[part_758] Part 758 - Export Clearance Requirements -- Part 758 of the Export Administration Regulations establishes the procedures exporters must follow to obtain clearance from the Bureau of Industry and Security. It details licensing requirements, filing processes and the documentation needed to ensure compliance with U.S. export control laws.
[part_760] Part 760 - Restrictive Trade Practices or Boycotts -- Part 760 of the Export Administration Regulations sets U.S. policy on restrictive trade practices and boycotts, prohibiting U.S. persons from supporting foreign boycotts that are not U.S.-sanctioned. It defines prohibited actions, reporting obligations and penalties, and offers compliance guidance for exporters and related parties.
[part_762] Part 762 - Recordkeeping -- Part 762 of the Export Administration Regulations establishes the recordkeeping requirements for parties subject to the EAR. It obligates exporters, manufacturers, and other licensees to retain transaction, licensing and related documents for at least five years and to make those records available to the Bureau of Industry and Security upon request
[part_764] Part 764 - Enforcement and Protective Measures -- Part 764 of the Export Administration Regulations sets out the enforcement framework for the EAR, detailing how violations are investigated and penalties imposed, and describing protective measures such as licensing suspensions, civil fines, and criminal penalties. It also provides guidance on administrative and judicial remedies, record-keeping requirements, and the role of the Department of Commerce in ensuring compliance with export control laws.
[part_766] Part 766 - Administrative Enforcement Proceedings -- Part 766 of the Export Administration Regulations sets out the procedures for administrative enforcement actions, detailing how investigations, hearings, penalties and appeals are conducted when violations of the EAR are alleged. It provides the framework for the Department of Commerce to enforce compliance and impose civil penalties.
[part_768] Part 768 - Foreign Availability Determination -- Part 768 of the Export Administration Regulations establishes the criteria and procedures for making a foreign-availability determination, which assesses whether an item controlled under the EAR is obtainable outside the United States. The determination influences licensing requirements by identifying items that may be exempt from restrictions if they are already accessible abroad.

[part_772] Part 772 - Definitions of Terms -- Part 772 of the Export Administration Regulations contains the official definitions of key terms used throughout the EAR, establishing the precise meaning of concepts such as "technology," "dual-use," and "controlled item."
