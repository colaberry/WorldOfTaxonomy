[EML] EML Sections -- The WHO Essential Medicines List (EML) is organized into distinct sections that group medicines by therapeutic class, disease focus or clinical use, facilitating selection of essential drugs for national formularies. Each EML section lists specific medicines, dosage forms, and strength recommendations relevant to that category, supporting evidence-based procurement and prescribing. The sections serve as a framework for health systems to assess and prioritize essential medicines.
[EML.01] Anaesthetics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.01 groups anaesthetic agents needed for safe surgery, obstetric care and painful procedures. It includes inhalational, intravenous and local-acting drugs that provide rapid, controllable loss of sensation and consciousness, and are selected for their proven efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness in diverse health systems.
[EML.02] Analgesics and antipyretics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.02, Analgesics and antipyretics, comprises medicines that relieve pain and lower fever, including agents such as paracetamol, ibuprofen and aspirin, and are deemed essential for inclusion in basic health-care services worldwide.
[EML.03] Antiallergics and anaphylaxis -- EML.03 identifies a set of essential medicines used to treat allergic reactions and prevent anaphylaxis, such as antihistamines and epinephrine auto-injectors. These drugs are recommended for inclusion in national formularies to manage acute allergic episodes and chronic allergic conditions.
[EML.04] Antidotes and substances used in poisoning -- EML.04 groups essential antidotes and other agents that neutralize or mitigate the effects of poisons, providing life-saving treatment in acute poisoning emergencies. The list includes specific antivenoms, chelating agents, enzyme reactivators and supportive substances needed for rapid reversal of toxic exposures.
[EML.05] Anticonvulsants/antiepileptics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.05 designates anticonvulsants and antiepileptic drugs as essential medicines for the treatment and prevention of seizures and epilepsy. These medicines are selected for their efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness and are recommended for inclusion in national formularies and health systems worldwide.
[EML.06] Anti-infective medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.06 groups anti-infective medicines, which are drugs used to prevent or treat infections caused by bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. These include antibiotics, antivirals, antifungals and antiparasitic agents that are considered essential for meeting the most important health needs of populations.

[EML.08] Immunomodulators and antineoplastics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.08 groups drugs that modulate the immune system and treat malignant diseases, covering agents such as cytokine inhibitors, monoclonal antibodies, and chemotherapy compounds. These medicines are deemed vital for basic health-care systems to manage autoimmune disorders, transplant rejection, and various cancers.
[EML.09] Antiparkinsonism medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.09 groups antiparkinsonism medicines, core drugs needed to manage Parkinson's disease and related movement disorders. It includes levodopa and carbidopa combinations, dopamine agonists, MAO-B inhibitors and other agents that improve motor symptoms and quality of life.
[EML.10] Medicines affecting the blood -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.10 groups medicines that act on the circulatory system to treat blood-related conditions, including anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, hematopoietic growth factors, iron and vitamin supplements, and drugs for anemia, clotting disorders and sickle-cell disease. These essential drugs are selected for their proven efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness in managing disorders of blood formation, function and clotting.
[EML.11] Blood products and plasma substitutes -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.11 covers blood products and plasma substitutes, including whole blood, red blood cells, platelets, fresh frozen plasma and albumin solutions used to restore volume, correct deficiencies and treat bleeding disorders. These items are deemed essential for effective health systems because they address life-threatening conditions such as severe anemia, trauma, surgery and coagulation disorders.
[EML.12] Cardiovascular medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.12 groups medicines that are essential for preventing and treating cardiovascular diseases, covering drugs for hypertension, heart failure, and ischemic heart disease. These medicines are chosen for proven efficacy, safety, and cost-effectiveness to address the global burden of heart-related conditions.
[EML.13] Dermatological medicines -- The World Health Organization's Essential Medicines List entry EML.13 covers essential dermatological medicines, encompassing drugs used to treat common skin disorders such as infections, inflammation, psoriasis and acne, and includes topical antibiotics, antifungals, corticosteroids and specific systemic agents deemed necessary for basic health systems.
[EML.14] Diagnostic agents -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.14, titled Diagnostic agents, includes medicines and substances essential for clinical and laboratory diagnosis such as contrast media, reagents and agents used in imaging and testing procedures, supporting accurate disease detection, monitoring and treatment decisions worldwide.
[EML.15] Disinfectants and antiseptics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.15 covers disinfectants and antiseptics, which are chemical agents used to destroy or inhibit pathogenic microorganisms on inanimate surfaces, skin, and mucous membranes. These products are considered essential for preventing infections in health-care settings and for community hygiene, and the list specifies which agents meet safety, efficacy, and cost criteria for inclusion.
[EML.16] Diuretics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.16 designates the therapeutic class of diuretics, drugs that increase urine output to manage fluid retention, hypertension, and certain kidney or heart conditions. It includes thiazide, loop and potassium-sparing agents that are considered indispensable for basic health-care systems worldwide.
[EML.17] Gastrointestinal medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.17 designates a core set of medicines for managing gastrointestinal conditions, including agents for acid-related disorders, anti-emetics, antidiarrheals, laxatives and drugs for inflammatory bowel disease and gastro-intestinal infections. These medicines are considered vital for basic health-care systems worldwide.
[EML.18] Hormones and other endocrine -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.18 groups all essential hormonal and endocrine agents needed for basic health care, including insulin for diabetes, thyroid hormones, contraceptive steroids, glucocorticoids, mineralocorticoids, and other pituitary or adrenal hormone preparations. These medicines are selected for their proven efficacy, safety, and cost-effectiveness in treating endocrine disorders worldwide.
[EML.19] Immunologicals -- EML.19 on the WHO Essential Medicines List groups immunological medicines such as vaccines, immunoglobulins, antitoxins and other biologics that modify or enhance the immune response. These products are considered essential for preventing and treating infectious diseases and certain immune disorders worldwide.
[EML.20] Muscle relaxants -- The WHO Essential Medicines List designates muscle relaxants as core drugs needed to treat spasticity, muscle pain and involuntary contractions in a range of conditions such as multiple sclerosis, cerebral palsy, severe musculoskeletal injuries and postoperative recovery. The entry typically includes agents like baclofen, tizanidine, dantrolene and other centrally-acting or direct-acting relaxants that are considered essential for basic health systems.
[EML.21] Ophthalmological preparations -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.21 groups together essential ophthalmological preparations such as antibiotic, anti-inflammatory, anti-glaucoma and mydriatic eye drops, ointments and solutions that are needed for the prevention and treatment of common eye conditions worldwide. It guides procurement and prescribing to ensure basic eye care is available in all health systems.
[EML.22] Oxytocics and antioxytocics -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.22 groups drugs that either stimulate or inhibit uterine activity. Oxytocics such as oxytocin and misoprostol are used to induce labor, augment contractions and treat postpartum hemorrhage, while antioxytocics like magnesium sulfate and nifedipine are employed to delay premature labor or manage uterine hyper-stimulation. This category ensures access to essential medicines for safe obstetric care.
[EML.23] Peritoneal dialysis solution -- Peritoneal dialysis solution is a sterile, glucose-based fluid used to remove waste products and excess water from patients with renal failure by exchanging fluid through the peritoneal cavity. Listed on the WHO Essential Medicines List as a core product for basic renal replacement therapy, it is recommended for use in both high-resource and low-resource health settings.
[EML.24] Psychotherapeutic medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.24 designates a core group of psychotherapeutic medicines that are considered indispensable for treating common mental-health disorders such as depression, anxiety, schizophrenia and bipolar disorder. It includes antidepressants, antipsychotics, anxiolytics, mood stabilisers and related agents that have proven efficacy, safety and cost-effectiveness for inclusion in national essential-medicine programmes.
[EML.25] Respiratory medicines -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.25 groups medicines used to treat respiratory conditions such as asthma, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and other airway diseases. It includes bronchodilators, inhaled corticosteroids, combination inhalers and other agents essential for managing acute and chronic respiratory disorders.
[EML.26] Vitamins and minerals -- The WHO Essential Medicines List entry EML.26 groups together essential vitamin and mineral preparations used to prevent or treat micronutrient deficiencies. It includes supplements such as vitamin A, iron, folic acid, calcium and multivitamin formulations that are deemed critical for public health, especially in vulnerable populations.
