[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Clause 10.1 of ISO 22301:2019 specifies how an organization must identify and document any nonconformities in its business continuity management system, determine their causes, and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence. It also requires verification of the actions' effectiveness and the maintenance of appropriate records.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 22301:2019 mandates that an organization continuously improve its Business Continuity Management System by monitoring performance, identifying gaps, and applying corrective actions to enhance resilience and overall effectiveness over time.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 requires the organization to identify and analyze internal and external factors that affect its ability to achieve business continuity objectives. This includes assessing the organization's purpose, strategic direction, legal and regulatory environment, market conditions, and stakeholder expectations. The analysis provides the basis for defining the scope and requirements of the Business Continuity Management System.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 of ISO 22301:2019 directs an organization to identify all relevant interested parties-such as customers, suppliers, regulators, and employees-and to determine their needs, expectations, and any applicable legal or regulatory requirements that affect the business continuity management system, ensuring this information is documented, communicated, and regularly reviewed to guide the BCMS's scope and objectives.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the BCMS -- It outlines how organizations define the boundaries and applicability of their Business Continuity Management System, considering internal and external issues, products, services, and locations. The scope must be documented, aligned with the organization's context, and reviewed for relevance and compliance.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment by establishing, supporting, and continuously improving the business continuity management system, ensuring its integration with organizational processes, providing necessary resources, and communicating its importance throughout the organization.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- ISO 22301:2019 clause 5.2 requires top management to define and document a Business Continuity Management System policy that reflects the organization's strategic direction, commitment to resilience and compliance obligations. The policy must establish overall objectives, assign responsibilities and be communicated to relevant stakeholders to guide the implementation and continual improvement of the BCMS.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- The entry outlines the need to define and document the specific roles, responsibilities and authority levels required for an effective Business Continuity Management System. It requires top management to assign clear accountability, ensure competence, and communicate these duties throughout the organization so that each function knows its duties during normal operations and disruptions.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 requires the organization to identify risks and opportunities affecting its business continuity objectives, determine appropriate actions to treat those risks and exploit the opportunities, and integrate these actions into its Business Continuity Management System, with documented evidence of planning, implementation, and review.
[6_2] 6.2 Business continuity objectives -- The entry requires an organization to establish, document and maintain business continuity objectives that align with its BCMS policy, are measurable, appropriate to its context and reviewed regularly. These objectives must address the needs of interested parties, support continuity planning and be communicated throughout the organization.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 requires the organization to establish, implement and maintain processes for planning and controlling its business continuity activities, ensuring that objectives, resources, responsibilities and timelines are defined and that actions are monitored and adjusted as needed.
[8_2] 8.2 Business impact analysis and risk assessment -- Clause 8.2 of ISO 22301:2019 requires organizations to conduct a business impact analysis and risk assessment to identify critical activities, determine recovery priorities, and evaluate threats and vulnerabilities that could affect continuity, forming the basis for continuity objectives and strategies.
[8_3] 8.3 Business continuity strategies and solutions -- The entry outlines how organizations develop and select business continuity strategies and solutions to protect critical activities during disruptions, detailing the analysis of threats, recovery options, resource requirements and the integration of these strategies into the overall Business Continuity Management System.
[8_4] 8.4 Business continuity plans and procedures -- Clause 8.4 of ISO 22301:2019 requires organizations to create, implement and maintain documented business continuity plans and procedures that define response actions, responsibilities, resources and communication processes for handling disruptions, and to regularly test and update them to ensure they meet recovery objectives and remain effective.
[8_5] 8.5 Exercise programme -- The exercise programme defines how an organisation plans, conducts and records tests of its business continuity arrangements, specifying objectives, scope, frequency, methods and responsible parties to validate effectiveness and identify improvements. It ensures regular simulation of disruptions, from tabletop scenarios to full-scale drills, and requires documented results, corrective actions and review to maintain a resilient continuity management system.
[8_6] 8.6 Evaluation of documentation and capabilities -- Entry 8.6 requires organizations to assess the completeness, accuracy and suitability of their business continuity documentation and to verify that required capabilities, such as resources, skills and processes, are in place and effective for meeting the BCMS objectives. This evaluation ensures that documented procedures are current, that gaps are identified, and that corrective actions are taken to maintain resilience.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- In ISO 22301:2019 the clause 9.1 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation of their Business Continuity Management System. It calls for defining what is to be measured, how data are collected and analyzed, and how results are reviewed to determine effectiveness, identify opportunities for improvement and ensure compliance with the standard.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Entry 9.2 requires the organization to conduct regular internal audits of its business continuity management system to verify compliance with ISO 22301, assess the effectiveness of processes, and identify areas for improvement. Audits must be planned, documented, and results communicated to relevant personnel, with corrective actions taken to address any non-conformities found. The audit program should cover all elements of the BCMS and be reviewed periodically for adequacy.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Entry 9.3 requires top management to conduct periodic reviews of the Business Continuity Management System to confirm its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must consider performance metrics, audit findings, incident reports, corrective actions and changes in internal or external context, and result in decisions on improvements, resource needs and any necessary modifications to the BCMS.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 22301:2019 addresses improvement of the Business Continuity Management System, requiring organizations to monitor performance, identify non-conformities and take corrective action, and to pursue continual enhancement through lessons learned, internal audits and management reviews.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 22301:2019 directs organizations to define the context in which their Business Continuity Management System operates, identifying relevant internal and external issues, interested parties and their requirements, and establishing the scope of the system. This foundational analysis informs the planning, implementation and continual improvement of business continuity measures.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 of ISO 22301:2019 outlines the responsibilities of top management in a Business Continuity Management System, requiring leadership to demonstrate commitment, establish a continuity policy, assign roles, and ensure integration of the system into organizational processes. It emphasizes strategic direction, resource allocation, and continual improvement driven by senior oversight.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 22301:2019 outlines the planning requirements for a Business Continuity Management System, describing how organizations must identify risks, set continuity objectives, and determine the processes, resources, and responsibilities needed to achieve resilience and meet stakeholder expectations.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 22301:2019 outlines the support required for a Business Continuity Management System, specifying the resources, competence and awareness needed, the internal and external communication processes, and the control of documented information to ensure effective implementation and continual improvement.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 22301:2019 specifies the operational requirements for a Business Continuity Management System, detailing how organisations must implement, execute and control processes to ensure continuity of critical activities during disruptions. It includes guidance on deploying resources, activating response plans, monitoring performance, and applying corrective actions to maintain resilience.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 22301:2019 specifies the performance evaluation requirements for a business continuity management system, mandating that organizations monitor, measure, analyse and evaluate the BCMS effectiveness, conduct internal audits, and perform management reviews to ensure continual improvement and alignment with resilience objectives.
