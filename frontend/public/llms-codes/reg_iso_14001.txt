[10_1] 10.1 General -- Clause 10.1 requires the organization to identify and act on opportunities for continual improvement of its environmental management system, ensuring that any changes lead to enhanced environmental performance, compliance with objectives and the overall effectiveness of the EMS.
[10_2] 10.2 Nonconformity and corrective action -- ISO 14001:2015 clause 10.2 requires organizations to identify any nonconformities in their EMS, document the causes and take corrective actions to prevent recurrence, and to evaluate the effectiveness of those actions. It also mandates the maintenance of records of the nonconformities, corrective actions taken and any follow-up verification.
[10_3] 10.3 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.3 of ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to continually improve its environmental management system by establishing measurable objectives, monitoring performance, analyzing data, and implementing corrective and preventive actions, thereby enhancing overall environmental performance over time.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 of ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to identify and analyze internal and external factors that affect its environmental management system, including legal, technological, social, and market influences. It also calls for recognition of interested parties, their expectations, and how these elements shape the scope and objectives of the EMS. This understanding forms the foundation for planning and continual improvement.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 requires an organization to identify the relevant interested parties-such as customers, regulators, suppliers, communities and employees-and determine their needs and expectations that could influence its environmental management system. This understanding must be documented, incorporated into the EMS scope and objectives, and periodically reviewed to ensure the system remains aligned with stakeholder requirements.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the EMS -- Clause 4.3 of ISO 14001:2015 guides organizations in defining the boundaries and applicability of their Environmental Management System, considering internal functions, external influences, and the environmental aspects they intend to control, ensuring the EMS covers all relevant activities and locations.
[4_4] 4.4 Environmental management system -- Section 4.4 of ISO 14001:2015 specifies the environmental management system an organization must establish, implement, maintain and continually improve. It requires documented processes, defined responsibilities and integration with business operations to achieve the environmental policy, meet legal obligations and enhance overall environmental performance.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment by establishing, implementing and maintaining the environmental management system, ensuring the policy aligns with the organization's strategic direction, and providing necessary resources, communication and continual improvement.
[5_2] 5.2 Environmental policy -- Clause 5.2 requires an organization to define a documented environmental policy that reflects its commitment to protect the environment, comply with applicable legal and other requirements, prevent pollution, and pursue continual improvement. The policy must be appropriate to the organization's context, be communicated internally and externally as needed, and serve as the basis for setting environmental objectives and targets.

[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 of ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to identify environmental risks and opportunities it faces, then plan and implement actions to eliminate or reduce those risks while capitalizing on opportunities. The actions must be integrated into the EMS, documented, and reviewed to ensure they support continual improvement and compliance with environmental obligations.
[6_2] 6.2 Environmental objectives and planning to achieve them -- Clause 6.2 of ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to set measurable environmental objectives that align with its policy, legal obligations and significant impacts, and to define the actions, resources, responsibilities and timelines needed to reach them. It also mandates monitoring and reviewing progress toward those objectives.

[7_2] 7.2 Competence -- The entry 7.2 Competence in ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to identify the knowledge and skills needed for personnel influencing its environmental management system, to ensure they acquire and maintain that competence through training or other actions, and to keep documented evidence of their competence.
[7_3] 7.3 Awareness -- Clause 7.3 requires the organization to ensure that anyone performing work that can affect the environment is aware of the environmental policy, relevant objectives, their roles and the significant environmental aspects linked to their activities. This awareness must be maintained through training, communication and documented information to support effective EMS implementation.
[7_4] 7.4 Communication -- Section 7.4 of ISO 14001:2015 requires organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for internal and external communication regarding environmental aspects, impacts and EMS performance. It mandates that relevant information be identified, documented, shared with interested parties, and updated as needed to support continual improvement.
[7_5] 7.5 Documented information -- Section 7.5 of ISO 14001:2015 requires organizations to create and maintain documented information needed for the environmental management system, ensuring it is suitably protected, accessible, and retained for appropriate periods. It specifies controls for approval, review, updating, and availability of these documents and records to support effective implementation and continual improvement.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 14001:2015 requires an organization to determine the processes needed to achieve its environmental objectives, identify associated environmental aspects, and establish criteria for operational control. It mandates planning, implementing, and maintaining controls to manage these aspects, ensuring that activities are carried out consistently with the environmental management system and that performance can be measured and improved.
[8_2] 8.2 Emergency preparedness and response -- The 8.2 clause requires an organization to identify potential emergency situations that could have environmental impacts, plan and implement procedures, allocate resources, train personnel and conduct testing to ensure prompt and effective response, thereby minimizing environmental harm.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 14001:2015 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring and measuring environmental performance, analyzing the data, and evaluating results against environmental objectives and legal requirements, ensuring that information is reliable, documented, and used to drive continual improvement of the EMS.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 of ISO 14001:2015 requires the organization to conduct internal audits at planned intervals to assess the conformity of its environmental management system with the standard and its own requirements, and to evaluate its effective implementation and performance. Audits must be objective, impartial and documented, with findings used to drive corrective actions and continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- The 9.3 Management review clause requires top management to periodically assess the environmental management system's performance, its suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. It mandates reviewing audit results, compliance status, objectives, risks and opportunities, and documenting decisions, actions and responsibilities to drive continual improvement.

This foundational step ensures that goals, processes, and controls are aligned with the organization's actual environmental impacts and stakeholder requirements.
