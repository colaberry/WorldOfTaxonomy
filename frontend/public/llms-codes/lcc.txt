[A] General Works -- Class A in the Library of Congress Classification covers general reference works, including encyclopedias, bibliographies, dictionaries, and assorted miscellany that do not fit into more specific subject classes. It also contains collections of essays, general interest periodicals, and other broadly oriented materials.
[AC] Collections, Series, Collected Works -- AC in the Library of Congress Classification designates material that comprises collections, series or collected works, bringing together writings, documents or artistic productions that share a common author, subject, or institutional origin. It is used for multi-volume sets, anthologies, festschrifts and other grouped publications that are not treated as individual monographs. This class often includes bibliographies, festschrifts, and compilations of a creator's output.
[AE] Encyclopedias -- AE is the Library of Congress classification for encyclopedic works, encompassing general reference books, dictionaries, and specialized encyclopedias. Materials are arranged alphabetically by subject and may appear as single-volume or multivolume sets, covering all fields of knowledge.
[AG] Dictionaries and Other General Reference Works -- AG is the Library of Congress class for dictionaries, encyclopedias, atlases and other general reference works, including bilingual and specialized reference volumes. The schedule arranges items alphabetically by language and subject and also encompasses biographical dictionaries, gazetteers and similar comprehensive reference publications.
[B] Philosophy, Psychology, Religion -- B is the top-level class for works dealing with philosophy, psychology and religion. It includes general treatises on metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, mind and behavior, as well as comparative and systematic studies of religious traditions and theology.
[BC] Logic -- BC is the Library of Congress class for works on logic, covering the principles and methods of reasoning, both formal and informal. It includes treatises on symbolic and mathematical logic, deductive and inductive techniques, and the history and philosophy of logical theory.
[BD] Speculative Philosophy -- BD covers works in speculative philosophy, including metaphysics, ontology, cosmology and the study of fundamental questions about reality, being, and existence. It groups texts that explore abstract theoretical frameworks beyond empirical science, ranging from ancient philosophical traditions to modern systematic thought.
[BF] Psychology -- The class BF in the Library of Congress Classification is dedicated to psychology, encompassing the scientific study of behavior, mental processes, and the methods used to investigate them. It includes works on experimental psychology, developmental, social, clinical, and applied branches, as well as theoretical and philosophical aspects of the discipline.
[BL] Religions, Mythology, Rationalism -- BL is the Library of Congress class covering religions, mythology and rationalist thought. It groups works on comparative religion, world mythologies, religious history, and secular or philosophical critiques of religion, ranging from ancient myths to modern theological studies.
[C] Auxiliary Sciences of History -- C is the class for auxiliary sciences of history in the Library of Congress Classification system. It includes subjects such as archaeology, genealogy, heraldry, chronology and archival science, covering the methods and tools used to study and interpret historical records.
[D] World History -- In the Library of Congress Classification the single-letter class D is assigned to world history, covering all periods from prehistoric times to the present. It includes general surveys, thematic studies, chronologies and comparative works, and is further divided into subclasses that focus on specific eras, regions, archaeology and historiography.
[DG] History of Italy and Surrounding Regions -- DG is the Library of Congress Classification for the history of Italy and adjacent areas, covering ancient, medieval, and modern periods as well as neighboring territories such as the Balkans and the Adriatic coast. The class includes political, social and cultural histories, biographies, and regional studies.
[E] History of the Americas (United States) -- The LCC class E encompasses works on the history of the United States and its territories, covering political, economic, social and cultural developments from pre-colonial times to the present. It includes books, maps, biographies, and primary source collections that document American historical events and trends.
[F] History of the Americas (Other) -- Class F in the Library of Congress Classification groups works on the history of the Americas that are not covered by more specific national or regional subclasses. It includes general surveys, comparative studies, thematic treatments and other miscellaneous topics concerning the continent's past.
[G] Geography, Anthropology, Recreation -- Class G in the Library of Congress Classification groups works on physical and human geography, ethnology, and related recreational subjects such as tourism and outdoor activities. It includes materials on maps, regional studies, cultural anthropology, and the study of leisure pursuits.
[GE] Environmental Sciences -- GE is the Library of Congress Classification for environmental sciences, covering the study of the Earth's physical environment, natural resources, pollution, and ecological processes. It includes works on climate, ecosystems, conservation, environmental policy, and related interdisciplinary research.
[GF] Human Ecology, Anthropogeography -- GF is the Library of Congress subclass for human ecology and anthropogeography, covering the study of how humans interact with and adapt to their natural environment. It includes works on settlement patterns, cultural landscapes, resource use, environmental impact, and the spatial relationships of populations and societies.
[H] Social Sciences -- In the Library of Congress Classification, the single-letter class H designates works on the social sciences. It gathers literature on sociology, anthropology, economics, political science and related interdisciplinary studies that examine human societies and their structures.
[HA] Statistics -- HA is the class for statistics in the Library of Congress Classification system. It covers the theory and methods of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data, including probability, statistical theory, applied statistics, and related mathematical techniques. Works in this class include textbooks, research monographs, and reference works on statistical methodology and applications.
[HB] Economic Theory, Demography -- HB is the Library of Congress subclass that gathers books on economic theory and demography, covering principles of economics, economic history, mathematical and statistical methods, and the study of population size, structure and trends. Materials range from theoretical models to applied analyses of labour, growth and demographic change.
[HC] Economic History and Conditions -- HC represents works on economic history and the analysis of economic conditions, covering the development of economies, comparative economic systems, and the impact of economic forces on societies. It includes studies of trade, industry, agriculture, and financial institutions across different periods and regions. The class is used for scholarly research on how economies have evolved and shaped human life.
[HD] Industries, Land Use, Labor -- HD is the Library of Congress class for economic subjects dealing with industry, manufacturing, agriculture, land use, natural resources and related economic geography. It also covers labor, employment relations, workforce history and policy issues affecting production and the use of land.
[HE] Transportation and Communications -- HE is the Library of Congress class covering transportation systems and communication technologies. It includes topics such as railroads, highways, aviation, shipping, telegraphy, telephony, radio, television and the internet, documenting their development, operation and regulation.
[HF] Commerce -- HF is the class for commerce, covering the theory and practice of trade, transportation, and business operations. It includes subjects such as domestic and international trade, marketing, finance, shipping, logistics, and the regulation of commercial activity. The schedule also addresses economic aspects of trade, distribution of goods, and related legal and statistical materials.
[HG] Finance -- HG is the Library of Congress class for finance, encompassing monetary and economic aspects of banking, credit, insurance, securities and financial institutions, as well as public finance, fiscal policy and financial markets. The subclass gathers works on the theory, practice and regulation of money and capital management.
[HJ] Public Finance -- HJ is the Library of Congress class for public finance, covering government revenue and expenditure, taxation systems, public debt, budgeting, fiscal policy, and related economic regulation. Works in this range examine how states raise and allocate funds, the legal frameworks of taxation, and the financial administration of public programs.
[HM] Sociology -- HM is the Library of Congress subclass for sociology, encompassing the systematic study of societies, social behavior, institutions, and relationships. It includes works on social theory, research methods, social statistics, comparative and historical sociology, as well as subfields such as demography, social change, and social problems.
[HN] Social History and Conditions -- HN is the Library of Congress subclass that groups works on social history and conditions, focusing on the everyday life, customs, institutions and demographic patterns of societies. It includes studies of family structures, labor and employment, education, health, welfare, social movements and other aspects of collective human experience.
[HQ] The Family, Marriage, Women -- HQ is the Library of Congress class for works on the family, marriage and women, covering social, legal and cultural aspects of domestic life, kinship patterns, marital institutions, gender roles and the history and status of women in society.
[HS] Societies - Secret, Benevolent, Etc. -- HS in the Library of Congress Classification covers organizations and associations, focusing on secret societies, fraternal orders, benevolent societies, and similar groups, including their histories, rituals, and membership structures. It also includes literature on mutual aid societies and charitable clubs.
[HT] Communities, Classes, Races -- HT is the Library of Congress class for works on social groups, focusing on communities, social classes, and racial or ethnic groups. It covers sociological studies of group structures, stratification, intergroup relations and the social issues that arise within and between these categories.
[HV] Social Pathology, Social and Public Welfare -- HV covers works on social pathology, welfare policy, and public assistance, addressing issues such as poverty, crime, health, and social reform. It includes studies of charitable institutions, government programs, and the historical development of social services, providing a comprehensive view of efforts to improve societal well-being.
[HX] Socialism, Communism, Anarchism -- HX is the Library of Congress classification for works on socialism, communism and anarchism, encompassing theoretical treatises, historical studies, biographies of key figures and analyses of movements and parties. It includes comparative examinations of these ideologies, their philosophical foundations, political programs and social impact across different countries and periods.
[J] Political Science -- Class J in the Library of Congress Classification groups works on political science, covering political theory, government structures, public policy, comparative politics, and international relations, and includes literature on political thought, administration, and political behavior.
[K] Law -- The class K in the Library of Congress Classification system covers law and legal topics. It includes general works on jurisprudence, legal theory, and the practice of law, as well as comparative and international law, and is subdivided into subclasses for specific jurisdictions and specialized fields.
[KF] Law of the United States -- KF designates the section of the Library of Congress Classification devoted to the law of the United States, encompassing federal statutes, constitutional law, and the legal systems of individual states, as well as related judicial decisions and legal scholarship. It includes topics such as civil procedure, criminal law, property, and administrative regulation, providing a comprehensive framework for organizing U.S. legal materials in research libraries.
[KJV] Law of France -- KJV is the Library of Congress subclass for contemporary French law, encompassing books, codes and commentary on the modern French legal system, its statutes, regulations and current judicial practice.
[KK] Law of Germany -- KK is the Library of Congress subclass that collects works on German law, encompassing the historical development, institutions, civil, criminal, commercial, and administrative statutes of the Federal Republic and its predecessor states, as well as comparative and scholarly analyses of German legal systems.
[L] Education -- In the Library of Congress Classification, the class L designates works on education, encompassing the theory and practice of teaching, school organization, curricula, educational psychology, and related administrative and policy topics. Materials range from historical surveys to contemporary research on teaching methods, adult and higher education, and educational technology.
[M] Music and Books on Music -- The LCC class M designates works on music, including theory, history, composition, performance practice, and literature about musical instruments. It encompasses both scholarly studies and general books on music, covering a wide range of topics from analysis of works to biographical and cultural contexts.
[ML] Literature on Music -- ML is the LCC subclass covering literature about music, including histories, biographies, criticism, and general works on music theory and practice. It includes works that discuss music as a subject rather than musical scores themselves.
[MT] Musical Instruction and Study -- MT is the Library of Congress classification for musical instruction and study, encompassing textbooks, method books, pedagogy, theory, ear training and other materials used to teach voice, instrumental performance and music appreciation at all levels.
[N] Fine Arts -- The class N in the Library of Congress Classification designates Fine Arts, encompassing visual and applied arts such as architecture, sculpture, painting, and related crafts; it organizes works on aesthetics, art history, and artistic techniques.
[NA] Architecture -- NA is the Library of Congress Classification for works on architecture. It covers general and historical studies of building design, construction techniques, architectural theory, and related fields such as city-planning, landscape architecture, and historic preservation.
[NB] Sculpture -- NB is the Library of Congress class for sculpture, encompassing the history, theory and techniques of three-dimensional art, materials, styles and individual sculptors, as well as museum, conservation and restoration issues. The class also includes exhibition catalogs, biographies and reference works.
[NC] Drawing, Design, Illustration -- NC is the Library of Congress class for drawing, design, and illustration, encompassing fine-art drawing, graphic design, commercial illustration, decorative arts, and related techniques; it includes studies of line, composition, perspective, and the history and theory of visual representation in both traditional and modern media.
[ND] Painting -- The ND subclass in the Library of Congress Classification is dedicated to painting, encompassing works on the history, theory, techniques, and practice of the visual arts. It includes studies of individual artists, movements, styles, materials, and regional or period surveys, as well as instructional manuals and critical analyses related to painting.

[P] Language and Literature -- In the Library of Congress Classification, the class P covers language and literature. It includes works on general linguistics, language families, comparative studies, and literature of individual languages and regions. Materials are organized by language group and literary period.
[PA] Greek Language and Literature -- PA is the subclass of the Library of Congress Classification for Greek language and literature, covering works in ancient, medieval and modern Greek, including linguistics, grammar, poetry, drama, history, philosophy and critical studies. It groups materials from the classical period through contemporary Greek scholarship.
[PB] Modern Languages -- PB is the Library of Congress class for modern languages, covering the study, teaching, and literature of contemporary European languages such as English, French, German, Spanish, Italian and related linguistic works. It includes language instruction materials, dictionaries, grammars and comparative studies, while classical languages are classified elsewhere.
[PQ] French, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese Literature -- PQ is the Library of Congress class for the literature of France, Italy, Spain and Portugal, covering poetry, drama, prose and related literary forms in those languages. It also includes literary history, criticism and ancillary works for these four national literatures.
[PR] English Literature -- PR is the Library of Congress subclass that encompasses English literature, ranging from medieval poetry to modern fiction and drama. It includes the works of British authors, literary criticism, biographies, and studies of literary history within the English language.
[PS] American Literature in English -- The LCC subclass PS covers American literature written in English, including works by authors from the United States, Canada, and other English-language writers of the Americas. It spans poetry, fiction, drama, and criticism from colonial times to the present, organized chronologically and by author.
[PT] German, Dutch, and Scandinavian Literatures -- PT is the Library of Congress Classification for the literatures of Germanic language families, covering works in German, Dutch, Danish, Norwegian, Swedish, Icelandic and related languages, as well as comparative and historical studies of these literary traditions.
[Q] Science -- Q is the Library of Congress Classification main class for Science, encompassing the natural and physical sciences such as physics, chemistry, astronomy, earth sciences, and related interdisciplinary fields. Works on scientific methods, theory, research and all sub-disciplines are organized under its subdivisions from QA to QM.
[QA] Mathematics -- QA is the main class in the Library of Congress Classification for pure and applied mathematics. It includes sub-classes covering topics such as arithmetic, algebra, geometry, analysis, number theory, and mathematical logic, as well as texts on the history and philosophy of mathematics.
[QB] Astronomy -- QB is the Library of Congress Classification section for astronomy, covering the scientific study of celestial objects, phenomena, and the universe. It includes works on observational and theoretical astronomy, astrophysics, planetary science, cosmology, and related instrumentation and methods. The subclass also groups reference materials such as star catalogs, atlases, and histories of astronomical research.
[QC] Physics -- QC is the Library of Congress Classification for physics, encompassing works on classical mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics, relativity, atomic and nuclear physics, and related experimental and theoretical studies. It groups foundational texts, research monographs, and applied physics materials, providing a systematic arrangement for the discipline within the broader Q - science class.
[QD] Chemistry -- QD is the Library of Congress class for chemistry, covering the science of the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter. It includes general chemistry, inorganic and organic chemistry, analytical methods, physical chemistry, and applied topics such as industrial and laboratory techniques, with further subdivisions for specific elements, compounds, and processes.
[QE] Geology -- The QE subclass in the Library of Congress Classification groups books and resources on geology, covering mineralogy, petrology, stratigraphy, structural geology, regional studies, geological mapping, and the physical processes that shape the Earth's crust.
[QH] Natural History (General), Biology -- QH is the Library of Congress class for natural history and general biology, encompassing works on comparative anatomy, physiology, ecology, evolution, taxonomy, and related scientific studies.
[QK] Botany -- QK is the class in the Library of Congress Classification dedicated to botany, the scientific study of plants and plant life. It encompasses works on plant physiology, structure, genetics, ecology, taxonomy, and related fields such as algae, fungi, and bryophytes, and includes sub-classes for horticulture, plant pathology, and agricultural botany.
[QL] Zoology -- QL is the Library of Congress subclass for zoology, covering the scientific study of animals. It includes works on animal structure, physiology, behavior, ecology, taxonomy and related fields such as comparative anatomy and evolutionary biology. Materials are arranged alphabetically by specific animal groups and thematic topics.
[QM] Human Anatomy -- QM is the Library of Congress Classification subclass for Human anatomy, covering the structure of the human body, including gross anatomy, histology, developmental and comparative anatomy, as well as related medical imaging and functional aspects.
[QP] Physiology -- QP is the Library of Congress subclass dedicated to physiology, encompassing the study of functions and mechanisms in living organisms. [QR] Microbiology -- QR is the Library of Congress Classification subclass for microbiology, encompassing the study of microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, algae and protozoa. [R] Medicine -- R is the main class for Medicine in the Library of Congress Classification system, encompassing works on the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease. [RA] Public Aspects of Medicine -- RA is the Library of Congress class for works that discuss medicine from a societal perspective, covering topics such as health policy, medical ethics, public health administration, health economics, and the social implications of medical practice. [RB] Pathology -- RB is the Library of Congress classification for pathology, encompassing the scientific study of disease, its causes, development, and effects on the body. [RC] Internal Medicine -- RC is the Class within the Library of Congress Classification that encompasses internal medicine and related clinical specialties. It includes works on diagnosis, treatment, and research in adult medicine, covering subspecialties such as cardiology, gastroenterology, infectious diseases, and general medical practice.
[RD] Surgery -- RD is the LCC class for Surgery, covering surgical practice, techniques, instruments, and related medical specialties. It includes subfields such as operative procedures, anesthesia, postoperative care, and the history of surgery.
[RE] Ophthalmology -- RE is the Library of Congress Classification subclass for ophthalmology, encompassing works on eye anatomy, diseases, surgery, visual optics, and related clinical and research topics. [RF] Otorhinolaryngology -- RF designates the section of the Library of Congress Classification devoted to otorhinolaryngology, encompassing medical works on the anatomy, physiology, diseases, diagnosis and treatment of the ear, nose, throat and related structures.
[RG] Gynecology and Obstetrics -- RG is the Library of Congress Classification subclass that gathers works on women's health, focusing on the medical fields of gynecology and obstetrics.
