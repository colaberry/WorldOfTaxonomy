[EF] EF Scale -- The EF Scale is the Enhanced Fujita tornado damage rating system used in the United States and Canada to estimate wind speeds from observed damage. It supersedes the original Fujita scale and assigns tornadoes a rating from EF0 to EF5, each linked to a specific wind-speed range.
[EF.0] EF0: Light Damage (65-85 mph) -- EF0 marks the weakest category on the Enhanced Fujita scale, indicating wind speeds of roughly 65 to 85 miles per hour. Damage is limited to minor effects such as broken tree branches, shallow roof damage, and light damage to signs and awnings. The tornado is typically brief and causes minimal structural impact.
[EF.1] EF1: Moderate Damage (86-110 mph) -- EF1 denotes moderate tornado damage on the Enhanced Fujita Scale, with estimated wind speeds of 86 to 110 miles per hour. Damage includes roof shingle loss, broken windows, and minor structural damage to homes and mobile homes. The tornado may uproot shallow-rooted trees and cause moderate agricultural losses.
[EF.2] EF2: Considerable Damage (111-135 mph) -- An EF2 tornado on the Enhanced Fujita Scale produces wind speeds of 111-135 mph and causes considerable damage. Well-built homes may lose their roofs
[EF.3] EF3: Severe Damage (136-165 mph) -- EF3 indicates severe tornado damage with wind speeds estimated between 136 and 165 mph. Structures such as well-built homes may suffer major roof loss and wall collapse, large trees can be uprooted, and vehicles may be tossed. The rating aids post-event damage assessment and classification.
[EF.4] EF4: Devastating Damage (166-200 mph) -- EF4 signifies devastating damage from tornadoes with wind speeds estimated at 166 to 200 mph. At this intensity entire homes may be destroyed, poorly anchored structures can collapse, and large vehicles and debris are tossed many yards. The rating aids damage assessment and emergency planning.
[EF.5] EF5: Incredible Damage (200+ mph) -- EF5 on the Enhanced Fujita scale denotes a tornado with estimated wind speeds exceeding 200 mph, producing incredible damage such as complete destruction of well-constructed homes, foundations swept clean, and severe debarking of trees. This level represents the most extreme and catastrophic tornado events.
[EF.DI] Damage Indicators (28 types) -- EF.DI lists the 28 damage indicators employed by the Enhanced Fujita Scale, covering structures, trees, and other objects, each with defined criteria to correlate observed damage with estimated wind speeds.
[EF.DOD] Degrees of Damage -- The EF.DOD entry defines the specific degrees of damage used by the Enhanced Fujin  ta Scale to assess tornado impacts on buildings, vegetation and infrastructure. It outlines twelve detailed damage levels, from minor cosmetic harm to total structural collapse, each linked to estimated wind speed ranges for consistent rating across surveys.
[EF.UB] Upper/Lower Bound Wind Speeds -- EF.UB denotes the upper and lower bound wind-speed estimates that bracket each EF rating, defining the range of velocities associated with the observed damage. These limits allow meteorologists to assign a tornado to a specific EF category when exact wind speeds are not directly measured.
