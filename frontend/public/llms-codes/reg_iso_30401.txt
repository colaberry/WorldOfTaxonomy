[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Clause 10.1 of ISO 30401:2018 mandates that organizations detect and document any nonconformities in their knowledge-management system, determine their causes, and take corrective action to prevent recurrence. It also requires reviewing the effectiveness of those actions and maintaining records to demonstrate continual improvement.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 requires the organization to establish a process for continual improvement of its knowledge management system, using performance metrics, internal audits and feedback to identify opportunities, implement corrective actions and monitor results. The aim is to enhance knowledge creation, sharing and utilization over time.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 requires the organization to identify internal and external issues that affect its knowledge objectives, determine relevant interested parties, and assess how these factors influence the planning, implementation and continual improvement of its knowledge management system.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Entry 4.2 in ISO 30401:2018 requires organizations to identify all relevant interested parties-such as employees, customers, suppliers, regulators and stakeholders-and systematically determine their specific needs, expectations and requirements for knowledge management, using this insight to shape policies, processes and performance metrics.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the KMS -- In ISO 30401:2018, clause 4.3 requires an organization to define the boundaries and applicability of its Knowledge Management System, specifying the processes, stakeholders, products and services, and external and internal issues it will address. The scope must be documented, aligned with strategic objectives, and reviewed periodically to ensure relevance and effectiveness.
[4_4] 4.4 Knowledge management system -- The entry defines a knowledge management system as the collection of policies, processes, structures and tools an organization uses to create, capture, share, and apply knowledge to meet its objectives, requiring defined governance, roles and continuous improvement.
[4_5] 4.5 Knowledge management culture -- ISO 30401:2018 clause 4.5 defines the knowledge-management culture an organization must develop, requiring leadership to foster attitudes, values and behaviours that encourage the creation, sharing and application of knowledge. It calls for policies, incentives and communication that align with strategic goals and support continuous learning, collaboration and openness across all levels of the organization.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to exhibit active leadership and commitment toward the knowledge management system, establishing a clear KM policy, aligning it with organizational strategy, allocating necessary resources, and assigning responsibilities to ensure the system's effective implementation and continual improvement.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- Clause 5.2 of ISO 30401:2018 mandates that an organization create a formal knowledge-management policy that aligns with its strategic goals, defines the scope of knowledge activities, assigns responsibilities, and demonstrates top-management commitment to fostering a culture of knowledge sharing and continual improvement.
[5_3] 5.3 Roles, responsibilities and authorities -- This clause specifies that organizations must define and document the roles, responsibilities, and authorities related to the knowledge management system, ensuring accountability and clear assignment of tasks across all levels. It requires that these assignments be communicated and reviewed periodically to support effective knowledge management.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 30401:2018 requires organizations to establish, implement and maintain processes for planning, operating, monitoring and controlling knowledge-management activities so that defined objectives are met and continual improvement of the knowledge system is ensured.
[8_2] 8.2 Knowledge life cycle -- The knowledge life cycle defines how an organization creates, captures, shares, applies, retains and disposes of knowledge, outlining the processes, roles and controls needed to manage knowledge assets throughout their useful life and to support strategic objectives.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- The standard requires organizations to put in place processes that monitor and measure knowledge-management performance, collect relevant data, analyze the results and evaluate their effectiveness against defined objectives. It calls for documented procedures, appropriate metrics and regular reviews to ensure continual improvement of the knowledge management system.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 requires an organisation to conduct regular internal audits of its knowledge-management system to verify compliance with ISO 30401, identify non-conformities and assess effectiveness. The audit must be planned, executed and reported in accordance with a documented programme, and any findings must trigger corrective actions that support continual improvement and alignment with strategic objectives.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 of ISO 30401:2018 mandates that top management periodically review the knowledge-management system to assess its performance, effectiveness and alignment with strategic goals. The review must consider inputs such as audit findings, stakeholder feedback, and improvement opportunities, and produce outputs that include decisions on corrective actions, resource allocation and changes needed for continual improvement.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 covers the continual improvement of a knowledge-management system, requiring organisations to monitor performance, assess effectiveness and act on gaps or opportunities. It mandates the use of metrics, audits and feedback to identify corrective actions, update objectives and enhance processes, ensuring the KM system evolves and delivers greater value over time.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 30401:2018 requires organizations to identify relevant internal and external issues, determine interested parties and their needs, and define the scope of their knowledge management system, ensuring it aligns with strategic objectives and operational context.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 mandates that senior leadership actively champion the knowledge management system, establishing a clear policy aligned with organizational goals, allocating necessary resources, and assigning accountable roles. It requires integration of knowledge objectives into the overall strategy, regular review of performance, and fostering a culture that values knowledge sharing and continuous improvement.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 30401:2018 sets out the planning requirements for a knowledge-management system, mandating that organisations define knowledge-related objectives, assess internal and external issues, identify risks and opportunities, and allocate appropriate resources and responsibilities to achieve the intended outcomes. It also requires establishing processes for monitoring, reviewing and updating the knowledge strategy.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 30401:2018 specifies the support mechanisms required for an effective knowledge-management system, including the provision of resources, competence and awareness, internal and external communication, and the creation and control of documented information to ensure the system operates consistently and achieves its objectives.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 30401:2018 defines the operational requirements for a knowledge management system, specifying how organizations must plan, execute, and control knowledge-related activities, allocate resources, and ensure consistent performance monitoring and continual improvement of knowledge processes.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 30401:2018 defines the requirements for performance evaluation of knowledge-management systems. It outlines processes for monitoring, measuring, analysing and evaluating system effectiveness, including internal audits and management reviews, to support continual improvement.
