[comm] Commodity Committees -- The Codex Alimentarius "comm" entry refers to Commodity Committees, which are expert groups that develop and maintain standards, guidelines and codes of practice for specific food commodities, ensuring safety, quality and fair trade across nations.
[comm_1] CCFO - Committee on Fats and Oils -- The Committee on Fats and Oils (CCFO) is a Codex Alimentarius body that develops international standards, guidelines and recommended practices for edible fats, oils and related products, ensuring safety, quality and fair trade across member nations.
[comm_2] CCFFP - Committee on Fish and Fishery Products -- The CCFFP - Committee on Fish and Fishery Products is a Codex Alimentarius body that develops international standards, guidelines and codes of practice for fish, seafood and related products. Its work aims to protect consumer health, promote fair trade and ensure the quality and safety of fishery commodities worldwide.
[comm_3] CCS - Committee on Spices and Culinary Herbs -- The CCS is the Codex committee responsible for developing and maintaining international standards, guidelines and recommendations for spices and culinary herbs, covering safety, quality and labeling aspects. It brings together experts from member countries, industry and consumer groups to achieve globally harmonised practices.
[comm_4] CCSCH - Committee on Sugars -- The CCSCH, or Committee on Sugars, is a Codex Alimentarius body that develops and reviews international standards, guidelines and recommendations for sugars and related sweeteners. It addresses safety, quality, labelling and food-technology issues to promote fair trade and consumer protection. The committee also coordinates with national authorities and industry stakeholders on sugar-related matters.
[coord] Regional Coordinating Committees -- Regional Coordinating Committees are bodies that bring together national members from a geographic region to coordinate Codex activities, promote implementation of standards, and facilitate information exchange. They support the Codex Commission, organize meetings, and help harmonize food safety regulations across member countries.
[coord_1] CCAFRICA - Coordinating Committee for Africa -- The CCAFRICA, or Coordinating Committee for Africa, is a regional body within the Codex Alimentarius system that brings together African member states, observers and experts to develop and harmonise food safety and quality standards across the continent, supporting trade and consumer protection.
[coord_2] CCASIA - Coordinating Committee for Asia -- The CCASIA is the Codex Alimentarius Coordinating Committee for Asia, which facilitates cooperation among Asian member countries in developing and harmonising food safety and quality standards. It serves as a regional liaison, reviewing Asian food-related issues and contributing to the formulation of international Codex guidelines.
[coord_3] CCEURO - Coordinating Committee for Europe -- The CCEURO, or Coordinating Committee for Europe, is the regional body that represents European member countries within the Codex Alimentarius system. It facilitates cooperation, harmonises national food standards with Codex guidelines, and advises on the development and implementation of international food safety and quality standards across Europe.
[coord_4] CCLAC - Coordinating Committee for Latin America -- The CCLAC, or Coordinating Committee for Latin America, is a Codex Alimentarius body that facilitates cooperation among Latin American member countries in developing and implementing international food standards. It promotes information exchange, aligns regional food safety policies with Codex guidelines, and supports joint participation in Codex meetings
[coord_5] CCNASWP - Coordinating Committee for North America and South West Pacific -- The Codex Alimentarius entry "coord_5 - CCNASWP" refers to the Coordinating Committee for North America and South West Pacific. It brings together member countries from those regions to harmonise food safety and quality standards, exchange technical information and support the implementation of Codex guidelines.
[coord_6] CCNE - Coordinating Committee for Near East -- The CCNE, or Coordinating Committee for Near East, is a regional body within the Codex Alimentarius system that brings together Near Eastern nations to cooperate on the development and implementation of international food safety and quality standards, facilitating their participation in Codex meetings and the exchange of technical information.
[gen] General Subject Committees -- The "General Subject Committees" (code gen) are standing committees of the Codex Alimentarius Commission that handle cross-cutting food issues such as hygiene, additives, labelling, contaminants and nutrition. They develop and coordinate international standards, guidelines and recommendations that apply to many food categories, ensuring consistency and consumer protection worldwide.
[gen_1] CCFH - Committee on Food Hygiene -- The CCFH is the Codex Committee on Food Hygiene, tasked with developing international guidelines and standards that promote safe handling, processing and distribution of food. It collaborates with member countries to draft codes of practice, risk assessments and recommendations that are incorporated into the Codex Alimentarius.
[gen_2] CCFL - Committee on Food Labelling -- The CCFL, or Committee on Food Labelling, is a Codex Alimentarius body that drafts and updates international standards, guidelines and recommended practices for the labeling of foods. It addresses nutrition information, ingredient lists, claims, country of origin and other labeling elements to facilitate trade and protect consumer health.
[gen_3] CCFA - Committee on Food Additives -- The CCFA, or Codex Committee on Food Additives, is the body within the Codex Alimentarius that develops international standards, guidelines and recommendations for the safety, use and labeling of food additives. It reviews scientific data, sets maximum levels and specifications, and aims to protect consumer health while facilitating global trade.
[gen_4] CCPR - Committee on Pesticide Residues -- The CCPR, or Committee on Pesticide Residues, is a standing body within the Codex Alimentarius system that develops international standards, guidelines and code of practice for pesticide residues in food and feed. Its work helps protect consumer health and facilitates fair trade by establishing maximum residue limits and risk-assessment methods.
[gen_5] CCRVDF - Committee on Residues of Veterinary Drugs in Foods -- The CCRVDF (Committee on Residues of Veterinary Drugs in Foods) is a Codex Alimentarius body that establishes international standards and guidelines for veterinary drug residue limits in food commodities. Its work integrates scientific risk assessments to set maximum residue limits, promote trade safety and protect consumer health worldwide.
[gen_6] CCMAS - Committee on Methods of Analysis and Sampling -- The CCMAS, or Committee on Methods of Analysis and Sampling, is a standing committee of the Codex Alimentarius that develops and harmonizes international guidelines for food testing, sampling procedures, and analytical methods to support food safety and trade. Its work underpins the scientific basis of Codex standards and helps ensure consistent, reliable results across laboratories worldwide.
[gen_7] CCNFSDU - Committee on Nutrition and Foods for Special Dietary Uses -- The CCNFSDU, the Codex Committee on Nutrition and Foods for Special Dietary Uses, is a subsidiary body of the Codex Alimentarius Commission that develops international standards, guidelines and codes of practice for foods intended for specific nutritional needs, such as infant formulas, medical foods and diet-restricted products, and provides scientific input on nutrition labeling and health claims.
[gen_8] CCCF - Committee on Contaminants in Foods -- The Codex Alimentarius Committee on Contaminants in Foods (CCCF) develops international standards, guidelines and recommendations to limit and monitor chemical and biological contaminants in food, ensuring safety and facilitating trade.
[gen_9] CCFICS - Committee on Food Import and Export Inspection and Certification -- The CCFICS (Committee on Food Import and Export Inspection and Certification) is a Codex Alimentarius body that develops and harmonizes guidelines for the inspection, certification and trade of food across borders. It works with member countries and international partners to ensure safety, quality and fair trade practices for imported and exported foods.
