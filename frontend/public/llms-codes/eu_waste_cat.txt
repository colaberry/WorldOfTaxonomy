[EWC.01] Wastes from exploration and mining -- EWC.01 includes wastes produced during the exploration for and extraction of mineral resources such as waste rock, tailings, drill cuttings and associated processing residues. These inorganic materials often contain trace metals and must be managed and disposed of in ways that prevent environmental contamination.
[EWC.02] Wastes from agriculture and food -- EWC.02 groups wastes generated by farming and food-related activities, such as animal manure, crop residues, processing by-products, spoiled or unused food, and related organic material. These wastes are typically biodegradable and may be recycled, composted, or used for energy recovery, but they must be managed to prevent pollution and disease.
[EWC.03] Wastes from wood processing and paper -- EWC.03 includes waste produced in wood and paper processing such as sawdust, shavings, off-cuts, contaminated paper residues and similar by-products. These wastes are generally non-hazardous but must be managed to avoid environmental pollution.
[EWC.04] Wastes from leather and textile -- EWC 04 covers wastes generated during the manufacture, processing, repair or disposal of leather and textile products, including off-cuts, shavings, sludges, finishing chemicals and contaminated scrap. These wastes may contain dyes, solvents, tanning agents, adhesives and metal salts, giving them potential toxic, flammable or environmentally hazardous properties that require appropriate treatment, recovery or disposal in accordance with waste-management regulations.
[EWC.05] Wastes from petroleum refining -- EWC.05 includes all waste streams produced during the refining of crude oil into petroleum products, such as spent catalysts, oily sludges, contaminated soils and residues from desulphurisation units. These wastes are generally hazardous because of their high hydrocarbon content, the presence of heavy metals and other toxic substances, and therefore require specialised handling, treatment and disposal.
[EWC.06] Wastes from inorganic chemical processes -- EWC.06 covers wastes generated during inorganic chemical manufacturing such as slag, residues, process liquids, and by-products from the production of acids, salts, metals, and ceramics. It includes both hazardous and non-hazardous waste streams that originate from reactions, purification, and waste-water treatment in inorganic processes.
[EWC.07] Wastes from organic chemical processes -- EWC.07 includes waste generated in the manufacture, processing or treatment of organic chemicals such as solvents, intermediates and finished products. The waste may contain residues of acids, bases, reactive compounds or other hazardous substances and is usually classified further according to specific hazard subclasses.
[EWC.08] Wastes from coatings and sealants -- EWC 08 covers waste generated during the production, application or removal of paints, varnishes, lacquers, inks, adhesives, sealants and similar coating materials, including residues, sludge and contaminated equipment. It includes both liquid and solid fractions as well as packaging that has become contaminated with these substances. The category is used across the EU to track and manage hazardous and non-hazardous waste streams from coating operations.
[EWC.09] Wastes from photographic industry -- EWC.09 covers wastes generated by the photographic industry, including spent photographic emulsions, silver-containing fixer and developer solutions, developer and processing chemicals, photographic paper residues, and related solvents. These materials often contain heavy metals and organic compounds that may be hazardous and require specialised treatment or recovery before disposal.
[EWC.10] Wastes from thermal processes -- EWC.10 includes waste generated by thermal treatment processes such as incineration, co-incineration, and other high-temperature operations. It covers residues like ash, slag and dust that result from the combustion or heat-treatment of waste streams. These materials are subject to specific handling and disposal rules due to potential hazardous content.
[EWC.11] Wastes from chemical surface treatment -- EWC 11 covers wastes generated during chemical surface-treatment operations such as metal plating, galvanising, anodising, pickling, passivation, coating and related rinsing or cleaning steps. It includes spent baths, sludges, spent solvents, rinsate, contaminated fabrics and filter media that contain metal salts, acids, bases or organic additives used in these processes. These wastes are often hazardous because they may contain heavy metals, cyanides, fluorides or other toxic substances and therefore require special handling and disposal.
[EWC.12] Wastes from shaping and treatment of metals -- EWC.12 includes wastes generated during the shaping, treating or finishing of metallic products, such as metal-working turnings, shavings, swarf, grinding dust, cuttings, slag, and residues from heat-treating, annealing or surface-treatment processes. These are considered non-hazardous metal waste unless they contain hazardous substances.
[EWC.13] Oil wastes and liquid fuel wastes -- EWC.13 includes waste generated from the production, handling, use or disposal of petroleum products and other liquid fuels. It covers used lubricating oils, waste gasoline, diesel, fuel oil, residues from oil refining and related contaminated water or sludges.
[EWC.14] Waste organic solvents -- EWC.14 refers to waste organic solvents, which are liquids or solids containing mainly organic compounds used for cleaning, degreasing or as reaction media. These wastes are often flammable, toxic or contain hazardous residues, so they must be collected, treated and disposed of according to strict environmental regulations.
[EWC.15] Waste packaging and absorbents -- EWC.15 covers waste generated from packaging materials and absorbent products used to contain, protect or clean other wastes. It includes used cardboard, plastic films, rubber gloves, sorbent pads and similar items that have become contaminated and are destined for treatment or disposal.

[EWC.17] Construction and demolition waste -- EWC.17 covers waste generated during the construction, renovation, repair and demolition of buildings, civil-engineering works and related structures. It includes materials such as concrete, bricks, wood, metal, glass and plastics and is generally considered non-hazardous unless contaminated with hazardous substances.
[EWC.18] Wastes from human and animal health care -- EWC 18 includes all wastes produced by human and animal health-care activities, such as hospitals, clinics, veterinary practices, laboratories and related services. It covers discarded tissues, sharps, contaminated materials, unused medicines and other biologically hazardous residues that require specialised handling, treatment and disposal to protect public health and the environment.
[EWC.19] Wastes from waste management facilities -- EWC 19 covers all residues that arise from the operation of waste-management installations, including material collected, sorted, treated, stored or disposed of at such facilities. Typical examples are incineration ash, landfill leachate, contaminated soils, sludge from treatment plants and residues from recycling or recovery processes. These wastes are classified separately because they originate from the handling of other waste streams rather than from primary production activities.
[EWC.20] Municipal wastes -- EWC 20 refers to municipal wastes - the mixed household and similar waste streams generated by residences, commercial premises and public services, including food scraps, paper, plastics, garden residues and similar non-hazardous materials. These wastes are typically managed through recycling, composting or disposal in landfills and are not subject to hazardous waste regulations.
