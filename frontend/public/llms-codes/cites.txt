[CITES] CITES Structure -- CITES is the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, an international treaty
[CITES.AM] Amphibia -- CITES.AM designates all species of amphibians that are covered by the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora. These species are listed in one or more of the CITES Appendices, meaning international trade in them is monitored, restricted, or prohibited to prevent over-exploitation and protect their survival.
[CITES.AV] Aves -- The CITES.AV entry groups all bird species (class Aves) that are regulated under the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora. Species of birds listed in any of the three CITES Appendices appear under this code, indicating that their international trade requires permits or is prohibited to safeguard conservation status.
[CITES.EN] Enforcement Mechanisms -- The CITES.EN entry outlines the legal and administrative tools used to ensure compliance with CITES, including inspection, seizure, penalties, and international cooperation. It details how parties conduct trade-monitoring, enforce permits, and prosecute violations to protect listed species.
[CITES.F] Fauna (Animals) -- CITES.F refers to the fauna section of the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species, which lists animal species in Appendices I, II and III. These entries define the level of protection and trade controls applied to each species to prevent over-exploitation and support conservation worldwide.
[CITES.FL] Flora (Plants) -- CITES.FL designates the plant component of the CITES Appendices, covering all flora listed for international trade regulation under the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species. Species of plants placed in Appendices I, II or III are included, with Appendix I affording the highest protection, Appendix II allowing controlled trade, and Appendix III permitting trade based on a cooperating country's request. This classification helps monitor and prevent over-exploitation of vulnerable plant species worldwide.
[CITES.I] Appendix I - Threatened with Extinction -- Appendix I of CITES enumerates species that are threatened with extinction and therefore receive the highest level of protection, restricting international commercial trade to exceptional cases such as scientific research, conservation or other non-commercial purposes.
[CITES.II] Appendix II - Trade Must Be Controlled -- CITES Appendix II includes species that are not currently threatened with extinction but could become so if their trade is not closely regulated. International commerce in these species is allowed only when it is demonstrated that the trade will not be detrimental to their survival, requiring export permits and ongoing monitoring.
[CITES.III] Appendix III - Protected by Party -- Appendix III of CITES includes species that a CITES Party has requested to be protected within its own jurisdiction, creating a cooperative framework where other parties must monitor and regulate trade of those species when they cross borders. Inclusion is voluntary and applies only to the species identified by the requesting Party.
[CITES.IN] Invertebrates -- The CITES IN entry groups all invertebrate species that appear in any of the three CITES Appendices, covering insects, mollusks, crustaceans and other non-vertebrate taxa. These species are subject to international trade controls to ensure that exporting, importing or commercial use does not threaten their survival in the wild.
[CITES.MA] Mammalia -- CITES.MA designates the class Mammalia within the CITES Appendices, covering all mammal species that appear in Appendices I, II, or III and the corresponding international trade restrictions. It serves as a taxonomic reference for enforcing CITES regulations and monitoring the legal trade of mammals worldwide.
[CITES.P1] Orchidaceae -- Orchidaceae, the orchid family, is listed in CITES Appendix I, the treaty's most restrictive tier. This classification means that commercial international trade in wild-collected orchids is prohibited except under exceptional circumstances such as scientific research, with both export and import requiring CITES permits. The listing aims to curb over-exploitation and preserve the biodiversity of this globally prized plant group.
[CITES.P2] Cactaceae -- Cactaceae, the cactus family, is listed in CITES Appendix II, meaning international trade is allowed but regulated to prevent over-exploitation. Exporting specimens requires a CITES export permit that verifies the plants were legally obtained and that the trade will not be detrimental to the species' survival. The listing aims to ensure sustainable use while protecting wild cactus populations.
[CITES.PE] Permit System -- CITES.PE denotes the permit system governing trade in species listed on the CITES Appendices. It requires that any import, export or re-export of listed wildlife be accompanied by an official CITES permit issued by the appropriate authority, confirming that the transaction does not threaten the species' survival. Non-compliance can lead to penalties and suspension of trade privileges.
[CITES.PI] Pisces (Fish) -- CITES.PI is the CITES Appendix code used for fish. It identifies any Pisces species listed in Appendix I, II or III, indicating that international trade in those species is subject to the permit and monitoring requirements of the Convention to prevent over-exploitation.
[CITES.RE] Reptilia -- The CITES RE entry groups all species of the class Reptilia-snakes, lizards, turtles, crocodilians and related reptiles-under the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species. It indicates that any listed reptile is subject to CITES trade regulations, with required permits or restrictions depending on its appendix listing.
