[IR] IRENA RE Technologies -- IR is the umbrella code in IRENA's Renewable Energy Technology Categories that designates the full set of renewable energy technologies, encompassing solar, wind, hydro, bioenergy, geothermal and emerging options. It is used to tag data, analyses and reports that address the entire spectrum of RE technologies rather than a specific sub-type.
[IR.01] Solar Photovoltaic -- Solar Photovoltaic (PV) is a technology that converts sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials, typically silicon, and includes utility-scale, commercial and residential installations. It is a core component of IRENA's Renewable Energy Technology Categories, supporting grid-connected and off-grid power generation worldwide.
[IR.02] Concentrated Solar Power -- IR.02 in the IRENA Renewable Energy Technology Categories refers to Concentrated Solar Power, a solar thermal technology that uses mirrors or lenses to focus sunlight onto a receiver, producing high-temperature heat that drives a fluid-powered turbine for electricity generation, often incorporating thermal storage to enable dispatchable power output.
[IR.03] Onshore Wind -- IR.03 under IRENA's Renewable Energy Technology Categories denotes onshore wind power systems installed on land, encompassing turbines that convert wind kinetic energy into electricity using fixed or variable-speed generators. [IR.04] Offshore Wind -- IR.04 Offshore Wind refers to the technology category for generating electricity from wind turbines located at sea or in coastal waters.
[IR.05] Hydropower (Large) -- IR.05 refers to large-scale hydropower, encompassing projects that typically exceed 10 MW of installed capacity and use dams or reservoirs to store and release water for electricity generation. These facilities harness the potential energy of flowing or stored water, often delivering baseload power with long operational lifetimes, and are classified separately from small- and micro-hydropower due to their greater scale, infrastructure complexity, and grid-integration impacts.
[IR.06] Small Hydropower -- IR.06 Small Hydropower covers low-head or run-of-the-river hydroelectric installations that generate electricity from water flows without large dams or reservoirs. These systems typically produce up to 10 MW, offering a flexible, low-impact renewable option for local energy supply and grid support.
[IR.07] Bioenergy (Solid) -- IR.07 Bioenergy (Solid) covers technologies that convert solid biomass-such as wood, agricultural residues, and organic waste-into heat, electricity, or solid fuels like pellets, charcoal and bio-char. These processes include direct combustion, gasification and pyrolysis and are used for residential heating, industrial power generation and sustainable energy supply in both small-scale and large-scale applications.
[IR.08] Biogas -- Biogas is a renewable energy carrier produced through anaerobic digestion of organic waste such as agricultural residues, municipal solid waste, and sewage sludge. It consists mainly of methane and carbon dioxide and can be used for electricity generation, heat production, or upgraded to biomethane for transport fuels and grid injection, offering a flexible solution for waste management and low-carbon energy supply.
[IR.09] Liquid Biofuels -- IR.09 covers technologies that convert biomass into liquid fuels such as biodiesel, bioethanol, renewable diesel and other bio-based transport fuels. These processes use feedstocks like agricultural residues, energy crops, waste oils or algae and aim to replace fossil diesel and gasoline with lower-carbon alternatives for mobility and heating. The category groups production methods, feedstock supply chains and end-use applications within IRENA's renewable energy technology framework.
[IR.10] Geothermal Power -- IR.10 designates geothermal power, the technology that extracts heat from the Earth's interior to produce electricity or provide direct heating. It utilizes naturally occurring steam or hot water from underground reservoirs, employing dry-steam, flash-steam, or binary-cycle systems to convert thermal energy into usable power.
[IR.11] Geothermal Direct Use -- IR.11 - Geothermal Direct Use refers to the exploitation of low- to moderate-temperature geothermal resources for heating, cooling and industrial processes without converting the heat to electricity, encompassing applications such as space heating, greenhouse heating, aquaculture, desalination, spa facilities and district heating systems.
[IR.12] Marine Energy (Tidal/Wave) -- IR.12 refers to marine energy technologies that convert tidal and wave motion into electricity, encompassing tidal barrages, tidal stream turbines, oscillating water columns and wave energy converters, as well as their development, deployment and grid integration.
[IR.13] Solar Heating and Cooling -- IR.13 Solar Heating and Cooling covers technologies that capture sunlight to provide space heating, water heating and cooling, typically through solar thermal collectors, absorption chillers or desiccant systems. These applications are used in residential, commercial and industrial buildings to reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse-gas emissions.
[IR.14] Renewable Hydrogen -- Renewable hydrogen refers to hydrogen generated through water electrolysis powered by renewable electricity such as solar, wind or hydro, resulting in a low-carbon fuel that can be stored, transported and used for power generation, industrial processes, and transport applications, thereby supporting energy system decarbonisation.

[IR.16] Pumped Hydro Storage -- Pumped hydro storage uses two water reservoirs at different elevations; surplus electricity powers pumps that move water to the upper reservoir, and when demand rises the water is released downhill through turbines to generate power. This mature technology provides large-scale, long-duration storage, rapid response, and grid-balancing services.
