[OR] Orphanet Categories -- OR is the top-level node in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification, representing the umbrella "Orphanet Categories" that groups all subsequent disease groups and subgroups within the system. It serves as the root of the hierarchical taxonomy used to organize and index rare diseases for clinicians, researchers and policy makers.
[OR.01] Rare Neurological Diseases -- OR.01 is the top-level category in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification that groups all rare neurological diseases, which are disorders of the nervous system with a prevalence of fewer than 5 cases per 10,000 people. It encompasses hereditary, developmental, neurodegenerative, demyelinating and other low-frequency conditions affecting the brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves and muscle control. This code serves as a primary reference for clinicians and researchers seeking information on rare neurological conditions.
[OR.02] Rare Genetic Diseases -- OR.02 is the Orphanet category that groups all rare genetic diseases, encompassing inherited disorders caused by single-gene mutations, chromosomal abnormalities, or mitochondrial defects. It provides a framework for clinicians and researchers to identify, classify, and compare these low-prevalence hereditary conditions worldwide.
[OR.03] Rare Oncological Diseases -- OR.03 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification groups rare oncological diseases, defined as cancers affecting fewer than 1-2,000 individuals. It includes uncommon sarcomas, pediatric malignancies, and other infrequent tumor types that often lack extensive research and approved treatments.
[OR.04] Rare Immunological Diseases -- OR.04 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification (Skeleton) groups rare immunological diseases, encompassing inherited and acquired disorders that affect the immune system. It includes primary immunodeficiencies, autoimmune and autoinflammatory conditions, and other immune-dysregulation syndromes that are each individually uncommon but collectively significant for rare-disease research and care.
[OR.05] Rare Hematological Diseases -- OR.05 groups rare hematological diseases, encompassing disorders of blood cells, coagulation, and the bone-marrow microenvironment. It includes conditions such as inherited anemias, platelet function defects, immune-mediated cytopenias, and bone-marrow failure syndromes that affect less than five per 10,000 individuals.
[OR.06] Rare Cardiac Diseases -- OR.06 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification groups together rare cardiac diseases, encompassing congenital, structural, and functional heart conditions that affect fewer than 5 per 10,000 individuals. The category includes inherited cardiomyopathies, channelopathies, vascular anomalies, and other uncommon disorders of cardiac tissue and conduction. It provides a framework for clinical research, epidemiology, and health-policy planning specific to uncommon heart diseases.
[OR.07] Rare Hepatological Diseases -- OR.07 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification designates the group of rare hepatological diseases, covering uncommon genetic, metabolic, infectious, inflammatory and structural disorders that primarily affect the liver. The category is used to organize clinical information, research data and health-policy resources for these low-prevalence liver conditions.
[OR.08] Rare Respiratory Diseases -- OR.08 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification groups rare respiratory diseases, encompassing inherited and sporadic conditions that primarily affect the airways, lung parenchyma, pleura, or respiratory muscles. The category includes disorders such as cystic fibrosis, primary ciliary dyskinesia, pulmonary alveolar proteinosis, and rare interstitial lung diseases, all characterized by low prevalence and often requiring specialized diagnosis and management.
[OR.09] Rare Renal Diseases -- OR.09 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification aggregates rare renal diseases, covering hereditary and congenital kidney disorders such as cystic kidney disease, tubular dysfunctions, glomerulopathies, and renal malformations that affect kidney structure or function and have a prevalence of fewer than five cases per 10 000 individuals.
[OR.10] Rare Endocrine Diseases -- OR.10 groups rare endocrine diseases, covering inherited and acquired disorders that disrupt hormone synthesis, secretion or action, as well as structural abnormalities of endocrine glands. It includes conditions such as congenital adrenal hyperplasia, rare pituitary adenomas, and genetic syndromes affecting thyroid, pancreas, gonads or growth hormone pathways. This category helps clinicians and researchers identify and study uncommon endocrine pathologies.

[OR.12] Rare Skin Diseases -- OR.12 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification groups rare skin diseases, covering inherited and acquired dermatological disorders with low prevalence such as ichthyoses, epidermolysis bullosa and other genodermatoses. These conditions are characterized by abnormal skin structure or function and often have a genetic basis.
[OR.13] Rare Metabolic Diseases -- OR.13 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification groups rare metabolic diseases, which are inherited disorders that disrupt normal biochemical pathways, often due to enzyme deficiencies or transport defects. These conditions lead to accumulation or deficiency of metabolites and can affect multiple organ systems.
[OR.14] Rare Eye Diseases -- OR.14 groups together rare eye diseases, encompassing a variety of uncommon ocular conditions that affect vision, eye structure, or ocular function. It serves as a high-level category in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification for epidemiological reporting, research coordination, and health-policy planning of visual disorders that occur in small patient populations.
[OR.15] Rare Infectious Diseases -- OR.15 in the Orphanet Rare Disease Classification (Skeleton) groups low-prevalence infectious diseases, covering bacterial, viral, fungal and parasitic infections that affect fewer than about 1 in 2 000 people worldwide. The category organizes clinical, epidemiological and genetic data for these exceptionally uncommon conditions.
