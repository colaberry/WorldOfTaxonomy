[CS] CMS Star Domains -- CS refers to the set of performance domains that the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services uses to calculate Medicare Star Ratings for health plans. The domains cover areas such as clinical quality, member experience, access to care, plan effectiveness, and safety, each contributing to a composite star score that rates plans from one to five stars. This framework helps beneficiaries compare plan quality and informs payment adjustments.
[CS.01] Staying Healthy: Screenings -- CS.01 outlines the preventive health screenings that Medicare Advantage plans must offer and track, such as cancer, cardiovascular, diabetes, and immunization checks. It specifies the recommended services, timing, and evidence-based criteria used to assess plan performance and contribute to the overall star rating.
[CS.02] Managing Chronic Conditions -- CS.02 focuses on how Medicare Advantage plans support enrollee care for chronic diseases, measuring the effectiveness of care coordination, medication management, and health outcomes. The rating reflects the plan's ability to monitor conditions, provide personalized care plans, and reduce hospitalizations, influencing overall star scores.
[CS.03] Member Experience (CAHPS) -- CS.03 evaluates a Medicare Advantage plan's member experience through the CAHPS survey, capturing beneficiaries' views on communication, access to care and overall satisfaction. The standardized questionnaire scores feed into the plan's overall-Star Rating.
[CS.04] Member Complaints -- The CS.04 entry in the CMS Medicare Star Rating System records member complaints, tallying the number and seriousness of grievances filed by beneficiaries about their Medicare Advantage or Part D plans. These data are used to assess plan responsiveness and quality of service, influencing the overall star rating awarded by CMS.
[CS.05] Health Plan Customer Service -- The CS.05 rating evaluates a Medicare Advantage or Part D plan's customer service by measuring how quickly and effectively members' calls and inquiries are answered, the resolution of complaints, and overall satisfaction from member surveys. Results contribute to the plan's overall star rating, influencing bonus payments and enrollment decisions.
[CS.06] Drug Plan Customer Service -- CS.06 evaluates how well Medicare prescription drug plans serve their members, measuring satisfaction with call-center accessibility, clarity of information, timeliness of issue resolution, and overall helpfulness of customer-service representatives.
[CS.07] Drug Safety and Accuracy -- CS.07 evaluates how well Medicare prescription drug plans protect beneficiaries from medication errors and adverse events, measuring processes such as verification of drug eligibility, monitoring of high-risk medications, and accuracy of dosing information. Higher scores indicate stronger safeguards and fewer safety incidents in the plan's pharmacy network.
[CS.08] Drug Plan Cost/Coverage -- The CMS Medicare Star Rating System code CS.08 evaluates a prescription drug plan's cost-sharing structure and the scope of its formulary coverage, assessing member out-of-pocket expenses and the breadth of drugs covered. This metric helps beneficiaries compare plan affordability and therapeutic options when choosing Medicare Part D coverage.

[CS.10] Overall Star Rating (1-5) -- The Overall Star Rating compiles a Medicare Advantage plan's performance across quality, safety, patient experience and other metrics into a single score ranging from one to five stars. The rating is used by CMS to compare plans, guide beneficiary choice and determine incentive payments, with higher-rated plans receiving greater benefits and enrollment bonuses.
[CS.11] Part C Summary (1-5) -- The CMS Medicare Star Rating System assigns each Medicare Advantage (Part C) plan a composite score from one to five stars, reflecting overall quality and performance across clinical, member experience, and safety measures. CS.11 provides the summarized star rating that beneficiaries and regulators use to compare plans.
[CS.12] Part D Summary (1-5) -- CS.12 in the CMS Medicare Star Rating System refers to the Part D Summary, which presents the overall performance rating of a Medicare prescription-drug plan on a scale from one to five stars, reflecting measures such as member satisfaction, safety, and drug-pricing accuracy.
