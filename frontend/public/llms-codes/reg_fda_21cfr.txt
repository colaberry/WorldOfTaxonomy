[biologics] Biologics Regulations -- The biologics section of Title 21 CFR governs the development, licensing, manufacturing, labeling, and distribution of biological products such as vaccines, blood components, and gene therapies, outlining requirements for safety, efficacy, and quality control to protect public health.
[devices] Medical Device Regulations -- The devices section of Title 21 of the Code of Federal Regulations holds the rules that govern medical devices, covering how they are classified, the requirements for labeling and performance standards, the pre-market approval and clearance pathways, and the post-market reporting and enforcement provisions that ensure safety and effectiveness.
[drugs] Drug Regulations -- The "drugs" portion of Title 21 of the Code of Federal Regulations sets out the FDA's rules for the approval, labeling, manufacturing, and distribution of pharmaceutical products in the United States. It details requirements for new drug applications, good manufacturing practices, and post-market surveillance to protect safety and efficacy.
[electronic] Electronic Records and Signatures -- The electronic records and signatures section of Title 21 CFR sets the standards for using computer-generated records and electronic signatures in FDA-regulated activities. It defines requirements for authenticity, integrity, confidentiality, validation, audit trails and signature controls to ensure that electronic data are reliable and compliant with the law.
[food] Food Safety Regulations -- Food safety regulations under Title 21 of the Code of Federal Regulations set standards for production, processing, packaging, and labeling of foods to protect public health. They include requirements for sanitary practices, hazard analysis, preventive controls, and compliance inspections conducted by the FDA.
[part_11] Part 11 - Electronic Records; Electronic Signatures -- Part 11 sets the FDA's criteria for electronic records and electronic signatures, requiring that they be trustworthy, reliable, and retainable in the same manner as paper documents. It applies to any FDA-regulated entity that creates, modifies, maintains, or transmits electronic data in support of compliance.
[part_110] Part 110 - Current Good Manufacturing Practice (Food) -- Part 110 of Title 21 CFR sets the minimum requirements for current good manufacturing practice in the food industry, covering sanitation, equipment design, facility construction, processing controls, and recordkeeping to ensure that foods are produced under sanitary conditions and are safe for consumers.
[part_117] Part 117 - Current Good Manufacturing Practice (FSMA) -- Part 117 sets the current good manufacturing practice rules under the Food Safety Modernization Act, establishing preventive controls, hazard analysis, sanitation, and record-keeping requirements for facilities that manufacture, process, pack or hold food, to ensure a consistent level of safety and compliance across the food supply chain.
[part_11_records] Part 11 Subpart B - Electronic Records -- Part 11 Subpart B establishes the FDA's requirements for electronic records used in clinical trials, manufacturing, and other regulated activities, mandating that such records be trustworthy, reliable, and equivalent to paper documentation. It defines controls for system validation, access authorization, audit trails, record retention, and the ability to generate accurate, permanent copies for review.
[part_11_scope] Part 11 Subpart A - General Provisions -- Part 11 Subpart A provides the general provisions for the FDA's electronic records and signatures requirements. It defines the scope, applicability, and basic obligations such as system validation, audit trails, and user authentication, establishing the foundation for compliance with electronic-record standards.

[part_120] Part 120 - Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) for Juice -- Part 120 of Title 21 of the CFR establishes the HACCP system for juice processors, requiring them to conduct a hazard analysis, identify critical control points, implement monitoring procedures, adopt corrective actions, and keep appropriate records. The rule applies to manufacturers of juice and juice-based beverages to ensure product safety under federal food safety regulations.
[part_210] Part 210 - Current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) in Manufacturing -- Part 210 outlines the minimum current Good Manufacturing Practice requirements for drug manufacturing, processing, packing, and holding, ensuring products meet safety and quality standards. It details regulations on personnel, facilities, equipment, production and process controls, documentation, and quality-assurance systems.
[part_211] Part 211 - cGMP for Finished Pharmaceuticals -- Part 211 sets forth the current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) requirements for finished pharmaceuticals, detailing standards for quality control, documentation, sanitation, equipment, and personnel to ensure that drug products are consistently produced and meet safety and efficacy criteria.
[part_312] Part 312 - Investigational New Drug (IND) Application -- Part 312 of Title 21 CFR sets the requirements for filing an Investigational New Drug (IND) application, which allows a sponsor to begin clinical trials of a new drug in the United States. It outlines the information needed on safety, manufacturing, and study protocols, the categories of INDs, and the FDA's responsibilities for review, approval, and ongoing oversight.
[part_314] Part 314 - New Drug Application (NDA) -- Part 314 of Title 21 CFR governs the New Drug Application process, specifying the content, format and evidentiary standards required for manufacturers to obtain FDA approval of a drug. It details submission procedures, review timelines, labeling requirements, safety and efficacy data expectations, and post-approval obligations such as reporting and inspections.
[part_320] Part 320 - Bioavailability and Bioequivalence -- Part 320 sets the requirements for demonstrating bioavailability and bioequivalence of generic drug products, outlining study design, statistical analysis, and acceptance criteria. It guides sponsors in conducting comparative clinical pharmacology studies to ensure therapeutic equivalence with reference-listed drugs.
[part_58] Part 58 - Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) -- Part 58 of Title 21 CFR sets the Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) standards for non-clinical health and safety testing of pharmaceuticals, medical devices, cosmetics and related products, detailing requirements for organization, personnel, facilities, equipment, test procedures, documentation, quality assurance and recordkeeping to ensure data integrity and reliability.
[part_600] Part 600 - Biological Products: General -- Part 600 sets out the general regulatory framework for biological products, defining biologics, establishing licensing requirements, and outlining standards for manufacturing, labeling, and record-keeping. It provides the basic provisions that govern the safety, purity, and potency of vaccines, blood products, and other biologically derived therapies under FDA oversight.
[part_601] Part 601 - Licensing (BLA) -- Part 601 of Title 21 of the Code of Federal Regulations outlines the FDA's licensing process for biologics, detailing the submission, review, and approval of Biologics License Applications. It specifies the requirements for product safety, efficacy, labeling, and post-approval reporting, and sets the procedural rules for granting, amending, or revoking biologics licenses.
[part_610] Part 610 - General Biological Products Standards -- Part 610 sets the fundamental standards for biological products, detailing definitions, licensing, labeling, and manufacturing requirements to protect safety and effectiveness. It governs vaccines, blood components, and other biologics, specifying the duties of manufacturers and the FDA's oversight procedures.
[part_807] Part 807 - Establishment Registration and Device Listing -- Part 807 requires manufacturers and initial distributors of medical devices to register their establishments with the FDA and to list each device they market, providing details such as intended use, classification, and ownership. The rule sets procedures for registration, annual updates, and penalties for non-compliance, ensuring traceability and regulatory oversight.
[part_812] Part 812 - Investigational Device Exemptions (IDE) -- Part 812 of Title 21 CFR governs Investigational Device Exemptions, allowing manufacturers to conduct clinical studies on medical devices that have not yet received marketing approval. It sets requirements for IDE applications, sponsor responsibilities, monitoring, and reporting to ensure participant safety and data integrity during the investigational phase.
[part_814] Part 814 - Premarket Approval (PMA) -- Part 814 of Title 21 CFR outlines the Premarket Approval (PMA) pathway for high-risk medical devices, detailing the data, testing, and clinical evidence manufacturers must submit for FDA review. It describes the application components, review timelines, and criteria for approval, denial, or request for additional information, establishing the regulatory framework for devices that require the most rigorous premarket evaluation.
[part_820] Part 820 - Quality System Regulation (QSR) -- Part 820 of Title 21 CFR sets the Quality System Regulation that FDA requires for medical device manufacturers. It outlines mandatory practices for design controls, document management, production processes, corrective actions and recordkeeping to ensure devices are safe and effective. Compliance with these rules is essential for market approval and ongoing regulatory oversight.

[part_890] Part 890 - Physical Medicine Devices -- Part 890 of Title 21 CFR sets the regulatory framework for physical-medicine devices, including therapeutic ultrasound, electromagnetic and light-therapy equipment, and transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulators. It establishes device classifications, labeling requirements, and performance standards to ensure safety and effectiveness. The part also outlines pre-market notification and exemption criteria for these medical devices.
[quality] Quality System Regulations -- The Quality System Regulations (QSR) are the FDA's mandatory requirements for medical device manufacturers, outlined in 21 CFR 820. They establish standards for design controls, production processes, record-keeping, and corrective actions to ensure that devices are safe and effective throughout their lifecycle. Compliance with the QSR is essential for obtaining and maintaining market approval in the United States.
