[annex_i] Annex I - Essential Sectors (energy, transport, banking, health, etc.) -- Annex I of the EU NIS2 Directive identifies the essential sectors whose operators must meet heightened cybersecurity requirements, including energy, transport, banking, health, digital infrastructure, public administration and other critical services, thereby defining the scope of the regulation's protective obligations.
[annex_ii] Annex II - Important Sectors (postal, waste, food, digital providers, etc.) -- Annex II of the EU NIS2 Directive (2022/2555) defines the "important sectors" that must meet enhanced cybersecurity obligations, covering areas such as postal services, waste management, food supply, digital service providers and other critical economic activities. These sectors are identified because a disruption could significantly affect the functioning of society and the economy, prompting stricter risk-management and incident-reporting requirements.
[art_10] Art 10 - CSIRTs -- Article 10 of the EU Directive 2022/2555 requires each Member State to designate one or more Computer Security Incident Response Teams (CSIRTs) to handle network and information-system incidents, promote cooperation among CSIRTs, and provide assistance to operators of essential services and digital service providers.
[art_14] Art 14 - EU-CyCLONe -- Article 14 of the EU Directive on Security of Network and Information Systems creates the EU-CyCLONe framework, a coordinated European capability to support Member States in responding to large-scale cyber incidents and threats, enabling rapid information exchange, joint operational support and the pooling of technical expertise across national authorities.
[art_15] Art 15 - Cooperation Group -- Article 15 creates the EU Cooperation Group, a forum where member states, the European Commission and ENISA cooperate to coordinate the implementation of the NIS2 Directive, share best practices, and advise on risk management, incident response and cybersecurity policy.
[art_2] Art 2 - Scope (essential and important entities) -- Article 2 sets out the scope of the NIS 2 Directive by distinguishing essential entities, which include sectors such as health, energy, transport and digital services, from important entities that have a significant but less critical role, and defines the security and reporting obligations each group must fulfill.
[art_20] Art 20 - Governance -- Article 20 of the EU Directive 2022/2555 outlines governance obligations for essential and important entities, requiring them to adopt appropriate organizational structures, risk-based policies and procedures, and to assign clear responsibilities for managing network and information-system security. It also mandates regular reporting to competent authorities and the implementation of internal controls to ensure continuous compliance with the directive's security requirements.
[art_21] Art 21 - Cybersecurity risk-management measures -- Article 21 of the EU NIS2 Directive requires essential and important entities to adopt cybersecurity risk-management measures, including the identification, assessment and mitigation of risks to their network and information systems, the implementation of appropriate technical and organizational safeguards, and regular reviews of their security posture.
[art_23] Art 23 - Reporting obligations -- Article 23 requires operators of essential and important entities to notify the competent authority of incidents that significantly affect service continuity, typically within 24 hours of becoming aware of the event. The report must include details on the nature of the incident, its impact, the measures taken to contain it and any follow-up actions planned.
[art_26] Art 26 - Jurisdiction and territoriality -- Article 26 outlines the rules determining which Member State has jurisdiction over incidents affecting network and information systems, establishing that the competent authority of the Member State where the affected entity is established or where the impact is felt may act, and clarifies cross-border cooperation.
[art_29] Art 29 - Cybersecurity information-sharing arrangements -- Article 29 of the EU Directive on the security of network and information systems establishes a framework for cybersecurity information-sharing arrangements, outlining how public authorities, private operators and computer-security incident response teams may exchange threat data, under confidentiality safeguards and reporting obligations, to improve overall cyber resilience.
[art_3] Art 3 - Essential and important entities -- Article 3 of the EU NIS 2 Directive sets out the criteria for designating essential and important entities, distinguishing between sectors that are critical to societal and economic functions and those with a significant but less critical role. It specifies the obligations each category must meet regarding risk management, incident reporting and supervisory oversight.
[art_31] Art 31 - General supervision for essential entities -- Article 31 establishes the general supervision framework for essential entities under the EU NIS Directive, obliging competent authorities to monitor compliance, conduct inspections and enforce corrective actions. It defines the powers, procedures and coordination mechanisms that supervisors must use to ensure the security of network and information systems.
[art_32] Art 32 - Enforcement measures for essential entities -- Article 32 of the EU Directive 2022/2555 establishes the enforcement powers that national authorities may use against essential entities that do not meet NIS2 security obligations, allowing inspections, orders to remediate, and the imposition of penalties. It also outlines the procedural steps for monitoring compliance and applying sanctions to protect the resilience of critical services.
[art_34] Art 34 - Penalties -- Article 34 of the EU Directive 2022/2555 establishes the penalty framework for breaches of the NIS2 security obligations, specifying administrative fines that reflect the gravity and impact of non-compliance and allowing national authorities to impose additional measures, including possible criminal liability for especially serious violations.
[art_7] Art 7 - National cybersecurity strategy -- Article 7 obliges EU Member States to adopt and regularly update a national cybersecurity strategy that outlines objectives, risk-management measures, incident-response capabilities and governance structures. The strategy must be aligned with the EU NIS framework and reviewed at least every two years.
[art_8] Art 8 - Competent authorities and CSIRTs -- Art 8 requires each Member State to designate competent authorities and one or more Computer Security Incident Response Teams (CSIRTs) to oversee the implementation of the NIS 2 measures, to monitor compliance and to handle incident reporting. It specifies the tasks, cooperation mechanisms and resource needs of these bodies, ensuring a coordinated national response to cybersecurity threats.
[ch_1] Chapter I - General Provisions -- Chapter I establishes the purpose and scope of the EU NIS 2 Directive, defining key terms such as essential and important entities, operators of essential services and digital service providers. It outlines the overall objectives to enhance cybersecurity across the Union and sets the legal basis for coordinated risk management, incident reporting and supervisory cooperation among Member States.
[ch_2] Chapter II - Coordinated Cybersecurity Frameworks -- Chapter II of the EU Directive 2022/2555 on Security of Network and Information Systems sets out coordinated cybersecurity frameworks. It requires member states to adopt national strategies, appoint competent authorities, and strengthen cross-border cooperation to protect critical infrastructure.
[ch_3] Chapter III - Cooperation -- Chapter III of the EU Directive 2022/2555 establishes the cooperation framework among Member States, defining the duties of the Cooperation Group, the network of CSIRTs, and procedures for sharing information, coordinating incident response, and developing joint cybersecurity strategies across the Union.
[ch_4] Chapter IV - Cybersecurity Risk-Management and Reporting Obligations -- Chapter IV of the EU Directive 2022/2555 sets out mandatory cybersecurity risk-management and reporting duties for essential and digital service entities, requiring them to assess threats, implement appropriate technical and organisational safeguards, and promptly notify competent authorities of incidents. It also obliges member states to designate national authorities, create incident-handling teams, and ensure a coordinated EU-wide response framework.
[ch_5] Chapter V - Jurisdiction and Registration -- Chapter V sets out the jurisdictional reach of the NIS2 framework and the mandatory registration of operators of essential and important digital services, specifying which entities fall under the rules, the territories in which they must comply and the procedures for notifying national competent authorities. It also defines the coordination mechanisms between Member States to ensure consistent oversight and enforcement across the Union.
