[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- ISO 28000:2022 clause 10.1 defines how an organization must identify any nonconformity within its security management system, evaluate its cause and impact, and implement corrective action to eliminate the issue and prevent recurrence, with appropriate records and review of effectiveness.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Continual improvement in ISO 28000:2022 requires organizations to systematically enhance their security management system by monitoring performance, analyzing incidents and audit results, and implementing corrective actions. The process encourages proactive risk assessment, adoption of best practices and the use of measurable objectives to drive ongoing resilience and effectiveness.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- ISO 28000 2022 requires the organization to determine internal and external issues that affect its security and resilience objectives, identify interested parties and their needs, and define the scope of the security management system. This understanding informs risk assessment, policy development and appropriate controls.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 of ISO 28000:2022 requires an organization to identify all interested parties relevant to its security and resilience objectives, determine their needs and expectations, and assess how these influence the security management system. This analysis ensures that the system is designed to meet stakeholder requirements and supports continual improvement of security performance.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the security management system -- ISO 28000:2022 clause 4.3 requires an organization to define the boundaries and applicability of its security management system, identifying relevant sites, activities, products, services, and interested parties. This establishes what will be covered, excluded and the context for security objectives and controls, ensuring the system is tailored to the organization's risk profile and operational environment.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- It requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment for the security-management system by establishing a security policy, setting objectives, allocating resources, and integrating security into the organization's strategic direction. This engagement ensures accountability, promotes a culture of security, and drives continual improvement across all relevant processes.
[5_2] 5.2 Security management policy -- ISO 28000:2022 clause 5.2 requires an organization to define, document, and maintain a security management policy that reflects its commitment to protecting assets, people, and information against security threats. The policy must be aligned with the organization's strategic objectives, communicated to all relevant parties, and periodically reviewed for effectiveness.
[5_3] 5.3 Roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 of ISO 28000:2022 requires an organization to define, document and communicate the specific roles, responsibilities and authorities needed for its security management system, ensuring that each function knows its duties and has the authority to act. Top management must ensure these assignments are understood across the organization and that any changes are promptly updated and disseminated.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 requires the organization to identify security risks and opportunities, determine appropriate actions to mitigate those risks and capitalize on the opportunities, and integrate those actions into its security management system. The actions must be documented, implemented, monitored and periodically reviewed for effectiveness and continual improvement.
[6_2] 6.2 Security objectives and plans to achieve them -- ISO 28000:2022 clause 6.2 requires an organization to set clear, measurable security objectives that are consistent with its security policy and risk assessment, and to develop documented plans outlining the actions, resources, responsibilities and timelines needed to achieve those objectives. The plans must be integrated into the overall management system and reviewed regularly to ensure effectiveness and continual improvement.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 28000:2022 requires an organization to establish, implement and maintain processes for planning and controlling security activities, ensuring they align with the security policy and objectives. It directs the definition of security requirements, allocation of resources, documentation of procedures, and monitoring of performance to achieve consistent, effective protection.
[8_2] 8.2 Security risk assessment -- Section 8.2 requires organizations to systematically identify, analyze and evaluate security threats, vulnerabilities and associated consequences that could affect their objectives, determining likelihood and impact to produce a documented risk assessment that forms the basis for selecting appropriate security controls.
[8_3] 8.3 Security risk treatment -- Section 8.3 of ISO 28000:2022 outlines the process for treating security risks, requiring organizations to select appropriate controls and mitigation measures based on the risk assessment. It directs the implementation, documentation and monitoring of these treatments to reduce risk to acceptable levels while ensuring alignment with the overall security management objectives.

[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the performance of their security management system, ensuring that security objectives are achieved, compliance is maintained, and opportunities for continual improvement are identified.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 requires the organization to plan and carry out internal audits of its security management system at defined intervals, using competent, independent auditors to assess compliance with ISO 28000 requirements, evaluate the system's effectiveness, report findings, and ensure corrective actions are taken.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 of ISO 28000:2022 requires top management to conduct periodic reviews of the security-management system, confirming its continued suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must assess performance data, audit findings, nonconformities, corrective actions and any opportunities for improvement.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 in ISO 28000:2022 outlines the requirements for continual improvement of a security management system, directing organizations to monitor performance, identify non-conformities, implement corrective actions, and pursue opportunities for enhancing security and resilience. It emphasizes systematic review, corrective-preventive actions, and the integration of lessons learned to ensure the system evolves in response to changing risks and stakeholder expectations.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 in ISO 28000:2022 requires an organization to determine the internal and external factors that affect its security and resilience objectives, identify relevant interested parties and their needs, and define the scope of its security management system, establishing the contextual basis for all subsequent requirements.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 outlines the responsibilities of top-management in establishing, implementing and maintaining the security management system. It requires leadership to define the security policy, assign roles, ensure resources, and promote a culture of continual improvement and risk awareness.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 28000:2022 specifies the planning requirements for a security management system, directing organizations to assess security risks, set measurable security objectives, and determine the processes, resources, and responsibilities needed to achieve those objectives while aligning with overall business goals.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 28000:2022 outlines the support requirements for a security management system, detailing necessary resources, competence and awareness of personnel, internal and external communication processes, and the creation and control of documented information essential to implement and maintain effective security and resilience measures.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 28000:2022 defines the operational requirements for a security management system. It specifies how organizations must plan, implement, control and monitor security processes, including risk assessment, resource allocation, incident response and continual improvement to ensure the resilience of supply-chain activities.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 outlines the requirements for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the effectiveness of the security management system, covering internal audits, management review and continual improvement. It ensures that organizations gather relevant performance data, assess compliance with security objectives, and implement corrective actions to maintain and enhance resilience.
