[cip_003] CIP-003 - Security Management Controls -- CIP-003 defines the security management controls that bulk electric system owners and operators must implement, requiring a documented security program, defined policies and procedures, and assigned responsibilities. It also mandates risk assessments, continuous monitoring, and corrective actions to protect critical cyber assets and ensure compliance.

[cip_004] CIP-004 - Personnel and Training -- CIP-004 addresses the security awareness and training requirements for personnel who access, operate, or manage critical electric infrastructure. [cip_004_r1] R1 - Security Awareness Program -- The NERC CIP-004 R1 requirement mandates that owners and operators of bulk-power facilities develop and maintain a security awareness program for all personnel who have access to critical cyber assets. [cip_004_r2] R2 - Training Program -- The NERC CIP-004 Revision 2 training program requirement defines how owners, operators and other relevant personnel must be educated on cyber security responsibilities for bulk-power systems. It mandates initial, refresher and specialized training, documentation of attendance and qualifications, and regular assessment to confirm that staff understand and can apply the protection standards.
[cip_004_r3] R3 - Personnel Risk Assessment -- R3 requires entities to evaluate the risk each individual poses to bulk electric reliability, covering employees, contractors, and other authorized personnel. The assessment must consider factors such as background, access level, job function, and any known security concerns, and it must be documented, reviewed regularly, and updated when circumstances change.
[cip_004_r4] R4 - Access Management Program -- The NERC CIP-004 R4 Access Management Program requirement establishes a documented program for controlling both electronic and physical access to Critical Cyber Assets. It mandates policies, procedures, and assigned responsibilities to authorize, monitor, and review access, ensuring that only qualified personnel can reach the assets.

[cip_006] CIP-006 - Physical Security of BES Cyber Systems -- CIP-006 defines the requirements for protecting the physical security of Bulk-Electric-System (BES) cyber assets, specifying controls to prevent unauthorized access, tampering, or damage to equipment and facilities that house critical cyber systems. [cip_006_r3] R3 - Physical Access Control System Maintenance -- R3 requires entities to establish documented procedures for maintaining physical access control systems that protect bulk electric system facilities. [cip_007] CIP-007 - System Security Management -- CIP-007 sets out the NERC requirements for managing the security of cyber assets that support the bulk electric system. [cip_007_r1] R1 - Ports and Services -- R1 defines the processes for identifying, documenting and securing network ports and services used by critical cyber assets, ensuring only authorized ports are open and unnecessary services are disabled to reduce exposure to cyber-threats. [cip_007_r2] R2 - Security Patch Management -- CIP-007 R2 requires owners of bulk electric system cyber assets to implement a formal security patch management program. The program must inventory hardware and software, evaluate vulnerability information, test and apply patches within defined timeframes, and retain documentation of all actions for compliance verification.
[cip_007_r3] R3 - Malicious Code Prevention -- The R3 requirement under NERC CIP Standard 007 mandates that registered entities develop and enforce a documented malicious code prevention program, including anti-virus controls, regular updates, and procedures for detection, containment, and removal of malware across all critical cyber assets. It also calls for periodic testing, monitoring of compliance, and training of personnel to ensure effective protection against malicious software.
[cip_007_r4] R4 - Security Event Monitoring -- The CIP-007 R4 requirement mandates that owners of critical cyber assets implement continuous security event monitoring, maintain detailed logs of identified incidents, and retain those records for the period specified by the standard. It also calls for documented procedures to analyze, respond to, and report security events to appropriate authorities.
[cip_007_r5] R5 - System Access Controls -- CIP-007 R5 requires entities to implement and maintain logical access controls for all cyber assets, ensuring that only authorized personnel can access systems and that access rights are reviewed regularly. It mandates documented procedures for account creation, authentication, privilege management and timely removal of access when it is no longer needed.

The standard also mandates documentation of incidents, post-incident analysis, and corrective actions to improve overall system resilience.

[cip_009_r1] R1 - Recovery Plans -- The R1 Recovery Plans requirement in NERC CIP-009 mandates that responsible entities develop, maintain and regularly test documented recovery plans to restore the operation of critical cyber assets after a disruption. [cip_009_r3] R3 - Recovery Plan Review and Update -- The R3 requirement in NERC CIP-009 obligates entities to periodically review and update their recovery plans to keep them current with system changes and operational lessons learned.
