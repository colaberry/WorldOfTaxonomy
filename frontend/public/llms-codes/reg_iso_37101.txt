[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- ISO 37101:2016 specifies that organizations must identify, document, and address any nonconformities in their community sustainability management system. The standard requires corrective actions to be taken, recorded and reviewed to prevent recurrence, with responsibilities and timelines defined.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 37101:2016 requires a community-focused organization to establish and maintain processes that drive continual improvement of its sustainable development management system, using performance data, corrective actions and identified opportunities. It emphasizes regular review, monitoring and the adoption of enhancements to better achieve the community's sustainability objectives.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the community and its context -- Entry 4.1 stresses that a community must first be accurately profiled, including its demographic, economic, cultural, environmental and governance characteristics, as well as the needs, expectations and capabilities of its stakeholders. This understanding forms the basis for setting objectives, planning actions and measuring progress within a sustainable-development management system.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- The clause requires organizations to determine who the interested parties are-such as residents, local authorities, businesses, NGOs and service users-and to gather, analyze and keep up-to-date information on their needs, expectations and any relevant requirements. This understanding must be documented and used to shape the community's sustainable-development objectives and the management system's planning and performance.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the management system -- ISO 37101:2016 clause 4.3 requires an organization to define the scope of its sustainable-development management system, specifying the geographical, functional and stakeholder boundaries, the activities and locations covered, and any exclusions. This ensures the system's objectives, processes and performance indicators are applied consistently to the relevant community context.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 of ISO 37101:2016 requires top management of a community organization to demonstrate leadership and commitment to sustainable development by establishing the policy, allocating resources, and integrating the management system into core business processes.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy on sustainable development -- The policy on sustainable development sets the organization's commitment to integrating sustainability into community planning, defines relevant objectives, and outlines the framework for implementing, monitoring and reviewing actions that support the goals of ISO 37101, ensuring alignment with local context and stakeholder expectations.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 of ISO 37101:2016 requires the community organization to identify, assign and communicate the specific roles, responsibilities and authorities needed for its sustainable-development management system, ensuring that each function has the competence and authority to perform its tasks and that the structure supports effective planning, implementation and continual improvement.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- The entry requires the community management system to identify potential risks and opportunities that could affect its sustainable development goals, plan and implement appropriate actions to mitigate risks and exploit opportunities, and regularly monitor and review their effectiveness.
[6_2] 6.2 Sustainability objectives and planning -- Clause 6.2 of ISO 37101:2016 requires a community organization to define clear sustainability objectives that reflect local priorities and legal requirements, and to establish documented plans detailing responsibilities, required resources, timelines and performance indicators for achieving those objectives.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 37101:2016 specifies that a community's management system must establish and maintain processes for planning, implementing, and controlling operations that support sustainable development goals. It requires setting measurable objectives, allocating resources, monitoring performance, and taking corrective actions to ensure effective, continual improvement.
[8_2] 8.2 Framework for sustainable development in communities -- The 8.2 entry defines the overarching framework that guides organizations in applying ISO 37101 to achieve sustainable development in communities, establishing the scope, context, leadership responsibilities and the cycle of planning, implementation, monitoring and continual improvement that underpins the management system.
[8_3] 8.3 Strategies and programmes for sustainability -- ISO 37101 2016 clause 8.3 requires organizations to define, implement and regularly review strategies and programmes that advance community sustainability, integrating environmental, social and economic objectives. It guides the alignment of these initiatives with local policies, stakeholder expectations and measurable targets, and mandates monitoring, evaluation and continual improvement to ensure lasting impact.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Entry 9.1 requires organizations to establish processes for systematically monitoring, measuring, analyzing, and evaluating the performance of their community sustainability initiatives, ensuring data-driven decision-making and continual improvement of the management system.

[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 37101:2016 outlines the requirement for continual improvement of the community-sustainability management system, mandating that organisations monitor performance, identify non-conformities, implement corrective actions and revise objectives as needed to enhance overall effectiveness and stakeholder value.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Community -- Clause 4 - Context of the Community requires organizations to identify internal and external factors that affect the community's sustainable development, understand the needs and expectations of interested parties, and define the scope of the management system. This analysis establishes the baseline for planning and performance evaluation.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment to sustainable development in communities by establishing a policy, setting objectives, allocating resources and ensuring the management system is integrated throughout the organization. It also mandates clear communication of responsibilities, fostering a culture of continual improvement and active engagement with stakeholders to support the system's effectiveness.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 37101:2016 outlines the planning requirements for a community-focused sustainable development management system, directing organizations to establish the scope, set measurable objectives, assess risks and opportunities, and determine necessary actions and resources. This clause ensures that planning aligns with the overall policy and leads to continual improvement toward sustainable outcomes.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 37101:2016 outlines the support mechanisms required for an effective sustainable-development management system in communities, specifying the provision of resources, competence and awareness of personnel, internal and external communication processes, and the control of documented information needed to maintain and improve the system.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 37101:2016 defines the operational requirements for a community's sustainable-development management system, detailing how to plan, implement, control and monitor activities that deliver the system's objectives. It guides the allocation of resources, execution of processes, performance evaluation and continual improvement to ensure effective, ongoing operation.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 specifies the requirements for monitoring and measuring the effectiveness of a community's sustainable-development management system, including the collection of performance data, internal audits, and management review. It guides organizations to analyze results, identify non-conformities, and take corrective actions, ensuring continual improvement and alignment with the intended sustainability objectives.
