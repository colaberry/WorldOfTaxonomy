[HE] HEDIS Domains -- The HEDIS Domains (code HE) are the broad categories used in the Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set to group related quality measures. They organize performance metrics into areas such as preventive care, chronic condition management, and patient experience, allowing health plans to evaluate and compare overall service quality.
[HE.01] Effectiveness of Care -- HE.01 is a HEDIS measure that gauges the effectiveness of care by tracking clinical outcomes and adherence to evidence-based guidelines for key conditions such as diabetes, hypertension and preventive services. It compares patients' health status improvements and appropriate treatment over the measurement period, providing health plans with a benchmark for quality performance and areas needing improvement.
[HE.02] Access/Availability of Care -- HE.02 assesses how easily patients can obtain needed medical services, tracking factors such as appointment wait times, provider network adequacy, and the availability of after-hours or urgent care. It evaluates whether members have timely access to primary and specialty care, reflecting overall availability of care within a health plan.
[HE.03] Experience of Care -- HE.03 assesses members' perceptions of the care they receive, covering domains such as communication with providers, access to services, and overall satisfaction. It aggregates survey responses to gauge the quality of patient-provider interactions and identify areas for improvement.

[HE.05] Health Plan Descriptive Information -- HE.05 is a HEDIS data-collection element that records a health plan's basic descriptive information, such as its name, contact details, plan type, and network characteristics. The data are used for reporting, comparison, and consumer transparency across plans.
[HE.06] Prevention and Screening -- HE.06 is a HEDIS metric that measures the proportion of eligible members who receive recommended preventive services and screenings, including vaccinations, cancer detection tests, and wellness exams. It enables health plans to assess how effectively they provide early-detection care and to identify gaps for quality improvement.
[HE.07] Respiratory Conditions -- The HEDIS measure HE.07 tracks the quality of care provided for patients with respiratory conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. It assesses whether clinicians adhere to evidence-based guidelines for diagnosis, treatment, and follow-up, including medication management and patient education, to improve outcomes and reduce avoidable hospitalizations.
[HE.08] Cardiovascular Conditions -- HE.08 is a HEDIS quality measure that evaluates how health plans manage cardiovascular conditions, tracking key indicators such as blood-pressure control, cholesterol management and appropriate use of cardiac medications. The metric helps assess preventive care, treatment effectiveness and follow-up adherence for patients with heart disease.
[HE.09] Diabetes -- HE.09 is a HEDIS measure that assesses diabetes care by tracking the percentage of adult members with a diabetes diagnosis who receive at least one hemoglobin A1c test during the measurement year. The indicator helps health plans evaluate the effectiveness of disease management and identify gaps in preventive testing for patients with diabetes.
[HE.10] Musculoskeletal Conditions -- HE.10 is a HEDIS measure that evaluates the management of musculoskeletal conditions, focusing on the appropriate use of imaging and referrals for patients with low back pain or other musculoskeletal complaints. It tracks adherence to evidence-based guidelines to ensure timely, non-invasive treatment and reduce unnecessary procedures.
[HE.11] Behavioral Health -- HE.11 is a HEDIS quality measure that evaluates an insurer's performance in identifying and managing members with behavioral health conditions. It tracks rates of screening, diagnosis, treatment initiation, and follow-up for mental health and substance-use disorders, helping compare care effectiveness across plans.
[HE.12] Medication Management -- HE.12 measures the proportion of patients receiving appropriate medication management, tracking whether clinicians review, adjust, or reconcile prescribed drugs for chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, or hyperlipidemia. It assesses adherence to evidence-based prescribing practices and aims to improve therapeutic outcomes and reduce medication-related problems.
[HE.13] Overuse/Appropriateness -- HE.13 in the HEDIS set monitors the overuse and appropriateness of health services, measuring how often clinicians order low-value tests or procedures that may not be medically necessary. The metric helps health plans identify patterns of unnecessary care and supports efforts to improve resource use and patient safety.
[HE.14] Patient Safety -- HE.14 Patient Safety is a HEDIS measure that assesses how well health plans identify and reduce safety risks for their members, covering areas such as medication safety, infection control, fall prevention and adverse event reporting. The metric uses claims and clinical data to calculate performance scores that guide quality improvement and public reporting.
