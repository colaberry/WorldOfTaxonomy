[A] SEEA Agriculture, Forestry and Fisheries (SEEA-AFF) -- The SEEA-AFF (code A) is the component of the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting that integrates data on agriculture, forestry and fisheries with economic accounts, enabling analysis of the contributions and impacts of these sectors on the environment and vice-versa. It provides standardized concepts, classifications and methods for measuring resource use, production, and environmental pressures across the three sectors. This facilitates consistent policy-relevant reporting at national and international levels.
[A1] Crop Production Accounts -- Crop Production Accounts (A1) record the physical and monetary flows of crops cultivated within a territory, detailing quantities produced, harvested, consumed, exported, and the associated revenues and costs. They form part of the SEEA framework to integrate agricultural activity into environmental-economic accounting.
[A2] Livestock Production Accounts -- The SEEA Livestock Production Accounts (code A2) provide a framework for compiling statistical information on the quantity, value and resource use of livestock products, linking agricultural output with environmental flows such as feed, land, water and emissions. They enable integration of livestock sector data into national accounts and support analysis of economic and environmental performance.

They integrate data on production, resource stocks, employment, and economic value to support sustainable management and policy analysis.
[A5] Land Use in Agriculture Accounts -- A5 "Land Use in Agriculture Accounts" provides guidance for measuring and reporting the area of land allocated to agricultural activities, distinguishing between arable, permanent crops, pastures and other uses. It links land resource stocks with agricultural production, enabling integrated monitoring of land use changes and their economic and environmental impacts.
[A6] Soil and Nutrient Accounts -- The Soil and Nutrient Accounts (code A6) in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting provide a framework for measuring the quantity, quality and changes in soil stocks and the flows of essential nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium. They link soil conditions and nutrient cycles to economic activities, supporting policy analysis of land use, agricultural productivity and environmental sustainability.
[CF] SEEA Central Framework (SEEA-CF) -- The SEEA Central Framework is the core international standard that guides the compilation of integrated environmental-economic accounts, enabling systematic measurement of the links between natural resources, ecosystem services, and economic activity. It provides concepts, definitions, classifications and methodological guidelines for reporting on environmental assets, flows, and pressures alongside traditional economic statistics, supporting policy analysis and sustainable development planning.
[CF1] Physical Flow Accounts -- CF1 Physical Flow Accounts record the quantity and type of material inputs and outputs associated with economic activities, linking physical resource use to monetary accounts. They enable analysis of resource efficiency, waste generation and the environmental implications of production and consumption across sectors.

[CF1.2] Material Flow Accounts (MFA) -- CF1.2 in the SEEA framework defines Material Flow Accounts, which record the physical inputs, outputs and accumulation of natural resources and commodities within an economy over a given period. These accounts quantify stocks, flows and balances of materials such as minerals, biomass, water and energy, providing a basis for linking environmental resource use with economic activity.
[CF1.3] Energy Flow Accounts -- CF1.3 Energy Flow Accounts are a component of the SEEA framework that records the physical flows of energy carriers-such as coal, oil, gas and renewables-through an economy, linking production, transformation, consumption and final use with associated environmental stocks. The accounts support analysis of energy efficiency, emissions and policy impacts across sectors.
[CF1.4] Water Flow Accounts -- The SEEA Water Flow Accounts (CF1.4) collect and present data on the physical quantities of water entering, leaving, and being used within an economy, including withdrawals, returns, and internal transfers among sectors. They are organized by source, purpose, and geographic unit to link water resource availability with economic activity, supporting integrated environmental-economic analysis and policy making.

They provide standardized data on emissions such as CO₂, NOₓ and SO₂ to support policy analysis, cross-sectoral comparisons and integration of environmental and economic statistics.

[CF2.1] Environmental Protection Expenditure Accounts (EPEA) -- CF2.1 covers the Environmental Protection Expenditure Accounts, a component of the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting that records public and private spending on measures that prevent, control or remediate environmental degradation. The accounts link environmental protection costs with economic activity, enabling analysis of resource use, policy effectiveness and sustainable development.
[CF2.2] Environmental Goods and Services Sector (EGSS) -- The Environmental Goods and Services Sector (EGSS) groups industries that produce goods and services directly used for environmental protection, management and restoration, such as water supply, waste treatment, renewable energy and pollution control. In the SEEA framework it enables the systematic accounting of economic activity linked to environmental objectives and the integration of environmental and monetary statistics.
[CF2.3] Environmental Taxes and Subsidies -- CF2.3 records government fiscal instruments that influence environmental outcomes, distinguishing taxes levied on activities or resources that generate pollution or deplete natural capital from subsidies that support environmental protection, conservation or the use of cleaner technologies. This data enable analysts to assess how fiscal policy shapes environmental pressures and resource use.
[CF3] Environmental Activity Accounts -- CF3 Environmental Activity Accounts document the use and transformation of natural resources and environmental services by economic units, linking environmental stocks and their changes to the production activities that consume or generate them, thereby supporting integrated analysis of environmental and economic performance.
[CF4] Asset Accounts -- CF4 Asset Accounts cover the measurement and reporting of the stocks of natural resources and environmental assets held by economic units, detailing their quantities, values, and changes over time. These accounts link environmental resources to economic activity, supporting analysis of sustainability, resource use, and the contribution of natural capital to wealth creation.
[CF4.1] Mineral and Energy Resource Assets -- CF4.1 Mineral and Energy Resource Assets represents the stock of natural resources such as metals, minerals, coal, oil, gas and other fossil fuels that are owned or controlled by an economy, measured in physical or monetary terms for environmental-economic accounting. It captures the quantity and value of these extractable resources, providing a basis for tracking their depletion, contribution to production and sustainable management within the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting.
[CF4.2] Land Assets -- CF4.2 Land Assets in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting defines the stock and changes of land resources that are used for production, habitation or recreation, distinguishing cultivated, forest, peat, and other natural land categories, and provides guidelines for measuring their physical extent, quality and economic value within national accounts.
[CF4.3] Soil Resource Assets -- CF4.3 in the SEEA classification covers soil resource assets, referring to the natural soil base that provides ecosystem services and supports agricultural production. It includes measurements of soil stock, quality, and changes, enabling accounting of soil as a capital asset in environmental-economic accounts.
[CF4.4] Timber Resource Assets -- Timber Resource Assets represent the stock of standing timber in forests measured in volume or monetary value, including trees grown for commercial use. The asset is recorded as part of natural capital in the SEEA, covering all forest land where timber is harvested or intended for harvest, and is valued at market price or replacement cost.
[CF4.5] Aquatic Resource Assets -- CF4.5 Aquatic Resource Assets in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting records the stocks of biological resources found in marine and freshwater environments, such as fish, shellfish, seaweed and other aquatic organisms. It treats these stocks as economic assets, quantifying their quantities, biological conditions and monetary value for use in integrated environmental and economic analysis.
[CF4.6] Other Biological Resource Assets -- CF4.6 Other Biological Resource Assets refers to stocks of living organisms that are not classified under more specific categories, such as wild fish, game, timber, and non-timber forest products, as well as other renewable biological resources. These assets are recorded in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting to capture their physical quantities and monetary values, supporting the assessment of natural capital and its contribution to the economy.
[CF4.7] Water Resource Assets -- CF4.7 Water Resource Assets in the SEEA system records the stock of surface and groundwater that are owned, controlled or managed by institutional units, expressed in physical volumes and, where possible, monetary values for integration with economic accounts.
[CF5] Cross-Cutting and Other Accounts -- The CF5 entry in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting gathers supporting information that cuts across multiple accounts, such as methodological guidelines, data quality indicators, and metadata. It also includes any supplementary accounts that do not fit into the standard thematic categories, ensuring consistency and comparability across the SEEA framework.

[EA] SEEA Ecosystem Accounts (SEEA-EA) -- The SEEA Ecosystem Accounts (SEEA-EA) is a UN-endorsed framework that integrates data on ecosystem extent, condition and services with national economic accounts. It provides standardized methods for measuring and reporting the contribution of ecosystems to economic activity, supporting sustainable-development policy and resource-management decisions.
[EA1] Ecosystem Extent Account -- The EA1 Ecosystem Extent Account records the physical area of each ecosystem type within a defined geographic boundary, providing a baseline of land and water cover that can be linked to economic and environmental data. It classifies ecosystems by standardized categories, tracks changes over time, and supports analyses of resource use, valuation, and sustainability within the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting framework.
[EA2] Ecosystem Condition Account -- The EA2 ecosystem condition account is a component of the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting that records the biophysical state and quality of ecosystems, such as biodiversity, water purity, and land health. It provides data on ecosystem condition to complement accounts of extent and services, supporting integrated environmental-economic analysis.
[EA3] Ecosystem Services - Provisioning Services -- EA3 in the SEEA classification covers provisioning ecosystem services, the tangible benefits humans obtain directly from ecosystems such as food, fresh water, timber, fuel, fiber, and genetic material. These services are measured to assess their contribution to the economy and to inform sustainable resource management.
[EA4] Ecosystem Services - Regulating and Maintenance Services -- EA4 in the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting records the value of ecosystem functions that regulate climate, water quality, disease control and other natural processes essential for human wellbeing. It quantifies how natural habitats such as forests, wetlands and oceans contribute to pollution mitigation, flood protection and pollination, linking these benefits to economic accounts for policy-making and sustainability assessment.
[EA5] Ecosystem Services - Cultural Services -- EA5 covers cultural ecosystem services, the non-material benefits people obtain from ecosystems such as recreation, spiritual fulfillment, aesthetic appreciation and heritage values. It provides a framework for measuring and reporting these benefits to inform policies that enhance well-being and preserve cultural identities tied to natural environments.
