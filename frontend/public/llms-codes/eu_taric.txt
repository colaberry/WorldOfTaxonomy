[TR] TARIC Sections -- TR denotes the TARIC Sections, the top-level grouping of the EU's integrated customs tariff. The sections divide all goods into 28 broad categories that help users locate tariff rates, quotas and regulatory measures across the Union.
[TR.I] Live Animals and Animal Products -- The TARIC section TR.I groups all live animals and animal-derived products such as meat, dairy, eggs and honey for customs and tariff purposes. It sets the duty rates, import licences and health-sanitary requirements that apply when these goods move into or out of the EU.
[TR.II] Vegetable Products -- TR.II is the TARIC heading that consolidates duties, measures and statistical codes for all vegetable products entering the EU, including fresh, chilled, frozen, dried and processed forms such as canned or dried legumes, tubers and other edible plant parts, and outlines any applicable preferential rates or restrictions.
[TR.III] Animal/Vegetable Fats and Oils -- The TARIC code TR.III groups all animal and vegetable fats and oils, including butter, cream, animal greases, plant oils, and their derivatives such as waxes and solid fats. It serves as the customs classification for import, export and tariff purposes across the EU, determining duties, quotas and regulatory requirements for these commodities.
[TR.IV] Prepared Foodstuffs, Beverages, Tobacco -- This TARIC chapter groups together a wide range of processed food items, drinks and tobacco products that are subject to EU customs duties, import licensing and health regulations. It covers everything from canned goods and ready-to-eat meals to alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages and all forms of tobacco, including cigarettes and cigars.

[TR.V] Mineral Products -- TR.V in the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC) groups all mineral products, including natural stones, sand, gravel, clay, and other raw or minimally processed minerals used in construction, manufacturing, and industrial processes. Items classified under this code are subject to the EU's common customs duties, origin rules, and any applicable sanitary, phytosanitary or environmental measures.
[TR.VI] Chemical Products -- TR.VI in the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC) groups a wide variety of chemical products, ranging from inorganic and organic compounds to fertilizers, polymers and related intermediates intended for industrial or commercial use. The heading determines the applicable customs duties, import controls and any licensing or safety measures required under EU regulations.
[TR.VII] Plastics and Rubber -- TR.VII in the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC) groups all goods made of plastics and rubber, covering raw materials, semi-finished and finished articles such as sheets, plates, tubes, hoses, molded parts and footwear components. The chapter specifies applicable customs duties, preferential rates, anti-dumping measures and any import licensing or health-safety requirements for these materials.
[TR.VIII] Raw Hides, Skins, Leather -- Under the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC), code TR.VIII classifies raw hides, skins and leather that have not undergone any tanning or finishing processes. It includes unprocessed animal pelts and partially prepared leather intended for the leather-manufacturing sector, and determines the customs duties and trade measures applicable to these goods when imported into or exported from the EU.
[TR.X] Pulp, Paper, Paperboard -- The TARIC entry TR.X groups together all types of pulp, paper and paperboard, including uncoated and coated sheets, rolls and folded products, as well as the raw cellulose fibres used to make them. It is the reference point for customs duties, trade statistics and any tariff or trade measures that apply to these paper-based goods across the EU.
[TR.XI] Textiles and Textile Articles -- The TARIC section TR.XI groups all textile goods and articles, including raw fibers, yarns, fabrics, knitted and woven cloth, and finished products such as garments, home textiles and industrial textiles. It is the basis for customs duties, import quotas and other trade measures applied by the European Union to textile commodities.
[TR.XII] Footwear, Headgear, Umbrellas -- The EU Integrated Tariff groups together footwear, headgear and umbrellas under a single heading that covers shoes, boots, gaiters, hats, caps, visors, umbrellas, parasols, walking sticks and related accessories as well as the parts and components of these articles for customs and trade reporting.
[TR.XIII] Stone, Plaster, Cement, Ceramics, Glass -- TR.XIII groups together commodities such as natural stone, building plaster, hydraulic cement, ceramic products and glassware for customs purposes. The chapter defines the duty rates, any preferential measures, anti-dumping duties and technical standards that apply to imports and exports within the EU. It is used by customs authorities and traders to determine tariff treatment and compliance obligations.
[TR.XIV] Precious Metals and Stones -- This TARIC heading groups the customs duties, measures and regulations that apply to the trade of precious metals such as gold, silver, platinum and palladium, as well as precious stones including diamonds, rubies and emeralds. It covers both raw forms and semi-finished articles, specifying any anti-dumping duties, licensing requirements and preferential rates that may be attached to these high-value commodities.
[TR.XIX] Arms and Ammunition -- TR.XIX in the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC) groups all firearms, weapons and their ammunition, including related components and accessories. The code identifies items subject to strict customs controls, import/export licensing and, depending on the specific product, may attract duties or be prohibited.

[TR.XVI] Machinery and Mechanical Appliances -- TR.XVI of the EU Integrated Tariff groups together all machinery and mechanical appliances, encompassing industrial, agricultural and construction equipment, engines, pumps, and related components and accessories. The heading is used for customs duties, import controls and statistical reporting on a wide range of manufactured mechanical goods.
[TR.XVII] Vehicles, Aircraft, Vessels -- TR.XVII groups together all means of transport-motor vehicles, aircraft and vessels-along with their parts and accessories, setting out the customs duties, import quotas and regulatory requirements that apply, with rates varying by engine type, weight, propulsion and intended use.
[TR.XVIII] Optical, Photographic, Medical -- The TARIC chapter XVIII groups goods used in vision, imaging and health care, encompassing optical instruments such as spectacles, lenses and microscopes, photographic equipment including cameras, lenses, film and related accessories, and medical devices ranging from diagnostic and therapeutic apparatus to surgical instruments and implants. Items are classified here for customs duties, trade statistics and regulatory measures across the European Union.
[TR.XX] Miscellaneous Manufactured Articles -- The TARIC entry TR.XX groups together assorted manufactured items that are not classified elsewhere in the tariff schedule, such as miscellaneous metal or plastic components, fittings, brackets and similar products. It serves as a catch-all category for goods that lack a more specific heading, applying the standard EU customs duties and regulations for such items.
[TR.XXI] Works of Art, Antiques -- TR.XXI in the EU Integrated Tariff (TARIC) covers the import and export of works of art, antiques and other cultural objects such as paintings, sculptures, archaeological artefacts and collectibles. These items are often eligible for reduced or zero customs duties but require proper documentation of age, provenance and, where applicable, authenticity certificates, and may be subject to specific licensing or export-control measures.
