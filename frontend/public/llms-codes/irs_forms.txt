[IF.01] Individual Income Tax (1040 series) -- IF.01 refers to the core individual income-tax returns in the IRS 1040 series, encompassing Form 1040 and its primary schedules used by U.S. taxpayers to report earnings, claim deductions and credits, and compute total tax liability, including related versions such as 1040-SR and 1040-NR.
[IF.02] Corporate Tax (1120 series) -- IF.02 denotes the IRS Tax Form category for corporate income tax filings, primarily the Form 1120 series used by C-corporations to report earnings, deductions, and calculate tax liability, along with associated schedules
[IF.03] Partnership Returns (1065) -- IF.03 designates the IRS Form 1065 used by partnerships to report their income, deductions, credits, and other tax items. The filing provides a consolidated return that details each partner's share of the partnership's financial activity for the tax year and must be submitted annually.
[IF.04] Employment Tax (940/941) -- IF.04 covers the filing of employer employment taxes using IRS Forms 940 and 941. Form 940 reports federal unemployment tax, while Form 941 is the quarterly return for withheld income, social security and Medicare taxes. These filings are required for businesses that have employees.
[IF.05] Excise Tax (720 series) -- IF.05 is the IRS classification for excise tax forms in the 720 series, covering taxes on specific goods and activities such as fuel, firearms, and certain services. These forms are used by businesses to report and remit the applicable excise taxes to the Internal Revenue Service.
[IF.06] Estate and Gift Tax (706/709) -- IF.06 refers to IRS forms used to report estate and gift taxes, specifically Form 706 for estate tax and Form 709 for gift tax. These forms are filed by estates and donors to calculate liability, claim deductions, and report transfers subject to the federal tax.
[IF.07] Information Returns (1099 series) -- IF.07 refers to the Information Returns category covering the 1099 series of IRS tax forms, which are used by businesses and payers to report non-employee compensation, interest, dividends, and other miscellaneous income to both recipients and the Internal Revenue Service.
[IF.08] Exempt Organizations (990 series) -- The IF.08 category groups the IRS Form 990 series, which tax-exempt organizations file each year to disclose financial activities, governance, and compliance with charitable-status rules. It includes the standard Form 990, the shorter Form 990-EZ, the Form 990-PF for private foundations, and related schedules such as Form 990-N.
[IF.09] International (5471/5472/8865) -- IF.09 designates the International category for IRS information returns, covering Forms 5471, 5472, and 8865 that report U.S. persons' ownership or participation in foreign corporations, partnerships, and certain foreign subsidiaries. It is used to ensure compliance with reporting requirements for cross-border investments and corporate structures.

[IF.11] Withholding (W-2/W-4) -- The IF.11 category groups resources related to employee tax-withholding documentation, specifically the W-2 form that reports annual wages and tax withheld, and the W-4 form that employees complete to set their withholding allowances. Materials in this group cover filing requirements, calculation methods, and compliance guidelines for payroll and payroll-processing systems.
[IF.12] Retirement Plans (5500) -- IF.12 denotes the category for filing Form 5500, the annual report required of employee benefit and retirement plans subject to the Employee Retirement Income Security Act. It covers compliance documentation, financial information, and plan characteristics for public and private pension, profit-sharing, and other qualified retirement schemes. This entry guides users to the appropriate tax-form series for reporting and disclosure obligations.
