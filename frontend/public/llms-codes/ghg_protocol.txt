[scope_1] Scope 1 - Direct Emissions -- Scope 1 refers to direct greenhouse gas emissions that occur from sources owned or controlled by an organization, such as fuel combustion in company boilers, company-owned vehicle fleets, on-site industrial processes and fugitive emissions from equipment leaks.
[scope_1_mobile] Mobile Combustion and Transportation -- Scope 1 Mobile covers direct greenhouse-gas emissions from fuel combustion in company-owned or controlled mobile sources, including vehicles, fleets, machinery, and equipment used for transportation and on-site mobility. It is a Category 1 entry within the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard's Scope and Category Framework, capturing emissions from all on-road, off-road and airborne activities that burn fossil fuels.
[scope_1_stat] Stationary Combustion -- Scope 1 Stat denotes direct emissions from stationary combustion, covering fuel burned in on-site boilers, furnaces, turbines, generators and other fixed equipment owned or controlled by the reporting organization.
[scope_2] Scope 2 - Indirect Emissions from Purchased Energy -- Scope 2 covers indirect greenhouse-gas emissions that result from the generation of electricity, heat, steam or cooling purchased or acquired by a company. It quantifies the emissions associated with the energy the organization consumes but does not produce directly, enabling firms to account for the impact of their purchased energy use.
[scope_3] Scope 3 - All Other Indirect Emissions -- Scope 3 in the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard covers all other indirect emissions that occur in a company's value chain, both upstream and downstream of its own operations. It includes categories such as purchased goods and services, fuel-related activities, transportation and distribution, waste generated, business travel, and the use and end-of-life treatment of sold products, capturing the broader climate impact beyond direct (Scope 1) and energy-related (Scope 2) emissions.
[scope_3_cat_1] Cat 1 - Purchased Goods and Services -- Category 1 of the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard Scope 3 covers emissions from the extraction, production and transport of goods and services that a company purchases. It includes all upstream activities linked to raw materials, components, capital equipment, and outsourced services, representing the largest share of many organisations' indirect carbon footprints.
[scope_3_cat_10] Cat 10 - Processing of Sold Products -- Category 10 captures emissions from the processing of sold products, including any downstream activities such as assembly, packaging, or modification performed by customers after the product is sold. These indirect emissions fall under Scope 3 because they occur outside the reporting company's direct operational control.
[scope_3_cat_11] Cat 11 - Use of Sold Products -- Category 11 in the GHG Protocol corporate scope-3 framework covers emissions that occur when a company's sold products are used by customers, reflecting the climate impact of product functionality, energy consumption and related processes throughout the product's use phase. It is a downstream scope-3 category that companies quantify to assess indirect emissions generated by end-users.
[scope_3_cat_12] Cat 12 - End-of-Life Treatment of Sold Products -- Category 12 captures the greenhouse-gas emissions associated with how sold products are managed at the end of their useful life. It covers emissions from disposal methods such as landfill, incineration, recycling and other treatment processes, reflecting the downstream impact of the product after it leaves the company's control.
[scope_3_cat_13] Cat 13 - Downstream Leased Assets -- Category 13 in the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard addresses downstream leased assets, covering Scope 3 emissions from the use of assets a company leases to other parties. It requires reporting the emissions generated by the lessee's operation of those assets, typically calculated using the lessee's energy consumption or applicable emission factors.
[scope_3_cat_14] Cat 14 - Franchises -- Category 14 in the GHG Protocol corporate scope-3 framework records emissions associated with franchised operations that the reporting company does not own or control, such as retail or service outlets operated by independent franchisees. It captures both upstream and downstream emissions arising from the franchisees' energy use, waste, and other activities linked to the company's brand.
[scope_3_cat_15] Cat 15 - Investments -- Category 15 - Investments captures greenhouse-gas emissions that arise from the equity and debt investments a company holds, reflecting the upstream climate impacts of the assets it finances. These emissions are reported as Scope 3 and are quantified using attributional or consequential approaches that allocate a share of the investee's emissions to the investor.

[scope_3_cat_3] Cat 3 - Fuel and Energy-Related Activities -- Category 3 in the Greenhouse Gas Protocol corporate standard covers fuel- and energy-related activities, capturing emissions from the extraction, production, and transportation of fuels as well as the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity, steam, heat and cooling that a company purchases or sells. These upstream and downstream emissions are reported under Scope 3.

[scope_3_cat_5] Cat 5 - Waste Generated in Operations -- Category 5 of the Scope 3 GHG Protocol covers emissions from waste generated in a company's own operations, encompassing the treatment and disposal of solid and liquid waste such as landfill, incineration, recycling and biological treatment. It quantifies the carbon impact of waste handling activities that occur after the waste leaves the point of generation within the organization.
[scope_3_cat_6] Cat 6 - Business Travel -- Category 6 of the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard's Scope-3 framework covers emissions from employee business travel, including air, rail, car rentals, taxis and other modes used for work-related trips. It requires companies to quantify fuel-combustion and distance-based emissions from these activities and report them as part of their overall value-chain carbon footprint.
[scope_3_cat_7] Cat 7 - Employee Commuting -- Scope 3 Category 7 under the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard categorises emissions from employee commuting, covering travel by private car, public transport, biking, walking or other modes between home and work. It quantifies these indirect, non-energy-related emissions to help organisations assess and manage the climate impact of their workforce's daily travel.
[scope_3_cat_8] Cat 8 - Upstream Leased Assets -- Category 8 of the Greenhouse Gas Protocol Corporate Standard Scope 3 categories refers to upstream leased assets, capturing emissions associated with the extraction, production and delivery of energy and other inputs for assets a company rents but does not own. These upstream emissions are counted as part of the company's indirect (scope 3) inventory.
[scope_3_cat_9] Cat 9 - Downstream Transportation and Distribution -- Category 9 covers emissions from the downstream transport and distribution of sold products, including the fuel used by third-party carriers, freight-forwarding services, and any subsequent warehousing or handling until the goods reach the end customer. It accounts for all GHG released in moving products through road, rail, air, sea and storage activities that are not owned or controlled by the reporting company.
