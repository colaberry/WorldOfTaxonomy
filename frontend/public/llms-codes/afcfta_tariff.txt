[AF] AfCFTA Categories -- AF is the code for the African Continental Free Trade Area tariff classification, identifying goods and services grouped under AfCFTA categories for preferential trade among member states. It signals eligibility for reduced or zero duties under the continent-wide free trade agreement.
[AF.01] Sensitive Products -- AF.01 refers to the category of "Sensitive Products" within the African Continental Free Trade Area tariff schedule. These are goods that member states may protect with higher duties or limited market access, allowing each country to maintain selective tariffs on items deemed crucial for domestic industries or food security while the rest of the market moves toward deeper liberalisation.
[AF.02] Semi-Sensitive Products -- AF.02 marks the "Semi-Sensitive Products" tier in the African Continental Free Trade Area tariff schedule, covering goods that receive a modest preferential duty reduction rather than full duty-free treatment. These items are subject to specific rules of origin and may retain limited tariff protection to support regional industry development.
[AF.03] Non-Sensitive Products -- AF.03 under African Continental Free Trade Area Tariff covers non-sensitive products, meaning goods that are not subject to high tariffs or safeguard measures and are eligible for reduced or zero duties to promote intra-regional trade.
[AF.04] Excluded Products -- AF.04 in the African Continental Free Trade Area tariff schedule designates products that are excluded from the continent-wide preferential tariff regime, meaning they continue to be subject to each member's own customs duties and rules of origin rather than receiving reduced or zero rates.
[AF.05] Agricultural Products -- AF.05 designates the tariff schedule for agricultural products under the African Continental Free Trade Area. It sets preferential duty rates, rules of origin and customs procedures for crops, livestock and related goods traded between member states, aiming to reduce barriers and promote intra-African agriculture.
[AF.06] Industrial Goods -- AF.06 denotes the tariff schedule for industrial goods under the African Continental Free Trade Area, outlining the duty rates and preferential treatment applied to manufactured products such as machinery, chemicals, and metal items when traded between member states. This code guides customs valuation and trade policy.
[AF.07] Textiles and Clothing -- AF.07 refers to the tariff schedule for textiles and clothing under the African Continental Free Trade Area, encompassing raw fibers, yarns, fabrics, finished garments and related accessories. The code groups these products for uniform duty rates that may differ by item type and origin, aiming to support regional textile manufacturing and simplify cross-border trade.
[AF.08] Automotive Products -- The AF.08 tariff line covers motor vehicles, trucks, buses, motorcycles and related components such as engines, transmissions, chassis parts and accessories imported into African Continental Free Trade Area markets. Duties are applied according to the product's HS sub-heading and may qualify for reduced or zero rates when originating from a signatory country that meets rules of origin.

[AF.11] Chemicals -- AF.11 designates the tariff line for chemicals under the African Continental Free Trade Area. It groups a broad range of inorganic and organic substances used in industry, agriculture and consumer products, specifying the most-favored duty rates and any preferential reductions for member states.
[AF.12] Mining and Mineral Products -- AF.12 covers tariffs on mining and mineral products traded within the African Continental Free Trade Area, including raw ores, concentrates and processed minerals such as iron ore, copper concentrate, limestone and other extracted commodities for customs and trade reporting.
[AF.13] Petroleum Products -- AF.13 designates the African Continental Free Trade Area tariff category for petroleum products, including crude oil, gasoline, diesel, kerosene and other refined fuels. The code applies to intra-African trade, setting specific duty rates and preferences intended to harmonise and liberalise the movement of energy commodities across member states.
