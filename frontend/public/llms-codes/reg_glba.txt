[reg_p] Regulation P (12 CFR Part 1016) - Privacy of Consumer Financial Information -- Regulation P, part of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, establishes federal privacy rules for non-public personal information held by financial institutions. It mandates clear privacy notices, safeguards for consumer data, and gives individuals the right to opt out of certain information sharing with non-affiliated third parties.
[safeguards_rule] FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR Part 314) -- The FTC Safeguards Rule, codified at 16 CFR § 314, implements the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act's requirement that financial institutions establish, maintain, and regularly update a written information-security program to protect customer data. It mandates risk assessments, employee training, oversight of service providers, and incident-response procedures, and it applies to any entity that offers or provides financial products or services to consumers.
[sec_101] Sec 101 - Financial Holding Company Structure -- Section 101 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act defines the permissible structure of a financial holding company, outlining the types of banking, securities, and insurance subsidiaries it may own and the regulatory requirements for consolidating operations under a single corporate umbrella.
[sec_102] Sec 102 - Activities of Financial Holding Companies -- Section 102 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act outlines the permissible activities of financial holding companies, specifying the range of banking, securities, and insurance services they may own or control, and establishing supervisory requirements for the Federal Reserve to ensure safety, soundness, and compliance with consumer privacy standards. This provision sets the regulatory framework for how holding companies can expand and operate across multiple financial sectors.
[sec_103] Sec 103 - Conforming Amendments -- Section 103 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act makes conforming amendments to related financial-services statutes, updating definitions and cross-references so that existing laws align with the Act's new provisions on privacy, data sharing and the integration of banking, securities and insurance activities.
[sec_201] Sec 201 - Broker-Dealer Functions in Banks -- Section 201 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act permits banks to perform broker-dealer activities, allowing them to buy, sell, and underwrite securities while still operating under banking regulations. The provision sets standards for supervision, capital, and record-keeping to safeguard the safety and soundness of the institution and protect consumers. It integrates securities functions into depository institutions with oversight by banking and securities regulators.
[sec_202] Sec 202 - Securities Activities of Banks -- Sec 202 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act permits banks to engage in securities activities, outlining permissible underwriting, dealing and brokerage services while requiring separate accounting and risk controls. It also establishes regulatory oversight to ensure compliance with consumer-protection and financial-stability objectives.
[sec_211] Sec 211 - Investment Advisers Act Amendments -- Section 211 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act amends the Investment Advisers Act by permitting affiliated non-bank subsidiaries of banks to provide investment-advisory services and receive compensation for those services, and by allowing banks to engage in securities and insurance activities through such affiliates, thereby expanding the range of permissible financial activities for bank holding companies.
[sec_301] Sec 301 - Functional Regulation of Insurance -- Section 301 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act establishes a functional regulatory framework for insurance, directing that insurers be overseen based on the specific activities they perform rather than solely on their corporate structure. It requires coordination among state and federal agencies to apply consistent standards, promote consumer protection, and prevent regulatory gaps in the insurance market.
[sec_302] Sec 302 - State Insurance Regulation -- Section 302 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act permits states to continue regulating insurance entities operating within their borders, provided that such regulation does not conflict with the federal preemption provisions of the law. It authorizes state insurance departments to enforce standards on solvency, consumer protection and market conduct while maintaining the integrated banking-securities-insurance framework created by the act.
[sec_303] Sec 303 - Nondiscrimination for Insurance Sales -- Section 303 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act bars insurance companies from refusing to sell, underwriting, or offering less favorable terms to a consumer because the consumer has exercised privacy protections, such as opting out of the sharing of personal financial information. The rule ensures that privacy choices do not affect eligibility, pricing, or coverage conditions.
[sec_401] Sec 401 - Unitary Thrift Holding Company Provisions -- Section 401 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act sets out the regulatory framework for unitary thrift holding companies, requiring them to meet consolidated capital standards, adhere to unified management and supervision requirements, and file comprehensive reports so that the holding company and its subsidiaries are treated as a single, supervised financial entity.

[sec_503] Sec 503 - Disclosure of Institution Privacy Policy -- Section 503 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires financial institutions to disclose, in a clear and conspicuous privacy notice, their policies and practices for protecting nonpublic personal information, how that information may be shared, and the consumer's right to opt out of certain disclosures; the notice must be provided at account opening and annually thereafter.
[sec_504] Sec 504 - Rulemaking -- Section 504 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act authorizes the Federal Trade Commission to issue rules that implement the law's privacy and data-security requirements for financial institutions. It sets out the rulemaking process, including notice, public comment, and final adoption, to create standards that protect consumers' nonpublic personal information while permitting limited disclosures.
[sec_505] Sec 505 - State Laws -- Section 505 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act governs how states may implement privacy protections for consumers' financial information, requiring that any state law be at least as stringent as the federal standards and that it provide an opt-out mechanism for the disclosure of nonpublic personal data.
[sec_507] Sec 507 - Opt-Out Right for Financial Information -- Section 507 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act gives consumers the right to opt out of a financial institution's sharing of their nonpublic personal information with non-affiliated third parties, provided the institution first gives clear notice. The rule applies to most consumer financial products, though certain disclosures for fraud prevention or public-interest purposes are exempt.
[sec_509] Sec 509 - Definitions -- Section 509 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act supplies the definitions that apply throughout the law, explaining key terms such as "financial institution," "nonpublic personal information," and "affiliated persons," thereby setting the scope for the Act's privacy and disclosure requirements.
[sec_521] Sec 521 - Privacy Protection for Customer Information of Financial Institutions -- Section 521 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act mandates that financial institutions safeguard customers' nonpublic personal information, provide clear privacy notices describing data practices, and give consumers the right to opt out of sharing their information with nonaffiliated third parties, thereby ensuring basic privacy protection for financial data.
[sec_522] Sec 522 - Prohibitions on Obtaining Customer Information by False Pretenses -- Section 522 of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act makes it illegal for any person to obtain a consumer's financial information by using false or misleading statements, deceptive practices, or fraudulent means, and authorizes civil penalties for violations. It reinforces consumer privacy protections by targeting deceptive acquisition of personal data.
[sub_a] Subtitle A - Disclosure of Nonpublic Personal Information -- Subtitle A of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act of 1999 establishes rules for financial institutions to disclose their privacy practices and protect nonpublic personal information. It requires clear notices to customers about what data is collected, how it may be shared, and the options customers have to limit or opt out of certain disclosures.
[sub_b] Subtitle B - Fraudulent Access to Financial Information (Pretexting) -- Subtitle B of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act makes it illegal to obtain a consumer's nonpublic personal financial information through false pretenses, known as pretexting, and authorizes both civil penalties and criminal sanctions for such fraudulent access in order to protect consumer privacy.
[title_1] Title I - Facilitating Affiliation Among Banks, Securities Firms, and Insurance Companies -- Title I of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act of 1999 authorizes banks, securities firms and insurance companies to affiliate and form consolidated financial holding companies, effectively repealing the restrictions of the Glass-Steagall Act. It also sets out supervisory standards and consumer-privacy safeguards that apply to these newly integrated institutions.
[title_2] Title II - Functional Regulation -- Title II of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act creates a functional regulatory framework that assigns oversight of banks, securities firms, and insurance companies to the appropriate federal agencies, allowing them to affiliate while maintaining distinct supervisory responsibilities. It also establishes standards for protecting customers' nonpublic personal information.
[title_3] Title III - Insurance -- Title III of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act sets privacy requirements for insurance entities, obligating them to inform customers about their data-sharing practices, safeguard nonpublic personal information, and provide consumers with the option to opt out of certain disclosures.
[title_4] Title IV - Unitary Savings and Loan Holding Companies -- Title IV of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act creates the unitary savings and loan holding company model, allowing a single holding company to own both a savings association and a bank or other financial subsidiaries under unified supervision. It requires registration with the Federal Reserve, imposes capital standards, and subjects the holding company to consolidated supervision and consumer-privacy rules.
[title_5] Title V - Privacy -- Title V of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act sets federal privacy standards for financial institutions, mandating clear consumer notices about data collection and sharing practices. It requires firms to safeguard personal information and provides customers the right to opt out of non-affiliated third-party disclosures.
