[4_1] 4.1 Integrity -- Section 4.1 of ISO 19011:2018 defines integrity as the fundamental ethical requirement for auditors, mandating honesty, impartiality, confidentiality and compliance with professional standards throughout the audit process, and emphasizing that any breach compromises the credibility of the audit and the management system being assessed.
[4_2] 4.2 Fair presentation -- Section 4.2 of ISO 19011:2018 requires auditors to ensure the audit report provides a fair presentation of findings, meaning the information must be accurate, unbiased, complete and supported by evidence, with any limitations or uncertainties clearly disclosed.
[4_3] 4.3 Due professional care -- It requires auditors to exercise due professional care by applying appropriate competence, diligence and judgment throughout the audit, maintaining integrity, confidentiality and impartiality, and ensuring that findings are based on reliable evidence.
[4_4] 4.4 Confidentiality -- The 4.4 Confidentiality clause in ISO 19011:2018 requires auditors to protect all information obtained during an audit, ensuring it is used only for audit purposes and not disclosed without proper authorization. It emphasizes the need for written agreements, secure handling of records, and adherence to legal and contractual confidentiality obligations.
[4_5] 4.5 Independence -- Section 4.5 of ISO 19011:2018 explains the principle of independence for auditors, requiring them to be free of any relationships or interests that could affect impartiality. It mandates the avoidance of conflicts of interest so that audit judgments remain objective and unbiased.
[4_6] 4.6 Evidence-based approach -- Section 4.6 explains that auditors must base their conclusions on objective, verifiable evidence collected during the audit, rather than on personal opinions or assumptions, ensuring audit findings are reliable, consistent and defensible.
[4_7] 4.7 Risk-based approach -- The entry 4.7 in ISO 19011:2018 explains that auditors should apply a risk-based approach, focusing audit planning and execution on areas where non-conformities could have the greatest impact on the management system's effectiveness. This method helps allocate resources efficiently and ensures that higher-risk processes receive proportionally more scrutiny.

[5_2] 5.2 Establishing audit programme objectives -- Section 5.2 of ISO 19011:2018 advises organizations to define clear, measurable objectives for each audit programme, ensuring they align with the overall management system goals, legal and regulatory requirements, and stakeholder expectations. It stresses linking objectives to the audit scope, criteria and desired outcomes to guide planning and performance evaluation.
[5_3] 5.3 Determining and evaluating audit programme risks and opportunities -- The entry outlines how auditors must identify and assess risks and opportunities that could affect the audit programme's effectiveness, using risk-based planning to prioritize resources, adjust audit scope, and implement controls that support achievement of audit objectives and continual improvement.
[5_4] 5.4 Establishing the audit programme -- Section 5.4 of ISO 19011:2018 outlines how an organization should create an audit programme, detailing the process for defining audit objectives, scope, frequency, resources and responsibilities to ensure systematic and effective auditing of management systems.
[5_5] 5.5 Implementing the audit programme -- Clause 5.5 of ISO 19011:2018 provides guidance on establishing and operating an audit programme, detailing the steps for planning, allocating resources, scheduling audits and monitoring performance to ensure they achieve the intended objectives and align with the organization's management system.
[5_6] 5.6 Monitoring the audit programme -- Section 5.6 of ISO 19011:2018 describes the processes for monitoring an audit programme, including collecting performance data, comparing results against audit objectives, and identifying any non-conformities or areas for improvement. It requires regular review of audit schedules, resources and outcomes, and calls for corrective actions and documented records to ensure the programme remains effective and aligned with organizational goals.
[5_7] 5.7 Reviewing and improving the audit programme -- Section 5.7 directs organizations to regularly evaluate the performance of their audit programme, analyse results and stakeholder feedback, and identify corrective actions. It stresses continual improvement by updating audit objectives, scope, criteria and resources to ensure future audits are more effective and aligned with the organization's strategic goals.

[6_2] 6.2 Initiating the audit -- In ISO 19011:2018, clause 6.2 outlines the process for initiating an audit, requiring the audit manager to define audit objectives, scope, criteria, and allocate resources, then issue an audit plan and notification to the auditee. It emphasizes clear communication and documented approval before audit activities begin.

[6_5] 6.5 Preparing and distributing the audit report -- It explains the auditor's responsibilities for drafting the audit report, ensuring it contains objective findings, conclusions and any non-conformities, and that it is reviewed for accuracy. The clause also outlines the process for promptly distributing the report to relevant parties while maintaining confidentiality and record-keeping requirements.
[6_6] 6.6 Completing the audit -- In ISO 19011:2018, clause 6.6 details the steps for completing an audit, requiring auditors to confirm that audit objectives were met, compile findings, and communicate results to the auditee. It also mandates proper documentation of conclusions, identified nonconformities and agreed corrective actions before formally closing the audit.
[6_7] 6.7 Conducting audit follow-up -- The entry outlines procedures for audit follow-up, requiring the auditor to verify that corrective actions have been implemented and are effective, to assess any residual risks, and to document the results. It emphasizes timely communication with auditee, monitoring of action plans, and closure of audit findings once compliance is confirmed. This ensures continual improvement and accountability within the management system.

It provides guidance on assessing these elements, selecting appropriate training, and maintaining competence through continual professional development.
[7_3] 7.3 Selecting appropriate audit team members -- The entry explains how to choose audit team members who possess the necessary competence, knowledge of the auditee's processes, and appropriate independence. It outlines the need to consider auditors' technical expertise, auditing skills, and any language or cultural requirements. It also stresses documenting the selection process to ensure the team can effectively evaluate the management system.

[7_5] 7.5 Maintaining and improving auditor competence -- Section 7.5 of ISO 19011:2018 outlines how organizations should ensure auditors retain and enhance the knowledge, skills, and attitudes needed for effective audits, recommending continual training, performance evaluation, and development plans, and emphasizing the link between competence and audit quality.
