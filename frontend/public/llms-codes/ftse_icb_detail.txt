[FI] ICB Industries -- FI denotes the ICB Industries group, which aggregates companies that produce industrial equipment, machinery, construction materials, and related services. It covers a broad range of manufacturing and infrastructure activities, from heavy engineering to building supplies, reflecting the core industrial sector of the economy.
[FI.01] Technology -- The FTSE Russell ICB code FI.01 denotes the Technology segment, covering companies that develop or manufacture hardware, software, and related services. This includes producers of computer equipment, telecommunications gear, semiconductors, and firms providing IT consulting, data processing, and internet services. It groups businesses whose primary activities revolve around the creation, maintenance, and support of technology solutions.
[FI.02] Telecommunications -- FI.02 in the FTSE Russell Industry Classification Benchmark (Detailed) identifies the Telecommunications sub-industry, grouping companies that provide voice, data, broadband and mobile services, as well as network infrastructure and related equipment. It covers operators, service providers and firms involved in the transmission of information.
[FI.03] Health Care -- FI.03 groups companies that deliver medical services, produce pharmaceuticals and biotechnology products, and manufacture health-care equipment and supplies. It includes hospitals, clinics, drug and biotech firms, medical device makers and related service providers.
[FI.04] Financials -- FI.04 in the FTSE Russell ICB Detailed classification denotes the Financial Services sub-sector within the broader Financials industry. It groups companies that provide investment, brokerage, asset management, and other financial intermediation services, distinct from banks, insurance and diversified financials. This code helps investors identify firms whose primary business is facilitating capital markets and managing client assets.
[FI.05] Real Estate -- FI.05 Real Estate covers companies that own, develop, manage or lease property, including commercial, residential and industrial real estate, as well as real estate investment trusts. It also includes firms providing related services such as property management and brokerage, focusing on income generation from rental or capital appreciation.
[FI.06] Consumer Discretionary -- FI.06 is the ICB detailed code for the Consumer Discretionary industry, covering firms that sell non-essential goods and services. It includes retailers, automotive manufacturers, leisure and entertainment providers, and other businesses whose demand rises with consumer confidence and disposable income. This sector is part of the broader Consumer sector in the FTSE Russell classification system.
[FI.07] Consumer Staples -- FI.07 in the FTSE Russell Industry Classification Benchmark detailed scheme identifies the Consumer Staples sector, grouping companies that manufacture essential products such as food, beverages, tobacco and household items. These firms are seen as non-cyclical because demand stays relatively stable regardless of broader economic swings.
[FI.08] Industrials -- FI.08 is a detailed sub-industry within the FTSE Russell Industrials sector that groups companies producing industrial equipment and machinery, such as electrical, mechanical and related industrial products used in manufacturing, construction and infrastructure projects. It covers firms that design, fabricate and sell a broad range of industrial goods and related services.
[FI.09] Basic Materials -- FI.09 is the ICB detailed code for the Basic Materials sector, grouping companies that extract, produce or process raw natural resources such as metals, minerals, chemicals, forestry products and building materials. It captures firms ranging from mining and oil-and-gas producers to commodity chemicals and paper manufacturers, reflecting the core supply side of industrial activity.
[FI.10] Energy -- FI.10 groups companies that explore for, develop and produce crude oil and natural gas, including the extraction of these resources and related field operations. It covers firms whose primary business is the upstream segment of the oil-and-gas value chain.
[FI.11] Utilities -- The FI.11 Utilities category groups companies that generate, transmit, and distribute electricity, gas, water, and waste-management services. These firms are typically regulated and provide essential infrastructure to residential, commercial, and industrial customers. The classification captures both traditional utilities and emerging renewable-energy providers.
