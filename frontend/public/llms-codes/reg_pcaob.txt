[as_1005] AS 1005 - Independence -- AS 1005 establishes the independence requirements for auditors of public companies, defining relationships, financial interests and other circumstances that could impair objectivity, and provides guidance on assessing, preserving and documenting auditor independence throughout the audit engagement.
[as_1010] AS 1010 - Training and Proficiency -- AS 1010 establishes requirements for auditors to acquire and maintain the knowledge, skills, and experience necessary to perform audits competently. It obligates firms to assess, document, and monitor each staff member's proficiency and to provide ongoing training and continuing-education programs that keep auditors current with evolving standards and techniques.
[as_1015] AS 1015 - Due Professional Care -- AS 1015 sets the requirement that auditors must exercise due professional care throughout the audit, meaning they must act with competence, diligence and professional skepticism in planning, performing and reporting. The standard details responsibilities for maintaining audit quality, supervising staff, documenting work and complying with all applicable PCAOB standards.
[as_1105] AS 1105 - Audit Evidence -- AS 1105, issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, establishes the auditor's responsibilities for obtaining sufficient appropriate audit evidence. It outlines the nature and extent of procedures required to support audit opinions, including considerations of relevance, reliability, and the use of testing, sampling, and analytical techniques.
[as_1201] AS 1201 - Supervision of the Audit Engagement -- AS 1201 requires auditors to establish appropriate supervision over audit engagements, ensuring staff are adequately trained, work is reviewed, and the engagement complies with PCAOB standards. It outlines the lead engagement partner's responsibilities and the documentation needed to demonstrate effective supervision.
[as_1301] AS 1301 - Communications with Audit Committees -- AS 1301 sets the requirements for auditors to communicate effectively with an audit committee, outlining the content, timing and form of reports on audit matters, financial statements, internal controls and significant accounting issues. It ensures the committee receives consistent, relevant information to fulfill its oversight responsibilities.
[as_2101] AS 2101 - Audit Planning -- AS 2101, the PCAOB's audit planning standard, sets requirements for auditors to develop a comprehensive audit plan that addresses the nature, timing and extent of audit procedures. It emphasizes risk assessment, coordination of team members and documentation of the plan to ensure an effective, efficient audit and to meet the PCAOB's objectives.
[as_2105] AS 2105 - Consideration of Materiality in Planning -- AS 2105 is a PCAOB auditing standard that requires auditors to assess materiality during audit planning, establishing quantitative and qualitative thresholds for misstatements that shape the nature, timing and extent of audit procedures. It guides the use of professional judgment to determine materiality levels that affect risk assessment and overall audit strategy.
[as_2110] AS 2110 - Identifying and Assessing Risks of Material Misstatement -- AS 2110, issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, provides guidance for auditors on how to identify and evaluate risks that could lead to material misstatements in financial statements. It outlines procedures for understanding the entity and its environment, assessing internal controls, and determining the nature, timing, and extent of substantive testing required to address those risks. The standard emphasizes a risk-based approach to planning and performing audits.
[as_2201] AS 2201 - Audit of Internal Control Over Financial Reporting (ICFR) -- AS 2201 is a PCAOB standard that requires auditors to evaluate a public company's internal control over financial reporting and to issue an integrated report covering both the financial statements and the related controls. The auditor must design procedures to test the effectiveness of the controls, determine whether material weaknesses exist, and communicate findings to management and the audit committee. The standard aligns the control assessment with the overall audit to enhance reliability of financial reporting.
[as_2301] AS 2301 - Auditor's Responses to Risks of Material Misstatement -- AS 2301, issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, provides guidance for auditors on how to respond to identified risks of material misstatement in financial statements. [as_2401] AS 2401 - Consideration of Fraud -- AS 2401 sets out the auditor's responsibilities for addressing fraud in a financial-statement audit, requiring assessment of fraud risk, design of appropriate substantive procedures, and communication of identified fraud to management and those charged with governance, while emphasizing professional skepticism throughout the engagement.
[as_2501] AS 2501 - Auditing Accounting Estimates -- AS 2501 provides guidance to auditors on evaluating and testing accounting estimates made by management, covering the nature of estimates, risk assessment and documentation requirements. It emphasizes professional judgment, the need to consider bias, uncertainty and changes in assumptions.
[as_2502] AS 2502 - Auditing Fair Value Measurements -- AS 2502 provides auditors with guidance on evaluating and testing fair-value measurements performed by management. It outlines procedures for understanding valuation techniques, assessing the reasonableness of inputs, reviewing model assumptions, and documenting the audit evidence required to support the fairness and reliability of reported amounts. The standard also addresses reporting responsibilities when fair-value estimates are complex or lack observable market data.
[as_2503] AS 2503 - Auditing Derivative Instruments -- AS 2503, issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, outlines the auditor's responsibilities and procedures for auditing a client's derivative instruments, covering identification, valuation, disclosure, and testing of related internal controls to ensure fair-value measurement and compliance with applicable reporting standards.
[as_2601] AS 2601 - Using the Work of Other Auditors -- AS 2601 sets requirements for auditors when they rely on the work of other auditors in a PCAOB audit. It outlines procedures for evaluating the other auditor's competence, independence and the adequacy of their work, and specifies the documentation needed to support that reliance.
[as_2605] AS 2605 - Consideration of the Internal Audit Function -- AS 2605 guides auditors in evaluating an entity's internal-audit function, outlining how to assess its competence, objectivity, scope and methodology, and explaining how the internal auditors' work can be relied upon to support the external audit objectives.
[as_3101] AS 3101 - The Auditor's Report on an Audit (Critical Audit Matters) -- AS 3101 establishes the format and content of the auditor's report for public-company audits, requiring a separate section that identifies and discusses critical audit matters, explains why each matter is considered critical, and describes the auditor's response. The standard also updates overall report language and disclosures to enhance transparency and comparability for users of financial statements.
[as_3105] AS 3105 - Departures from Unqualified Opinions -- AS 3105 sets forth the PCAOB's requirements for auditors when they cannot issue an unqualified opinion, detailing the conditions that lead to qualified, adverse, or disclaimer opinions and the specific language that must be used in the audit report. It guides auditors on how to communicate departures from a clean opinion while maintaining consistency with professional standards.
[as_3110] AS 3110 - Dating of the Independent Auditor's Report -- AS 3110, issued by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, establishes the requirements for dating an independent auditor's report. It mandates that the report be dated the day the auditor completes the audit fieldwork and is ready to express an opinion, ensuring the date reflects the most recent evidence obtained.
[as_6101] AS 6101 - Letters for Underwriters (Comfort Letters) -- AS 6101 establishes the audit firm's responsibilities when issuing comfort letters to underwriters, detailing the procedures, content, and limitations of such letters. It requires the auditor to confirm that the financial statements are fairly presented and to disclose any qualifications, while ensuring the letter is based on audit evidence obtained in accordance with applicable standards. The standard also guides communication between the auditor, issuer, and underwriter to manage reliance and liability.
[as_6105] AS 6105 - Reporting on Agreed-Upon Procedures -- AS 6105 is a PCAOB auditing standard that sets out the requirements for auditors who perform agreed-upon procedures engagements. It details the auditor's responsibilities, the types of procedures to be performed, and the format of the report, which must describe the procedures and findings without offering an opinion.
[evidence] Audit Evidence Standards -- Audit evidence standards set by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board define the nature, timing and extent of evidence auditors must obtain to support their conclusions. They require evidence to be sufficient, relevant and reliable, and specify procedures for gathering documentation, confirmations and analytical reviews.
[general] General Auditing Standards -- The General Auditing Standards set by the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board outline the fundamental requirements for performing audits of public company financial statements, covering auditor independence, due professional care, planning and supervision, and the evaluation of evidence. These standards ensure consistency, reliability, and accountability in audit practice across all PCAOB-registered firms.
[other] Other Standards -- The "Other Standards" category groups PCAOB auditing standards that do not fall under the main numbered sections, covering miscellaneous guidance such as updates to audit reports, quality-control requirements, and other specialized procedures that support overall audit practice.

[risk_assess] Risk Assessment Standards -- The PCAOB's risk-assessment standards require auditors to identify and evaluate the risks of material misstatement, including fraud, before planning detailed procedures. They set expectations for gaining an understanding of the client's business, internal controls, and the significance of identified risks, and for designing audit responses that are proportionate to those risks.
