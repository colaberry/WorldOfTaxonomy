[LS] LinkedIn Skills Categories -- LinkedIn Skills Categories (code LS) are the primary groupings within the LinkedIn Skills Taxonomy, organizing individual skills into broad thematic clusters such as Business, Technology, Creative, and Management. These top-level categories help users select relevant skills for their profiles and enable LinkedIn's matching algorithms to surface appropriate job opportunities, networking suggestions, and learning content.
[LS.01] Information Technology -- LS.01 Information Technology in the LinkedIn Skills Taxonomy (Skeleton) groups fundamental technical competencies such as computer hardware, operating systems, networking, software development, cybersecurity, data management and cloud services, providing a broad framework for categorizing IT-related skills across professional profiles.
[LS.02] Business Development -- LS.02 in the LinkedIn Skills Taxonomy (Skeleton) represents the Business Development skill category, encompassing activities such as identifying growth opportunities, cultivating client relationships, and formulating strategies to expand revenue and market presence.
[LS.03] Marketing and Advertising -- LinkedIn's LS.03 category groups professional capabilities related to planning, creating, and delivering promotional messages across media channels. It includes competencies such as market research, brand management, digital advertising, content strategy, and campaign analytics, covering both traditional and online marketing disciplines. This taxonomy entry helps users identify and showcase expertise in driving product awareness and customer engagement.
[LS.04] Finance and Accounting -- LS.04 in the LinkedIn Skills Taxonomy (Skeleton) designates the Finance and Accounting skill cluster, grouping competencies such as financial reporting, budgeting, cost analysis, tax preparation, auditing, and related financial management practices that support corporate and personal fiscal oversight.
[LS.05] Healthcare and Medical -- LS.05 groups skills associated with the healthcare and medical sector, encompassing clinical practice, patient care, medical terminology, health administration, public health, and related research and technical competencies. It serves as a reference for professionals and recruiters seeking expertise in various medical disciplines.
[LS.06] Engineering and Manufacturing -- LS.06 denotes the Engineering and Manufacturing category in LinkedIn's Skills Taxonomy, grouping together competencies for designing, developing, and producing mechanical systems, processes, and products. It also covers the oversight of manufacturing operations, planning, automation, and quality control activities.
[LS.07] Education and Training -- LS.07 Education and Training groups competencies related to designing, delivering, and evaluating instructional programs for learners of all ages. It covers curriculum development, teaching methods, adult-learning principles, training facilitation, instructional design, learning technology integration, and assessment techniques used in academic, corporate, and nonprofit settings.
[LS.08] Legal and Compliance -- LS.08 Legal and Compliance groups skills needed to interpret and apply laws, regulations, and internal policies, manage risk, ensure corporate governance, and oversee regulatory reporting. It includes expertise in contract management, compliance monitoring, legal research, and advising on statutory requirements across industries.
[LS.09] Human Resources -- LS.09 represents the Human Resources skill category in LinkedIn's Skills Taxonomy. It groups competencies related to recruitment, employee relations, talent-management, compensation, and compliance, helping users identify and showcase expertise in managing an organization's workforce.
[LS.10] Design and Creative -- LS.10 Design and Creative groups skills related to visual and artistic work such as graphic design, illustration, branding, user-experience design, and creative problem-solving. It captures abilities used in advertising, media production, product styling, and other fields that require aesthetic judgment and innovative thinking.
[LS.11] Sales and Retail -- LS.11 - Sales and Retail encompasses skills for promoting and selling products or services, managing customer relationships, handling transactions, merchandising, inventory control, and achieving sales targets across various retail environments. It includes knowledge of sales techniques, store operations, and customer service best practices.
[LS.12] Operations and Logistics -- LS.12 Operations and Logistics encompasses skills involved in planning, coordinating, and optimizing the flow of goods, services, and information within an organization. It includes expertise in supply chain management, inventory control, distribution, process improvement, and the use of logistics software to enhance efficiency and reduce costs.

[LS.15] Project Management -- Project Management is the discipline of initiating, planning, executing, monitoring, and closing projects to meet defined objectives within scope, schedule, budget, and quality constraints while managing risks, resources, and stakeholder expectations. It integrates methods and tools for coordinating tasks, teams, and deliverables to achieve successful outcomes.
[LS.16] Communication and Writing -- LS.16 "Communication and Writing" groups skills focused on conveying ideas effectively through spoken, written, and visual means. It covers abilities such as public speaking, business writing, storytelling, editing, and interpersonal dialogue, which are essential for collaboration, persuasion, and information sharing in most professional contexts.
