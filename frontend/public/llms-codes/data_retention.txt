[DR] Retention Periods -- The DR code marks the standard retention periods applied to data assets, indicating how long records must be kept before they may be archived or destroyed to meet policy or regulatory requirements.
[DR.01] Real-Time (No Retention) -- DR.01 designates a real-time data handling category in which information is processed instantly and not retained after use. It applies to systems that keep no logs, files, or records beyond the moment of processing, ensuring that no archival data is stored.
[DR.02] 24 Hours -- DR.02 designates a retention category where data is kept for a period of 24 hours before deletion, commonly applied to transient logs, temporary session information, or short-term monitoring records that are not needed beyond a single day. This interval balances operational utility with privacy and storage efficiency.
[DR.03] 30 Days -- DR.03 designates a data retention period of 30 days, meaning the information should be kept for no longer than one month before it is deleted, archived, or otherwise disposed of, and is commonly applied to short-term logs, temporary files, or other transient records.
[DR.04] 90 Days -- DR.04 designates a retention category in which records are kept for a period of ninety days before disposal or archival, typically applied to short-term operational data, preliminary reports, or temporary logs that are required for compliance monitoring but not for long-term reference. This timeframe balances accessibility for audit purposes with the need to limit storage of transient information.
[DR.05] 1 Year -- DR.05 denotes a data retention category where records are kept for a period of one year, after which they are typically reviewed for deletion, archiving, or extended retention based on legal or operational requirements. This timeframe is commonly applied to routine business documents, transaction logs, or temporary files that do not require long-term preservation.
[DR.06] 3 Years -- DR.06 designates a standard data retention period of three years. It is used in policies and guidelines to specify that records, files, or other information should be kept for exactly three years before they may be archived, destroyed, or otherwise disposed of according to applicable regulations.
[DR.07] 5 Years (HIPAA Minimum) -- DR.07 denotes a data retention period of five years, which aligns with the minimum requirement under HIPAA regulations for certain protected health information. Organizations must keep records for at least this duration to comply with federal standards.
[DR.08] 6 Years (UK GDPR) -- DR.08 denotes a standard retention period of six years, reflecting the UK GDPR requirement to keep personal data for the duration of statutory obligations such as tax, employment or contract enforcement. After the six-year term, the data must be securely deleted or anonymised unless a longer period is justified by another legal basis.
[DR.09] 7 Years (Financial/Tax) -- The DR.09 category designates a retention interval of seven years for financial and tax records, reflecting statutory requirements in many jurisdictions. Organizations assign this period to documents such as accounting statements, tax filings and related supporting paperwork to ensure compliance and readiness for audits.
[DR.10] 10 Years (Pharmaceutical) -- The DR.10 entry signifies a ten-year retention period for pharmaceutical data, covering records such as drug development, manufacturing, clinical trials, and regulatory compliance. Organizations must preserve these documents for a full decade to meet industry standards and legal obligations.
[DR.11] 15 Years (Medical Records) -- DR.11 denotes a standard retention interval of fifteen years for medical records. This period aligns with many national health regulations requiring long-term preservation of patient data for legal, research, and continuity-of-care purposes.
[DR.12] 25 Years (Nuclear/Environmental) -- DR.12 designates a data retention period of 25 years and is applied to records related to nuclear activities or environmental matters, reflecting regulatory or policy requirements for long-term preservation of such sensitive information.
[DR.13] Permanent/Indefinite -- DR.13 indicates that the data must be kept permanently or for an indefinite period, with no planned disposal date. This category is applied to records required for legal, historical, or archival purposes where loss could have lasting consequences. It signals that the information should remain accessible as long as it remains relevant or mandated by regulation.
[DR.14] Until Consent Withdrawn -- The "DR.14 - Until Consent Withdrawn" category denotes that personal or other data must be kept only while the data subject's consent remains active, and it must be deleted or anonymised as soon as that consent is revoked. This approach aligns with privacy regulations that require data retention periods to be tied to the purpose and the ongoing permission of the individual.
[DR.15] Until Purpose Fulfilled -- DR.15 denotes a retention schedule where records are kept only until the specific purpose for which they were collected is satisfied. Once that purpose is achieved, the data must be destroyed, anonymized, or otherwise rendered unusable.
