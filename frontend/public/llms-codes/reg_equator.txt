[cat_a] Category A - Projects with potentially significant adverse impacts -- Category A in the Equator Principles (EP4) identifies projects that could cause potentially significant adverse environmental or social impacts. Financial institutions must conduct a thorough risk assessment, develop detailed mitigation plans, and implement robust monitoring to ensure that such risks are managed throughout the project's lifecycle.
[cat_b] Category B - Projects with limited adverse impacts -- Category B under the Equator Principles (EP4) identifies projects that are expected to cause only limited adverse environmental or social impacts. Such projects typically require standard mitigation measures and monitoring, and they are generally considered acceptable within the financial industry's risk framework.
[cat_c] Category C - Projects with minimal or no adverse impacts -- Category C comprises projects assessed to have minimal or no adverse environmental and social impacts, requiring only standard due-diligence and routine monitoring. These activities are considered low risk and do not trigger additional mitigation or remedial actions under the Equator Principles framework.
[ep_1] Principle 1 - Review and Categorisation -- Principle 1 of the Equator Principles requires financial institutions to conduct an initial environmental and social risk assessment for each proposed project and to categorise it as A (high risk), B (medium risk) or C (low risk) based on the potential impact. This categorisation determines the level of due-diligence, mitigation measures and reporting required, aligning the lender's review process with recognized international standards.
[ep_10] Principle 10 - Reporting and Transparency -- Principle 10 requires project sponsors and lenders to disclose how environmental and social risks are identified, assessed and managed, and to provide regular, publicly accessible reports on the performance of mitigation measures. This transparency enables stakeholders to monitor compliance and fosters accountability throughout the project lifecycle.
[ep_2] Principle 2 - Environmental and Social Assessment -- Principle 2 of the Equator Principles obliges lenders to conduct a thorough environmental and social assessment of any proposed project before providing financing, ensuring that risks are identified, quantified and compared against accepted standards and the client's own policies. The assessment must be documented, disclosed to stakeholders and form the basis for any required mitigation measures.
[ep_3] Principle 3 - Applicable Environmental and Social Standards -- Principle 3 of the Equator Principles requires project sponsors to identify and adhere to all relevant environmental and social laws, regulations, and internationally recognised standards that apply to the financed activity. Financial institutions must verify that the borrower's management system incorporates these standards and that compliance is monitored throughout the project's life cycle.
[ep_4] Principle 4 - Environmental and Social Management System and Action Plan -- Principle 4 requires borrowers to establish and maintain an Environmental and Social Management System (ESMS) and an Action Plan that identify, assess, and mitigate environmental and social risks associated with a project. The ESMS must include clear responsibilities, monitoring, reporting procedures, and corrective actions to ensure compliance with the Equator Principles throughout the project's lifecycle.
[ep_5] Principle 5 - Stakeholder Engagement -- Principle 5 requires project sponsors and lenders to actively engage with affected stakeholders, disclosing relevant information, identifying concerns, and incorporating feedback into project planning and decision-making to mitigate environmental and social risks. This engagement should be transparent, inclusive, and documented, ensuring that the views of communities, NGOs, and other interested parties are considered throughout the project lifecycle.
[ep_6] Principle 6 - Grievance Mechanism -- Principle 6 of the Equator Principles requires lenders to establish a transparent grievance mechanism that enables affected communities and stakeholders to raise concerns about project impacts, ensures timely response and remediation, and integrates feedback into project management.
[ep_7] Principle 7 - Independent Review -- Principle 7 mandates that the project's environmental and social assessment be subjected to an independent review by a qualified third party, confirming that the analysis complies with the Equator Principles and relevant standards. The reviewer must provide written assurance of compliance and any recommended corrective actions.
[ep_8] Principle 8 - Covenants -- Principle 8 requires lenders to embed covenants in financing documents that obligate borrowers to identify, assess and manage environmental and social risks throughout the project life-cycle, and to provide regular reports and allow lender monitoring to ensure compliance with the Equator Principles.
[ep_9] Principle 9 - Independent Monitoring and Reporting -- Principle 9 requires lenders to arrange independent, third-party monitoring of a project's environmental and social performance throughout its life-cycle, and to ensure that the findings are reported regularly to all relevant stakeholders, including borrowers, financiers and affected communities. The monitoring must verify compliance with the Equator Principles, identify any deviations, and trigger corrective actions or remedial measures as needed.
[s_1] Project Finance (>USD 10M) -- The Equator Principles version 4 are a set of environmental and social risk management standards that financial institutions apply to project-finance transactions larger than ten million US dollars. Under code s_1, lenders must ensure borrowers conduct impact assessments, develop mitigation plans and adhere to internationally recognised safeguards throughout project development and operation. Compliance is monitored through independent reviews and ongoing reporting.
[s_2] Project-Related Corporate Loans (>USD 100M) -- s_2 refers to the Equator Principles EP4 category for large corporate loans tied to specific projects, defined as financing of at least 100 million US dollars. It applies the financial-industry framework for assessing and managing environmental and social risks that may arise from the funded project.
