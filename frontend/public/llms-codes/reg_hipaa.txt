It details the administrative requirements specified in section 164.530 of the law.
[priv_rights] Individual Rights (164.520-164.528) -- The HIPAA privacy rule sections 164.520-164.528 define the rights individuals have over their protected health information. They grant patients the ability to access, obtain copies of, and request amendment of their records, to receive an accounting of disclosures, to request restrictions on certain uses, to request confidential communications, and to be notified of breaches.
[priv_use] Use and Disclosure of PHI (164.502-164.514) -- The priv_use entry outlines the HIPAA rules for when covered entities may use or disclose protected health information without patient authorization, summarizing sections 164.502-164.514. It details permissible purposes such as treatment, payment, health-care operations, public-health activities, and required disclosures for law-enforcement, research and other legally mandated situations.
[rule_breach] HIPAA Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164 Subpart D) -- The HIPAA Breach Notification Rule mandates that covered entities and business associates promptly disclose any unsecured protected health information to affected individuals, the Secretary of Health and Human Services, and, when a breach affects 500 or more individuals, the media. Notifications must be sent without unreasonable delay, no later than 60 days after discovery, and must include a description of the breach, the types of information involved, steps taken to mitigate harm, and contact information for further inquiries.
[rule_enforcement] HIPAA Enforcement Rule (45 CFR Part 160 Subparts C, D, E) -- The HIPAA Enforcement Rule, codified at 45 CFR Part 160 Subparts C, D, and E, establishes the civil and criminal penalties for violations of the Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules, outlines procedures for investigations and hearings, and defines the authority of the Office for Civil Rights to impose fines and corrective actions. It details the process for reporting violations, the criteria for determining penalty amounts, and the obligations of covered entities and business associates to comply with enforcement actions.
[rule_omnibus] HIPAA Omnibus Rule (2013 Final Rule) -- The HIPAA Omnibus Rule, issued in 2013, updates the Privacy, Security, and Enforcement Rules to strengthen protections for individuals' health information. It expands the definition of covered entities to include business associates, tightens breach-notification requirements, and raises civil penalties for non-compliance.

It regulates how covered entities may use and disclose protected health information, grants patients rights to access and amend their records, and requires safeguards to ensure confidentiality.

[sec_101] Sec 101 - Increased Portability Through Limitation on Preexisting Condition Exclusions -- Section 101 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 expands portability by limiting insurers' ability to exclude coverage for preexisting conditions, generally capping such exclusions at a short period. This provision helps individuals maintain continuous health coverage when changing plans.
[sec_102] Sec 102 - Prohibiting Discrimination Based on Health Status -- Section 102 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 prohibits group health plans and health insurers from discriminating against individuals because of their health status, including pre-existing conditions, and requires that they offer the same coverage terms and benefits to all eligible persons.
[sec_103] Sec 103 - Guaranteed Renewability in Multi-Employer Plans -- Section 103 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 requires multi-employer group health plans to provide guaranteed renewal of coverage, meaning the plan cannot be denied renewal as long as the employer continues to meet its financial obligations. The provision protects employees by ensuring continuity of benefits when they change jobs within the multi-employer arrangement.
[sec_104] Sec 104 - Clarification of Certain Continuation Coverage Requirements -- Section 104 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 clarifies the rules governing continuation of health coverage when an individual experiences a loss of employment or other qualifying event. It specifies the timing, eligibility criteria, and the obligations of both insurers and employers to ensure uninterrupted coverage for affected persons.
[sec_201] Sec 201 - Fraud and Abuse Control Program -- Section 201 of the 1996 Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act establishes the Fraud and Abuse Control Program, directing the Secretary of Health and Human Services to develop and enforce measures that detect, prevent, and sanction fraud, waste, and abuse in health-care programs, including the creation of enforcement mechanisms, penalties, and oversight responsibilities for both public and private entities.
[sec_211] Sec 211 - Medicare Integrity Program -- Section 211 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act creates the Medicare Integrity Program, which coordinates federal and state efforts to detect, prevent, and correct fraud, waste, and abuse in Medicare. The program authorizes the Secretary of Health and Human Services to develop policies, conduct investigations, and impose penalties to protect the integrity of the Medicare system.
[sec_221] Sec 221 - Administrative Simplification -- Section 221 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act covers administrative simplification, establishing national standards for electronic health-care transactions, unique identifiers for providers, health plans and employers, and the privacy and security rules that protect individually identifiable health information. It aims to improve efficiency, reduce costs and enhance the confidentiality of patient data across the health-care system.
[sec_261] Sec 261 - Purpose of Administrative Simplification -- Section 261 defines the purpose of HIPAA's administrative simplification provisions, which is to create uniform standards for electronic health-care transactions, safeguard the privacy and security of patient information, and lower administrative costs for health-care providers, insurers, and clearinghouses.
[sec_262] Sec 262 - Code Sets and Transaction Standards -- Section 262 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act establishes the national standards for electronic health-care transactions, mandating the use of specific code sets-such as CPT, HCPCS, and ICD-and uniform data formats to ensure consistent, secure exchange of billing, eligibility, and other administrative information among providers, insurers, and clearinghouses.
[sec_263] Sec 263 - Privacy of Individually Identifiable Health Information -- Section 263 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act establishes federal protection for individually identifiable health information. It requires covered entities to implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards, limits uses and disclosures without patient authorization, and grants individuals rights to access, amend, and receive an accounting of disclosures of their medical records.
[sec_264] Sec 264 - Recommendations for Privacy Standards (Privacy Rule) -- Section 264 sets out recommendations for the HIPAA-Privacy Rule, establishing national standards that protect individually-identifiable health information. It defines permissible uses and disclosures, required safeguards, and individuals' rights to access, amend, and control their protected health information.
[sec_301] Sec 301 - Medical Savings Accounts -- Section 301 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 establishes medical savings accounts, allowing individuals to set aside pre-tax funds for qualified medical expenses and to facilitate consumer-driven health care options.
[sec_311] Sec 311 - COBRA Clarifications -- Section 311 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 clarifies how COBRA applies, specifying employer duties to provide continuation coverage, the eligibility of individuals and families, the maximum benefit duration, and the required notification procedures.
[sec_401] Sec 401 - ERISA Amendments for Group Health Plans -- Section 401 amends the Employee Retirement Income Security Act to set standards for group health plans, defining coverage requirements, nondiscrimination rules, and coordination with HIPAA provisions. It also outlines fiduciary duties, reporting obligations, and enforcement mechanisms for plan sponsors.
[sec_421] Sec 421 - Enforcement and Penalties -- Section 421 sets out the enforcement framework for HIPAA, giving the Department of Health and Human Services authority to investigate violations and levy civil monetary penalties. Penalties range from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with higher amounts for willful neglect and adjustments based on the size of the covered entity.
[sec_501] Sec 501 - Individual Health Insurance Tax Deduction -- Section 501 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 establishes a tax deduction for individuals who purchase qualified health insurance, allowing them to subtract premium costs from their taxable income, thereby reducing the overall tax liability. This provision is intended to encourage personal health coverage.
[sec_511] Sec 511 - Revenue Offset Provisions -- Section 511 of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 sets out revenue-offset provisions, describing how certain payments, penalties, or adjustments may be applied to reduce overall program costs, including mechanisms for recouping funds through premium, tax, or other financial adjustments.
[sec_admin_safeguards] Administrative Safeguards (164.308) -- Administrative Safeguards are the policies and procedures required by HIPAA to manage the selection, development, implementation, and maintenance of security measures protecting electronic protected health information. They include risk analysis, workforce training, incident response, and oversight responsibilities. These safeguards ensure that covered entities and business associates establish a security management process and assign a security official.
[sec_org_requirements] Organizational Requirements (164.314) -- Section 164.314 of the HIPAA Security Rule sets out the organizational requirements that covered entities and business associates must implement, including documented policies, procedures and oversight responsibilities to protect electronic protected health information. It also mandates workforce training, security incident response planning and periodic review of compliance measures to ensure ongoing risk management.
[sec_physical_safeguards] Physical Safeguards (164.310) -- Physical safeguards are HIPAA requirements that protect electronic protected health information by controlling physical access to facilities, equipment and media. They encompass facility access controls, workstation security, device and media controls, and policies for proper disposal of hardware and storage media.
[sec_policies] Policies and Procedures and Documentation (164.316) -- The entry "Policies and Procedures and Documentation" (164.316) identifies the HIPAA requirements for covered entities to develop, implement and maintain written policies, standards and procedures that protect the privacy and security of protected health information, and to keep appropriate documentation of those policies and any revisions.
[sec_technical_safeguards] Technical Safeguards (164.312) -- Technical safeguards under HIPAA's Security Rule (164.312) require covered entities and business associates to protect e-PHI through technology-based measures such as access controls, audit controls, integrity checks, and transmission security. These safeguards ensure that only authorized users can access data, that system activity is logged, that information remains unaltered, and that electronic communications are secured against unauthorized interception.
[title_1] Title I - Health Care Access, Portability, and Renewability -- Title I of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 sets national standards to improve health-care access by protecting the continuity of health-insurance coverage when individuals change jobs, limiting exclusions for pre-existing conditions, and ensuring policies can be renewed without discrimination.
[title_2] Title II - Preventing Health Care Fraud and Abuse; Administrative Simplification -- Title II of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act sets out provisions to prevent health-care fraud and abuse and establishes an administrative simplification program that standardizes electronic transactions, safeguards patient privacy, and enforces security standards.
[title_3] Title III - Tax-Related Health Provisions -- Title III of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act sets out tax-related health provisions, creating a refundable tax credit for small-business health insurance, allowing tax-free contributions to health savings accounts, and establishing rules for the tax treatment of medical savings and flexible spending accounts.
[title_4] Title IV - Application and Enforcement of Group Health Plan Requirements -- Title IV outlines how group health plan requirements under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act are applied and enforced, detailing the duties of the Department of Labor and the Secretary of Health and Human Services, the processes for filing complaints, investigations, and sanctions, and the mechanisms for ensuring plan compliance with coverage, eligibility, and privacy standards.
[title_5] Title V - Revenue Offsets -- Title V of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 sets out the revenue-offset provisions that fund the law's implementation, authorizing assessments on group health plans and health insurers and establishing procedures for collecting those fees.
