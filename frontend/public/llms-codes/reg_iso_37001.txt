[4_4] 4.4 Anti-bribery management system -- Section 4.4 of ISO 37001:2016 requires an organization to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an anti-bribery management system that integrates anti-bribery policies, procedures, and controls throughout its operations, ensuring alignment with the standard's overall requirements and the organization's risk profile.
[4_5] 4.5 Bribery risk assessment -- Section 4.5 of ISO 37001:2016 requires organizations to perform a systematic bribery risk assessment, identifying potential sources of bribery, evaluating their likelihood and impact, and documenting the results. The assessment forms the basis for establishing, implementing and maintaining appropriate anti-bribery controls and for ongoing monitoring and review.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Clause 5.1 requires top management to take an active role in the anti-bribery management system, establishing and communicating a clear anti-bribery policy, setting objectives, allocating resources and fostering a culture that prevents bribery throughout the organization.
[5_2] 5.2 Anti-bribery policy -- Entry 5_2 of ISO 37001:2016 requires the organization to develop a documented anti-bribery policy that reflects its commitment to preventing bribery, outlines prohibited conduct, and sets out the responsibilities of personnel at all levels. Top management must approve the policy, communicate it throughout the organization, and ensure it is regularly reviewed for effectiveness.
[5_3] 5.3 Anti-bribery compliance function -- The standard mandates that an organization set up an anti-bribery compliance function to oversee the anti-bribery management system, ensure policies are applied, monitor performance, and report non-compliance to top management. This function must have sufficient authority, resources and independence to act effectively.
