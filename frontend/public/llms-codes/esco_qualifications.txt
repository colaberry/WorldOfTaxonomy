[EQ] ESCO Qualifications -- EQ is the entry for ESCO Qualifications in the European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations framework. It defines a standardized set of identifiers for formal and informal qualifications recognised across EU member states, linking each qualification to the skills, competences and occupations it validates to support labour-market analysis and mobility.
[EQ.01] Bachelor Degrees -- EQ.01 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework refers to Bachelor degrees, which are higher education programmes typically lasting three to four years and awarding a bachelor's level qualification upon successful completion.
[EQ.02] Master Degrees -- EQ.02 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework designates Master Degrees, which are advanced academic programmes completed after a bachelor's qualification and usually last one to two years. They provide specialised knowledge, research competence and professional skills that enable graduates to pursue senior or specialised occupational roles.
[EQ.03] Doctoral Degrees -- Doctoral degrees represent the highest level of academic qualification in the ESCO framework, requiring several years of original research and the submission of a dissertation that contributes new knowledge to a field. Holders are prepared for careers in research, university teaching, and senior specialist or consultancy positions across industry and the public sector.
[EQ.04] Vocational Certificates -- EQ.04 designates vocational certificates, which are formal qualifications awarded after completing occupation-specific training programmes, usually at secondary or post-secondary level. These certificates validate practical skills and knowledge, and are recognised by employers and education systems across Europe as evidence of job-ready competence.
[EQ.05] Professional Diplomas -- EQ.05 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework designates professional diplomas, which are post-secondary, non-university qualifications that provide targeted practical training for specific occupations. These programmes typically last one to three years, combine theoretical instruction with hands-on experience, and are recognised across EU labour markets for entry-level and mid-career roles.
[EQ.06] Apprenticeship Certificates -- EQ.06 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework refers to Apprenticeship Certificates, which are formal documents confirming that an individual has completed a structured apprenticeship program combining workplace training with theoretical learning. These certificates validate the acquisition of specific occupational skills and knowledge recognized by employers and vocational education systems.
[EQ.07] Continuing Education Credits -- EQ.07 refers to Continuing Education Credits, a form of non-formal learning credit used to record participation in short-term training or professional development activities.
[EQ.08] Short-Cycle Tertiary -- Short-cycle tertiary qualifications are post-secondary programmes usually lasting one to two years that combine theoretical knowledge with practical skills for a specific occupation or further study. Positioned below bachelor's degrees in the European Qualifications Framework, they award credits and often lead to professional credentials.
[EQ.09] Post-Secondary Non-Tertiary -- EQ.09 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework denotes post-secondary non-tertiary education, covering programmes completed after secondary school that do not lead to a university degree. It includes vocational training, certificates, diplomas and other short-cycle qualifications aimed at specific occupations or skills.
[EQ.10] Upper Secondary Certificates -- EQ.10 represents upper secondary certificates, which confirm that a learner has successfully completed the secondary education stage that follows compulsory schooling. These qualifications, comparable to a high-school diploma, grant access to tertiary education and qualify holders for a range of skilled occupations across the European labour market.
[EQ.11] Recognition of Prior Learning -- EQ.11 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework designates the Recognition of Prior Learning, a systematic approach that assesses and validates skills and knowledge acquired through work experience, informal or non-formal education. It enables individuals to obtain formal credentials for previously unrecorded competencies, supporting career mobility, lifelong learning and alignment with European labour-market standards.
[EQ.12] Joint Degrees -- EQ.12 in the ESCO Qualifications Framework designates joint degrees, which are academic programmes delivered collaboratively by two or more higher-education institutions that award a single graduate with two separate qualifications, often across national borders, under a common curriculum and shared accreditation.
[EQ.13] Micro-Credentials (EU) -- Micro-credentials (EU) are short, focused learning units that certify the acquisition of specific skills or competencies and are designed to be stackable, portable and recognized across EU member states. They complement traditional qualifications, often delivered digitally, and support rapid upskilling and lifelong learning.
[EQ.14] Qualification Frameworks (NQF) -- EQ.14 refers to the system of national qualification frameworks that organise and compare educational credentials across the EU. It describes how qualifications are grouped by level, learning outcomes and descriptors, facilitating mobility, lifelong learning and labour market recognition.
