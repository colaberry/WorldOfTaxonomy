[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 requires the organization to identify and analyze internal and external factors that affect its AI management system, such as market conditions, regulatory environment, technological capabilities and stakeholder expectations. This analysis establishes the context in which AI processes operate and informs the definition of the system's scope, objectives and relevant requirements.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Entry 4.2 requires an organization to systematically identify all parties interested in its AI activities, assess their needs and expectations, and determine how those factors influence the AI management system. It advises monitoring changes in stakeholder requirements and integrating this insight into planning, risk evaluation, and continuous improvement.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the AIMS -- Entry 4.3 outlines the process for establishing the boundaries of an Artificial Intelligence Management System, specifying which organizational units, products, services, and activities are included. It requires analysis of internal and external issues, stakeholder needs, and regulatory requirements to define the scope clearly, ensuring that all relevant AI-related processes are covered by the management system.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Entry 5.1 requires top management to demonstrate active leadership and commitment for the AI Management System, ensuring it is integrated into the organization's strategic direction and daily operations. Leaders must establish an AI policy, set measurable objectives, allocate necessary resources, and communicate the importance of AI risk mitigation and continuous improvement throughout the organization. This commitment drives accountability and alignment with the standard's requirements.
[5_2] 5.2 AI policy -- Entry 5_2 requires an organization to establish a documented AI policy that defines the purpose, guiding principles, responsibilities and compliance requirements for AI use, aligning with legal, ethical and societal expectations. The policy must be approved by top management, communicated throughout the organization and periodically reviewed to ensure it remains effective and up-to-date.
[5_3] 5.3 Roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies that an organization must define and document the roles, responsibilities and authorities needed to operate an AI management system, ensuring clear accountability and appropriate competence for each function. It requires assigning authority for decision-making, oversight and resource allocation to support effective AI governance.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 requires the organization to define and implement concrete actions that eliminate or reduce identified AI-related risks and exploit identified opportunities, embedding these measures in processes, responsibilities and resources to ensure the AI system achieves its intended outcomes.
[6_2] 6.2 AI objectives and planning to achieve them -- The entry 6.2 in ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies that organizations must define clear AI objectives and develop a structured plan to achieve them, integrating risk assessment, resource allocation, and performance metrics
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- The 8.1 Operational planning and control clause requires organizations to define, implement and maintain processes for achieving AI-related objectives, allocating resources, and establishing criteria for performance, risk and compliance. It also mandates monitoring, measurement and corrective actions to ensure the AI management system operates effectively and adapts to changing conditions.
[8_2] 8.2 AI risk assessment -- Entry 8.2 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 defines the AI risk assessment process, requiring organizations to systematically identify potential hazards, evaluate their likelihood and impact, and determine risk levels for AI systems. It mandates documentation of assessment methods, criteria, and results, and integration of findings into the overall AI management system to support informed decision-making and continual improvement.
[8_3] 8.3 AI risk treatment -- Clause 8.3 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 describes how an organization must treat identified AI risks, requiring the selection, implementation and verification of risk-treatment measures, documentation of control objectives and responsibilities, and ongoing monitoring to ensure residual risk remains within acceptable limits.
[8_4] 8.4 AI system impact assessment -- Entry 8.4 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 requires organizations to conduct a systematic impact assessment of each AI system before deployment, examining effects on users, society, the environment and ethical considerations, and to document the findings and any mitigation actions in the AI management system.
[a10] A.10 Third-party and customer relationships -- A.10 defines the requirements for managing third-party and customer relationships within an AI Management System, ensuring that external providers and users are assessed for competence, security, and ethical compliance, and that contractual and communication processes address AI-related risks, responsibilities, and performance monitoring.
[a2] A.2 Policies related to AI -- The a2 entry details the policies that govern an organization's AI development, deployment and oversight, covering ethical principles, compliance requirements, risk-management and stakeholder responsibilities. It ensures AI activities align with the ISO/IEC 42001:2023 framework and support responsible AI practices.
[a3] A.3 Internal organization -- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 A.3 defines the internal organization required for an Artificial Intelligence Management System, specifying roles, responsibilities, authority and reporting lines that ensure effective governance of AI activities. It mandates documented structures, competence requirements and communication channels to align AI processes with the organization's policies and objectives.
[a4] A.4 Resources for AI systems -- A.4 specifies the resources required to develop, operate, monitor and improve AI systems, covering human expertise, technical infrastructure, data sets, tools, and supporting documentation. It outlines how organizations should allocate, maintain and evaluate these resources to ensure the AI management system functions effectively and complies with the standard's requirements.
[a5] A.5 Assessing impacts of AI systems -- ISO/IEC 42001:2023 outlines requirements for an Artificial Intelligence Management System. Clause A.5 requires organizations to systematically evaluate the social, ethical, economic and environmental impacts of AI solutions throughout their lifecycle, using defined metrics and stakeholder input to identify risks and benefits.
[a6] A.6 AI system lifecycle -- Entry a6 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 defines the AI system lifecycle, outlining the sequential phases of planning, design, development, deployment, operation, monitoring, maintenance, and eventual retirement. It emphasizes integrated governance, risk management, and compliance activities throughout each stage to ensure responsible and effective AI performance.
[a7] A.7 Data for AI systems -- Entry A.7 specifies the requirements for data used in AI systems, covering data acquisition, labeling, storage, and quality assurance, as well as governance practices to ensure relevance, accuracy, completeness, and bias mitigation. It also addresses privacy, security, and compliance with legal and ethical standards throughout the data lifecycle.
[a8] A.8 Information for interested parties of AI systems -- This clause requires organizations to identify and communicate appropriate information about AI systems to all interested parties, such as users, regulators and affected individuals. It specifies the content, format, timing and accessibility of disclosures to promote transparency, accountability and informed decision-making.
[a9] A.9 Use of AI systems -- A.9 Use of AI systems specifies the requirements for planning, deploying, operating and monitoring AI applications within an organization's AI Management System. It mandates documented purpose, suitability assessments, risk evaluation, transparency measures and ongoing performance review to ensure ethical, lawful and effective use of AI.
[annex_a] Annex A - AI Controls -- Annex A of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 outlines the specific AI controls that organizations must implement within an Artificial Intelligence Management System. It details requirements for risk assessment, data governance, model validation, monitoring, and corrective actions to ensure AI systems operate responsibly and comply with the standard's overall governance framework.
[annex_b] Annex B - AI Impact Assessment Guidance -- Annex B of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 provides guidance for conducting AI impact assessments, outlining procedures to identify, evaluate, and mitigate potential ethical, social, and environmental effects of AI systems. It details criteria for risk analysis, stakeholder involvement, documentation, and decision-making to ensure responsible AI deployment. This annex supports organizations in aligning AI initiatives with regulatory expectations and best-practice sustainability goals.
[b1] B.1 Overview of AI impact assessment -- The B.1 entry provides an introductory overview of AI impact assessment within the ISO/IEC 42001:2023 standard, outlining the purpose, scope, and significance of evaluating AI systems' effects on stakeholders, ethics, and compliance. It sets the context for subsequent requirements by describing key concepts, risk categories, and the role of assessment in a structured AI management system.
[b2] B.2 AI system impact assessment process -- The B.2 entry outlines the required process for assessing the potential impacts of an AI system throughout its lifecycle, including identification of risks, evaluation of ethical, social and environmental consequences, and documentation of mitigation measures before deployment. This systematic impact assessment supports compliance with ISO/IEC 42001:2023.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 outlines the processes for continual improvement of the AI management system, requiring organizations to monitor performance, identify nonconformities, and implement corrective actions. It emphasizes risk-based evaluation, learning from incidents, and updating policies to enhance effectiveness and compliance.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 directs organizations to identify and analyze internal and external issues, relevant interested parties, and the boundaries of their AI management system, ensuring that the system's scope aligns with strategic goals and regulatory expectations.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 outlines the leadership responsibilities required for an effective Artificial Intelligence Management System. It requires top management to demonstrate commitment, establish an AI policy aligned with organizational goals, ensure provision of resources, assign roles and authority, and promote a culture of ethical AI use throughout the organization.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies the planning requirements for an AI Management System, requiring organizations to define AI policy objectives, assess risks and opportunities, allocate resources, and establish processes that align AI initiatives with broader business goals and enable continual improvement.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 outlines the support mechanisms required for an AI management system, specifying the provision of resources, competence and awareness of personnel, effective internal and external communication, and the control of documented information. It ensures that organizations maintain the infrastructure and knowledge needed to implement, operate, and continually improve AI governance.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 outlines the operational requirements for an Artificial Intelligence Management System, detailing how AI processes must be executed, monitored, and controlled during use. It covers performance tracking, incident response, data handling, model deployment, and continuous improvement to ensure compliance and risk mitigation.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO/IEC 42001:2023 specifies how organizations must monitor, measure, analyze and improve the effectiveness of their AI management system, requiring defined performance metrics, regular internal audits, and corrective actions to ensure AI applications achieve intended outcomes while maintaining risk controls.
