[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- ISO 39001:2012 clause 10.1 requires an organization to identify any nonconformities in its road-traffic safety management system, document the nature and cause of each, and implement corrective actions to eliminate them. The corrective actions must be proportionate to the impact of the nonconformity, be verified for effectiveness, and be recorded to prevent recurrence. This process ensures continual improvement of safety performance.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 39001:2012 requires an organization to establish, implement and maintain a systematic approach for continually improving its road-traffic-safety management system. It mandates the use of performance data, internal audits, corrective actions and management reviews to identify opportunities and drive higher safety outcomes over time.

This understanding provides the foundation for establishing measurable goals, allocating resources, and planning actions that address the specific risks and opportunities within the organization's context.

This understanding helps align safety initiatives with stakeholder requirements and regulatory obligations.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the RTS management system -- The organization must define the boundaries and applicability of its Road Traffic Safety (RTS) management system, identifying the processes, activities, products and sites that are included while considering relevant internal and external issues. This documented scope ensures that the RTS system covers all areas where traffic safety risks can affect the organization's performance.
