[clause_7012] DFARS 252.204-7012 - Safeguarding Covered Defense Information (CDI) -- This clause requires contractors to protect covered defense information by implementing NIST SP 800-171 security controls, reporting cyber incidents within 72 hours, and ensuring subcontractors also comply. It establishes safeguarding, incident-reporting, and flow-down obligations for all DoD contracts involving CDI.
[clause_7019] DFARS 252.204-7019 - NIST SP 800-171 DoD Assessment -- DFARS 252.204-7019 obligates contractors and subcontractors to assess and report their compliance with NIST SP 800-171 controls for safeguarding covered defense information. The clause sets requirements for documenting the assessment, identifying any deficiencies, and implementing corrective actions within specified timelines. It also establishes reporting procedures to the Department of Defense to ensure consistent protection of controlled unclassified information.
[clause_7020] DFARS 252.204-7020 - NIST SP 800-171 DoD Assessment Requirements -- The DFARS clause 252.204-7020 establishes the Department of Defense's requirements for contractors to assess compliance with NIST Special Publication 800-171, which governs the protection of Controlled Unclassified Information in non-federal systems. It mandates reporting of assessment results, remediation plans, and periodic updates to a DoD designated authority to ensure ongoing security of CUI handled by defense contractors.
[clause_7021] DFARS 252.204-7021 - CMMC Requirements -- DFARS clause 252.204-7021 mandates that contractors and subcontractors performing work for the Department of Defense meet the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC) requirements specified in the solicitation. It defines the applicable CMMC level, assessment procedures, and obligations for protecting controlled unclassified information, and it allows contract termination or suspension for non-compliance.
[part_201] Part 201 - Federal Acquisition Regulations System -- Part 201 sets out the purpose, authority, and organization of the Federal Acquisition Regulations System, describing how the FAR and its supplements, including the Defense FAR Supplement, are applied to federal procurement. It defines the scope, applicability, and hierarchical relationship of acquisition regulations across agencies.
[part_204] Part 204 - Administrative and Information Matters -- Part 204 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement covers administrative and information matters related to defense procurement. It outlines policies for recordkeeping, reporting, and the use of electronic systems
[part_207] Part 207 - Acquisition Planning -- Part 207 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement outlines the requirements for acquisition planning in defense contracts, directing agencies to develop comprehensive, risk-based strategies that identify program objectives, schedule milestones, cost estimates, and source-selection criteria. It emphasizes coordination among stakeholders, documentation of decisions, and periodic updates to ensure alignment with mission goals and regulatory compliance.
[part_209] Part 209 - Contractor Qualifications -- Part 209 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement establishes the criteria for determining contractor eligibility and qualifications for defense contracts, including requirements for financial responsibility, past performance, and compliance with applicable laws. It outlines the procedures for evaluating and certifying contractors, covering debarment, suspension, and exclusion rules, and provides guidance on how agencies assess and document a contractor's suitability to receive government awards.
[part_211] Part 211 - Describing Agency Needs -- Part 211 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement outlines how agencies must define and document their acquisition requirements, including the preparation of need statements, market research, and justification of the need. It establishes procedures for describing the specific items or services required, assessing alternatives, and ensuring that the need is clearly articulated to support effective procurement planning.
[part_212] Part 212 - Acquisition of Commercial Products and Services -- Part 212 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement governs how the Department of Defense purchases commercial products and services, setting requirements for using commercial item contracts, market research, and pricing data. It outlines procedures that promote competition, streamline acquisition, and ensure compliance with applicable commercial practices while protecting government interests.
[part_215] Part 215 - Contracting by Negotiation -- Part 215 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement establishes the policies and procedures for awarding contracts through negotiated procurements, detailing how agencies must prepare solicitations, evaluate offers, conduct source selection, and document contract awards to ensure fair, competitive negotiations.
[part_217] Part 217 - Special Contracting Methods -- Part 217 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement outlines the use of special contracting methods in DoD procurements, detailing procedures for indefinite-delivery, indefinite-quantity contracts, multiple-award contracts, and other non-standard arrangements. It establishes criteria for selecting these methods, required documentation, and oversight responsibilities to ensure fairness, competition and cost-effectiveness.
[part_219] Part 219 - Small Business Programs -- Part 219 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement outlines the Department of Defense's policies and procedures for supporting small businesses in federal contracting. It details the goals for small-business participation, eligibility criteria, subcontracting requirements, and the administration of programs such as the 8(a) Business Development and HUBZone initiatives. The part also explains reporting obligations and the mechanisms for monitoring compliance with small-business utilization objectives.
[part_225] Part 225 - Foreign Acquisition (Berry Amendment, ITAR) -- Part 225 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets rules for foreign acquisition, incorporating the Berry Amendment's requirement to source certain materials domestically and enforcing compliance with the International Traffic in Arms Regulations. It establishes procurement policies that limit the use of foreign-made items, safeguarding national security and meeting statutory sourcing mandates.
[part_227] Part 227 - Patents, Data, and Copyrights -- Part 227 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets policy for handling patents, data, and copyrights in DoD contracts. It outlines rights in technical data, licensing and government use, and defines procedures for patent rights, data rights and copyright protections for contractors.
[part_231] Part 231 - Contract Cost Principles and Procedures -- Part 231 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement establishes the cost principles and procedures that govern the allowability, allocation, and reimbursement of contract costs incurred by contractors under Department of Defense contracts, aligning civilian cost accounting standards with defense acquisition requirements.
[part_232] Part 232 - Contract Financing -- Part 232 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement outlines the policies, procedures and authorities governing contract financing for Department of Defense procurements, detailing permissible funding sources, advance payments, progress payments and related financial limits. It establishes the mechanisms by which contractors receive funds and the requirements for documentation, certification and audit to ensure proper use of federal resources.
[part_234] Part 234 - Major System Acquisition -- Part 234 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement establishes policies and procedures for acquiring major defense systems, defining a major system as one that exceeds specified cost or performance thresholds and involves integrated hardware, software, and support. It outlines planning, development, testing, production, and sustainment requirements to ensure effective lifecycle management and fiscal responsibility.
[part_235] Part 235 - Research and Development Contracting -- Part 235 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement provides policies, procedures, and contract clauses governing research and development efforts by the Department of Defense, covering topics such as contract types, cost principles, data rights, milestones, and the transition from research to production. It ensures R&D work is managed consistently and aligns with defense acquisition objectives.
[part_237] Part 237 - Service Contracting -- Part 237 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets forth the policies, procedures and requirements for DoD service contracting, including definitions, contract types, competition standards, pricing arrangements, performance monitoring and contract administration, and provides guidance for procuring professional, technical, and support services.
[part_239] Part 239 - Acquisition of Information Technology -- Part 239 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets out the policies and procedures the Department of Defense must follow when acquiring information technology. It covers planning, procurement, licensing, cybersecurity, and sustainment requirements, ensuring that IT acquisitions meet mission needs and comply with applicable standards, and provides guidance on commercial items, software, and related services.
[part_242] Part 242 - Contract Administration and Audit Services -- Part 242 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement establishes the policies and procedures for contract administration and audit services within the Department of Defense, outlining the duties of contracting officers, requirements for contract modifications, and guidelines for conducting audits to ensure compliance and cost integrity.
[part_245] Part 245 - Government Property -- Part 245 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets forth policies and procedures for the acquisition, use, accountability, and disposal of government-owned property in defense contracts, outlining contractor and government responsibilities, record-keeping requirements, and reporting obligations to ensure proper stewardship of federal assets.
[part_246] Part 246 - Quality Assurance -- Part 246 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets forth quality assurance requirements for defense contracts, establishing standards for inspection, testing, and acceptance of supplies and services, and outlines contractor responsibilities for maintaining compliance and reporting.
[part_252] Part 252 - Solicitation Provisions and Contract Clauses -- Part 252 of the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement sets the standard solicitation provisions and contract clauses used in U.S. Department of Defense procurements, outlining mandatory language, definitions, and requirements that contractors must follow. It serves as a primary reference for incorporating legal, cost-allowance, and performance terms into defense acquisition contracts.
