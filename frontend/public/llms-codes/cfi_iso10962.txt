[D] Debt Instruments -- The D code in ISO 10962:2015 groups debt instruments, which are contractual claims obligating the issuer to repay principal and usually interest to the holder. It includes bonds, notes, loans, debentures, and other fixed-income securities issued by governments, corporations, or other entities.
[DA] Asset-Backed Securities -- DA designates asset-backed securities, which are debt instruments whose cash flows are derived from a pool of underlying assets such as consumer loans, credit-card receivables, or mortgages. The securities are structured so that payments to investors are serviced by the performance of those assets, providing a means to transfer asset-related risk and financing.
[DB] Bonds -- DB designates Bonds, a debt security in which an issuer obligates to repay the principal at maturity together with periodic interest payments. Under ISO 10962, bonds are classified as standardized debt instruments issued by governments, corporations or other entities to raise capital, distinguished by features such as coupon type, maturity, and seniority.
[DC] Convertible Bonds -- DC denotes convertible bonds, debt securities that can be exchanged for a predetermined number of the issuer's equity shares under specified conditions. They combine features of bonds and options, offering regular interest payments and the potential for conversion into stock.
[DD] Municipal Bonds -- DD denotes municipal bonds, debt securities issued by state, provincial or local governments to fund public infrastructure or services. They typically provide investors with tax-exempt interest income and are backed by the issuing authority's power to levy taxes or revenues.
[DM] Others (Debt) -- DM denotes debt instruments that are not otherwise classified under the CFI system, covering generic or miscellaneous debt securities such as unsecured bonds, notes or other obligations that lack specific features required for more detailed codes.
[DT] Money Market Instruments -- DT denotes short-term debt securities that are highly liquid, such as Treasury bills, commercial paper and certificates of deposit, used for cash management. These instruments generally mature within one year and are traded in money markets.

[DY] Mortgage-Backed Securities -- In ISO 10962 the code DY designates mortgage-backed securities, which are debt instruments backed by a pool of residential or commercial mortgages. Investors receive payments derived from the underlying mortgage cash flows, often structured in tranches that vary in risk and yield.
[E] Equities -- E identifies equity securities, which represent ownership interest in a company, including common and preferred shares, convertible equity, and other instruments that confer voting rights and participation in residual assets.
[EC] Common Convertible Shares -- EC denotes common convertible shares, which are ordinary equity securities that may be converted into a predetermined number of additional shares of the same issuer under specified terms. They provide holders with voting rights like regular common stock while offering the option to convert and potentially increase their ownership.
[ED] Depositary Receipts on Equities -- ED denotes depositary receipts that represent shares of foreign equities and are issued by a depositary bank, enabling investors to trade the underlying securities on a local market. They give holders the economic benefits of the underlying shares, such as dividends and voting rights, while simplifying settlement and custody.
[EF] Preferred Convertible Shares -- EF designates preferred convertible shares, a hybrid security that provides holders with a preferential dividend or fixed return like preferred stock and the right to convert the shares into a specified number of ordinary shares under predetermined terms, combining features of equity and convertible debt.
[EL] Limited Partnership Units -- EL denotes limited partnership units, representing an ownership interest in a limited partnership that confers a right to a share of the partnership's profits and, in some cases, capital, while limiting the holder's liability to the amount invested. These units are typically issued privately and are not listed on public exchanges.
[EM] Others (Equities) -- The EM code in ISO 10962:2015 groups equity securities that do not fit any other specific category, including atypical or special-purpose shares, hybrid equity instruments and other miscellaneous equity-type products. It serves as a catch-all for equities that cannot be classified elsewhere in the financial instrument taxonomy.
[EP] Preferred / Preference Shares -- EP identifies preferred or preference shares, a class of equity securities that receive priority over common stock for dividend payments and liquidation proceeds but typically carry limited or no voting rights, offering investors a more fixed income-like return while remaining subordinate to debt holders.
[ES] Shares (Common / Ordinary) -- ES designates common or ordinary equity shares that give the holder ownership in a corporation, typically including voting rights and the right to receive dividends. In the ISO 10962 classification these instruments are treated as standard common stock.
[EY] Structured Instruments (Equities) -- EY denotes structured instruments whose underlying assets are equities, encompassing securities that combine equity components with derivatives or other features to create customized payoff profiles. These products may include equity-linked notes, basket options, or hybrid bonds whose returns are tied to the performance of individual stocks or equity indices. It is a sub-category within the ISO 10962 classification of financial instruments.
[F] Futures -- F denotes futures, a standardized exchange-traded contract that obligates the holder to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price on a specified future date; the contract terms such as quantity, quality and settlement method are fixed by the exchange, making futures useful for hedging price risk or for speculative positions.
[FC] Commodity Futures -- FC is the code for a commodity futures contract, a standardized agreement to buy or sell a specified quantity of a physical commodity at a predetermined price on a set future date. These contracts are traded on exchanges, providing price discovery and risk-management tools for producers, consumers and investors.

[FM] Others (Futures) -- FM is the ISO 10962 code for futures contracts that do not fall into any other specific futures sub-category, such as commodity, interest-rate, equity or currency futures. It serves as a residual classification for all other types of futures instruments.

[HC] Non-listed Call Options -- HC identifies a non-listed call option, a derivative that gives the holder the right but not the obligation to buy an underlying asset at a set price before expiration, and is traded over-the-counter rather than on an exchange.
[HM] Others (Non-listed Options) -- HM designates financial instruments that are non-listed options not covered by any other specific category. It includes bespoke or over-the-counter option contracts on equities, commodities, interest rates, currencies or other underlying assets that are traded privately rather than on an exchange.
[HP] Non-listed Put Options -- HP designates a non-listed put option, an over-the-counter derivative that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell a specified underlying asset at a predetermined price before or at expiration. It is classified under the derivative category of the ISO 10962 financial instrument taxonomy.
[I] Spot -- I denotes a "Spot" instrument, meaning a transaction that settles on the same day or within the standard short settlement period (typically two business days) after trade execution. Spot instruments involve immediate delivery of the underlying asset and are distinguished from forward or derivative contracts that settle at a future date. This classification is used in ISO 10962 to identify instruments with prompt settlement.
[IC] Currencies (Spot) -- IC denotes a spot currency instrument, meaning a direct trade of one currency for another with delivery on the standard spot settlement date, typically two business days after the transaction. It covers outright foreign-exchange trades that are not structured as forwards, options, or other derivatives.
[IM] Others (Spot) -- IM is the ISO 10962 code for spot-market instruments that are not covered by any other specific category, serving as a catch-all for miscellaneous or unconventional cash-settled transactions.
[J] Forwards -- The code J in ISO 10962 denotes forward contracts, which are agreements to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a specified future date. These instruments are commonly employed for hedging or speculative purposes and are settled at maturity.
[JC] Currency Forwards -- JC designates currency forwards, which are bilateral contracts to exchange a set amount of one currency for another at a predetermined rate on a future date. These instruments are used to hedge exchange-rate risk or for speculative purposes and settle at maturity, typically without physical delivery.
[JF] Rates Forwards -- JF denotes a rates forward, a derivative contract in which two parties agree today on a future exchange of cash flows based on a reference interest rate. The agreement fixes the rate for the underlying loan or deposit at the forward's settlement date, allowing parties to hedge or speculate on future interest-rate movements, with settlement typically in cash or by physical delivery of the notional amount.
[JM] Others (Forwards) -- JM identifies forward contracts that do not fit into any of the more specific forward categories defined in the ISO 10962 taxonomy, covering all other forward transactions regardless of underlying asset, price or settlement terms.
[K] Strategies -- K identifies the strategic purpose of a financial instrument, covering approaches such as investment, hedging, arbitrage or speculation. It signals the intended economic use of the instrument within a portfolio or market context rather than its structural characteristics.
[KE] Equity Strategies -- KE designates equity strategies, covering investment approaches that select, weight, and manage stocks or equity derivatives to meet specific objectives. It includes active or passive funds, long-only, long/short, market-neutral and factor-based equity portfolios, encompassing equity securities and related derivatives.
[KM] Others (Strategies) -- KM designates financial instruments that are strategy-based products not captured by other specific ISO 10962 categories, including custom baskets, hedging or replication schemes and other composite approaches that combine multiple underlying assets. It serves as a catch-all for innovative or unconventional strategy instruments.
[KR] Rates Strategies -- KR denotes Rates Strategies, a category of financial instruments whose returns are derived from movements in interest rates. It includes products such as interest-rate swaps, caps, floors, collars and other derivatives that hedge or speculate on changes in benchmark rates.
[L] Financing -- The code L denotes financing instruments, which represent the provision of capital to borrowers and include loans, bonds, credit facilities and similar debt-raising arrangements, typically involving repayment of principal with interest.

[LM] Others (Financing) -- LM denotes financial instruments that are financing-related but do not fit any specific predefined category in the ISO 10962 classification. It is used for miscellaneous or hybrid financing arrangements such as bespoke loan structures, certain asset-backed securities, or other non-standard debt instruments, providing a catch-all label for "others (financing)".
[LR] Repos -- LR identifies repurchase agreements, a short-term secured financing arrangement in which a seller transfers a security to a buyer with a commitment to repurchase it at a specified future date and price, usually higher. These instruments provide liquidity and are classified as money-market securities under ISO 10962.
[M] Others / Miscellaneous -- M designates financial instruments that do not fit into any of the defined primary categories, serving as a catch-all for miscellaneous or hybrid securities such as structured products, unconventional derivatives and other atypical instruments.
[O] Listed Options -- The code O identifies listed options, which are exchange-traded derivative contracts giving the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price before a set expiry date. These instruments are standardized, centrally cleared and provide transparent, liquid market access for investors.
[OC] Call Options -- OC designates a call option, a derivative that grants the holder the right but not the obligation to purchase the underlying asset at a specified strike price within a set time frame. It is used for hedging or speculative strategies and falls under the equity-derivative category in the ISO 10962 classification.

[OP] Put Options -- The OP code identifies a put option, a derivative that grants the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell a specified underlying asset at a predetermined price on or before a set expiration date. It is used for hedging downside risk or for speculative purposes and is classified under the "Options - Put" category in ISO 10962.
[R] Entitlements (Rights) -- R designates instruments that confer a contractual right or entitlement rather than direct ownership, such as warrants, options, and other claims to receive cash, assets or services under specified conditions.
[RA] Allotment Rights -- RA denotes allotment rights, which give the holder the entitlement to subscribe for newly issued shares or other securities, typically at a predetermined price and within a set time frame. These rights are often issued to existing shareholders to maintain their proportional ownership when a company raises capital.

[RP] Purchase Rights -- RP indicates purchase rights, a type of derivative that gives the holder a contractual entitlement to acquire a specified financial instrument, such as equity, at a predetermined price and within a defined time window, often issued as a component of securities offerings.
[RS] Subscription Rights -- RS designates subscription rights, which are securities granting the holder the right to subscribe for additional shares or other instruments, often issued in a rights offering to existing shareholders. These rights may be transferable and are exercised within a specified period.
[RW] Warrants -- RW denotes warrants, which are derivative securities that give the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price before expiration. Warrants are typically issued by the company itself, may be listed or over-the-counter, and are classified separately from options.
[S] Swaps -- S denotes swaps, a class of derivative contracts in which two parties exchange cash-flows based on underlying variables such as interest rates, currencies, credit spreads or commodities. Swaps are used for hedging, speculation or restructuring of financial positions and can be cleared through a central counterparty or arranged bilaterally.
[SC] Currency Swaps -- SC denotes a currency swap, a bilateral agreement in which counterparties exchange principal and interest payments in different currencies according to predefined terms. The swap typically involves one party paying interest in one currency while receiving interest in another, with exchange of the notional amounts at inception and maturity to hedge exchange-rate risk or obtain cheaper financing.
[SD] Commodity Swaps -- SD designates a commodity swap, a derivative contract in which two parties agree to exchange cash flows based on the price performance of a specified physical commodity. One leg typically pays a fixed rate while the other pays a floating rate linked to the commodity's spot or index price, allowing participants to hedge or speculate on commodity price movements.
[SE] Equity Swaps -- SE identifies an equity swap, a bilateral derivative contract in which one party receives the total return of a specified equity or equity index while paying a fixed or floating cash-flow stream, allowing participants to gain or hedge equity exposure without owning the underlying securities.
[SM] Others (Swaps) -- The ISO 10962 code SM is used for swap contracts that do not fit into any of the more specific swap categories defined in the standard. It groups together miscellaneous swap arrangements such as total-return, equity, commodity or other bespoke swaps that are otherwise unclassified.
[SR] Rates Swaps -- SR denotes rate swaps, which are derivative contracts in which two parties exchange cash flows based on different interest rate bases, typically swapping a fixed rate for a floating rate. These instruments are used to manage interest rate exposure or to speculate on rate movements.
[T] Referential Instruments -- In ISO 10962 the code T identifies referential instruments, which are securities whose payoff is linked to a reference value such as an index, rate, or benchmark rather than direct ownership of an underlying asset. Examples include swaps, futures and options that reference those external measures.
[TB] Baskets -- TB designates a basket, which is a collection of financial instruments combined into a single investment vehicle, typically used to diversify risk or track a specific market segment. Baskets may be structured as funds, indices, or synthetic products, giving investors exposure to multiple underlying assets
[TC] Currencies (Referential) -- TC denotes referential currencies, which are foreign-exchange units used only as a reference for pricing, valuation or accounting purposes and are not intended for direct settlement or trading. These codes identify currencies that serve as benchmarks in financial instruments without representing an actual tradable asset.
[TI] Indices -- TI identifies a financial instrument that is an index, a statistical composite that tracks the performance of a specified group of assets, markets or economic variables. It is not a tradable security itself but serves as reference for derivatives, funds and performance benchmarks.
[TM] Others (Referential) -- TM is a catch-all category for financial instruments that do not fit any specific ISO 10962 class and are identified mainly by reference to external sources such as indices, benchmarks or bespoke arrangements. It groups referential instruments whose defining features are derived from the referenced entity rather than intrinsic characteristics.
