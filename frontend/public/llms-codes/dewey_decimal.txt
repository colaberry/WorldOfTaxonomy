[DD] DDC Main Classes -- The DDC Main Classes are the ten broad categories that form the foundation of the Dewey Decimal Classification system, each covering a thousand-number range from 000 to 900. They organize all knowledge into General Works, Philosophy, Religion, Social Sciences, Language, Natural Sciences, Applied Sciences, Arts, Literature, and History & Geography, providing a hierarchical framework for more specific subclassifications.
[DD.000] Computer Science, Info, General -- DD.000 is the top-level class for computer science and information technology in the Dewey Decimal Classification skeleton. It covers general topics, foundations, and introductory material related to computing, data processing, and information systems.
[DD.100] Philosophy and Psychology -- DD.100 in the Dewey Decimal Classification denotes the broad class for Philosophy and Psychology, encompassing works on philosophical systems, logic, ethics, epistemology, metaphysics, as well as the scientific study of the mind, behavior, and mental processes. It serves as the primary category for texts that explore fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, and human experience.
[DD.200] Religion -- DD.200 is the primary DDC class for Religion, encompassing works on religious beliefs, practices, theology, and the comparative study of world faiths, as well as the history, philosophy, and social aspects of religion.
[DD.300] Social Sciences -- DD.300 designates the Social Sciences, encompassing works that examine human society, its institutions, and relationships. It includes subfields such as sociology, anthropology, political science, economics, and social research methods, organizing material on social theory, cultural patterns, and collective behavior.
[DD.400] Language -- DD.400 represents the broad discipline of language in the Dewey Decimal system, encompassing the scientific study of human communication, its structure, history, and classification. It includes subfields such as phonetics, grammar, semantics, sociolinguistics, and the comparison of language families.
[DD.500] Science -- DD.500 in the Dewey Decimal Classification denotes the main class for natural sciences and mathematics, covering subjects such as physics, chemistry, biology, and related interdisciplinary fields. It serves as the central hub for scientific knowledge in libraries, with more specific subclasses ranging from 510 to 590.
[DD.600] Technology -- DD.600, the Technology class in the Dewey Decimal system, gathers works on applied sciences, engineering, and industrial processes. It includes broad topics such as mechanical, civil, and chemical engineering, as well as manufacturing, inventions, and practical uses of scientific knowledge. This main class is subdivided into specific fields that address particular technical disciplines and their real-world applications.
[DD.700] Arts and Recreation -- The 700 class in the Dewey Decimal system covers arts and recreation, encompassing visual and performing arts, music, theater, film, and a range of leisure and sporting activities. It groups together subjects that explore creative expression, cultural performances, and organized play, providing a broad framework for works on artistic practice, criticism, history, and recreational pursuits.
[DD.800] Literature -- DD.800 in the Dewey Decimal Classification covers literature, grouping works of poetry, drama, fiction, essays, and literary criticism. It organizes texts by language and form, providing a systematic location for creative and scholarly writing across cultures. This class serves as the primary hub for all written artistic expression in library collections.
[DD.900] History and Geography -- DD.900 in the Dewey Decimal Classification skeleton groups together broad works on history and geography, offering general surveys, overviews and introductory material about world events, regions, and physical landscapes. It serves as a catch-all for interdisciplinary texts that do not fit into more specific historical or geographical subclasses.
