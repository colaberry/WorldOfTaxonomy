[DC] DigComp Areas -- DC in DigComp 2.2 identifies the overall framework of digital competence, grouping the five competence areas that define the standard. It covers information and data literacy, communication and collaboration, digital content creation, safety, and problem solving, providing a structure for assessing
[DC.1] Information and data literacy -- DC.1 Information and data literacy refers to the competence to identify, locate, retrieve, evaluate and manage digital information and data, understanding its relevance, credibility and ethical use. It includes recognizing how data are produced, selecting appropriate sources, and organizing information for effective communication and problem-solving.
[DC.1.1] Browsing, searching, filtering -- DC.1.1 focuses on the ability to efficiently browse digital environments, formulate effective search queries, and apply filters to locate relevant information, demonstrating basic proficiency in navigating and extracting content from the internet and other digital repositories.
[DC.1.2] Evaluating data -- DC.1.2 focuses on the ability to assess data for relevance, quality and reliability, applying criteria such as accuracy, timeliness, bias and ethical considerations. It includes selecting appropriate evaluation methods, interpreting results and judging the significance of data to support informed decisions.
[DC.1.3] Managing data -- DC.1.3 Managing data covers the ability to locate, select, store, organise, retrieve and protect digital information, applying appropriate methods for data handling, ensuring accuracy, security and compliance with legal and ethical standards.
[DC.2] Communication and collaboration -- DC.2 in DigComp 2.2 refers to Communication and Collaboration, encompassing the skills needed to effectively exchange information, interact and work together using digital technologies, including selecting appropriate channels, respecting netiquette, co-creating content, and managing collaborative processes online.
[DC.2.1] Interacting through technologies -- DC.2.1 Interacting through technologies denotes the ability to communicate, collaborate and exchange information using digital tools such as email, social media, video-conferencing and collaborative platforms, while applying appropriate netiquette, privacy and security practices.
[DC.2.2] Sharing through technologies -- DC.2.2 - Sharing through technologies describes the competence to distribute, exchange and collaborate on digital content using online platforms and tools, while applying appropriate licensing, respecting intellectual property rights and ensuring accessibility for intended audiences.
[DC.2.3] Engaging in citizenship -- DC.2.3 Engaging in citizenship covers the ability to use digital technologies to take part in public life, exercise rights and responsibilities, and contribute to community and societal decision-making through online platforms, e-voting, advocacy, and informed participation in digital public discourse.
[DC.2.4] Collaborating through technologies -- DC.2.4 refers to the ability to use digital tools and platforms to work together with others, share resources, co-create content, and manage joint tasks while following appropriate communication and collaboration norms. It covers selecting suitable technologies, coordinating contributions, and maintaining effective, secure interactions in virtual teams.
[DC.2.5] Netiquette -- Netiquette refers to the etiquette rules that guide respectful and effective communication in digital environments, covering appropriate language, tone, privacy, and the responsible use of symbols such as emojis. Mastering netiquette helps individuals maintain professional, courteous behavior across online platforms.
[DC.2.6] Managing digital identity -- DC.2.6 Managing digital identity covers the ability to create, maintain and protect one's online presence, including personal data, privacy settings and reputation across digital platforms. It involves understanding how personal information is shared, applying security measures and responsibly curating one's digital footprint.
[DC.3] Digital content creation -- DC.3 Digital content creation covers the skill of producing and editing various forms of digital media-including text, images, audio, video, and animation-by using suitable software tools and applying basic design, copyright, and accessibility principles.
[DC.3.1] Developing digital content -- Developing digital content involves planning, creating, editing and publishing information in digital formats such as text, images, audio, video or interactive media using appropriate software tools and platforms. It includes adapting existing resources, combining different media, applying basic design principles and ensuring the content is accessible and suitable for the intended audience.
[DC.3.2] Integrating and re-elaborating -- Integrating and re-elaborating is the competence to select, adapt and combine existing digital resources, data and tools to produce new content, ensuring that the resulting material respects copyright, licensing and quality standards.
[DC.3.3] Copyright and licenses -- DC.3.3 covers the legal and ethical aspects of using digital content, teaching learners to recognize copyright rules, distinguish between different types of licenses, and apply proper attribution when reusing material. It also emphasizes respecting creators' rights and navigating open-access resources.
[DC.3.4] Programming -- Programming in the DigComp 2.2 framework refers to the ability to create, adapt, test and debug simple computer programs or scripts, using appropriate languages and tools. It involves understanding algorithmic logic, applying basic coding concepts, and troubleshooting errors to achieve functional digital solutions.
[DC.4] Safety -- DC.4 Safety refers to the knowledge, skills and attitudes needed to protect devices, personal data and digital identities from threats, and to use digital tools responsibly. It includes understanding privacy settings, recognizing phishing attempts, applying security measures such as passwords and updates, and promoting safe online behaviour. This competence helps individuals mitigate risks and maintain trust in digital environments.
[DC.4.1] Protecting devices -- DC.4.1 Protecting devices refers to the competence to secure hardware and software against threats by applying appropriate safeguards such as regular updates, antivirus tools, strong authentication, encryption and safe configuration, ensuring that personal and organisational devices remain functional and resilient in digital environments.
[DC.4.2] Protecting personal data -- DC.4.2 focuses on safeguarding personal data when using digital technologies, including understanding data protection principles, applying privacy settings, and ensuring secure storage and transmission. It involves recognizing legal frameworks, managing consent, and mitigating risks of unauthorized access.
[DC.4.3] Protecting health and well-being -- Protecting health and well-being refers to the ability to use digital technologies responsibly to safeguard personal and others' physical and mental health, recognize and mitigate online risks such as misinformation, cyber-bullying, and excessive screen time, and promote healthy digital habits.
[DC.4.4] Protecting the environment -- DC.4.4 addresses the use of digital technologies to protect the environment, including applying digital tools for monitoring resources, reducing carbon footprints, and promoting sustainable practices. It emphasizes understanding the environmental impact of digital activities and adopting eco-friendly behaviours such as recycling e-waste, optimizing energy use and supporting green initiatives online.
[DC.5] Problem solving -- DC.5 Problem solving denotes the capacity to recognize digital challenges, select and apply suitable tools or strategies, and create effective solutions, including troubleshooting, adapting resources and evaluating outcomes within digital environments.
[DC.5.1] Solving technical problems -- DC.5.1 focuses on the ability to detect, analyse and resolve technical issues that arise when using digital devices, software or networks, applying systematic troubleshooting steps, selecting appropriate resources and documenting solutions.
[DC.5.2] Identifying needs and responses -- DC.5.2 in DigComp 2.2 refers to the capability to recognise a problem, opportunity or requirement and to determine the appropriate digital actions or resources needed to address it, including assessing potential solutions and planning an effective response.
[DC.5.3] Creatively using technologies -- DC.5.3 refers to the skill of applying digital tools in innovative ways to generate original content, solve problems or develop new ideas. Learners show flexibility in selecting, combining and adapting technologies to different contexts, experimenting beyond standard uses.
[DC.5.4] Identifying digital competence gaps -- DC.5.4 refers to the ability to recognise where personal or others' digital skills are lacking, to assess current competence levels and to pinpoint specific areas for development. It involves using self-assessment tools or feedback to map gaps and inform targeted learning plans.
