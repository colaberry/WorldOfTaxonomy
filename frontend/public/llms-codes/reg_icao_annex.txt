It guides member states in harmonising regulations to protect populations from aviation-related acoustic impacts.
[16_2] Volume II - Aircraft Engine Emissions -- ICAO Annexes to the Convention on International Civil Aviation compile the organization's standards and recommended practices for global aviation. Volume II, identified by code 16_2, addresses aircraft engine emissions, specifying measurement methods, emission limits and compliance procedures aimed at reducing pollutants such as NOx, CO, HC and particulate matter from civil aircraft engines.
[16_3] Volume III - Aeroplane CO2 Emissions -- It details standards and recommended practices for measuring, reporting and reducing carbon-dioxide emissions from aircraft, forming part of ICAO's environmental sustainability framework. The guidance supports the global push for greener aviation by giving operators and states methods to monitor performance and meet emission-reduction targets.
[16_4] Volume IV - CORSIA -- Volume IV - CORSIA is part of the ICAO Annex series to the Convention on International Civil Aviation. It sets out the global Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International Aviation, detailing standards, monitoring, reporting and verification requirements for airlines to offset CO₂ emissions from international flights, and provides guidance on implementation, compliance and market mechanisms.

[6_2] Part II - International General Aviation -- Part II of ICAO's Annexes to the Convention on International Civil Aviation sets standards and recommended practices for international general aviation, covering aircraft certification, airworthiness, flight operations, navigation, communications and safety oversight for non-commercial flights among member states.
[6_3] Part III - International Operations - Helicopters -- ICAO Annexes to the Convention on International Civil Aviation, entry 6_3, covers Part III which sets standards and recommended practices for international helicopter operations. The annex also outlines procedures for cross-border helicopter flights, air traffic services, and emergency response coordination.
[ann_1] Annex 1 - Personnel Licensing -- Annex 1 sets the international standards and recommended practices for the licensing of aircrew, including pilots, flight engineers and other personnel, covering qualifications, training, medical fitness, issuance, renewal and privileges. It provides a uniform framework for member states to certify and maintain competence, thereby promoting safety and interoperability in civil aviation worldwide.
[ann_10] Annex 10 - Aeronautical Telecommunications -- Annex 10 to the ICAO Convention establishes international standards and recommended practices for aeronautical telecommunications, covering voice and data communication, radio navigation, surveillance, and frequency management to ensure safe, efficient global air-traffic operations. It specifies equipment performance, spectrum use, and procedures for air navigation service providers.
[ann_11] Annex 11 - Air Traffic Services -- Annex 11 of the ICAO Convention sets the standards and recommended practices for air traffic services, defining the responsibilities of service providers, procedures for air traffic control, flight information, alerting, and the required communication, navigation and surveillance infrastructures to ensure safe and efficient aircraft movements worldwide.
[ann_12] Annex 12 - Search and Rescue -- Annex 12 to the ICAO Convention on International Civil Aviation establishes the standards and recommended practices for air-search and rescue, defining state responsibilities, SAR region coordination, operational procedures and the use of aircraft, ships and other resources to locate and assist distressed aircraft and persons in need.
[ann_13] Annex 13 - Aircraft Accident and Incident Investigation -- Annex-13 to the Chicago Convention sets standards and recommended practices for the investigation of aircraft accidents and serious incidents. It defines state responsibilities, outlines procedures for collecting and analysing evidence, and provides guidelines for reporting findings to improve aviation safety.
[ann_14] Annex 14 - Aerodromes -- Annex 14 to the Chicago Convention sets international standards and recommended practices for the planning, design, construction, operation and maintenance of aerodromes, detailing runway and taxiway dimensions, lighting, markings, signage, rescue-fire services and safety management to ensure uniform safety and efficiency in civil aviation worldwide.
[ann_15] Annex 15 - Aeronautical Information Services -- Annex 15 sets standards and recommended practices for the provision of aeronautical information, covering the collection, processing, dissemination and management of data needed for safe and efficient flight operations. It defines responsibilities of service providers, data formats, and procedures for updating charts, notices and flight planning information.
[ann_16] Annex 16 - Environmental Protection -- Annex 16 is the International Civil Aviation Organization's environmental protection module, establishing global standards and recommended practices to limit aviation-related noise and air-pollutant emissions, and providing guidelines for measuring, monitoring and mitigating these impacts.
[ann_17] Annex 17 - Security -- Annex 17 to the ICAO Convention on International Civil Aviation sets out standards and recommended practices for safeguarding civil aviation against acts of unlawful interference. It covers security measures for aircraft, airports, personnel, cargo and supporting infrastructure, requiring member states to adopt risk-based security programmes and maintain coordinated protection across the aviation system.
[ann_18] Annex 18 - Safe Transport of Dangerous Goods by Air -- Annex 18 to the Chicago Convention establishes standards and recommended practices for the safe transport of dangerous goods by air, covering classification, packaging, labeling, handling and emergency response. It aligns with ICAO's safety framework and is regularly updated to reflect new technology and regulatory changes.
[ann_19] Annex 19 - Safety Management -- Annex 19 to the Chicago Convention establishes the international framework for aviation safety management, requiring States to implement safety oversight and for service providers to adopt safety management systems. It replaces earlier safety provisions and provides guidance on risk-based oversight, data collection, and performance monitoring.
[ann_2] Annex 2 - Rules of the Air -- Annex 2 to the ICAO Convention sets international standards and recommended practices for the rules of the air, covering flight operations, air traffic control, and the responsibilities of aircraft and pilots to ensure safe navigation.
[ann_3] Annex 3 - Meteorological Service -- Annex 3 establishes the international standards and recommended practices for meteorological services that support civil aviation, defining how states must observe, collect, forecast, and disseminate weather information, including aerodrome reports, forecasts, warnings and SIGMETs, to ensure safe and efficient flight operations.
[ann_4] Annex 4 - Aeronautical Charts -- Annex 4 of the ICAO Convention on International Civil Aviation establishes standards and recommended practices for the design, production, and use of aeronautical charts, ensuring they provide consistent, accurate geographic and navigational information worldwide. It defines chart symbols, scales, and content requirements for topographic, aerodrome, and en-route charts to support safe flight operations. This annex is periodically updated to incorporate new technologies and procedures.
[ann_5] Annex 5 - Units of Measurement -- Annex 5 defines the standard units of measurement used in international civil aviation, prescribing the International System of Units (SI) as the primary system and outlining supplemental units for specific aeronautical applications. It ensures consistency in how data such as distance, speed, mass, temperature and pressure are expressed in flight operations, navigation, and aircraft documentation worldwide.
[ann_6] Annex 6 - Operation of Aircraft -- Annex 6 to the ICAO Convention contains the international standards and recommended practices governing the operation of aircraft, covering both commercial air transport and general aviation. It specifies requirements for flight operations, crew qualifications, maintenance procedures, flight planning, and safety management to ensure uniformity and safety in worldwide aviation activities.
[ann_7] Annex 7 - Aircraft Nationality and Registration Marks -- Annex 7 establishes the standards for indicating an aircraft's nationality and registration marks, specifying the format, placement and size of marks required on civil aircraft and their components. It defines the procedures for registration, the responsibilities of State authorities, and the use of appropriate codes to ensure consistent international identification and air traffic control.
[ann_8] Annex 8 - Airworthiness of Aircraft -- Annex 8 establishes the international standards and recommended practices for aircraft airworthiness, covering design, production, maintenance, and continuing airworthiness of airplanes, helicopters, engines, propellers and related equipment. It defines the certification processes, performance criteria and inspection requirements that states must apply to ensure that aircraft operating under the Convention meet uniform safety levels worldwide.
[ann_9] Annex 9 - Facilitation -- Annex 9 - Facilitation establishes ICAO standards and recommended practices that streamline the movement of passengers, crew, cargo and mail through airports, covering customs, immigration, health, security and related procedures to ensure efficient border and transport operations.
