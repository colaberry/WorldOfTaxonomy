[consent] Parental Consent -- Parental consent under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act requires online services to obtain a verifiable agreement from a child's parent or guardian before collecting, using, or sharing personal data from users under age 13, ensuring parents can review and control how their child's information is handled.
[consent_exceptions] Exceptions to Prior Consent (312.5(c)) -- Under the COPPA provision 312.5(c), operators may collect personal information from children without prior parental consent for limited purposes such as protecting the operator's security, responding to a child's emergency medical needs, and when a child initiates a communication that the operator is required to answer.
[consent_revocation] Right to Revoke Consent (312.6) -- Under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, the "consent_revocation" entry (312.6) outlines a child's or parent's right to withdraw previously granted consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of the child's personal information, requiring operators to provide a clear, reasonably accessible mechanism for revocation and to cease further processing of that data once revocation is confirmed.
[consent_verifiable] Verifiable Parental Consent Methods (312.5) -- Verifiable parental consent methods define the acceptable ways a website can confirm a parent's permission before collecting personal information from children under thirteen, including signed forms, credit-card verification, a phone call, or use of authentication software, thereby satisfying the requirements of the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act.
[data_collection] Limitations on Collection (312.7) -- The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act limits operators of websites and online services from collecting personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent, and restricts the type and amount of data that may be gathered. Operators must retain data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected.
[data_practices] Data Collection and Use -- The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 (15 USC 6501-6506) mandates that any website or online service directed to children under 13, or that knowingly collects information from them, provide a clear privacy policy describing what personal data is collected, how it will be used, and with whom it may be shared, and obtain verifiable parental consent before collection. It also requires reasonable safeguards, limited retention, and deletion of data when no longer needed.
[data_retention] Data Retention and Deletion (312.10) -- The entry details COPPA's mandate that operators of websites or online services directed to children must keep personal information only as long as it is needed for the purpose for which it was collected and must delete it afterward. It specifies allowed retention periods, secure storage requirements, and proper disposal procedures to protect children's privacy.
[data_security] Confidentiality and Security (312.8) -- The entry "Confidentiality and Security (312.8)" under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (15 USC 6501-6506) outlines the data-security obligations for operators that collect personal information from children. It requires reasonable procedures to protect the confidentiality, integrity and safety of that information, including safeguards against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration or destruction.
[enf_civil] Civil Penalties (312.9) -- The entry identifies the civil monetary sanctions authorized by the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, which impose fines on operators that violate the law's requirements for protecting personal information of children under 13. Penalties are assessed per violation and can reach substantial amounts, adjusted for inflation, and are administered under the civil enforcement provision designated by the code enf_civil.
[enf_ftc] FTC Enforcement Authority (312.9) -- The entry "FTC Enforcement Authority (312.9)" refers to the Federal Trade Commission's power to enforce the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998, codified at 15 U.S.C. § 6501-6506. It outlines the FTC's role in regulating online collection of personal information from children under 13.
[enforcement] Enforcement and Penalties -- The enforcement provision of the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act outlines how the Federal Trade Commission may investigate violations, issue orders to halt unlawful practices, and pursue civil actions. It authorizes civil monetary penalties, up to $43,280 per violation, and permits injunctions and corrective measures to protect children's personal data.
[enf_state_ag] State Attorney General Actions -- The entry records actions taken by state attorneys general to enforce the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998, including investigations, complaints and settlements against entities that violate the statute's requirements for protecting personal information of children under 13. It serves as a reference for how state-level enforcement complements federal oversight.
[notice] Notice Requirements -- The notice requirements under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 mandate that any website or online service directed to children or knowingly collecting personal information from children under 13 must provide a clear, conspicuous privacy policy describing the data being collected, how it will be used, and the parental consent procedures. Operators must make this notice readily available before any personal information is gathered.
[notice_content] Content of Notice (312.4(c)) -- Under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, the notice_content entry defines the elements a website must include in its privacy notice to parents, covering the categories of personal information collected, how it will be used and shared, the rights parents have to review or delete the data, and the operator's contact information, as required by 15 USC 6501-6506 §312.4(c).
[notice_direct] Direct Notice to Parents (312.4(b)) -- The Direct Notice to Parents provision under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act requires operators of websites or online services directed to children to give parents a clear, conspicuous notice describing the personal information collected, how it will be used, and the parents' rights to review or delete it, as part of the verifiable consent process.
[notice_online] Online Notice Requirements (312.4(a)) -- The entry outlines the online notice obligations under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, requiring operators of websites or online services directed to children to post a clear, accessible privacy notice. The notice must explain what personal information is collected, how it is used, and how parents can review or delete their child's data.
[operator_duties] Operator Obligations -- Under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, operators of websites or online services directed to children must obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information, provide a clear privacy notice, keep the data secure and confidential, allow parents to review or delete their child's information, and retain records of compliance for the required period.
[op_schools] School Authorization (312.5(b)(2)) -- The entry references the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998 (15 USC 6501-6506) and notes the school-authorization provision in §312.5(b)(2). It permits a school to give a website permission to collect personal information from students when specific criteria are met, including parental consent and restrictions on use, disclosure, and retention of the data.
[op_third_party] Third-Party Collection Oversight (312.2) -- The rule requires operators of child-directed online services to oversee any third party that gathers personal information from children, ensuring contracts impose COPPA-compliant safeguards, limits on data use, and mechanisms for monitoring and enforcement. This oversight helps protect children's privacy by extending the operator's responsibilities beyond its own direct collection activities.
[op_website] Website and Online Service Obligations -- The entry outlines the duties that websites and online services owe under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, requiring them to post a clear privacy notice, obtain verifiable parental consent before gathering personal information from children under 13, and to keep that data reasonably secure.
[safe_harbor] FTC Safe Harbor Programs -- The FTC Safe Harbor Programs offer a voluntary certification system for online services that collect personal information from children under 13, enabling participants to demonstrate compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (15 USC 6501-6506) and receive limited liability and streamlined enforcement.
[sh_programs] Approved Safe Harbor Programs (312.11) -- The entry lists the Safe Harbor programs that the Federal Trade Commission has approved under the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act of 1998, permitting participating websites and online services to meet the law's notice and consent requirements through a standardized framework. Identified by the code sh_programs, it corresponds to section 312.11, which enumerates these approved programs.
