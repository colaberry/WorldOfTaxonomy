[TF] TCFD Pillars -- The TCFD Pillars are the four core elements-Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, and Metrics & Targets-used to guide companies in reporting climate-related financial information. They help organizations disclose how climate risks and opportunities affect their business and performance.
[TF.01] Governance: Board Oversight -- TF.01 outlines the board's responsibility for climate-related financial oversight, requiring the board to embed climate considerations into its governance framework, set strategic direction, and regularly monitor risk and opportunity metrics to ensure accountability and alignment with TCFD recommendations.
[TF.02] Governance: Management Role -- TF.02 covers the governance aspect of climate-related financial disclosures, specifying the role of senior management in overseeing and integrating climate risks and opportunities into the organization's strategy and operations. It details how executives ensure appropriate oversight, allocate resources, and report on climate-related performance to the board and stakeholders.
[TF.03] Strategy: Risks and Opportunities -- TF.03 outlines how organizations should integrate climate-related risks and opportunities into their overall business strategy, detailing governance, scenario analysis, and alignment of financial planning with transition and physical climate impacts. It guides firms in identifying material climate factors, assessing their effect on objectives, and embedding appropriate responses into strategic decision-making.
[TF.04] Strategy: Impact on Business -- TF.04 details how an organization should evaluate and disclose the influence of climate-related risks and opportunities on its overall business strategy, covering resilience, alignment with net-zero objectives, and integration of climate considerations into strategic planning and decision-making.
[TF.05] Strategy: Resilience (Scenarios) -- TF.05 requires organizations to disclose how they evaluate the resilience of their business strategy against a range of plausible climate-related scenarios, including both transition and physical risk pathways. The disclosure should explain the methods, assumptions and outcomes used to assess whether the strategy remains viable under these future conditions.
[TF.06] Risk Management: Identifying Risks -- TF.06 outlines the process for identifying climate-related risks within an organization's risk management framework. It guides entities to catalog physical, transition and liability hazards, assess their likelihood and potential impact, and integrate these findings into existing risk registers and governance structures. This enables consistent, transparent disclosure of how climate factors could affect financial performance.
[TF.07] Risk Management: Managing Risks -- TF.07 details how organizations should integrate climate-related risks into their overall risk management framework, describing methods for identifying, assessing, and prioritizing these risks and outlining strategies for mitigation, monitoring, and governance to protect financial stability.
[TF.08] Risk Management: Integration -- TF.08 addresses how firms should embed climate-related risks into their overall risk-management framework, requiring systematic identification, assessment, and monitoring of these risks alongside traditional financial risks. It emphasizes alignment of governance, strategy, and reporting processes to ensure climate considerations are fully integrated into decision-making. This promotes consistent, board-level oversight and operational resilience to physical and transition impacts.
[TF.09] Metrics: GHG Emissions -- The TF.09 entry guides organizations on quantifying and reporting their greenhouse-gas emissions in line with the Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures framework. It defines Scope 1, 2 and 3 categories, specifies calculation methodologies, data sources and verification practices, and sets disclosure requirements to help stakeholders assess climate-related risks and performance.
[TF.10] Metrics: Climate-Related Risks -- TF.10 outlines the quantitative metrics that firms should use to evaluate climate-related risks, covering both physical exposures such as extreme-weather events and transition factors like carbon-pricing impacts. It guides the selection of indicators, data sources, and calculation methods to express the financial magnitude of these risks in a comparable and decision-relevant format.
[TF.11] Metrics: Targets -- TF.11 outlines the specific quantitative metrics that entities must disclose when reporting climate-related targets, including the type of target (e.g., emission reductions, renewable-energy adoption), the baseline year, the horizon year, and the methodology used to align the target with recognized climate-science scenarios.
[TF.12] Physical Risk Assessment -- TF.12 refers to the TCFD's Physical Risk Assessment component, which guides organizations in identifying and evaluating the acute and chronic climate-related hazards that could affect their assets, operations, and supply chains. The framework emphasizes measuring exposure, sensitivity and adaptive capacity to inform disclosure of potential financial impacts.
[TF.13] Transition Risk Assessment -- TF.13 covers the systematic evaluation of financial risks arising from the shift toward a low-carbon economy, including policy changes, technology adoption, and market dynamics. It guides institutions in identifying, measuring, and managing exposure to assets that may lose value as economies transition away from carbon-intensive activities.
