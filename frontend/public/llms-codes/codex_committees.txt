[CX] Codex Committees -- The CX entry outlines the organizational framework of the Codex Alimentarius committees, detailing the hierarchy, roles, and interaction of the Codex Alimentarius Commission, its Standing Committee, and the various specialized sub-committees and task forces that develop international food standards, guidelines and related texts.
[CX.01] General Principles (CCGP) -- The CX.01 entry defines the General Principles that govern the Codex Alimentarius Committee Structure, establishing the foundation for transparent, science-based decision making, inclusive stakeholder participation, and consensus-oriented processes that guide the development of international food standards.
[CX.02] Food Labelling (CCFL) -- The Food Labelling (CCFL) is a Codex Alimentarius sub-committee charged with developing international standards, guidelines and codes of practice for food labeling. It addresses nutrition information, ingredient lists, health and nutrient claims, allergen declarations and related consumer-information requirements, helping harmonize labeling requirements across member countries.
[CX.03] Food Hygiene (CCFH) -- The CX.03 entry identifies the Codex Committee on Food Hygiene (CCFH), the body responsible for developing international food-hygiene standards, guidelines and recommendations. It operates under the Codex Alimentarius Commission and coordinates scientific work to protect public health by ensuring safe food handling and production practices worldwide.
[CX.04] Food Additives (CCFA) -- CX.04 Food Additives (CCFA) designates the Codex Alimentarius Commission's Committee on Food Additives, which develops international standards, guidelines and recommendations for the safety, use and labeling of food additives. The committee evaluates scientific data, assesses risk and advises member countries on permissible levels and acceptable daily intakes.
[CX.05] Contaminants in Foods (CCCF) -- The Contaminants in Foods (CCCF) committee, identified as CX.05 in the Codex Alimentarius structure, develops international standards, guidelines and codes of practice to identify, evaluate and control chemical, biological and radiological contaminants in food, promoting safety and facilitating trade. It coordinates expert input, risk assessments and scientific reviews to ensure consistent, science-based approaches to contaminant management worldwide.
[CX.06] Pesticide Residues (CCPR) -- CX.06 refers to the Codex Alimentarius Committee on Pesticide Residues (CCPR), a permanent technical committee that develops international food standards, guidelines and codes of practice for pesticide residues in foods. Its work includes evaluating scientific data, establishing maximum residue limits and providing risk-assessment advice to protect consumer health worldwide.
[CX.07] Residues of Vet Drugs (CCRVDF) -- The CX.07 entry in the Codex Alimentarius Committee Structure refers to the Committee on Residues of Veterinary Drugs (CCRVDF), which develops and advises on international standards, guidelines and risk assessments for veterinary-drug residues in food commodities, establishing maximum residue limits to protect consumer health.
[CX.08] Methods of Analysis (CCMAS) -- CX.08 refers to the Codex Alimentarius Committee on Methods of Analysis and Sampling, which develops and harmonises scientific procedures for testing food composition, contaminants and residues, and establishes sampling protocols to support international food-safety standards and trade. It provides guidance to ensure analytical results are reliable, comparable and accepted worldwide.
[CX.09] Food Import/Export (CCFICS) -- CX.09 refers to the Codex Alimentarius Committee on Food Import and Export, known as CCFICS, which develops international standards, guidelines and recommendations to ensure safe and fair trade of food products across borders. The committee coordinates member-country input, reviews scientific data and issues harmonized measures to protect consumer health while facilitating global food commerce.
[CX.10] Nutrition and Foods (CCNFSDU) -- CX.10 identifies the Codex Alimentarius Commission's Committee on Nutrition and Foods (CCNFSDU), which coordinates expert work on nutrition-related standards, dietary guidelines, and food composition. The committee develops scientific advice and international guidelines to promote safe, nutritious and properly labeled foods worldwide.
[CX.11] Fats and Oils -- CX.11 refers to the Codex Alimentarius Committee on Fats and Oils, which advises the Codex Commission on standards, guidelines and codes of practice for edible fats, oils and related products. The committee comprises experts from member countries, industry and consumer groups and works through sub-committees to develop specifications, labeling requirements and safety assessments.
[CX.12] Fish and Fishery Products -- CX.12 is the Codex Alimentarius Committee dedicated to fish and fishery products, tasked with formulating international food standards, guidelines and codes of practice for these commodities. It oversees the work of experts and sub-committees on safety, quality, hygiene, labeling and trade issues, ensuring consistent, science-based recommendations worldwide.

Its work ensures consistent international requirements and consumer protection for these products.
[CX.15] Cereals, Pulses, Legumes -- CX.15 identifies the Codex Alimentarius sub-committee responsible for developing and harmonizing international food standards, guidelines and codes of practice for cereals, pulses and legumes. It oversees safety, quality and labeling criteria for these staple crops, facilitating trade and consumer protection worldwide.
[CX.16] Milk and Milk Products -- CX.16 designates the Codex Alimentarius Committee responsible for milk and milk products, overseeing the development of international food standards, guidelines, and codes of practice for dairy items such as fluid milk, cheese, butter, yogurt, and related processed products. It ensures safety, quality, and fair trade.
