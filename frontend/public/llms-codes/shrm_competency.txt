[SH] SHRM Competencies -- The SHRM Competencies are a core component of the Society for Human Resource Management's Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge, outlining the essential behavioral and professional capabilities HR professionals need to succeed. They cover areas such as communication, ethical practice, business acumen, relationship management, and critical evaluation.
[SH.01] Leadership and Navigation -- The SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge entry SH.01, Leadership and Navigation, defines the competencies needed for HR professionals to guide organizations toward their strategic goals. It emphasizes influencing and directing people, making sound decisions, managing change, and aligning HR initiatives with business objectives while fostering ethical and inclusive workplaces.
[SH.02] Ethical Practice -- SH.02 Ethical Practice denotes the competency for HR professionals to consistently apply integrity, fairness and confidentiality in all actions, aligning decisions with legal requirements, professional standards and organizational values. It includes recognizing ethical dilemmas, using structured decision-making processes and promoting a culture of ethical behavior throughout the workplace.
[SH.03] Business Acumen -- SH.03 Business Acumen is a competency in the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge that focuses on understanding an organization's financial and operational environment, interpreting data to make strategic decisions, and aligning HR practices with overall business objectives to drive value and competitive advantage.
[SH.04] Relationship Management -- SH.04 Relationship Management in the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge focuses on developing and sustaining effective interactions with individuals and groups inside and outside an organization. It covers communication, conflict resolution, networking, and influencing techniques that foster trust, collaboration, and strategic partnerships.
[SH.05] Consultation -- SH.05 Consultation covers the HR professional's ability to act as a trusted advisor, gathering and analyzing organizational data to diagnose issues, develop solutions, and influence decision-makers. It includes facilitating discussions, presenting evidence-based recommendations, and supporting implementation while maintaining ethical standards and confidentiality. This skill enables HR to add strategic value across the enterprise.
[SH.06] Critical Evaluation -- Critical Evaluation is the SHRM skill that requires professionals to objectively assess data, research findings, and workplace information to determine relevance, accuracy, and implications for decision-making. It involves applying evidence-based analysis, questioning assumptions, and synthesizing insights to support strategic HR actions and policies.
[SH.07] Global and Cultural Effectiveness -- SH.07 in the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge covers global and cultural effectiveness, focusing on understanding diverse cultural contexts, managing cross-border workforces, developing inclusive practices, and adapting HR strategies to international environments.

[SH.09] People (HR Expertise) -- SH.09 People (HR Expertise) in the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge defines the core competencies required to manage the employee lifecycle, including talent acquisition, onboarding, performance management, compensation, employee relations and development, enabling HR professionals to align workforce strategies with overall organizational objectives.
[SH.10] Organization (HR Expertise) -- The SH.10 Organization competency focuses on an HR professional's ability to analyze and align an organization's structure, culture and processes with strategic goals. It includes knowledge of organizational design, development, change management, and how workforce planning, talent management and employee engagement function within various organizational models.
[SH.11] Workplace (HR Expertise) -- SH.11 in the SHRM Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge addresses the workplace from an HR expertise perspective, covering the creation and management of policies, practices, and programs that influence employee relations, health and safety, and organizational culture while ensuring alignment with strategic goals and legal requirements.
[SH.12] Talent Acquisition -- Talent acquisition involves sourcing, attracting, and hiring candidates to fill organizational roles, using strategic workforce planning, employer branding, and recruitment technologies. Practitioners assess job requirements, develop sourcing strategies, manage candidate-pipeline processes, and ensure legal compliance throughout the hiring cycle.
[SH.13] Employee Engagement -- SH.13 Employee Engagement focuses on developing and implementing practices that promote worker commitment, motivation, and satisfaction within an organization. It includes assessing engagement levels, designing interventions, and linking engagement to performance, retention, and organizational outcomes.
[SH.14] Learning and Development -- The Learning and Development competency focuses on assessing organizational training needs, designing and delivering effective learning programs, and evaluating their impact. It emphasizes applying adult-learning principles, leveraging technology, and aligning development initiatives with strategic goals to enhance employee performance and career growth.
[SH.15] Total Rewards -- SH.15 Total Rewards covers the strategic design and management of employee compensation, benefits, work-life programs, and recognition systems to attract, motivate, and retain talent. It emphasizes aligning reward practices with organizational goals, assessing market competitiveness, ensuring legal compliance, and measuring the effectiveness of total-reward plans in supporting overall performance.
