[sec_493_1100] 493.1100 - Condition: Specimen Collection and Test Requisition -- Section 493.1100 sets the standards labs must follow for specimen collection and test requisition, requiring documented procedures that ensure proper patient identification, correct specimen type, appropriate container and preservation, and complete, accurate test request information to maintain specimen integrity and reliable results.
[sec_493_1200] 493.1200 - Standard: Establishment and Verification of Performance Specifications -- The CLIA standard 493.1200 requires clinical laboratories to define performance specifications for each test, such as accuracy, precision, and detection limits, based on regulatory criteria, peer-reviewed literature, or manufacturer data. Laboratories must then verify that the test meets these specifications before reporting patient results.
[sec_493_1250] 493.1250 - Standard: General Quality Control Procedures -- Section 493.1250 sets the baseline quality-control requirements that CLIA-certified clinical laboratories must follow for all testing. It mandates that each test system have procedures to monitor performance, establish acceptable control ranges, document results, and take corrective action when controls fall outside those limits, ensuring consistent reliability of patient test results.
[sec_493_1443] 493.1443 - Standard: Laboratory Director Responsibilities -- The CLIA 1988 regulation, Part 493, section 1443 defines the duties of a laboratory director, including ensuring compliance with all CLIA standards, supervising personnel qualifications, overseeing test validation and quality control, and maintaining the overall integrity of laboratory operations.
[sec_493_1451] 493.1451 - Standard: Technical Consultant Responsibilities -- The CLIA standard 493.1451 defines the duties of a technical consultant in a clinical laboratory, requiring them to oversee testing processes, ensure compliance with quality-control and proficiency-testing requirements, and provide technical guidance and training to laboratory personnel. It also mandates that the consultant be available for consultation on complex or unusual tests and to assist in interpreting results.
[sec_493_15] 493.15 - Testing Categories (Waived, Moderate, High) -- Section 493.15 of the CLIA regulations defines the three testing complexity categories-waived, moderate and high-used to determine a laboratory's certification requirements, personnel qualifications and quality-control standards.
[sec_493_1800] 493.1800 - Inspection Basis and Frequency -- The CLIA provision at 493.1800 establishes the criteria and schedule for inspecting clinical laboratories, detailing which facilities are subject to routine, periodic, or targeted examinations based on risk, complexity, and compliance history. It outlines the inspection frequency, ranging from annual visits for high-risk labs to less frequent reviews for lower-risk sites, ensuring continual adherence to quality and safety standards.
[sec_493_3] 493.3 - Applicability -- Section 493.3 defines the scope of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments, stating that the regulations apply to all laboratories that test human specimens for diagnosis, prevention, treatment or health assessment, except for certain waived tests and facilities exempted by specific statutory provisions. It establishes the baseline requirement that any entity performing such testing must comply with CLIA standards.
[sec_493_801] 493.801 - Proficiency Testing Enrollment and Testing -- Section 493.801 outlines the requirements for clinical laboratories to enroll in proficiency testing programs and to conduct the testing as mandated by CLIA, specifying the types of tests covered, the frequency of participation, and the procedures for reporting results to ensure ongoing quality assessment.
[sub_a] Subpart A - General Provisions -- Subpart A establishes the overarching framework for CLIA-certified laboratories, outlining the scope, applicability, and basic requirements for quality assurance, personnel qualifications, testing procedures, and record-keeping that all clinical testing facilities must follow.

[sub_c] Subpart C - Registration Certificate and Certificate for PPM Procedures -- Subpart C of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (42 CFR Part 493) outlines the requirements for a laboratory's registration certificate and the specific certificate needed to conduct provider-performed microscopy (PPM) procedures, specifying eligibility, application procedures, and conditions under which a laboratory may offer these direct-patient services.
[sub_d] Subpart D - Certificate of Accreditation -- Subpart D sets forth the requirements for clinical laboratories to obtain a Certificate of Accreditation, describing the standards they must meet, the application and inspection process, and the agencies authorized to grant and renew accreditation under the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments. It ensures quality and compliance for testing services.
[sub_e] Subpart E - Accreditation by a Private, Nonprofit Organization -- This subpart defines the requirements for clinical laboratories to receive accreditation through a private nonprofit organization, detailing standards for quality control, proficiency testing, personnel qualifications, and operational oversight. It outlines the accreditation process, inspection procedures, reporting obligations, and enforcement actions to ensure compliance with CLIA regulations.
[sub_h] Subpart H - Participation in Proficiency Testing -- Subpart H of the CLIA regulations establishes mandatory proficiency-testing requirements for clinical laboratories, requiring participation in approved programs that correspond to the specific tests performed. It details the frequency of testing, qualifications of testing providers, and the procedures for reporting, reviewing and correcting results to ensure ongoing accuracy and reliability of laboratory testing.
[sub_j] Subpart J - Patient Test Management for Moderate and High Complexity Testing -- Subpart J establishes the requirements that laboratories performing moderate- and high-complexity testing must follow to manage patient tests, covering order verification, specimen collection and handling, test performance, result verification, and timely communication of findings to health-care providers, thereby ensuring quality, safety, and consistency in patient test management.
[sub_k] Subpart K - Quality Control for Tests of Moderate Complexity -- Subpart K of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments outlines quality-control requirements for moderate-complexity tests, mandating procedures for specimen handling, reagent verification, instrument maintenance, proficiency testing and ongoing performance monitoring to ensure reliable laboratory results.
[sub_m] Subpart M - Personnel for Moderate and High Complexity Testing -- Subpart M establishes the personnel qualifications, training and competency standards for laboratories performing moderate- and high-complexity testing under CLIA. It outlines required education, experience and ongoing proficiency assessments for directors, consultants and testing staff to ensure reliable test results.
[sub_q] Subpart Q - Inspection -- Subpart Q of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (42 CFR Part 493) sets the inspection requirements for CLIA-certified laboratories. It defines the authority, frequency and scope of inspections, the standards laboratories must meet, and the procedures for corrective actions. The subpart ensures that laboratories maintain quality and regulatory compliance through regular oversight.
[sub_r] Subpart R - Enforcement Procedures -- Subpart R of the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (42 CFR § 493) sets out the enforcement mechanisms CMS uses to ensure laboratories meet CLIA standards, including inspection protocols, certification revocation, civil monetary penalties and corrective action requirements. It details the procedural steps for investigations, hearings and appeals when non-compliance is identified.
