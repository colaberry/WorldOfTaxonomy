[c100] C100 - Equal Remuneration Convention -- C100, the Equal Remuneration Convention, is an ILO core instrument that requires member states to promote and enforce the principle of equal pay for men and women for work of equal value, defining the concepts of equal remuneration and providing a framework for legislation, collective bargaining and administrative measures. Adopted in 1951, it obliges ratifying countries to eliminate gender-based wage differentials and to ensure effective remedies for violations.
[c105] C105 - Abolition of Forced Labour Convention -- C105, the Abolition of Forced Labour Convention, is an International Labour Organization core convention that requires ratifying states to suppress all forms of forced or compulsory labor, protect individuals from recruitment and use of such labor, and adopt effective measures for its prevention, elimination and punishment.
[c111] C111 - Discrimination (Employment and Occupation) Convention -- The ILO's C111 Convention defines discrimination in employment and occupation as any distinction, exclusion or preference based on race, colour, sex, religion, political opinion, national origin or social origin that impairs equality of opportunity or treatment. It obliges ratifying states to adopt policies and legislation to promote equal access to work, eliminate discriminatory practices, and provide remedies for victims. The convention aims to create a workplace free from bias and open to all qualified individuals.
[c138] C138 - Minimum Age Convention -- C138 - Minimum Age Convention is an ILO core convention that sets the general minimum age for admission to employment at 15 years, allows lighter work for younger persons, and permits developing countries to initially apply a lower age while pursuing progressive elimination of child labour.
[c155] C155 - Occupational Safety and Health Convention -- The Occupational Safety and Health Convention (C155) is an ILO core convention that establishes international standards for preventing workplace accidents and occupational diseases. It obliges ratifying members to adopt national policies, enforce preventive measures, and promote safe and healthy working conditions, thereby safeguarding workers' health and safety worldwide.

[c187] C187 - Promotional Framework for Occupational Safety and Health Convention -- C187 is an ILO convention that creates a promotional framework for occupational safety and health, urging member states to adopt policies, programs and measures that prevent workplace accidents and diseases. It reinforces core labour standards by stressing preventive action, worker participation and the integration of OSH into national development strategies.
[c29] C029 - Forced Labour Convention -- C029, the ILO Forced Labour Convention, is a core instrument adopted in 1930 that obliges ratifying states to suppress and eliminate all forms of forced or compulsory labour. It defines forced labour, requires the repeal of laws authorising it, and mandates protective measures for vulnerable groups, promoting freedom of employment worldwide.
[c87] C087 - Freedom of Association and Protection of the Right to Organise -- C087 is the International Labour Organization's Freedom of Association and Protection of the Right to Organise Convention, a core convention that guarantees workers' and employers' right to form and join organisations of their choice without interference and obliges member states to protect these rights and promote collective bargaining.
[c98] C098 - Right to Organise and Collective Bargaining -- C098 is an ILO core convention that guarantees workers the right to form and join organisations of their own choosing and to engage in collective bargaining with employers. It enshrines freedom of association as a fundamental principle of decent work and sets standards for protecting union activities worldwide.

These standards aim to protect children from exploitation and ensure their right to education and health.
