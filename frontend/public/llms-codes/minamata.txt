[HG] Minamata Convention Articles -- HG covers the individual articles of the Minamata Convention on Mercury, detailing the treaty's obligations, control measures, and implementation provisions that parties must follow to protect health and the environment from mercury pollution.
[HG.03] Mercury supply sources and trade -- The HG.03 entry outlines the origins of mercury used worldwide, including primary mining, by-product recovery and recycled sources, and details the international trade flows of the metal. It sets out requirements for reporting, monitoring and controlling mercury supply to support the Minamata Convention's efforts to reduce its production, distribution and use.
[HG.04] Mercury-added products -- The entry HG.04 addresses products that contain added mercury, including items such as thermometers, fluorescent lamps, batteries, and dental amalgam. It outlines the Minamata Convention's measures to phase out, restrict, or manage these mercury-added products to reduce environmental release and protect human health.
[HG.05] Manufacturing processes using mercury -- HG.05 addresses manufacturing processes that incorporate mercury, covering industrial activities such as the production of chlorine and caustic soda, the manufacture of mercury-containing batteries, fluorescent lamps, and other products where mercury is used as a catalyst or component. The entry outlines the obligations of parties to control, phase-out, or limit these processes under the Minamata Convention.
[HG.07] Artisanal and small-scale gold mining -- The entry outlines the Minamata Convention's requirements for controlling mercury use in artisanal and small-scale gold mining, mandating phase-out strategies, alternative technologies and the safe management of mercury waste. It also calls on parties to develop national action plans and provide technical and financial assistance to miners to meet these obligations.
[HG.08] Emissions -- HG.08 covers the provisions of the Minamata Convention that require parties to control, monitor, and reduce mercury emissions to air and water from industrial processes, waste incineration, and other sources, setting emission limits and reporting obligations.
[HG.09] Releases -- The "Releases" entry (HG.09) of the Minamata Convention on Mercury defines the scope of mercury emissions and discharges that parties must control, monitor, and report, covering releases to air, water and soil from industrial processes, waste management and product use. It establishes reporting thresholds and national inventories to track progress toward the Convention's goal of reducing global mercury pollution.
[HG.10] Environmentally sound storage -- HG.10 outlines the need for mercury to be kept in facilities that prevent releases to air, water, or soil, using sealed containers, secondary containment, and regular monitoring, thereby supporting the Minamata Convention's goal of minimizing environmental mercury pollution.
[HG.11] Mercury wastes -- HG.11 - Mercury wastes covers the generation, handling, transport and disposal of waste materials that contain mercury, as defined by the Minamata Convention on Mercury. The entry sets out obligations for parties to manage these wastes in an environmentally sound manner to prevent releases into air, water and soil.
[HG.12] Contaminated sites -- HG.12 covers the Convention's obligations for parties to locate, evaluate and remediate sites polluted with mercury or mercury compounds, ensuring that contaminated soils, sediments or water bodies are cleaned up to stop further releases and protect human health and the environment.
[HG.13] Financial resources and mechanism -- The entry outlines the financial framework of the Minamata Convention, describing the Global Mercury Fund and related mechanisms that provide grants, loans and technical assistance to developing and vulnerable countries for implementing mercury-reduction measures, capacity-building, and environmental health projects. It details how resources are mobilized, administered and monitored to support the Convention's objectives.
[HG.14] Capacity building and technical assistance -- HG.14 details the Convention's framework for enhancing the abilities of Parties to apply mercury-control measures, including programs that deliver training, expertise and resources to develop institutional, technical and legislative capacities, with special emphasis on support for developing countries.
[HG.16] Health aspects -- HG.16 addresses the health aspects of mercury under the Minamata Convention, outlining the toxicological effects of exposure and the need for risk assessments, public health surveillance, and preventive measures. It emphasizes protecting vulnerable populations, establishing medical guidelines, and promoting education to reduce mercury-related disease and disability.
[HG.17] Information exchange -- HG.17 - Information exchange under the Minamata Convention on Mercury establishes obligations for parties to provide, receive and share data, reports and best-practice information on mercury emissions, releases and mitigation actions, supporting transparent monitoring and collaborative policy development.
[HG.19] Research and monitoring -- The HG.19 entry under the Minamata Convention on Mercury outlines the Convention's requirements for scientific research and systematic monitoring of mercury in the environment, human health, and related emissions. It calls for the collection and analysis of data, development of methodologies, and regular reporting to assess progress toward the treaty's objectives.
