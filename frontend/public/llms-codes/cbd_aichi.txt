[CA] CBD Aichi Targets -- The Aichi Biodiversity Targets are a suite of 20 global goals adopted under the Convention on Biological Diversity in 2010 to halt biodiversity loss by 2020. They address ecosystem protection, pressure reduction, sustainable use of resources and equitable benefit sharing. The entry code CA identifies this specific CBD target framework.
[CA.01] Target 1: Awareness of Biodiversity -- Target 1 under the Aichi Biodiversity Targets of the Convention on Biological Diversity calls for widespread awareness of biodiversity and its importance for human well-being, encouraging education, public communication and the integration of biodiversity values into decision-making at all levels.
[CA.02] Target 2: Biodiversity in Planning -- Target 2 calls for the integration of biodiversity considerations into national, regional and local development planning, poverty reduction strategies and environmental impact assessments, ensuring that actions across sectors are coordinated to conserve and sustainably use ecosystem services.

[CA.04] Target 4: Sustainable Production -- CA.04 Target 4 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the adoption of sustainable production practices that reduce the environmental impact of goods and services, improve resource efficiency, and encourage the use of environmentally friendly technologies. It seeks to integrate biodiversity considerations into supply chains and promote responsible consumption patterns worldwide.
[CA.05] Target 5: Habitat Loss Halved -- Target 5 seeks to cut the rate of natural-habitat loss by at least 50 percent by 2020, supporting ecosystem and species conservation. It urges countries to embed habitat protection in planning, restore degraded lands, and promote sustainable land-use practices.
[CA.06] Target 6: Sustainable Fisheries -- Target 6 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets (CA.06) calls for the sustainable management of fisheries, aiming to maintain fish stocks at levels that can produce the maximum sustainable yield and to protect marine and freshwater ecosystems from over-exploitation, habitat loss and pollution.
[CA.07] Target 7: Sustainable Agriculture -- Target 7 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the development and implementation of sustainable agriculture practices that protect ecosystems, maintain productive soils and water resources, and enhance food security while reducing negative impacts on biodiversity. It encourages policies, research and incentives that support agro-ecological methods, integrated pest management and climate-resilient farming.
[CA.08] Target 8: Pollution Reduced -- Target 8 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the reduction of pollution that threatens ecosystems and species, urging parties to adopt measures that minimize the release of harmful substances into air, water and soil. Implementation involves stricter regulations, improved waste management and the promotion of cleaner production and consumption practices to safeguard biodiversity.
[CA.09] Target 9: Invasive Species Controlled -- Target 9 seeks to identify and prioritize invasive alien species, develop and implement strategies to prevent their introduction, control or eradicate established populations, and mitigate their ecological and socioeconomic impacts.
[CA.10] Target 10: Vulnerable Ecosystems -- Target 10 calls for the protection and restoration of vulnerable ecosystems to ensure their continued contribution to biodiversity and human wellbeing. It seeks to prevent ecosystem degradation, safeguard ecosystem services and promote sustainable management by integrating conservation actions into policies.
[CA.11] Target 11: Protected Areas (17%/10%) -- Target 11 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the conservation of at least 17 percent of terrestrial and inland water areas and 10 percent of marine and coastal zones by establishing effective, well-managed protected-area networks that integrate ecological planning, ensure equitable benefit sharing and contribute to long-term biodiversity preservation.
[CA.12] Target 12: Species Extinction Prevented -- Target 12 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets, under the Convention on Biological Diversity, calls for measures that halt the extinction of known species and improve their conservation status. It urges governments and stakeholders to develop and implement effective actions, including habitat protection, threat mitigation and monitoring, to keep species from disappearing.
[CA.13] Target 13: Genetic Diversity -- Target 13 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets under the Convention on Biological Diversity calls for the preservation of genetic diversity within all species, including cultivated plants, farm animals and wild populations, and for the implementation of measures that protect and sustainably use genetic resources.
[CA.14] Target 14: Ecosystem Services -- Target 14 aims to sustain the contribution of ecosystems to human well-being by maintaining or restoring their capacity to provide services such as food, water, flood regulation, and climate mitigation, while preventing degradation that undermines these benefits. It encourages integrated management and monitoring of ecosystems to safeguard their resilience and the benefits they deliver to societies worldwide.
[CA.15] Target 15: Ecosystem Restoration (15%) -- Target 15 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets under the Convention on Biological Diversity calls for restoring at least 15 percent of degraded ecosystems by 2020, enhancing ecosystem services, resilience and climate mitigation. It encourages national and sub-national actions to rehabilitate habitats, improve landscape connectivity and promote sustainable land-use practices.
[CA.16] Target 16: Nagoya Protocol -- Target 16 calls for the full implementation of the Nagoya Protocol on access to genetic resources and the fair and equitable sharing of benefits arising from their use, supporting the Convention on Biological Diversity's objectives for sustainable use and conservation.
[CA.17] Target 17: National Strategies -- Target 17 requires each country to develop and implement a national biodiversity strategy-action plan that integrates conservation and sustainable use into development planning, sets clear, measurable objectives and mobilises financial and technical resources.
[CA.18] Target 18: Traditional Knowledge -- Target 18 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the respect, preservation and maintenance of traditional knowledge held by indigenous peoples and local communities, and for its appropriate use in biodiversity conservation and sustainable use, while ensuring that benefits derived from such knowledge are shared equitably.
[CA.19] Target 19: Knowledge Sharing -- Target 19 of the Aichi Biodiversity Targets calls for the sharing of knowledge, information and technologies related to biodiversity, including traditional and indigenous knowledge, to support research, policy and capacity-building. It emphasizes open access to data, collaborative networks and the dissemination of best practices among parties. The goal is to improve decision-making and public awareness while respecting intellectual-property rights and benefit-sharing agreements.
[CA.20] Target 20: Resource Mobilization -- Target 20 seeks to mobilize financial resources and other support from all sectors to implement the Aichi Biodiversity Targets, aiming to increase public and private investment in biodiversity conservation and sustainable use, with particular emphasis on developing countries.
