[PC] Card Schemes -- PC - Card Schemes are the
[PC.01] Visa -- PC.01 designates Visa, a worldwide payment-card network that connects merchants, issuing banks and acquiring institutions to process electronic transactions. It supplies brand recognition, routing and settlement services for credit, debit and prepaid cards across the globe.
[PC.02] Mastercard -- Mastercard is a worldwide payment card network that enables electronic transactions among consumers, merchants, and financial institutions. It offers credit, debit and prepaid card products and operates a secure processing infrastructure used by banks across the globe.
[PC.03] American Express -- American Express is a global payment card network that issues its own cards and provides transaction processing services, known for its closed-loop system and premium card offerings. It operates both credit and charge cards and partners with merchants worldwide.
[PC.04] Discover/Diners Club -- PC.04 denotes the Discover and Diners Club payment card network schemes, which operate global debit and credit card services. The networks provide transaction processing, merchant acceptance, and cardholder benefits distinct from Visa and MasterCard.
[PC.05] UnionPay -- UnionPay, designated PC.05, is China's principal payment-card network established in 2002 under the China UnionPay Association. It issues debit, credit and prepaid cards, processes domestic transactions and provides global acceptance through partnerships, making it one of the world's largest card schemes.
[PC.06] JCB -- JCB is a global payment card network founded in Japan in 1961, offering credit, debit and prepaid cards accepted in more than 190 countries. It operates alongside other major schemes, providing transaction processing, fraud protection and loyalty programs, and is particularly prevalent in Asia and among travelers seeking acceptance in Japanese merchants.
[PC.07] RuPay (India) -- RuPay is India's domestic card payment network launched by the National Payments Corporation of India to offer a low-cost alternative to international schemes. It supports debit, credit and prepaid cards and is widely issued by Indian banks for use at ATMs, online merchants and point-of-sale terminals.
[PC.08] Elo (Brazil) -- Elo is a Brazilian payment card network launched in 2011 by a consortium of major banks to provide domestic debit and credit card services. It operates alongside international schemes, offering a proprietary infrastructure that is widely accepted at merchants and ATMs throughout Brazil. The brand aims to increase competition and reduce reliance on foreign card networks.
[PC.09] Interac (Canada) -- Interac is Canada's domestic debit card network that links consumers' bank accounts to merchants for point-of-sale and online purchases. Operated by a consortium of financial institutions, it facilitates electronic funds transfers, bill payments and cash withdrawals across the country.
[PC.10] EFTPOS (Australia) -- EFTPOS (Australia) is an electronic funds-transfer-at-point-of-sale network that enables Australian debit-card holders to pay for goods and services directly from their bank accounts. Managed by a consortium of major Australian banks, it processes in-store, online and mobile transactions, supporting real-time authorisation and settlement across the national retail market.
[PC.11] Mir (Russia) -- Mir is Russia's national payment-card system introduced in 2015 by the Central Bank to provide a domestically controlled alternative to international schemes. It operates for debit, credit and prepaid cards, is accepted at millions of retail outlets across the country, and supports online, contactless and magnetic-stripe transactions. The network is also used for government benefit disbursements and is expanding into neighboring markets.
[PC.12] Troy (Turkey) -- Troy is Turkey's domestic payment card scheme launched in 2015 by the Interbank Card Center (BKM) to provide debit and credit card services linked to local banks. The network supports contactless transactions and integrates with international schemes, helping reduce reliance on foreign card brands.

[PC.14] BC Card (South Korea) -- BC Card is a South Korean payment-card network operating under the PC.14 code, issuing co-branded credit, debit and prepaid cards for domestic and international use and facilitating interbank settlement and transaction processing.
