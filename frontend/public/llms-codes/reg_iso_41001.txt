[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Section 10.1 of ISO 41001:2018 requires organizations to establish a process for identifying and documenting nonconformities in the facility-management system, determine their causes, and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence. The clause also mandates reviewing the effectiveness of those actions, maintaining records, and ensuring continual improvement of the management system.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.2 of ISO 41001:2018 obliges a facility-management organization to establish and maintain processes for continual improvement of its management system, including monitoring performance, analyzing data, taking corrective actions, and updating objectives to enhance effectiveness and efficiency.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Clause 4.1 requires a facility-management organization to identify and analyze internal and external factors that affect its purpose and FM system, assess the needs and expectations of interested parties, and use this understanding to define the scope and objectives of the management system.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 of ISO 41001:2018 requires the organization to determine who the interested parties are and to understand their needs and expectations that can affect its facility-management system. It involves gathering relevant information, analysing those requirements and documenting how they shape the scope, objectives and continual improvement of the FM system.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the FM system -- This clause requires the organization to define the boundaries and applicability of its facility management system, identifying relevant processes, sites, and services to be covered. It ensures that the scope reflects the context, interested parties, and regulatory requirements, forming the basis for subsequent planning and implementation.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- The entry defines the top management's role in demonstrating leadership for the facility management system, ensuring it is integrated into the organization's strategic direction, providing necessary resources, establishing a clear FM policy, and promoting a culture of continual improvement and stakeholder engagement.
[5_2] 5.2 Facility management policy -- Clause 5.2 requires the organization to create, document, implement and maintain a facility management policy that aligns with its strategic direction and objectives, demonstrates commitment to continual improvement, and addresses stakeholder needs, scope of activities and responsibilities within the overall management system.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- Clause 5.3 of ISO 41001:2018 specifies how a facility-management organization must define and document the roles, responsibilities and authorities of its personnel, ensuring clear accountability and effective coordination across all functions, from top management to operational staff.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- Clause 6.1 of ISO 41001:2018 requires a facility-management organization to determine internal and external risks and opportunities that could affect its management system, evaluate their significance, and plan appropriate actions to mitigate negative impacts or capitalize on positive possibilities, integrating these actions into its overall FM processes.
[6_2] 6.2 FM objectives and planning to achieve them -- Clause 6.2 of ISO 41001:2018 requires facility-management organizations to establish measurable objectives that align with their strategic direction, and to develop detailed plans outlining responsibilities, resources, timelines and performance indicators needed to achieve those objectives.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Section 8.1 mandates that a facility-management organization defines, implements and maintains processes for planning, executing and controlling its operations to achieve specified objectives, specifying required resources, responsibilities and performance criteria and monitoring their effectiveness.
[8_2] 8.2 Coordination of demand and services -- The standard requires organizations to align user demand with the delivery of facility-management services through systematic planning, communication and resource allocation. This involves identifying service needs, matching them to appropriate service providers, and continuously monitoring performance to ensure that demand is met efficiently and effectively.
[8_3] 8.3 Management of service delivery -- Clause 8.3 requires organizations to plan, implement and control the delivery of facility-management services, ensuring they meet defined client needs and performance criteria. It calls for clear service agreements, monitoring of service execution, and mechanisms for corrective action and continual improvement of delivery processes.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 41001:2018 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring, measuring, analyzing and evaluating the performance of their facility management system. It mandates the collection of relevant data, assessment against objectives, and the use of findings to identify improvement opportunities and ensure compliance with the management system requirements.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 requires the organization to plan and conduct internal audits of its facility-management system at defined intervals, ensuring that processes meet ISO 41001 requirements and identifying non-conformities and opportunities for improvement. Audits must be objective, documented, and results communicated to relevant personnel for corrective action.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 mandates that top-management perform regular reviews of the facility-management system to evaluate its performance, compliance with ISO 41001, and alignment with organizational goals. The review must consider audit findings, stakeholder feedback, and improvement opportunities, and result in decisions on necessary changes to policies, resources, or processes.
[cl_10] Clause 10 - Improvement -- Clause 10 of ISO 41001:2018 defines the requirements for continual improvement of a facility-management system, obligating the organization to monitor performance, address nonconformities, implement corrective actions and pursue opportunities that enhance the effectiveness and efficiency of its FM processes.

[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 41001:2018 outlines the planning requirements for a facility-management system, demanding that organizations establish objectives, identify risks and opportunities, and determine necessary processes to achieve intended outcomes. It requires documented plans that address resource needs, responsibilities, and criteria for evaluating performance, ensuring that the FM system aligns with strategic goals and complies with applicable legal and stakeholder expectations.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 41001:2018 defines the support requirements for a facility-management system, specifying the resources, competence and awareness needed, the internal and external communications to be managed, and the creation, control and retention of documented information that ensures the system operates effectively.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 41001:2018 specifies the operational requirements for a facility-management system, detailing how an organization plans, executes and controls its FM activities, allocates resources, manages risks and ensures service delivery meets defined objectives. It also requires monitoring and measurement of performance, handling of nonconformities and continual improvement of operational processes.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 41001:2018 requires organizations to monitor, measure, analyze and evaluate the performance of their facility-management system. It mandates internal audits, management review and the use of relevant data to determine conformity, effectiveness and opportunities for continual improvement.
