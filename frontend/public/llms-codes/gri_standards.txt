[GRI-1] GRI 1: Foundation 2021 -- GRI 1: Foundation 2021 is the core GRI standard that sets out the reporting principles, requirements and concepts that underpin all other GRI disclosures. It guides organizations in identifying material topics, engaging stakeholders and ensuring the quality and comparability of sustainability reports.
[GRI-2] GRI 2: General Disclosures 2021 -- GRI-2 is a core module of the Global Reporting Initiative Standards that sets out the basic information organizations must disclose in sustainability reports, including the organization's profile, strategy, ethics, governance, stakeholder engagement and reporting principles, to ensure transparent and comparable reporting.
[GRI-201] GRI 201: Economic Performance -- GRI 201 outlines how organizations should disclose their economic performance, including metrics such as direct economic value generated, financial implications of climate-related risks, contributions to local economies, and indirect economic impacts. It provides guidance on data collection, calculation methods, and reporting formats to ensure transparent and comparable economic information.

[GRI-203] GRI 203: Indirect Economic Impacts -- GRI 203 covers an organization's indirect economic impacts, such as effects on local suppliers, employment and income generation, and contributions to regional economic development. It guides reporting on the magnitude, distribution and sustainability of these impacts, encouraging disclosure of both positive and negative outcomes for stakeholders. This standard helps assess how business activities influence broader economic conditions beyond direct operations.
[GRI-204] GRI 204: Procurement Practices -- GRI 204 addresses an organization's procurement practices, outlining how to disclose policies, strategies and processes used to select suppliers and evaluate the environmental, social and economic impacts of purchased goods and services. It guides reporting on supplier assessments, sustainability criteria integration, and the proportion of spending directed toward sustainable and locally sourced suppliers.
[GRI-205] GRI 205: Anti-Corruption -- GRI 205 is the Global Reporting Initiative Standard that guides organizations in disclosing their anti-corruption policies, practices and performance. It requires reporting on governance structures, risk assessments, due-diligence procedures, training programmes and monitoring mechanisms aimed at preventing bribery and other corrupt activities.
[GRI-206] GRI 206: Anti-Competitive Behavior -- GRI 206 addresses how an organization identifies, mitigates and reports on anti-competitive behavior within its operations and supply chain, including policies, training and compliance with competition law. It requires disclosure of any instances, corrective actions taken and the effectiveness of measures to promote fair market practices.
[GRI-207] GRI 207: Tax 2019 -- GRI 207 Tax 2019 is a sustainability reporting standard that guides organizations in disclosing their tax strategies, governance and risk management practices, and the amount of tax paid in the reporting period. It emphasizes transparency about the tax policies, approaches to tax planning, and the impact of tax contributions on economic development, helping stakeholders assess an entity's fiscal responsibility and compliance.
[GRI-3] GRI 3: Material Topics 2021 -- GRI-3 refers to the Global Reporting Initiative's 2021 standard on material topics, guiding organisations in identifying and prioritising economic, environmental and social issues that are significant to stakeholders and affect business value. The standard outlines processes for topic selection, stakeholder engagement and reporting to ensure transparent sustainability disclosure.

[GRI-302] GRI 302: Energy -- GRI 302, a component of the Global Reporting Initiative Standards, guides organizations in disclosing their energy performance, covering total energy consumption, efficiency measures, and reduction initiatives. It helps entities report on energy use across different scopes, set improvement targets, and describe actions toward greater efficiency and renewable-energy adoption.
[GRI-303] GRI 303: Water and Effluents -- GRI 303 is a Global Reporting Initiative standard that guides organizations in disclosing their water-related performance, including water withdrawal, consumption, and the quality of effluent discharges. It helps assess risks, management practices, and impacts on water resources across operations and supply chains.
[GRI-304] GRI 304: Biodiversity -- GRI 304 is the Global Reporting Initiative standard that directs companies to disclose their impacts on biodiversity, covering habitats, species and ecosystem services as well as management approaches, conservation areas and mitigation actions. It helps stakeholders assess environmental performance and biodiversity risks.
[GRI-305] GRI 305: Emissions -- GRI 305 is a Global Reporting Initiative standard that provides guidance for organizations to measure, manage and disclose their greenhouse-gas emissions, including direct (Scope 1) and indirect (Scope 2) emissions as well as energy consumption and reduction initiatives. It enables stakeholders to assess a company's climate impact and track progress toward sustainability targets.
[GRI-306] GRI 306: Waste -- GRI 306 addresses an organization's waste management practices, covering the types, quantities and disposal methods of waste generated, as well as recycling, reuse and treatment processes. It guides reporting on waste-related impacts, policies, targets and performance to help assess environmental stewardship and facilitate improvement.
[GRI-308] GRI 308: Supplier Environmental Assessment -- GRI 308 covers an organization's approach to assessing and managing the environmental impacts of its suppliers, requiring disclosure of policies, assessment methods, and actions taken to improve supply-chain performance. The standard helps stakeholders understand how a company evaluates supplier practices on issues such as resource use, emissions, waste and biodiversity.

It covers topics such as hiring, contracts, wages and benefits, training, diversity, employee turnover and representation, and aims to show how companies manage and promote decent work within their workforce.

It guides entities to disclose policies, practices, and outcomes that affect workplace dynamics, aiming to improve transparency on how labor issues are handled and resolved.
[GRI-403] GRI 403: Occupational Health and Safety -- GRI 403 offers guidance for reporting on occupational health and safety performance, including injury rates, occupational diseases, preventive measures and health promotion programs. It helps organizations disclose how they manage workplace safety risks, comply with relevant regulations and track improvements over time.

It outlines metrics for measuring training hours, coverage, and effectiveness, encouraging continuous learning that aligns with the entity's environmental and social objectives.

It also calls for reporting any incidents or complaints, the outcomes of actions taken, and the mechanisms used for dialogue with workers and their representatives.

It guides reporting on age verification, risk assessments, corrective actions and collaboration with stakeholders to eliminate child labor.

[GRI-413] GRI 413: Local Communities -- GRI 413 focuses on an organization's impact on local communities, requiring disclosure of stakeholder engagement, community development initiatives, and socioeconomic contributions.
