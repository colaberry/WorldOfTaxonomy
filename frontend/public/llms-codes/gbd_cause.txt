[GBD] GBD Level 1 Causes -- GBD is the top-level category in the Global Burden of Disease cause list, grouping all diseases and injuries into broad Level 1 cause groups such as non-communicable diseases, communicable, maternal, neonatal and nutritional disorders, and injuries. These aggregates are used worldwide to compare health loss across populations and over time.
[GBD.1] Communicable, maternal, neonatal -- GBD.1 groups together communicable diseases, maternal health conditions, and neonatal disorders within the Global Burden of Disease cause list. It serves as a broad category for estimating the combined mortality and disability impact of infectious illnesses, pregnancy-related complications, and health problems affecting newborns. This classification helps policymakers track and compare the overall burden of these interrelated health challenges across regions and over time.
[GBD.1A] HIV/AIDS and STIs -- GBD.1A groups together HIV/AIDS and other sexually transmitted infections as a single cause category for measuring global disease burden, tracking both mortality and morbidity. This aggregation supports comparative analyses of health losses across regions and informs policy and resource allocation for prevention and treatment programs.
[GBD.1B] Respiratory infections and TB -- The GBD.1B category groups respiratory infections-including pneumonia, bronchitis, and other acute lower and upper respiratory diseases-with tuberculosis as major causes of illness and death globally, especially in low- and middle-income nations where they together account for a large share of the disease burden.
[GBD.1C] Enteric infections -- GBD.1C denotes enteric infections, a group of diseases caused by bacterial, viral, or parasitic pathogens transmitted through contaminated food or water and typically presenting as diarrheal illness. In the Global Burden of Disease framework they are classified as a major cause of morbidity and mortality, especially in low-resource settings, contributing substantially to child deaths and disability-adjusted life years worldwide.
[GBD.1D] Neglected tropical diseases -- Neglected tropical diseases are a group of communicable diseases that disproportionately affect people living in impoverished tropical and subtropical regions, causing chronic disability, death, and economic loss. In the GBD Cause List they are aggregated under code GBD.1D to capture the combined burden of conditions such as dengue, leishmaniasis, schistosomiasis, lymphatic filariasis and several other endemic infections.
[GBD.1E] Malaria -- GBD.1E refers to malaria, a mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by Plasmodium parasites that produces fever, chills, anemia and can be fatal if untreated. It is a leading contributor to global morbidity and mortality, especially in low-income tropical regions, and is tracked in the Global Burden of Disease framework to assess health losses and inform control strategies.
[GBD.1F] Maternal and neonatal disorders -- GBD.1F designates maternal and neonatal disorders, covering health conditions that affect pregnant women, the process of childbirth, and newborn infants, such as complications, infections, and congenital anomalies. This category is used in the Global Burden of Disease study to quantify the morbidity and mortality associated with these conditions.
[GBD.2] Non-communicable diseases -- GBD.2 denotes non-communicable diseases, a group of chronic health conditions not caused by infectious agents. It includes cardiovascular disease, cancer, diabetes, chronic respiratory diseases, and musculoskeletal disorders, which together represent a leading source of global mortality and disability in the Global Burden of Disease framework.
[GBD.2A] Neoplasms -- GBD.2A "Neoplasms" groups all malignant and benign tumors, covering the full spectrum of cancers that contribute substantially to global mortality and disability. It includes cancers of any organ system and is used in the Global Burden of Disease study to quantify disease burden and track trends worldwide.
[GBD.2B] Cardiovascular diseases -- GBD.2B designates cardiovascular diseases, encompassing conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels such as coronary artery disease, stroke, and hypertension; these disorders are among the leading causes of death and disability globally.
[GBD.2C] Chronic respiratory diseases -- GBD.2C in the Global Burden of Disease cause list groups chronic respiratory diseases, a set of long-term conditions that impair breathing and lung function. It includes chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, asthma, occupational lung diseases, interstitial lung disorders and other persistent airway ailments that contribute significantly to worldwide morbidity and mortality.
[GBD.2D] Digestive diseases -- GBD.2D denotes digestive diseases within the Global Burden of Disease cause list. It groups conditions affecting the gastrointestinal tract, including disorders of the esophagus, stomach, intestines and related organs, and serves as a category for estimating mortality and morbidity worldwide.
[GBD.2E] Neurological disorders -- GBD.2E designates the group of neurological disorders within the Global Burden of Disease cause list, covering conditions that affect the brain, spinal cord and nerves such as stroke, epilepsy, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis and migraine, and is used for estimating mortality and disability worldwide.
[GBD.2F] Mental disorders -- GBD.2F is the code for mental disorders in the Global Burden of Disease cause list. It aggregates psychiatric conditions such as depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and substance-use disorders, representing their contribution to mortality, morbidity and disability-adjusted life years worldwide.
[GBD.2G] Substance use disorders -- Substance use disorders encompass harmful patterns of alcohol, illicit drug, or prescription medication use that lead to significant health loss and disability. In the Global Burden of Disease framework they are quantified by mortality, years lived with disability, and overall disease burden, reflecting their impact on mortality and morbidity worldwide.
[GBD.2H] Diabetes and kidney diseases -- GBD.2H identifies the combined category of diabetes and kidney diseases in the Global Burden of Disease cause list, grouping metabolic and renal disorders that often co-occur and are analyzed together for mortality
[GBD.2I] Skin and subcutaneous diseases -- The GBD.2I entry groups all conditions that affect the skin and subcutaneous tissues, such as infections, inflammatory disorders, neoplasms and other dermatological diseases. It is used in the Global Burden of Disease cause list to estimate the morbidity and mortality associated with these conditions.
[GBD.2J] Musculoskeletal disorders -- The Global Burden of Disease (GBD) Cause List code GBD.2J groups all musculoskeletal disorders, encompassing conditions that affect bones, joints, muscles, and connective tissues such as arthritis, low-back pain, osteoporosis and musculoskeletal injuries. These disorders are a leading source of disability and health loss worldwide.
[GBD.3] Injuries -- GBD.3 refers to injuries, a category in the Global Burden of Disease cause list that aggregates all fatal and non-fatal injury outcomes
[GBD.3A] Transport injuries -- GBD.3A in the Global Burden of Disease cause list groups all injuries that result from transport-related incidents, including road traffic crashes, air travel accidents, railway and waterway collisions, and exposure to vehicles as pedestrians or cyclists. These are quantified to assess mortality and disability attributable to transport.
[GBD.3B] Unintentional injuries -- GBD.3B groups all injuries that happen without intent, covering accidents such as road-traffic crashes, falls, drowning, burns, poisoning and other unplanned events. The category is used in the Global Burden of Disease study to quantify worldwide mortality and disability from these unintentional injuries.
[GBD.3C] Self-harm and interpersonal violence -- GBD.3C groups deaths and disability resulting from self-inflicted injuries and interpersonal violence, including homicide, assault, and conflict-related injuries. It quantifies the contribution of these external causes to the global burden of disease.
