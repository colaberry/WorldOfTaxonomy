[arb] Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration -- The UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration (code arb) provides a unified legal framework for the conduct of international commercial arbitrations, aiming to harmonize procedural rules across jurisdictions. It addresses the arbitration agreement, appointment of arbitrators, conduct of proceedings, and enforcement of awards, facilitating greater predictability and efficiency in cross-border dispute resolution.

[arb_2] Arbitration agreement -- The UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration defines an arbitration agreement as a contract by which parties consent to submit present or future disputes to arbitration, specifying the scope, form and procedural autonomy of the arbitration. It sets standards for the validity, enforcement and recognition of such agreements across jurisdictions.
[arb_3] Composition of arbitral tribunal -- The entry outlines the rules governing how an arbitral tribunal is formed under the UNCITRAL Model Laws, including the number of arbitrators, methods for their appointment by the parties or an appointing authority, and provisions for replacing arbitrators or adjusting the tribunal's size to suit the dispute.
[arb_4] Conduct of arbitral proceedings -- The UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration sets out the procedural framework for conducting arbitral proceedings, including the organization of hearings, the handling of evidence, and the duties of the arbitral tribunal. It seeks to promote fairness, efficiency and uniformity in international commercial arbitration across different legal systems.

These rules aim to ensure clarity, enforceability and efficiency in international commercial arbitration.

[proc] Model Law on Public Procurement -- The UNCITRAL Model Law on Public Procurement offers a uniform legal framework for governments to carry out procurement in a transparent, non-discriminatory, and efficient way, helping to foster cross-border trade and curb corruption. It is designed for adoption by national legislatures to harmonize procurement procedures and promote fair competition.

[sec] Model Law on Secured Transactions -- The UNCITRAL Model Law on Secured Transactions provides a uniform legal framework for creating, perfecting and enforcing security interests in personal property, aiming to modernize and harmonize secured-credit practices worldwide. It sets standards for priority rules, collateral enforcement and creditor-debtor relations, and has been adopted or adapted by many national legislatures to facilitate cross-border lending.
