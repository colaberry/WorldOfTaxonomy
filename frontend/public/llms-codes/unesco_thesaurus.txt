[UT] UNESCO Domains -- UT is a top-level term in the UNESCO Thesaurus that designates the principal thematic domains employed by UNESCO. It groups major areas such as education, science, culture, communication and information, providing a hierarchical anchor for more specific concepts within the controlled vocabulary.
[UT.01] Education -- UT.01 in the UNESCO Thesaurus (Skeleton) denotes the concept of education, covering formal, non-formal and informal learning processes, institutions, curricula, pedagogy and policy frameworks aimed at the acquisition of knowledge, skills and values. It serves as a primary descriptor for any resource dealing with teaching, training, lifelong learning, literacy and the development of human potential.
[UT.02] Science -- UT.02 Science in the UNESCO Thesaurus (Skeleton) denotes the body of systematic knowledge and methods that investigate natural phenomena, human behavior, and the physical universe, encompassing both natural and social sciences and their related disciplines, methodologies and institutions.
[UT.03] Culture -- Culture in the UNESCO Thesaurus refers to the shared patterns of behavior, beliefs, symbols, and material objects that together define a society's way of life, encompassing its arts, customs, social institutions, knowledge systems, and heritage. It is the collective expression of human creativity and identity.
[UT.04] Social and Human Sciences -- A broad disciplinary domain that groups knowledge areas concerned with the study of societies, cultures, human behavior and social institutions, including sociology, anthropology, psychology, economics, political science, law, education and related fields.
[UT.05] Information and Communication -- Information and Communication refers to the integrated processes of creating, processing, storing, transmitting and disseminating data, knowledge and messages through various media and technological systems. It encompasses telecommunications, broadcasting, the internet, digital platforms, and related infrastructure that enable interactive exchange and access to information worldwide.
[UT.06] Politics, Law, Economics -- UT.06 groups the core concepts of political science, legal studies and economics, focusing on institutions, policies, governance mechanisms, legal frameworks, market structures and the interplay among power, regulation and resource allocation. It serves as a cross-disciplinary reference point for topics such as public policy, international law, economic development and comparative political-economic analysis.
[UT.07] Countries and Country Groupings -- UT.07, "Countries and Country Groupings," refers to individual nation-states, their political boundaries, and collections of nations such as regions, unions, and economic blocs. It is used to index works dealing with geography, international relations, comparative studies, and development policies.
[UT.08] Natural Sciences and Earth Sciences -- UT.08 in the UNESCO Thesaurus (Skeleton) designates the broad field of Natural Sciences and Earth Sciences, encompassing the systematic study of the physical world and the planet. It includes disciplines such as physics, chemistry, biology, geology, meteorology and related research areas that investigate natural phenomena and Earth processes.
[UT.09] Engineering and Technology -- Engineering and Technology encompasses the systematic application of scientific knowledge to design, develop, and maintain structures, machines, processes, and systems. It includes disciplines such as civil, mechanical, electrical, and computer engineering, as well as applied sciences and innovation in industry and infrastructure.
[UT.10] Environmental Sciences -- UT.10 Environmental Sciences denotes the interdisciplinary field that examines natural and built environments, their processes, and the interactions between human societies and ecosystems. It encompasses the study of ecosystems, climate, pollution, resource management, and sustainability, integrating knowledge from biology, chemistry, physics, geology, and social sciences to address environmental challenges.
[UT.11] Health and Nutrition -- UT.11 Health and Nutrition encompasses concepts related to the physical well-being of individuals and populations, including the study and promotion of dietary practices, disease prevention, public health policies, and nutrition science. It covers health education, food security, and the impact of nutrition on human development.
[UT.12] Agriculture and Food -- UT.12 Agriculture and Food groups concepts related to the cultivation of crops, livestock rearing, fisheries, and associated activities that produce, process, distribute and consume food. It encompasses research, policy, sustainability, nutrition, food security and rural development within the global food system.

[UT.14] Demographics and Population -- UT.14 Demographics and Population denotes the statistical study of human groups, covering size, composition, geographic distribution, birth and death rates, migration, and related social characteristics. It includes methods for collecting, analyzing and interpreting population data to support policy, planning and research on societal trends.
