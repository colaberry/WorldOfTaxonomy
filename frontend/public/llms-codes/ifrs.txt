[IAS1] Presentation of Financial Statements -- IAS 1 establishes the overall requirements for the presentation of financial statements, specifying the minimum components such as the statement of financial position, profit or loss and other comprehensive income, cash-flow statement and notes. It provides guidance on the format, structure and disclosure of information to ensure comparability, relevance and clarity for users.
[IAS10] Events After Reporting Period -- IAS 10 prescribes the accounting treatment for events that occur after the end of the reporting period but before the financial statements are authorized for issue. It requires adjusting events to be reflected in the statements, while non-adjusting events are disclosed if material.
[IAS12] Income Taxes -- IAS 12, the International Accounting Standard on Income Taxes, prescribes how to account for current and deferred tax consequences of an entity's transactions and other events. It requires recognition of tax assets and liabilities based on taxable profit, tax rates and the tax base of assets and liabilities, and guides measurement, presentation, and disclosure of tax effects in the financial statements.
[IAS16] Property, Plant and Equipment -- IAS 16 establishes the accounting framework for property, plant and equipment that an entity uses in its operations, detailing when such assets should be recognised, how they are measured at cost or revalued amount, the method of allocating depreciation over useful lives, and the required disclosures in the financial statements.
[IAS19] Employee Benefits -- IAS 19 specifies the accounting treatment for employee benefits, covering short-term benefits such as wages and annual leave, post-employment benefits like pensions, other long-term benefits such as long service leave, and termination benefits. It requires entities to recognize and measure obligations using actuarial assumptions and to disclose the nature, timing and amount of the benefits.

[IAS21] Foreign Exchange Rates -- IAS 21 sets out the accounting and disclosure requirements for foreign currency transactions and the translation of foreign operations in financial statements, defining the functional currency and the exchange rates to be used. It requires translation at the reporting-date rate and mandates that exchange differences be recognised in profit or loss, except for items specifically recognised in equity.

[IAS24] Related Party Disclosures -- IAS 24 sets out the requirements for a reporting entity to disclose information about its relationships with parties that have the ability to influence or be influenced by the entity, and the transactions and outstanding balances with those parties. The standard aims to enhance transparency and enable users of financial statements to assess the effects of related-party relationships on the entity's financial position and performance.
[IAS27] Separate Financial Statements -- IAS 27, part of the IFRS Standards, prescribes how an investing entity should prepare separate financial statements, detailing the accounting treatment for investments in subsidiaries, associates and joint ventures, typically using the equity method or cost model, and outlining disclosure requirements for those holdings.
[IAS28] Investments in Associates -- IAS 28 sets out the accounting for investments in associates, defining an associate as an entity over which the investor has significant influence but not control or joint control. It requires the equity method, with the investment initially recognized at cost and subsequently adjusted for the investor's share of the associate's profit or loss, other comprehensive income and dividends received.
[IAS32] Financial Instruments: Presentation -- IAS 32 establishes the accounting and presentation requirements for financial instruments, guiding how entities classify items as financial assets, financial liabilities or equity
[IAS33] Earnings Per Share -- IAS 33 sets out the principles for calculating and presenting earnings per share in an entity's financial statements, requiring the distinction between basic and diluted EPS and the disclosure of the number and weighted-average shares used in the computation. It ensures comparability of profit per share information across companies.
[IAS36] Impairment of Assets -- IAS 36 prescribes the procedures for identifying, measuring and recognising impairment losses on assets, ensuring they are not carried above their recoverable amount. The recoverable amount is the higher of fair value less costs of disposal and value in use, and any loss is recorded in profit or loss when indicators of impairment arise.
[IAS37] Provisions and Contingencies -- IAS 37, "Provisions and Contingencies," sets out the criteria for recognizing and measuring provisions, assets and liabilities of uncertain timing or amount, and requires disclosure of contingent liabilities and assets, ensuring that an entity's financial statements reflect probable future obligations and possible gains in a consistent, comparable manner.
[IAS38] Intangible Assets -- IAS 38, Intangible Assets, prescribes how entities should recognize, measure and disclose intangible assets such as patents, trademarks, software and goodwill. An asset is recognized when it is identifiable, the entity controls it and probable future economic benefits can be measured reliably; thereafter it is initially recorded at cost and subsequently carried at cost less accumulated amortization and impairment losses, unless it has an indefinite useful life.
[IAS40] Investment Property -- IAS 40 defines investment property as real estate held to earn rentals or for capital appreciation, not for use in production. The standard prescribes measurement, recognition, and disclosure requirements, allowing either a cost model or a fair value model for subsequent measurement.
[IAS7] Statement of Cash Flows -- IAS 7 sets out the requirements for presenting a statement of cash flows, detailing cash inflows and outflows from operating, investing and financing activities, and provides guidance on classification, measurement and disclosure to help users assess liquidity and cash management.
[IAS8] Accounting Policies -- IAS 8 sets out the principles for selecting and applying accounting policies, dealing with changes in accounting estimates and correcting material errors. It requires entities to apply policies consistently and to disclose any changes, the reasons for them, and the effects on financial statements, using retrospective or prospective approaches as appropriate.
[IFRS] IFRS Standards -- IFRS Standards are a set of globally recognized accounting principles issued by the International Accounting Standards Board. They provide consistent guidelines for preparing and presenting financial statements, facilitating comparability across international markets.
[IFRS1] First-time Adoption of IFRS -- IFRS 1 outlines the procedures for entities adopting International Financial Reporting Standards for the first time, specifying how to prepare an opening balance sheet, apply IFRS retrospectively and use limited exemptions to ease the transition.

[IFRS11] Joint Arrangements -- IFRS 11 establishes the accounting for joint arrangements, separating them into joint ventures, which are accounted for using the equity method, and joint operations, where parties recognise directly their share of assets, liabilities, revenues and expenses.
[IFRS12] Disclosure of Interests -- IFRS 12 sets out the disclosure requirements for an entity's interests in subsidiaries, associates, joint ventures and other entities, ensuring that users of financial statements understand the nature, extent and risks of those interests and the related financial effects.
[IFRS13] Fair Value Measurement -- IFRS 13 establishes a single framework for measuring fair value that applies to all IFRS standards requiring such measurement. It defines fair value, sets out a hierarchy of observable input levels, and outlines the valuation techniques and disclosures needed for transparent financial reporting.
[IFRS15] Revenue from Contracts -- IFRS 15 establishes the principles for recognizing revenue from contracts with customers, requiring entities to apply a five-step model that identifies performance obligations, determines the transaction price, allocates that price to each obligation, and recognizes revenue when control transfers. It also provides guidance on contract modifications, variable consideration, and the presentation of revenue in financial statements.

[IFRS17] Insurance Contracts -- IFRS 17 is an International Financial Reporting Standard that prescribes how insurers should recognise, measure and disclose insurance contracts, replacing IFRS 4. It requires measurement using current estimates of future cash flows, a risk adjustment and a contractual service margin, aiming to improve comparability and transparency across the insurance industry.
[IFRS2] Share-based Payment -- IFRS 2 establishes the accounting framework for share-based payment transactions, requiring entities to recognise the fair value of goods or services received in exchange for equity instruments. It applies to all share-based arrangements, such as employee stock options, and prescribes measurement, recognition and disclosure requirements.
[IFRS3] Business Combinations -- IFRS 3 sets out the accounting rules for business combinations, requiring the acquiring entity to apply purchase accounting by recognising identifiable assets, liabilities and any non-controlling interest at fair value and measuring goodwill as the excess of consideration over the fair value of these identifiable items. The standard also prescribes detailed disclosure requirements to help users understand the nature and financial effects of the acquisition.
[IFRS5] Non-current Assets Held for Sale -- IFRS 5 establishes the accounting and presentation requirements for non-current assets that are classified as held for sale and for discontinued operations, mandating that such assets be measured at the lower of carrying amount and fair value less costs to sell and that they be presented separately from continuing-operation assets on the balance sheet.
[IFRS7] Financial Instruments: Disclosures -- IFRS 7 requires entities to disclose the significance of their financial instruments, the nature and extent of associated risks, and the policies for managing those risks. It enhances transparency by mandating both qualitative and quantitative information on credit, liquidity and market risk exposures, helping users assess the financial position and performance of the entity.

[IFRS9] Financial Instruments -- IFRS 9 sets out the accounting principles for financial instruments, covering classification, measurement, impairment and hedge accounting. It requires entities to classify assets as amortised cost, fair value through other comprehensive income or fair value through profit or loss, and introduces a forward-looking expected-credit-loss model for impairment. The standard supersedes IAS 39 and aims to improve transparency and comparability in financial reporting.
