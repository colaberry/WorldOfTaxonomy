[IRB] Internal Ratings-Based Approach Classes -- The IRB entry denotes the Internal Ratings-Based approach used in the Basel III/IV exposure-category framework. Under this method banks calculate credit-risk weights from their own estimates of probability of default, loss-given-default and exposure at default, either in the advanced or foundation form, to determine regulatory capital requirements. This classification groups exposures that are assessed using those internal models.
[IRB-BNK] Bank -- The IRB-BNK category groups banks that apply the internal ratings-based (IRB) approach for assessing credit risk under the Basel III/IV framework. Entities in this class are subject to the capital adequacy, risk-weighting, and supervisory standards set out in BCBS 189/424 for banking institutions.
[IRB-CORP] Corporate -- IRB-CORP denotes corporate exposures assessed under the Basel III/IV internal-ratings-based (IRB) approach. It covers loans and other credit facilities granted to non-financial corporations, with risk-weights derived from the borrower's internal credit rating and the regulator-prescribed mapping tables. This category is used for capital adequacy calculations.
[IRB-EQ] Equity -- IRB-EQ denotes the internal-ratings-based exposure category for equity assets in the Basel III/IV framework. It includes holdings of listed or unlisted shares, convertible securities and similar equity-type instruments that a bank assesses using its own internal credit models to determine capital requirements. This classification guides risk weighting and regulatory capital calculations.
[IRB-ORPE] Other Retail -- IRB-ORPE denotes the "Other Retail" exposure category in the Basel III/IV framework, covering retail credit exposures that do not fall under the standard consumer, credit-card or residential-mortgage sub-segments. It includes small-business loans, point-of-sale financing, and other retail-type credit lines that are assessed using the internal-ratings-based approach, with risk-weighting calibrated to the institution's own PD, LGD and EAD estimates.
[IRB-QRRE] Qualifying Revolving Retail -- IRB-QRRE designates qualifying revolving retail exposures under the Basel III/IV IRB framework. These are short-term consumer credit facilities such as credit-card balances or unsecured personal lines that meet BCBS criteria for low-default risk and therefore receive preferential risk-weight treatment. The classification is used by banks to assign capital requirements to retail revolving loans that are deemed to have strong credit quality and stable repayment behavior.
[IRB-RET] Retail - Residential Mortgage -- IRB-RET identifies the retail exposure category for residential mortgage loans in the Basel III/IV internal-ratings-based framework. It applies to credit facilities secured by private dwellings, whether owner-occupied or rental, and defines the risk-weighting and capital charge rules specific to these mortgage assets.
[IRB-SOV] Sovereign -- IRB-SOV designates sovereign exposures that banks assess using the internal-ratings-based (IRB) approach under Basel III/IV. These exposures are treated as sovereign credit risk, with risk-weight calculations based on the sovereign's own rating and specific supervisory parameters, influencing the capital charge required for such assets.
[IRB-SPEC] Specialised Lending (Project, Object, Commodity) -- IRB-SPEC denotes specialised lending exposures-project finance, object finance and commodity finance-that are assessed under the internal-ratings-based (IRB) approach. These assets involve financing a single project, a specific physical asset, or a commodity portfolio, and require tailored risk parameters distinct from standard corporate lending. The classification guides banks in applying Basel III/IV capital requirements to these concentrated, often higher-risk exposures.
[MKT] Market Risk Exposure Classes -- MKT denotes Market Risk Exposure Classes, grouping banking assets and off-balance-sheet items whose value fluctuates with market movements such as interest rates, equity prices, foreign exchange rates or commodity prices, and that are subject to capital requirements under the Basel III/IV framework. This category includes positions in securities, derivatives, and other instruments for which market risk is the primary driver of loss potential.
[MKT-DRC] Default Risk Charge -- MKT-DRC is the Default Risk Charge element within the Basel III/IV market-risk framework (BCBS 189/424). It assigns capital for the potential loss from defaults of trading-book positions, complementing the other market-risk capital charges.
[MKT-IMA] Internal Model Approach -- MKT-IMA identifies the Internal Model Approach, a Basel III/IV method that lets banks calculate market-risk capital charges using their own approved risk-factor models instead of the standardized formula. The approach requires regulatory validation of the models and is applied to market-risk exposure categories.
[MKT-RNNM] Residual Risk Add-On -- MKT-RNNM denotes the Residual Risk Add-On component within the Basel III/IV market exposure framework. It represents an extra capital charge applied to market positions to capture residual risks that are not fully captured by primary risk models, ensuring a more comprehensive risk coverage.
[MKT-SENSI] Sensitivity-Based Method -- The MKT-SENSI entry refers to the Sensitivity-Based Method used in the Basel III/IV market-risk framework, where a bank's exposure is derived from the sensitivities of instrument values to underlying risk-factor changes. It is part of the standardized approach for calculating capital requirements for market risk, applying predefined risk-weight formulas rather than internal models.
[OP] Operational Risk Approaches -- OP denotes the Operational Risk Approaches category, covering the methodologies banks use to assess and capitalise operational risk under Basel III/IV, including the Basic Indicator Approach, Standardized Approach and Advanced Measurement Approach.
[OP-BIA] Basic Indicator Approach -- The Basic Indicator Approach (code OP-BIA) is a Basel III/IV method for measuring operational-risk capital, where banks calculate a capital charge as a fixed percentage of their gross income. It is the simplest of the three approved approaches, applying a uniform factor to the annual gross income to determine the required regulatory capital.

[OP-TSA] Standardised Approach (OpRisk) -- OP-TSA is the code for the Standardised Approach to Operational Risk under the Basel III/IV exposure-category framework (BCBS 189/424). It defines the method banks must use to calculate operational-risk capital by applying prescribed risk-weight formulas to business lines and event types, replacing the older Advanced Measurement Approach. This approach standardises capital requirements across institutions while allowing limited internal model use.
[SA] Standardised Approach Exposure Classes -- SA denotes the Standardised Approach exposure classes defined in the Basel III/IV framework for credit-risk weighting. It groups exposures - for example corporate, sovereign, retail, equity-investment and specialised lending - so that banks can apply uniform risk-weight formulas prescribed by the BCBS.
[SA-ADC] Land Acquisition, Development and Construction -- SA-ADC designates exposure to land acquisition, development and construction projects, covering financing of raw land purchases, site preparation, infrastructure build-out and building erection before a borrower generates cash flow. It is treated as a real-estate-related credit under Basel III/IV, with risk-weighting reflecting construction-phase risk, collateral quality and the borrower's leverage.
[SA-BNK] Banks and Covered Bonds -- The SA-BNK category groups exposures to banks and to covered-bond issuers, reflecting their high credit quality and strong regulatory capital treatment under Basel III/IV. These assets are generally assigned lower risk-weightings because they benefit from stringent supervisory oversight and the collateral backing of covered bonds. This classification helps institutions calculate capital requirements for such low-risk exposures.
[SA-CORP] Corporates -- SA-CORP designates corporate borrowers within the Basel III/IV standardized-approach exposure classification. It covers non-financial firms, both listed and unlisted, whose credit risk is measured using the Basel-prescribed risk weights based on external ratings or the internal-ratings-based proxy. Under this category, banks apply the standard risk-weight formulas to determine capital requirements for corporate exposures.
[SA-CRE] Commercial Real Estate -- SA-CRE is the Standardised Approach category for Commercial Real-Estate exposures in the Basel III/IV framework (BCBS 189/424). It covers loans and other credit facilities to non-residential property developers, owners and investors, and assigns a prescribed risk-weight based on the asset-type, loan-to-value and borrower-sector risk characteristics.
[SA-CRET] Income-Producing Real Estate (IPRE) -- SA-CRET designates Income-Producing Real Estate, a credit-risk exposure category in the Basel III/IV framework that covers owned or financed properties generating rental or lease income, such as office, retail, industrial or residential assets. These exposures are subject to specific risk-weighting rules reflecting the cash-flow stability and market liquidity of the underlying real estate.
[SA-DEF] Defaulted Exposures -- SA-DEF denotes exposures that have been declared in default under the Basel III/IV framework. It includes loans, bonds or other credit facilities where the borrower has missed required payments for a specified period or is otherwise judged unable to meet obligations, triggering heightened capital and risk-weight requirements.
[SA-EQ] Equity Exposures -- SA-EQ identifies an exposure to equity instruments, covering holdings of listed or unlisted shares, equity funds and other equity-linked securities that are subject to the capital-treatment rules of Basel III/IV.
[SA-MDB] Multilateral Development Banks -- SA-MDB designates exposures to multilateral development banks, which are internationally-backed institutions that fund infrastructure and development projects in emerging economies. Under Basel III/IV these exposures are assigned a low risk-weight because the banks enjoy strong sovereign support and high credit quality.

[SA-OTH] Other Assets -- SA-OTH identifies "Other Assets" that are not captured by the standard exposure categories in the Basel III/IV framework. These are residual or miscellaneous items such as cash, accrued interest or other non-risk-weighted holdings, and they typically receive a zero risk weight unless a specific rule assigns a different treatment.
[SA-RET] Retail Exposures -- SA-RET denotes retail exposures, which are credit exposures to households, small-business customers and other retail-type borrowers such as credit-card and mortgage loans. In the Basel III/IV exposure-category framework these assets are assigned a specific risk-weighting rule that reflects their relatively low individual credit risk and high diversification, influencing a bank's capital adequacy calculations.
[SA-RRET] Regulatory Retail -- SA-RRET denotes the regulatory retail exposure category under the Basel III/IV classification (BCBS 189/424). It groups credit exposures that are broadly retail in nature, such as consumer loans, credit-card balances, small-business lending and other low-granularity retail assets, which are assessed using the standardised approach for risk-weight calculations. This category informs banks' capital requirement reporting and supervisory monitoring.

[SA-SME] SME Corporates -- The SA-SME exposure category groups corporate borrowers that meet the Basel III/IV definition of a small or medium-sized enterprise, typically having annual revenues under 50 million euros or a limited number of employees. These firms receive a reduced risk-weighting relative to larger corporates, reflecting their generally lower credit risk and the regulatory intent to support financing for the SME sector.
[SA-SOV] Sovereigns and Central Banks -- SA-SOV identifies exposures to sovereign governments and their central banks, which are generally treated as low-risk assets with high liquidity and an implicit government guarantee. Under the Basel III/IV framework this category is used to calculate capital requirements, reflecting the credit quality and preferential treatment of such exposures.
[SA-SPEC] Specialised Lending -- SA-SPEC denotes the "Specialised Lending" exposure category in the Basel III/IV framework, covering loans and credit facilities that target narrow, high-risk asset classes such as project finance, real estate, infrastructure, commodities or other sector-specific financing. Because these exposures exhibit greater credit concentration and complexity, they attract higher risk weights and more stringent capital buffers than standard corporate lending.
[SA-SUB] Subordinated Debt -- SA-SUB identifies subordinated debt positions that rank below senior obligations in a bank's capital structure, qualifying as Tier 2 capital under Basel III/IV. The classification is used to assess loss-absorbing capacity and exposure limits for regulatory capital calculations.
