[IEEE] IEEE Standards Areas -- The IEEE entry groups the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers' standards by technical area, covering fields such as communications, computers, power, and signal processing. It serves as a taxonomy for locating and referencing IEEE-issued specifications and guidelines across engineering and technology domains.

[IEEE.1547] Distributed Energy Resources -- IEEE 1547 is the core IEEE standard that defines the technical requirements and performance criteria for interconnecting distributed energy resources-such as solar photovoltaics, wind turbines, and storage systems-with electric power grids, covering safety, testing, and operational compatibility.
[IEEE.1588] Precision Time Protocol -- IEEE 1588 defines the Precision Time Protocol, a network-based method for synchronizing clocks of devices with sub-microsecond accuracy. It is widely used in industrial automation, telecommunications and financial trading to ensure coordinated timing across Ethernet networks.
[IEEE.1901] Broadband over Power Line -- IEEE 1901 is a standard that specifies the physical and media access control layers for broadband communication over existing power-line wiring, enabling high-speed data transfer-up to roughly 200 Mbps-between devices in homes, offices, and smart-grid applications. It defines signal modulation, channel coding, and networking protocols to ensure reliable operation across the noisy electrical infrastructure.
[IEEE.2030] Smart Grid -- IEEE 2030 is a guide that outlines the interoperability framework for integrating energy and information technologies within a smart grid. It defines reference models, standards, and best practices to enable reliable, secure, and efficient communication among diverse power system components, facilitating the transition to advanced, automated grid operations.
[IEEE.2048] Virtual Reality and AR -- IEEE.2048 is the IEEE standard that defines terminology, performance metrics, and interoperability guidelines for virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) systems. It addresses hardware interfaces, sensor integration, and user-experience considerations to promote compatibility across devices and applications.
[IEEE.2413] IoT Architecture -- IEEE 2413 defines a standard framework for the architecture of the Internet of Things, specifying layers, interfaces, and functional components to promote interoperability and scalability across IoT systems. It outlines reference models and terminology to guide designers and developers in building robust, secure IoT solutions.
[IEEE.2621] Wireless Diabetes Devices -- IEEE.2621 is a draft IEEE standard that defines wireless communication requirements for diabetes management devices such as continuous glucose monitors and insulin pumps. It specifies interoperable protocols, data security measures, power-efficiency guidelines and standardized data formats to enable reliable, safe exchange of health information between devices and external systems.
[IEEE.7000] Ethics in System Design -- IEEE 7000 defines a systematic approach for integrating ethical principles into the design and development of systems. It guides engineers to identify stakeholders, assess potential societal impacts, and embed values such as safety, privacy and fairness throughout the product lifecycle, helping organizations manage ethical risks and promote responsible innovation.
[IEEE.754] Floating Point Arithmetic -- IEEE 754 defines formats and operations for binary and decimal floating-point arithmetic, specifying precision, rounding, exception handling, and interoperability across hardware and software. It is the core standard for representing real numbers in computing.
[IEEE.802] 802 Networking (LAN/MAN) -- IEEE 802 is a family of standards that define physical and data-link layer protocols for local and metropolitan area networks. Managed by the IEEE LAN/MAN Standards Committee, it includes specifications such as 802.3 Ethernet, 802.11 wireless LAN, and 802.15.1 Bluetooth.
[IEEE.C37] Power Systems Relay -- The IEEE C37 series defines standards for power system relays and protection devices, covering specifications for design, testing, performance, and application of electromechanical, static, and digital relays used in transmission and distribution networks. It ensures interoperability, reliability, and safety across protective equipment from manufacturers and utilities.
