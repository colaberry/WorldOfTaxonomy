[C30] CRYSTAL GROWTH -- C30 covers the science and technology of growing crystals, including methods, apparatus and conditions for producing single-crystal or polycrystalline materials. It addresses solution, melt, vapor-phase and epitaxial techniques for inorganic, organic and hybrid substances, as well as related monitoring and control processes.
[C30B] SINGLE-CRYSTAL GROWTH -- **Definition:** This place covers:  Single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline material with a defined structure; production thereof.  Processes and apparatus used in obtaining these crystals and materials.  Processes and apparatus for the after-treatment of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline material with a defined structure.  **Limiting references (this place does not cover):** - Formation of diamonds using ultra-high pressure -> B01J3/06 - Casting of metals, casting of other substances by the same processes or devices -> B22D - Zone-refining of metals or alloys -> C22B - Changing the physical structure of non-ferrous metals or alloys by heat treatment or by hot or cold working -> C22F - Semiconductor devices; Electric solid-state devices not otherwise provided for -> H10  **Glossary:** - amorphous: description of a solid material not having long-range crystalline lattice structure. - bulk/layer: bulk crystals have dimensions which are comparable in all three dimensions whereas layers have one dimension (thickness) significantly less than the other two dimensions (surface area). Often a bulk layer is grown from a discrete "seed" whereas a layer is grown epitaxially on a substrate. - crystal: description of a solid material having long-range order of atoms or molecules arranged in a regularly-repeating lattice structure. - defined structure: the structure of a solid material with grains which are oriented in a preferential way or have larger dimensions than normally obtained. - epitaxy: the formation of a crystalline layer on a substrate in such a manner that the formed crystal bears a definite crystallographic relationship to the substrate. - eutectic: description of a mixture or solution containing two or more phases at a composition that has the lowest melting point and where the phases simultaneously crystallise from solution at this temperature. - grains: crystalline regions in a solid material, each grain generally being a single crystalline region. - homogeneous polycrystalline material: a solid material with crystal particles, all of which have the same chemical composition. - lattice: an ordered arrangement of atoms or molecules within a solid material. - nanocrystals: single crystals having at least one dimension less than 100 nm. The term includes nanowires, nanotubes, nanorods etc. - oriented crystal: a polycrystalline structure in which the grains are generally aligned in a preferential direction such as obtained in columnar growth. - seed: a material, usually itself a small single-crystal, upon which a single-crystal is grown, the seeded crystal growth proceeding by the alignment of atoms or molecules or clusters into a thermodynamically favoured arrangement determined by the nature of the seed. - single-crystal: description of a solid material having long-range order of atoms or molecules in a regularly-repeating lattice structure. Also includes twin crystals and a predominantly single crystal product. - superlattice: a single-crystal having an internal structure of more than two layers, each layer having a composition different from the next adjacent layer. - twin crystal: a crystalline material in which the adjoining crystalline lattices have a mirror-image symmetrical relationship, the interface between the adjoining crystals being termed the twin plane. - whiskers/needles: discrete solid crystalline particles of generally elongated shape. Dimensions are superior to 100 nm and are not considered as nanocrystals (nanowires, nanorods etc.) - zone melting: description of a process in which a crystallised body is formed by melting a zone of a starting material with subsequent cooling and crystallisation while either the zone or the starting product is displaced so that all or part of the starting material is converted into the crystallised body.

[C30B 1/02] by thermal treatment, e.g. strain annealing -- This subgroup covers methods of producing single crystals by applying heat, such as strain annealing, where thermal treatment relieves internal stresses and induces crystal growth from solid or gel precursors.
[C30B 1/023] from solids with amorphous structure -- C30B 1/023 covers methods for obtaining a single crystal by converting a solid material that has an amorphous structure-such as a glass, powder or amorphous polymer-into a crystalline form, typically using temperature gradients, solvent diffusion or other controlled processes that induce nucleation and growth. [C30B 1/026] Solid phase epitaxial growth through a disordered intermediate layer -- **Definition:** This place covers:  Growth methods where for example the crystallographic structure of a substrate is transferred via e.g. an intermediate amorphous layer to an overlying layer during a heat treatment below the melting point of the different layers.
[C30B 1/04] Isothermal recrystallisation -- Isothermal recrystallisation is a single-crystal growth technique in which a solid or gel is maintained at a constant temperature to promote dissolution of imperfect parts and redeposition of a more ordered lattice, enhancing crystal size and quality without changing temperature conditions.
[C30B 1/06] Recrystallisation under a temperature gradient -- C30B 1/06 covers methods of recrystallising a solid or gel into a single crystal by applying a temperature gradient across the material. The technique exploits differential solubility or dissolution rates caused by the gradient to promote controlled nucleation and growth of larger, defect-free crystals.
[C30B 1/08] Zone recrystallisation -- C30B 1/08 pertains to the method of zone recrystallization, a process used in single-crystal growth from solids or gels. This technique involves moving a zone of molten material through a polycrystalline solid to refine and grow a single crystal.
[C30B 1/10] by solid state reactions or multi-phase diffusion -- C30B 1/10 covers methods of obtaining single crystals by inducing solid-state reactions or allowing diffusion between different phases within solids or gels. The approach relies on chemical transformations occurring directly in the solid matrix rather than from melt or solution.
[C30B 11/00] Single-crystal growth by normal freezing or freezing under temperature gradient, e.g. Bridgman-Stockbarger method -- This CPC subclass covers techniques for producing single crystals by solidifying a melt under normal freezing conditions or by applying a temperature gradient, such as the Bridgman-Stockbarger directional solidification process. [C30B 11/001] Continuous growth -- Continuous growth covers methods for extending a single crystal by moving the melt or solid through a stable temperature gradient so that crystallization proceeds uninterrupted, as in the Bridgman-Stockbarger technique. [C30B 13/00] Single-crystal growth by zone-melting -- C30B 13/00 covers the process of producing single crystals by zone-melting, where a narrow molten region is passed along a solid material to dissolve impurities and promote orderly crystal growth. [C30B 13/005] Continuous growth -- C30B 13/005 covers continuous growth methods in single-crystal production by zone-melting, where a molten zone moves along a solid rod to add material without interruption. [C30B 31/20] Doping by irradiation with electromagnetic waves or by particle radiation -- The subclass C30B 31/20 covers post-growth treatments in which single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline materials are doped by exposing them to electromagnetic waves or to particle radiation. It addresses the introduction of impurity atoms or ions into the crystal lattice using irradiation techniques after the material has been formed. This falls under chemistry → crystal growth → single-crystal growth → after-treatment of single crystals.
[C30B 31/22] by ion-implantation -- This CPC subclass pertains to post-growth treatment of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline materials by implanting ions into their lattice. Ion implantation is employed to modify properties such as conductivity, hardness, doping level or other physical-chemical characteristics. It falls under Chemistry → Crystal Growth → Single-Crystal Growth.

[C30B 33/005] Oxydation -- Oxidation is the chemical after-treatment of grown single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline solids in which oxidising agents-such as gases, plasma or solutions-are applied to modify the surface, composition or bulk structure, often to improve material properties or prepare the crystal for subsequent steps. This process is classified under CPC code C30B 33/005.
[C30B 33/02] Heat treatment -- C30B 33/02 covers heat-treatment procedures applied to single crystals or uniformly polycrystalline materials that have a defined lattice structure, aiming to modify properties such as stress, phase, or defect distribution. The classification falls under chemistry → crystal growth → single-crystal growth.
[C30B 33/04] using electric or magnetic fields or particle radiation -- This CPC subclass concerns post-growth processing of single crystals or uniformly structured polycrystalline materials where electric or magnetic fields or particle radiation are applied to modify properties such as orientation, defect density, or surface condition.
[C30B 33/06] Joining of crystals -- C30B 33/06 relates to methods for joining separate crystals or crystal fragments to create a larger single crystal or composite structure, including welding, diffusion bonding, or adhesive techniques applied after growth. This subclass falls under after-treatment of single crystals within the chemistry, crystal growth, single-crystal growth hierarchy.
[C30B 33/08] Etching -- C30B 33/08 covers etching methods applied as an after-treatment of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline materials with a defined structure. It includes chemical, electrochemical, plasma or other processes that selectively remove material to modify surface morphology, reveal crystallographic features or create micro-patterns.
[C30B 33/10] in solutions or melts -- The C30B 33/10 subclass covers post-growth processing of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline material with a defined structure that is carried out while the material is immersed in a solution or molten medium. Techniques include annealing, chemical etching, ion exchange or other treatments performed in liquid or melt phases to modify properties such as composition, defect density or surface quality.
[C30B 33/12] in gas atmosphere or plasma -- This subclass deals with post-growth processing of single crystals or uniform polycrystalline solids carried out in a controlled gas atmosphere or by plasma treatment, aiming to alter surface chemistry, anneal defects, or introduce dopants.
[C30B 35/00] Apparatus not otherwise provided for, specially adapted for the growth, production or after-treatment of single crystals or of a homogeneous polycrystalline material with defined structure -- C30B 35/00 covers devices specially designed for the growth, production, or post-treatment of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline materials with defined structures that are not covered by other categories. These apparatuses may include customized furnaces, reactors, or handling equipment tailored to precise crystal-forming conditions.
[C30B 35/002] Crucibles or containers -- C30B 35/002 pertains to specialized apparatus for the growth, production, or after-treatment of single crystals or homogeneous polycrystalline materials with defined structure, focusing specifically on crucibles or containers used in these processes.
[C30B 35/005] Transport systems -- This CPC subclass covers devices that move, convey, or otherwise transport materials, reagents, or substrates within apparatus used for single-crystal or polycrystalline growth, production, or post-processing. It includes conveyors, pumps, robotic arms and handling mechanisms specially adapted for crystal-growth systems.

[C30B 7/00] Single-crystal growth from liquids -- C30B 7/00 covers methods for growing single crystals directly from liquid media, including melts, solutions or suspensions, using techniques such as controlled cooling, solvent evaporation, or seeding to produce uniform crystalline material for scientific or industrial applications.
[C30B 7/005] Epitaxial layer growth -- C30B 7/005 covers the formation of epitaxial layers, where a crystalline film is grown on a substrate with a matching lattice orientation, typically from liquid-phase precursors. The subclass is part of the single-crystal growth from liquids hierarchy, addressing methods that control layer orientation, thickness and interface quality for semiconductor and optical applications.
[C30B 7/02] by evaporation of the solvent -- C30B 7/02 covers methods of growing single crystals from liquid solutions where the solvent is removed by evaporation, allowing the solute to crystallize directly onto a substrate or within the solution. The technique is used to obtain high-purity crystals of organic, inorganic or organometallic compounds for structural analysis or material applications.
[C30B 7/04] using aqueous solvents -- This subclass covers methods of growing single crystals from liquid phases where water is used as the solvent. It includes techniques such as solution growth, hydrothermal processes, and other aqueous-based crystallization.
[C30B 7/06] using non-aqueous solvents -- This subclass concerns methods for growing single crystals from liquid media where the solvent is not water. It covers the selection, preparation, and use of organic, inorganic or mixed non-aqueous solvents to dissolve the material and control nucleation and growth. Applications include producing high-purity crystals for optics, electronics, or pharmaceuticals.
[C30B 7/08] by cooling of the solution -- This CPC subclass covers methods of growing single crystals from liquid solutions by cooling the solution to induce crystallization. Temperature reduction creates supersaturation, allowing the solute to precipitate as a single crystal, a technique common in chemistry and materials engineering.
[C30B 7/10] by application of pressure, e.g. hydrothermal processes -- C30B 7/10 covers methods of growing single crystals from liquid media by applying pressure, especially hydrothermal techniques where high temperature and pressure promote crystal formation from aqueous solutions. It is a subclass within chemistry, crystal growth, single-crystal growth and is used to classify patents that rely on pressure-driven crystallization processes.
[C30B 7/105] using ammonia as solvent, i.e. ammonothermal processes -- C30B 7/105 covers single-crystal growth from liquid media in which ammonia is employed as the solvent, known as ammonothermal processes. These methods use high-pressure, high-temperature ammonia solutions to dissolve and recrystallize material, enabling growth of crystals such as nitrides and semiconductors.
[C30B 7/12] by electrolysis -- This CPC subclass covers methods for growing a single crystal from a liquid medium using electrolysis, where an electric current drives the deposition or crystallization of the material from solution. It applies to metals, semiconductors and ionic compounds and falls under the broader category of single-crystal growth from liquids.
[C30B 7/14] the crystallising materials being formed by chemical reactions in the solution -- C30B 7/14 concerns single-crystal growth from liquids where the crystallising material is produced directly by a chemical reaction in the solution, meaning the solid phase forms in situ as the reaction proceeds rather than being introduced from an external source. This subclass falls under crystal growth in chemistry, specifically single-crystal growth from liquids.

[C30B 9/02] by evaporation of the molten solvent -- A method for producing a single crystal from a melt solution in which the molten solvent is deliberately evaporated, causing the solute to become supersaturated and crystallize. This technique falls under the CPC subclass C30B 9/02, part of the hierarchy for single-crystal growth from melt solutions using molten solvents.
[C30B 9/04] by cooling of the solution -- C30B 9/04 covers the production of single crystals by cooling a melt solution that contains a molten solvent, allowing the solute to crystallise as the temperature drops. The method is used when crystals are grown from the melt rather than from vapor or liquid-phase precipitation, and it falls under the hierarchy Chemistry → Crystal Growth → Single-Crystal Growth.
[C30B 9/06] using as solvent a component of the crystal composition -- C30B 9/06 covers processes where a component of the crystal composition is used as the molten solvent during single-crystal growth from melt solutions. The method employs the crystal material itself in a liquid phase to dissolve and recrystallize, enabling growth of single crystals from a melt that contains that component. It falls under the chemistry hierarchy of crystal growth, single-crystal growth, melt-solution techniques.
[C30B 9/08] using other solvents -- This subclass covers methods that use molten solvents other than the primary melt to dissolve the material and promote single-crystal growth from melt solutions. It includes alternative solvent systems, mixed solvent melts or additives that modify crystal-growth conditions.
[C30B 9/10] Metal solvents -- C30B 9/10 deals with the use of metal solvents in single-crystal growth from melt solutions. It covers processes where a molten metal acts as the solvent to dissolve a material and then recrystallize it, enabling the formation of high-quality single crystals.
[C30B 9/12] Salt solvents, e.g. flux growth -- C30B 9/12 covers single-crystal growth from melt solutions in which a salt or molten flux is used as the solvent. The salt solvent lowers the melting point and dissolves the material, allowing controlled crystallisation as the melt cools. This subclass is applied to flux-growth techniques for producing high-quality crystals of inorganic compounds.
[C30B 9/14] by electrolysis -- C30B 9/14 covers single-crystal growth from molten solvents where the crystallisation is driven by electrolysis, meaning an electric current is used to decompose a compound in the melt and deposit the desired crystal. The method applies to materials that can be formed directly from a molten solution under the influence of the electrolytic reaction.
