[IT] ITU-T Series -- The ITU-T Series (code IT) is the comprehensive set of recommendations issued by the International Telecommunication Union's Telecommunication Standardization Sector. It covers technical standards, protocols, and operational guidelines for all aspects of global telecommunications, ensuring interoperability and consistent performance across networks and services.
[IT.A] Series A - Organization -- Series A defines the organisational framework of the ITU-T, detailing the structure, functions and procedures of the Study Groups, secretariat and related bodies that develop telecommunication standards.
[IT.D] Series D - Tariff Principles -- The ITU-T Series D collects the tariff principles that guide the development of telecommunications charging structures. It defines cost-oriented, transparent and non-discriminatory methods for setting rates, including concepts such as cost recovery, market-based pricing and consumer protection, and provides a framework for regulators and operators to design fair and sustainable tariff regimes.
[IT.E] Series E - Telephone Network -- Series E of the ITU-T standards collection defines technical specifications for the public switched telephone network, covering aspects such as signalling protocols, transmission characteristics, network architecture and interfacing requirements that enable consistent, interoperable voice communication across national and international telephone systems.
[IT.F] Series F - Non-Telephone Services -- Series F of the ITU-T recommendations gathers standards that address services using telecommunication networks but not traditional voice telephone functions, encompassing data transmission, video, broadcasting and other multimedia applications. It specifies technical requirements, performance criteria and interworking procedures to ensure consistent operation of these non-telephone services across global networks.
[IT.G] Series G - Transmission Systems -- Series G covers the technical specifications for transmission systems used in telecommunication networks, including analog and digital line codes, signal coding, multiplexing and physical-layer characteristics. It defines performance criteria, network interfaces and test methods for both copper and optical media, supporting local and wide-area links.
[IT.H] Series H - Audiovisual Systems -- Series H of the ITU-T standards addresses audiovisual systems, setting technical specifications for equipment and interfaces used in broadcasting, video conferencing and related services. It provides guidelines on performance, interoperability and safety for audio and video components within telecommunication networks.
[IT.I] Series I - ISDN -- Series I (code IT.I) of the ITU-T Telecommunication Standards covers Integrated Services Digital Network specifications, defining the architecture, protocols and interfaces for digital transmission of voice, data and video over traditional telephone networks.
[IT.J] Series J - Cable Networks -- Series J of the ITU-T recommendations defines technical standards for cable networks, covering the design, operation and performance of coaxial, hybrid fiber-coaxial and all-IP cable distribution systems used for broadband and broadcasting services. It includes specifications for physical plant, transmission technologies, network management and service interfaces to ensure interoperability and quality of service across cable operators worldwide.
[IT.K] Series K - Protection Against Interference -- Series K gathers ITU-T recommendations that define measures for preventing and mitigating electromagnetic interference in telecommunication systems, including shielding, grounding, filtering and network-planning guidelines. The standards specify design and installation practices for equipment to maintain reliable operation across diverse frequency bands.
[IT.L] Series L - Environment and ICTs -- Series L of the ITU-T standards addresses the intersection of environmental considerations and information and communication technologies. It provides guidelines, measurement methods and best-practice recommendations for assessing and reducing the ecological impact of network infrastructure, equipment and services, supporting sustainable development and greener ICT deployment.
[IT.M] Series M - Telecom Management -- Series M of the ITU-T recommendations focuses on telecom management, defining procedures and frameworks for operational support, fault handling, performance monitoring and network configuration across fixed and mobile networks. The standards aim to improve reliability, efficiency and coordinated control of network resources in diverse telecommunications environments.
[IT.O] Series O - Measuring Equipment -- Series O of the ITU-T Telecommunication Standards defines the technical specifications and performance criteria for measuring equipment used in telephone networks, including test sets, line analyzers and calibration devices. It establishes uniform test methods and accuracy requirements to ensure interoperability and reliable network monitoring.
[IT.P] Series P - Telephone Quality -- Series P of the ITU-T recommendations addresses telephone quality, defining objective measurement methods and performance criteria for voice transmission over networks.
[IT.Q] Series Q - Signaling -- Series Q covers signalling protocols and procedures used in telecommunication networks, defining formats, interworking, and performance requirements for call setup, routing and management. [IT.V] Series V - Data Communication (Telephone) -- Series V of the ITU-T recommendations defines standards for data communication over the public telephone network, including specifications for modems, terminal equipment, and signaling methods that enable reliable digital transmission across analog lines, and provides guidance on performance, testing and interworking of data services.
[IT.X] Series X - Data Networks/Security -- Series X of the ITU-T standards defines specifications for data-network architectures, protocols and services, focusing on secure transmission, network management and protection mechanisms. It provides guidelines for LAN, MAN and WAN technologies, addressing authentication, encryption, integrity and resilience to threats. The series supports interoperability and the development of robust, secure data communication infrastructures worldwide.
[IT.Y] Series Y - Global Info Infrastructure/IoT -- Series Y (IT.Y) is an ITU-T reference group that compiles standards on the global information infrastructure and the Internet of Things. It defines architecture, protocols, security and interoperability guidelines to support worldwide IoT deployment and integration with existing networks.
[IT.Z] Series Z - Languages and Software -- Series Z in the ITU-T standards catalog groups recommendations that specify programming languages, software development tools, and related interfaces for telecommunications equipment, providing common frameworks for implementing and testing network functions. It covers syntax definitions, software architecture guidelines, and interoperability specifications that aid vendors and operators in creating compatible digital services.
