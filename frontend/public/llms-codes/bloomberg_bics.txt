[BICS] BICS Sectors -- The Bloomberg Industry Classification System (BICS) organizes companies into a hierarchy of sectors, industry groups and sub-industries based on their primary business activities. The BICS Sectors level is the top tier, grouping firms into broad categories such as Energy, Financials, Healthcare and Technology to enable consistent market analysis and benchmarking. It is used by investors and data providers for portfolio construction and performance attribution.
[BICS.01] Communications -- BICS.01 Communications includes firms that deliver voice, data, video and related services such as telecom carriers, satellite operators, cable and broadband providers, and network-infrastructure companies. The sector also covers manufacturers and service providers of equipment and technology that enable wired and wireless information transmission.
[BICS.02] Consumer Discretionary -- BICS 02 designates the Consumer Discretionary sector, covering firms that provide non-essential goods and services whose demand varies with consumer confidence and income. It includes industries such as retail, automobiles, leisure, media, and consumer durables, reflecting spending on items that are not basic necessities.
[BICS.03] Consumer Staples -- BICS.03 identifies the Consumer Staples sector in the Bloomberg Industry Classification System. It groups firms that produce or sell essential items such as food, beverages, household goods and personal care products, which generally enjoy steady demand regardless of economic cycles.
[BICS.04] Energy -- BICS 04 groups companies that generate, transmit, or supply energy, covering activities such as exploration, extraction, refining, and distribution of oil, natural gas, coal, and renewable fuels, as well as electricity generation and related equipment and services. It serves as the broad sector identifier for firms whose primary business is the production and delivery of energy resources.
[BICS.05] Financials -- BICS 05, the Financials sector, groups companies that provide financial services such as banking, insurance, asset management, brokerage, and real-estate investment. It encompasses firms that generate revenue from lending, underwriting, investment activities, and the management of capital and risk for individuals and institutions. This sector is a core component of the Bloomberg Industry Classification System for identifying and analyzing financial-industry participants.
[BICS.06] Health Care -- BICS.06 designates the Health Care sector, encompassing firms that develop, produce, or distribute medical products and services. It includes pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, medical devices, health-care providers, and related support industries. This classification groups companies shaping the delivery and advancement of health care worldwide.
[BICS.07] Industrials -- BICS.07 - Industrials groups companies that produce capital goods, provide commercial services and operate transportation and logistics networks. It includes manufacturers of machinery, aerospace and defense equipment, construction firms, and firms offering freight, shipping, and infrastructure services, reflecting the sector's role in supporting economic activity through tangible assets and essential services.
[BICS.08] Materials -- BICS 08 is the Materials sector, covering companies that extract, produce or process raw inputs such as metals, minerals, chemicals, paper and building products. These firms supply the basic materials needed for industrial manufacturing, construction and other downstream industries. The sector groups businesses involved in the discovery, mining, refining and conversion of natural resources into usable commodities.
[BICS.09] Technology -- BICS.09 denotes the Technology sector in Bloomberg's Industry Classification System. It groups companies that develop, manufacture, or provide hardware, software, and related services, covering areas such as computers, telecommunications equipment, semiconductors, and internet services. The category is used to benchmark performance and analyze market trends across the tech industry.
[BICS.10] Utilities -- BICS.10 marks the Utilities sector, which includes companies that generate, transmit, and distribute essential services such as electricity, natural gas, water, and renewable energy. These firms are typically regulated and provide critical infrastructure for households and businesses. The classification groups together traditional power utilities, gas distributors, and emerging clean-energy providers.
[BICS.11] Government -- BICS.11 groups entities that operate as sovereign or sub-national governments, including ministries, agencies and publicly funded bodies responsible for legislation, public administration, defense, taxation, and the provision of social services. It excludes state-owned enterprises that are classified separately under corporate sectors. This category captures the economic activity of the public sector at national, regional and local levels.
[BICS.12] Diversified -- BICS.12 - Diversified groups companies whose primary activities span several unrelated industries rather than concentrating on a single sector. These firms typically operate as conglomerates, holding businesses in areas such as manufacturing, services, finance, and technology, and they generate revenue from a broad mix of products and markets. This classification helps investors identify firms with diversified exposure and multi-industry risk profiles.
