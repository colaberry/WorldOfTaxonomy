[asc_230] ASC 230 - Statement of Cash Flows -- ASC 230 establishes the framework for preparing and presenting a statement of cash flows, requiring cash receipts and payments to be classified into operating, investing, and financing activities and permitting either the direct or indirect method, while specifying required disclosures and the definition of cash equivalents.
[asc_260] ASC 260 - Earnings Per Share -- The ASC 260 section of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification sets out the principles for calculating and presenting earnings per share under US GAAP. It defines basic and diluted EPS, specifies how to adjust the share count for conversions, options, and other potentially dilutive securities, and outlines disclosure requirements for reporting EPS in financial statements.
[asc_310] ASC 310 - Receivables -- ASC 310 outlines the accounting for receivables, including classification, measurement, impairment, and presentation. It provides guidance on trade, notes, and other receivables, covering initial recognition, subsequent measurement, and disclosure requirements.
[asc_320] ASC 320 - Investments - Debt Securities -- ASC 320, part of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification, provides guidance on the accounting and reporting of debt securities. It outlines classification categories-held-to-maturity, trading, and available-for-sale-detailing measurement, amortization, impairment, and the presentation of interest income and unrealized gains or losses.
[asc_330] ASC 330 - Inventory -- ASC 330 sets out the accounting and reporting requirements for inventory, including how to measure inventory cost, the permissible cost-flow assumptions such as FIFO, LIFO, weighted-average and specific identification, and the requirement to value inventory at the lower of cost or market. It also prescribes the disclosures needed for inventory balances and related accounting policies.
[asc_340] ASC 340 - Other Assets and Deferred Costs -- ASC 340 - Other Assets and Deferred Costs sets out the U.S. GAAP guidance for accounting for assets that are not covered by other ASC topics, such as prepaid expenses, deferred charges, and certain intangible assets. It details how to recognize, measure, amortize, and evaluate impairment of these items, and clarifies presentation and disclosure requirements for financial statement users.
[asc_350] ASC 350 - Intangibles - Goodwill and Other -- ASC 350 provides guidance on accounting for goodwill and other intangible assets, describing how to initially recognize, measure, and subsequently evaluate them for impairment rather than systematic amortization. The standard requires entities to test goodwill for impairment at least annually using a two-step valuation approach, while other intangibles are generally amortized over their useful lives unless they are indefinite-life assets, which are also subject to annual impairment testing.
[asc_360] ASC 360 - Property, Plant, and Equipment -- ASC 360 provides guidance on accounting for property, plant and equipment, covering initial recognition, measurement, depreciation and impairment of tangible long-lived assets. It establishes criteria for capitalizing costs, selecting depreciation methods, and testing assets for recoverability when indications of decline arise.
[asc_410] ASC 410 - Asset Retirement and Environmental Obligations -- ASC 410 provides guidance on accounting for costs associated with retiring long-lived assets and for environmental obligations such as site remediation, de-commissioning, and dismantlement. It requires entities to recognize a liability when an obligation is incurred, measure it at the present value of expected cash outflows, and disclose the nature, timing and amount of related expenses and commitments.
[asc_420] ASC 420 - Exit or Disposal Cost Obligations -- ASC 420, "Exit or Disposal Cost Obligations," sets the accounting framework for costs a company incurs when it terminates a line of business, facility or product. It requires entities to recognize termination benefits, asset impairments and related expenses in the period the exit is announced, and to disclose the nature, timing and amount of those obligations.

[asc_480] ASC 480 - Distinguishing Liabilities from Equity -- ASC 480 provides the framework for determining whether an instrument issued by an entity should be presented as a liability or as equity. It outlines criteria based on the entity's obligation to deliver cash, other assets, or services, covering items such as redeemable preferred stock, fixed-rate preferred shares, and instruments with contingent settlement features, ensuring consistent classification across financial statements.
[asc_505] ASC 505 - Equity -- ASC 505, part of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification, provides guidance on accounting for equity instruments, including the classification, measurement, and presentation of equity securities and related transactions. It addresses issues such as issuance, repurchase, conversion, and dividend reporting, and requires specific disclosures for equity activities.
[asc_606] ASC 606 - Revenue from Contracts with Customers -- ASC 606 establishes a five-step model for recognizing revenue from contracts with customers, detailing how to identify performance obligations, determine transaction prices, allocate those prices, and recognize revenue as obligations are satisfied. It supersedes previous industry-specific guidance, promoting consistent and comparable financial reporting across entities and sectors.
[asc_718] ASC 718 - Compensation - Stock Compensation -- ASC 718 provides guidance on accounting for share-based compensation, outlining how companies must recognize, measure and disclose equity- and cash-settled stock awards. It prescribes expense recognition based on fair value at grant, vesting conditions, and the impact on earnings and equity, ensuring consistent reporting of employee compensation involving stock instruments.
[asc_740] ASC 740 - Income Taxes -- ASC 740, part of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification, provides guidance on accounting for income taxes under US Generally Accepted Accounting Principles. It addresses the recognition, measurement, and disclosure of current and deferred tax assets and liabilities, and outlines the required documentation for tax positions and uncertain tax treatments.
[asc_805] ASC 805 - Business Combinations -- ASC 805, part of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification, sets forth the accounting and reporting requirements for business combinations, covering acquisition method procedures, purchase price allocation, goodwill measurement, and related disclosures. It guides entities in recognizing and measuring assets acquired, liabilities assumed, and any non-controlling interests at the acquisition date.
[asc_810] ASC 810 - Consolidation -- ASC 810 outlines the criteria and methods for consolidating entities under US Generally Accepted Accounting Principles, specifying when a parent must combine the financial statements of subsidiaries into a single set of reports. It defines control, variable interest entities, and exceptions such as carve-outs and joint ventures, guiding how assets, liabilities, equity, revenues, and expenses are presented in consolidated financial statements.
[asc_815] ASC 815 - Derivatives and Hedging -- ASC 815 provides the framework for accounting for derivative instruments and hedging activities, outlining how to identify, measure at fair value, and disclose derivatives, as well as the criteria for applying hedge accounting to offset gains and losses against related items. It sets standards for recognizing changes in fair value in earnings or other comprehensive income, ensuring consistent reporting of risk management strategies.
[asc_820] ASC 820 - Fair Value Measurement -- ASC 820 provides the definition of fair value, a measurement framework, and a three-level hierarchy for inputs used to determine fair value in financial reporting. It outlines the valuation techniques appropriate for different assets and liabilities and requires extensive disclosures about fair-value measurements, methods, and assumptions.
[asc_842] ASC 842 - Leases -- ASC 842 is the FASB Accounting Standards Codification topic that establishes the lease accounting framework for U.S. GAAP. It requires lessees to record a right-of-use asset and a corresponding lease liability for most contracts, while providing guidance on classification, measurement, and disclosure for both lessees and lessors, replacing the earlier ASC 840 standard.
[asc_860] ASC 860 - Transfers and Servicing -- ASC 860, part of the FASB Accounting Standards Codification, sets the accounting guidance for the transfer of financial assets and for servicing those assets.
