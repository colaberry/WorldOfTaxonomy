[BM] BMI Categories -- The WHO BMI classification BM defines categories based on body mass index ranges: underweight (<18.5), normal weight (18.5-24.9), overweight (25-29.9), and obesity (≥30) with further classes I-III.
[BM.01] Severe Thinness (< 16.0) -- The WHO Body Mass Index classification BM.01 denotes severe thinness, defined as a BMI value below 16.0, indicating a markedly underweight condition associated with heightened health risks such as malnutrition, weakened immunity, and increased mortality.
[BM.02] Moderate Thinness (16.0-16.9) -- Moderate thinness is a WHO body-mass-index category indicating a BMI between 16.0 and 16.9, signifying a level of underweight that may increase health risks and often warrants nutritional assessment and monitoring.
[BM.03] Mild Thinness (17.0-18.4) -- Mild thinness is the WHO BMI category for adults whose body mass index falls between 17.0 and 18.4. It reflects a modest degree of undernutrition and may be associated with a slightly elevated risk of health issues such as weakened immunity or reduced bone density. Monitoring and nutritional counseling are often recommended to prevent progression to more severe thinness.
[BM.04] Normal Range (18.5-24.9) -- The WHO BMI classification BM.04 defines the normal weight range as a body mass index of 18.5-24.9, indicating a weight considered healthy for most adults. Values below 18.5 are underweight and those above 24.9 are overweight.
[BM.05] Pre-Obese (25.0-29.9) -- The WHO designates a BMI of 25.0 to 29.9 as the pre-obese range, indicating excess weight that elevates health risk but does not meet the criteria for obesity. Individuals in this category may experience higher chances of hypertension, dyslipidemia, and type 2 diabetes, prompting lifestyle modifications to prevent progression.
[BM.06] Obese Class I (30.0-34.9) -- Obese Class I designates individuals with a body-mass index between 30.0 and 34.9, indicating moderate obesity and a heightened risk of cardiovascular disease, type-2 diabetes, and related health problems. This category is part of the WHO BMI classification used for population health monitoring and clinical assessment.
[BM.07] Obese Class II (35.0-39.9) -- In the WHO Body Mass Index system, code BM.07 denotes Obese Class II, defined by a BMI of 35.0 to 39.9. Individuals in this range face a substantially increased risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and other obesity-related complications.
[BM.08] Obese Class III (40.0+) -- Obese Class III, coded BM.08, refers to a body-mass index of 40.0 or higher. This level indicates severe or morbid obesity, markedly raising the risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, and other serious health conditions.
[BM.09] Asian BMI Cutoffs -- The WHO Body Mass Index Classification entry BM.09 defines lower BMI thresholds for Asian populations, reflecting higher health risks at modest weight levels. It sets normal weight at 18.5-22.9 kg/m², overweight at risk from 23.0-27.4 kg/m², obesity I from 27.5-32.4 kg/m², and obesity II at 32.5 kg/m² and above. These cutoffs differ from the global standards.
[BM.10] Pediatric BMI-for-Age -- BM.10 denotes the WHO pediatric BMI-for-age reference, which assesses a child's body mass index in relation to age and sex for those aged 5 to 19 years. Using WHO growth-standard Z-score charts, it classifies underweight, normal weight, overweight and obesity based on how many standard deviations a child's BMI deviates from the median.
