[P2] PRINCE2 Categories -- P2 refers to the overarching group of PRINCE2 categories, covering the seven processes, seven themes, and seven principles that together form the project management method. It provides a structural framework for organizing and applying the methodology's core components throughout a project's lifecycle.
[P2.01] Starting Up a Project (SU) -- Starting Up a Project (SU) is the first PRINCE2 process that checks the project's viability, appoints the project manager and key roles, and creates the project brief and outline business case. It ensures that the essential information, authority and resources are in place before the project formally initiates.
[P2.02] Directing a Project (DP) -- Directing a Project is a PRINCE2 process that gives the Project Board authority to approve, monitor, and control a project from initiation through closure. It defines key decision points for authorising initiation, stage boundaries, and project closure, ensuring alignment with business objectives.
[P2.03] Initiating a Project (IP) -- PRINCE2's Initiating a Project (IP) process establishes the project's foundations, defining scope, objectives, and governance structures while assembling the Project Initiation Documentation. It aligns stakeholder expectations, assesses risks, and sets baseline plans for time, cost, and quality, ensuring the project has a clear, approved mandate before execution begins.
[P2.04] Controlling a Stage (CS) -- In PRINCE2, the Controlling a Stage process manages day-to-day activities within a stage, monitoring progress against the stage plan, handling issues and risks, and reporting status to the Project Board. It ensures tolerances are respected and corrective actions are taken to keep the stage on track.
[P2.05] Managing Product Delivery (MP) -- Managing Product Delivery (MP) is a PRINCE2 process that coordinates the creation and provision of products according to the project plan, ensuring they meet quality expectations and are delivered on time. It links the Project Manager with team members and suppliers, monitors work packages, and updates progress information for further decision-making. This process falls under the broader category of PRINCE2 process and theme classifications.
[P2.06] Managing a Stage Boundary (SB) -- Managing a Stage Boundary is a PRINCE2 process that occurs at the end of each stage, reviewing progress, updating the project plan, and deciding whether to continue. It involves assessing stage performance, confirming completed products, and preparing the next stage plan and risk log.
[P2.07] Closing a Project (CP) -- P2.07 identifies the PRINCE2 Closing a Project (CP) process, which formalises the finalisation of a project by confirming that all products have been delivered, obtaining acceptance, reviewing performance, recording lessons learned and handing over assets to the client or operations team.
[P2.08] Business Case Theme -- The Business Case Theme provides the justification for a project, defining costs, benefits and risks and assessing viability throughout the project's lifecycle. It ensures that decisions are based on a documented, regularly reviewed case that aligns with organisational strategy.
[P2.09] Organization Theme -- The Organization theme in PRINCE2 defines the project's governance structure, outlining the roles, responsibilities and lines of communication among the project board, project manager, team members and corporate or programme stakeholders. It ensures that accountability is clearly assigned and that the organizational hierarchy supports effective decision-making and resource allocation throughout the project lifecycle.
[P2.10] Quality Theme -- The Quality Theme in PRINCE2 sets out how the project's products will meet agreed quality requirements, detailing the processes for quality planning, quality control, quality assurance and quality management. It ensures that quality expectations are defined, agreed and continually reviewed throughout the project lifecycle.
[P2.11] Plans Theme -- The Plans theme provides the mechanisms for defining, approving, and maintaining the project's plans, ensuring that products and activities are clearly outlined and controlled throughout the project lifecycle. [P2.12] Risk Theme -- The Risk Theme in PRINCE2, identified by code P2.12, is one of the seven core themes that embed risk management throughout a project. It defines how risks are identified, assessed, and controlled, requiring a risk register, regular risk assessments, and clear responsibilities to ensure that potential threats and opportunities are continuously monitored and addressed.
[P2.13] Change Theme -- The Change Theme in PRINCE2 (code P2.13) governs how project deviations are identified, assessed, and controlled, ensuring that any alterations to scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, or benefits are formally recorded, evaluated, and approved or rejected. It provides mechanisms to capture change requests, analyse impacts, and maintain an up-to-date baseline throughout the project lifecycle.
[P2.14] Progress Theme -- The Progress Theme in PRINCE2 establishes the processes for monitoring and controlling a project's performance against its plan, providing the Project Board with current information on status, forecasts and issues so that informed decisions can be made and tolerances maintained.
