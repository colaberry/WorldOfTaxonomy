[I3] ISIC Rev 3 Sections -- The entry I3 identifies the collection of all ISIC Rev 3.1 sections, which are the top-level categories (A-U) used to group economic activities in the International Standard Industrial Classification. It serves as a reference point for the full set of section codes that structure the classification hierarchy.
[I3.A] Agriculture, Hunting, Forestry -- The entry covers the cultivation of crops, raising of livestock, hunting of wild animals, and management of forest resources, including timber production, silviculture, and related support services such as seed supply, animal breeding, and forest conservation.
[I3.B] Fishing -- Fishing comprises the capture of fish, crustaceans, molluscs and other aquatic organisms from marine, coastal and inland water bodies. It includes activities such as commercial and artisanal harvesting, as well as related support services that prepare the catch for transport or sale, but excludes aquaculture production. This sector supplies raw seafood to processing and distribution chains.
[I3.C] Mining and Quarrying -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 code I3.C designates establishments that extract minerals and other geological materials from the earth, covering activities such as coal and metal ore mining, stone, sand and gravel quarrying, and associated support services.
[I3.D] Manufacturing -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 entry I3.D "Manufacturing" groups all economic activities that transform raw materials, components or parts into finished products through physical, chemical or mechanical processes, including processing, assembling and packaging. It covers a broad range of industries such as food, textiles, chemicals, machinery and electronics, but excludes extraction, construction, utilities and services.
[I3.E] Electricity, Gas, Water Supply -- The I3.E class comprises activities that generate, transmit and distribute electricity, produce and distribute gaseous fuels such as natural gas and coal-derived gases, and collect, treat, store and supply water for residential, industrial and commercial use. It includes the operation of power plants, gas pipelines, water treatment facilities and related distribution networks.

[I3.H] Hotels and Restaurants -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 code I3.H covers establishments primarily engaged in providing short-term accommodation and meals to the public. This includes hotels, motels, guest houses and similar lodging facilities, as well as restaurants, cafes, bars and other food-service outlets that serve prepared food and beverages on the premises.
[I3.I] Transport, Storage, Communications -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 entry I3.I groups together activities that provide the movement of passengers and freight, the holding of goods in warehouses or other facilities, and the transmission of information through postal, telecommunications and related services. This includes road, rail, air and water transport, storage, and all core communication operations.
[I3.J] Financial Intermediation -- Financial intermediation covers institutions that accept deposits, provide loans and other credit facilities, and facilitate the allocation of capital between savers and borrowers. It includes banking, credit unions, savings banks, and other entities that manage payment systems, issue and trade securities, and offer related financial services.
[I3.K] Real Estate, Renting, Business -- The I3.K category covers activities involving the acquisition, ownership, rental, leasing and management of real estate, including residential, commercial and industrial properties. It also includes real-estate agencies, brokerage services and other business functions related to buying, selling and letting property.
[I3.L] Public Administration, Defence -- The I3.L category covers activities of central, regional and local government entities that formulate and implement public policies, provide general services, and maintain public order, as well as the organization and administration of national defence and the armed forces.
[I3.M] Education -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 code I3.M covers establishments providing education at all levels, from pre-primary and primary schooling through secondary, vocational, tertiary and adult learning programs. It includes public and private institutions that deliver instruction, training, and related support services, as well as entities that issue recognized qualifications.
[I3.N] Health and Social Work -- The ISIC Rev 3.1 entry I3.N, titled "Health and Social Work," covers establishments that provide medical, dental and hospital services as well as residential and non-residential care, rehabilitation and social assistance. It includes hospitals, outpatient clinics, nursing homes, child-care facilities, and organizations delivering welfare and support services to individuals and families. This sector groups together all activities aimed at preserving health, treating illness, and providing social support.
[I3.O] Other Community, Social, Personal -- The I3.O "Other Community, Social, Personal" class groups activities that provide community-based services, social assistance, and personal support not captured elsewhere, including voluntary organisations, charitable work, advocacy groups and informal personal care or counseling services. It covers any non-market-driven social or personal assistance activities that fall outside the more specific categories.
[I3.P] Private Households with Employed Persons -- Private households that hire workers for domestic tasks, such as cleaning, cooking, child-care, gardening, or personal assistance, are classified under this code. It includes families or individuals who employ staff on a regular or occasional basis, whether full-time, part-time or casual, to support household operations.
[I3.Q] Extra-Territorial Organizations -- Extra-territorial organizations are entities that operate across national boundaries and are not confined to a single country's jurisdiction, such as intergovernmental bodies, multinational NGOs, and transnational alliances that coordinate activities, set standards, or provide services on a global or regional scale. Their activities span multiple territories, often involving policy development, humanitarian aid, or international cooperation.
