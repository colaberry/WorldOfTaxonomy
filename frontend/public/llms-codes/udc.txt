[UD] UDC Main Tables -- UD denotes the UDC Main Tables, which present the fundamental schedule of the Universal Decimal Classification. It contains the ten main classes and the auxiliary tables that structure the notation for all subjects, serving as the core reference for organizing library collections.
[UD.0] Science and Knowledge -- UD.0 is the top-level class in the Universal Decimal Classification that groups all scientific and scholarly fields. It denotes the systematic body of knowledge, covering natural, formal, applied and social sciences, and serves as the foundation for more specific subclassifications.
[UD.1] Philosophy, Psychology -- UD.1 is the top-level class in the Universal Decimal Classification that groups works on philosophy and psychology. It includes fundamental concepts, theories and methods such as metaphysics, ethics, epistemology, cognition, behavior and related interdisciplinary studies.

[UD.5] Natural Sciences, Mathematics -- UD.5 is the primary UDC class for the natural sciences and mathematics, encompassing fields such as physics, chemistry, biology and all branches of mathematics. It serves as a broad entry point from which more specific scientific and mathematical subjects are subdivided in the classification system.
[UD.6] Applied Sciences, Technology -- UD.6 designates the field of applied sciences and technology, encompassing practical disciplines such as engineering, industrial processes, and emerging technological innovations. It groups works that translate scientific knowledge into usable solutions and products.
[UD.7] Arts, Recreation, Sport -- UD.7 in the UDC skeleton groups subjects related to creative, leisure and physical activity. It includes the visual and performing arts, hobbies, tourism, games and organized sports, as well as related institutions and cultural events.
[UD.8] Language, Linguistics, Literature -- UD.8 is the UDC class for works on language, its scientific study, and literary expression. It covers grammar, phonetics, semantics, comparative linguistics, as well as literary theory, criticism, and the history of literature.
[UD.9] Geography, History -- UD.9 in the Universal Decimal Classification groups materials that treat the physical and human dimensions of the Earth together with the study of past events. It includes general works on geography, atlases, maps, and broad-range histories that combine spatial description with chronological narrative. This class provides a unified entry point for sources that integrate place and time in their analysis.
