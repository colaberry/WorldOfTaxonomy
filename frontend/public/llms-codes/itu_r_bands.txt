[RB] ITU-R Bands -- RB is the ITU-R designation for a set of radio-frequency bands defined in the International Telecommunication Union's recommendations. The code groups contiguous frequency ranges-such as VHF, UHF, L-band and others-used worldwide for broadcasting, mobile, satellite and other radiocommunication services, providing a standardized reference for spectrum allocation and coordination.
[RB.01] VLF (3-30 kHz) -- RB.01 designates the Very Low Frequency band covering 3 to 30 kHz. It is employed for submarine communication, navigation beacons, time-signal transmitters and other applications that benefit from its long wavelengths and ability to penetrate water and earth.
[RB.02] LF (30-300 kHz) -- RB.02 designates the low-frequency band ranging from 30 to 300 kHz, used mainly for navigation signals, time-keeping broadcasts, and some maritime and aeronautical communications. The band's long-range ground-wave propagation makes it suitable for services that require reliable coverage over large areas.
[RB.03] MF (300 kHz - 3 MHz) -- The MF (medium frequency) band spans 300 kHz to 3 MHz and is chiefly used for standard AM broadcast services, maritime distress and safety communications, and navigation aids such as non-directional beacons. It sits between the LF and HF bands in the ITU spectrum allocation.
[RB.04] HF (3-30 MHz) -- RB.04 designates the high-frequency band from 3 to 30 MHz, a range used for long-range ionospheric propagation. It supports services such as amateur radio, short-wave broadcasting, maritime and aeronautical communications.
[RB.05] VHF (30-300 MHz) -- RB.05 identifies the VHF band, which spans frequencies from 30 MHz to 300 MHz. This range is used globally for FM broadcasting, land mobile communications, air-traffic control, maritime services and other radiocommunication applications as allocated by the ITU.
[RB.06] UHF (300 MHz - 3 GHz) -- RB.06 designates the ultra-high-frequency band covering 300 MHz to 3 GHz, a range employed for terrestrial broadcasting, mobile communications, radar, satellite links and various other radio services.
[RB.07] SHF (3-30 GHz) -- The ITU designates the SHF band, covering frequencies from 3 GHz to 30 GHz, as a key portion of the microwave spectrum. It supports applications such as radar, satellite communications, point-to-point links and certain radar and navigation services, benefiting from moderate atmospheric attenuation and the ability to use relatively small antennas.

[RB.09] Sub-6 GHz (5G NR FR1) -- The RB.09 entry designates the sub-6 GHz spectrum allocated for 5G New Radio Frequency Range 1, spanning roughly 410 MHz to 7.125 GHz. It supports mobile broadband, IoT and fixed wireless services and includes the commonly used 5G bands such as n1, n3, n5, n7, n28 and n78 under ITU's global allocation framework.
[RB.10] mmWave (5G NR FR2) -- RB.10 designates the millimetre-wave spectrum allocated for 5G New Radio Frequency Range 2, covering roughly 24.25 GHz to 71 GHz. It is used for high-capacity, low-latency mobile services such as enhanced mobile broadband and ultra-reliable low-latency communications. The band supports wide bandwidths and advanced antenna techniques like beamforming to overcome propagation challenges at these frequencies.
[RB.11] Ka-Band (Satellite) -- The Ka-Band for satellite use, designated RB.11 by the ITU, spans roughly 26.5 to 40 GHz and is allocated for high-capacity space-based communications such as broadband Internet, telemetry and Earth-observation links. Its wide bandwidth enables high data-rate services but requires careful handling of atmospheric attenuation, especially rain fade.
[RB.12] Ku-Band (Satellite) -- RB.12 designates the Ku-band, a microwave segment from 12 GHz to 18 GHz allocated by the ITU for satellite communications. The band supports space-to-Earth and Earth-to-space links such as broadcasting, broadband internet, and data transmission, with specific sub-ranges assigned to various services and regions.
[RB.13] C-Band (Satellite/5G) -- RB.13 designates the C-Band, a frequency range of roughly 3.4 to 4.2 GHz for satellite downlink and 5.9 to 6.4 GHz for uplink, which is also allocated for 5G mobile broadband in many regions.
[RB.14] L-Band (Navigation) -- RB.14 designates the L-Band frequencies allocated for navigation and radiolocation services, spanning roughly 1 GHz to 2 GHz. It includes the 960-1215 MHz range used by terrestrial radionavigation aids and the 1575-1600 MHz bands employed by satellite navigation systems such as GPS, GLONASS and Galileo, supporting aircraft surveillance, maritime positioning and related navigation applications.
[RB.15] S-Band (Weather Radar) -- RB.15 designates the S-Band used for weather radar, covering frequencies from 2.0 to 4.0 GHz, which provides moderate range and resolution for meteorological observations and is allocated for dedicated meteorological radar services worldwide.
