[de] DE - Detect -- The Detect function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 guides organizations in identifying cybersecurity events promptly, emphasizing continuous monitoring, detection processes, and analysis of anomalies. It supports timely awareness of threats to enable effective response and containment.
[de_ae] DE.AE - Adverse Event Analysis -- The DE.AE function guides organizations to examine cybersecurity adverse events, identifying root causes, impacts, and contributing factors. It ensures that lessons learned are documented, shared, and integrated into risk management and response processes to strengthen future prevention and detection capabilities.
[de_cm] DE.CM - Continuous Monitoring -- DE.CM addresses the ongoing collection, analysis, and response to security-relevant information across the organization, ensuring that assets, vulnerabilities, and threats are continuously identified and assessed. It establishes processes for real-time situational awareness, enabling timely detection of changes and rapid remediation to maintain an up-to-date security posture.
[gv] GV - Govern -- GV - Govern defines the organization's top-level policies, processes, and governance structures that guide cybersecurity risk management. By aligning cybersecurity activities with this broader context, it provides a foundation for tailored risk management, strategic decision-making, and effective protection of critical assets.

[gv_rr] GV.RR - Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities -- GV.RR outlines the need for an organization to clearly assign, document, and communicate cybersecurity roles, responsibilities, and authorities, ensuring that each function is understood and aligned with risk management objectives. [gv_sc] GV.SC - Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management -- GV.SC defines the management of cybersecurity risks associated with an organization's supply chain, requiring the establishment of policies, processes and controls to identify, assess and mitigate threats from suppliers, service providers and third-party products throughout their lifecycle. It emphasizes continuous monitoring, verification of security practices, and contractual requirements to ensure that supply-chain components do not weaken the organization's overall cyber resilience.
[id] ID - Identify -- The ID (Identify) function of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 defines the organizational understanding needed to manage cybersecurity risk to systems, assets, data, and capabilities. It covers asset management, business environment, governance, risk assessment, risk management strategy, and supply chain risk management, forming the foundation for the framework's other core functions.
[id_am] ID.AM - Asset Management -- ID.AM refers to the Asset Management function, which requires organizations to identify, inventory, and maintain accurate records of physical devices, software, data, and supporting infrastructure across the enterprise. It emphasizes establishing a comprehensive, up-to-date asset database to enable effective risk assessment, prioritization, and protection of critical assets.
[id_im] ID.IM - Improvement -- ID.IM - Improvement is a subcategory of the Identify function that ensures an organization continuously enhances its cybersecurity posture by incorporating lessons learned, performance metrics, and updates to policies, processes, and resources. It guides systematic review and refinement of risk management activities to keep defenses aligned with evolving threats and business objectives.
[id_ra] ID.RA - Risk Assessment -- ID.RA - Risk Assessment focuses on identifying and evaluating threats, vulnerabilities, and potential impacts to the organization's systems and data, then prioritizing those risks based on likelihood and severity. It informs the development of risk profiles and supports decisions about how to manage, mitigate, or accept identified risks.
[pr] PR - Protect -- The Protect function in the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 outlines safeguards to limit or contain the impact of a cybersecurity event. It encompasses access control, data security, awareness training, protective technologies, and the implementation of policies and procedures that maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information systems.
[pr_aa] PR.AA - Identity Management, Authentication, and Access Control -- PR.AA focuses on establishing and maintaining robust identity management, authentication, and access-control processes. It ensures that only authorized individuals and devices can access resources, that credentials are securely provisioned and managed, and that privileges are regularly reviewed and adjusted to match organizational policies and risk tolerance.
[pr_at] PR.AT - Awareness and Training -- The PR.AT subcategory of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework focuses on establishing and maintaining a cybersecurity awareness and training program for all personnel. It ensures that individuals understand the organization's security policies, recognize threats, and possess the skills needed to respond effectively, thereby reducing human-related risk.
[pr_ds] PR.DS - Data Security -- PR.DS-Data Security in the NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 addresses the protection of information throughout its lifecycle. It includes safeguards such as encryption, access controls, integrity checks, backup and recovery processes, and policies that ensure data confidentiality, integrity, and availability across all storage and transmission mediums.
[pr_ir] PR.IR - Technology Infrastructure Resilience -- PR.IR focuses on building and maintaining technology systems that can withstand, adapt to, and recover from disruptions, ensuring critical functions remain available. It emphasizes redundancy, fault-tolerance, continuous monitoring, and rapid restoration procedures to sustain operational resilience across the infrastructure.
[pr_ps] PR.PS - Platform Security -- PR.PS - Platform Security focuses on safeguarding the underlying hardware, operating systems, firmware and supporting software that host critical functions. It calls for implementing controls that protect against unauthorized changes, ensure integrity of system components, and maintain a trusted computing environment throughout the lifecycle. This helps prevent exploitation of platform vulnerabilities that could compromise overall cybersecurity posture.
[rc] RC - Recover -- The RC (Recover) function guides organizations in restoring services and capabilities after a cybersecurity event. It emphasizes recovery planning, improvements based on lessons learned, and coordination of communication to return to normal operations efficiently.
[rc_co] RC.CO - Incident Recovery Communication -- The RC.CO function guides organizations in communicating during the recovery phase of a cyber incident, ensuring stakeholders receive clear, timely information about status, actions taken, and next steps. It coordinates messages across internal teams, external partners, customers and regulators to maintain confidence and support the restoration of normal operations.
[rc_rp] RC.RP - Incident Recovery Plan Execution -- RC.RP describes the organization's procedures for executing its incident recovery plan, coordinating resources and actions to restore affected systems to normal operation after a cybersecurity event. It ensures that recovery activities are documented, roles are defined, and validation steps are performed to confirm restored functionality before resuming business processes.
