It also specifies the documentation, audit and reporting obligations required of participating states and exporters.

[3_1] Import requirements -- The Kimberley Process Certification Scheme's import requirements mandate that every rough-diamond shipment entering a participant country be accompanied by a valid KP certificate confirming the stones are conflict-free. Importing parties must verify the certificate's authenticity, retain records and report any discrepancies to the national authority.
[3_2] Export requirements -- The Kimberley Process Certification Scheme mandates that every shipment of rough diamonds intended for export be accompanied by a government-issued certificate confirming the diamonds are conflict-free and sourced from a participating country. Exporters must submit a validated KPCS declaration, ensure the goods are sealed and marked, and retain records for verification by customs and authorities. Non-compliance can result in shipment denial, fines, or suspension from the scheme.
[3_3] Shipment controls -- Shipment controls under the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme are mandatory procedures that track and verify the movement of rough diamonds between participating countries. Exporting parties must attach a valid Kimberley Process certificate, seal containers, maintain detailed records, and allow authorities to inspect shipments to ensure only conflict-free diamonds are traded.
[4_1] Internal controls for rough diamonds -- The Kimberley Process Certification Scheme entry 4_1 details the internal controls required for handling rough diamonds to prevent the flow of conflict stones. It mandates tracking, record-keeping and verification procedures throughout the supply chain to ensure compliance with the international certification standard.
[4_2] System of warranties for polished diamonds -- The system of warranties for polished diamonds establishes contractual guarantees that each stone conforms to the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme's standards, confirming it is conflict-free and traceable from mine to market. These warranties obligate sellers to provide documentation verifying the diamond's origin, compliance with ethical sourcing rules, and eligibility for certification, thereby supporting transparency and consumer confidence in the polished-diamond trade.
[4_3] Record keeping and auditing -- The Kimberley Process Certification Scheme requires participants to maintain detailed records of all rough-diamond transactions, including origin, shipment dates, and buyer information, and to retain these documents for at least five years. Regular internal and external audits verify compliance, ensuring the integrity of the certification chain and preventing conflict diamonds from entering legitimate markets.

It enables transparent assessment, identifies gaps, and supports corrective action to maintain the integrity of the diamond trade.

These definitions establish a common language that underpins the scheme's monitoring and compliance procedures.
[sec_2] Section II - Kimberley Process Certificate -- Section II of the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme defines the requirements for issuing a Kimberley Process Certificate, confirming that a shipment of rough diamonds meets the scheme's standards for conflict-free status. It details the documentation, eligibility criteria and procedural steps that exporters and certifying authorities must follow.
[sec_3] Section III - Undertakings by Participants -- Section III outlines the specific commitments participants must make under the Kimberley Process, requiring each party to implement internal controls that prevent conflict diamonds from entering the market, to provide transparent documentation for each shipment, and to cooperate in verification and reporting mechanisms that uphold the scheme's conflict-free standards.
[sec_4] Section IV - Internal Controls -- Section IV of the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme outlines the internal control system required of participants, detailing procedures for record-keeping, verification of shipments, audit trails, and compliance monitoring to ensure that diamonds are sourced, handled, and exported in accordance with the scheme's anti-conflict standards.
[sec_5] Section V - Cooperation and Transparency -- Section V of the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme sets out the cooperation and transparency obligations for participating countries, detailing how they must share information, submit regular reports, and work together to prevent conflict diamonds from entering the market. It establishes mechanisms for mutual assistance, verification, and the public disclosure of relevant data to ensure the system's integrity.
[sec_6] Section VI - Administrative Matters -- Section VI of the Kimberley Process Certification Scheme outlines the administrative framework for the programme, detailing responsibilities of the secretariat, procedures for meetings, reporting requirements, fee structures, and mechanisms for dispute resolution and amendment of the scheme.
