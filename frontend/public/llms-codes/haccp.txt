[HA] HACCP Principles -- HA designates the core HACCP Principles, a systematic framework for ensuring food safety by identifying potential hazards, establishing critical control points, setting critical limits, monitoring procedures, corrective actions, verification processes, and maintaining comprehensive records. This structured approach helps producers prevent, reduce, or eliminate risks throughout the food production chain.
[HA.01] Principle 1: Hazard Analysis -- Principle 1 of HACCP requires a systematic hazard analysis to identify any biological, chemical, or physical hazards that could be introduced at each step of food production. This analysis establishes the basis for determining critical control points and appropriate preventive measures to ensure food safety.
[HA.02] Principle 2: Critical Control Points (CCPs) -- Principle 2 identifies points in the production process where a loss of control could cause a food-safety hazard, and where measures can be applied to prevent, eliminate, or reduce the hazard to an acceptable level; these are designated as critical control points.
[HA.03] Principle 3: Critical Limits -- Principle 3 of HACCP establishes critical limits, the maximum or minimum values-such as temperature, time, pH, or moisture-that must be met to ensure a hazard is controlled. These limits are scientifically based thresholds that, when exceeded, indicate a loss of control and trigger corrective actions to protect food safety.
[HA.04] Principle 4: Monitoring Procedures -- Principle 4 requires the establishment of monitoring procedures to ensure each critical control point stays within its limits. Monitoring must be continuous or at defined intervals, using objective measurements and documented records, allowing immediate corrective action when deviations occur.
[HA.05] Principle 5: Corrective Actions -- Principle 5 of HACCP requires that any deviation from critical limits be addressed promptly through corrective actions. It specifies that the cause of the deviation be identified, the affected product evaluated, and actions taken to prevent recurrence, including documentation and verification.
[HA.06] Principle 6: Verification -- Principle 6: Verification involves confirming that the HACCP system is operating as intended by reviewing records, conducting internal audits, and testing finished products. This step ensures that control measures are effective, identifies any deviations, and prompts corrective actions to maintain food safety.
[HA.07] Principle 7: Record-Keeping -- Principle 7 of the HACCP system mandates comprehensive record-keeping to document all monitoring activities, verification procedures, and corrective actions taken at critical control points. These records must be accurate, legible, and retained for a period that allows traceability and regulatory review, ensuring the integrity of the food safety plan.
[HA.08] Preliminary Step: HACCP Team -- The HA.08 entry outlines the first phase of implementing HACCP, which is assembling a multidisciplinary HACCP team. It details the required expertise, roles, and responsibilities for team members to collaboratively develop and maintain a food safety system.
[HA.09] Preliminary Step: Product Description -- HA.09 outlines the first HACCP step, requiring a clear and detailed description of the product, including its intended use, composition, physical form, packaging, shelf life, and any allergens, to establish baseline parameters for hazard analysis.
[HA.10] Preliminary Step: Intended Use -- HA.10 outlines the first HACCP step of identifying the food's intended use, including the target consumer, normal preparation and storage conditions, and any special handling requirements. This information establishes the basis for subsequent hazard analysis and control measures.
[HA.11] Preliminary Step: Flow Diagram -- A flow diagram maps the sequence of steps a product follows from receiving raw materials through processing, packaging and distribution, identifying each point where hazards could arise. It is the first HACCP activity, establishing a clear visual overview that guides the subsequent hazard analysis and control planning.
[HA.12] Preliminary Step: On-Site Confirmation -- The preliminary step involves an on-site verification that the facility's environment, equipment, processes and documentation match the planned HACCP system. Inspectors confirm that prerequisite programs are in place, critical control points are correctly identified, and records accurately reflect current operations before proceeding to detailed hazard analysis.
