[4_1] 4.1 General requirements -- ISO 13485:2016 clause 4.1 sets the overall expectations for a medical-device quality management system, requiring manufacturers to establish, document, implement and maintain a QMS that covers the entire product lifecycle, satisfies applicable regulatory requirements and meets customer needs. It emphasizes management responsibility, the provision of resources, and the integration of risk-based processes to ensure consistent product safety and performance.
[4_2] 4.2 Documentation requirements -- ISO 13485:2016 requires that organizations establish, control, and maintain documented information necessary for an effective quality management system, including a quality manual, procedures, work instructions, and records. Documentation must define responsibilities, specify processes, and be reviewed and approved before issue, with changes controlled to ensure that current versions are available where needed. All required documents and records must be retained for a period stipulated by regulatory or contractual obligations.
[5_1] 5.1 Management commitment -- Clause 5.1 mandates that top management demonstrate commitment to the medical device quality management system by establishing a quality policy, setting measurable objectives, providing the necessary resources, and ensuring the system's effectiveness across the organization.
[5_2] 5.2 Customer focus -- ISO 13485:2016 clause 5.2 mandates that a medical-device organization continuously determines, meets and monitors customer and regulatory requirements, ensuring that product and service delivery align with those expectations. It requires documented processes for gathering feedback, addressing complaints and using the information to improve satisfaction and compliance.
[5_3] 5.3 Quality policy -- The quality policy states the organization's commitment to complying with regulatory requirements, ensuring the safety and performance of medical devices, and continuously improving the quality management system. It must be documented, communicated throughout the organization, and reviewed by top management to confirm its relevance and effectiveness.

[5_5] 5.5 Responsibility, authority and communication -- Clause 5.5 of ISO 13485:2016 mandates that a medical device organization establish and document the responsibilities, authorities, and communication processes for its quality management system, ensuring that personnel understand their roles and that relevant information is effectively shared throughout the organization.
[5_6] 5.6 Management review -- The 5.6 Management review clause requires top management to conduct periodic evaluations of the quality management system, assessing its suitability, effectiveness, and alignment with strategic objectives. Reviews must consider audit results, customer feedback, process performance, corrective actions and opportunities for improvement, and result in documented decisions and actions.
[6_1] 6.1 Provision of resources -- Clause 6.1 requires an organization to identify and supply all necessary resources for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving its quality management system, covering personnel competence, infrastructure, work environment, and financial support, and to ensure these resources remain adequate through ongoing review.
[6_2] 6.2 Human resources -- Clause 6.2 of ISO 13485:2016 mandates that medical device manufacturers establish and maintain a competent workforce, defining responsibilities, required qualifications and training for all personnel who influence product conformity. It requires ongoing education and awareness programs to ensure the quality management system operates effectively.
[6_3] 6.3 Infrastructure -- Clause 6.3 of ISO 13485:2016 requires organizations to determine, provide, and maintain the infrastructure needed to achieve conformity of medical devices, covering facilities, equipment, support services and related resources. The infrastructure must be suitable, reliable and adequately controlled to ensure product safety and regulatory compliance.
[6_4] 6.4 Work environment and contamination control -- Clause 6.4 requires organizations to define, implement and maintain a work environment that minimizes contamination risks to medical devices, including controls for personnel hygiene, cleaning procedures, environmental monitoring and protective measures, ensuring that product quality and safety are not compromised by adverse conditions.
[7_1] 7.1 Planning of product realization -- Clause 7.1 requires organizations to plan and develop the processes needed for product realization, defining quality objectives, required resources, responsibilities and risk controls to ensure medical devices meet regulatory and customer requirements.
[7_2] 7.2 Customer-related processes -- Section 7.2 of ISO 13485:2016 sets out the requirements for customer-related processes that a medical-device organization must establish, implement and maintain. It addresses how customer needs are identified, feedback and complaints are handled, and how product conformity and corrective actions are communicated to ensure continual improvement.
[7_3] 7.3 Design and development -- ISO 13485:2016 clause 7.3 requires manufacturers to plan, execute, and control the design and development of medical devices, ensuring that design inputs are defined, risks are assessed, and outputs meet intended use. It mandates documentation of design reviews, verification, validation, and changes, linking each stage to quality-management requirements.
[7_4] 7.4 Purchasing -- Section 7.4 of ISO 13485:2016 specifies the requirements for purchasing processes, requiring organizations to select and evaluate suppliers, define criteria for purchased products and services, and maintain documented controls to verify that inputs meet defined quality and safety standards.
[7_5] 7.5 Production and service provision -- Section 7.5 defines the controls needed for production and service provision, requiring that manufacturing processes be planned, validated and continuously monitored to meet device specifications. It also mandates equipment maintenance, product identification and traceability, and procedures for release, storage, handling and delivery.
[7_6] 7.6 Control of monitoring and measuring equipment -- ISO 13485:2016 clause 7.6 requires organizations to identify all monitoring and measuring equipment needed to verify product conformity, ensure it is suitable for its intended use, and maintain it under controlled conditions. Equipment must be calibrated or verified at specified intervals, with traceability to acceptable standards, and any non-conforming device must be clearly marked and removed from use until it meets requirements.
[8_1] 8.1 General -- Section 8.1 sets out the general requirements for a medical-device quality management system, stating that an organization must establish, document, implement and maintain a QMS that meets regulatory obligations and is capable of continual improvement. It also defines the need for top-management commitment, the allocation of responsibilities and the provision of resources to achieve effective quality outcomes.
[8_2] 8.2 Monitoring and measurement -- Clause 8.2 requires organizations to establish and maintain processes for monitoring and measuring the performance of the quality management system and the conformity of medical devices, including the use of appropriate methods, equipment and data analysis. It also mandates regular calibration, verification and maintenance of measuring resources to ensure reliable results.
[8_3] 8.3 Control of nonconforming product -- Clause 8.3 of ISO 13485:2016 mandates that manufacturers identify, document and evaluate any product that fails to meet specified requirements and implement controls to prevent its unintended use or delivery. The organization must segregate the nonconforming item, decide on disposition such as rework, acceptance under concession or scrap, record the decision and any corrective actions, and maintain traceability throughout the process.
[8_4] 8.4 Analysis of data -- Section 8.4 of ISO 13485:2016 requires manufacturers to plan, collect, and evaluate data related to product performance, processes, and compliance, using statistical or other appropriate methods. The analysis must identify trends, non-conformities, and opportunities for improvement, and the results must be documented and acted upon to maintain the effectiveness of the quality management system.
[8_5] 8.5 Improvement -- Section 8.5 of ISO 13485:2016 requires a medical device organization to establish and maintain processes that drive continual improvement of its quality management system. It calls for monitoring performance, analyzing data and trends, and implementing corrective and preventive actions to enhance product safety, regulatory compliance and overall system effectiveness.
[cl_4] Clause 4 - Quality Management System -- Clause 4 defines the requirements for a medical-device organization to establish, document, implement and maintain a quality management system, specifying the scope, processes, responsibilities and interactions needed to meet regulatory and customer demands. It emphasizes a risk-based approach, ongoing monitoring and continuous improvement to ensure the safety and performance of devices throughout their lifecycle.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Management Responsibility -- Clause 5 of ISO 13485:2016 outlines top-management responsibilities within a medical-device quality management system. It requires leadership to define and maintain a quality policy, ensure adequate resources, fulfill regulatory obligations, and drive continual improvement throughout the organization.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Resource Management -- Clause 6 of ISO 13485:2016 addresses resource management, requiring manufacturers to ensure sufficient infrastructure, work environment, and human resources to achieve product conformity and meet regulatory requirements. It specifies responsibilities for competence, training, and the maintenance of facilities and equipment that support the quality management system.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Product Realization -- Clause 7 of ISO 13485:2016 outlines the product realization process for medical device manufacturers, detailing requirements for planning, design and development, production, and post-market activities. It ensures that each stage-from specifying customer needs to manufacturing controls and validation-aligns with regulatory and quality objectives, supporting consistent, safe device delivery.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Measurement, Analysis and Improvement -- Clause 8 requires organizations to plan and implement processes for monitoring, measuring, analysing, and improving the quality management system, including internal audits, product conformity testing, corrective and preventive actions, and management review.
