[AQF] AQF Levels -- The Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) classifies qualifications into ten sequential levels, from Certificate I at level 1 to Doctoral degrees at level 10. Each level defines the expected knowledge, skills and autonomy required, providing a consistent benchmark for education and training across Australia.
[AQF.1] Level 1 - Senior Secondary Certificate -- Level 1 on the Australian Qualifications Framework is the senior secondary certificate earned after completing Year 12. It signifies that a learner has achieved the basic knowledge, literacy and numeracy skills required for further study, training or entry-level work.
[AQF.10] Level 10 - Doctoral Degree -- AQF Level 10 represents the highest qualification in the Australian Qualifications Framework
[AQF.2] Level 2 - Certificate I -- AQF Level 2 - Certificate I is the second tier of the Australian Qualifications Framework, representing an entry-level qualification that provides learners with basic functional knowledge and skills for routine tasks in a specific industry or occupation.
[AQF.3] Level 3 - Certificate II -- AQF Level 3 - Certificate II is a vocational qualification that provides foundational skills and knowledge for specific trades or occupations. It usually requires 1-2 years of structured training and includes competency-based assessment, preparing learners for entry-level work or further study.
[AQF.4] Level 4 - Certificate III -- AQF Level 4, known as Certificate III, is a sub-bachelor qualification that provides practical skills and knowledge for skilled work and further learning. It typically follows secondary schooling and prepares individuals for supervisory or technical roles in industry, community services, or trades. Completion often leads to entry-level positions or pathways to higher AQF levels.
[AQF.5] Level 5 - Certificate IV / Diploma -- AQF Level 5 covers Certificate IV and Diploma qualifications, indicating advanced technical and vocational training. It prepares learners for skilled work, supervisory responsibilities and specialised knowledge in a particular field. Graduates are equipped to enter the workforce at a higher level or continue to higher AQF levels.
[AQF.6] Level 6 - Advanced Diploma / Associate Degree -- AQF Level 6 denotes an advanced diploma or associate degree, a higher-education qualification that builds on prior learning and provides specialised knowledge and skills for professional work. It usually requires two to three years of full-time study and can lead to further study at Level 7.
[AQF.7] Level 7 - Bachelor Degree -- AQF Level 7 denotes a Bachelor degree, the standard undergraduate qualification in Australia. It requires at least three years of full-time study, combines theory and applied knowledge, and prepares graduates for professional work or further study.
[AQF.8] Level 8 - Bachelor Honours / Grad Cert / Grad Dip -- AQF Level 8 denotes the highest undergraduate qualification, encompassing bachelor honours degrees as well as graduate certificates and graduate diplomas, and serves as the entry point for postgraduate coursework. Holders possess advanced knowledge, specialised skills and research capabilities that prepare them for professional practice, specialist roles and further study at higher AQF levels.
[AQF.9] Level 9 - Masters Degree -- AQF Level 9 corresponds to a Master's degree, representing advanced academic or professional study beyond the bachelor's. It typically involves research, specialised coursework, and the development of expert knowledge, preparing graduates for high-level practice or further research.
[AQF.A] Application Dimension -- AQF.A, the Application dimension, outlines the degree to which a qualification expects graduates to use knowledge in practical contexts. It ranges from simple, routine tasks and direct application of known procedures at lower levels to complex, autonomous problem-solving, decision-making and innovation in professional settings at the highest levels. This dimension helps differentiate how theory is turned into action across the AQF.
[AQF.K] Knowledge Dimension -- In the Australian Qualifications Framework the code AQF.K denotes the Knowledge dimension, which classifies the type and depth of knowledge a qualification expects learners to acquire. It ranges from basic factual knowledge to advanced theoretical and conceptual understanding, guiding the design and assessment of outcomes across all qualification levels.
[AQF.S] Skills Dimension -- The Skills Dimension (AQF.S) is one of the three core components of the Australian Qualifications Framework, alongside knowledge and application. It defines the practical abilities, techniques and behaviours a graduate must demonstrate, specifying the level of hands-on competence required for each qualification.
