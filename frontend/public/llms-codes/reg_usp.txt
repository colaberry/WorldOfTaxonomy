[dietary] Dietary Supplements (<2000>-<2999>) -- The United States Pharmacopeia Key General Chapters section titled "Dietary Supplements" (codes 2000-2999) provides the official standards and specifications for dietary supplement products. It covers identity, composition, purity, labeling, packaging and testing requirements to ensure quality, safety and compliance across a wide range of supplement ingredients and formulations.
[general_info] General Information Chapters (<1000>-<1999>) -- The United States Pharmacopeia Key General Chapters numbered 1000-1999 are listed under the entry "general_info." They supply foundational guidance on terminology, units of measurement, labeling requirements, storage conditions and other essential information needed to interpret and apply the detailed USP monographs.
[general_tests] General Tests and Assays (<1>-<999>) -- The General Tests and Assays chapter in the USP provides standardized procedures for performing a wide range of analytical tests on pharmaceutical substances and dosage forms, covering methods numbered from 1 to 999. It serves as a reference for quality control, ensuring consistency and compliance with regulatory requirements.
[usp_1] <1> Injections and Implanted Drug Products -- The USP Key General Chapter 1 sets official standards for sterile injectable and implantable drug products, covering requirements for manufacturing, sterility testing, packaging, labeling, and quality control to ensure safety and efficacy throughout the product's shelf life.
[usp_1058] <1058> Analytical Instrument Qualification -- USP Chapter 1058 outlines procedures for qualifying analytical instruments to ensure they perform reliably and meet predefined criteria. It covers design qualification, installation qualification, operational qualification, and performance qualification, specifying documentation, testing, and acceptance criteria for labs.
[usp_1072] <1072> Disinfectants and Antiseptics -- USP General Chapter 1072 sets official specifications for disinfectants and antiseptics used in healthcare and related settings. It defines requirements for identity, purity, strength, labeling and performance testing, ensuring these products meet safety, efficacy and quality standards mandated by the United States Pharmacopeia.
[usp_1116] <1116> Microbiological Control and Monitoring of Aseptic Processing Environments -- USP Chapter 1116 outlines procedures for microbiological control and monitoring of aseptic processing environments. It provides guidance on sampling methods, acceptable microbial limits, and corrective actions to ensure sterile product integrity.
[usp_1211] <1211> Sterilization and Sterility Assurance -- USP General Chapter 1211 details the principles and procedures for achieving and verifying sterility of pharmaceutical products and devices. It defines sterility-assurance levels, outlines validation methods for physical, chemical and microbiological sterilization processes, and specifies testing, acceptance criteria and documentation required to confirm that a product meets the defined sterility standards.
[usp_1225] <1225> Validation of Compendial Procedures -- USP chapter 1225 defines the requirements for validating analytical procedures used in compendial testing, ensuring methods are accurate, precise, specific, linear and robust. It provides guidance on the design, execution and documentation of validation studies to support reliable pharmaceutical analysis.
[usp_1226] <1226> Verification of Compendial Procedures -- USP General Chapter 1226 offers guidance on verifying that laboratory methods meet the requirements of the United States Pharmacopeia, describing the design, execution and documentation of validation studies to confirm accuracy, precision, specificity, detection limits and robustness of compendial procedures. It outlines acceptance criteria, statistical tools and record-keeping practices to ensure consistent, compliant results across pharmacopeial testing.
[usp_232] <232> Elemental Impurities - Limits -- USP Chapter <232> establishes permissible limits for elemental impurities in drug products to ensure patient safety. It specifies acceptable daily exposure values for 23 elements, outlines testing methods such as ICP-MS, and provides guidance on risk assessment and control strategies for manufacturers.
[usp_233] <233> Elemental Impurities - Procedures -- USP Chapter <233> defines the procedures for determining elemental impurities in drug substances and products, specifying testing methods, acceptance criteria and limits based on daily exposure thresholds. It guides the use of techniques such as ICP-MS or AAS and outlines steps for sample preparation, analysis, and reporting to ensure compliance with safety standards.
[usp_467] <467> Residual Solvents -- USP Chapter 467 defines the requirements for testing and limiting residual solvents in drug substances and products, classifying solvents into three toxicity categories and setting maximum permissible concentrations. It outlines analytical methods, such as gas chromatography, and provides guidance to ensure that any remaining solvents do not compromise safety or efficacy.

[usp_71] <71> Sterility Tests -- USP Chapter <71> Sterility Tests outlines the procedures for determining the absence of viable microorganisms in pharmaceutical products, covering both direct inoculation and membrane filtration methods. [usp_731] <731> Loss on Drying -- The USP Chapter 731 outlines the loss on drying test, which measures the moisture content of a sample by heating it under defined temperature and time conditions, and sets the acceptable limits for the amount of weight loss.

[usp_905] <905> Uniformity of Dosage Units -- USP <905> Uniformity of Dosage Units establishes procedures to verify that each tablet, capsule or other unit contains the labeled amount of drug within specified limits. The chapter outlines test methods such as content uniformity and weight variation, defines acceptance criteria using statistical limits, and provides guidance on sampling, calculation of the acceptance value and corrective actions when units fail to meet the uniformity requirements.
