[IL] ITIL 4 Practices -- IL denotes the ITIL 4 Practices category, which groups the 34 general management, service management and technical practices that form the core of the ITIL 4 framework, providing guidance on how to perform activities such as incident management, continual improvement and service design to deliver value-focused services.
[IL.01] General: Architecture Management -- IL.01 - General: Architecture Management is the ITIL 4 practice that defines, maintains, and governs the design of service and solution architectures to ensure they meet business objectives and integrate cohesively across the organization. It provides standards, principles, and guidance for evolving architectures while managing risks and dependencies.
[IL.02] General: Continual Improvement -- IL.02 is part of the General group in the ITIL 4 practice categories and defines the Continual Improvement focus. It describes a systematic, ongoing approach to identify, assess and implement enhancements across services, processes and practices to increase value, efficiency and alignment with business objectives.
[IL.03] General: Information Security Management -- IL.03 General: Information Security Management is an ITIL 4 practice that defines how organizations protect information assets, ensuring confidentiality, integrity and availability while supporting business objectives. It establishes policies, risk assessments, controls and continual improvement processes to manage security risks across services and technology.
[IL.04] General: Knowledge Management -- Knowledge Management (IL.04) is a general ITIL 4 practice that ensures the creation, capture, sharing, and continual improvement of relevant information across an organization. By making the right knowledge available to the right people at the right time, it supports informed decision-making, improves efficiency, and fosters a culture of learning and reuse.
[IL.05] General: Measurement and Reporting -- IL.05 is the ITIL 4 practice category for General: Measurement and Reporting, which defines how organizations collect, analyse and communicate performance data. It establishes consistent metrics, reporting structures and improvement insights to support informed decision-making and alignment with service objectives.
[IL.06] General: Organizational Change Management -- IL.06 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories refers to the General practice of Organizational Change Management, which focuses on preparing, supporting, and helping individuals, teams, and the wider organization to adopt new ways of working. It aligns people-focused change activities with service-value system goals, ensuring that cultural, behavioral, and skill shifts are managed efficiently and sustainably.
[IL.07] General: Portfolio Management -- IL.07 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories refers to the General practice of Portfolio Management, which guides organizations in selecting, prioritising and governing the mix of services, projects and initiatives to align with strategic goals and optimise value. It provides a framework for evaluating investment decisions, balancing demand and capacity, and ensuring resources are allocated to the most beneficial outcomes.
[IL.08] General: Project Management -- IL.08 General: Project Management in ITIL 4 defines the systematic approach for planning, executing, monitoring and completing projects that deliver services and value. It provides guidance on scope definition, resource allocation, risk handling and stakeholder communication, and integrates with other ITIL practices to ensure alignment with service-oriented goals.
[IL.09] General: Relationship Management -- IL.09 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories identifies the General practice of Relationship Management, which guides organizations in establishing, nurturing and sustaining productive relationships with customers, users, suppliers and other stakeholders. It emphasizes understanding needs, fostering collaboration and delivering value through ongoing engagement.
[IL.10] General: Risk Management -- IL.10 - General: Risk Management is the ITIL 4 practice that identifies, assesses and controls threats to service value, providing a systematic approach to evaluate potential events, their likelihood and impact, and to apply mitigation or acceptance measures that support informed decision-making across the organization.
[IL.11] General: Service Financial Management -- IL.11, General: Service Financial Management, defines the practice that ensures services are delivered within agreed budgetary constraints and provide value for money. It covers budgeting, accounting, charging and cost transparency, aligning financial planning with service strategy and operational delivery.
[IL.12] General: Strategy Management -- IL.12 - General: Strategy Management is an ITIL 4 practice category that defines how organizations create, maintain and communicate their overall business and service strategies. It ensures that strategic objectives align with customer needs and market conditions, guiding decision-making and resource allocation across the service value system.
[IL.13] General: Supplier Management -- IL.13 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories identifies the General practice of Supplier Management, which ensures that an organization's suppliers and third-party providers deliver the required products and services in alignment with business needs. It involves selecting, monitoring, and managing supplier relationships to optimize value, mitigate risk, and maintain agreed service levels.

[IL.15] Service Mgmt: Availability Management -- Availability Management is the ITIL 4 practice that ensures services meet agreed-upon availability targets, monitors performance, and analyses the root causes of downtime. It designs and implements processes to improve reliability, capacity planning and recovery, aligning service availability with business needs.
[IL.16] Service Mgmt: Business Analysis -- IL.16 Service Mgmt: Business Analysis is an ITIL 4 practice that focuses on understanding and articulating business needs to shape effective services. It involves gathering requirements, analyzing processes, and defining service specifications that align with organizational goals, ensuring that new or changed services deliver measurable value to stakeholders.
[IL.17] Service Mgmt: Capacity/Performance Management -- IL.17 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories covers Service Management for Capacity and Performance Management, ensuring that services have the right amount of resources and meet agreed performance standards. It involves monitoring demand, planning capacity, optimizing performance, and aligning resources with business needs to maintain efficiency and user satisfaction.
[IL.18] Service Mgmt: Change Enablement -- IL.18 Change Enablement is the ITIL 4 practice that ensures all modifications to services, technology and processes are assessed, authorized and implemented with minimal risk to service continuity. It provides a structured approach for planning, reviewing, coordinating and evaluating changes to achieve stable, reliable outcomes.
[IL.19] Service Mgmt: Incident Management -- IL.19 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories identifies the Incident Management practice, a core component of Service Management that aims to restore normal service operation as quickly as possible after an interruption. It defines processes for logging, categorizing, prioritising and resolving incidents, reducing impact on business continuity and improving user satisfaction.
[IL.20] Service Mgmt: Problem Management -- Problem Management, identified as IL.20 in the ITIL 4 Practice Categories, focuses on identifying the root causes of incidents to prevent recurrence and minimize impact. It involves systematic analysis of incidents, creation of known error records, and coordination of permanent solutions, thereby improving service reliability and supporting continual improvement.
[IL.21] Service Mgmt: Service Desk -- IL.21 refers to the Service Desk practice in ITIL 4, which acts as the single point of contact between users and service providers, handling incidents, service requests, and communication. It focuses on restoring normal service operation quickly, providing information, and facilitating efficient resolutions while maintaining user satisfaction and supporting overall service delivery.
[IL.22] Service Mgmt: Service Level Management -- Service Level Management is the ITIL 4 practice that defines, negotiates, and monitors service level agreements to ensure services meet agreed-upon performance and quality targets. It aligns business expectations with provider capabilities, tracks compliance, and drives continual improvement of service delivery.
[IL.23] Technical: Deployment Management -- Technical: Deployment Management (IL.23) is the practice that plans, schedules and controls the movement of new or changed services into live environments, ensuring releases are delivered efficiently, securely and with minimal disruption. It coordinates activities across development, operations and support teams, tracks configuration items and validates that deployments meet predefined acceptance criteria.
[IL.24] Technical: Infrastructure/Platform Management -- IL.24 identifies the Technical practice of Infrastructure/Platform Management in ITIL 4, covering the design, delivery, operation and continual improvement of the technology components that underpin services, such as servers, networks, cloud platforms and related tools. It focuses on ensuring these foundational elements are reliable, scalable and aligned with business needs.
