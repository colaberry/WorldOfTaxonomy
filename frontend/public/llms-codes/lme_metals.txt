[LM] LME Categories -- LM is the code used to designate the set of commodity categories traded on the London Metal Exchange. These LME categories group the primary base and precious metals, such as aluminium, copper, zinc and nickel, that are listed for futures and options contracts on the exchange.
[LM.01] Copper (Grade A) -- The LM.01 entry designates Grade A copper traded on the London Metal Exchange, representing a standard purity of 99.99 percent copper in the form of cathodes that meet LME specifications for physical delivery and settlement. This grade is the benchmark metal for global pricing and is used in futures contracts, options and spot transactions across the exchange.
[LM.02] Aluminium (Primary) -- LM.02 denotes primary aluminium that is listed for trading on the London Metal Exchange. It represents the base metal produced directly from alumina and is used in sectors such as aerospace, automotive, construction and packaging, with pricing and contract specifications governed by LME rules.
[LM.03] Zinc (SHG) -- Zinc (SHG) is a primary industrial metal listed under the London Metal Exchange's LM.03 classification, traded via the standard LME zinc contract. It is widely used for galvanising steel, producing alloys and in various chemical applications, with pricing and delivery governed by LME market rules.
[LM.04] Nickel (Primary) -- LM.04 designates primary nickel, a base metal traded on the London Metal Exchange. It is a silvery-white, corrosion-resistant metal widely used in stainless steel, batteries and aerospace applications, and is priced in U.S. dollars per metric ton.
[LM.05] Lead (Standard) -- LM.05 designates the standard lead contract traded on the London Metal Exchange, serving as a global pricing benchmark for refined lead. The contract specifies a minimum purity of 99.8 percent, delivered in 25-tonne ingots that meet LME's physical and quality standards.
[LM.06] Tin -- LM.06 denotes tin, a soft, silvery-white metal traded on the London Metal Exchange. It is a primary component in solder, corrosion-resistant coatings, and various alloys such as bronze, and its market price serves as a benchmark for global tin contracts.
[LM.07] Aluminium Alloy -- LM.07 denotes aluminium alloy contracts traded on the London Metal Exchange, covering standardized alloy grades such as 6061, 7075 and other high-strength variants used in aerospace, automotive and construction. The contract specifies delivery of the alloy in designated forms and quantities under LME settlement rules.
[LM.08] NASAAC -- LM.08 NASAAC is a listed contract on the London Metal Exchange that tracks the price of a specific alloy used primarily in aerospace and high-performance engineering applications. The code designates a proprietary blend of nickel, aluminum and trace elements, offering traders a standardized benchmark for hedging material costs in the aerospace sector.
[LM.09] Cobalt -- LM.09 designates cobalt, a hard silvery-gray transition metal traded on the London Metal Exchange. It is prized for its high melting point and is a key ingredient in rechargeable batteries, aerospace superalloys, and specialty steels, and is covered by LME price reporting and futures contracts.
[LM.10] Molybdenum -- Molybdenum, designated LM.10 on the London Metal Exchange, is a refractory metal with a high melting point used to strengthen steel and superalloys. It is traded in alloy and powder forms and serves as a catalyst in petroleum refining and chemical production.

[LM.12] Steel Rebar -- Steel Rebar, classified as LM.12 on the London Metal Exchange, refers to standardized carbon-steel reinforcement bars used in concrete construction. The contract specifies nominal diameter ranges, mechanical properties and delivery terms for bulk shipments, offering a benchmark price for the global rebar market.
[LM.13] Steel HRC -- LM.13 denotes hot-rolled coil steel (HRC) that is listed for trading on the London Metal Exchange. The contract specifies standard dimensions and composition for flat-rolled carbon steel, used widely in construction and manufacturing.
[LM.14] Lithium Hydroxide -- Lithium hydroxide (LM.14) is listed on the London Metal Exchange as a traded metal product. It is a white, hygroscopic solid used primarily in the production of lithium-ion batteries and as a precursor for lithium carbonate. Its market price is quoted in US dollars per metric ton.
