[benchmarking] Country Benchmarking System -- The Country Benchmarking System is the EU's tool for measuring and comparing how third-country governments meet the deforestation-free requirements of Regulation (EU) 2023/1115. It assigns scores based on criteria such as forest governance, commodity traceability and compliance monitoring, enabling the European Commission to identify high-risk producers and guide trade decisions.
[cattle] Cattle -- Cattle are listed under the EU Regulation 2023/1115 as a commodity that must comply with deforestation-free criteria, meaning imports and production must be traceable, sourced without contributing to forest loss and meet due-diligence verification. The entry defines the product scope for reporting, labeling and market access requirements aimed at preventing indirect deforestation linked to cattle supply chains.
[checks] Competent Authority Checks -- The entry "Competent Authority Checks" details the duties of national authorities to verify that operators comply with Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, requiring on-site inspections, document reviews and sampling of products to confirm they are deforestation-free, and to enforce corrective measures where non-compliance is found.
[cocoa] Cocoa -- Under Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, cocoa is listed as a covered product that must be deforestation-free, requiring traders to conduct due-diligence, trace its supply chain, and verify compliance with EU standards before placing it on the market.
[coffee] Coffee -- Coffee covered by Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 must meet strict due-diligence and traceability requirements to verify that it is produced without contributing to deforestation or forest degradation. Importers and traders must submit geo-referenced data on the land where the coffee was cultivated and certify compliance with EU standards before the product can be placed on the market.
[dd_info] Information Collection (geolocation, date of production) -- The entry "dd_info" records the mandatory data that operators must gather to prove compliance with Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 on deforestation-free products, specifically the geographic coordinates of where a commodity was produced and the exact production date. This information is used by authorities to trace supply chains and verify that the product does not contribute to deforestation.
[dd_mitigation] Risk Mitigation -- The dd_mitigation entry outlines the risk-mitigation measures required by Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 to ensure products placed on the EU market do not contribute to deforestation. It details due-diligence actions such as traceability, verification of land-use data, and corrective steps for identified high-risk supply-chain links.
[dd_risk] Risk Assessment -- The dd_risk entry outlines the methodology for assessing the risk that a product contributes to deforestation, requiring operators to evaluate geographic, commodity and supply-chain factors and to document findings in the EU Deforestation Regulation's due-diligence system.
[dd_statement] Due Diligence Statement -- The dd_statement entry requires companies to provide a due diligence statement confirming that their products do not contribute to deforestation, as mandated by EU Regulation 2023/1115. It specifies the information to be disclosed, the verification methods and the timeline for submission.
[due_diligence] Due Diligence Requirements -- The due-diligence requirements set out in Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 obligate operators placing products on the EU market to verify that their supply chains do not contribute to forest degradation or conversion. They must collect geographic and commodity data, assess deforestation risk, implement mitigation measures where needed, and maintain a traceable record for authorities.
[enforcement] Enforcement -- The enforcement entry details the monitoring, verification and compliance procedures established under Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, specifying the duties of national authorities, the penalties for non-compliant operators and the mechanisms for market surveillance to ensure that only deforestation-free products are placed on the EU market.

[penalties_defor] Penalties (proportionate, dissuasive) -- The entry penalties_defor denotes the sanctions stipulated in Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 for breaches of the deforestation-free product rules, requiring that penalties be proportionate and sufficiently dissuasive to deter non-compliance.
[rubber] Rubber -- Rubber refers to natural rubber sourced from latex-producing trees and is listed as a commodity under Regulation (EU) 2023/1115. Companies placing rubber on the EU market must conduct due-diligence to confirm it does not originate from land cleared after 2020 and provide geo-referenced traceability information.
[scope] Scope and Covered Commodities -- The scope defines which economic operators and product types fall under the regulation, applying to exporters, importers and producers placing certain agricultural and forestry commodities on the EU market. Covered commodities include coffee, cocoa, soy, palm oil, beef, rubber, timber and related products, as well as derived goods, and the rule extends to any supply chain stage that influences the risk of deforestation linked to these items.
[soya] Soya -- The entry "Soya" designates soybeans and soy-derived products that fall under Regulation (EU) 2023/1115, which obliges operators placing these goods on the EU market to prove they are sourced without contributing to deforestation, using traceability and compliance documentation.
[standard_risk] Standard-Risk Countries -- The entry identifies countries that the EU Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 classifies as presenting a standard risk of supplying commodities linked to deforestation. These jurisdictions are subject to the baseline due-diligence obligations of the law but do not trigger the enhanced verification or reporting measures applied to high-risk countries.
[wood] Wood -- Wood denotes timber and other forest-derived material subject to the EU deforestation-free regulation, which requires that any wood placed on the EU market originates from plantations or forests not linked to illegal clearing after 2020. The entry sets out the criteria for compliance, traceability and verification that must be met for wood and wood-based products.
