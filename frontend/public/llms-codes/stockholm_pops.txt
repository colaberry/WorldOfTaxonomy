[POPS] Stockholm Convention Annexes -- The Stockholm Convention Annexes are the core documents of the international treaty on Persistent Organic Pollutants, listing chemicals that are subject to restriction, phase-out, or elimination. Annex I identifies POPs that parties must eliminate or restrict, while Annex II includes chemicals slated for addition after thorough risk evaluation. This framework guides global action to reduce the release and impact of harmful, long-lasting substances.
[POPS.01] Aldrin -- Aldrin is a chlorinated insecticide formerly used to control soil pests, now listed under the Stockholm Convention as a Persistent Organic Pollutant because of its high toxicity, tendency to bioaccumulate, and ability to travel long distances in the environment. It is subject to global phase-out or severe restrictions to protect human health and ecosystems.
[POPS.02] Chlordane -- Chlordane is an organochlorine pesticide formerly used for termite control and agricultural purposes. It is persistent-bioaccumulative and toxic to wildlife and humans, which led to its inclusion in the Stockholm Convention's POPs list under code POPS.02, requiring parties to phase out its production and use.
[POPS.03] DDT -- DDT is a synthetic organochlorine insecticide once widely used for malaria control and agricultural pest management. Its extreme environmental persistence, tendency to bioaccumulate, and links to adverse human health effects led to its inclusion in the Stockholm Convention, which now restricts production and mandates phase-out in most countries.
[POPS.04] Dieldrin -- Dieldrin is a chlorinated-cyclodiene insecticide that is highly toxic, persistent in the environment, and prone to bioaccumulation. Once used on many crops, it was phased out because of its health and ecological risks and is listed under the Stockholm Convention as a substance that should be eliminated.
[POPS.05] Endrin -- Endrin is a chlorinated cyclodiene insecticide once used on cotton, corn and other crops, noted for its high acute toxicity, environmental persistence and tendency to bioaccumulate in wildlife. Because of its long-range transport, severe health effects and role in contaminating food chains, it was listed as a persistent organic pollutant and is subject to elimination under the Stockholm Convention.
[POPS.06] Heptachlor -- Heptachlor is a chlorinated cyclodiene insecticide that was once used for soil and wood pest control; it is highly persistent, bioaccumulative and toxic to wildlife and humans, which led to its listing under the Stockholm Convention on POPs for elimination or strict restriction.
[POPS.07] Hexachlorobenzene -- Hexachlorobenzene is a chlorinated aromatic compound listed under the Stockholm Convention as a persistent organic pollutant. It was historically used as a fungicide and pesticide, but its high toxicity, resistance to degradation, and tendency to bioaccumulate in food chains have led to strict global restrictions and phase-out measures.
[POPS.08] Mirex -- Mirex is a chlorinated cyclodiene insecticide that was widely used for termite control and as a fire-retardant before being identified as a persistent organic pollutant. Under the Stockholm Convention it is listed as a POP and subject to elimination or strict restriction because of its environmental persistence,
[POPS.09] Toxaphene -- Toxaphene is a chlorinated petroleum distillate historically used as a broad-spectrum insecticide on cotton, corn and other crops. It persists in the environment, bioaccumulates in wildlife and humans, and poses risks of neurotoxicity, liver damage and cancer, leading to its inclusion under the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants.
[POPS.10] PCBs -- Polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) are a group of synthetic chlorinated organic chemicals once widely used as dielectric fluids, coolants and in hydraulic systems. Their high stability makes them persistent in the environment, they bioaccumulate in food webs and pose risks of cancer and immune, reproductive and developmental effects. Under the Stockholm Convention they are listed for elimination or strict restriction to protect human health and ecosystems.
[POPS.11] Dioxins and Furans -- Dioxins and furans are a group of chemically related, highly toxic compounds that persist in the environment, bioaccumulate through the food chain, and pose serious health risks such as cancer and developmental damage. Under the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants, they are listed as POPS.11, requiring parties to eliminate or restrict their production, use, and release to protect human health and ecosystems.
[POPS.12] PFOS and derivatives -- POPS.12 refers to perfluorooctanesulfonic acid (PFOS) and its related compounds, which are persistent, bioaccumulative and toxic substances targeted by the Stockholm Convention for restriction or phase-out due to their environmental and health hazards.
[POPS.13] PFOA and related compounds -- POPS.13 lists perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) and chemically related per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances for control under the Stockholm Convention. These compounds are highly persistent, bioaccumulative and toxic, prompting international efforts to phase out their production, limit use and manage existing releases to protect human health and the environment.
[POPS.14] PFHxS -- PFHxS, perfluorohexanesulfonic acid, is a synthetic fluorinated compound used in applications such as firefighting foams and stain-resistant textiles. It is highly persistent, bioaccumulative and toxic, leading to its inclusion in the Stockholm Convention's list of persistent organic pollutants under entry POPS.14. Its widespread environmental presence and potential health impacts prompted global regulatory control.
[POPS.A] Annex A - Elimination -- POPS.A refers to Annex A of the Stockholm Convention on Persistent Organic Pollutants, which enumerates substances that parties must eliminate through bans or strict restrictions, aiming to phase out production, use and release of these hazardous chemicals.
[POPS.B] Annex B - Restriction -- Annex B of the Stockholm Convention lists persistent organic pollutants that are subject to restriction, requiring parties to limit or phase out their production, use and release while allowing specific exemptions and mandating best-available techniques for control.
[POPS.C] Annex C - Unintentional Production -- This entry refers to Annex C of the Stockholm Convention, which lists chemicals that are unintentionally produced as by-products of industrial processes, combustion, or waste treatment and therefore require monitoring and mitigation. It covers substances such as dioxins, furans and unintentionally generated PCBs, obliging parties to report releases and apply best-available techniques to reduce environmental emissions.
[POPS.NIP] National Implementation Plans -- National Implementation Plans are country-specific strategies that detail how a party will fulfill its obligations under the Stockholm Convention, specifying measures to phase out, reduce, or safely manage POPs, assigning institutional responsibilities, setting timelines, and establishing reporting mechanisms.
