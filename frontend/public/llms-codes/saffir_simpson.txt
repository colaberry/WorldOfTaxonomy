[SH] Hurricane Categories -- The code SH identifies the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, which classifies tropical cyclones into six categories based on their sustained wind speeds. Categories range from Category 1 (74-95 mph) up to Category 5 (157 mph or higher), indicating increasing potential for damage and informing warnings.
[SH.01] Tropical Depression (< 39 mph) -- SH.01 designates a tropical depression, the lowest category on the Saffir-Simpson scale, with sustained winds below 39 mph. At this stage the system lacks the organization of a tropical storm and causes limited wind damage, though it can still bring heavy rain and flooding.
[SH.02] Tropical Storm (39-73 mph) -- SH.02 designates a tropical storm on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, with sustained wind speeds ranging from 39 to 73 miles per hour. At this stage the system can produce heavy rainfall, gusty winds, and localized flooding but does not yet meet the criteria for a hurricane.
[SH.03] Category 1 (74-95 mph) -- SH.03 denotes a Category 1 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson scale, with sustained wind speeds of 74-95 miles per hour. At this level damage is usually limited to some roof and siding loss, broken tree branches and power lines, and minor coastal flooding.
[SH.04] Category 2 (96-110 mph) -- Category 2 hurricanes on the Saffir-Simpson Scale have sustained wind speeds of 96-110 mph. They produce very strong winds that can cause extensive roof damage, uproot many trees, and result in significant power outages and coastal flooding, though structures built to code usually remain intact.
[SH.05] Category 3 (111-129 mph) Major -- SH.05 denotes a Category 3 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson scale, with sustained wind speeds of 111 to 129 miles per hour. It is classified as a major hurricane, capable of causing devastating damage to well-built homes, uprooting trees, and generating significant storm surge.
[SH.06] Category 4 (130-156 mph) Major -- SH.06 designates Category 4 on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, indicating sustained winds of 130-156 mph. This major classification signifies storms that can cause catastrophic damage to homes, uproot large trees, and result in widespread power loss and severe coastal flooding.

[SH.08] Post-Tropical Cyclone -- SH.08 identifies a system that has moved beyond the Saffir-Simpson categories after losing its tropical characteristics, becoming a post-tropical cyclone. It may still generate strong winds, heavy rain, and surf, but is no longer classified by the hurricane wind scale.
[SH.09] Subtropical Storm -- In the Saffir-Simpson system the code SH.09 denotes a subtropical storm, a cyclonic system with sustained winds of 39-73 mph (34-63 knots) that exhibits both tropical and extratropical characteristics. It is not assigned a hurricane category but can develop into a tropical storm.
[SH.10] Tropical Wave/Disturbance -- SH.10 identifies a tropical wave or disturbance, an early, loosely organized area of low pressure with enhanced convection and wind speeds below 39 mph. It marks a pre-cyclonic system that has not yet reached tropical depression status, and therefore is not assigned a Saffir-Simpson hurricane category.
[SH.11] Invest (Potential System) -- SH.11 designates an "Invest," a weather disturbance that the National Hurricane Center monitors as a potential tropical cyclone before it reaches tropical depression status. These systems are assigned numbers and a suffix (e.g., 90L) to track data and model forecasts, helping forecasters assess whether they may develop into a tropical storm or hurricane. The invest designation does not indicate any wind speed or intensity on the Saffir-Simpson scale.
