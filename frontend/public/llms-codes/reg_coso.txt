[ctrl_env] Control Environment -- The control environment is the foundation of an organization's internal control system, reflecting the integrity, ethical values and competence of its people, the board's oversight, management's philosophy, and the organizational structure. It sets the tone at the top and influences the effectiveness of all other COSO control components.
[erm_governance] ERM - Governance and Culture -- The "ERM - Governance and Culture" component of the COSO Internal Control Integrated Framework (2013) addresses the organization's governance structures, oversight responsibilities and the establishment of risk-management philosophy and culture. It emphasizes setting risk appetite, aligning policies with strategic objectives and fostering ethical behavior throughout the entity.
[erm_info] ERM - Information, Communication, and Reporting -- The erm_info entry represents the Information, Communication, and Reporting component of COSO's 2013 Enterprise Risk Management - Integrated Framework. It identifies the processes that capture relevant risk data, disseminate it throughout the organization, and produce internal and external reports that support effective decision-making and oversight.
[erm_performance] ERM - Performance -- The "ERM - Performance" entry (code erm_performance) in the COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework (2013) outlines how organizations assess and monitor the effectiveness of their enterprise risk management processes, linking risk appetite to strategic objectives and measuring outcomes against established risk-based performance indicators. It emphasizes ongoing evaluation, reporting, and continuous improvement to ensure risk-aware decision making.
[erm_review] ERM - Review and Revision -- The ERM - Review and Revision entry outlines the periodic assessment and updating of an organization's enterprise risk management program in line with the COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework (2013). It guides firms to evaluate risk identification, control activities, monitoring procedures and risk-response strategies, ensuring the system remains aligned with the entity's objectives, risk appetite and evolving external conditions.
[erm_strategy] ERM - Strategy and Objective-Setting -- The erm_strategy entry outlines the COSO framework component that links enterprise risk management to an organization's strategy and objective-setting process. It describes how entities define risk appetite, embed risk considerations in strategic planning, and establish objectives that align with their overall mission and value creation goals.
[info_comm] Information and Communication -- Information and Communication is a core component of the 2013 COSO Internal Control Framework that requires organizations to capture, share, and use relevant data in a timely manner. It ensures that objectives, responsibilities, and performance expectations are communicated up- and down- and across the entity, while supporting reliable reporting through effective information systems.
[monitoring] Monitoring Activities -- Monitoring activities are ongoing and separate evaluations that assess whether internal control components operate effectively over time, identifying deficiencies and ensuring corrective actions are taken. This process helps an organization sustain reliable financial reporting, compliance, and operational performance.
[p1] Principle 1 - Demonstrates Commitment to Integrity and Ethical Values -- Principle 1 requires the board and management to establish and communicate a culture of integrity and ethical values, setting the tone-at-the-top through policies, actions, and leadership that promote responsible behavior across the organization.
[p10] Principle 10 - Selects and Develops Control Activities -- Principle 10 of the 2013 COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework emphasizes that organizations must choose and design control activities that align with identified risks and objectives, ensuring they are properly implemented and maintained across processes. These activities may include approvals, reconciliations, segregation of duties, and physical safeguards, all tailored to mitigate specific threats and support reliable operations.
[p11] Principle 11 - Selects and Develops General Controls over Technology -- Principle 11 states that an organization must identify, implement and maintain general IT controls that support the reliability of application controls and overall objectives. These controls address infrastructure, system development, security and change management to ensure data integrity, availability and confidentiality.
[p12] Principle 12 - Deploys through Policies and Procedures -- Principle 12 requires an entity to create and maintain formal policies and procedures that translate control objectives into actionable guidance, define responsibilities, and standardize how controls are applied throughout the organization, ensuring consistent and effective execution of the internal-control system.
[p13] Principle 13 - Uses Relevant Information -- Principle 13 in the 2013 COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework stresses that an organization must gather, assess and apply relevant information-including both internal data and external sources-to support its control activities, risk assessments, and decision-making processes, ensuring that all information used is timely, reliable and directly linked to the entity's objectives.
[p14] Principle 14 - Communicates Internally -- Principle 14 of the 2013 COSO Integrated Framework stresses the need for organizations to establish effective internal communication channels that convey information about internal-control responsibilities, objectives, and activities. It ensures that relevant personnel receive timely, accurate data to perform their duties, fostering awareness, coordination, and accountability throughout the enterprise.
[p15] Principle 15 - Communicates Externally -- Principle 15 of the 2013 COSO Integrated Framework requires the organization to share relevant information with external parties, ensuring that stakeholders such as regulators, investors, and the public receive timely, accurate disclosures about the entity's internal-control system and its performance. This external communication supports transparency, builds trust, and helps external users assess the reliability of the organization's financial reporting and risk-management practices.
[p16] Principle 16 - Conducts Ongoing and/or Separate Evaluations -- Principle 16 requires an organization to regularly assess the design and operation of its internal-control system, either through continuous monitoring activities or periodic, standalone evaluations. These assessments use established criteria to gauge effectiveness, identify deficiencies, and prompt timely remediation to sustain reliable control over operations, reporting, and compliance.
[p17] Principle 17 - Evaluates and Communicates Deficiencies -- Principle 17 requires management to identify, evaluate and report any weaknesses in the internal-control system, communicating those deficiencies to individuals who can remediate them and tracking corrective actions. This ensures timely mitigation of risks and supports continuous improvement of control processes.
[p2] Principle 2 - Board of Directors Exercises Oversight Responsibility -- Principle 2 of the COSO 2013 Internal Control - Integrated Framework requires the board of directors to exercise oversight responsibility for the organization's internal control system, ensuring that management establishes appropriate policies, monitors performance and addresses risks. The board must maintain vigilance, provide strategic direction and hold management accountable for effective control and ethical conduct.
[p3] Principle 3 - Establishes Structure, Authority, and Responsibility -- Principle 3 of the 2013 COSO Integrated Framework requires an organization to define and document its governance structure, delineating clear lines of authority, responsibility, and reporting relationships. This ensures that relevant personnel have the power and accountability needed to execute and oversee internal-control activities effectively.
[p4] Principle 4 - Demonstrates Commitment to Competence -- Principle 4 of the 2013 COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework requires an organization to ensure its people have the necessary knowledge, skills and abilities, and to provide ongoing training and development so they can fulfill control responsibilities effectively, fostering a culture of competence and regularly assessing personnel capabilities.
[p5] Principle 5 - Enforces Accountability -- Principle 5 emphasizes that organizations must assign clear responsibilities and hold individuals answerable for their actions, ensuring that duties are well defined and performance is monitored. It calls for policies, documentation, and consistent consequences that reinforce personal accountability in achieving control objectives.
[p6] Principle 6 - Specifies Suitable Objectives -- Principle 6 requires an entity to define clear, realistic objectives that support its mission and strategy, covering operational performance, reliable reporting and compliance with laws. These objectives must be specific, measurable and aligned with the organization's risk tolerance, providing a basis for designing and evaluating internal controls.

[p8] Principle 8 - Assesses Fraud Risk -- Principle 8 requires organizations to assess the risk of fraud by identifying potential fraud scenarios, evaluating their likelihood and impact, and considering the incentives, pressures, and opportunities that may exist. It directs management to incorporate fraud-risk assessments into the overall risk-assessment process and to design controls that mitigate identified fraud risks.
[p9] Principle 9 - Identifies and Analyzes Significant Change -- Principle 9 requires an organization to systematically detect any significant internal or external changes that could affect its objectives and to evaluate the related risks. By analyzing these changes, management can adjust control activities, information processes, or risk-responses to keep the internal-control system effective.
[risk_assess] Risk Assessment -- Risk assessment in the 2013 COSO Internal Control - Integrated Framework refers to the process of identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing internal and external events that could affect the achievement of objectives. It guides organizations in evaluating the likelihood and impact of risks, determining appropriate responses, and integrating risk-aware decisions into control activities and monitoring.
