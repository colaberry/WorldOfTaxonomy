[CT] Contract Types -- CT is the code for categorizing commercial contract types, grouping standard agreements such as fixed-price, cost-plus, and time-and-material contracts. It helps organizations classify and manage procurement, legal, and financial aspects of agreements across industries.
[CT.01] Fixed-Price Contract -- CT.01 Fixed-Price Contract refers to an agreement where the total price for goods or services is established upfront and remains unchanged regardless of actual costs incurred. This format places most financial risk on the seller, who must deliver within the agreed budget, and provides the buyer with cost certainty and simplified budgeting. It is commonly used when project scope and specifications are well defined.
[CT.02] Time and Materials (T&M) -- CT.02 refers to a Time and Materials (T&M) contract, in which the buyer reimburses the seller for the actual labor hours and materials consumed, usually at pre-agreed unit rates. This format provides flexibility for scope changes but offers limited cost certainty for the buyer.
[CT.03] Cost-Plus Contract -- A Cost-Plus Contract (CT.03) reimburses the contractor for all allowable direct and indirect costs incurred on a project and adds a pre-agreed fee or profit margin. This arrangement shifts most cost risk to the buyer, making it useful when project scope or quantities are uncertain, and is common in government, construction, and research contracts.
[CT.04] Unit-Price Contract -- A unit-price contract sets a fixed amount for each defined unit of work, such as per square foot or per item, and the total contract value is calculated by multiplying these rates by the actual quantities delivered. It allows the buyer to adjust payment as project scope changes while providing the contractor with clear pricing for each measurable component.
[CT.05] Retainer Agreement -- A retainer agreement is a contract in which a client pays an ongoing fee to secure services from a professional or firm, typically outlining scope, payment terms, duration, and termination conditions. It is used in legal, consulting, and other service industries to ensure availability of the provider.
[CT.06] Master Service Agreement (MSA) -- CT.06 Master Service Agreement (MSA) is a standard contract that outlines the general terms and conditions under which a service provider delivers ongoing services to a client, covering scope, performance metrics, fees, confidentiality, and termination provisions.
[CT.07] Statement of Work (SOW) -- CT.07 designates a Statement of Work, a contractual document that details the specific tasks, deliverables, timelines, performance standards, and payment terms for a project, clarifying the responsibilities of each party and providing a measurable framework for execution and acceptance.
[CT.08] Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) -- CT.08 designates a Non-Disclosure Agreement, a contract used to protect confidential information exchanged between parties. It obligates signatories to refrain from revealing proprietary data, trade secrets or other sensitive material without permission, typically for a specified term and with limited exceptions.

[CT.11] Joint Venture Agreement -- CT.11 refers to a Joint Venture Agreement, a contract in which two or more parties combine resources, expertise, and capital to pursue a specific business project while remaining separate legal entities. The agreement outlines each party's contributions, profit-sharing, governance structure, decision-making processes, and exit provisions, establishing the terms for collaboration and risk allocation.
[CT.12] Distribution Agreement -- A distribution agreement outlines the terms by which a supplier authorizes a distributor to market and sell its products in a specified territory, detailing responsibilities such as pricing, delivery, inventory management, and intellectual property use. It often includes performance targets, exclusivity provisions, and termination conditions to protect both parties.
[CT.13] Franchise Agreement -- A franchise agreement is a contract in which a franchisor grants a franchisee the right to operate a business using the franchisor's brand, system and support in exchange for fees and adherence to established standards. It details obligations such as territory, training, royalty payments and quality-control requirements to maintain consistent brand performance.
[CT.14] Employment Contract -- CT.14 refers to employment contracts, the legal agreements that define the relationship between an employer and an employee. These contracts specify duties, compensation, duration, benefits, termination conditions, and any confidentiality or non-compete obligations.
[CT.15] Independent Contractor Agreement -- The Independent Contractor Agreement is a contract used when a business hires an individual or entity to perform specific services while maintaining the contractor's autonomy
