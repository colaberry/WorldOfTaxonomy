[capacity_label] Capacity and Performance Labels -- The capacity_label entry sets out the mandatory capacity and performance labeling requirements for batteries covered by Regulation (EU) 2023/1542. It specifies the information that must appear on the label, such as nominal capacity, energy content, and key performance indicators, to ensure transparent consumer information and support proper waste management.
[carbon_fp] Carbon Footprint Declaration and Thresholds -- The carbon_fp entry sets out the mandatory carbon-footprint declaration for batteries covered by Regulation (EU) 2022/1542, defining the methodology and reporting thresholds that manufacturers must meet to disclose lifecycle greenhouse-gas emissions and ensure compliance with EU climate-reduction goals.
[collection] Collection Targets -- The entry defines the quantitative collection targets that Member States must achieve for waste batteries and accumulators, setting minimum percentages of batteries placed on the market that must be collected each year. It establishes monitoring and reporting obligations to ensure that collection rates meet the EU's circular-economy goals for battery reuse and recycling.
[data_access] Data Access and Interoperability -- The Data Access and Interoperability entry sets out the EU-wide obligations for making battery-related information - such as composition, performance, durability and end-of-life handling - available in standardized digital formats to authorities, recyclers and market participants, ensuring consistent access and compatibility across the battery value chain.
[dd_policy] Due Diligence Policy (Art 48) -- The entry outlines the mandatory due-diligence procedures that economic operators must follow to ensure compliance with environmental and safety requirements for batteries and waste batteries, as set out in Article 48 of Regulation (EU) 2023/1542. It requires documentation, risk assessment, monitoring and reporting to demonstrate responsible sourcing, production and end-of-life handling.
[digital_passport] Digital Battery Passport Requirements -- The Digital Battery Passport Requirements under Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 define the mandatory electronic record that must accompany each battery placed on the EU market, detailing its type, capacity, chemistry, provenance of raw materials, recycling pathways and compliance with safety and environmental standards, and stipulating how the data are to be stored, accessed and updated throughout the battery's lifecycle.
[due_diligence] Due Diligence and Supply Chain -- The due_diligence entry outlines the obligations set out in Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 for manufacturers, importers and distributors of batteries to assess and monitor their supply chains. It requires systematic verification of compliance with environmental, health, safety and human-rights standards, documentation of findings and reporting to authorities to ensure responsible sourcing and waste-battery management.
[end_of_life] End-of-Life Management -- The "End-of-Life Management" entry defines the obligations for producers and waste operators to ensure that batteries reaching the end of their useful life are collected, treated, recycled or safely disposed of in accordance with the EU's circular-economy goals. It sets targets for recovery rates, outlines reporting duties and establishes a framework for financing the collection and recycling system.
[hazardous_restrict] Hazardous Substance Restrictions -- The hazardous_restrict entry records substances that the EU Regulation 2023/1542 limits or bans in batteries and waste batteries, such as lead, mercury, cadmium and certain flame retardants. It specifies maximum allowed concentrations, compliance deadlines and testing methods that manufacturers must follow to reduce environmental and health risks.
[labelling] Labelling and Information -- The labelling and information provisions of Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 require batteries and waste batteries to display standardized safety symbols, performance data and recycling instructions on the product and its accompanying documents. Manufacturers must include details on chemical composition, capacity, and end-of-life handling to ensure consumer awareness and proper waste management, supporting the EU's circular-economy goals.
[material_recovery] Material Recovery Targets (Co, Li, Ni, Cu) -- The EU Regulation 2023/1542 sets mandatory recovery targets for key battery metals, requiring that a specified percentage of cobalt, lithium, nickel and copper be reclaimed from waste batteries by designated dates. These targets aim to secure material supply chains, reduce reliance on primary extraction and support the circular economy for batteries.

[performance] Performance and Durability Requirements -- The Performance and Durability Requirements entry outlines the technical criteria that batteries placed on the EU market must meet, covering aspects such as capacity retention, cycle life, safety performance, and resistance to degradation under normal use conditions. These standards aim to ensure reliable operation and long service life while protecting consumer safety and environmental goals.
[qr_label] QR Code and Labelling Requirements -- The QR Code and Labelling Requirements set out in Regulation (EU) 2022/1542 mandate that all batteries placed on the EU market carry a machine-readable QR code and standardized label, providing details on product type, capacity, chemical composition, and compliance with safety and recycling obligations to enhance traceability and consumer information.
[recycled_content] Recycled Content Requirements -- The recycled_content entry sets mandatory minimum percentages of recycled material that must be incorporated into different categories of batteries covered by Regulation (EU) 2023/1542, obliging producers to demonstrate compliance through documentation and periodic reporting, and allowing market surveillance authorities to verify that the stated recycled fractions are met.
[recycling] Recycling Efficiency Targets -- The EU Regulation 2023/1542 establishes mandatory recycling efficiency targets for batteries and waste batteries, specifying minimum percentages of material recovery that must be achieved for each battery chemistry. It outlines the calculation methodology, reporting obligations and verification procedures that producers and recyclers must follow to ensure consistent, higher-quality recycling across the Union.
[risk_management] Supply Chain Risk Management -- The risk_management entry outlines mandatory supply-chain procedures for manufacturers, importers and distributors of batteries and waste batteries under Regulation (EU) 2023/1542. It requires documented due-diligence, traceability and mitigation plans to address environmental, health and safety risks, and obliges entities to report compliance with sustainability and circular-economy criteria throughout the product lifecycle.
[sustainability] Sustainability and Safety Requirements -- The sustainability entry in Regulation (EU) 2023/1542 sets out environmental and safety criteria that batteries and waste batteries must meet, covering durability, carbon-footprint limits, use of recycled content, reduced hazardous substances and design for efficient collection and recycling throughout their life-cycle.
