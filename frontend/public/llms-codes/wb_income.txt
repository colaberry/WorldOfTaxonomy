[IBRD] IBRD Eligible -- IBRD Eligible refers to countries that meet the World Bank's criteria for borrowing from the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development, typically middle-income or creditworthy low-income economies. These nations are not classified as IDA recipients but can access IBRD loans and guarantees for development projects. The designation indicates eligibility for the IBRD's financial instruments and support.
[IBRD-GRAD] Graduated from World Bank -- The IBRD-GRAD code marks a country that has graduated from the World Bank's income-based eligibility categories and is now classified as a graduate economy, meaning it no longer qualifies for IDA concessional assistance and is treated as a middle-income borrower within the IBRD framework.
[IBRD-ONLY] IBRD-Only Borrowers -- IBRD-Only Borrowers are countries that qualify for financing solely from the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development and not from the International Development Association. They are typically middle-income economies that do not meet the low-income eligibility criteria for IDA support, so they receive only IBRD loans and guarantees.
[IDA] IDA Eligible -- IDA Eligible refers to low-income countries that meet the criteria for receiving concessional financing and grants from the World Bank's International Development Association, which supports development projects with favorable terms. These nations are typically characterized by limited fiscal capacity and higher vulnerability, qualifying them for assistance aimed at reducing poverty and promoting sustainable growth.
[IDA-BLEND] IDA-IBRD Blend Countries -- IDA-IBRD blend countries are low-income or lower-middle-income nations that qualify for both the International Development Association's concessional loans and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development's non-concessional financing. The classification signals a blended financing arrangement where projects receive a mix of grant-like and market-rate funding to support development goals.
[IDA-FCS] Fragile and Conflict-Affected -- IDA-FCS denotes World Bank-listed low-income countries that are also classified as fragile or conflict-affected, qualifying them for special concessional financing from the International Development Association to address heightened vulnerability and support stability-building and development efforts.
[IDA-ONLY] IDA-Only Countries -- The World Bank's IDA-Only classification identifies countries that qualify exclusively for financing from the International Development Association, the Bank's concessional arm for the poorest nations. These economies are generally low-income and are not eligible for standard International Bank for Reconstruction and Development loans.
