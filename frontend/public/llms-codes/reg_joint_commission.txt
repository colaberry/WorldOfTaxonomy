[ec] EC - Environment of Care -- The EC (Environment of Care) standard in The Joint Commission Accreditation Standards for Hospitals specifies requirements for a safe, functional, and compliant physical environment, covering building safety, utilities, engineering, security, and environmental services. It ensures that facilities maintain appropriate design, maintenance, and emergency preparedness to protect patients, staff, and visitors.
[ec_01] EC.01 - Safety and Security Management Plans -- EC.01 requires hospitals to create and maintain comprehensive safety and security management plans that address emergency preparedness, risk assessment, staff training, and response procedures
[ec_02] EC.02 - Hazardous Materials and Waste Management -- EC.02 sets requirements for hospitals to safely store, handle, and dispose of hazardous materials and waste, protecting patients, staff, and the environment. The standard calls for documented procedures, proper labeling, segregation, staff training, and compliance with applicable federal and state regulations.
[em] EM - Emergency Management -- The EM standard defines the requirements for a hospital's emergency management program, including risk assessment, preparedness planning, response coordination, and recovery procedures to maintain safe and effective patient care during disasters and other emergencies.
[hr] HR - Human Resources -- HR - Human Resources in The Joint Commission Accreditation Standards for Hospitals outlines requirements for staffing, credentialing, employee competency, orientation, performance evaluation, and ongoing workforce development, ensuring that hospitals maintain qualified personnel and promote safe, high-quality patient care.
[ic] IC - Infection Prevention and Control -- IC - Infection Prevention and Control is a Joint Commission accreditation standard that requires hospitals to establish comprehensive programs to prevent the spread of infectious agents. It covers policies for hand hygiene, sterile technique, surveillance, isolation precautions, environmental cleaning and staff education, ensuring consistent practices that protect patients and staff from healthcare-associated infections.
[im] IM - Information Management -- The IM standard defines the policies, procedures and responsibilities required for managing patient health information within a hospital, emphasizing accuracy, confidentiality, accessibility and regulatory compliance. It addresses electronic health-record systems, data security, record-retention schedules, information exchange and the oversight needed to protect and effectively use clinical data.
[ld] LD - Leadership -- LD - Leadership sets out the duties of hospital leaders to create a safety-focused culture, define strategic goals, allocate resources, and ensure compliance with accreditation requirements. It calls for documented governance structures, performance monitoring, and continuous improvement processes.
[ld_01] LD.01 - Governance and Leadership Structure -- LD.01 outlines the requirements for a hospital's governance and leadership structure, requiring the board of directors and senior management to establish clear authority, accountability, and oversight of patient care, safety, compliance and quality improvement initiatives. It mandates documented policies, defined roles and responsibilities, and regular evaluation of leadership effectiveness to ensure alignment with the organization's mission and regulatory obligations.
[ls] LS - Life Safety -- LS denotes the Life Safety standards in The Joint Commission's hospital accreditation program, outlining requirements for fire protection, building construction, means of egress, emergency power, and related safety systems to ensure patient and staff protection during emergencies.
[mm] MM - Medication Management -- MM in The Joint Commission's hospital accreditation standards designates Medication Management. It specifies the policies, procedures and documentation required for safe prescribing, dispensing, administration and monitoring of drugs, emphasizing staff training, error prevention, and patient-specific assessment to ensure safe, effective medication use.
[mm_01] MM.01 - Medication Procurement and Storage -- MM.01 outlines the Joint Commission's requirements for hospitals to establish and maintain safe processes for purchasing, receiving, and storing medications. It mandates documented procedures that verify the legitimacy of suppliers, ensure proper labeling, temperature control, and segregation of hazardous drugs, and require regular inventory checks to prevent errors, contamination, and theft. Compliance with these standards is essential for accreditation and patient safety.
[mm_03] MM.03 - Medication Administration -- MM.03 outlines the Joint Commission's requirements for safe medication administration in hospitals, specifying standards for verification of orders, proper labeling, accurate dosage calculations, patient identification, and documentation. It mandates policies and procedures that ensure medication is given to the right patient, at the right time, by the right route, and in the right dose, supporting staff competence and monitoring for errors.
[ms] MS - Medical Staff -- MS, the Medical Staff section of the Joint Commission Accreditation Standards for Hospitals, outlines requirements for the qualifications, credentialing, peer review, and ongoing evaluation of physicians and allied health professionals. It mandates processes for appointing privileged staff, maintaining competence, and ensuring compliance with hospital policies to promote safe, high-quality patient care.
[npsg] NPSG - National Patient Safety Goals -- The National Patient Safety Goals (NPSG) are a core component of The Joint Commission's hospital accreditation standards, outlining evidence-based objectives that hospitals must meet to improve safety outcomes. They target high-risk areas such as infection prevention, medication accuracy, effective handoff communication, and fall reduction, guiding facilities toward consistent, measurable improvements in patient care.
[npsg_01] NPSG.01 - Improve the accuracy of patient identification -- NPSG.01 directs hospitals to improve patient-identification accuracy by using at least two distinct identifiers, such as name and date of birth, before any care, treatment, or documentation. The goal is to prevent misidentification errors that can lead to wrong-patient procedures, medication mistakes, or incorrect test results, thereby enhancing overall patient safety.
[npsg_02] NPSG.02 - Improve the effectiveness of communication -- NPSG.02 mandates that hospitals develop and implement systematic processes to improve the effectiveness of communication among caregivers, patients and families. It requires clear policies, standardized tools such as SBAR for handoffs, regular staff training and ongoing measurement of communication outcomes to reduce errors and enhance patient safety.
[npsg_03] NPSG.03 - Improve the safety of using medications -- NPSG.03 requires hospitals to implement a comprehensive medication safety program that includes standardized prescribing, verification, labeling, administration, patient education, and systematic reporting and analysis of medication errors. The standard aims to reduce medication-related harm by ensuring safe practices throughout the medication-use process.
[npsg_06] NPSG.06 - Reduce the harm associated with clinical alarm systems -- The NPSG.06 standard requires hospitals to identify and mitigate risks posed by clinical alarm systems, ensuring that alarms are appropriately set, maintained, and responded to. Organizations must develop policies, conduct regular staff education, monitor alarm performance, and implement processes that reduce unnecessary or false alarms while preserving patient safety.
[npsg_07] NPSG.07 - Reduce the risk of health care-associated infections -- The Joint Commission's NPSG.07 standard requires hospitals to implement evidence-based practices that prevent health-care-associated infections, including hand hygiene, environmental cleaning, sterilization, and antimicrobial stewardship, and to measure and monitor infection rates in order to drive continuous improvement.
[npsg_15] NPSG.15 - Identify safety risks inherent in the patient population (suicide) -- NPSG.15 is a National Patient Safety Goal that obliges hospitals to systematically identify patients at risk of suicide, conduct standardized risk assessments, and implement protective interventions such as safety planning and staff education to mitigate self-harm. Compliance requires documented protocols, staff competency verification, and ongoing monitoring of outcomes.
[npsg_16] NPSG.16 - Improve the safety of clinical alarm systems -- NPSG.16 mandates that hospitals create policies and procedures to improve the safety of clinical alarm systems, ensuring alarms are set appropriately, devices are maintained, staff receive regular education, and timely-response to critical alerts
[pc] PC - Provision of Care, Treatment, and Services -- PC covers the Joint Commission's requirements for hospitals to ensure safe, effective, patient-centered care across all clinical
[pc_01] PC.01 - Assessment of Patients -- The PC.01 standard requires hospitals to perform a complete assessment of each patient at admission, during the stay, and at discharge, documenting physical, psychosocial, and functional status. It mandates that assessments be timely, based on evidence-based criteria, and used to develop individualized care plans that guide treatment and monitor progress. Compliance is verified through chart reviews and staff interviews.
[pc_02] PC.02 - Planning Care -- PC.02 requires hospitals to develop a comprehensive plan of care for each patient based on a complete assessment, incorporating the patient's needs, goals and preferences. The plan must involve an interdisciplinary team, be documented clearly, and be updated as the patient's condition changes to ensure safe, coordinated delivery of services.
[pi] PI - Performance Improvement -- The Joint Commission's PI (Performance Improvement) standard requires hospitals to establish systematic processes for monitoring, evaluating, and enhancing patient care and safety. Organizations must collect data, analyze outcomes, implement evidence-based changes, and track results to demonstrate continual quality improvement and compliance.
[rc] RC - Record of Care, Treatment, and Services -- RC requires hospitals to create and maintain complete, accurate records of each patient's care, treatment and services, documenting all clinical and ancillary activities in a timely, legible manner. The standard also mandates proper storage, confidentiality and easy access for authorized staff to support continuity of care, quality monitoring and regulatory reporting.
[ri] RI - Rights and Responsibilities of the Individual -- The RI standard defines the rights patients are entitled to receive-such as respectful treatment, privacy, informed consent and access to their medical records-and outlines their responsibilities, including providing accurate information and cooperating with care plans, to promote safe and ethical hospital care.
[ts] TS - Transplant Safety -- TS - Transplant Safety outlines the Joint Commission's mandatory standards for hospitals that perform organ transplantation, detailing requirements for donor and recipient screening, organ procurement, surgical procedures, infection control, post-operative monitoring, and reporting of adverse events to ensure safe and compliant transplant practices.
[waived] WT - Waived Testing -- In the Joint Commission's hospital accreditation standards, "WT - Waived Testing" designates laboratory examinations that are classified as waived under CLIA. These tests are considered simple, low-risk procedures that may be performed at the point of care with minimal regulatory oversight, allowing staff to conduct them without the full laboratory compliance requirements.
