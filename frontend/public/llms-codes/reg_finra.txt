[conduct] Conduct Rules -- The FINRA Conduct Rules are a set of regulations that govern the ethical and professional behavior of brokerage firms and their registered representatives. They establish standards for honesty, fairness, and the protection of investors, covering topics such as communications, trade execution, conflict of interest, and disciplinary procedures. Non-compliance can result in fines, sanctions, or suspension of securities licenses.
[customer_protection] Customer Protection -- The customer_protection entry in the FINRA Rulebook sets out the obligations of broker-dealers to safeguard client assets, including the segregation of customer securities and funds from firm property and the prompt return of those assets upon request. It also specifies record-keeping, reporting, and net-capital requirements intended to prevent misuse or loss of client property.
[market_integrity] Market Integrity -- The FINRA Rulebook entry "market_integrity" sets standards and requirements designed to preserve fair, orderly, and transparent trading environments. It outlines prohibitions against manipulative, deceptive, or abusive practices, mandates reporting of market-abnormal activity, and provides guidance for surveillance and disciplinary actions to protect investors and uphold confidence in securities markets.
[registration] Registration and Qualification -- The FINRA rulebook entry "registration" defines the procedures and standards that broker-dealers and associated persons must meet to become and remain members of FINRA. It outlines required filings, background checks, examinations, and ongoing qualifications such as continuing education and compliance with capital and supervisory requirements.
[rule_1010] Rule 1010 Series - Membership Application Procedures -- Rule 1010 Series outlines the procedures FINRA members must follow when applying for membership, including required documentation, background checks, and submission timelines. It details the review process by the regulator, criteria for approval or denial, and the obligations of applicants to disclose pertinent information.
[rule_1122] Rule 1122 - Filing of Misleading Information -- Rule 1122 prohibits FINRA members and associated persons from creating, submitting, or causing the filing of any false, inaccurate, or misleading information in regulatory filings or disclosures. Violations can result in disciplinary action, including fines, suspension, or revocation of membership, to protect market integrity and investor confidence.
[rule_1240] Rule 1240 - Continuing Education Requirements -- Rule 1240 sets the continuing education standards that FINRA-member broker-dealers must meet, requiring periodic training on regulatory, ethical and product-specific topics. The rule outlines the frequency of mandatory core and supplemental modules, specifies record-keeping obligations, and establishes deadlines for completion to ensure ongoing competency and compliance.
[rule_2010] Rule 2010 - Standards of Commercial Honor and Principles of Trade -- Rule 2010 in the FINRA Rulebook sets the ethical framework for member firms, outlining expectations for honesty, fairness and integrity in all business dealings. It requires adherence to recognized commercial honor standards, prohibits deceptive practices, and obligates firms to act in the best interests of clients and the market. Non-compliance can result in disciplinary action by FINRA.
[rule_2020] Rule 2020 - Use of Manipulative, Deceptive or Other Fraudulent Devices -- Rule 2020 of the FINRA Rulebook bars members from employing any manipulative, deceptive or fraudulent devices, schemes or artful contrivances in connection with securities transactions. The rule defines prohibited practices such as price manipulation, false statements, and schemes that mislead investors, and sets forth disciplinary measures for violations. It is a core safeguard intended to preserve market integrity and protect investors from fraud.
[rule_2090] Rule 2090 - Know Your Customer -- FINRA Rule 2090 sets the "Know Your Customer" standards that broker-dealers must follow, requiring firms to obtain and verify essential identifying information for each client, assess the client's investment profile, and maintain accurate records to help prevent fraud, money-laundering, and unsuitable transactions. The rule establishes procedures for ongoing monitoring and updating of client data throughout the relationship.
[rule_2111] Rule 2111 - Suitability -- Rule 2111, the Suitability rule, obliges FINRA member firms and their associated persons to have a reasonable basis for recommending a security, ensuring the recommendation aligns with the client's financial situation, investment objectives, and risk tolerance, and that the investment is suitable for the client's needs.
[rule_2121] Rule 2121 - Fair Prices and Commissions -- Rule 2121, part of the FINRA Rulebook, requires member firms to charge customers fair and reasonable commissions, prohibiting excessive or discriminatory pricing. It also mandates that firms execute orders in a manner that seeks the best possible price for the client, consistent with prevailing market conditions.
[rule_2150] Rule 2150 - Improper Use of Customers' Securities or Funds -- Rule 2150, part of the FINRA Rulebook, prohibits a broker or associated person from using, or having unauthorized access to, any customer's securities or funds for personal benefit, including the settlement of personal obligations or the purchase of personal assets. The rule mandates strict segregation of client assets from firm property and requires immediate restitution if misuse occurs, with violations subject to disciplinary action by FINRA.
[rule_2210] Rule 2210 - Communications with the Public -- Rule 2210, part of the FINRA Rulebook, governs how member firms may communicate with the public, setting standards for content, approval, recordkeeping and supervision to ensure fairness and accuracy. It applies to advertisements, sales literature and other promotional materials, requiring that communications be based on reliable data and not be misleading.
[rule_3110] Rule 3110 - Supervision -- Rule 3110 establishes the supervisory framework that broker-dealers must maintain, requiring a written supervisory system, designated supervisors for each function, and ongoing monitoring of personnel and activities to ensure compliance with securities laws and FINRA rules. It also mandates periodic reviews, documentation of supervisory actions, and prompt remediation of identified deficiencies.
[rule_3120] Rule 3120 - Supervisory Control System -- Rule 3120 requires FINRA members to maintain a supervisory control system that monitors and ensures compliance with regulatory requirements, enabling timely detection and correction of violations. The rule outlines chief compliance officer responsibilities, mandates periodic reviews, and specifies documentation and record-keeping standards.
[rule_3130] Rule 3130 - Annual Certification of Compliance -- FINRA Rule 3130 mandates that every broker-dealer submit an annual certification confirming that its operations and supervisory systems are in full compliance with all applicable FINRA rules and regulations, including those governing trade reporting, communications and market conduct. The certification must be signed by a senior officer and is subject to review and possible audit by FINRA staff.
[rule_3310] Rule 3310 - Anti-Money Laundering Compliance Program -- Rule 3310 requires FINRA member firms to establish and maintain an anti-money laundering compliance program that includes written policies, a designated compliance officer, ongoing employee training, and independent testing to detect and prevent illicit financial activity.
[rule_4210] Rule 4210 - Margin Requirements -- Rule 4210 sets the minimum collateral that members must maintain when extending credit to customers, outlining permissible margin calculations and required equity levels. It establishes how firms calculate and enforce margin on securities positions to protect investors and ensure market stability.
[rule_4370] Rule 4370 - Business Continuity Plans -- Rule 4370 requires FINRA member firms to develop, maintain, and test comprehensive business continuity plans to ensure critical operations can continue during disruptions. The rule outlines requirements for risk assessment, recovery strategies, communication protocols, and periodic review to protect investors and market integrity.
[rule_4512] Rule 4512 - Customer Account Information -- Rule 4512 of the FINRA Rulebook establishes requirements for how member firms collect, retain, and safeguard customer account information. It mandates identity verification, record-keeping for a prescribed period, and protection of data from unauthorized access to ensure regulatory and privacy compliance.
[rule_4530] Rule 4530 - Reporting Requirements -- Rule 4530 outlines FINRA's mandatory reporting obligations for member firms and associated persons, requiring timely disclosure of disciplinary actions, criminal convictions, bankruptcies, civil judgments, and other material events that could affect market integrity. The rule specifies the form, content, and filing deadlines for each type of report to ensure regulatory oversight and investor protection.
[rule_5110] Rule 5110 - Corporate Financing Rule (Underwriting) -- Rule 5110, the Corporate Financing Rule, sets FINRA's standards for underwriting activities by broker-dealers, outlining eligibility, disclosure, and financial responsibility requirements for securities offerings. It governs how firms conduct due diligence, manage conflicts of interest, and ensure compliance with capital formation regulations.
[rule_5130] Rule 5130 - Restrictions on Spinning and Flipping of IPOs -- Rule 5130 prohibits FINRA members from engaging in or facilitating "spinning," where a firm allocates IPO shares to a client in exchange for future business, and from "flipping," the immediate resale of those shares for profit. The rule is designed to protect investors by ensuring that IPO allocations are based on merit rather than undisclosed arrangements.
[rule_5210] Rule 5210 - Publication of Transactions and Quotations -- FINRA Rule 5210 obligates member firms to publicly disclose their customers' transaction and quotation information, ensuring that real-time best-bid-and-offer data and trade details are accessible to the market. The rule promotes transparency, supports price formation, and helps regulators monitor trading activity and compliance.
[rule_5310] Rule 5310 - Best Execution and Interpositioning -- Rule 5310 requires FINRA member firms to obtain the most favorable terms for customer orders, considering price, speed, and likelihood of execution, and to disclose any interpositioning practices where the firm places its own orders ahead of client orders. It outlines the standards for monitoring, reporting, and documenting best-execution compliance, and mandates that firms maintain policies to prevent conflicts of interest that could disadvantage customers.
[rule_6100] Rule 6100 Series - Quoting and Trading in NMS Stocks -- Rule 6100 series outlines the obligations of FINRA members when quoting and trading NMS (National Market System) stocks, requiring firm quotes, best-execution practices, and adherence to market-data rules. It also establishes standards for order handling, price priority and trade reporting to ensure a fair and orderly market.
[supervision] Supervision and Compliance -- The FINRA Rulebook entry "Supervision and Compliance" sets out broker-dealer obligations to establish and maintain supervisory systems that monitor employee activities, enforce written procedures, and ensure adherence to securities laws and FINRA rules. It details supervisory responsibilities, reporting requirements and remedial actions to prevent violations and protect investors.
