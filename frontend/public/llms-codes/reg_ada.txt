[t1_reasonable] Reasonable Accommodation Requirements -- Under the ADA, employers, public entities, and service providers must make reasonable accommodations to enable qualified individuals with disabilities to perform essential job functions or access services, unless doing so would cause undue hardship. Accommodations can include modifications to policies, physical workspace changes, or provision of auxiliary aids.
[t1_sec101] Sec 101 - Definitions (Disability, Qualified Individual) -- Section 101 of the Americans with Disabilities Act defines "disability" as a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities, a record of such impairment, or being regarded as having it, and defines a "qualified individual" as a person with a disability who meets job standards and can perform essential functions with or without reasonable accommodation. This section establishes the core terms used throughout the ADA.
[t1_sec102] Sec 102 - Discrimination Prohibited -- Section 102 of the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 outlines that it is unlawful for public entities, public accommodations, and private employers to discriminate against individuals with disabilities in employment, services, programs, and access. The provision mandates equal opportunity and requires reasonable accommodations, prohibiting exclusion, segregation, or denial of benefits based on disability.
[t1_sec103] Sec 103 - Defenses (Undue Hardship, Direct Threat) -- This section outlines defenses to ADA compliance claims, specifying that an employer is not required to make accommodations that would cause undue hardship, defined as a significant difficulty or expense, and may refuse accommodation if the individual's condition poses a direct threat to health or safety of themselves or others.
[t1_sec104] Sec 104 - Illegal Use of Drugs and Alcohol -- Section 104 of the Americans with Disabilities Act clarifies that the law does not extend protection to persons who are currently using illegal drugs or alcohol, treating such conduct as a disqualifying factor for disability status and related employment accommodations.
[t1_sec105] Sec 105 - Posting Notices -- Section 105 of the Americans with Disabilities Act obligates public entities to post conspicuous notices that inform individuals of their rights under the ADA, describe the grievance procedures, and provide contact information for the enforcing agency. The notices must be displayed at each public facility and, where appropriate, on the entity's website.
[t1_sec106] Sec 106 - Regulations (EEOC) -- Section 106 authorizes the EEOC to issue regulations that implement the ADA's employment provisions, defining prohibited discrimination, outlining employer responsibilities, and specifying requirements for reasonable accommodations and accessibility. These rules guide covered employers in complying with the act's civil rights protections.
[t1_sec107] Sec 107 - Enforcement (Remedies and Procedures) -- Section 107 details the enforcement framework of the Americans with Disabilities Act, granting individuals the right to file civil actions against violators and authorizing courts to issue injunctive relief, damages, and attorneys' fees. It also sets procedural rules for filing complaints, jurisdiction, and the issuance of subpoenas and discovery.
[t2_sec201] Sec 201 - Definition of Qualified Individual -- Section 201 of the ADA provides the definition of "qualified individual," referring to a person with a disability who, with or without reasonable accommodation, can perform the essential functions of a job and meet the employer's legitimate job-related requirements. This definition guides employment discrimination assessments.
[t2_sec202] Sec 202 - Discrimination Prohibited -- Section 202 of the Americans with Disabilities Act makes it unlawful for state and local governments to discriminate against individuals with disabilities in the full and equal enjoyment of public services, programs, and activities. It requires public entities to provide reasonable modifications, effective communication, and accessible facilities, applying to all programs regardless of where they are provided.
[t2_sec203] Sec 203 - Enforcement -- Section 203 sets out the enforcement provisions of the Americans with Disabilities Act, granting the Department of Justice authority to investigate complaints, bring civil actions and impose penalties for violations. It also permits individuals to file private lawsuits, seek injunctive relief, compensatory damages and attorneys' fees.
[t2_sec204] Sec 204 - Regulations (DOJ) -- Section 204 contains the Department of Justice regulations that implement the ADA, setting technical standards for accessibility of public accommodations, transportation, and communications and outlining enforcement procedures and compliance guidance.
[t2_sub_a] Subtitle A - General Prohibitions Against Discrimination -- This entry covers Subtitle A of the Americans with Disabilities Act, which establishes the broad prohibition on discrimination against individuals with disabilities in employment, public services, public accommodations and telecommunications, setting nationwide civil rights standards.
[t2_sub_b] Subtitle B - Public Transportation (Non-Rail) -- t2_sub_b refers to Subtitle B of the Americans with Disabilities Act, which governs public transportation that does not involve rail, such as buses, vans, and demand-response services. The provision requires these services to be accessible to individuals with disabilities, mandates complementary paratransit options where fixed-route service cannot be made readily accessible, and establishes standards for equipment, boarding, and service policies.
[t3_sec301] Sec 301 - Definitions (Public Accommodation, Commercial Facility) -- Section 301 of the Americans with Disabilities Act provides the foundational definitions for "public accommodation" and "commercial facility," outlining the types of private entities and places of business to which the ADA's nondiscrimination requirements apply. It specifies the categories of goods, services, and facilities covered, establishing the scope of the law's accessibility obligations.
[t3_sec302] Sec 302 - Prohibition of Discrimination by Public Accommodations -- Section 302 of the Americans with Disabilities Act bars public accommodations-from hotels and restaurants to retail stores and theaters-from denying services, imposing different terms, or otherwise discriminating against individuals because of a disability, requiring reasonable modifications and accessibility unless it would cause undue hardship.
[t3_sec303] Sec 303 - New Construction and Alterations -- Section 303 of the Americans with Disabilities Act requires that all newly constructed facilities and any alterations to existing ones be designed and built to be readily accessible to and usable by individuals with disabilities, unless it would impose undue burden or fundamental alteration of the program. This mandate applies to architectural features, utilities, and services, ensuring compliance with ADA accessibility standards for new construction and renovation projects.

[t3_sec308] Sec 308 - Enforcement (Private Actions, AG Actions) -- Section 308 of the Americans with Disabilities Act sets out the enforcement framework, permitting private individuals to bring lawsuits for violations and authorizing the Attorney General to pursue actions on behalf of the public. It specifies procedural rules, available remedies, and the Department of Justice's role in ensuring compliance.
[t4_sec401] Sec 401 - Telecommunications Relay Services -- Section 401 of the Americans with Disabilities Act creates a nationwide mandate for telecommunications relay services, requiring telephone providers to ensure that people who are deaf, hard-of-hearing, or have speech disabilities can place and receive calls through relay operators. The provision also directs the FCC to establish technical standards and funding mechanisms to support these services.
[t4_sec402] Sec 402 - Closed Captioning -- Section 402 of the Americans with Disabilities Act requires that television programs broadcast, cable or satellite provide closed captioning to make the content accessible to individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing. The rule applies to new programming and mandates captioning for pre-recorded content, with limited exceptions for live broadcasts.
[t5_adaaa] ADA Amendments Act of 2008 (ADAAA) Conforming Changes -- The entry documents the conforming amendments made to the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 to bring it into compliance with the ADA Amendments Act of 2008. It outlines revisions to definitions of disability, major life activities, and employer obligations, clarifying coverage and enforcement.
[t5_sec501] Sec 501 - Retaliation and Coercion Prohibited -- Section 501 of the Americans with Disabilities Act prohibits any employer, labor organization, or employment agency from retaliating against a person who files a discrimination complaint, participates in an investigation, or opposes discriminatory practices, and bars coercion to force such individuals to abandon or settle their claims. This safeguards the rights of people with disabilities to pursue legal remedies without fear of reprisal.

[t5_sec507] Sec 507 - Relationship to Other Laws -- Section 507 outlines how the ADA relates to other statutes, stating that the act does not supersede or diminish any existing federal, state, or local law unless the provisions conflict, and that agencies may interpret the ADA in conjunction with other civil-rights legislation.
[t5_sec508] Sec 508 - Transvestism Not Covered -- The entry notes that Section 508 of the Americans with Disabilities Act deals with accessibility requirements for electronic and information technology, and it expressly does not provide coverage or protections for transvestism or related gender-identity issues.
[title_1] Title I - Employment -- Title I of the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 prohibits employment discrimination against qualified individuals with disabilities, requiring employers with 15 or more employees to provide reasonable accommodations and to ensure equal opportunity in hiring, promotion, pay, and other job-related activities.
[title_2] Title II - Public Services (State and Local Government) -- Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act obligates state and local governments to give people with disabilities equal access to all public programs, services and activities. It bars discrimination and requires reasonable modifications, auxiliary aids and accessible facilities so that individuals can fully participate.
[title_3] Title III - Public Accommodations and Commercial Facilities -- Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act, codified at 42 USC 12101 et seq., prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities in places of public accommodation and in commercial facilities that are open to the public. It requires accessible design, reasonable modifications, and effective communication for services, goods, and programs offered to the public.
[title_4] Title IV - Telecommunications -- Title IV of the Americans with Disabilities Act obliges telecommunications carriers and providers to ensure that people with disabilities have equal access to phone services, including the provision of relay and captioned telephone services, and to remove barriers that impede effective communication.
[title_5] Title V - Miscellaneous Provisions -- Title V of the Americans with Disabilities Act gathers assorted provisions that define terms, outline enforcement procedures, and establish relationships with other civil rights statutes. It includes rules on state and local government obligations, attorney fees, and the Department of Justice's authority to enforce the act.
