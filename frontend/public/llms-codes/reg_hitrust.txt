[cat_0] Category 0 - Information Security Management Program -- Category 0 establishes the overarching information security management program, defining governance structures, policies, and procedures that guide the organization's security efforts. It includes risk assessment processes, roles and responsibilities, and continuous monitoring to ensure alignment with regulatory requirements and business objectives. This foundational category sets the strategic direction for implementing and maintaining a robust security posture.
[cat_1] Category 1 - Access Control -- Category 1 - Access Control defines the policies, procedures and technical safeguards that limit system and data access to authorized users, enforce authentication, manage privileges and monitor access activities to protect confidentiality, integrity and availability.
[cat_10] Category 10 - Information Systems Acquisition, Development, and Maintenance -- Category 10 addresses the security requirements for acquiring, developing, and maintaining information systems, ensuring that risk assessments, secure design, coding standards, testing, change management, and vendor oversight are applied throughout the system lifecycle.
[cat_11] Category 11 - Information Security Incident Management -- Category 11 defines the controls and procedures needed to detect, contain, investigate, and remediate information security incidents. It requires documented incident response plans, designated roles, communication protocols, timely reporting, and post-incident analysis to improve defenses and meet regulatory obligations.
[cat_12] Category 12 - Business Continuity Management -- Category 12 - Business Continuity Management requires organizations to develop, implement, and maintain plans that ensure critical functions can continue or be restored after disruptive events, including risk assessments, incident response, and regular testing of recovery procedures.
[cat_13] Category 13 - Privacy Practices -- Category 13 addresses privacy practices, requiring organizations to establish and maintain policies and procedures that protect personally identifiable information throughout its lifecycle. It covers consent management, data-subject rights, minimisation, purpose limitation, retention schedules, and mechanisms for responding to privacy incidents, ensuring compliance with applicable privacy regulations and standards.
[cat_2] Category 2 - Human Resources Security -- Category 2 deals with Human Resources Security, defining controls that protect the organization through the employee lifecycle. It requires background screening, confidentiality agreements, security awareness training, defined onboarding and off-boarding processes, and the management of user access rights aligned with job responsibilities.
[cat_3] Category 3 - Risk Management -- Category 3 in the HITRUST Common Security Framework v11 defines the organization's risk-management program, requiring the identification, assessment, treatment and continuous monitoring of information-security risks. It calls for documented policies, regular risk analyses, mitigation plans aligned with business objectives, and oversight by senior leadership to ensure risks are appropriately prioritized and addressed.
[cat_4] Category 4 - Security Policy -- Category 4 defines the organization's security policy framework, requiring the creation, approval, dissemination and regular review of written policies, procedures and guidelines that govern the protection of information assets. It ensures that security objectives are aligned with legal, regulatory and contractual obligations, and that responsibilities, enforcement mechanisms and compliance monitoring are clearly articulated.
[cat_5] Category 5 - Organization of Information Security -- Category 5 of the HITRUST CSF v11 addresses the organization of information security, defining the governance structure, policies, and procedures that guide a program's overall direction. It outlines responsibilities for security leadership, risk management processes, and the coordination of resources needed to protect data and ensure compliance.
[cat_6] Category 6 - Compliance -- Category 6 in the HITRUST Common Security Framework v11 focuses on compliance requirements, guiding organizations to identify, adopt, and maintain applicable laws, regulations, contracts and internal policies. It includes processes for assessing obligations, documenting controls, monitoring changes, and reporting alignment with external and internal compliance mandates. This category ensures that security practices remain consistent with legal and contractual expectations.

It includes requirements for facility access controls, surveillance, environmental monitoring, and procedures that maintain the availability and integrity of hardware and data-center environments.

[obj_01_a] 01.a - Access Control Policy -- The entry requires an organization to create, document and maintain an Access Control Policy that governs how access to information systems and data is authorized, managed and reviewed. The policy must align with regulatory and business requirements, be communicated to personnel, and be periodically evaluated for effectiveness.
[obj_01_b] 01.b - User Registration -- The HITRUST CSF v11 control 01.b requires an organization to establish a formal user-registration process that verifies the identity of individuals before granting access, assigns each user a unique identifier, records pertinent registration details, and periodically reviews accounts to ensure only authorized users retain access.
[obj_01_d] 01.d - User Password Management -- The obj_01_d control in HITRUST CSF v11 specifies that organizations must implement formal user password management processes, including requirements for password complexity, minimum length, periodic change, secure storage (e.g., hashing), and protection against reuse or guessing, while ensuring administrative oversight and auditability of password-related activities.
[obj_01_j] 01.j - Access Control to Program Source Code -- The entry mandates that access to program source code be restricted to authorized individuals only, requiring authentication, role-based permissions and documented approval processes. It calls for logging of all access attempts, regular review of access rights, and safeguards to prevent unauthorized viewing, modification or distribution of the code.
[obj_01_v] 01.v - Information Access Restriction -- In HITRUST CSF version 11, object 01.v defines the requirement to limit access to sensitive information to authorized individuals only, ensuring that users are granted permissions based on need-to-know and role. It mandates controls for authentication, authorization and periodic review of access rights to protect data confidentiality.
[obj_06_d] 06.d - Data Protection and Privacy -- The 06.d control in the HITRUST Common Security Framework v11 mandates that organizations implement comprehensive safeguards for the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal and sensitive information throughout its lifecycle, including collection, storage, processing, transmission, and disposal. It requires policies, procedures, and technical measures such as encryption, access controls, and privacy impact assessments to ensure data is protected and privacy rights are upheld.
[obj_09_aa] 09.aa - Audit Logging -- The Audit Logging control requires organizations to create and protect tamper-resistant logs that record security-relevant events such as user activity, system changes, and access to protected data, with accurate timestamps and source information. Logs must be retained for a defined period, stored securely, and regularly reviewed to support incident detection, investigation, and compliance reporting.
[obj_09_ab] 09.ab - Monitoring System Use -- This control requires organizations to continuously monitor how their information systems are used, collecting logs of user activity, access attempts and changes to configurations. It mandates regular review of these logs to detect unauthorized or abnormal behavior and to support incident response and compliance reporting.
[obj_09_m] 09.m - Network Controls -- Network Controls require organizations to implement technical and administrative safeguards that protect the internal and external network infrastructure, such as firewalls, intrusion detection/prevention systems, segmentation, secure configurations, and continuous monitoring. These measures help ensure only authorized traffic flows, detect anomalies, and reduce the risk of unauthorized access or data compromise.
[obj_10_a] 10.a - Security Requirements Analysis and Specification -- Object 10.a in HITRUST CSF v11 requires organizations to perform a thorough analysis of security requirements for each system or application and to document those requirements in a formal specification. The analysis must consider applicable laws, regulations, contractual obligations and risk assessments, ensuring that security controls are defined, justified and aligned with business objectives.
[obj_10_h] 10.h - Control of Technical Vulnerabilities -- Control 10.h requires organizations to identify, assess, and remediate technical vulnerabilities in their information systems promptly. It mandates regular scanning, patch management, and tracking of remediation activities to reduce the risk of exploitation.
[obj_11_a] 11.a - Reporting Information Security Events -- The 11.a control requires the organization to implement a formal process for identifying, documenting, and promptly reporting information security events to designated internal and external stakeholders. It specifies defined reporting procedures, escalation paths, and timelines to ensure timely response and compliance with legal or regulatory obligations.
[obj_11_c] 11.c - Responsibilities and Procedures -- In HITRUST CSF version 11, obj_11_c outlines the need for clearly defined security responsibilities and documented procedures, requiring organizations to assign roles, describe duties, and establish consistent processes for implementing and maintaining controls, thereby ensuring accountability and effective protection of information.
