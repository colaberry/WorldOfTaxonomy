[US] USC Titles -- The US entry refers to the United States Code Title Index, which lists the 54 titles that organize the permanent federal statutes. It serves as a reference guide for locating laws by subject area within the codified U.S. law.
[US.01] Title 1: General Provisions -- US.01 denotes Title 1 of the United States Code, the foundational "General Provisions" section. It establishes basic definitions, outlines the structure and authority of the federal government, and sets forth rules governing the creation, publication, and amendment of federal statutes.
[US.05] Title 5: Government Organization -- Title 5 of the United States Code, indexed as US.05, sets out the organization, powers, and duties of the federal government. It details the structure of executive agencies, the civil service system, and the rules governing administrative procedures and personnel management.
[US.07] Title 7: Agriculture -- US.07 refers to Title 7 of the United States Code, which consolidates federal statutes dealing with agriculture. It covers laws on farming, irrigation, crop insurance, agricultural research, and related programs administered by the Department of Agriculture, establishing the legal framework for food production, land use, and rural development.
[US.10] Title 10: Armed Forces -- Title 10 of the United States Code outlines the legal framework for the organization, administration and operation of the U.S. Armed Forces, including the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps and Coast Guard, and addresses matters such as personnel, procurement, training, and military justice.
[US.11] Title 11: Bankruptcy -- Title 11 of the United States Code governs bankruptcy law, outlining procedures for individuals and businesses to seek relief from debts, including Chapter 7 liquidation, Chapter 11 reorganization, and Chapter 13 wage earner plans. It details the powers of bankruptcy courts, the rights of creditors, and the discharge of obligations, providing a legal framework for orderly debt resolution.
[US.12] Title 12: Banks and Banking -- US.12 refers to Title 12 of the United States Code, which consolidates federal statutes governing banks, banking operations, and related financial institutions. It covers the regulation of national and state banks, the Federal Reserve System, credit unions, and consumer financial protections, providing the legal framework for supervisory authority, monetary policy implementation, and the enforcement of banking standards.
[US.15] Title 15: Commerce and Trade -- US.15 refers to Title 15 of the United States Code, which compiles federal statutes governing commerce and trade. It includes laws on interstate and international commerce, banking, securities, consumer protection, and related regulatory programs, providing the legal framework for economic activity and market oversight.

[US.21] Title 21: Food and Drugs -- Title 21 of the United States Code sets out the federal legal framework for food safety, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, cosmetics and related products. It incorporates the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, standards for labeling and manufacturing, and grants enforcement authority to the FDA.
[US.26] Title 26: Internal Revenue Code -- Title 26 of the United States Code, commonly known as the Internal Revenue Code, contains the federal tax laws governing income, estate, gift, employment, and excise taxes, as well as procedures for tax administration, enforcement, and penalties. It outlines the authority of the Internal Revenue Service, defines taxable entities and activities, and establishes rules for filing returns, claiming deductions, credits, and refunds.
[US.28] Title 28: Judiciary -- Title 28 of the United States Code governs the federal judiciary, detailing the structure, jurisdiction and powers of the Supreme Court, lower federal courts and related agencies. It also contains procedural rules, judicial administration provisions and statutes on bankruptcy, civil and criminal proceedings.
[US.29] Title 29: Labor -- The United States Code Title 29 encompasses federal statutes governing labor relations, employment standards, and workplace safety. It includes provisions such as the Fair Labor Standards Act, collective bargaining rights, unemployment insurance, and occupational health regulations, forming the primary legal framework for workers' rights and employer obligations.
[US.33] Title 33: Navigation -- Title 33 of the United States Code compiles federal statutes that regulate navigation, waterways, and related maritime activities. It includes provisions for the operation and maintenance of navigation aids, the authority of the Coast Guard, safety standards for vessels, and the management of ports and harbors.
[US.35] Title 35: Patents -- Title 35 of the United States Code governs patents, outlining the legal framework for obtaining, enforcing, and protecting patent rights. It covers patentability criteria, application procedures, inventor rights, licensing, infringement remedies, and the role of the United States Patent and Trademark Office.
[US.38] Title 38: Veterans Benefits -- Title 38 of the United States Code consolidates federal statutes that provide benefits to former members of the armed forces and their families. It covers compensation, pensions, health care, education assistance, vocational training, and other support programs administered primarily by the Department of Veterans Affairs.
[US.42] Title 42: Public Health/Welfare -- US.42 refers to Title 42 of the United States Code, which consolidates federal statutes governing public health, social welfare, and related programs. The title includes laws on disease control, social security, civil rights in health services, and funding for medical research and community assistance. It serves as the primary legal framework for nationwide health and welfare policy.
[US.44] Title 44: Public Printing -- Title 44 of the United States Code sets out the laws governing federal printing and publishing, defining the duties of the Government Publishing Office and related agencies, and establishing standards for the production, distribution, and preservation of official government documents.
[US.47] Title 47: Telecommunications -- United States Code Title 47 governs federal telecommunications law, covering radio, television, satellite, and wire communications. It establishes the authority of the Federal Communications Commission, sets rules for spectrum allocation, licensing, and broadcasting standards, and includes provisions on privacy, wiretapping, and consumer protections in the communications sector.
[US.49] Title 49: Transportation -- Title 49 of the United States Code contains the federal statutes governing transportation, covering highways, railroads, aviation, shipping, and related safety and regulatory programs. It establishes the legal framework for agencies such as the Department of Transportation and the Federal Aviation Administration, prescribing standards for infrastructure, commerce, and environmental compliance across all modes of transport. This title is regularly updated to reflect new legislation and policy changes.
[US.50] Title 50: War and National Defense -- Title 50 of the United States Code contains the federal statutes governing war, national defense, and related matters. It outlines the organization, powers, and duties of the armed forces, defense procurement, intelligence, and emergency powers, providing the legal framework for military and security operations.
[US.52] Title 52: Voting and Elections -- Title 52 of the United States Code governs federal voting and election laws, covering the administration of elections, voter registration, campaign finance, and protections against discrimination. It consolidates statutes related to the conduct of federal elections, the role of the Election Assistance Commission, and the enforcement of the Help America Vote Act. The title also outlines procedures for absentee voting and the handling of contested elections.
[US.54] Title 54: National Park Service -- Title 54 of the United States Code, identified as US.54, establishes the legal framework for the National Park Service. It outlines the agency's organization, powers, and duties, including the preservation of historic and natural resources, management of national parks and monuments, and provisions for funding, staffing, and cooperation with states and private entities.
