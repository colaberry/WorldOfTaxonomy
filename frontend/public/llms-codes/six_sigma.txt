[SS] Six Sigma -- The SS code identifies the Six Sigma methodology, a data-driven approach for process improvement that uses the DMAIC cycle
[SS.01] DMAIC: Define -- The Define stage of DMAIC establishes the problem statement, project objectives, and scope, identifies key stakeholders and customers, and documents the Voice of the Customer and critical metrics. It creates a high-level process map and a project charter to guide subsequent phases.
[SS.02] DMAIC: Measure -- In the DMAIC framework the Measure phase focuses on gathering data to quantify the current process performance, establishing baseline metrics and identifying sources of variation. It involves selecting key process variables, developing data collection plans, and using tools such as process mapping, measurement system analysis, and statistical charts to ensure reliable data for analysis.
[SS.03] DMAIC: Analyze -- The Analyze phase of DMAIC focuses on examining process data to pinpoint the root causes of defects and variation. It employs statistical tests, process mapping, and cause-and-effect analysis to validate hypotheses and prioritize improvement opportunities before moving to the Improve step.
[SS.04] DMAIC: Improve -- The Improve phase of DMAIC focuses on developing and testing solutions that address the root causes identified in the Analyze step. Teams generate potential improvements, select the most effective ones, and implement pilot experiments using tools such as design of experiments, mistake-proofing, and process redesign to achieve measurable performance gains.

[SS.06] DMADV: Define -- In the DMADV framework the Define phase establishes the project's purpose, scope, and customer requirements, translating them into measurable goals. It involves creating a project charter, identifying key stakeholders, and outlining high-level process maps to guide the subsequent design steps.
[SS.07] DMADV: Measure -- In the DMADV methodology the Measure phase focuses on defining critical-to-quality characteristics, gathering reliable data on current performance, and establishing baseline capability metrics that reflect customer requirements. This quantitative foundation guides the subsequent design steps and helps set target specifications.
[SS.08] DMADV: Analyze -- The SS.08 entry outlines the "Analyze" phase of the DMADV (Define-Measure-Analyze-Design-Verify) Six Sigma approach, focusing on identifying root causes of process variation and evaluating data to determine how design specifications can be met. It details statistical tools used to assess relationships among variables, prioritize critical factors, and develop a data-driven basis for subsequent design decisions.
[SS.09] DMADV: Design -- DMADV (Define-Measure-Analyze-Design-Verify) is the Six Sigma methodology used to create new processes, products, or services that meet customer requirements and performance goals. It guides teams through defining objectives, measuring critical factors, analyzing data, designing solutions, and verifying that the final design delivers the intended quality.
[SS.10] DMADV: Verify -- The Verify phase of DMADV, identified as SS.10, is the final step in the Six Sigma DMADV methodology where the completed design is tested and validated against customer requirements and performance specifications. It involves pilot production, statistical analysis, and confirmation that the product or process meets all intended goals before full-scale launch.

[SS.12] Belt Levels: Yellow -- The Yellow Belt is the introductory level in Six Sigma's DMAIC/DMADV methodology, providing basic awareness of process improvement concepts and terminology. Holders typically support data collection, assist in project documentation, and help identify simple inefficiencies under the guidance of higher-level belts. This level establishes foundational knowledge before advancing to Green or Black Belt certifications.
[SS.13] Belt Levels: Green -- The SS.13 entry outlines the Green Belt level in Six Sigma, where practitioners support DMAIC and DMADV projects by collecting data, performing basic statistical analysis, and assisting Black Belts in implementation. Green Belts typically lead small-scale improvement initiatives, document findings, and help sustain results, serving as a bridge between project teams and organizational leadership. This tier requires moderate training in Six Sigma tools and an understanding of project management fundamentals.
[SS.14] Belt Levels: Black -- The Black Belt is the highest professional level within Six Sigma's DMAIC and DMADV framework, signifying mastery of advanced statistical tools and project leadership. Black Belts direct complex improvement initiatives, mentor lower-level Belts, and integrate strategic objectives across the organization.
[SS.15] Belt Levels: Master Black -- The SS.15 entry outlines the Master Black Belt level within Six Sigma's DMAIC/DMADV framework, describing it as the highest certification that guides organizational strategy, oversees project selection, and mentors Black and Green Belts. Master Black Belts typically possess extensive statistical expertise and leadership experience.
