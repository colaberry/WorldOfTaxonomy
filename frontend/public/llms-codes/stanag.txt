[SG] STANAG Areas -- SG denotes the classification of STANAG Areas, which are geographical or functional zones defined in NATO standardization agreements for coordination of operations, training, and logistics. These areas are used to organize responsibilities and procedures across member nations.
[SG.01] Operations and Exercises -- SG.01 defines the standardized procedures, terminology and responsibilities for planning, conducting and evaluating NATO operations and exercises, ensuring joint-action interoperability among member forces. It establishes coordination mechanisms and documentation requirements to support combined training and mission execution.
[SG.02] Intelligence -- SG.02 designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category for Intelligence, encompassing doctrines, procedures and technical standards used to collect, analyze, process and disseminate information about adversary capabilities, intentions and activities in support of operational planning and decision-making.
[SG.03] Personnel -- SG.03 designates the Personnel category within NATO Standardization Agreements, covering all matters related to human resources, training, staffing, and personnel management across NATO forces. It provides standardized terminology, procedures, and guidelines to ensure consistent handling of personnel issues, from recruitment and career development to welfare and deployment, facilitating interoperability among member nations.
[SG.04] Logistics -- SG.04 designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category that addresses logistics, encompassing the planning, coordination, and execution of supply, transport, maintenance, and support functions needed to sustain military operations. It provides common procedures and terminology to ensure interoperability among member nations.
[SG.05] Communications and Information Systems -- SG.05 is the NATO Standardization Agreement (STANAG) category that defines standards and procedures for communications and information systems across allied forces. It covers the design, operation, and interoperability of radio, data-link, networking, and software tools to ensure secure, reliable exchange of information during joint missions.

[SG.07] Materiel Standardization -- SG.07 in the NATO Standardization Agreement categories deals with Materiel Standardization, defining the processes and requirements for harmonizing equipment, spare parts, and logistics across NATO forces. It establishes common specifications, testing procedures and procurement guidelines to ensure interoperability and efficient support among member nations.
[SG.08] Medical -- SG.08 - Medical designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category that defines common doctrines, procedures, and requirements for medical support in joint and combined operations, covering health service provision, casualty evacuation, medical logistics, and field treatment standards to ensure interoperable care across allied forces.
[SG.09] Training -- SG.09 designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category for Training, specifying the common policies, methods and requirements that member nations use to prepare personnel for joint operations. It covers doctrine, curriculum development, evaluation standards and the exchange of best practices to ensure interoperable skills across forces.
[SG.10] Land Operations -- SG.10 designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category that addresses land operations, encompassing the development, harmonization and dissemination of doctrine, tactics, techniques, procedures and equipment standards for ground forces. It facilitates interoperability among member nations' armies, ensuring coordinated planning, execution and support of combined-arms and joint ground missions.
[SG.11] Maritime Operations -- SG.11 designates the NATO Standardization Agreement category for Maritime Operations, encompassing the standardized doctrines, procedures, training requirements and equipment specifications used by naval and amphibious forces. It guides joint sea-based activities such as surface warfare, submarine operations, mine countermeasures and littoral support.

[SG.13] Joint Operations -- SG.13 is a NATO Standardization Agreement (STANAG) category that outlines standards for planning, executing, and coordinating joint operations involving multiple services or allied forces. It provides common terminology, procedures, and interoperability guidelines to ensure cohesive command and control across combined military efforts.
[SG.14] Cyber Defence -- SG.14 defines NATO's cyber-defence standards, outlining policies, technical measures and operational procedures to protect NATO information systems and networks from cyber threats. It provides guidance for risk assessment, intrusion detection, incident response, and resilience, ensuring interoperability and consistent protection across member nations.
