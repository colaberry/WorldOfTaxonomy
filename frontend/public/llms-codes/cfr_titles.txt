[CF.01] Title 1: General Provisions -- CF.01 in the CFR Title Index refers to Title 1, General Provisions, which provides the overarching regulatory framework, definitions, and administrative rules that apply across all CFR titles and outline agency responsibilities.
[CF.03] Title 3: The President -- The CFR Title 3, identified by entry code CF.03, contains the rules and regulations governing the Office of the President, including executive orders, presidential proclamations, and administrative procedures that affect federal agencies and national policy. It serves as the primary repository for presidential directives that have the force of law.
[CF.05] Title 5: Administrative Personnel -- Title 5 of the Code of Federal Regulations, indexed as CF.05, compiles the federal rules governing the civil service and personnel management of U.S. government agencies, covering employee hiring, classification, pay scales, benefits, workplace conduct, and disciplinary procedures.
[CF.07] Title 7: Agriculture -- CF.07 refers to Title 7 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which consolidates the federal rules governing agricultural programs, policies, and related administrative procedures for the United States. It includes regulations on farm subsidies, conservation, food safety, and the oversight of agencies such as the USDA. This title serves as the primary legal reference for agricultural regulatory matters.
[CF.10] Title 10: Energy -- CF.10 designates Title 10 of the Code of Federal Regulations, a collection of federal rules that govern energy matters. It encompasses regulations on nuclear safety, energy policy, and the activities of agencies such as the Department of Energy and the Nuclear Regulatory Commission.
[CF.12] Title 12: Banks and Banking -- CF.12 refers to Title 12 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which compiles the federal rules governing banks, banking institutions, and related financial services. It covers licensing, supervision, consumer protection, and the operations of the Federal Reserve and other banking agencies.
[CF.14] Title 14: Aeronautics and Space -- CF.14 refers to Title 14 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which governs aeronautics and space. It contains federal rules covering aviation safety, aircraft operations, licensing, air traffic control, and regulations for space activities.
[CF.17] Title 17: Commodity/Securities Exchanges -- CFR Title 17 contains the federal regulations governing commodity and securities exchanges, outlining the authority, registration, trading practices, and compliance requirements enforced by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and related agencies. It sets standards for market participants, clearing organizations, and the operation of exchange facilities to protect investors and maintain orderly markets.
[CF.21] Title 21: Food and Drugs -- CF.21 refers to Title 21 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which consolidates the federal rules administered by the Food and Drug Administration. It covers standards for food safety, pharmaceutical manufacturing, labeling, and medical device regulation, providing the legal framework for protecting public health.
[CF.26] Title 26: Internal Revenue -- CF.26 designates Title 26 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which compiles the federal rules administered by the Internal Revenue Service. The title covers the implementation of tax laws, including income, estate, gift, and excise tax regulations, providing guidance for compliance and enforcement.
[CF.29] Title 29: Labor -- CF.29 refers to Title 29 of the Code of Federal Regulations, the section that compiles federal labor statutes and rules. It contains regulations on wages, hours, workplace safety, collective bargaining, employee benefits and other policies administered by the Department of Labor.
[CF.33] Title 33: Navigation and Navigable Waters -- CF.33 refers to Title 33 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which consolidates the federal rules governing navigation, safety, and the management of navigable waters in the United States, primarily administered by the Coast Guard and the Army Corps of Engineers.
[CF.40] Title 40: Protection of Environment -- The entry CF.40 refers to Title 40 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which compiles the federal rules governing environmental protection. It includes regulations issued by the Environmental Protection Agency covering air and water quality, hazardous waste management, pesticide control, and related pollution prevention measures. This title serves as the primary legal framework for enforcing U.S. environmental policies.
[CF.42] Title 42: Public Health -- CF.42 refers to Title 42 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which consolidates federal rules governing public health. It includes regulations on disease prevention, health services, sanitation, medical research, and the administration of health programs by agencies such as the HHS and CDC.
[CF.47] Title 47: Telecommunication -- CF.47 refers to Title 47 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which consolidates federal rules governing telecommunication services, broadcasting, satellite communications, and related spectrum management. The title sets standards for licensing, technical operations, and public safety communications overseen by agencies such as the FCC.
[CF.48] Title 48: Federal Acquisition Regulations -- CF.48 refers to Title 48 of the Code of Federal Regulations, which contains the Federal Acquisition Regulations. This title establishes the policies and procedures governing the acquisition of goods and services by federal agencies, covering everything from contract planning and competition to award, administration, and contract closeout.
