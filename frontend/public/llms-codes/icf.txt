[b] Body Functions -- In the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health, code b designates the component "Body Functions," which covers the physiological functions of body systems, including mental, sensory, cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive and metabolic processes. It provides a systematic framework for describing the range of normal and altered functions that contribute to an individual's health status.

[b2] Sensory Functions and Pain -- In the ICF, b2 designates the domain of sensory functions and pain, covering the detection and perception of sensory stimuli and the experience of nociception. It includes visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, gustatory and pain functions, and is used to describe impairments, activity limitations and participation restrictions related to these senses.
[b3] Voice and Speech Functions -- b3 refers to the body functions that enable the production of vocal sounds and the articulation of speech. It includes the physiological mechanisms of the larynx, respiratory system, and articulators that generate voice quality, pitch, volume, as well as the coordination required for clear speech. This component is used to assess how impairments affect communication and participation.
[b4] Functions of the Cardiovascular, Haematological, Immunological and Respiratory Systems -- The b4 category encompasses the physiological functions of the cardiovascular, haematological, immunological and respiratory systems, covering processes such as heart rhythm, blood pressure, blood cell formation, immune defence, gas exchange and oxygen transport throughout the body.

[b7] Neuromusculoskeletal and Movement-Related Functions -- The ICF code b7 encompasses body functions that involve the neuromusculoskeletal system and the control of movement. It covers physiological functions of muscles, bones, joints, and the nervous system that enable posture, balance, coordination, and voluntary and involuntary motions. This domain assesses the integrity and capacity of structures that generate and regulate movement.
[b8] Functions of the Skin and Related Structures -- This component addresses physiological functions of the integumentary system, including skin, subcutaneous tissue, sweat glands, hair and nails. [d] Activities and Participation -- In the ICF the code d represents the Activities and Participation component, describing how a person carries out tasks and engages in life situations. It includes domains such as learning, communication, mobility, self-care, domestic life, interpersonal interactions, and community involvement.
[d1] Learning and Applying Knowledge -- The ICF code d1 refers to the activity of learning and applying knowledge, encompassing the processes of acquiring, processing, storing, retrieving, and using information to solve problems or make decisions. It covers tasks such as reading, studying, reasoning, and applying learned concepts in everyday life.
[d2] General Tasks and Demands -- d2 in the ICF refers to general tasks and demands, encompassing the basic requirements of daily and work life such as planning, organizing, executing, and monitoring activities, as well as handling the mental and physical demands inherent in personal, educational, and occupational settings.
[d3] Communication -- d3 refers to communication, the set of processes through which information is exchanged between individuals or systems, including speaking, listening, reading, writing, signing and the use of electronic media; it also covers the linguistic and auditory functions and any assistive technologies that support these activities.
[d4] Mobility -- In the International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health, the code d4 Mobility covers activities that involve moving oneself and transporting objects, such as walking, climbing, transferring, and using various means of transport. It addresses the capacity and performance of these movements in daily life.
[d5] Self-Care -- The ICF code d5 designates the Self-Care category, covering activities that enable an individual to look after personal health and meet basic bodily needs such as eating, drinking, washing, dressing, and maintaining personal hygiene. It falls within the Activities and Participation component and describes the capacity and performance of these everyday tasks in varying environmental contexts.
[d6] Domestic Life -- The ICF code d6 Domestic Life covers activities that involve managing one's own household and caring for family members, such as preparing meals, cleaning, laundry, shopping for home needs, and providing personal care or support to others living in the home.
[d7] Interpersonal Interactions and Relationships -- The d7 component of the ICF addresses the ability to initiate, maintain and adapt interpersonal interactions and relationships, covering family, friends, acquaintances and broader social networks. It includes the capacity and performance of skills such as communicating, sharing emotions, establishing intimacy, cooperating and providing or receiving social support, and the impact of these interactions on participation.
[d8] Major Life Areas -- In the ICF, code d8 designates the "major life areas" domain of participation. It covers involvement in work or employment, education, domestic tasks, and other central roles that structure everyday life, and records the extent to which health conditions facilitate or hinder performance in these settings.
[d9] Community, Social and Civic Life -- The d9 domain of the ICF covers participation in community, social and civic life, including activities such as interacting with community members, engaging in social networks, volunteering, voting, and taking part in public affairs. It reflects how individuals function within broader societal contexts and the extent to which they can fulfill roles and responsibilities in their community.
[e] Environmental Factors -- In the ICF, the "e" component denotes environmental factors, the physical, social and attitudinal elements in a person's surroundings that influence functioning and health. These include objects, technology, support services, policies, attitudes and the built environment, and are classified as either barriers or facilitators.
[e1] Products and Technology -- e1 refers to the range of products, devices and technologies that influence a person's functioning, including assistive equipment, environmental adaptations, and everyday objects that enable or limit activities and participation. It encompasses items such as wheelchairs, communication aids, prostheses and any technological tools that interact with the individual's health condition.
[e2] Natural Environment and Human-Made Changes -- The ICF code e2 covers environmental factors that involve the natural surroundings and alterations created by human activity, such as climate, terrain, natural resources, pollution, urban development and other changes that can facilitate or hinder a person's functioning, participation and health.
[e3] Support and Relationships -- e3 designates the environmental factor of support and relationships, encompassing the people and groups that offer assistance, emotional backing, or social contact such as family, friends, caregivers, and community networks. It highlights how these interpersonal connections can facilitate or impede a person's participation and performance in everyday activities.
[e4] Attitudes -- Attitudes (ICF code e4) are mental functions that involve the way a person evaluates people, objects, events, or ideas, reflecting personal beliefs, values, and predispositions toward them. In the ICF framework, they are considered part of the environmental factors that can facilitate or hinder participation, influencing behavior and social interaction in various contexts.
[e5] Services, Systems and Policies -- The ICF code e5 designates the environmental domain of services, systems and policies, covering societal structures that influence health and functioning such as health-care delivery, education, social security, labor market regulations, and public policy. These factors can either facilitate participation and wellbeing or act as barriers, depending on their availability, quality and accessibility.
[s] Body Structures -- In the ICF, the code "s" designates Body Structures, referring to the anatomical parts of the body such as organs, limbs and their component parts. It provides a standardized way to describe the physical makeup of the body that may be affected by health conditions, serving as a basis for linking structural impairments to functional outcomes.
[s1] Structures of the Nervous System -- The code s1 designates the anatomical components of the nervous system in the ICF. It includes the brain, spinal cord, peripheral nerves and associated structures that transmit and process neural signals, providing the basis for evaluating body functions and related impairments.
[s2] The Eye, Ear and Related Structures -- s2 identifies the anatomical components of the eye, ear and their associated structures within the ICF's body-structures component, encompassing the visual and auditory organs and related supporting tissues. It is used to classify impairments affecting sight, hearing and related sensory functions.

[s4] Structures of the Cardiovascular, Immunological and Respiratory Systems -- s4 designates the anatomical structures of the cardiovascular, immunological and respiratory systems, encompassing the heart, blood and lymphatic vessels, organs such as the spleen and thymus, and the airways and lungs. In the ICF framework it groups these body structures to assess how their integrity influences health and functioning.
[s5] Structures Related to the Digestive, Metabolic and Endocrine Systems -- s5 refers to the anatomical components of the gastrointestinal tract, associated metabolic organs and endocrine glands such as the stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas, thyroid and related ducts and vessels. These structures are evaluated in ICF assessments of body functions and health conditions.
[s6] Structures Related to the Genitourinary and Reproductive Systems -- The ICF code s6 denotes anatomical structures of the genitourinary and reproductive systems, encompassing organs such as the kidneys, bladder, urethra, testes, ovaries, uterus, and associated ducts. It is used to classify body structures affected by health conditions, injuries, or functional impairments related to these systems.
[s7] Structures Related to Movement -- s7 refers to the anatomical components that enable locomotion and posture, such as bones, joints, muscles, tendons, ligaments and related connective tissues. It is part of the ICF body-structures domain used to identify impairments that affect movement.
[s8] Skin and Related Structures -- The ICF code s8 refers to the body-structures domain of skin and related structures, encompassing the epidermis, dermis, subcutaneous tissue, hair, nails and associated glands. It is used to classify anatomical components when describing health, functioning and disability in the ICF framework.
