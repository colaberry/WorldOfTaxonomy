It limits the discharge of any substances that could pose a hazard to the marine environment and imposes specific labeling, stowage, and emergency response requirements to prevent accidental releases.

[ann_v] Annex V - Garbage -- Annex V of MARPOL 73/78 establishes rules for the handling and discharge of ship-generated garbage to protect the marine environment. [vi_4] Energy Efficiency Design Index (EEDI) -- The Energy Efficiency Design Index (EEDI) is an IMO-mandated metric that quantifies the grams of CO₂ emitted per tonne-mile of cargo capacity for newly built ships, setting a minimum efficiency baseline that tightens over time. It applies to specified vessel types constructed after 2013 as part of MARPOL Annex VI's strategy to curb air-pollution from maritime transport.
[vi_5] Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan (SEEMP) -- The Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan (SEEMP) is a mandatory shipboard document that outlines actions to improve a vessel's energy efficiency and reduce CO₂ emissions over its operational life, as required by MARPOL Annex VI. It includes baseline data, performance indicators and a continuous improvement strategy.
[vi_6] Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) -- The Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) is a performance metric introduced under MARPOL Annex VI that quantifies a ship's CO₂ emissions per unit of transport work, expressed in grams of CO₂ per cargo-tonne-nautical-mile. It is used to assess and rank vessels' energy efficiency and to enforce progressive emission reduction targets.
[vi_7] Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) -- The Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) is a mandatory technical measure under MARPOL Annex VI that quantifies the baseline energy efficiency of existing vessels, calculated from ship type, size, and installed engine power. Ships must achieve an EEXI value not exceeding a reference threshold to comply, prompting modifications or operational adjustments to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
