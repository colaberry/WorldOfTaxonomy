[IB] ISBN Groups -- ISBN groups are
[IB.0] Group 0/1: English Language -- The ISBN registration group 0/1 designates books published in the English language, covering major English-speaking markets such as the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom and Ireland, and is the most widely used group for English-language titles.
[IB.10] Group 99901-99981: Small Countries -- The ISBN registration group range 99901-99981 is assigned to small countries, allowing publishers in those nations to be uniquely identified within the International Standard Book Number system. This range is part of the broader group of registration prefixes managed under the IB.10 classification.
[IB.11] ISBN-10 (Legacy) -- IB.11 refers to the legacy 10-digit ISBN format that was used internationally before the 13-digit system was adopted in 2007. The code identifies the registration-group element, publisher identifier, title identifier and check digit that together uniquely identify a book edition.
[IB.12] ISBN-13 (Current) -- IB.12 designates the current 13-digit ISBN format, which began use in 2007 to replace the older 10-digit version. It includes a GS1 prefix, registration group, registrant, publication element and check digit, providing a globally unique identifier for books and related media.
[IB.13] ISBN-A (DOI-Based) -- IB.13 designates the ISBN-A format, a DOI-based identifier that aligns the traditional International Standard Book Number with the Digital Object Identifier system. It enables seamless linking of print and electronic versions of a title, facilitating consistent citation and discovery across publishing platforms.
[IB.2] Group 2: French Language -- IB.2 designates the ISBN registration group 2, which is allocated to publishers producing books in the French language. Numbers beginning with the digit 2 identify titles issued in France and other French-language markets, signaling their linguistic and geographic origin within the international ISBN system.
[IB.3] Group 3: German Language -- IB.3 designates the ISBN registration group for the German language, covering publishers in Germany, Austria, Switzerland and other German-speaking territories. Books issued with a prefix from this group are identified as belonging to the German-language market segment.
[IB.4] Group 4: Japanese -- IB.4 denotes the ISBN registration group for Japan. Publishers and agents in Japan use the prefix 4 to assign ISBNs to books printed or distributed there, and the group covers all formats and languages published within the Japanese market.
[IB.5] Group 5: Russian -- IB.5 identifies the ISBN registration group for Russian-language publications, covering books published in Russia and other Russian-speaking territories. It designates the prefix 5 in the 13-digit ISBN, allowing publishers to register titles under this national group.
[IB.7] Group 7: Chinese -- Group 7 of the ISBN registration identifies books published in the Chinese language, covering mainland China, Hong Kong, Macau, Taiwan and other Chinese-speaking territories. Publishers in these regions are assigned numbers that start with the digit 7, which serves as the group identifier in each ISBN.
[IB.8] Group 80-94: Various Countries -- IB.8 identifies the ISBN registration group covering the numeric range 80-94, which is allocated to a collection of countries rather than a single nation. Publishers operating in any of these listed countries receive ISBNs that begin with one of these group numbers, allowing for unified cataloguing across the region.
[IB.9] Group 950-989: Various Countries -- IB.9 designates ISBN registration groups 950-989, covering a variety of countries mainly in the Middle East, Central Asia, and parts of the Caribbean. Publishers in these nations use the corresponding group identifier as the first element of an ISBN to register their titles worldwide.
