[art_11] Art 11 - Packaging and labelling -- Article 11 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control requires parties to adopt measures that ensure tobacco product packaging and labeling convey health warnings that are clear, visible and legally mandated; it calls for large, graphic warnings covering a significant portion of the pack, standardized branding restrictions, and the prohibition of misleading statements that could downplay health risks.
[art_12] Art 12 - Education, communication, training and public awareness -- Article 12 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control obliges parties to promote and coordinate comprehensive public education, communication, training and awareness programmes about the health risks of tobacco use and the benefits of quitting, targeting all segments of the population and especially vulnerable groups.
[art_13] Art 13 - Tobacco advertising, promotion and sponsorship -- Article 13 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control obliges Parties to implement comprehensive bans or severe restrictions on all forms of tobacco advertising, promotion and sponsorship, including at points of sale, in the media and through corporate branding, with particular attention to cross-border and digital activities.
[art_14] Art 14 - Demand reduction measures concerning tobacco dependence -- Article 14 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control requires parties to adopt demand-reduction measures aimed at tobacco dependence, emphasizing the provision of cessation services, treatment accessibility, and public awareness initiatives. It calls for policies that support individuals in quitting tobacco and for resources to make effective cessation assistance widely available.
[art_15] Art 15 - Illicit trade in tobacco products -- Article 15 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control requires parties to adopt comprehensive measures to prevent, detect and eliminate illicit trade in tobacco products. It calls for licensing and regulation of the supply chain, international cooperation, and the use of tracking and tracing systems to curb smuggling and illegal sales.
[art_16] Art 16 - Sales to and by minors -- Article 16 obliges Parties to prohibit the sale of tobacco products to anyone below a legally defined minimum age and to forbid persons under that age from selling tobacco. It also requires the establishment of appropriate penalties and measures to enforce these restrictions.
[art_17] Art 17 - Provision of support for economically viable alternatives -- Article 17 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control urges parties to provide economic and technical assistance that enables tobacco growers and workers to shift to sustainable, economically viable alternative livelihoods, thereby reducing dependence on tobacco production and supporting broader development goals.
[art_18] Art 18 - Protection of the environment and health of persons -- Article 18 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control requires parties to protect the environment and the health of persons by addressing tobacco-related waste, pollution and fire hazards, and by implementing measures to reduce litter, toxic emissions and other environmental impacts of tobacco production, distribution and consumption.
[art_3] Art 3 - Objective -- Article 3 establishes the Convention's objective to protect present and future generations from the health, social, environmental and economic consequences of tobacco use, and to strengthen national action by promoting effective public-health policies.
[art_4] Art 4 - Guiding principles -- Article 4 establishes the guiding principles for applying the Convention, requiring parties to develop comprehensive tobacco-control policies based on scientific evidence, promote coordination among government agencies, and cooperate internationally while respecting each nation's sovereignty and public-health priorities.
[art_6] Art 6 - Price and tax measures to reduce demand -- Article 6 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control requires parties to implement price and tax policies that raise the cost of tobacco products, aiming to lower consumption, particularly among youth and low-income groups. It urges regular tax adjustments to keep real prices rising and suggests directing revenue to health-promotion initiatives.
[art_8] Art 8 - Protection from exposure to tobacco smoke -- Article 8 obliges parties to adopt measures that protect people from exposure to tobacco smoke in indoor public places, workplaces and public transport. It requires enacting legislation to achieve smoke-free environments, while permitting designated smoking areas only under strict conditions.
[art_9] Art 9 - Regulation of contents of tobacco products -- Article 9 of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control obliges parties to regulate the contents of tobacco products, requiring limits on additives, nicotine, tar and other harmful constituents, and to mandate disclosure of product composition to authorities and consumers, with the aim of reducing addiction, toxicity and the appeal of tobacco.
[pt_1] Part I - Introduction -- Part I - Introduction outlines the treaty's purpose, scope and key definitions, establishing the global health objective of reducing tobacco use and providing the legal framework for parties to cooperate in implementing the convention.
[pt_2] Part II - Objective and Guiding Principles -- Part II of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control states the treaty's objective to protect present and future generations from the health, social and environmental harms of tobacco, and sets guiding principles that include the promotion of public health, the use of evidence-based measures, the precautionary approach and international cooperation in tobacco control.
[pt_3] Part III - Measures Relating to Reduction of Demand -- Part III of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control sets out policy measures aimed at reducing the demand for tobacco, covering price and tax strategies, protection from tobacco advertising and promotion, smoke-free environments, health warnings, public education and the provision of cessation services.
[pt_4] Part IV - Measures Relating to Reduction of Supply -- Part IV of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control sets out obligations for parties to reduce the availability of tobacco. It requires licensing of manufacturers, importers and retailers, enforces measures against illicit trade, and promotes economic alternatives for those dependent on tobacco farming or sales. The article also calls for public-sector procurement policies that favor low-tobacco-content products and for the regulation of tobacco product standards.
[pt_5] Part V - Protection of the Environment -- Part V of the WHO Framework Convention on Tobacco Control addresses the protection of the environment by requiring parties to adopt measures that limit the impact of tobacco cultivation, manufacturing, and consumption on ecosystems. It includes provisions for safe disposal of tobacco waste, prevention of fire hazards, and the reduction of harmful emissions associated with tobacco-related activities.
