[l1_ac] AC - Access Control (Level 1) -- Level 1 Access Control requires organizations to limit system use to authorized individuals, ensure each user is uniquely identified, and employ basic mechanisms such as passwords or other simple credential checks to protect Federal Contract Information. It establishes a foundational guard against unauthorized access without requiring advanced monitoring or multi-factor authentication.
[l1_ia] IA - Identification and Authentication (Level 1) -- The Level 1 Identification and Authentication (IA) domain in CMMC 2.0 requires organizations to implement basic controls that verify a user's identity before granting system access, including the use of unique usernames and passwords, multi-factor authentication for privileged accounts, and periodic review of account validity to ensure only authorized individuals can log in.
[l1_mp] MP - Media Protection (Level 1) -- MP - Media Protection at Level 1 requires basic safeguarding of Federal Contract Information stored on physical or digital media, including limiting access, labeling, and proper disposal to prevent unauthorized disclosure.
[l1_pe] PE - Physical Protection (Level 1) -- Level 1 Physical Protection requires organizations to implement basic safeguards that restrict unauthorized physical access to facilities and equipment. Measures include locked doors, visitor controls, and basic monitoring to protect critical assets from casual intrusion.
[l1_sc] SC - System and Communications Protection (Level 1) -- Level 1 System and Communications Protection defines the most fundamental safeguards for protecting information system boundaries and data in transit. It requires organizations to implement basic measures such as network segmentation, firewall configurations, and encryption of sensitive communications, ensuring a baseline defense against common cyber threats.
[l1_si] SI - System and Information Integrity (Level 1) -- l1_si denotes the Level 1 System and Information Integrity domain in CMMC 2.0. At this level organizations must implement basic anti-malware protection, apply timely security updates, and monitor for unauthorized changes to maintain the integrity of system and data.
[l2_ac] AC - Access Control (Level 2) -- The AC-Access Control Level 2 domain in CMMC 2.0 requires organizations to establish and maintain documented access-control policies, enforce role-based and least-privilege permissions, and implement multi-factor authentication for privileged accounts, while regularly reviewing and revoking unnecessary access.
[l2_at] AT - Awareness and Training (Level 2) -- At Level 2, organizations must deliver role-specific cybersecurity awareness and training that includes periodic refresher sessions, assessment of comprehension, and documented proof of participation. The program goes beyond basic security basics to cover policies, incident response procedures, and the responsibilities of privileged users.
[l2_au] AU - Audit and Accountability (Level 2) -- The AU - Audit and Accountability domain at Level 2 of the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification 2.0 requires organizations to implement safeguards that record, review, and retain security-relevant events, ensuring that audit logs are protected from tampering and provide sufficient detail for investigations. It also mandates regular analysis of log data to detect and respond to inappropriate or unauthorized activities.
[l2_ca] CA - Assessment, Authorization (Level 2) -- The l2_ca entry defines the Level 2 requirements for conducting a formal security assessment and gaining an authorization to operate under the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification 2.0. It mandates documented assessment plans, independent testing of security controls, risk analysis, and a written authorization decision that demonstrates the system meets the required security posture.
[l2_cm] CM - Configuration Management (Level 2) -- Level 2 Configuration Management requires organizations to establish documented policies and procedures for managing hardware, software, and firmware configurations, ensuring that any changes are tracked, approved, and implemented consistently. The focus is on maintaining baseline configurations, protecting against unauthorized modifications, and verifying that configurations support required security controls.
[l2_ia] IA - Identification and Authentication (Level 2) -- At Level 2 of the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification 2.0, the IA (Identification and Authentication) domain requires organizations to enforce unique user IDs, implement multi-factor authentication for privileged and remote access, and maintain robust password management and account-locking policies to protect system access.
[l2_ir] IR - Incident Response (Level 2) -- The l2_ir entry in the Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification 2.0 defines the Incident Response practices required at Level 2. Organizations must establish documented procedures for detecting, reporting, analyzing, and responding to cyber incidents, maintain incident logs, and coordinate appropriate actions with relevant stakeholders and authorities within prescribed timeframes.
[l2_ma] MA - Maintenance (Level 2) -- The Level 2 Maintenance domain of the CMMC 2.0 requires organizations to establish documented procedures for ongoing system upkeep, including timely patch management, configuration control, and routine vulnerability remediation. It emphasizes consistent application of maintenance activities, verification of corrective actions, and evidence of compliance to sustain the security posture of covered assets.
[l2_mp] MP - Media Protection (Level 2) -- The MP (Media Protection) Level 2 practice in CMMC 2.0 requires organizations to safeguard both physical and digital media that contain Controlled Unclassified Information. It mandates documented policies for media handling, labeling, storage, transport, and disposal, as well as encryption or other protective measures to prevent unauthorized access, alteration, or loss throughout the media lifecycle.
[l2_pe] PE - Physical Protection (Level 2) -- The l2_pe entry defines Level 2 Physical Protection requirements within the CMMC 2.0 framework, specifying that organizations must implement controlled access to facilities, use monitoring systems, and maintain environmental safeguards to protect CUI. [l2_ra] RA - Risk Assessment (Level 2) -- In CMMC 2.0 the l2_ra entry defines the Level 2 risk-assessment practice, requiring organizations to perform formal assessments of threats, vulnerabilities and potential impacts, determine residual risk, and use the results to guide security planning and mitigation.
[l2_sc] SC - System and Communications Protection (Level 2) -- The l2_sc entry denotes the Level-2 System and Communications Protection domain in CMMC 2.0. It requires organizations to apply basic safeguards that protect system boundaries, enforce encryption and integrity for data in transit, and monitor and control inbound and outbound communications.
[l2_si] SI - System and Information Integrity (Level 2) -- At CMMC 2.0 Level 2, the SI domain requires organizations to establish policies and procedures that maintain system and information integrity. This includes implementing anti-malware defenses, applying timely patches, conducting regular vulnerability scans, and reporting integrity-related incidents within defined timeframes.
