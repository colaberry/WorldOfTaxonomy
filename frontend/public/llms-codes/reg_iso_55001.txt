[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- Clause 10.1 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organisations to identify and record any nonconformities with the asset management system, analyse their causes and implement corrective actions to eliminate them and prevent recurrence.
[10_2] 10.2 Preventive action -- In ISO 55001:2014 clause 10.2 the organization must identify potential causes of nonconformities and plan preventive actions based on risk analysis and performance monitoring, implement those actions, and verify their effectiveness to maintain and improve the asset management system.
[10_3] 10.3 Continual improvement -- Clause 10.3 mandates that the asset management system be continually improved by establishing processes to capture performance data, conduct internal audits, and implement corrective and preventive actions, thereby enhancing the system's effectiveness, efficiency and alignment with organizational objectives.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- The entry requires the organization to determine internal and external issues that affect its asset management objectives, identify relevant interested parties and their needs, and use this context to shape the management system. It emphasizes systematic analysis of factors influencing asset performance and strategic alignment of policies and processes.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of stakeholders -- Clause 4.2 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organizations to identify and analyze the needs, expectations and requirements of all relevant stakeholders affecting asset management, ensuring these insights inform the asset management policy, objectives and planning. It emphasizes systematic engagement, documentation and periodic review to align asset decisions with stakeholder interests and regulatory obligations.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the asset management system -- Clause 4.3 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organizations to define the scope of their asset management system by identifying which assets and processes are included, considering relevant internal and external issues, and aligning the boundaries with strategic objectives and stakeholder requirements. This determination establishes the limits and applicability of the management system throughout the enterprise.

[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- Top management must show leadership and commitment by establishing and maintaining an asset management policy aligned with strategic objectives, ensuring adequate resources, assigning clear responsibilities, and promoting a culture of continual improvement. They must integrate asset management into the organization's overall governance, communicate its importance, and regularly review performance to demonstrate accountability.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- The organization shall define an asset-management policy that aligns with its strategic objectives, demonstrates commitment to continual improvement, and sets the scope, responsibilities and authority for the asset-management system. The policy must be appropriate to the organization's context, communicated to relevant parties and periodically reviewed for continuing suitability.
[5_3] 5.3 Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities -- The clause requires the organization to define and document the roles, responsibilities and authorities needed for effective asset management, ensuring that each function has clear accountability and decision-making power. It mandates that these assignments be communicated, understood and maintained throughout the asset lifecycle.
[6_1] 6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities -- ISO 55001:2014 clause 6.1 requires an organization to identify and evaluate risks and opportunities that can affect its asset-management objectives, then plan and implement actions to address them. These actions must be integrated into the management system, documented, and regularly reviewed for effectiveness.
[6_2] 6.2 Asset management objectives and planning -- ISO 55001 6.2 requires organizations to establish clear asset-management objectives that support the overall policy, set measurable targets, and develop detailed plans outlining the actions, resources, responsibilities and timelines needed to achieve those objectives, ensuring systematic implementation and continual improvement.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- Clause 8.1 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organizations to define and implement operational planning and control processes that ensure asset management activities are performed in accordance with established objectives, policies and risk assessments, allocating appropriate resources, setting performance criteria, and continuously monitoring and adjusting actions to maintain asset value and reliability.
[8_2] 8.2 Management of change -- Clause 8.2 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organizations to establish a systematic process for managing any change that could affect asset management objectives, including identification, evaluation, approval, documentation and communication of the change, to maintain control over risks and performance impacts.
[8_3] 8.3 Outsourcing -- Clause 8.3 of ISO 55001:2014 specifies the requirements for outsourcing within an asset management system, mandating that any external provider be selected, controlled and monitored to ensure its activities meet the organization's asset-management objectives, risk criteria and performance standards. It requires documented agreements, verification of competence, and ongoing oversight to maintain conformity with the standard's overall requirements.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- Clause 9.1 of ISO 55001:2014 requires organizations to establish processes for monitoring and measuring asset-related performance, collecting data, analyzing results and evaluating effectiveness against defined objectives. It ensures that information is available to support decision-making, identify improvement opportunities and confirm compliance with the asset management system requirements.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 of ISO 55001:2014 requires the organization to plan and carry out internal audits at defined intervals to verify that its asset management system conforms to the standard's requirements and is effectively implemented, to detect any non-conformities, and to provide input for continual improvement.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- Clause 9.3 of ISO 55001:2014 requires top management to conduct periodic reviews of the asset-management system to confirm its continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness. The review must assess performance against objectives, compliance obligations, risk status, resource needs and improvement opportunities, and record any decisions or actions needed.

[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 55001:2014 requires the organization to identify relevant internal and external issues, understand the needs and expectations of interested parties, and define the scope of its asset-management system, establishing the context in which asset-management objectives will be set and pursued.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 of ISO 55001:2014 requires top management to demonstrate leadership and commitment to the asset-management system, setting a clear vision, policy and objectives that align with organizational strategy. It mandates that leaders ensure the system's integration, allocate resources, communicate the importance of effective asset management, and review performance to drive continual improvement.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 of ISO 55001:2014 specifies the planning requirements for an asset management system, requiring organizations to establish asset-management objectives, assess risks and opportunities, and define the processes needed to achieve those objectives. It emphasizes aligning asset plans with business strategy, allocating resources, and setting measurable targets to ensure effective implementation.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 55001:2014 specifies the support required for an effective asset-management system, detailing the provision of resources, competence and awareness of personnel, internal and external communication, and the creation, control and retention of documented information essential for the system's operation.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 55001:2014 specifies the operational requirements for an asset management system, detailing how organizations must plan, execute, monitor, and control asset-related activities to achieve intended performance and risk objectives. It also outlines the need for competent personnel, appropriate communication, documented procedures, and continual evaluation of operational effectiveness.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 55001:2014 sets out the performance-evaluation requirements for an asset management system. It requires organizations to monitor and measure system effectiveness, conduct internal audits, and perform management reviews to ensure continual improvement and alignment with strategic objectives.
