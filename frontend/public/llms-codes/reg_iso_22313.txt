[10_1] 10.1 Nonconformity and corrective action -- The section explains how an organization should identify, document, and evaluate nonconformities in its business continuity management system, then determine the root causes and implement corrective actions. It emphasizes tracking the effectiveness of those actions, updating relevant procedures, and preventing recurrence to maintain continual improvement and resilience.
[10_2] 10.2 Continual improvement -- Continual improvement requires the organization to regularly assess the performance of its business continuity management system, identify gaps or non-conformities, and implement corrective actions to enhance effectiveness. It involves monitoring objectives, analyzing incidents and audit findings, and using management review to drive systematic upgrades that increase resilience and align the system with evolving risks and stakeholder expectations.
[4_1] 4.1 Understanding the organization and its context -- Section 4.1 of ISO 22313:2020 guides organizations to identify and analyze internal and external factors that affect their business continuity objectives, including stakeholder needs, regulatory requirements, and strategic context, providing the foundation for a tailored Business Continuity Management System.
[4_2] 4.2 Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties -- Clause 4.2 instructs organizations to determine who the interested parties are, capture their relevant needs and expectations, and assess how these influence the business continuity management system. It requires systematic analysis of stakeholder requirements, legal and other obligations, and alignment of continuity objectives with those expectations.
[4_3] 4.3 Determining the scope of the BCMS -- The entry explains how an organization defines the boundaries and applicability of its Business Continuity Management System, taking into account internal and external issues, interested parties, and relevant processes. It guides the selection of activities, locations and products to be covered so the BCMS aligns with ISO 22301 requirements and focuses on the most critical functions.
[5_1] 5.1 Leadership and commitment -- The entry requires top management to demonstrate active leadership and commitment to the Business Continuity Management System, establishing a clear policy, ensuring alignment with strategic objectives, allocating necessary resources, and fostering a culture of resilience throughout the organization. This leadership drives the implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of the system.
[5_2] 5.2 Policy -- The entry defines the Business Continuity Management System policy, specifying that top management must establish, document, and maintain a formal policy that states the organization's commitment, scope, objectives and alignment with its context and strategic direction, and that the policy is communicated, reviewed and updated as part of continual improvement.
[8_1] 8.1 Operational planning and control -- The guidance in 8.1 explains how an organization should plan, implement and control its business continuity processes, defining the scope, objectives and required resources, assessing risks and opportunities, establishing procedures for execution, monitoring performance and taking corrective actions to ensure the continuity management system operates effectively.
[8_2] 8.2 Business impact analysis -- Section 8.2 of ISO 22313:2020 offers guidance on performing a Business Impact Analysis, explaining how organizations identify critical processes, assess the consequences of disruption, and set recovery-time objectives and resource needs to shape effective business continuity planning.
[8_3] 8.3 Risk assessment -- Section 8.3 of ISO 22313:2020 provides guidance on conducting risk assessments within a Business Continuity Management System. It advises organisations to identify, analyze and evaluate threats and impacts to determine appropriate continuity strategies and maintain resilience.
[8_4] 8.4 Business continuity strategies and solutions -- Entry 8.4 of ISO 22313:2020 offers guidance on developing business continuity strategies and solutions, advising organizations on how to select, design and implement appropriate approaches to maintain critical functions during disruptions. It emphasizes aligning strategies with risk assessments, resource needs and overall organisational objectives, and highlights options such as recovery, redundancy, relocation and collaboration to ensure resilience.
[8_5] 8.5 Business continuity plans and procedures -- Section 8.5 of ISO 22313:2020 provides guidance on developing, documenting and maintaining business continuity plans and procedures. It emphasizes aligning plans with the organization's context, defining roles, establishing recovery strategies and ensuring regular testing
[8_6] 8.6 Exercise programme -- It advises organisations to design, schedule and carry out regular exercises that test the effectiveness of their business continuity plans, specifying objectives, scenarios, participants, evaluation criteria and documentation requirements. The results are to be analysed, corrective actions identified and lessons learned integrated into the BCMS to enhance overall resilience.
[8_7] 8.7 Evaluation of documentation and capabilities -- ISO 22313:2020 advises organizations to systematically assess the completeness, relevance and effectiveness of their business continuity documentation and the capabilities supporting it. The guidance outlines methods for reviewing policies, procedures, plans and resource allocations, verifying that they meet defined objectives and are fit for current risks. It also recommends regular monitoring, testing and corrective actions to maintain continual improvement of the BCMS.
[9_1] 9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation -- ISO 22313:2020 provides guidance on the processes needed to monitor, measure, analyse and evaluate a business continuity management system, ensuring that performance data are collected, reviewed and used to verify achievement of continuity objectives. Clause 9.1 outlines how organizations should implement systematic tracking, internal auditing and management review to drive continual improvement and informed decision-making.
[9_2] 9.2 Internal audit -- Clause 9.2 mandates that an organization plan and perform internal audits of its Business Continuity Management System to verify conformity with the BCMS policy, objectives and applicable requirements, record audit findings, and ensure corrective actions are taken and reported to top management.
[9_3] 9.3 Management review -- The 9.3 Management review clause guides top management to regularly evaluate the Business Continuity Management System's performance, effectiveness, and alignment with the organization's policy and objectives, using documented evidence, audit results, and incident analyses, and to identify improvement actions and resource needs.

[cl_4] Clause 4 - Context of the Organization -- Clause 4 of ISO 22313:2020 guides organizations in determining the internal and external issues that affect their business continuity management system, identifying relevant interested parties and their requirements, and defining the scope of the BCMS to ensure alignment with strategic objectives and resilience goals.
[cl_5] Clause 5 - Leadership -- Clause 5 outlines the leadership responsibilities required for an effective Business Continuity Management System, specifying that top management must demonstrate commitment, establish a BCMS policy aligned with organizational strategy, assign clear roles and authority, ensure necessary resources, and promote a culture of continual improvement and resilience.
[cl_6] Clause 6 - Planning -- Clause 6 in ISO 22313:2020 outlines the planning requirements for a Business Continuity Management System, directing organisations to define the scope, context and interested parties, conduct risk and impact analyses, and set measurable continuity objectives. It also specifies the development of strategies, resources and actions needed to achieve those objectives and maintain resilience, forming the basis for subsequent implementation and performance evaluation.
[cl_7] Clause 7 - Support -- Clause 7 of ISO 22313:2020 outlines the support requirements for a Business Continuity Management System, specifying the resources, competence, awareness and communication needed to implement and maintain the system. It also details how documented information must be controlled, retained and made accessible to ensure effective continuity planning.
[cl_8] Clause 8 - Operation -- Clause 8 of ISO 22313:2020 provides guidance on the operational phase of a Business Continuity Management System, detailing how organizations should plan, execute, and control continuity activities, manage resources, and ensure effective response and recovery during incidents, thereby maintaining essential functions and resilience.
[cl_9] Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation -- Clause 9 of ISO 22313:2020 outlines the requirements for evaluating the performance of a Business Continuity Management System, including monitoring, measurement, analysis, and internal audit of processes, as well as management review to ensure continual improvement and alignment with security and resilience objectives.
