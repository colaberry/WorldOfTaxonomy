The code 2E identifies it in the protocol's schedule of controlled substances, reflecting its phase-out and restricted use to limit atmospheric impacts.

[art_1] Article 1 - Definitions -- Article 1 of the Montreal Protocol sets out key definitions used throughout the treaty, clarifying terms such as "controlled substance," "federal government," "party," and "ozone-depleting substance." These definitions establish the legal scope for obligations, reporting, and compliance measures aimed at protecting the ozone layer.
[art_10] Article 10 - Financial Mechanism -- Article 10 establishes the financial mechanism that provides funding to developing countries for compliance with the protocol, administered by the Multilateral Fund, and covering technology transfer, capacity building and the phase-out of ozone-depleting substances.
[art_2] Article 2 - Control Measures -- Article 2 sets out the control measures for ozone-depleting substances, requiring parties to limit production and consumption according to agreed schedules, with provisions for essential uses and exemptions. It establishes a framework for periodic review and adjustment of control levels to protect the ozone layer.
[art_4] Article 4 - Trade Control -- Article 4 establishes the trade-control system for ozone-depleting substances, requiring parties to prohibit production, consumption and import or export of listed chemicals except under a licensing and reporting regime. It obliges governments to monitor trade, enforce compliance and exchange information to prevent illicit trade and protect the ozone layer.
[art_5] Article 5 - Special Situation of Developing Countries -- Article 5 of the Montreal Protocol acknowledges the special circumstances of developing (Article 9) nations, granting them extended timelines and financial assistance to phase out ozone-depleting substances, while encouraging technology transfer and capacity-building to support compliance.

It guides these countries in meeting their obligations to limit ozone-depleting substances while transitioning to climate-friendly alternatives.

[k3] HFC baseline calculations -- The k3 entry in the Montreal Protocol classification outlines the methodology for calculating baseline emissions of hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) before phase-down measures take effect. It defines standard assumptions, data sources and calculation steps used to establish reference levels for reporting and compliance.
[kigali] Kigali Amendment (HFCs) -- The Kigali Amendment to the Montreal Protocol targets hydrofluorocarbons, setting phasedown schedules to curb climate warming. It builds on the original treaty that bans ozone-depleting substances, guiding parties to reduce HFC production and consumption over decades.
