[IR] ISRC Components -- IR identifies the parts of an International Standard Recording Code, a unique identifier for sound recordings and music videos. The code is split into a country identifier, a registrant code, the year of reference, and a designation code, each indicating the recording's origin, owner and specific release.
[IR.01] Country Code (2 chars) -- IR.01 designates the two-character country code component of an International Standard Recording Code, using the ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 identifier of the nation where the recording was registered. It forms the first two positions of the 12-character ISRC and enables global tracking of copyrighted audio recordings.
[IR.02] Registrant Code (3 chars) -- IR.02 defines the Registrant Code, a three-character alphanumeric string that uniquely identifies the organization responsible for assigning an ISRC. It is allocated by national registration agencies and remains constant for all recordings issued by that entity.
[IR.03] Year of Reference (2 digits) -- The IR.03 component of the International Standard Recording Code designates the two-digit year of reference for the recording, indicating the calendar year in which the work was created, released, or otherwise relevant to the coding system. It is a numeric field ranging from 00 to 99, allowing concise representation of the year within the ISRC structure.
[IR.04] Designation Code (5 digits) -- IR.04 Designation Code is a five-digit numeric string assigned to a specific recording after the country, registrant and year components of an ISRC. The code is allocated by the national agency and uniquely distinguishes each individual work within the overall identifier.
[IR.05] Sound Recording -- IR.05 denotes the sound-recording component of an International Standard Recording Code, providing a unique identifier for each specific audio track. This code enables precise tracking, rights management and royalty distribution for recorded music across digital and physical formats.
[IR.06] Music Video -- IR.06 identifies a music video, the audiovisual component that pairs a recorded sound recording with synchronized visual images. It applies to works where the primary purpose is to present the musical performance within a moving-picture format, covering official videos, promotional clips and any visual media that convey the audio track alongside imagery.
[IR.07] Spoken Word -- IR.07 designates recordings that consist primarily of spoken content rather than music, including poetry readings, storytelling, lectures, sermons and other verbal performances. These recordings are classified under the International Standard Recording Code to differentiate them from musical works and to facilitate cataloguing and rights management.
[IR.08] Podcast Episode -- IR.08 denotes the podcast episode component in the International Standard Recording Code, identifying a single episode within a podcast series for unique cataloguing, rights management and reporting. It follows the series identifier and precedes the version segment, allowing precise attribution of audio content.
[IR.09] Audio Drama -- IR.09 denotes the audio drama component within the International Standard Recording Code system, encompassing staged performances conveyed solely through sound, including scripts, voice actors, sound effects, and music. It is used to uniquely identify and categorize such recordings for licensing, royalty distribution, and archival
[IR.10] Radio Broadcast Recording -- IR.10 designates a Radio Broadcast Recording, which is a sound recording that captures a program as it was transmitted over the radio airwaves. It typically includes music, speech, advertisements and other content broadcast in a single, continuous take for archival or rebroadcast purposes.
[IR.11] Live Performance Recording -- IR.11 denotes a live performance recording component within the International Standard Recording Code system. It identifies recordings captured during a concert or other public event, including audience and venue ambience, and distinguishes them from studio-made recordings for accurate metadata and rights management.
[IR.12] Remix/Adaptation -- The IR.12 component of the International Standard Recording Code identifies a recording that has been remixed or adapted from an existing work, indicating that the audio content has been altered, re-combined, or otherwise transformed while retaining a link to the original source. This tag helps rights holders and databases track derivative versions for licensing, royalty distribution, and cataloguing purposes.
