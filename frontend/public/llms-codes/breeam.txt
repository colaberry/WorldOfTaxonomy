[BR] BREEAM Categories -- BR designates the set of BREEAM sustainability assessment categories, grouping the environmental criteria used to evaluate building performance. These categories cover areas such as energy, water, materials, waste, health and wellbeing, and ecology, each assigned credits that contribute to the overall BREEAM rating.
[BR.01] Management -- BR.01 Management addresses the organisational and procedural controls that ensure sustainable outcomes, requiring effective leadership, clear sustainability policies, stakeholder engagement, risk management and ongoing performance monitoring throughout the project lifecycle.
[BR.02] Health and Wellbeing -- BR.02 Health and Wellbeing assesses how a building supports the physical and mental comfort of its occupants, covering indoor air quality, thermal comfort, daylight, acoustic performance, and ergonomics. It evaluates design and operational measures such as ventilation systems, low-emission materials, lighting standards, and noise control to promote a healthy, productive indoor environment.
[BR.03] Energy -- BR.03 Energy in BREEAM addresses how a building minimises its energy consumption and carbon emissions through efficient design, high-performance systems, on-site renewable generation and robust monitoring. It evaluates operational energy use, compares it to benchmarks and awards credits for reductions and low-energy technologies.
[BR.04] Transport -- BR.04 Transport assesses the environmental impact of building-related travel, encouraging low-carbon and active transport options. It awards credits for proximity to public transport, provision of cycling facilities, electric vehicle charging, and travel plans that reduce car dependence.
[BR.05] Water -- BR.05 - Water assesses the efficient use and management of water resources in a building, including measures to reduce consumption, detect leaks, harvest rainwater, and install water-efficient fixtures, thereby lowering demand and protecting local water cycles.
[BR.06] Materials -- BR.06 Materials evaluates the environmental performance of building components by examining embodied carbon, resource efficiency, recycled content and responsible sourcing throughout a product's life-cycle. It awards credits for selecting low-impact materials, avoiding hazardous substances and ensuring transparent supply chains, thereby reducing the overall ecological footprint of the project.
[BR.07] Waste -- BR.07 covers waste management and reduction, requiring projects to assess total waste generated, promote diversion from landfill, and achieve measurable recycling and reuse targets. It encourages the adoption of waste minimisation strategies, such as material selection, construction waste segregation, and ongoing operational waste reduction.
[BR.08] Land Use and Ecology -- BR.08 Land Use and Ecology assesses how a project protects and enhances natural habitats, promotes biodiversity and uses land responsibly. It evaluates site selection, ecological design, green space provision, and measures to preserve existing ecosystems, ensuring the development contributes positively to the surrounding environment.
[BR.09] Pollution -- BR.09 Pollution evaluates a project's impact on air, water and soil, rewarding measures that prevent or reduce contaminant release. It looks at low-emission construction materials, waste and water management practices, and strategies to protect surrounding ecosystems, aiming to minimise both indoor and outdoor pollution.

[BR.11] Resilience -- BR.11 Resilience evaluates how a building can withstand and recover from climate-related stresses, extreme weather events, and other disruptions, encouraging design strategies that improve durability, adaptability, and long-term performance. It awards credits for measures such as flood risk assessment, heat-wave mitigation, and flexible use of spaces, helping occupants maintain safety and comfort under changing conditions.
[BR.12] Outstanding -- BR.12 denotes the "Outstanding" category in BREEAM, the highest tier of achievement awarded to buildings that score above the top threshold (typically around 85 % of the available credits). It signals exemplary performance across all sustainability criteria, demonstrating leadership in environmental design and operation. This rating is reserved for projects that substantially exceed standard good practice.
[BR.13] Excellent -- BR.13 denotes the "Excellent" rating in the BREEAM Sustainability Assessment, indicating that a building has achieved outstanding performance across the scheme's criteria such as energy efficiency, water use, waste management and indoor environmental quality. It is the second-highest classification, just below the top "Outstanding" level.
[BR.14] Very Good -- In BREEAM, the "Very Good" rating is the second-tier level of achievement, indicating that a building meets most sustainability criteria and exceeds basic compliance across categories such as energy, water, waste and materials. It reflects solid environmental performance while leaving room for further improvement toward the highest "Excellent" standard.
[BR.15] Good -- BR.15 denotes the "Good" rating within BREEAM's sustainability assessment categories, indicating a building meets a solid level of environmental performance across criteria such as energy, water, waste and materials. Projects achieving this level satisfy the benchmark standards set by BREEAM but fall short of the higher "Very Good" and "Excellent" thresholds. It reflects a commitment to sustainability while allowing room for further improvement.
[BR.16] Pass -- BR.16 Pass denotes the basic compliance threshold for a BREEAM credit, confirming that the project meets the minimum required standards for that sustainability category. It indicates that the design or construction satisfies the essential criteria but does not achieve the higher "Good," "Very Good," or "Excellent" performance levels.
