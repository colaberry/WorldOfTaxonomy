[b2b_unfair] Assessment of Unfair Terms in B2B Data Contracts (Art 13) -- The entry refers to the EU Data Act (2023/2854) requirement to evaluate clauses that create an imbalance in business-to-business data-sharing contracts, as set out in Article 13. It guides regulators and market participants in identifying, assessing and remedying unfair terms to ensure transparency and equitable bargaining power in B2B data arrangements.
[ch_2] Chapter II - B2C and B2B Data Sharing from IoT -- Chapter II of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) sets out the rules for sharing data generated by Internet-of-Things devices in both business-to-consumer (B2C) and business-to-business (B2B) contexts. It obliges data holders to provide non-discriminatory access to such data under fair, transparent terms, supporting innovation while protecting users' rights and competition.
[ch_3] Chapter III - Obligations for Data Holders -- Chapter III of the EU Data Act outlines the duties that data holders must fulfil, including providing fair and non-discriminatory access to data, maintaining appropriate security measures, and meeting transparency and reporting requirements when sharing data with third parties.
[ch_4] Chapter IV - Unfair Contractual Terms -- Chapter IV of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) establishes rules to prevent unfair contractual terms in data-sharing agreements, requiring transparency, proportionality and balance in obligations and rights. It empowers regulators to monitor compliance and offers parties legal remedies when clauses are deemed inequitable.
[ch_5] Chapter V - Public Sector Access to Private Data -- Chapter V of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) sets out the rules for public-sector bodies to access private-sector data. It defines the circumstances, procedures and safeguards that must be applied to ensure data is shared for public interest purposes while protecting commercial confidentiality and personal privacy. The chapter also establishes oversight mechanisms and compensation arrangements for data providers.
[ch_6] Chapter VI - Switching Between Cloud Services -- Chapter VI of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) sets out the framework for switching between cloud services, requiring providers to ensure data portability, transparent migration procedures and reasonable timeframes for users to move their data. It establishes technical standards, safeguards for data integrity and continuity, and obligations for contracts to support seamless transitions.
[ch_7] Chapter VII - International Data Access Safeguards -- Chapter VII sets out safeguards for international data access under the EU Data Act, defining conditions under which data may be shared across borders, ensuring protection of commercial confidentiality and personal data, and outlining oversight mechanisms.
[ch_8] Chapter VIII - Interoperability -- Chapter VIII of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) sets out the rules governing interoperability of data, requiring the development and use of common technical standards and interfaces to enable seamless data exchange between businesses and public sector bodies. It obliges data holders and intermediaries to provide access under fair, transparent, and non-discriminatory conditions, supporting a competitive digital market.

[exceptional_need] Access for Exceptional Need (Art 14-18) -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 grants a specific exemption allowing parties to access data for exceptional need under Articles 14 to 18, typically for public-interest purposes such as health emergencies or safety-critical functions. This entry defines the conditions, safeguards and procedural steps required to invoke the exceptional-need provision
[fair_terms] Fair, Reasonable and Non-Discriminatory Terms (Art 8-12) -- The Data Act obliges providers of data-sharing services to use fair, reasonable and non-discriminatory terms under Articles 8 to 12, guaranteeing transparent pricing, equal access and prohibiting abusive conditions. This aims to create a level playing field for all participants in the EU internal market.
[interop_standards] Interoperability Standards for Data Spaces (Art 33-36) -- The entry defines the technical and organisational standards that must be applied to ensure seamless, secure and legally compliant exchange of data across EU data spaces, as required by Articles 33-36 of the Data Act (EU 2023/2854). It covers common data models, APIs, metadata schemas and certification procedures that enable interoperability among public, private and research-sector data platforms.
[manufacturer_duty] Manufacturer Design Obligations (Art 3) -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 requires manufacturers to embed design features that enable the lawful sharing and reuse of data generated by their products. Under Article 3, they must ensure technical compatibility, provide clear documentation, and facilitate access for users and third parties while respecting security and privacy safeguards.
[portability] Data Portability Between Cloud Services -- The EU Data Act (2023/2854) mandates that cloud providers enable users to move their data to other services in a structured, commonly used format without undue technical or contractual barriers. It requires clear information on how to request, receive and transmit data, and safeguards the continuity of services during transfers.
[safeguards] Safeguards for Non-EU Government Data Requests (Art 27-28) -- The Data Act obliges providers to apply strict safeguards when a non-EU government asks for data, as set out in Articles 27 and 28. Requests must be based on a clear legal basis, be proportionate and respect the confidentiality of business-sensitive information, and are subject to judicial review and possible challenge, with transparency obligations for the provider.
[smart_contracts] Smart Contract Requirements (Art 36) -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 entry on smart contracts sets out the obligations for automated agreements that process personal data, requiring clear disclosure of data-processing purposes, robust security measures, and mechanisms to uphold data-subject rights such as access, correction and deletion. Compliance with Article 36 ensures that smart-contract code and its operators meet these transparency and accountability standards.
[switching_charges] Gradual Elimination of Switching Charges -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 entry on "switching_charges" outlines the EU's requirement to phase out fees charged to consumers when they change data service providers, supporting market competition and consumer freedom. It specifies timelines, conditions, and compliance obligations for businesses to ensure a smooth, cost-free transition for users.
[switching_rights] Right to Switch Cloud Provider (Art 23-26) -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 grants users the right to switch cloud providers, obliging providers to supply data in a portable, machine-readable format and to enable secure transfer within a set timeframe. Articles 23-26 set conditions for interoperability, prohibit lock-in practices, and require clear information on switching procedures and costs. This provision aims to enhance market competition and user control over their data.
[user_access] User Right to Access IoT Data (Art 4) -- The Data Act (EU) 2023/2854 grants individuals a right to request and receive data generated by IoT devices that relate to them. Under Article 4, providers must deliver the information in a structured, commonly-used format without undue delay, subject only to limited exceptions for security, privacy or other legitimate reasons.
[user_sharing] User Right to Share Data with Third Parties (Art 5) -- Article 5 of the EU Data Act (2023/2854) creates a user right to share data generated by or about them with third parties, such as service providers and data platforms. The provision outlines transparency, fairness and technical interoperability requirements to enable sharing while safeguarding confidentiality and competition.
