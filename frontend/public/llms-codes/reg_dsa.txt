[advertising] Online Advertising Transparency (Art 26) -- Article 26 of the EU Digital Services Act obliges online platforms to disclose key details about each advertisement they host, including the identity of the advertiser, the purpose of the ad and the criteria used for targeting. This transparency requirement aims to give users clear insight into why they see specific ads and to facilitate accountability for the content and practices of advertisers.

[board] European Board for Digital Services (Art 61) -- The European Board for Digital Services, established under Article 61 of the Digital Services Act, is an independent EU-level body that coordinates the work of national digital services authorities, ensures consistent enforcement of the DSA across member states, and provides guidance on compliance, risk assessment and mitigation for online platforms.

It outlines the general obligations of providers, including transparency, cooperation with authorities and the principle of proportionality, and sets out the procedural rules for the Commission's oversight of the Act.

[ch_3] Chapter III - Due Diligence Obligations -- Chapter III of the EU Digital Services Act sets out due-diligence obligations for online platforms, requiring systematic risk assessment, mitigation measures, transparent reporting and cooperation with authorities to protect fundamental rights and public safety. This chapter applies to very large online platforms and ensures accountability for illegal content, disinformation and other systemic harms.

The provision seeks to enhance transparency and evidence-based policy while protecting fundamental rights.

[notice_action] Notice and Action Mechanism (Art 16) -- The Notice and Action Mechanism in Article 16 of the EU Digital Services Act requires online platforms to set up clear procedures for receiving and assessing third-party notices about illegal content, to act promptly by removing or disabling access, and to provide transparent reporting on the actions taken.
[recommender] Recommender System Transparency (Art 27) -- The Digital Services Act (EU 2022/2065) requires online platforms to disclose how their recommender systems work, including the main parameters, data sources and any automated profiling that influence content suggestions. Providers must give users clear information on why specific items are suggested and allow them to adjust or opt out of such personalization. This transparency aim is set out in Article 27 of the regulation.
[risk_assess] Systemic Risk Assessment for VLOPs (Art 34) -- The entry requires Very Large Online Platforms to conduct a systemic risk assessment under Article 34 of the Digital Services Act, identifying and analysing risks to public security, fundamental rights, and societal cohesion that arise from their services. The assessment must be documented and reported to EU authorities.
[risk_mitigate] Risk Mitigation for VLOPs (Art 35) -- The entry refers to the Digital Services Act (EU) 2022/2065 requirement that Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) must assess systemic risks arising from the distribution of illegal or harmful content, disinformation, and manipulation, and implement measures to mitigate those risks under Article 35. This risk-mitigation duty includes ongoing monitoring, reporting, and the adoption of technical and organisational safeguards.
[sec_1] Section 1 - Obligations for All Intermediary Services -- Section 1 of the EU Digital Services Act establishes baseline duties for every intermediary service, obliging platforms to provide clear terms, implement transparent mechanisms for addressing illegal content, assess systemic risks, and cooperate with national authorities in enforcement and reporting. This foundational framework ensures a uniform standard of responsibility across the digital ecosystem.
[sec_2] Section 2 - Additional Obligations for Hosting Services -- Section 2 of the EU Digital Services Act (2022/2065) outlines extra duties that hosting services must meet, such as ensuring traceability of illegal content, implementing risk assessment and mitigation measures, and cooperating with authorities. It expands on transparency, user protection, and accountability requirements to curb the spread of harmful material.
[sec_3] Section 3 - Additional Obligations for Online Platforms -- Section 3 of the Digital Services Act sets out extra duties for online platforms, requiring them to assess systemic risks, implement preventive and mitigative measures, ensure transparency of algorithms and advertising, and cooperate with national authorities in investigations and enforcement. These obligations aim to protect users, uphold public order and support a fair digital market.
[sec_4] Section 4 - Additional Obligations for Online Marketplaces -- Section 4 of the EU Digital Services Act outlines extra duties for online marketplaces, requiring them to verify the legitimacy of traders, ensure that listed products comply with safety standards, provide clear information about sellers and listings, and cooperate with authorities on illegal or harmful content. These obligations aim to strengthen consumer protection, market transparency and accountability across the European digital single market.
[sec_5] Section 5 - Additional Obligations for VLOPs and VLOSEs -- Section 5 of the EU Digital Services Act sets extra duties for Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) and Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs). It requires these services to implement risk-assessment and mitigation measures, provide transparent advertising and recommendation systems, and establish independent audit mechanisms to protect users and ensure market fairness.
[terms_service] Terms of Service (Art 14) -- Article 14 of the Digital Services Act requires online platforms to publish clear, publicly-available terms of service that set out the conditions under which users may access and use the service, the rights and obligations of both parties, and the procedures for handling disputes or violations.
[transparency] Transparency Reporting (Art 15) -- The Digital Services Act (EU) 2022/2065 obliges designated online platforms to publish a transparency report under Article 15, detailing the removal of illegal content, enforcement actions, algorithmic recommendation systems and their impact, as well as the number of user complaints received and how they were handled.
